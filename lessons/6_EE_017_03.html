One-Step Multiplication Equations

Skill ID: 6.EE.017.03  •  [Student Name] placeholders shown in blue

Learning Objective
I can solve one-step multiplication equations by finding the unknown factor using the inverse operation of division.
Core Explanations (5 teaching variants — expand to review)
Academic — Concept-Forward
A One-Step Multiplication Equation is a mathematical statement where an unknown number is multiplied by a known number, and the entire expression is equal to a known product. Our goal is to discover that unknown number. For example, consider the equation 6 * ? = 48. Here, 6 is a known factor, 48 is the product, and ? represents the unknown factor we need to find. To solve this, we use the inverse operation: division. Multiplication and division are like two sides of the same coin – they undo each other. If multiplying 6 by ? gives us 48, then dividing 48 by 6 will reveal what ? must be. So, 48 / 6 = ?, which means ? = 8. This is similar to how a key unlocks a lock; division is the key that unlocks the value of the unknown in a multiplication equation. [Student Name], once you understand this fundamental inverse relationship, solving these equations becomes much clearer. You're simply performing the opposite action to find the missing piece, [Student Name]. This process is precise and always works.

Worked Examples
Scaffolded (Step-by-Step)
Problem: Maya is selling handmade friendship bracelets. She sells each bracelet for $4. If she earned a total of $36 this weekend, how many bracelets did she sell? [Student Name], let's break down how to solve this one-step multiplication equation step by step. We want to find out how many bracelets Maya sold, given her earnings and the price per bracelet. This type of problem is perfect for setting up an equation where a number (the price) times an unknown (the number of bracelets) equals a total. 1. 💡 Hint: What information do you know, and what are you trying to find? How can you write this relationship using a variable? First, let's identify what we know and what we need to discover. We know Maya sells each bracelet for $4. This is the constant amount per item. We also know she earned a total of $36. This is our grand total. What we don't know is the number of bracelets she sold. Let's use the variable b to represent this unknown quantity – the number of bracelets. Since the cost per bracelet multiplied by the number of bracelets equals the total earnings, we can set up the equation: 4b = 36 This equation clearly shows that 4 multiplied by b (the number of bracelets) equals 36. 2. 💡 Hint: How do you "undo" multiplication to get the variable by itself? Our goal is to isolate the variable b on one side of the equation. Currently, b is being multiplied by 4. To "undo" multiplication, we use its inverse operation, which is division. To keep the equation balanced and fair, whatever operation we perform on one side of the equation, we must perform on the other side. So, we will divide both sides of the equation by 4: 4b / 4 = 36 / 4 3. 💡 Hint: Now perform the calculation to find the value of the variable. When we divide 4b by 4, the 4s cancel out, leaving us with just b. On the right side, we perform the division: 36 divided by 4 equals 9. b = 9 This tells us that Maya sold 9 bracelets. 4. 💡 Hint: How can you make sure your answer is correct? It's always a good idea to check your solution! To do this, substitute the value you found for b back into the original equation: 4 * 9 = 36 36 = 36 Since both sides of the equation are equal, our answer is correct! Maya indeed sold 9 bracelets to earn $36. Great job, [Student Name]!
Standard
Problem: A local bakery uses 12 pounds of flour to make one batch of their popular apple pies. If they used a total of 180 pounds of flour yesterday, how many batches of apple pies did they make? Solving one-step multiplication equations involves finding the value of an unknown variable. Here’s how to approach this problem: 1. Identify the variable and write the equation. We know that 12 pounds of flour are used for one batch of pies. We also know the total flour used was 180 pounds. Let's use p to represent the number of batches of apple pies. The total flour used is the amount per batch multiplied by the number of batches, so our equation is: 12p = 180 2. Isolate the variable using the inverse operation. The variable p is being multiplied by 12. To get p by itself (isolate it), we need to perform the inverse operation of multiplication, which is division. We must divide both sides of the equation by 12 to keep it balanced. 12p / 12 = 180 / 12 3. Perform the calculation to solve for the variable. Dividing both sides: p = 15 So, the bakery made 15 batches of apple pies. 4. Check your solution. To ensure our answer is correct, substitute the value of p back into the original equation: 12 * 15 = 180 180 = 180 Since both sides are equal, [Student Name], our solution is correct!
Challenge
Problem: Coach Davis ordered new soccer goals for the school, and each goal cost $175. He received a total bill of $1400. How many soccer goals did Coach Davis order? This problem involves a slightly larger number, but the core idea of one-step multiplication equations remains the same. Let's tackle it, keeping an eye out for common errors. 1. Understand the problem and set up the equation. We know the cost of one soccer goal is $175. We also know the total cost was $1400. We need to find the number of goals. Let's use g for the number of goals. The equation is: 175g = 1400 Common Error: Some students might accidentally set up 1400g = 175, thinking the larger number should be the coefficient. Always remember the coefficient (a in ax=b) is the "per unit" amount. 2. Choose the correct inverse operation. Since g is being multiplied by 175, we need to divide both sides of the equation by 175 to isolate g. 175g / 175 = 1400 / 175 Common Error: A common mistake is to multiply both sides by 175. Remember, you want to "undo" the multiplication, so division is the key! 3. Perform the calculation carefully. g = 8 Coach Davis ordered 8 soccer goals. This division might require long division or careful mental math. Common Error: Calculation errors, especially with larger numbers, are frequent. Double-check your division. 4. Check your answer. Substitute g = 8 back into the original equation: 175 * 8 = 1400 1400 = 1400 Since the equation holds true, [Student Name], our solution is correct!

Parent ELI5
Hello [Parent Name]! Let's break down One-Step Multiplication Equations so [they] can feel super confident. Imagine an equation is like a balanced scale. Whatever is on one side must be equal to what's on the other. In math, we use a letter, often 'x,' to represent a 'mystery number' that makes the equation true. Here’s how it works: 1. Understand the Goal: When you see an equation like '3 times a number equals 15' (which in math looks like 3x = 15), your goal is to figure out what that 'x' (the mystery number) is. We need to get 'x' all by itself on one side of the scale. 2. Use the Inverse Operation: To 'undo' multiplication, we use its opposite, which is division. So, if 'x' is being multiplied by 3, we need to divide by 3 to get 'x' by itself. 3. Keep the Scale Balanced: This is crucial! Whatever you do to one side of the equation (like dividing by 3), you must do the exact same thing to the other side to keep it balanced. So, if you divide 3x by 3, you also have to divide 15 by 3. 4. Solve for the Mystery: After dividing both sides, you'll have 'x = 5' (because 3x divided by 3 is x, and 15 divided by 3 is 5). Example from everyday life: Let's say [Parent Name] bought 4 identical cases of soda for a party, and the total cost was $28. How much did each case cost? Equation: 4 (cost per case) = $28. Or, 4x = 28. To find x, divide both sides by 4: (4x)/4 = 28/4. So, x = 7. Each case cost $7. See? [Parent Name], [they] will master this concept and be able to teach [Student Name] confidently!
