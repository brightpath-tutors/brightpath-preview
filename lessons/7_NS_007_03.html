Core Explanations (5 variants — expand to review)
Academic — Concept-Forward

Adding rational numbers involves combining quantities that can be expressed as a fraction, a/b, where 'a' and 'b' are integers and 'b' is not zero. This includes fractions, mixed numbers, and terminating or repeating decimals. The fundamental mechanism relies on understanding signed numbers. If the signs are the same, you add their absolute values and keep the common sign. For instance, when adding (-2/3) + (-1/6), you first find a common denominator, changing it to (-4/6) + (-1/6). Since both are negative, add their absolute values 4/6 + 1/6 = 5/6, and keep the negative sign, resulting in -5/6. If the signs are different, you find the difference between their absolute values and use the sign of the number with the larger absolute value. For example, to calculate 3.5 + (-1.25), you find the difference between 3.5 and 1.25, which is 2.25. Since 3.5 has a larger absolute value and is positive, the answer is 2.25. Think of it like a tug-of-war, [Student Name]. The team pulling with more force (larger absolute value) determines the direction (the sign) of the final movement. Just as you combine forces, [they] can combine these numerical values to find a single, precise result. [Student Name], mastering this takes careful attention to signs and denominators.

A rational number is any number that can be written as a simple fraction, a/b, where a and b are integers and b is not zero. This broadens the types of numbers you can combine. To add them, you'll follow predictable steps: first, ensure you have a common format (like all fractions with a common denominator, or all decimals). Worked Examples
Scaffolded (Step-by-Step)

Alright [Student Name], let's break down adding rational numbers, step-by-step. Imagine you're calculating your total change in temperature for the day. The temperature started at 2.5°F, then dropped by 7.25°F, and later rose by 4°F. What's the final temperature change relative to the start? This means we need to add 2.5 + (-7.25) + 4. Let's tackle it:

  1. 💡 Hint: Make sure all your numbers are in the same format. First, let's make sure everything is a decimal. We have 2.5, -7.25, and 4. They are all decimals, which is great! Now, we can group positive numbers together and negative numbers together to simplify.
  2. 💡 Hint: Combine numbers with the same sign first. Let's add the positive numbers: 2.5 + 4. This gives us 6.5. Now our problem is 6.5 + (-7.25). We're adding a positive and a negative number.
  3. 💡 Hint: When adding numbers with different signs, think about their absolute values. We need to find the difference between their absolute values. The absolute value of 6.5 is 6.5. The absolute value of -7.25 is 7.25. The larger absolute value is 7.25. So, we subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger: 7.25 - 6.5 = 0.75.
  4. 💡 Hint: The sign of your answer matches the number with the larger absolute value. Since -7.25 had the larger absolute value, our answer will be negative. So, 6.5 + (-7.25) = -0.75.

Check: You can estimate: 2.5 is about 3, -7.25 is about -7, and 4 is 4. So 3 + (-7) + 4 = -4 + 4 = 0. Our answer, -0.75, is close to 0, which makes sense. The temperature dropped by a total of 0.75°F. [they] can definitely solve this type of problem!

Standard

[Student Name], let's walk through an example of adding rational numbers. Imagine you're keeping track of a hockey team's performance. In the first period, they scored 3 goals and conceded 2 goals, giving them a net score of +1. In the second period, they scored 1 goal but conceded 4, giving them a net score of -3. In the third period, they conceded 1 goal. What is their overall net score for the entire game?

This means we need to add the net changes from each period: (+1) + (-3) + (-1).

  1. Identify the numbers and their signs: We have 1 (positive), -3 (negative), and -1 (negative). We are adding these three rational numbers together.
  2. Combine numbers with the same sign: It's often easiest to group numbers with the same sign first. We have two negative numbers: -3 and -1. Adding them together gives us -4 (because when you add two negative numbers, you combine their absolute values and keep the negative sign). So now our problem is 1 + (-4).
  3. Add numbers with different signs: Now we have a positive number (1) and a negative number (-4). To add numbers with different signs, we find the difference between their absolute values. The absolute value of 1 is 1, and the absolute value of -4 is 4. The difference is 4 - 1 = 3.
  4. Determine the sign of the sum: The sign of the sum will be the same as the number with the larger absolute value. Since -4 has a larger absolute value than 1 (4 > 1), our answer will be negative. Therefore, 1 + (-4) = -3.

Check: We can think of it as starting at 1 on a number line, then moving 4 units to the left. You land on -3. The overall net score for the hockey team is -3, meaning they were down by 3 goals at the end of the game.

Challenge

Let's tackle a trickier problem, [Student Name]. Suppose a stock price changes throughout the week. On Monday, it gains $1.75. On Tuesday, it drops by $3.50. On Wednesday, it gains 2 1/4 dollars. On Thursday, it drops by $0.80. What is the total change in the stock price over these four days?

We need to find the sum: $1.75 + (-$3.50) + 2 1/4 + (-$0.80).

  1. Standardize the format: The first common error is mixing fractions and decimals without converting. We have decimals and a mixed fraction. Let's convert 2 1/4 to a decimal: 2 + 1/4 = 2 + 0.25 = 2.25. So our problem becomes: $1.75 + (-$3.50) + $2.25 + (-$0.80).
  2. Group by sign and combine: Another common error is adding/subtracting in order from left to right, which can lead to confusion with signs. It's often easier to add all the positive numbers together and all the negative numbers together first. Positive additions: $1.75 + $2.25 = $4.00. Negative additions: (-$3.50) + (-$0.80). Remember, when adding two negative numbers, you combine their absolute values and keep the negative sign. So, $3.50 + $0.80 = $4.30. This means the total negative change is -$4.30.
  3. Combine the total positive and total negative: Now we have $4.00 + (-$4.30). Here, a common error is to forget that you subtract absolute values and take the sign of the larger one. We have a positive $4.00 and a negative $4.30. The absolute value of $4.30 is greater than $4.00. Subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger: $4.30 - $4.00 = $0.30. Since -$4.30 had the larger absolute value, the final sum will be negative.

The total change in the stock price is -$0.30.

Check: We can quickly estimate: 2 + (-3) + 2 + (-1) = -0. This is close to our answer of -0.30, so it seems reasonable.

Hello [Parent Name]! Let's break down 'Adding Rational Numbers' so [they] can confidently help [Student Name].

Imagine [they] is managing a budget or a bank account. That’s exactly what adding rational numbers helps with!

Rational Numbers are just numbers that can be written as a fraction, like integers (whole numbers and their negatives), decimals, and fractions themselves. So, $5, -2, 1/2, -0.75$ are all rational numbers.
