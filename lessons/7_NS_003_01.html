Worked Examples
Scaffolded (Step-by-Step)

Alright, [Student Name], let's break down adding integers with different signs. It might seem tricky at first, but with a few simple steps, you'll see it’s like solving a mini-puzzle. Let's work through this problem: What is -12 + 7?

  1. 💡 Hint: Think about what the signs mean for movement or value. First, identify the signs of the numbers. We have -12 (a negative number) and +7 (a positive number). Since their signs are different, we know we won't just be adding their values together. Instead, they will 'work against' each other.
  1. 💡 Hint: To find out how much 'impact' each number has, ignore its sign for a moment. Next, find the absolute value of each number. The absolute value of -12 is 12 (it's 12 units away from zero). The absolute value of +7 is 7 (it's 7 units away from zero).
  1. 💡 Hint: When signs are different, you're looking for the 'difference' or 'net change'. Now, subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger absolute value. In this case, subtract 7 from 12: 12 - 7 = 5. This '5' is the magnitude of our answer.
  1. 💡 Hint: The 'stronger' number (the one with the larger absolute value) determines the final direction or sign. Finally, apply the sign of the number with the larger absolute value to your answer. Since -12 has a larger absolute value (12 is greater than 7) and -12 is negative, our answer will be negative. So, -12 + 7 = -5.

Check: Imagine you owe your friend 12 dollars (-12). Then you earn 7 dollars and pay [them] back (+7). You still owe your friend 5 dollars (-5). The math works!

Standard

Let's work through another problem involving adding integers with different signs, [Student Name]. This skill is super useful, whether you're tracking your savings or understanding temperature changes. Here’s the problem: Calculate 9 + (-15).

  1. First, let's identify the signs of the integers involved. We have a positive integer, 9, and a negative integer, -15. Since they have different signs, we'll need to find the difference between their absolute values.
  1. Next, determine the absolute value for each integer. The absolute value of 9 is 9. The absolute value of -15 is 15. The absolute value tells us how far a number is from zero, regardless of its direction.
  1. Now, subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger absolute value. Comparing 9 and 15, 9 is the smaller absolute value. So, we calculate 15 - 9, which equals 6. This '6' represents the numerical value of our answer before we assign its sign.
  1. The final step is to apply the sign of the integer that had the larger absolute value. In this case, 15 (from -15) is greater than 9 (from +9). Since -15 is a negative number, our final answer will be negative. Therefore, 9 + (-15) = -6.

Check: Imagine the outside temperature is 9 degrees Fahrenheit (a positive 9). Then, a cold front moves in, and the temperature drops by 15 degrees (adding -15). To find the new temperature, you subtract 15 from 9, resulting in a temperature of -6 degrees Fahrenheit. This confirms our calculation.

Challenge

Tackling multi-step problems with mixed-sign integers can be a bit more complex, [Student Name], but knowing common pitfalls will help you avoid them. Let’s look at: Calculate -5 + 13 + (-8).

  1. Work with two numbers at a time. It's often easiest to combine the first two integers, then combine that result with the third. So, let's start with -5 + 13. A common error here is just adding 5 and 13 to get 18. Avoid this: Remember they have different signs! You need to subtract their absolute values (13 - 5 = 8) and take the sign of the larger absolute value (13 is positive). So, -5 + 13 = 8.
  1. Combine the result with the next integer. Now we have 8 + (-8). Another common error might be forgetting that adding a negative is like subtracting. Avoid this: When you add a positive number and a negative number with the same absolute value, they cancel each other out. Their sum is zero. 8 + (-8) = 0.
  1. Review your steps and the final answer. The result of the first combination (-5 + 13) was 8. The result of the second combination (8 + (-8)) was 0. So, -5 + 13 + (-8) = 0.

Check: You can try grouping differently. What if we did 13 + (-8) first? That's 5. Then add -5: 5 + (-5) = 0. Both ways give us the same answer, confirming our steps.
