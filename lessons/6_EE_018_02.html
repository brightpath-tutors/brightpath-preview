One-Step Division Equations

Skill ID: 6.EE.018.02  •  [Student Name] placeholders shown in blue

Learning Objective
I can solve one-step division equations by using inverse operations to find the unknown variable.
Core Explanations (5 teaching variants — expand to review)
Academic — Concept-Forward
A One-Step Division Equation is a mathematical statement showing that two expressions are equal, where an unknown variable is involved in a single division operation, and it requires only one inverse operation to solve. For example, consider the equation x / 5 = 12. Here, 'x' is the unknown variable, and it's being divided by 5. Our goal is to find the value of 'x' that makes the equation true. To do this, we use the inverse operation of division, which is multiplication. Just like balancing a perfectly weighted seesaw, whatever operation you perform on one side of the equation, you must perform on the other side to maintain equality. If [Student Name] has x / 5 on one side and 12 on the other, to 'undo' the division by 5 and isolate 'x', you multiply both sides by 5. So, (x / 5) 5 = 12 5. This simplifies to x = 60. [Student Name], by consistently applying the inverse operation, [they] can always find the correct solution. This mechanism ensures the equation remains balanced, leading you directly to the value of the variable.
Confidence — Encouraging Tone
Remember when you mastered solving one-step multiplication equations, like 3x = 21? You knew that to find 'x', you had to perform the opposite, or inverse, operation: division. You'd divide both sides by 3, right? Well, [Student Name], solving one-step division equations uses the exact same powerful idea! If you see an equation like x / 6 = 8, instead of seeing something entirely new, think of it as just the 'reverse' challenge. Just as division 'undoes' multiplication, multiplication 'undoes' division! To get 'x' by itself when it's being divided by 6, what's the inverse operation [they] should use? That's right, multiplication! You simply multiply both sides of the equation by 6. So, (x / 6) 6 = 8 6, which gives you x = 48. You've already built the foundation for this skill, [Student Name]! This isn't a huge leap; it's a confident step forward using a strategy you've already perfected.
Engagement — Real-World Hook
Did you know that mastering one-step division equations can literally help manage a major league sports team's budget? Imagine an NFL team that has a fixed amount of money, say $24,000,000, to equally distribute among 'x' number of coaches. If the target average salary per coach is $2,000,000, then you're essentially solving an equation like 24,000,000 / x = 2,000,000 to find 'x'! It might seem complex, but the core is a one-step division equation. Or consider a more common scenario, [Student Name]. Your school's student council has $300 to buy pizzas for a school event. If each pizza costs $15, you use 300 / 15 = x to find out 'x', the number of pizzas. But what if you knew how many pizzas you wanted, say 'x', and knew the total cost, but not the price per pizza? An equation like 300 / x = 15 emerges! You might not realize it, [Student Name], but these seemingly simple equations are the backbone of smart financial planning, from distributing funds for a national park project to ensuring every student in a class of 'x' shares 100 markers fairly. It's a powerful tool!
Stability — Clear & Structured
Let's get a clear, calm understanding of one-step division equations. First, the definition: A one-step division equation is an algebraic statement where a variable is divided by a single numerical value, and the equation can be solved in just one step using the inverse operation of multiplication. For example, consider the equation m / 8 = 11. Here, 'm' is the variable we need to find, and it is being divided by 8. To solve for 'm', we apply the inverse operation: multiplication. We multiply both sides of the equation by 8. So, (m / 8) 8 = 11 8. This simplifies to m = 88. [Student Name], the clear, systematic process ensures you always arrive at the correct answer. The application of this skill is straightforward: whenever you know a total amount that has been divided equally into parts, and you know either the number of parts or the size of each part, you can use a one-step division equation to find the missing information. For instance, if you know 9 friends shared 'm' dollars equally and each got $15, you'd set up m / 9 = 15. [Student Name] can then calmly multiply both sides by 9 to find m = 135. This predictable structure makes solving these equations reliable every time.
Bridge — Connects Prior Knowledge
Before we dive into division equations, let's quickly review a skill you've already mastered: solving one-step multiplication equations. Remember an equation like 4n = 28? To find the value of 'n', you understood that '4n' means 4 multiplied by 'n'. To 'undo' that multiplication, you performed the inverse operation: division. So, you divided both sides of the equation by 4, resulting in n = 7. This principle of using inverse operations to isolate the variable is a fundamental building block in algebra, and it's something [Student Name] already confidently uses. Now, for the bridge to our new skill: just as multiplication and division are inverse operations, they mirror each other in equations. If you know how to 'undo' multiplication with division, you're perfectly equipped to 'undo' division with multiplication! Think of it like putting on socks and then taking them off – one action reverses the other. This makes the leap to one-step division equations very straightforward for [Student Name]. Our new skill applies this inverse relationship. When you encounter an equation like k / 10 = 5, where 'k' is being divided by 10, your goal is still to get 'k' by itself. To 'undo' the division by 10, you simply multiply both sides of the equation by 10. So, (k / 10) 10 = 5 10, which simplifies to k = 50. You're leveraging your existing understanding of inverse operations to tackle a slightly different but equally conquerable challenge!
Worked Examples
Scaffolded (Step-by-Step)
Hey [Student Name]! Let's tackle a common problem that uses a one-step division equation. Imagine Isabella worked hard walking dogs and earned a total amount of money, but we don't know that total yet. She decided to save all [their] earnings for 8 weeks. If [they] put away $15 each week, what were [their] total earnings? We can write this as an equation: x / 8 = 15. 1. 💡 Hint: What operation is happening to 'x'? What's the opposite? Look at the equation x / 8 = 15. The variable 'x' (Isabella's total earnings) is being divided by 8 (the number of weeks). To figure out what 'x' is, we need to 'undo' that division. The inverse (opposite) operation of division is multiplication. 2. 💡 Hint: How do we keep the equation balanced? To 'undo' dividing by 8, we multiply both sides of the equation by 8. Remember, whatever you do to one side of the equal sign, you must do to the other side to keep the equation balanced, like a perfectly balanced seesaw! So, we write: (x / 8) 8 = 15 8. 3. 💡 Hint: Time to calculate the answer! On the left side, multiplying by 8 'cancels out' the division by 8, leaving just 'x'. On the right side, 15 * 8 equals 120. So, x = 120. 4. 💡 Hint: Does your answer make sense? Now, let's check our answer by plugging 'x' back into the original equation: 120 / 8 = 15. Is that true? Yes, 120 divided by 8 is indeed 15. So, Isabella's total earnings were $120. Great job, [Student Name]!
Standard
Let's work through another one-step division equation. Imagine a school in Michigan held a spirit week event, and a total of 225 students participated in the 'Dress Up Your Teacher' contest. If these students formed groups of 9 for the final presentation, how many groups (n) were there? The problem can be represented by the equation: 225 / n = 9. 1. Understand the Equation: Here, the total number of students (225) is divided by the unknown number of groups (n), resulting in 9 students per group. Our goal is to find the value of n. 2. Isolate the Variable: Since n is in the denominator, the easiest way to start is to get n out of the denominator. We do this by multiplying both sides of the equation by n. This transforms 225 / n = 9 into 225 = 9n. Now we have a multiplication equation. 3. Solve for n: Now n is being multiplied by 9. To isolate n and find its value, we perform the inverse operation of multiplication, which is division. We divide both sides of the equation by 9. So, 225 / 9 = (9n) / 9. 4. Calculate the Result: Performing the division, 225 / 9 equals 25. Therefore, n = 25. There were 25 groups. 5. Check Your Solution: To ensure our answer is correct, we substitute n = 25 back into the original equation: 225 / 25 = 9. This statement is true, as 225 divided by 25 is indeed 9. This means [Student Name] found the correct number of groups!
Challenge
This next problem might seem a little tricky, [Student Name], but we can break it down. Imagine a local farmer in Iowa harvested a total area of cornfield, but we don't know the exact size yet. The farmer decided to divide this total area into small plots, each measuring 0.5 acres, for special experimental crops. If there were exactly 48 of these 0.5-acre plots, what was the total area of the cornfield? The equation for this is: x / 0.5 = 48. 1. Identify the Operation: Here, an unknown total area 'x' is being divided by 0.5 (the size of each plot). The result is 48 (the number of plots). We need to find 'x'. 2. Apply the Inverse Operation: The inverse of division is multiplication. To isolate 'x', we need to multiply both sides of the equation by 0.5. Common Error Alert: A common mistake here is to divide 48 by 0.5, thinking you're 'undoing' a division. However, 'x' is being divided by 0.5. To get 'x' alone, you must multiply by 0.5. If you divided 48 by 0.5, you'd get 96, which would imply x = 96. If you put that back in: 96 / 0.5 = 192, not 48. So dividing 48 by 0.5 is incorrect for this equation. 3. Calculate: So, we multiply: x = 48 0.5. Multiplying by 0.5 is the same as finding half of a number. So, 48 * 0.5 = 24. 4. Check Your Answer: Let's substitute x = 24 back into the original equation: 24 / 0.5 = 48. Is this true? Yes! If you divide 24 by one-half, you're essentially asking how many halves are in 24, which is 48. The total area of the cornfield was 24 acres.
