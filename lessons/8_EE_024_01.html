Equations with Distribution

Skill ID: 8.EE.024.01  •  [Student Name] placeholders shown in blue

Learning Objective
I can solve linear equations in one variable that require using the distributive property to simplify expressions, including those with rational coefficients and variables on both sides.
Core Explanations (5 teaching variants — expand to review)
Academic — Concept-Forward
Equations with Distribution are linear equations where one or more terms involve a factor multiplied by an expression inside parentheses, requiring the distributive property as a fundamental step towards finding the unknown variable. Precisely, it's about transforming an expression like a(b + c) into ab + ac within an equation. For example, consider the equation 5(2x - 3) + 7 = 42. To solve this, [Student Name], your first step is to distribute the 5: 10x - 15 + 7 = 42. Then, combine like terms: 10x - 8 = 42. From there, you isolate the variable using inverse operations, just like in simpler equations. This process is similar to a mail carrier delivering mail: each piece (the outer factor) must be delivered to every mailbox (each term inside the parentheses). If a carrier only delivers to the first house, some mail goes undelivered, just as if you only multiply the outer factor by the first term, you'll get an incorrect result. [Student Name], mastering this ensures every part of the expression is accounted for, leading you to the correct solution.

Equations with distribution are the next logical step, not a completely new challenge. The core idea is the same: simplify, then solve. The "new" part is simply applying the distributive property first, transforming those familiar parenthetical expressions into something you can manage with your existing skills. [Student Name], you already have the tools in your math toolkit; we're just adding a new technique for the initial setup. This makes even complex-looking equations totally achievable for you.

Stability — Clear & Structured
Equations with Distribution are mathematical statements where two expressions are set equal, and one or both expressions contain terms that require the distributive property to simplify. The distributive property allows us to multiply a single term by each term inside a set of parentheses. For instance, in 2(4x - 6) = 20, the first step is to distribute the 2 to both 4x and -6. This yields 8x - 12 = 20. Once this step is complete, [Student Name], the equation transforms into a familiar two-step linear equation. You would then add 12 to both sides: 8x = 32, and finally, divide both sides by 8 to find x = 4. This methodical process ensures you simplify the equation correctly before isolating the variable. In real-world applications, [Student Name], this property helps calculate budgets for a school dance, where 2($15 + $8) might represent tickets and decorations costs for two groups, simplifying to 2 $15 + 2 $8.

Scaffolded (Step-by-Step)
Hey [Student Name]! Let's walk through solving an equation with distribution step-by-step. Remember, distribution means multiplying the number outside the parentheses by every term inside. Take a look at this problem: 3(x - 5) + 7 = 16. We need to figure out what x is. 1. 💡 Hint: Start by sharing the number outside the parentheses with everything inside. First, distribute the 3 to each term within the parentheses. So, 3 x becomes 3x, and 3 -5 becomes -15. The equation now looks like this: 3x - 15 + 7 = 16. 2. 💡 Hint: Now, tidy up the side with the variable by combining any simple numbers. Next, combine the like terms on the left side of the equation. We have -15 and +7. Adding these together gives us -8. So, our equation simplifies to: 3x - 8 = 16. 3. 💡 Hint: To get the variable term by itself, undo any addition or subtraction by doing the opposite operation on both sides. To isolate the 3x term, we need to get rid of the -8. The opposite of subtracting 8 is adding 8. We must do this to both sides of the equation to keep it balanced: 3x - 8 + 8 = 16 + 8. This simplifies to 3x = 24. 4. 💡 Hint: Finally, to solve for the variable, undo any multiplication or division. Now, x is being multiplied by 3. To find x, we perform the opposite operation, which is dividing by 3. We divide both sides by 3: 3x / 3 = 24 / 3. This gives us x = 8. CHECK: Let's make sure our answer is correct! Substitute x = 8 back into the original equation: 3(8 - 5) + 7 = 16. Inside the parentheses, 8 - 5 is 3. So, 3(3) + 7 = 16. This becomes 9 + 7 = 16, which is 16 = 16. It checks out! You did great, [Student Name]!
Standard
Let's solve another equation, [Student Name]! This time, we'll tackle 5(2y + 4) - 12 = 38. Remember the goal is to isolate the variable y and find its value. We'll follow a standard process, keeping the equation balanced as we go. 1. Our first step is to apply the distributive property. The 5 outside the parentheses needs to be multiplied by each term inside: 2y and 4. So, 5 2y becomes 10y, and 5 4 becomes 20. The equation transforms into: 10y + 20 - 12 = 38. 2. Next, we need to simplify the left side of the equation by combining any like terms. In this case, we have the constant terms +20 and -12. Adding these together (20 - 12) gives us 8. So, our equation is now more concise: 10y + 8 = 38. 3. Now, we want to start isolating the term with our variable, 10y. To do this, we need to undo the addition of 8. The inverse operation of adding 8 is subtracting 8. We must subtract 8 from both sides of the equation to maintain balance: 10y + 8 - 8 = 38 - 8. This simplifies our equation to: 10y = 30. 4. Finally, y is being multiplied by 10. To solve for y, we perform the inverse operation, which is division. We divide both sides of the equation by 10: 10y / 10 = 30 / 10. This yields our solution: y = 3. CHECK: To confirm our answer, substitute y = 3 back into the original equation: 5(23 + 4) - 12 = 38. First, 23 is 6. So, 5(6 + 4) - 12 = 38. Inside the parentheses, 6 + 4 is 10. This makes it 5(10) - 12 = 38. Then, 50 - 12 = 38. Finally, 38 = 38. Our solution is correct!
Challenge
Alright, [Student Name], let's tackle a trickier equation that includes distribution and variables on both sides: 2(3z - 6) + 4 = 5z + 10. This one requires careful attention to avoid common pitfalls. 1. Distribute Carefully: Begin by distributing the 2 to every term inside the parentheses. 2 3z gives 6z, and 2 -6 gives -12. The equation becomes 6z - 12 + 4 = 5z + 10. A common error here is to only multiply 2 by 3z and forget about the -6. Always check that you've distributed to all terms within the parentheses. 2. Combine Like Terms (One Side at a Time): Before moving terms across the equals sign, simplify each side independently. On the left side, we have -12 + 4, which combines to -8. The equation is now 6z - 8 = 5z + 10. A common mistake is trying to combine 6z with 5z at this stage, or -8 with 10. Remember, you can only combine terms that are on the same side of the equals sign and are of the same type (variables with variables, constants with constants). 3. Gather Variable Terms: Now, let's get all the z terms onto one side. It's usually easier to move the smaller variable term. So, we'll subtract 5z from both sides: 6z - 5z - 8 = 5z - 5z + 10. This simplifies to z - 8 = 10. An error to avoid here is adding 5z instead of subtracting, which would create 11z and 0z on the other side, making the problem harder or incorrect. 4. Gather Constant Terms: With the z term isolated on one side, move the constant term -8 to the other side. Add 8 to both sides: z - 8 + 8 = 10 + 8. This leaves us with z = 18. Incorrectly subtracting 8 instead of adding would lead to z = 2, which is wrong. CHECK: Substitute z = 18 into the original equation: 2(318 - 6) + 4 = 518 + 10. 2(54 - 6) + 4 = 90 + 10. 2(48) + 4 = 100. 96 + 4 = 100. 100 = 100. It’s correct!
