Converting Forms

Skill ID: 7.EE.007  •  [Student Name] placeholders shown in blue

Learning Objective

Core Explanations (5 variants — expand to review)
Academic — Concept-Forward00547C0

Converting forms in mathematics means expressing a number or value in an equivalent representation without changing its fundamental quantity. For Grade 7, this primarily focuses on transforming rational numbers between their fractional, decimal, and percentage forms. The mechanism for converting a fraction like 3/4 into a decimal involves division: you divide the numerator (3) by the denominator (4) to get 0.75. To convert this decimal to a percentage, you multiply by 100 and add a percent symbol, resulting in 75%. Think of it like converting units of measurement, [Student Name]. Just as 1 foot is exactly equivalent to 12 inches, or 1 dollar is equivalent to 100 cents, 3/4 is the same exact quantity as 0.75, and both are the same as 75%. No matter the form, the value remains constant. Understanding this equivalence is crucial for solving real-world problems involving quantities like discounts, interest rates, or proportions. For example, knowing that a 'quarter' is 1/4 of a dollar, $0.25, and 25% of a whole gives [them] flexibility in calculations, [Student Name].

Converting forms involves changing a number from one representation (like a fraction) to another (like a decimal or percentage) while keeping its value the same. It’s a core skill for interpreting numbers in various contexts. Let's look at a structured process for [Student Name].

Definition: Converting forms means finding equivalent ways to express a rational number as a fraction, decimal, or percentage.

Example: Suppose we want to convert 2/5 to a decimal and a percentage. 1. To convert 2/5 to a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator: 2 ÷ 5 = 0.40. 2. To convert the decimal 0.40 to a percentage, multiply by 100: 0.40 × 100 = 40. Then add the percent sign: 40%.

Scaffolded (Step-by-Step)00547W0

Hey [Student Name], let's convert a fraction into a decimal and then into a percentage. Imagine you bought 3/8 of a pizza. How would you write that as a decimal or tell someone the percentage of the pizza you ate?

Here’s how to do it step-by-step:

  1. 💡 Hint: To change a fraction to a decimal, you perform the division indicated by the fraction bar. To convert the fraction 3/8 to a decimal, we divide the numerator (3) by the denominator (8). Think of it like sharing 3 dollars among 8 friends; each friend gets a part of a dollar. So, 3 ÷ 8 = 0.375. This means 3/8 of the pizza is the same as 0.375 of the pizza.
  2. 💡 Hint: A percentage means 'out of 100,' so to change a decimal to a percentage, you multiply by 100. Now, let's take our decimal, 0.375, and convert it to a percentage. To do this, we multiply the decimal by 100. This is like moving the decimal point two places to the right. So, 0.375 × 100 = 37.5%. You ate 37.5% of the pizza.

Let's check our work: If 37.5% is 37.5 out of 100, that’s 37.5/100. We can write 37.5/100 as 375/1000. If we simplify this fraction by dividing both the numerator and denominator by 125, we get 3/8. Our conversion is correct!

Standard00547W0

Let's work through converting a percentage into both a decimal and a simplified fraction, [Student Name]. Suppose your favorite online store is having a summer sale, and you see a shirt advertised for 45% off. You want to know what this looks like as a decimal for your budget and as a fraction of the original price.

Here’s how you can convert 45%:

  1. To convert 45% to a decimal, remember that 'percent' means 'per one hundred' or 'out of one hundred.' So, 45% is literally 45 divided by 100. When you divide a number by 100, you move the decimal point two places to the left. Starting with 45 (which can be thought of as 45.0), moving the decimal two places left gives us 0.45. So, 45% as a decimal is 0.45.
  2. Now, to convert the decimal 0.45 into a fraction, we can think of it as 'forty-five hundredths.' This can be written as the fraction 45/100. The denominator (100) comes from the place value of the last digit in the decimal (the 5 is in the hundredths place).
  3. Finally, we need to simplify the fraction 45/100. Both 45 and 100 are divisible by 5. Dividing both the numerator and the denominator by 5 gives us 45 ÷ 5 = 9 and 100 ÷ 5 = 20. So, the simplified fraction is 9/20.

Let's check our work: If we divide 9 by 20 (9 ÷ 20), we get 0.45. And if we multiply 0.45 by 100, we get 45%. All conversions match!

Challenge00547W0

Let's try a trickier problem, [Student Name], involving a scenario from a high school football game. During the first half, a kicker successfully made 12 out of 15 field goal attempts. What was [their] kicking percentage for the first half? Then, in the second half, [they] unfortunately missed 1/3 of [their] next 9 attempts. What was [their] overall kicking percentage for the entire game?

Here's how to break it down, keeping an eye out for common errors:

  1. First, let's find the kicking percentage for the first half. The kicker made 12 out of 15 attempts, which is the fraction 12/15. To convert this to a decimal, divide 12 by 15: 12 ÷ 15 = 0.8. Then, multiply by 100 to get the percentage: 0.8 × 100 = 80%. This is [their] first-half percentage. Common Error Alert: Don't forget to multiply by 100 to get the percentage!
  2. Next, we need to figure out the second half's performance. The kicker attempted 9 field goals and missed 1/3 of them. To find how many were missed, calculate 1/3 of 9: (1/3) × 9 = 3 misses. If [they] attempted 9 and missed 3, then [they] made 9 - 3 = 6 field goals in the second half. Common Error Alert: Make sure to calculate the number of misses and then subtract to find the number of makes, not just use the fraction 1/3 directly in a percentage calculation.
  3. Now, let's combine the results for the entire game. Total field goals made: 12 (first half) + 6 (second half) = 18 field goals. Total attempts: 15 (first half) + 9 (second half) = 24 attempts. Common Error Alert: Don't try to average the percentages directly (e.g., (80% + second-half percentage)/2). You must combine the raw numbers of makes and attempts!
  4. Finally, calculate the overall kicking percentage. This is total makes divided by total attempts, then converted to a percentage: 18 ÷ 24 = 0.75. Convert the decimal to a percentage: 0.75 × 100 = 75%. [Student Name], [they] overall kicking percentage for the game was 75%.
