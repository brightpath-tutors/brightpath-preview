Equation for Proportional Line

Skill ID: 8.EE.019.01  •  [Student Name] placeholders shown in blue

Learning Objective
I can write the equation y = kx to represent a proportional relationship shown on a graph or in a table.
Core Explanations (5 variants — expand to review)
Academic — Concept-Forward

An equation for a proportional line precisely defines a direct variation between two quantities. It always takes the form y = kx, where 'x' and 'y' are variables that change together, and 'k' is a non-zero constant of proportionality. This 'k' represents the fixed ratio of y to x (y/x), meaning that for every unit increase in 'x', 'y' increases by 'k' units. For example, consider the equation y = 5x. Here, 'k' is 5. This equation means that any 'y' value will always be 5 times its corresponding 'x' value. If x = 2, then y = 5 2 = 10. If x = 4, then y = 5 4 = 20. When these points (x, y) are plotted on a coordinate plane, they form a straight line that passes directly through the origin (0,0), which is the fundamental characteristic of a proportional relationship. [Student Name], [they] will see how this 'k' value is crucial. Think of 'k' as a consistent scale factor, similar to how a map's scale (e.g., 1 inch = 10 miles) constantly relates distances on the map to real-world distances.

Stability — Clear & Structured

The equation for a proportional line describes a direct variation between two quantities, 'x' and 'y'. It always takes the consistent and predictable form y = kx, where 'k' is a constant value known as the constant of proportionality. This means that as 'x' changes, 'y' changes by a consistent factor of 'k'. Let's consider a practical example: a student is paid $12 per hour for tutoring. If 'x' represents the number of hours worked and 'y' represents the total earnings, the relationship is proportional. For 1 hour, earnings are $12; for 2 hours, $24; for 3 hours, $36. The constant of proportionality 'k' is 12 (since y/x = 12/1 = 24/2 = 12). Therefore, the equation for this proportional line is y = 12x. This equation clearly shows that total earnings are always 12 times the hours worked. [Student Name], this equation is incredibly useful for predicting outcomes. If [they] needs to earn $180, [they] can use y = 12x to find out how many hours [they] needs to tutor: 180 = 12x, so x = 15 hours. It provides a clear, reliable mathematical model for many real-world situations.

This is a proportional relationship because if you buy zero hot dogs, you pay zero dollars, and the cost per hot dog is constant.

Here’s how we can find the equation:

  1. 💡 Hint: Identify the independent and dependent variables. First, decide what changes and what depends on that change.   1. 💡 Hint: Substitute 'k' into the proportional equation form. Now, take the general equation for a proportional line, y = kx, and plug in the value we found for k. * y = 3.25x

This equation y = 3.25x represents the total cost (y) for any number of hot dogs (x).

Standard

Let's work through an example of finding the equation for a proportional line, [Student Name]. Suppose a car travels at a constant speed, covering 150 miles in 3 hours. We want to find an equation that describes the distance the car travels (y) over any given amount of time (x). This situation represents a proportional relationship because the car starts at 0 miles at 0 hours and maintains a constant speed.

Here are the steps to determine the equation:

  1. Identify the relationship type: The problem describes a constant speed, meaning the distance covered is directly proportional to the time spent traveling. This tells us the equation will be in the form y = kx, where k is the constant of proportionality (in this case, the speed in miles per hour).
  1. Calculate the constant of proportionality (k): We know the car travels 150 miles in 3 hours. The constant k is found by dividing the dependent variable (y, distance) by the independent variable (x, time). So, k = y/x = 150 miles / 3 hours = 50 miles per hour.
  1. Write the equation: Now that we have k = 50, we can substitute this value into the general proportional equation y = kx. * The equation is y = 50x.

This equation y = 50x allows us to calculate the distance (y) for any given time (x) the car travels at that constant speed.

Let's check our work! If the car travels for 2 hours, the equation predicts y = 50 * 2, which equals 100 miles. This is reasonable, as 2 hours is two-thirds of the initial 3 hours, and 100 miles is two-thirds of the initial 150 miles, confirming the constant speed.
