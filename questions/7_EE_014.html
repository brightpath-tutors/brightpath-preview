Question 1Grade 7 • EE
Sarah has less than $12 in her wallet. Which number line represents the amount of money Sarah has?
A. An open circle at 12, with the line shaded to the right.
B. A closed circle at 12, with the line shaded to the left.
C. An open circle at 12, with the line shaded to the left.
D. A closed circle at 12, with the line shaded to the right.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'm' represent the amount of money Sarah has. "Less than $12" means m < 12.
The phrase "less than" indicates a strict inequality where the value itself is not included. So, we use the '<' symbol.
2
Since the inequality is m < 12 (strictly less than), the endpoint at 12 should be an open circle.
An open circle indicates that the number itself is not part of the solution set.
3
Since 'm' is less than 12, the numbers to the left of 12 are included in the solution. Shade the line to the left of 12.
Shading to the left represents all numbers smaller than the endpoint.

Answer: An open circle at 12, with the line shaded to the left. (Option C)
