Question 1Grade 7 • RP
If 3 pencils cost $0.75, what is the cost of 9 pencils?
A. $9.75
B. $0.25
C. $2.25
D. $4.50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Given: 3 pencils : $0.75. Desired: 9 pencils : x.
We are comparing the number of pencils to their cost.
2
Scaling factor = 9 pencils / 3 pencils = 3.
To find an equivalent ratio, we multiply or divide both parts of the ratio by the same factor. Here, the number of pencils is multiplied by 3.
3
Cost = $0.75 * 3 = $2.25.
Since the number of pencils is 3 times greater, the cost will also be 3 times greater.

Answer: $2.25 (Option C)

2

Question 10Grade 7 • RP
To create a specific shade of purple paint, an artist mixes blue and red paint in a ratio of 7:5. If the artist needs a total of 36 liters of this purple paint, how many liters of blue paint are required?
A. 50.4 liters
B. 21 liters
C. 31 liters
D. 5.14 liters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Blue : Red = 7 : 5.
This shows the proportion of each color in the mixture.
2
Total parts = 7 (blue) + 5 (red) = 12 parts.
This represents the whole mixture in terms of ratio units.
3
Value per part = 36 liters (total paint) / 12 parts = 3 liters/part.
Divide the total quantity by the total number of ratio parts to find the quantity represented by one part of the ratio.
4
Blue paint = 7 parts * 3 liters/part = 21 liters.
Multiply the number of parts for blue paint by the value of one part.

Answer: 21 liters (Option B)
