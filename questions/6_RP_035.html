Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A shop offers a discount on a shirt. The original price of the shirt is $50, and the discount is $10. What percentage of the original price is the discount?
A. 500%
B. 0.2%
C. 20%
D. 2%
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Part = $10 (discount), Whole = $50 (original price)
To find the percentage, we need to determine what part of the whole the discount represents.
2
Fraction = 10/50
The discount is the part, and the original price is the whole.
3
10 ÷ 50 = 0.20
Divide the numerator by the denominator to get the decimal equivalent.
4
0.20 × 100 = 20%
Multiply the decimal by 100 to express it as a percentage. Alternatively, move the decimal point two places to the right and add the percent symbol.

Answer: 20% (Option C)

Question 10Grade 6 • RP
A new smartphone costs $800. If the sales tax is 6.5%, what is the total price of the smartphone, rounded to the nearest cent?
A. $52.00
B. $806.50
C. $1320.00
D. $852.00
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
6.5% = 6.5 ÷ 100 = 0.065
To use a percentage in calculations, it must first be converted to a decimal by dividing by 100 or moving the decimal point two places to the left.
2
Sales tax amount = $800 × 0.065 = $52.00
Multiply the original price by the decimal form of the sales tax rate.
3
Total price = Original price + Sales tax amount = $800 + $52.00 = $852.00
Add the calculated sales tax to the original price of the smartphone to get the final total.

Answer: $852.00 (Option D)
