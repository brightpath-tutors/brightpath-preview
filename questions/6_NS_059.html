Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A shopper has a store credit of $25. They then make a purchase that costs $12. What is their new balance?
A. $-13
B. $13
C. $37
D. $-37
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial balance = +$25 (store credit) Purchase cost = -$12 (money spent)
Store credit is a positive value, and a purchase is a negative value.
2
$25 + (-$12)
To find the new balance, combine the initial credit with the amount spent.
3
Since the signs are different, subtract the absolute values and keep the sign of the number with the larger absolute value. |25| - |-12| = 25 - 12 = 13. The number with the larger absolute value is 25, which is positive. So the answer is +13.
Subtracting 12 from 25 gives 13. The positive sign of 25 is retained.

Answer: $13 (Option B)

2

3
