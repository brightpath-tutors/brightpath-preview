B. 2 * 3
C. 2^3
D. 8
E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1
The virus multiplies by a factor of 2 every hour. We want to find the total number of viruses after 3 hours, starting with 1 virus.
This means the number of viruses doubles each hour.
2
1 * 2 = 2 viruses
After 1 hour, the initial virus doubles.
3
2 * 2 = 4 viruses
After 2 hours, the 2 viruses from Hour 1 double.
4

Question 6Grade 8 • EE
A digital artist is creating a pixel art image. The image has a width of 2^4 pixels and a height of 2^3 pixels. What is the total number of pixels in the image? Express your answer as a single exponent.
A. 4^7
B. 2^7
C. 2^12
D. 2^1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Width = 2^4 pixels. Height = 2^3 pixels.
The problem provides the dimensions in exponential form.
2
When multiplying exponents with the same base, you add the powers: a^m * a^n = a^(m+n).
The total number of pixels is found by multiplying the width by the height.
3
Total pixels = 2^4 * 2^3 = 2^(4+3) = 2^7.
Add the exponents 4 and 3 while keeping the base 2.

Answer: 2^7 (Option B)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A baker uses 5^6 grams of flour for a large cake. If he has 5^8 grams of flour in stock, how many times more flour does he have than he needs for the cake? Express your answer as a single exponent.
A. 1^2
B. 5^48
C. 5^14
D. 5^2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Flour in stock = 5^8 grams. Flour needed = 5^6 grams.
The problem provides the amounts in exponential form.
2
To find how many times more, we divide the larger amount by the smaller amount. When dividing exponents with the same base, you subtract the powers: a^m / a^n = a^(m-n).
We need to find the ratio of the flour in stock to the flour needed.
3
Times more flour = 5^8 / 5^6 = 5^(8-6) = 5^2.
Subtract the exponents 6 from 8 while keeping the base 5.

Answer: 5^2 (Option D)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A city's population grew at a rate of (3^2)^3 people per year for 5 years. What is the total growth rate per year? Express your answer as a single exponent.
A. 3^6
B. 9^3
C. 3^5
D. 3^2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Growth rate per year = (3^2)^3 people/year.
The problem provides the growth rate in the form of a power raised to another power.
2
When raising a power to another power, you multiply the exponents: (a^m)^n = a^(m*n).
This rule simplifies the expression into a single exponent.
3
(3^2)^3 = 3^(2*3) = 3^6.
Multiply the exponents 2 and 3 while keeping the base 3.

Answer: 3^6 (Option A)
