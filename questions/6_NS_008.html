Question 1Grade 6 • NS
Sarah is tracking her spending and savings. Her account balances over a week were: -$15 (overdrawn), $20 (saved), and -$5 (overdrawn). Order these balances from least to greatest.
A. $20, -$5, -$15
B. -$15, -$5, $20
C. -$5, -$15, $20
D. -$15, $20, -$5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Numbers: -15, 20, -5
Extract the numerical values from the problem statement.
2
On a number line, -15 is to the left of -5. So, -15 < -5.
For negative numbers, the number with the larger absolute value is actually smaller. Visualizing on a number line helps to correctly identify the order.
3
20 is a positive number. Positive numbers are always greater than any negative number.
Positive numbers are on the right side of zero on a number line, while negative numbers are on the left.
4
Combining the comparisons: -15 < -5 < 20. Therefore, the order is -$15, -$5, $20.
Arrange the numbers based on the established comparisons to get the final ordered list.

Answer: -$15, -$5, $20 (Option B)

A. -1, -3, 0, 5
B. 5, 0, -1, -3
C. 0, -1, -3, 5
D. -3, -1, 0, 5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Numbers: -3, 5, -1, 0
Extract the numerical values representing the goal differences.
2

3

B. -50 m, -120 m, -200 m, 40 m, 80 m

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Numbers: -120, 80, -200, 40, -50
Extract the numerical values representing the elevation changes.
2
Highest to lowest means greatest to least (descending order).
Clarify that 'highest to lowest' corresponds to descending order for elevation changes.
3
Positive numbers: 80, 40. In descending order: 80 > 40.
