Question 1Grade 6 • RP
If 3 notebooks cost $4.50, how much will 7 notebooks cost?
A. $10.50
B. $8.50
C. $1.50
D. $31.50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
3 notebooks / $4.50 = 7 notebooks / x
We set up a proportion where the ratio of notebooks to cost is constant. Let x be the unknown cost for 7 notebooks.
2
3 x = 4.50 7 3x = 31.50
Multiply the numerator of the first ratio by the denominator of the second, and vice-versa, to eliminate the fractions.
3
x = 31.50 / 3 x = 10.50
Divide both sides of the equation by 3 to isolate x and find the total cost.

Answer: $10.50 (Option A)

2
850 people / 5 years = x people / 3 years

3
850 3 = 5 x 2550 = 5x

4
