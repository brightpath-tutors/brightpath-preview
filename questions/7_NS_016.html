Question 1Grade 7 • NS
Sarah bought a toy car for $5.75. She paid with a $10 bill. How much change did she receive?
A. $4.35
B. $15.75
C. $5.25
D. $4.25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Change = Amount Paid - Cost
To find the change, subtract the cost of the toy from the amount paid.
2
$10.00 - $5.75
Align the decimal points. Add zeros to $10 to match decimal places for easier subtraction.
3
9 9 10 $10.00 - $5.75 ------- $4.25
Start from the rightmost digit. Borrow from the tens place, making it 9, and the hundredths place 10. 10 - 5 = 5. Then, 9 - 7 = 2. Then, 9 - 5 = 4. The 1 in 10 becomes 0.
4
$4.25
Sarah received $4.25 in change.

Answer: $4.25 (Option D)

1
Difference = First Lap Time - Second Lap Time
To find out how much faster the second lap was, subtract the second lap time from the first lap time.
2
13.5 - 12.8
Align the decimal points.
3
2 15 13.5 -12.8 ----- 0.7

Question 3Grade 7 • NS
A recipe calls for 3/4 cup of sugar. Emily only has 1/4 cup of sugar left. How much more sugar does Emily need? (in simplest form)
A. 2/0 cups
B. 4/8 cups
C. 1/2 cup
D. 4/4 cups
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Needed = Total - Had
To find out how much more sugar is needed, subtract the amount Emily has from the amount the recipe calls for.
2
3/4 - 1/4
The fractions already have a common denominator.
3
(3 - 1) / 4 = 2/4
Subtract the numerators (3 - 1) and keep the common denominator (4).
4
2/4 = 1/2
Both the numerator and denominator are divisible by 2. Divide both by 2 to simplify.
5
1/2 cup
Emily needs 1/2 cup more sugar.

Answer: 1/2 cup (Option C)

E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Difference = Monday's Distance - Tuesday's Distance
To find the difference in distance, subtract Tuesday's distance from Monday's distance.
2
150.50 - 87.25
Align the decimal points and add a zero to 150.5 to match the number of decimal places in 87.25.
3
4 10 150.50 - 87.25 ------- 63.25

63.25 miles

Question 5Grade 7 • NS
A student spent 1 1/2 hours on math homework and 3/4 hour on science homework. How much more time did the student spend on math than on science? (in simplest form)
A. 2/2 hours
B. 2 1/4 hours
C. 3/4 hour
D. 1 1/4 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Difference = Math Time - Science Time
To find the difference in time, subtract the science homework time from the math homework time.
2
1/2 = 2/4. So, 1 1/2 = 1 2/4.
The least common denominator for 1/2 and 3/4 is 4. Convert 1/2 to 2/4.
3
1 2/4 - 3/4
Now both fractions have the same denominator.
4
1 2/4 = (4/4 + 2/4) = 6/4
Since 2/4 is less than 3/4, borrow 1 (which is 4/4) from the whole number 1, and add it to 2/4 to get 6/4.
5
6/4 - 3/4 = (6 - 3) / 4 = 3/4
Subtract the numerators and keep the common denominator.
6
3/4 hour
The student spent 3/4 hour more on math.

Answer: 3/4 hour (Option C)

Question 6Grade 7 • NS
The temperature on Monday morning was -2.3 degrees Celsius. By the afternoon, it had dropped by 1.7 degrees Celsius. What was the temperature in the afternoon?
A. -4.0 degrees Celsius
B. 4.0 degrees Celsius
C. 0.6 degrees Celsius
D. -0.6 degrees Celsius
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Afternoon Temp = Morning Temp - Temperature Drop
A drop in temperature means subtracting a positive value. So, we are subtracting 1.7 from -2.3.
2
-2.3 - 1.7 = -(2.3 + 1.7)
Subtracting a positive number from a negative number moves it further left on the number line. This is equivalent to adding their absolute values and keeping the negative sign.
3
2.3 + 1.7 ----- 4.0
Add 2.3 and 1.7. (3 + 7 = 10, carry 1. 2 + 1 + 1 = 4).
4
-4.0
Since we are moving further into negative temperatures, the result is negative.
5
-4.0 degrees Celsius
The temperature in the afternoon was -4.0 degrees Celsius.

Answer: -4.0 degrees Celsius (Option A)

Question 7Grade 7 • NS
A construction worker cut 7/8 of a foot from a metal rod that was 1 1/4 feet long. What is the remaining length of the rod? (in simplest form)
A. 2 1/8 feet
B. 3/8 foot
C. 1 5/8 feet
D. 1 9/12 feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Remaining Length = Original Length - Cut Length
To find the remaining length, subtract the cut piece's length from the original length.
2
1/4 = 2/8. So, 1 1/4 = 1 2/8.
The least common denominator for 1/4 and 7/8 is 8. Convert 1/4 to 2/8.
3
1 2/8 - 7/8
Now both fractions have the same denominator.
4
1 2/8 = (8/8 + 2/8) = 10/8
Since 2/8 is less than 7/8, borrow 1 (which is 8/8) from the whole number 1, and add it to 2/8 to get 10/8.
5
10/8 - 7/8 = (10 - 7) / 8 = 3/8
Subtract the numerators and keep the common denominator.
6
3/8 foot
The remaining length of the rod is 3/8 foot.

Answer: 3/8 foot (Option B)

Question 8Grade 7 • NS
A digital clock shows 10:15 PM. If time moves backward by 2 3/5 hours, what time will the clock show?
A. 12:51 PM
B. 8:49 PM
C. 7:39 PM
D. 7:15 PM
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
New Time = 10:15 PM - 2 3/5 hours
To find the new time, subtract 2 3/5 hours from 10:15 PM.
2
3/5 hours 60 minutes/hour = (3 60) / 5 = 180 / 5 = 36 minutes. So, 2 3/5 hours = 2 hours and 36 minutes.
Multiply the fraction by 60 to convert it into minutes.
3
10:15 PM - 2 hours = 8:15 PM
First, subtract the whole hours from the current time.
4
8:15 PM - 36 minutes Since 15 minutes is less than 36 minutes, borrow 1 hour (60 minutes) from 8:00 PM. 8:15 PM = 7 hours + (60 + 15) minutes = 7 hours 75 minutes. 7 hours 75 minutes - 36 minutes = 7 hours 39 minutes.
Since 15 is less than 36, regroup one hour from the 8 hours, converting it to 60 minutes and adding it to 15 minutes. Then subtract 36 minutes.
5
7:39 PM
The clock will show 7:39 PM.

Answer: 7:39 PM (Option C)

LCD of 8 and 3 is 24. 3/8 = (3 3) / (8 3) = 9/24 1/3 = (1 8) / (3 8) = 8/24
The least common denominator (LCD) for 8 and 3 is 24. Convert both fractions to equivalent fractions with a denominator of 24.
3
185 9/24 - 179 8/24
Replace the original fractions in the mixed numbers with their equivalent forms.
4
9/24 - 8/24 = 1/24
Subtract the numerators and keep the common denominator.
5
185 - 179 = 6
Subtract the whole numbers.
6
6 1/24

7
6 1/24 pounds
