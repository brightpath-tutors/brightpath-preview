D. $7.50
E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1
Cost per bag = $3.75
The problem states that one bag of oranges costs $3.75.
2
Number of bags = 2
The customer buys 2 bags of oranges.
3

D. 50 miles
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total trip = 250 miles
The problem states the total trip length is 250 miles.
2
Fraction traveled = 3/5
The car has traveled 3/5 of the trip.
3
Miles traveled = 250 × (3/5) = 150 miles
Multiply the total trip length by the fraction traveled to find the distance covered: 250 / 5 = 50, then 50 * 3 = 150.

Question 9Grade 6 • NS
The population of a small town was 12,500. Due to new industry, the population increased by 1/8 in the first year and then decreased by 0.15 in the second year. What is the town's population after two years? Express your answer as a whole number.
A. 14,063 people
B. 10,625 people
C. 12,500 people
D. 11,953 people
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial population = 12,500
The starting population of the town is 12,500.
2
Increase factor = 1 + 1/8 = 1 + 0.125 = 1.125 Population after 1st year = 12,500 × 1.125 = 14,062.5
An increase by 1/8 means the new population is 1 + 1/8 times the original. Convert 1/8 to a decimal (0.125) and multiply by the initial population.
3
Decrease factor = 1 - 0.15 = 0.85 Population after 2nd year = 14,062.5 × 0.85 = 11,953.125
A decrease by 0.15 (15%) means the new population is 1 - 0.15 times the population after the first year. Multiply the population after the 1st year by this decrease factor.
4
11,953.125 ≈ 11,953 people
Since population must be a whole number, round the calculated value to the nearest whole number.

Answer: 11,953 people (Option D)

Question 10Grade 6 • NS
A software update downloads 0.6 GB of a 2.5 GB file. If the remaining portion is downloaded at a rate of 0.25 GB per minute, how long, in minutes, will it take to finish the download? Express your answer as a decimal.
A. 0.76 minutes
B. 7.6 minutes
C. 12.4 minutes
D. 10.0 minutes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total file size = 2.5 GB
The entire software file is 2.5 gigabytes.
2
Downloaded amount = 0.6 GB
0.6 gigabytes of the file have already been downloaded.
3
Remaining amount = 2.5 GB - 0.6 GB = 1.9 GB
Subtract the already downloaded portion from the total file size to find how much is left to download.
4
Download rate = 0.25 GB per minute
The speed at which the rest of the file downloads is 0.25 GB every minute.
5
Time = 1.9 GB / 0.25 GB/minute = 7.6 minutes
Divide the remaining amount of the file by the download rate to find the time it will take to complete the download.

Answer: 7.6 minutes (Option B)
