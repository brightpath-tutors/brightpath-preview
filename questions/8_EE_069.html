Combine Like Terms

Skill ID: 8.EE.069

Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A chef is preparing a large meal. He has 5x pounds of chicken, 3y pounds of beef, 2x pounds of fish, and 4y pounds of pork. Write an expression in simplest form for the total pounds of meat the chef has.
A. 7x + 3y + 2x + 4y pounds
B. 7x + 7y pounds
C. 14xy pounds
D. 14x + y pounds
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Chicken: 5x Beef: 3y Fish: 2x Pork: 4y Like terms are those with the same variable part. In this case, 'x' terms and 'y' terms are like terms.
The terms with 'x' are like terms, and the terms with 'y' are like terms.
2
(5x + 2x) + (3y + 4y)
Place terms with 'x' together and terms with 'y' together to make addition easier.
3
5x + 2x = (5 + 2)x = 7x
Add the coefficients of the 'x' terms.
4
3y + 4y = (3 + 4)y = 7y
Add the coefficients of the 'y' terms.
5
7x + 7y
Since 'x' and 'y' terms are not like terms, they cannot be combined further. The expression is 7x + 7y pounds.

Answer: 7x + 7y pounds (Option B)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
During a technology project, a student writes 6s + 8t + 3s - 2t lines of code. Write an expression in simplest form for the total number of lines of code.
A. 15st lines
B. 9s + 10t lines
C. 6s + 8t + 3s - 2t lines
D. 9s + 6t lines
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
6s, 3s are like terms. 8t, -2t are like terms.
Terms with the same variable are like terms.
2
(6s + 3s) + (8t - 2t)
Rearrange the expression to place like terms next to each other.
3
6s + 3s = (6 + 3)s = 9s
Add the coefficients of the 's' terms.
4
8t - 2t = (8 - 2)t = 6t
Subtract the coefficients of the 't' terms.
5
9s + 6t
The simplified expression is the sum of the combined like terms.

Answer: 9s + 6t lines (Option D)

Question 3Grade 8 • EE
An athlete trains by running 4k miles and cycling 7j miles in the morning. In the afternoon, they run 2k miles and cycle 3j miles. Write an expression in simplest form for the total distance covered.

B. 16k + j miles
C. 6k + 10j miles
D. 16kj miles
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Running distances: 4k, 2k Cycling distances: 7j, 3j
Terms with the same variable (k for running, j for cycling) are like terms.
2
(4k + 2k) + (7j + 3j)
Collect terms with 'k' together and terms with 'j' together.
3
4k + 2k = (4 + 2)k = 6k
Add the coefficients of the 'k' terms.
4
7j + 3j = (7 + 3)j = 10j
Add the coefficients of the 'j' terms.
5
6k + 10j
The final expression is the sum of the combined running and cycling distances.

Answer: 6k + 10j miles (Option C)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A construction worker uses 10p feet of electrical wire, 6q feet of plumbing pipe, 3p feet of ethernet cable, and 9q feet of ventilation duct. Write an expression in simplest form for the total length of materials used.
A. 13p + 15q feet
B. 28pq feet
C. 10p + 6q + 3p + 9q feet
D. 13p + 9q + 6q feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Electrical wire: 10p Plumbing pipe: 6q Ethernet cable: 3p Ventilation duct: 9q Like terms are 10p and 3p (for 'p' variable) and 6q and 9q (for 'q' variable).
Terms with the same variable are considered like terms.
2
(10p + 3p) + (6q + 9q)
Rearrange the expression to group terms with 'p' together and terms with 'q' together.
3
10p + 3p = (10 + 3)p = 13p
Add the coefficients of the 'p' terms.
4
6q + 9q = (6 + 9)q = 15q
Add the coefficients of the 'q' terms.
5
13p + 15q
Since 'p' and 'q' terms are unlike, they cannot be combined further. The simplified expression is 13p + 15q feet.

Answer: 13p + 15q feet (Option A)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE
A store manager tracks inventory. This week, they received 8h boxes of cereal, sold 5k boxes of pasta, received 2h boxes of crackers, and sold 3k boxes of cookies. Write an expression in simplest form for the net change in boxes of food products.
A. 10h - 8k boxes
B. 10h + 8k boxes
C. 10h - 2k boxes
D. 2h + 2k boxes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Received cereal: +8h Sold pasta: -5k Received crackers: +2h Sold cookies: -3k Like terms are 8h, 2h (for 'h' variable) and -5k, -3k (for 'k' variable).
Positive terms represent items received, negative terms represent items sold. Group terms with the same variable.
2
(8h + 2h) + (-5k - 3k)
Collect terms with 'h' and terms with 'k'.
3
8h + 2h = (8 + 2)h = 10h
Add the coefficients of the 'h' terms.
4
-5k - 3k = (-5 - 3)k = -8k
Combine the coefficients of the 'k' terms, remembering the signs.
5
10h - 8k
The simplified expression is 10h - 8k boxes, representing the net change.

Answer: 10h - 8k boxes (Option A)

Question 6Grade 8 • EE

D. 16a - 2b dollars
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Stock A gain: +12a Stock B loss: -7b Stock C gain: +4a Stock D loss: -5b Like terms are 12a, 4a (for 'a' variable) and -7b, -5b (for 'b' variable).
Gains are positive, losses are negative. Group terms with the same variable.
2
(12a + 4a) + (-7b - 5b)
Collect terms with 'a' and terms with 'b'.
3
12a + 4a = (12 + 4)a = 16a

4
-7b - 5b = (-7 - 5)b = -12b

5
16a - 12b
The simplified expression represents the total change in investment value.

Answer: 16a - 12b dollars (Option A)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A scientist is tracking bacterial growth. Culture A has 7m colonies, Culture B has 9n colonies, Culture C loses 3m colonies, and Culture D loses 2n colonies. Write an expression in simplest form for the total net change in bacterial colonies.

B. 4m + 11n colonies
C. 17mn colonies
D. 4m + 7n colonies
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Culture A: +7m Culture B: +9n Culture C loss: -3m Culture D loss: -2n Like terms are 7m, -3m (for 'm' variable) and 9n, -2n (for 'n' variable).
Gains are positive, losses are negative. Group terms with the same variable.
2
(7m - 3m) + (9n - 2n)
Collect terms with 'm' and terms with 'n'.
3
7m - 3m = (7 - 3)m = 4m
Subtract the coefficients of the 'm' terms.
4
9n - 2n = (9 - 2)n = 7n
Subtract the coefficients of the 'n' terms.
5
4m + 7n
The simplified expression is the sum of the combined net changes for 'm' and 'n' colonies.

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A school collects 15x^2 pencils, 8y erasers, 4x^2 notebooks, 3x pens, and 10y rulers. Write an expression in simplest form for the total number of school supplies.
A. 19x^2 + 18y items
B. 19x^2 + 21xy items
C. 19x^2 + 3x + 18y items
D. 35x^3y^2 items
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Pencils: 15x^2 Erasers: 8y Notebooks: 4x^2 Pens: 3x Rulers: 10y Like terms are terms with the same variable and the same exponent: 15x^2 and 4x^2; 8y and 10y. The term 3x is unique.
Like terms must have identical variable parts, including exponents.
2
(15x^2 + 4x^2) + 3x + (8y + 10y)
Collect terms with x^2, x, and y separately.
3
15x^2 + 4x^2 = (15 + 4)x^2 = 19x^2
Add the coefficients of the x^2 terms.
4
8y + 10y = (8 + 10)y = 18y
Add the coefficients of the y terms.
5
19x^2 + 3x + 18y
The term 3x remains as it has no like terms. The final simplified expression is 19x^2 + 3x + 18y items.

Answer: 19x^2 + 3x + 18y items (Option C)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
A city's population changes over time. In one decade, the population increased by 20c thousand people, decreased by 12d thousand, then increased by 8c thousand, and finally decreased by 5d thousand. Write an expression in simplest form for the net change in population in thousands.
A. 28c - 7d thousand people
B. 11cd thousand people
C. 28c + 17d thousand people
D. 28c - 17d thousand people
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Increased: +20c Decreased: -12d Increased: +8c Decreased: -5d Like terms are 20c, 8c (for 'c' variable) and -12d, -5d (for 'd' variable).
Increases are positive, decreases are negative. Group terms with the same variable.
2
(20c + 8c) + (-12d - 5d)
Collect terms with 'c' and terms with 'd'.
3
20c + 8c = (20 + 8)c = 28c
Add the coefficients of the 'c' terms.
4
-12d - 5d = (-12 - 5)d = -17d
Combine the coefficients of the 'd' terms, remembering that two negatives sum to a larger negative.
5
28c - 17d
The simplified expression is 28c - 17d thousand people, representing the net change.

Answer: 28c - 17d thousand people (Option D)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A farmer tracks animal feed. They have 25w^3 pounds of chicken feed, 18z^2 pounds of pig feed, 7w^3 pounds of cattle feed, 10z pounds of goat feed, and 4z^2 pounds of sheep feed. Write an expression in simplest form for the total pounds of animal feed.
A. 72w^3z^3 pounds
B. 32w^3 + 22z^2 + 10z pounds
C. 32w^3 + 32z^2 pounds
D. 32w^3 + 32z^3 pounds
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Chicken feed: 25w^3 Pig feed: 18z^2 Cattle feed: 7w^3 Goat feed: 10z Sheep feed: 4z^2 Like terms are 25w^3 and 7w^3; 18z^2 and 4z^2. The term 10z is unique.
Like terms must have the same variable and the same exponent.
2
(25w^3 + 7w^3) + (18z^2 + 4z^2) + 10z
Collect terms with w^3, z^2, and z separately.
3
25w^3 + 7w^3 = (25 + 7)w^3 = 32w^3
Add the coefficients of the w^3 terms.
4
18z^2 + 4z^2 = (18 + 4)z^2 = 22z^2
Add the coefficients of the z^2 terms.
5
32w^3 + 22z^2 + 10z
The term 10z remains as it has no like terms. The final simplified expression is 32w^3 + 22z^2 + 10z pounds.

Answer: 32w^3 + 22z^2 + 10z pounds (Option B)
