Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A cashier gives a customer $5 in change. Considering the monetary value, is the number 5 an integer, a rational number, or both?
A. Neither an integer nor a rational number.
B. A rational number only.
C. An integer only.
D. Both an integer and a rational number.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Number = 5
Identify the given number from the problem.
2
Definition of integer: a whole number (not a fraction or decimal) that can be positive, negative, or zero. Examples: ..., -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, ... 5 is a positive whole number.
Recall the definition of an integer. The number 5 fits this definition as it is a positive whole number.
3
Definition of rational number: any number that can be written as a fraction p/q, where p and q are integers and q is not zero. For 5, we can write 5/1.
Recall the definition of a rational number. Since 5 can be written as the fraction 5/1 (where 5 and 1 are integers and 1 is not zero), it is a rational number.
4
5 is an integer and 5 is a rational number.
Since the number 5 satisfies both definitions, it is both an integer and a rational number.

Answer: C (Option D)

Question 7Grade 6 • NS
A carpenter measures a board to be 7 1/4 inches long. Is the number 7 1/4 an integer, a rational number, or both?
A. An integer only.
B. A rational number only.
C. Neither an integer nor a rational number.
D. Both an integer and a rational number.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Number = 7 1/4
Identify the given number from the problem.
2
Convert mixed number to improper fraction: 7 1/4 = (7 * 4 + 1) / 4 = 29/4
Convert the mixed number to an improper fraction to make classification easier.
3
Definition of integer: a whole number (not a fraction or decimal) that can be positive, negative, or zero. 29/4 = 7.25. Since 29/4 is not a whole number without a fractional part (7.25 has a decimal), it is not an integer.
Recall the definition of an integer. Since 29/4 (or 7.25) is not a whole number, it is not an integer.
4
Definition of rational number: any number that can be written as a fraction p/q, where p and q are integers and q is not zero. 29/4 is already in p/q form where p=29 and q=4 are integers and q≠0.
Recall the definition of a rational number. Since 29/4 is already expressed as a fraction of two integers, it is a rational number.
5
7 1/4 is not an integer, but 7 1/4 is a rational number.
Since the number 7 1/4 is not an integer but is a rational number, it is a rational number only.

Answer: C (Option B)
