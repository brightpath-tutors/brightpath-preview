Question 1Grade 5 • NF
David ate 1/4 of a pizza and Sarah ate 1/4 of the same pizza. What fraction of the pizza did they eat altogether? Give your answer in simplest form.
A. 2/8
B. 2/4
C. 1/16
D. 1/2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
David ate = 1/4, Sarah ate = 1/4
Identify the fractions of pizza eaten by David and Sarah.
2
Total pizza eaten = 1/4 + 1/4
To find the total fraction eaten, add the two fractions.
3
1/4 + 1/4 = 2/4
Add the numerators since the denominators are the same.
4
2/4 = 1/2
Simplify the fraction to its simplest form by dividing the numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 2.

Answer: 1/2 (Option D)

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students

Question 10Grade 5 • NF
A movie director filmed 5/7 of a scene in the morning. After lunch, he filmed another 1/2 of what remained of the scene. What fraction of the entire scene still needs to be filmed?
A. 1/14
B. 6/7
C. 1/7
D. 1/2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Filmed in morning = 5/7
Identify the fraction of the scene filmed in the morning.
2
Remaining after morning = 1 - 5/7 = 2/7
Calculate the fraction of the scene that remained after the morning filming.
3
Filmed after lunch = 1/2 * 2/7 = 1/7
Calculate the fraction of the entire scene filmed after lunch (half of the remaining portion).
4
Total filmed = 5/7 + 1/7 = 6/7
Add the fractions filmed in the morning and after lunch to find the total fraction filmed.
5
Still needs to be filmed = 1 - 6/7 = 1/7
Subtract the total fraction filmed from 1 (the whole scene) to find the fraction still needing to be filmed.

Answer: 1/7 (Option C)
