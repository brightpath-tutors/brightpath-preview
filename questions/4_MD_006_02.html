Question 9Grade 4 • MD
A chef made 2 and 1/2 liters of soup. He served 3/4 liter for lunch. Then, he added another 1/3 liter of water to thin the remaining soup. How much soup is there now?
A. 2 and 11/12 liters
B. 2 and 7/12 liters
C. 2 and 1/12 liters
D. 1 and 7/12 liters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
2 1/2 liters (initial), 3/4 liter (served), 1/3 liter (added)
Identify the initial amount of soup, the amount served, and the amount of water added.
2
2 1/2 = 5/2
Convert the mixed number to an improper fraction.
3
5/2 - 3/4
Subtract the amount served from the initial amount of soup.
4
10/4 - 3/4 = 7/4
Find a common denominator (4) and perform the subtraction.
5
7/4 + 1/3
Add the amount of water to the remaining soup.
6
LCM(4, 3) = 12
Find a common denominator (12) for the fractions.
7
(7/4)(3/3) + (1/3)(4/4) = 21/12 + 4/12 = 25/12
Convert to equivalent fractions and perform the addition.
8
25/12 = 2 and 1/12 liters
Convert the improper fraction back to a mixed number and state the final answer.

Answer: 2 and 7/12 liters (Option B)
