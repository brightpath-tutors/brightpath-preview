Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A grocery store sells 3 cans of soup for $5. Complete the ratio table to find the cost of 9 cans of soup. | Cans | Cost ($) | |------|----------| | 3 | 5 | | 6 | ? | | 9 | ? |
A. 15
B. 12
C. 10
D. 18
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
To go from 3 cans to 9 cans, we multiply by 9 / 3 = 3.
We need to find how many times the initial quantity (3 cans) fits into the target quantity (9 cans) to determine the multiplicative relationship.
2
Multiply the initial cost by the same factor: $5 * 3 = $15.
To maintain the proportional relationship, whatever operation is applied to one quantity in the ratio must also be applied to the other quantity.
3
The cost of 9 cans of soup is $15.
The completed ratio table shows that 9 cans cost $15.

Answer: 15 (Option A)
