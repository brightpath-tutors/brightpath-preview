Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A small convenience store sells two types of coffee: regular and decaf. Each cup of regular coffee costs $3, and each cup of decaf coffee costs $4. On a busy morning, the store sold a total of 20 cups of coffee. The total revenue from coffee sales was $68. How many cups of each type of coffee were sold? To solve this, graph the system of equations where x represents the number of regular coffees and y represents the number of decaf coffees.
A. (12 regular coffees, 8 decaf coffees)
B. (8 regular coffees, 12 decaf coffees)
C. (10 regular coffees, 10 decaf coffees)
D. (15 regular coffees, 5 decaf coffees)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let x = number of regular coffees Let y = number of decaf coffees Equation 1 (Total cups): x + y = 20 Equation 2 (Total revenue): 3x + 4y = 68
We assign variables to the unknown quantities and translate the given information into two linear equations. The first equation represents the total number of cups sold, and the second represents the total revenue generated.
2
From Equation 1: x + y = 20 => y = -x + 20 From Equation 2: 3x + 4y = 68 => 4y = -3x + 68 => y = (-3/4)x + 17
Rewriting the equations in slope-intercept form makes them easier to graph. For the first equation, subtract x from both sides. For the second equation, subtract 3x from both sides, then divide by 4.
3
The y-intercept is (0, 20). The slope is -1. Plot (0, 20) and use the slope to find another point, for example, move down 1 unit and right 1 unit to (1, 19), or down 20 units and right 20 units to (20, 0). Draw a line through these points.
Start by plotting the y-intercept. Then, use the slope (rise over run) to find additional points on the line. A slope of -1 means for every 1 unit down, move 1 unit to the right.
4
The y-intercept is (0, 17). The slope is -3/4. Plot (0, 17) and use the slope to find another point, for example, move down 3 units and right 4 units to (4, 14), or down 6 units and right 8 units to (8, 11). Draw a line through these points.
Similarly, plot the y-intercept for the second equation. Use its slope of -3/4 (down 3, right 4) to find other points and draw the line.
5
The two lines intersect at the point (12, 8).
The point where the two lines cross represents the solution that satisfies both equations simultaneously. This point is (12, 8).
6
Check x = 12, y = 8 in Equation 1: 12 + 8 = 20 (True) Check x = 12, y = 8 in Equation 2: 3(12) + 4(8) = 36 + 32 = 68 (True)
Substitute the values of x and y into both original equations to ensure they are satisfied. Both equations hold true, so the solution is correct.

Answer: (12 regular coffees, 8 decaf coffees) (Option A)
