Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A grocery store sells 6 oranges for $3.00. What is the unit price of one orange?
A. $2.00 per orange
B. $0.60 per orange
C. $0.50 per orange
D. $18.00 per orange
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total cost = $3.00, Number of oranges = 6
We need to find the cost per single orange.
2
Unit price = Total cost ÷ Number of oranges = $3.00 ÷ 6
To find the cost of one orange, divide the total cost by the number of oranges.
3
$3.00 ÷ 6 = $0.50
Dividing 3 by 6 gives 0.5. So, the cost is $0.50 per orange.
4
$0.50 per orange
The unit price for one orange is $0.50.

Answer: $0.50 per orange (Option C)

Seeds = 15, Time = 2.5 minutes

2

3
Multiply both 15 and 2.5 by 10: 15 × 10 = 150, 2.5 × 10 = 25. The division becomes 150 ÷ 25.
Multiplying both the dividend and divisor by the same power of 10 does not change the quotient. This makes division easier.

150 ÷ 25 = 6
Dividing 150 by 25 gives 6.
5
6 seeds per minute
The gardener planted 6 seeds per minute.
