Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A shopper buys 3 items costing $4.55, $7.30, and $12.15. To mentally calculate the total, they add $12.15 + $4.55 + $7.30. Which property of real numbers justifies rearranging the order of addition?
A. Distributive Property
B. Associative Property of Addition
C. Identity Property of Addition
D. Commutative Property of Addition
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The shopper is adding the costs of the items: $4.55 + $7.30 + $12.15.
The problem involves calculating a total cost through addition.
2
The original order is $4.55 + $7.30 + $12.15. The new order is $12.15 + $4.55 + $7.30.
The numbers being added have been rearranged.
3
The Commutative Property states that the order of operands does not affect the result for addition (a + b = b + a) and multiplication (a × b = b × a).
Since only the order of addition changed, the Commutative Property of Addition is applicable.

Answer: Commutative Property of Addition (Option D)

Another coach first adds 15.3 + (13.9 + 16.6). Which property of real numbers allows them to group the numbers differently without changing the sum?
A. Distributive Property
B. Identity Property of Addition
C. Associative Property of Addition
D. Commutative Property of Addition
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The coaches are adding the segment times: 15.3 + 13.9 + 16.6.
The problem involves finding a total time, which is an additive process.
2
One expression is (15.3 + 13.9) + 16.6. The other is 15.3 + (13.9 + 16.6).
The order of numbers remains the same, but the grouping (which numbers are added first) has changed.
3
The Associative Property states that the grouping of operands does not affect the result for addition (a + (b + c) = (a + b) + c) and multiplication (a × (b × c) = (a × b) × c).

C. Associative Property of Addition
D. Commutative Property of Addition
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The chef is adding 0 liters to the initial amount of broth.
The problem describes an addition operation with 0.

1.75 liters + 0 liters = 1.75 liters.
The quantity of broth did not change after adding 0.
3

Question 4Grade 7 • NS
A teacher is buying supplies. They need 6 packs of pens at $2.80 each and 6 packs of notebooks at $3.30 each. To calculate the total cost, they use the expression 6 × ($2.80 + $3.30). Which property allows them to calculate this as (6 × $2.80) + (6 × $3.30)?
A. Associative Property of Multiplication
B. Commutative Property of Addition
C. Distributive Property
D. Identity Property of Addition
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The expression is 6 × ($2.80 + $3.30), which involves multiplication of a number by a sum.
We have a factor outside parentheses that multiplies a sum within the parentheses.
2
The expression 6 × ($2.80 + $3.30) becomes (6 × $2.80) + (6 × $3.30).
The factor 6 has been multiplied by each term inside the parentheses, and the results are then added.
3
The Distributive Property states that a(b + c) = ab + ac.
This matches the transformation shown in the problem, where the multiplication is distributed over the addition.

Answer: Distributive Property (Option C)

B. Commutative Property of Addition
C. Inverse Property of Addition
D. Identity Property of Addition
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The numbers are 11.25 kg and -11.25 kg. The operation is addition.
We are looking at the sum of a number and its additive inverse.
2
11.25 kg + (-11.25 kg) = 0 kg.

3

Question 8Grade 7 • NS
A town's population changed over two decades. In the first decade, it increased by a factor of 1.3. In the second decade, it increased by a factor of 0.9. The initial population was P. An expression for the population after two decades is P × (1.3 × 0.9). Which property allows them to also write this as (P × 1.3) × 0.9, meaning they can first find the population after the first decade, then apply the second decade's factor?
A. Associative Property of Multiplication
B. Commutative Property of Multiplication
C. Distributive Property
D. Identity Property of Multiplication
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The expressions are P × (1.3 × 0.9) and (P × 1.3) × 0.9. Both involve multiplication.
The context describes sequential changes in population, which are multiplicative factors.
2
The order of the factors (P, 1.3, 0.9) remains the same, but the grouping of the factors changes. (1.3 × 0.9) is grouped first in one, and (P × 1.3) is grouped first in the other.
This change in grouping without altering the final product is characteristic of a specific property.
3
The Associative Property of Multiplication states that for any real numbers a, b, and c, a × (b × c) = (a × b) × c.
This property directly applies to the given scenario, allowing the factors to be grouped differently.

Question 9Grade 7 • NS
A computer programmer is optimizing a calculation. They have an expression representing data processing time: 8 (x + 4) + (-8 x). Using properties of real numbers, simplify this expression.
A. 4
B. -32
C. 32
D. 16x + 32
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
8 (x + 4) = 8 x + 8 * 4 = 8x + 32. The expression becomes 8x + 32 + (-8x).
Multiply the factor outside the parentheses (8) by each term inside (x and 4).
2
Rearrange the terms: 8x + (-8x) + 32.
Place the 'x' terms together to make combining them easier.
3
8x + (-8x) = 0. The expression becomes 0 + 32.
A number added to its opposite (additive inverse) equals zero.
4
0 + 32 = 32.
Adding zero to any number results in the same number.

Answer: 32 (Option C)

Question 10Grade 7 • NS
An artist is planning a mural with repeating sections. The total length of the mural can be represented by the expression: 1/3 (6 (9 + y)) + (-2y + 15). Using properties of real numbers, simplify this expression.
A. 15
B. 33
C. 3y + 33
D. 18 + 4y
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
6 (9 + y) = 6 9 + 6 y = 54 + 6y. The expression becomes 1/3 (54 + 6y) + (-2y + 15).
Multiply 6 by each term inside its parentheses.
2
1/3 (54 + 6y) = (1/3 54) + (1/3 * 6y) = 18 + 2y. The expression becomes 18 + 2y + (-2y) + 15.
Multiply 1/3 by each term inside the parentheses.
3
Rearrange terms: 18 + 15 + 2y + (-2y).
Group the constant terms and the 'y' terms together.
4
2y + (-2y) = 0. The expression becomes 18 + 15 + 0.
A term added to its opposite results in zero.
5
18 + 15 + 0 = 33 + 0 = 33.
Adding zero to a number does not change the number, and sum the remaining constants.

Answer: 33 (Option B)
