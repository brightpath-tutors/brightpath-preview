Question 1Grade 6 • NS
Sophia has 3/4 of a liter of juice. She wants to pour it into glasses, with each glass holding 1/8 of a liter. How many glasses can she fill?
A. 2 glasses
B. 8 glasses
C. 6 glasses
D. 3 glasses
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total juice = 3/4 liter; Amount per glass = 1/8 liter
To find out how many glasses can be filled, we need to divide the total amount of juice by the amount each glass holds.
2
3/4 ÷ 1/8
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal.
3
3/4 × 8/1
The reciprocal of 1/8 is 8/1.
4
(3 × 8) / (4 × 1) = 24/4
Multiply the numerators and multiply the denominators.
5
24/4 = 6
Sophia can fill 6 glasses.

Answer: 6 glasses (Option C)

Question 8Grade 6 • NS
A group of artists needs to cover a wall that is 14 2/3 feet long with murals. If each mural is 2 1/6 feet wide, how many murals can they fit on the wall?
A. 8 murals
B. 7 murals
C. 5 murals
D. 6 murals
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
14 2/3 = (14 × 3 + 2)/3 = 44/3 2 1/6 = (2 × 6 + 1)/6 = 13/6
To divide mixed numbers, first convert them to improper fractions.
2
44/3 ÷ 13/6
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal.
3
44/3 × 6/13
The reciprocal of 13/6 is 6/13.
4
(44 × 6) / (3 × 13) = 264 / 39
Multiply the numerators and multiply the denominators.
5
264 ÷ 3 = 88 39 ÷ 3 = 13 88/13 = 6 with a remainder of 10. Since you can't have a fraction of a mural, round down to 6.
The artists can fit 6 murals on the wall.

Answer: 6 murals (Option D)
