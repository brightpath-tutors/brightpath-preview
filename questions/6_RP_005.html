Question 9Grade 6 • RP
In a nature reserve, there are 12 deer for every 18 rabbits. Which ordered pair represents the ratio of deer to rabbits, in simplest form? (Deer on the x-axis, Rabbits on the y-axis)
A. (2, 3)
B. (18, 12)
C. (3, 2)
D. (12, 18)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The problem states 'Deer on the x-axis, Rabbits on the y-axis'.
This directs how to construct the initial ordered pair.
2
Number of deer = 12, Number of rabbits = 18. So, the initial ordered pair is (12, 18).
This represents the direct ratio from the problem statement.
3
The ratio is 12:18. Both numbers are divisible by their greatest common factor, which is 6. 12 ÷ 6 = 2, 18 ÷ 6 = 3. The simplified ratio is 2:3.
To represent the ratio in simplest form, divide both parts of the ratio by their greatest common factor.
4
Simplified ordered pair = (2, 3).
The ordered pair (2, 3) represents the ratio of deer to rabbits in its simplest form.

Answer: (2, 3) (Option A)
