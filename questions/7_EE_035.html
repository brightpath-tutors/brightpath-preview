Question 1Grade 7 • EE
Sarah buys 3 packs of gum at $2.50 each and 3 candy bars at $1.50 each. Which expression uses the Distributive Property to represent the total cost of her purchase?
A. 3 + 2.50 + 1.50
B. 3 * (2.50 + 1.50)
C. (3 + 3) * (2.50 + 1.50)
D. 3 * 2.50 + 1.50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost of 1 pack of gum = $2.50. Cost of 1 candy bar = $1.50. Quantity of each item = 3.
Sarah buys 3 of each item.
2
Total cost = (3 2.50) + (3 1.50)
This shows the cost of 3 packs of gum plus the cost of 3 candy bars.
3
ab + ac = a*(b + c) 3 2.50 + 3 1.50 = 3 * (2.50 + 1.50)
The Distributive Property allows us to factor out the common quantity (3) from both terms.

Answer: 3 * (2.50 + 1.50) (Option B)

A. Identity Property of Addition
B. Distributive Property
C. Commutative Property of Addition
D. Associative Property of Addition
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Game 1: 4 + 2. Game 2: 2 + 4.
The problem provides the goals scored in two different orders.
2
4 + 2 is being compared to 2 + 4.
The numbers being added are the same, but their order is different.
3
The Commutative Property of Addition states that a + b = b + a.
This property directly applies to the scenario where the order of goals is swapped but the total remains the same.

Answer: Commutative Property of Addition (Option C)

Question 3Grade 7 • EE
A baker is making 4 batches of cookies. Each batch requires 2 eggs, and each egg weighs approximately 50 grams. Which expression correctly uses the Associative Property of Multiplication to find the total weight of eggs needed in grams?
A. 4 * 2 + 50
B. (4 2) 50
C. 4 2 50
D. 4 + 2 * 50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Number of batches = 4. Eggs per batch = 2. Weight per egg = 50 grams.
These are the factors for multiplication.
2
Total weight = 4 2 50
Multiplying the number of batches by eggs per batch, then by weight per egg gives the total weight.
3
a (b c) = (a b) c 4 (2 50) = (4 2) 50
The Associative Property allows us to group the factors differently without changing the product. Grouping 4 * 2 first is a valid application.

Answer: (4 2) 50 (Option B)

Question 4Grade 7 • EE
A delivery truck travels at a constant speed of s kilometers per hour. On Monday, it travels for 4 hours. On Tuesday, it travels for 6 hours. Which expression uses the Distributive Property to represent the total distance the truck traveled over these two days?
A. (s + 4) * 6
B. s + 4 + 6
C. s * (4 + 6)
D. s 4 6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Distance = speed × time. Distance on Monday = s * 4. Distance on Tuesday = s * 6.
The distance formula is applied for each day.
2
Total distance = (s 4) + (s 6)
The total distance is the sum of the distances traveled on Monday and Tuesday.
3
ab + ac = a*(b + c) s 4 + s 6 = s * (4 + 6)
The variable 's' is common to both terms, allowing us to factor it out using the Distributive Property.

Answer: s * (4 + 6) (Option C)

Question 5Grade 7 • EE
During a school fundraiser, Emily sold 12 raffle tickets. If she then sells 0 more tickets or if her initial sales are multiplied by 1 to find the total, her total sales remain 12 tickets. Which property is demonstrated by these two actions?
A. Identity Property
B. Commutative Property
C. Associative Property
D. Inverse Property
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
12 + 0 = 12.
When 0 is added to 12, the value remains 12. This is the Identity Property of Addition.
2
12 * 1 = 12.
When 12 is multiplied by 1, the value remains 12. This is the Identity Property of Multiplication.
3
Both scenarios show that an operation with a specific number (0 for addition, 1 for multiplication) leaves the original number unchanged.
This combined characteristic defines the Identity Property for both addition and multiplication.

Answer: Identity Property (Option A)

Question 6Grade 7 • EE
In a chemistry experiment, the temperature of a solution decreases by 3 degrees Celsius each minute for 5 minutes. After this, it decreases by another 2 degrees Celsius each minute for the same 5 minutes. Which expression uses the Distributive Property to represent the total temperature change over the experiment?
A. 5 * (-3 + -2)
B. 5 - 3 - 2
C. 5 * (3 + 2)
D. (5 - 3) * (5 - 2)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Decrease of 3 degrees = -3. Decrease of 2 degrees = -2.
A decrease in temperature is represented by a negative change.
2
First phase change = 5 * (-3). Second phase change = 5 * (-2).
The change occurs for 5 minutes in each phase.
3
Total change = 5 (-3) + 5 (-2).
The total change is the sum of the changes in both phases.
4
ab + ac = a*(b + c) 5 (-3) + 5 (-2) = 5 * (-3 + -2)
The common factor of 5 minutes can be factored out using the Distributive Property.

Answer: 5 * (-3 + -2) (Option A)

Question 7Grade 7 • EE
A contractor is ordering materials for two identical small rooms and one large room. Each small room requires x square meters of tiles. The large room requires 3x square meters of tiles. If the contractor orders enough tiles for 2 small rooms and 1 large room, which simplified expression represents the total area of tiles needed?
A. 3x
B. 6x
C. 5x
D. 2x + 3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Two small rooms * x square meters/room = 2x.
Since there are two identical small rooms, we multiply the area of one small room by 2.
2
One large room * 3x square meters/room = 3x.
The problem states the large room requires 3x square meters.
3
Total area = 2x + 3x.
The total area is the sum of the areas for all rooms.
4
2x + 3x = (2 + 3)x = 5x.
The Distributive Property allows us to factor out the common variable 'x' and sum the coefficients.

Answer: 5x (Option C)

Question 8Grade 7 • EE
A choreographer plans for dancers to spend h hours rehearsing a main routine and 0.25 hours rehearsing a warm-up. If 4 dance groups each rehearse 3 such routines (main + warm-up), which simplified expression represents the total hours spent rehearsing by all groups?
A. 12h + 1
B. 12h + 0.25
C. 7h + 0.25
D. 12h + 3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Time per routine = h + 0.25 hours.
This includes the main routine time (h) and the warm-up time (0.25).
2
Each group rehearses 3 routines. Total time per group = 3 * (h + 0.25).
The factor of 3 applies to both parts of the routine for each group.
3
There are 4 groups. Total time for all groups = 4 [3 (h + 0.25)].
The total time is multiplied by the number of groups.
4
4 [3 (h + 0.25)] = (4 3) (h + 0.25) = 12 * (h + 0.25).
We can multiply the constant factors first (4 and 3) to simplify.
5
12 (h + 0.25) = 12 h + 12 * 0.25 = 12h + 3.
The factor of 12 is distributed to both terms inside the parentheses.

Answer: 12h + 3 (Option D)

Question 9Grade 7 • EE

Additionally, a new family incentive program will increase the total projected population by an overall factor of 1.01. The total future population is given by the expression (1.03A + 1.06C) * 1.01. Which expression is equivalent, showing the distributed growth?

B. 1.0301A + 1.0601C
C. 1.03A + 1.06C + 1.01

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Expression = (1.03A + 1.06C) * 1.01.
This expression represents the population after initial growth and the incentive program.
2
1.01 (1.03A) + 1.01 (1.06C).

(1.01 1.03)A + (1.01 1.06)C 1.0403A + 1.0706C.

Question 10Grade 7 • EE
A tech company is developing an app. Each user interaction of type X consumes 5c megabytes of data and 2t seconds of processing time. Each user interaction of type Y consumes 3c megabytes of data and 4t seconds of processing time. For a test run involving p interactions of type X and p interactions of type Y, the total data consumed is (5c p) + (3c p) megabytes, and the total processing time is (2t p) + (4t p) seconds. Which simplified expression represents the combined total data and processing time, assuming c and t are distinct units and cannot be combined directly?
A. 8cp + 6tp
B. 8c + 6t
C. 14ctp
D. 14cp + 14tp
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total data = (5c p) + (3c p) = 5cp + 3cp.
Multiply the constants with the variables. Then, using the Distributive Property in reverse, factor out 'cp'.
2
5cp + 3cp = (5 + 3)cp = 8cp.
Add the coefficients of the like terms.
3
Total processing time = (2t p) + (4t p) = 2tp + 4tp.
Multiply the constants with the variables. Then, using the Distributive Property in reverse, factor out 'tp'.
4
2tp + 4tp = (2 + 4)tp = 6tp.
Add the coefficients of the like terms.
5
Combined total = 8cp + 6tp.
Since 'cp' and 'tp' are unlike terms (due to distinct 'c' and 't' units), they cannot be combined further. This is the final simplified expression.

Answer: 8cp + 6tp (Option A)
