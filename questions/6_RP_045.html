Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A store sells a pack of 12 pens for $36. What is the ratio of pens to dollars in simplest form?
A. 4 pens : $12
B. 12 pens : $3
C. 3 pens : $1
D. 1 pen : $3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The ratio of pens to dollars is 12 pens : $36.
This is given directly in the problem statement.
2
The numbers are 12 and 36. Factors of 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12. Factors of 36 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36. The GCF is 12.
To simplify a ratio, you need to divide both parts by their greatest common factor to reduce it to its lowest terms.
3
12 ÷ 12 = 1 36 ÷ 12 = 3 The simplified ratio is 1 pen : $3.
Dividing both numbers by their GCF results in an equivalent ratio in its simplest form.

Answer: 1 pen : $3 (Option D)

Question 7Grade 6 • RP
A construction crew used 525 bricks for a wall that is 105 feet long. What is the ratio of bricks to feet in simplest form?
A. 105 bricks : 21 feet
B. 525 bricks : 1 foot
C. 5 bricks : 1 foot
D. 1 brick : 5 feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The ratio of bricks to feet is 525 bricks : 105 feet.
This represents the quantity of bricks used for a certain length of wall.
2
The numbers are 525 and 105. Both are divisible by 5. 525 ÷ 5 = 105, 105 ÷ 5 = 21. Now consider 105 and 21. Both are divisible by 21. 105 ÷ 21 = 5, 21 ÷ 21 = 1. So the GCF is 5 × 21 = 105.
Finding the GCF allows for the most direct simplification of the ratio.
3
525 ÷ 105 = 5 105 ÷ 105 = 1 The simplified ratio is 5 bricks : 1 foot.
Dividing both numbers by their GCF results in an equivalent ratio in its simplest form.

Answer: 5 bricks : 1 foot (Option C)
