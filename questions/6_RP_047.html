Question 1Grade 6 • RP

B. $1.01
C. $3.78
D. $6.30
E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1
Unit cost = $2.52 / 6 = $0.42 per orange
First, find the cost of one orange by dividing the total cost by the number of oranges.
2
Cost of 15 oranges = $0.42 * 15 = $6.30
Multiply the unit cost by the desired number of oranges (15) to find the total cost.

Answer: $6.30 (Option D)

Question 4Grade 6 • RP
A car travels 210 miles in 3.5 hours. At this rate, how long would it take to travel 450 miles? Give your answer in hours and minutes.
A. 7 hours 50 minutes
B. 8 hours 15 minutes
C. 7 hours and 30 minutes
D. 1 hour 38 minutes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Speed = 210 miles / 3.5 hours = 60 miles per hour (mph)
Calculate the car's speed by dividing the distance by the time taken.
2
Time for 450 miles = 450 miles / 60 mph = 7.5 hours
Divide the new distance by the speed to find the total time in hours.
3
Convert decimal hours to minutes: 0.5 hours * 60 minutes/hour = 30 minutes
Convert the fractional part of the hours (0.5) into minutes.
4
Total time = 7 hours and 30 minutes
Combine the whole number of hours with the converted minutes.

Answer: 7 hours and 30 minutes (Option C)

Question 5Grade 6 • RP
In a school, the ratio of teachers to students is 2:25. If there are 88 teachers, how many students are there?
A. 44 students
B. 1000 students
C. 176 students
D. 1100 students
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Set up the proportion: 2 teachers / 25 students = 88 teachers / x students
Form a proportion relating the given ratio to the known number of teachers and unknown number of students.
2
Cross-multiply: 2 x = 25 88
Multiply the numerator of one ratio by the denominator of the other.
3
Simplify: 2x = 2200
Perform the multiplication on the right side of the equation.
4
Solve for x: x = 2200 / 2 = 1100 students
Divide both sides by 2 to isolate x and find the number of students.

Answer: 1100 students (Option D)

Question 8Grade 6 • RP
A clock gains 7 minutes every 4 hours. If the clock is set correctly at 8:00 AM on Monday, what time will it show at 8:00 PM on Tuesday?
A. 8:03 PM
B. 9:03 PM
C. 9:07 PM
D. 9:00 PM
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Calculate total elapsed time: 8:00 AM Mon to 8:00 AM Tue = 24 hours. 8:00 AM Tue to 8:00 PM Tue = 12 hours. Total = 24 + 12 = 36 hours.
Determine the total number of hours that pass from the initial setting time to the target time.
2
Set up proportion for time gained: 7 minutes / 4 hours = x minutes / 36 hours
Form a proportion using the given rate of gain and the total elapsed time to find the total minutes gained.
3
Cross-multiply and solve for x: 4x = 7 * 36 => 4x = 252 => x = 252 / 4 = 63 minutes gained.
Solve the proportion for x, which represents the total minutes the clock gained.
4
Convert minutes gained to hours and minutes: 63 minutes = 1 hour and 3 minutes.
Convert the total minutes gained into a more readable format of hours and minutes.
5
Add gained time to 8:00 PM: 8:00 PM + 1 hour 3 minutes = 9:03 PM (on Tuesday).
Add the gained time to the actual time (8:00 PM Tuesday) to find what the clock will show.

Answer: 9:03 PM (Option B)

Question 9Grade 6 • RP
A small town's population increases by 12 residents every 7 months. If the current population is 3,500, what will the population be in 2 years, assuming the rate stays constant?
A. 3524 residents
B. 3548 residents
C. 3541 residents
D. 3536 residents
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Convert 2 years to months: 2 years * 12 months/year = 24 months.
Convert the given time frame into a consistent unit (months) for the proportion.
2
Set up proportion for population increase: 12 residents / 7 months = x residents / 24 months
Form a proportion using the given rate of increase and the total number of months to find the total increase in population.
3
Cross-multiply and solve for x: 7x = 12 * 24 => 7x = 288 => x = 288 / 7 = 41.14... residents.
Solve the proportion for x. Since population must be whole numbers, round to the nearest whole resident.
4
Round to nearest whole resident: 41 residents.
Round the calculated increase to the nearest whole number as residents cannot be fractional.
5
Add increase to current population: 3500 + 41 = 3541 residents.
Add the calculated population increase to the current population to find the future population.

Answer: 3,541 residents (Option C)

Question 10Grade 6 • RP
A 3D printer takes 1 hour and 15 minutes to print an entire miniature model. If it prints at a constant rate, how much time (in minutes, rounded to the nearest minute) will it take to print the first 30% of the model?
A. 7.5 minutes
B. 25 minutes
C. 30 minutes
D. 23 minutes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Convert total time to minutes: 1 hour 15 minutes = (1 * 60) + 15 = 75 minutes.
Convert the total print time into a single unit (minutes) for easier calculation.
2
Set up proportion: x minutes / 30% = 75 minutes / 100%
Form a proportion relating the unknown time for 30% to the total time for 100%.
3
Cross-multiply and solve for x: 100 x = 30 75 => 100x = 2250 => x = 2250 / 100 = 22.5 minutes.
Solve the proportion for x, which represents the time taken to print 30% of the model.
4
Round to the nearest minute: 23 minutes.
Round the calculated time to the nearest whole minute as requested.

Answer: 23 minutes (Option D)
