Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A baker has $ \frac{7}{8} $ of a kilogram of sugar. If each cake requires $ \frac{1}{8} $ of a kilogram of sugar, how many cakes can the baker make?
A. 7 cakes
B. $ \frac{7}{64} $ cake
C. 1 cake
D. 56 cakes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total sugar = $ \frac{7}{8} $ kg Sugar per cake = $ \frac{1}{8} $ kg
The problem asks us to find how many portions of $ \frac{1}{8} $ kg are in $ \frac{7}{8} $ kg.
2
To find the number of cakes, divide the total sugar by the sugar per cake: $ \frac{7}{8} \div \frac{1}{8} $
Division is used to find how many times one quantity is contained within another.
3
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal: $ \frac{7}{8} \times \frac{8}{1} = \frac{7 \times 8}{8 \times 1} = \frac{56}{8} $
The reciprocal of $ \frac{1}{8} $ is $ \frac{8}{1} $. Multiplying the numerators and denominators gives $ \frac{56}{8} $.
4
$ \frac{56}{8} = 7 $
Divide 56 by 8 to get the whole number 7. The baker can make 7 cakes.

Answer: 7 cakes (Option A)

Question 4Grade 7 • NS
A car traveled $ \frac{15}{4} $ miles in $ \frac{3}{2} $ hours. What was its average speed in miles per hour, in simplest form?
A. $ \frac{2}{5} $ mph
B. $ \frac{45}{8} $ mph
C. $ 2 \frac{1}{2} $ mph
D. $ 2 \frac{3}{4} $ mph
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total distance = $ \frac{15}{4} $ miles Total time = $ \frac{3}{2} $ hours
To find the average speed, we need to divide the total distance by the total time.
2
Speed = Distance $ \div $ Time $ \frac{15}{4} \div \frac{3}{2} $
The formula for speed is distance divided by time.
3
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal: $ \frac{15}{4} \times \frac{2}{3} $
The reciprocal of $ \frac{3}{2} $ is $ \frac{2}{3} $. We convert the division problem into a multiplication problem.
4
$ \frac{15 \times 2}{4 \times 3} = \frac{30}{12} $ To simplify, divide the numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 6: $ \frac{30 \div 6}{12 \div 6} = \frac{5}{2} $
Multiplying gives $ \frac{30}{12} $. Simplifying this improper fraction by dividing both parts by 6 yields $ \frac{5}{2} $.
5
$ \frac{5}{2} = 2 \frac{1}{2} $
As an improper fraction, $ \frac{5}{2} $ means 5 divided by 2, which is 2 with a remainder of 1. So, $ 2 \frac{1}{2} $ mph is the average speed.

Answer: $ 2 \frac{1}{2} $ mph (Option C)

$ 6 \frac{1}{4} = \frac{(6 \times 4) + 1}{4} = \frac{24 + 1}{4} = \frac{25}{4} $
To perform division, it's easier to work with improper fractions. Multiply the whole number by the denominator and add the numerator, keeping the same denominator.
