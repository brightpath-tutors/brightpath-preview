Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A chef is preparing a recipe that calls for a specific amount of seasoning. The recipe indicates that the seasoning should be at the position of 1/4 on a measuring spoon that ranges from 0 to 1. Locate and plot the position of 1/4 on a number line.
A. A point at 4/1 (or 4)
B. A point at -1/4
C. A point between 0 and 1, specifically at the first mark if divided into 4 equal parts.
D. A point at 1/2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Fraction = 1/4
Identify the given fraction.
2
Divide the segment from 0 to 1 into 4 equal parts.
The denominator (4) indicates how many equal parts the whole is divided into.
3
Count 1 part from 0.
The numerator (1) indicates how many of those parts to count from 0. This marks the position of 1/4.

Answer: C (Option C)

Divide the segment from 0 to -1 into 8 equal parts.
The denominator (8) indicates how many equal parts the segment from 0 to -1 is divided into.

Count 3 parts from 0 in the negative direction.

A. A point between 0 and 1, specifically at the 9/10 mark.
B. A point between 1 and 2, specifically at the 9/10 mark.
C. A point between -1 and -2, specifically at the 9/10 mark.

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution

Initial elevation change = +17/5 meters. Decrease = 3/2 meters. Final elevation = 17/5 - 3/2.
Set up the subtraction problem to find the final position. 'Decreased by' implies subtraction.

Find a common denominator: 10. So, 17/5 = 34/10 and 3/2 = 15/10.
Convert fractions to have a common denominator.
3
Final elevation = 34/10 - 15/10 = 19/10.
Perform the subtraction.
4
Convert to mixed number: 1 9/10.
Convert the improper fraction to a mixed number to better understand its position.
5
Locate between 1 and 2 on the number line.
The whole number part (1) indicates the segment it falls in.
6
Divide the segment from 1 to 2 into 10 equal parts.
The denominator (10) indicates how many equal parts the segment between 1 and 2 is divided into.
7
Count 9 parts from 1.
The numerator (9) indicates how many of those parts to count from 1. This marks the position of 1 9/10.
