Question 1Grade 8 • F
A special collector's coin doubles its market value every day. If it starts with a value of $3 on Monday, what will its value be on Wednesday?
A. $12
B. $18
C. $6
D. $9
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The first term (\(a_1\)) is the value on Monday, which is $3. The value doubles every day, so the common ratio (\(r\)) is 2.
A geometric sequence has a constant ratio between consecutive terms. 'Doubles' indicates a ratio of 2.
2
Value on Tuesday (\(a_2\)) = \(a_1 \times r = 3 \times 2 = 6\).
To find the next term in a geometric sequence, multiply the current term by the common ratio.
3
Value on Wednesday (\(a_3\)) = \(a_2 \times r = 6 \times 2 = 12\).
Continue multiplying by the common ratio to find subsequent terms.

Answer: $12 (Option A)

Question 4Grade 8 • F
A car is traveling at 5 mph. After each hour, its speed increases by a factor of 1.5. What will its speed be after 3 hours?
A. 16.875 mph
B. 8 mph
C. 20 mph
D. 11.25 mph
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The initial speed (\(a_1\)) is 5 mph. The speed increases by a factor of 1.5, so the common ratio (\(r\)) is 1.5.
'Increases by a factor of 1.5' means to multiply by 1.5 each time.
2
We need the speed 'after 3 hours'. This means we calculate the initial speed (\(a_1\)), then the speed after 1 hour (\(a_2\)), after 2 hours (\(a_3\)), and after 3 hours (\(a_4\)). So, we need to find the 4th term (n = 4).
The 'initial' term is the 1st term. 'After 3 hours' refers to the value after 3 applications of the ratio, making it the 4th term in the sequence.
3
Initial speed (\(a_1\)): 5 mph Speed after 1 hour (\(a_2\)): $5 \times 1.5 = 7.5$ mph Speed after 2 hours (\(a_3\)): $7.5 \times 1.5 = 11.25$ mph Speed after 3 hours (\(a_4\)): $11.25 \times 1.5 = 16.875$ mph.
Multiply the previous hour's speed by the common ratio (1.5) repeatedly.

Answer: 16.875 mph (Option A)

Question 5Grade 8 • F

D. 3 pencils
E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1
The initial number of pencils (\(a_1\)) is 6. Using half of their pencils means the remaining portion is 1/2 or 0.5. So the common ratio (\(r\)) is 0.5.
'Uses half' implies multiplying by 0.5 (or dividing by 2).
2
We need the number of pencils 'at the end of 3 weeks'. This means we start with \(a_1\), then count after week 1 (\(a_2\)), after week 2 (\(a_3\)), and after week 3 (\(a_4\)). So, we need to find the 4th term (n = 4).
The 'initial' amount is the 1st term. 'At the end of 3 weeks' refers to the amount remaining after 3 applications of the ratio, making it the 4th term in the sequence.
3
Initial pencils (\(a_1\)): 6 Pencils after week 1 (\(a_2\)): $6 \times 0.5 = 3$ Pencils after week 2 (\(a_3\)): $3 \times 0.5 = 1.5$ Pencils after week 3 (\(a_4\)): $1.5 \times 0.5 = 0.75$.
Multiply the previous week's amount by the common ratio (0.5) repeatedly.

Question 8Grade 8 • F
A grandfather clock chimes 2 times on the first minute. On the second minute, it chimes 4 times. On the third minute, it chimes 8 times. Assuming this pattern continues, how many times will it chime on the 6th minute?
A. 64 times
B. 16 times
C. 12 times
D. 32 times
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The first term (\(a_1\)) is 2 chimes.
The number of chimes on the first minute is the starting term of the sequence.
2
The sequence is 2, 4, 8, ... To find the common ratio (\(r\)), divide a term by its preceding term: $4 \div 2 = 2$ or $8 \div 4 = 2$. So, the common ratio (\(r\)) is 2.
The common ratio is found by dividing any term by its previous term.
3
We need to find the number of chimes on the 6th minute, so \(n = 6\).
The '6th minute' corresponds to the 6th term in the sequence.
4
The formula is \(a_n = a_1 \times r^{(n-1)}\) Substitute the values: \(a_6 = 2 \times 2^{(6-1)}\) \(a_6 = 2 \times 2^5\) \(a_6 = 2 \times 32\) \(a_6 = 64\).
Apply the formula to directly calculate the 6th term, evaluating the exponent first.

Answer: 64 times (Option A)

Question 9Grade 8 • F
A small town's population is currently 2,000. If the population is projected to decrease by 10% each year, what will the approximate population be after 3 years?
A. 1400 people
B. 1700 people
C. 1620 people
D. 1458 people
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The initial population (\(a_1\)) is 2,000. A decrease of 10% means that 100% - 10% = 90% of the population remains each year. So, the common ratio (\(r\)) is 0.9.
A percentage decrease means the remaining percentage is the common ratio.
2
We need the population 'after 3 years'. This means we start with \(a_1\), then calculate after 1 year (\(a_2\)), after 2 years (\(a_3\)), and after 3 years (\(a_4\)). So, we need to find the 4th term (n = 4).
The 'initial' population is the 1st term. 'After 3 years' refers to the population after 3 applications of the ratio, making it the 4th term in the sequence.
3
Year 0 (initial, \(a_1\)): 2000 After 1 year (\(a_2\)): $2000 \times 0.9 = 1800$ After 2 years (\(a_3\)): $1800 \times 0.9 = 1620$ After 3 years (\(a_4\)): $1620 \times 0.9 = 1458$.
Multiply the previous year's population by the common ratio (0.9) repeatedly.

Answer: 1458 people (Option D)

Question 10Grade 8 • F
A computer chip's processing power doubles every 18 months. If a chip initially has a power of 100 units, what will its power be after 54 months?
A. 400 units
B. 600 units
C. 800 units
D. 1600 units
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The initial power (\(a_1\)) is 100 units. The power doubles, so the common ratio (\(r\)) is 2.
'Doubles' indicates a common ratio of 2.
2
The power doubles every 18 months. We want to find the power after 54 months. Number of periods = $54 \text{ months} \div 18 \text{ months/period} = 3$ periods.
Divide the total time by the period length to find how many times the ratio is applied.
3
Since we start with \(a_1\) (initial power), after 3 periods, we will be looking for the \((1 + 3) = 4\)th term. So, \(n = 4\).
If the initial state is the 1st term, then after 3 periods, you're looking for the 4th term.
4
The formula is \(a_n = a_1 \times r^{(n-1)}\) Substitute the values: \(a_4 = 100 \times 2^{(4-1)}\) \(a_4 = 100 \times 2^3\) \(a_4 = 100 \times 8\) \(a_4 = 800\).
Apply the formula to directly calculate the 4th term, evaluating the exponent first.

Answer: 800 units (Option C)
