Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A small business sells handmade jewelry. The cost to produce the jewelry can be modeled by a linear equation. If it costs $50 to produce 10 pieces of jewelry and the cost increases by $3 for each additional piece, what is the equation in point-slope form that represents the cost of producing jewelry?
A. y - 10 = 3(x - 50)
B. y = 3x + 20
C. y + 50 = 3(x + 10)
D. y - 50 = 3(x - 10)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Point (x1, y1) = (10, 50) Slope (m) = 3
The problem states that it costs $50 to produce 10 pieces of jewelry, giving us the point (10, 50). It also states that the cost increases by $3 for each additional piece, which is the definition of the slope.
2
Point-slope form: y - y1 = m(x - x1) y - 50 = 3(x - 10)
Substitute the x-coordinate of the point (10) for x1, the y-coordinate (50) for y1, and the slope (3) for m into the point-slope form equation.

Answer: y - 50 = 3(x - 10) (Option D)
