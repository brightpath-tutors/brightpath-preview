Question 1Grade 6 • NS
The temperature in Anchorage, Alaska, was -5 degrees Celsius. In Fairbanks, it was 3 degrees Celsius. Plot both temperatures on a number line. Which city had the warmer temperature?
A. They had the same temperature
B. Anchorage
C. Cannot be determined
D. Fairbanks
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Anchorage: -5°C Fairbanks: 3°C
We are given two temperatures for two different cities.
2
Numbers to the right are greater (warmer). Numbers to the left are smaller (colder).
On a number line, values increase as you move to the right and decrease as you move to the left. Therefore, a higher number indicates a warmer temperature.
3
3 > -5
Since 3 is to the right of -5 on the number line, 3 is greater than -5.
4
Fairbanks had 3°C, which is warmer than Anchorage's -5°C.
The city with the higher temperature is the warmer city. Fairbanks, with 3°C, is warmer than Anchorage, with -5°C.

Answer: Fairbanks (Option D)

Question 2Grade 6 • NS
A scuba diver is at -12 feet relative to sea level. A bird is flying at an altitude of 8 feet. Represent these positions on a number line. Which creature is further from sea level?
A. The scuba diver
B. Cannot be determined
C. The bird
D. They are the same distance
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Scuba diver: -12 feet Bird: 8 feet
The positions are given as signed numbers where sea level is 0.
2
Further from sea level means a larger absolute distance from 0.
Distance from a reference point (sea level, or 0 on the number line) is represented by the absolute value of the number.
3
|-12| = 12 |8| = 8
The absolute value of -12 is 12, and the absolute value of 8 is 8.
4
12 > 8
Since 12 is greater than 8, the scuba diver is further from sea level.
5
The scuba diver is 12 feet from sea level, while the bird is 8 feet from sea level. Therefore, the scuba diver is further.
The larger absolute value corresponds to being further from sea level.

Answer: The scuba diver (Option A)

Question 3Grade 6 • NS
During a stock market simulation, a student's portfolio lost 7 points on Monday and gained 11 points on Tuesday. If their starting balance was 0, plot these changes. What was their final balance?
A. 4 points
B. -18 points
C. -4 points
D. 18 points
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Lost 7 points: -7 Gained 11 points: +11
Losses are represented by negative integers, and gains by positive integers.
2
0 + (-7) + 11 = -7 + 11 = 4
Start at 0, move 7 units to the left, then 11 units to the right. The final position is 4.

Answer: 4 points (Option A)

Question 9Grade 6 • NS

C. 10th floor
D. 2nd floor
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Starts at: +3 Goes down 8: -8 Goes up 12: +12 Goes down 5: -5
Downward movements are negative, upward movements are positive.
2
3 + (-8) = -5
From the 3rd floor, move 8 floors down. The elevator is at -5 (5 floors below ground).
3
-5 + 12 = 7
From -5, move 12 floors up. The elevator is at the 7th floor.
4
7 + (-5) = 2
From the 7th floor, move 5 floors down. The final position is the 2nd floor.

Question 10Grade 6 • NS
A small construction company experiences financial fluctuations. In January, they had a profit of $5,000. In February, they had a loss of $12,000. In March, they recovered with a profit of $8,000. What is their net financial position at the end of March, starting from a 0 balance?
A. $1,000 profit
B. $25,000 profit
C. $1,000 loss
D. $15,000 profit
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
January profit: +$5,000 February loss: -$12,000 March profit: +$8,000
Profits are positive, losses are negative.
2
0 + 5000 = 5000
Starting at $0, they gained $5,000.
3
5000 + (-12000) = -7000
From a $5,000 profit, they lost $12,000, resulting in a $7,000 loss.
4
-7000 + 8000 = 1000
From a $7,000 loss, they gained $8,000, resulting in a $1,000 profit.

Answer: $1,000 profit (Option A)
