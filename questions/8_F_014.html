Question 1Grade 8 • F
A baker starts the day with 30 kilograms of flour. At the end of each day, 8 kilograms are used. Let \(a_n\) represent the amount of flour (in kg) remaining at the end of day \(n\). Which recursive formula describes the amount of flour remaining each day, given \(a_0 = 30\) kg?
A. \(a_n = a_{n-1} + 8\)
B. \(a_n = 30 - 8n\)
C. \(a_n = 8a_{n-1}\)
D. \(a_n = a_{n-1} - 8\), for \(n \ge 1\); \(a_0 = 30\)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The baker starts with 30 kg of flour. So, the initial amount at the start of day 0 is \(a_0 = 30\).
The problem states the initial amount of flour is 30 kg, which corresponds to \(a_0\).
2
8 kilograms are used at the end of each day. This means the amount of flour decreases by 8 kg each day.
The phrase '8 kilograms are used' indicates a subtraction of 8 from the previous day's amount.
3
To find the amount of flour on day \(n\) (\(a_n\)), we take the amount from the previous day (\(a_{n-1}\)) and subtract 8 kg. Thus, \(a_n = a_{n-1} - 8\).
A recursive formula defines a term based on previous terms. Since the amount decreases by 8 kg daily, we subtract 8 from the previous term.
4
\(a_n = a_{n-1} - 8\), for \(n \ge 1\); \(a_0 = 30\)
This gives the complete recursive definition for the sequence.

Answer: \(a_n = a_{n-1} - 8\), for \(n \ge 1\); \(a_0 = 30\) (Option D)

Question 8Grade 8 • F
A city's population is 150,000 people and decreases by 0.5% annually. Let \(P_n\) be the population after \(n\) years. Which recursive formula models this population change?
A. \(P_n = P_{n-1} \times (0.995)\), for \(n \ge 1\); \(P_0 = 150,000\)
B. \(P_n = 150,000 \times (0.005)^n\)
C. \(P_n = P_{n-1} - 0.005\)
D. \(P_n = P_{n-1} + 0.995\)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The initial population is 150,000 people. So, \(P_0 = 150,000\).
The starting population at year 0 is given.
2
The population decreases by 0.5% annually. This means the population retains 100% - 0.5% = 99.5% of its value each year. As a decimal, this is 0.995.
A percentage decrease means multiplying by (1 - percentage/100).
3
To find the population after \(n\) years (\(P_n\)), we take the population from the previous year (\(P_{n-1}\)) and multiply it by 0.995. Thus, \(P_n = P_{n-1} \times (0.995)\).
The recursive rule shows how each subsequent term is derived from the preceding one using multiplication for a percentage change.
4
\(P_n = P_{n-1} \times (0.995)\), for \(n \ge 1\); \(P_0 = 150,000\)
This provides the complete recursive definition.

Answer: \(P_n = P_{n-1} \times (0.995)\), for \(n \ge 1\); \(P_0 = 150,000\) (Option A)
