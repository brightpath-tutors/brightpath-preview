Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A grocery store sells oranges where the cost is directly proportional to the number of oranges bought. If 5 oranges cost $2.50, what is the constant of proportionality (cost per orange)?
A. $0.20 per orange
B. $0.50 per orange
C. $2.00 per orange
D. $3.00 per orange
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let y = cost and x = number of oranges.
The problem states that the cost is directly proportional to the number of oranges. We define 'y' as the dependent variable (cost) and 'x' as the independent variable (number of oranges).
2
For direct proportionality, the relationship is y = kx, where k is the constant of proportionality. To find k, use the formula k = y/x.
The constant of proportionality (k) represents the unit rate, in this case, the cost per orange.
3
y = $2.50, x = 5 oranges.
We are given that 5 oranges cost $2.50, so we plug these values into our equation.
4
k = $2.50 / 5 oranges = $0.50 per orange.
Dividing the total cost by the number of oranges gives us the cost for one orange, which is the constant of proportionality.

Answer: $0.50 per orange (Option B)
