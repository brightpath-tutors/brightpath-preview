Question 1Grade 8 • NS
A jewelry designer needs to cut a square piece of precious metal with an area of 22 square centimeters. To the nearest tenth of a centimeter, what is the approximate side length of the piece?
A. 5.0 cm
B. 4.7 cm
C. 11.0 cm
D. 4.6 cm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The area of a square is s², so the side length (s) is the square root of the area. We need to find √22.
To find the side length of a square given its area, we take the square root of the area.
2
4² = 16 5² = 25 Since 16 < 22 < 25, we know that 4 < √22 < 5.
Bounding the irrational number between two consecutive integers helps narrow down the approximation.
3
Since 22 is closer to 25 (difference of 3) than to 16 (difference of 6), √22 will be closer to 5. Let's test values near 5: 4.6² = 21.16 4.7² = 22.09 4.8² = 23.04
By squaring numbers with one decimal place, we can find a closer approximation.
4
Compare 22 to 4.6² (21.16) and 4.7² (22.09): Difference from 4.6²: |22 - 21.16| = 0.84 Difference from 4.7²: |22 - 22.09| = 0.09 Since 0.09 is smaller than 0.84, 4.7 is the closer approximation.
The value whose square is closest to the given number is the best approximation to that decimal place.
5
Rounded to the nearest tenth, the side length is 4.7 cm.
The final answer should be rounded as specified in the problem.

Answer: 4.7 cm (Option B)
