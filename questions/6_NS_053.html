Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A recipe calls for 7/8 cup of broth. If the recipe is to be divided into portions, and each portion uses 1/16 cup of broth, how many portions can be made?
A. 7/128 portions
B. 7/2 portions
C. 14 portions
D. 1/14 portions
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
We need to find out how many times 1/16 cup fits into 7/8 cup. This is a division problem: (7/8) ÷ (1/16).
Identify the operation needed to solve the problem, which is division to find out how many smaller parts are in a larger whole.
2
(7/8) × (16/1)
To divide fractions, multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second fraction. The reciprocal of 1/16 is 16/1.
3
(7 × 16) / (8 × 1) = 112 / 8
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.
4
112 ÷ 8 = 14
Simplify the resulting fraction by dividing the numerator by the denominator. Therefore, 14 portions can be made.

Answer: 14 portions (Option C)

Question 5Grade 6 • NS

B. 8/11 days
C. 11/18 days
D. 33/24 days
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
We need to find how many 2/3 page segments fit into 11/12 of a page. This is a division problem: (11/12) ÷ (2/3).
Identify the operation needed to solve the problem, which is division to find out how many smaller parts are in a larger whole.
2
(11/12) × (3/2)
To divide fractions, multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second fraction. The reciprocal of 2/3 is 3/2.
3
(11 × 3) / (12 × 2) = 33 / 24
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.
4
33 ÷ 3 = 11, 24 ÷ 3 = 8. So, 11/8. Convert 11/8 to a mixed number: 11 ÷ 8 = 1 with a remainder of 3. So, 1 3/8.
Simplify the resulting fraction by dividing the numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor (3). Then, convert the improper fraction to a mixed number as requested by 'simplest form' in this context. Therefore, it will take 1 3/8 days to finish the paper.
