Question 1Grade 6 • EE
A cashier needs to calculate the length of the side of a square display stand with an area of 75 square inches. What is the side length in simplest radical form?
A. 3√25 inches
B. 5√15 inches
C. 5√3 inches
D. 25√3 inches
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Area = 75 square inches. For a square, side length (s) = √Area. So we need to simplify √75.
The side length of a square is the square root of its area. Therefore, we need to simplify the radical expression √75.
2
Factors of 75: 1, 3, 5, 15, 25, 75. The perfect square factors are 1 and 25. The largest is 25.
To simplify a radical, we look for the largest perfect square that divides the number under the square root sign (the radicand).
3
√75 = √(25 × 3)
We express 75 as a product of its largest perfect square factor (25) and another factor (3).
4
√(25 × 3) = √25 × √3 = 5√3
According to the property √(ab) = √a × √b, we can separate the square root. We then calculate √25, which is 5, leaving √3 under the radical as it cannot be simplified further.

Answer: 5√3 inches (Option C)
