Question 1Grade 6 • EE
Maya bought a toy. She paid with a $10 bill and received $3.50 in change. How much did the toy cost? Let x represent the cost of the toy.
A. $6.50
B. $7.50
C. $6.00
D. $13.50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
10 - x = 3.50
Write the equation representing the problem. The initial amount minus the cost of the toy equals the change received.
2
x = 10 - 3.50
To solve for x, subtract the change from the initial amount paid.
3
x = 6.50
Perform the subtraction to find the cost of the toy.

Answer: $6.50 (Option A)

Question 9Grade 6 • EE
A small town had 5/6 of its population living within the city limits. After a new housing development was built outside the city, the fraction of the population living within city limits decreased to 1/3. What fraction of the original population moved outside the city limits? Let p be the fraction of population that moved. Give your answer in simplest form.
A. 1/2
B. 4/3
C. 7/6
D. 1/3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
5/6 - p = 1/3
Write the equation. The initial fraction of population in the city minus the fraction that moved out equals the remaining fraction in the city.
2
p = 5/6 - 1/3
To solve for p, subtract the new fraction in the city from the original fraction.
3
p = 5/6 - 2/6
Find a common denominator for 5/6 and 1/3. The least common multiple of 6 and 3 is 6. Convert 1/3 to an equivalent fraction with a denominator of 6 (1/3 = 2/6).
4
p = 3/6
Subtract the numerators and keep the common denominator.
5
p = 1/2
Simplify the fraction by dividing both the numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor, 3 (3/6 = 1/2).

Answer: 1/2 (Option A)
