Question 1Grade 5 • NBT
A small item costs $0.354. How would you read this price in words?
A. Zero point three five four dollars.
B. Zero and three hundred fifty-four thousandths of a dollar.
C. Three hundred fifty-four hundredths of a dollar.
D. Three hundred fifty-four thousandths of a dollar.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Whole number part = 0
Identify the whole number part of the decimal.
2
Decimal part digits = 354
Identify the digits in the decimal part.
3
Last digit '4' is in the thousandths place.
Determine the place value of the last digit in the decimal part. The first digit after the decimal is tenths, the second is hundredths, and the third is thousandths.
4
Read decimal part as 'three hundred fifty-four thousandths'.
Read the decimal part as a whole number, followed by the place value of its last digit. Since the whole number part is zero, 'and' is not used.
5
Final reading: Three hundred fifty-four thousandths of a dollar.
Combine the reading with the currency context.

Answer: Three hundred fifty-four thousandths of a dollar. (Option D)
