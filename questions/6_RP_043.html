Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A small rectangular price tag is 3 cm long and 2 cm wide. A larger similar price tag is 9 cm long. What is the width of the larger price tag?
A. 13.5 cm
B. 8 cm
C. 4.5 cm
D. 6 cm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the width of the larger price tag. Small Length / Large Length = Small Width / Large Width 3 cm / 9 cm = 2 cm / x cm
For similar figures, the ratio of corresponding sides is equal. We match length to length and width to width.
2
3/9 = 2/x 3 x = 9 2 (Cross-multiply) 3x = 18
Cross-multiplication is used to solve proportions.
3
3x = 18 x = 18 / 3 x = 6
Divide both sides by 3 to find the value of x, which is the width of the larger price tag.

Answer: 6 cm (Option D)

C. 16 km
D. 3 km
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Longest side of larger park = 8 km Shortest side of larger park = 5 km Shortest side of smaller park = 2.5 km Scale Factor (smaller to larger) = (Shortest side of smaller park) / (Shortest side of larger park) = 2.5 km / 5 km = 0.5
The scale factor is the ratio of corresponding side lengths between the two similar figures.
2
Longest side of smaller park = (Longest side of larger park) Scale Factor Longest side of smaller park = 8 km 0.5 Longest side of smaller park = 4 km
Multiply the longest side of the larger park by the scale factor to find the corresponding side on the smaller map.

Answer: 4 km (Option A)

A. 42 cm
B. 27 cm
C. 33 cm
D. 21 cm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Scale Factor (SF) = Large Length / Small Length = 10.5 cm / 7 cm = 1.5
The scale factor is the ratio of corresponding lengths between similar figures.
2
Large Width = Small Width SF = 4 cm 1.5 = 6 cm
Multiply the width of the smaller dish by the scale factor to find the width of the larger dish.
3
Perimeter = 2 (Length + Width) Perimeter = 2 (10.5 cm + 6 cm) Perimeter = 2 * (16.5 cm) Perimeter = 33 cm
The perimeter of a rectangle is found by adding its length and width, then multiplying the sum by 2.

2

The scale factor is the ratio of corresponding side lengths. We use the longest sides as they are given for both figures.
3

Multiply the shortest side of the model by the scale factor to find the corresponding shortest side of the actual support.
4
3000 cm = 3000 / 100 meters = 30 meters
Convert the length from centimeters back to meters as requested by the question.
