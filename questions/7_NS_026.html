Question 1Grade 7 • NS
Sarah has a ribbon that is 7/8 of a yard long. She wants to cut it into pieces that are each 1/4 of a yard long. How many pieces of ribbon can she make?
A. 7/32 pieces
B. 3 1/2 pieces
C. 2/7 pieces
D. 1 1/8 pieces
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
To find out how many pieces of ribbon can be made, we need to divide the total length of the ribbon by the length of each piece.
This is a division problem: total length ÷ length per piece.
2
\frac{7}{8} \div \frac{1}{4
The total length is 7/8 yards, and each piece is 1/4 yards.
3
\frac{7}{8} \times \frac{4}{1
To divide fractions, we 'Keep, Change, Flip': keep the first fraction, change division to multiplication, and flip (find the reciprocal of) the second fraction.
4
\frac{7 \times 4}{8 \times 1} = \frac{28}{8
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.
5
\frac{28}{8} = \frac{7}{2} = 3 \frac{1}{2
Divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common factor (4). Then convert the improper fraction to a mixed number. 7 divided by 2 is 3 with a remainder of 1, so it's 3 1/2.

Answer: 3 1/2 pieces (Option B)

Question 8Grade 7 • NS

D. 1 1/2 flower beds

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
To find the number of flower beds, we need to divide the total time spent by the time taken per flower bed.
This is a division problem: total time ÷ time per flower bed.
2
2 \frac{1}{3} = \frac{(2 \times 3) + 1}{3} = \frac{7}{3
Multiply the whole number by the denominator and add the numerator. Keep the same denominator.
3
\frac{7}{3} \div \frac{5}{6
Now we divide the improper fraction 7/3 by 5/6.
4
\frac{7}{3} \times \frac{6}{5
Keep the first fraction, change division to multiplication, and flip the second fraction.
5
\frac{7 \times 6}{3 \times 5} = \frac{42}{15
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.
6
\frac{42}{15} = \frac{14}{5} = 2 \frac{4}{5
Divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common factor (3). Then convert the improper fraction to a mixed number.
