Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Two cell phone plans are available. Plan A charges a $25 monthly fee plus $0.10 per minute. Plan B charges a $15 monthly fee plus $0.15 per minute. For how many minutes of usage will the total cost of the two plans be the same?
A. 50 minutes
B. 0.5 minutes
C. 40 minutes
D. 200 minutes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'm' be the number of minutes. Cost of Plan A = 25 + 0.10m Cost of Plan B = 15 + 0.15m To find when the costs are the same: 25 + 0.10m = 15 + 0.15m
We express the total cost for each plan as a linear equation, where 'm' is the variable for minutes. We set them equal to find the point where their costs are identical.
2
25 + 0.10m = 15 + 0.15m Subtract 0.10m from both sides: 25 = 15 + 0.05m
To begin solving for 'm', we move all terms involving 'm' to one side of the equation. Subtracting 0.10m from both sides achieves this while keeping the equation balanced.
3
25 = 15 + 0.05m Subtract 15 from both sides: 10 = 0.05m
Next, we gather all constant terms on the opposite side of the equation. Subtracting 15 from both sides isolates the 0.05m term.
4
10 = 0.05m Divide both sides by 0.05: m = 10 / 0.05 m = 200
Finally, we divide both sides by the coefficient of 'm' (0.05) to find the value of 'm'. This gives us the number of minutes for which the costs are equal.

Answer: 200 minutes (Option D)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
Runner X starts 50 meters ahead of Runner Y and runs at a speed of 7 meters per second. Runner Y starts at the beginning (0 meters) and runs at a speed of 9 meters per second. After how many seconds will Runner Y catch Runner X?
A. 8 seconds
B. 25 seconds
C. 50 seconds
D. 10 seconds
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 't' be the time in seconds. Distance for Runner X = 50 + 7t Distance for Runner Y = 9t To find when Runner Y catches Runner X: 50 + 7t = 9t
We formulate expressions for the distance covered by each runner. Runner X starts with a 50-meter advantage, while Runner Y starts at 0. We equate their distances to find when they meet.
2
50 + 7t = 9t Subtract 7t from both sides: 50 = 2t
To solve for 't', we move all terms containing 't' to one side. Subtracting 7t from both sides keeps the equation balanced and isolates the constant term.
3
50 = 2t Divide both sides by 2: t = 50 / 2 t = 25
Finally, we divide both sides by the coefficient of 't' (2) to determine the time in seconds when Runner Y catches Runner X.

Answer: 25 seconds (Option B)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
Train A travels at 60 miles per hour and leaves a station at 1:00 PM. Train B travels at 70 miles per hour and leaves the same station at 2:00 PM, traveling in the same direction. At what time will Train B catch Train A? (Assume t is the time in hours elapsed since 1:00 PM).
A. 7:00 PM
B. 8:00 PM
C. 6:00 PM
D. 9:00 PM
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 't' be the time in hours since 1:00 PM. Distance for Train A = 60t Train B leaves 1 hour later, so its travel time is (t - 1). Distance for Train B = 70(t - 1) To find when Train B catches Train A: 60t = 70(t - 1)
We define 't' as the elapsed time for Train A. Since Train B starts an hour later, its travel time is 't-1'. We equate their distance expressions to find when Train B catches Train A.
2
60t = 70(t - 1) 60t = 70t - 70
Apply the distributive property to the right side of the equation to expand 70(t - 1).
3
60t = 70t - 70 Subtract 70t from both sides: -10t = -70
To solve for 't', move all terms containing 't' to one side. Subtracting 70t from both sides isolates the constant term on the right.
4
-10t = -70 Divide both sides by -10: t = -70 / -10 t = 7 hours 7 hours after 1:00 PM is 8:00 PM.
Divide both sides by -10 to find 't'. Then, add this elapsed time to the starting time of 1:00 PM to get the final time.

Answer: 8:00 PM (Option B)

2

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
Person A has 5 hours of work remaining on a project and completes tasks at a rate of 2/3 of an hour of work per actual hour spent. Person B has 7 hours of work remaining and completes tasks at a rate of 3/4 of an hour of work per actual hour spent. After how many actual hours spent will both persons have the same amount of work remaining?
A. 1/6 hours
B. 24 hours
C. 12 hours
D. 2 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 't' be the actual hours spent. Work remaining for Person A = 5 - (2/3)t Work remaining for Person B = 7 - (3/4)t To find when the remaining work is equal: 5 - (2/3)t = 7 - (3/4)t
We write equations representing the remaining work for each person. The initial remaining work is the constant, and the rate of completion is the fractional coefficient of 't'. Equating these expressions finds when the remaining work is equal.
2
5 - (2/3)t = 7 - (3/4)t Add (3/4)t to both sides: 5 + (3/4)t - (2/3)t = 7
To consolidate the variable terms, we add (3/4)t to both sides of the equation. This isolates the constants on one side and groups the 't' terms.
3
5 + (3/4)t - (2/3)t = 7 Find a common denominator for 3/4 and 2/3, which is 12. (3/4) = 9/12, (2/3) = 8/12 5 + (9/12)t - (8/12)t = 7 5 + (1/12)t = 7
We find a common denominator for the fractions (3/4 and 2/3) to combine them. The least common multiple of 4 and 3 is 12. This simplifies the expression with 't'.
4
5 + (1/12)t = 7 Subtract 5 from both sides: (1/12)t = 2
Next, we isolate the term with 't' by subtracting 5 from both sides of the equation.
5
(1/12)t = 2 Multiply both sides by 12: t = 2 * 12 t = 24
Finally, we multiply both sides by the reciprocal of 1/12 (which is 12) to solve for 't', giving the number of hours until the remaining work is equal.

Answer: 24 hours (Option B)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
City X has a population of 25,000 and grows by 300 people per year. City Y has a population of 30,000 and declines by 200 people per year. After how many years will the populations of the two cities be equal?
A. 10 years
B. 50 years
C. 100 years
D. 5 years
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'y' be the number of years. Population of City X = 25000 + 300y Population of City Y = 30000 - 200y To find when populations are equal: 25000 + 300y = 30000 - 200y
We model the population of each city using linear equations. City X's population increases, so we add. City Y's population decreases, so we subtract. Equating the expressions finds when populations are equal.
2
25000 + 300y = 30000 - 200y Add 200y to both sides: 25000 + 500y = 30000
To consolidate the variable terms, we add 200y to both sides of the equation. This groups all terms involving 'y' on the left side.
3
25000 + 500y = 30000 Subtract 25000 from both sides: 500y = 5000
Next, we isolate the term with 'y' by subtracting 25000 from both sides of the equation.
4
500y = 5000 Divide both sides by 500: y = 5000 / 500 y = 10
Finally, we divide both sides by the coefficient of 'y' (500) to find the number of years after which the populations of both cities will be equal.

Answer: 10 years (Option A)
