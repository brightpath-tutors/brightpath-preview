Question 1Grade 8 • EE
The sum of two numbers is 30. Their difference is 10. What are the two numbers?
A. 15 and 15
B. 20 and 10
C. 25 and 5
D. 10 and 20
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let x be the first number and y be the second number. x + y = 30 x - y = 10
We translate the given information into two linear equations.
2
(x + y) + (x - y) = 30 + 10 2x = 40 x = 20
Adding the equations eliminates y because the coefficients of y are opposites (+1 and -1).
3
20 + y = 30 y = 30 - 20 y = 10
Using the first equation, we substitute x=20 and solve for y.
4
The two numbers are 20 and 10.
We have found both x and y.

Answer: 20 and 10 (Option B)

2
(c + o) + (c - o) = 50 + 10 2c = 60 c = 30

3
30 + o = 50 o = 50 - 30 o = 20
Using the first equation, we substitute c=30 and solve for o.
4
They sold 30 chocolate chip cookies and 20 oatmeal cookies.

Question 3Grade 8 • EE
A small movie theater sold 200 tickets for a matinee showing. Adult tickets cost $8 and child tickets cost $5. If the total revenue from ticket sales was $1300, how many adult tickets and child tickets were sold?
A. 80 adult tickets and 120 child tickets
B. 120 adult tickets and 80 child tickets
C. 150 adult tickets and 50 child tickets
D. 100 adult tickets and 100 child tickets
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let a be the number of adult tickets and c be the number of child tickets. a + c = 200 (Equation 1: total tickets) 8a + 5c = 1300 (Equation 2: total revenue)
We translate the given information into two linear equations based on the number of tickets and the total revenue.
2
-5(a + c) = -5(200) -5a - 5c = -1000
Multiplying by -5 will make the coefficient of c in the modified Equation 1 the opposite of the coefficient of c in Equation 2.
3
(-5a - 5c) + (8a + 5c) = -1000 + 1300 3a = 300 a = 100
Adding the equations eliminates c, allowing us to solve for a.
4
100 + c = 200 c = 200 - 100 c = 100
Using Equation 1, we substitute a=100 and solve for c.
5
100 adult tickets and 100 child tickets were sold.
We have found both a and c.

Answer: 100 adult tickets and 100 child tickets (Option D)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A group of athletes ran a total of 150 miles last week. One runner, Alice, ran twice as many miles as another runner, Bob. How many miles did each runner run?
A. Alice ran 50 miles, Bob ran 100 miles
B. Alice ran 60 miles, Bob ran 30 miles
C. Alice ran 100 miles, Bob ran 50 miles
D. Alice ran 75 miles, Bob ran 75 miles
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let A be the miles Alice ran and B be the miles Bob ran. A + B = 150 A = 2B => A - 2B = 0
The total miles ran is 150, and Alice ran twice as many miles as Bob. We rearrange the second equation for elimination.
2
2(A + B) = 2(150) 2A + 2B = 300
Multiplying the first equation by 2 will make the coefficient of B the opposite of the coefficient of B in the second rearranged equation.
3
(2A + 2B) + (A - 2B) = 300 + 0 3A = 300 A = 100
Adding the equations eliminates B, allowing us to solve for A.
4
100 + B = 150 B = 150 - 100 B = 50
Using the first equation, we substitute A=100 and solve for B.
5
Alice ran 100 miles, Bob ran 50 miles.
We have found both A and B.

Answer: Alice ran 100 miles, Bob ran 50 miles (Option C)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE
A chef is making a stew that requires 250 grams of vegetables in total. The recipe calls for carrots and potatoes. The amount of carrots should be 50 grams less than the amount of potatoes. How many grams of each vegetable should the chef use?
A. 150 grams of potatoes and 100 grams of carrots
B. 125 grams of potatoes and 125 grams of carrots
C. 200 grams of potatoes and 50 grams of carrots
D. 100 grams of potatoes and 150 grams of carrots
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let p be the grams of potatoes and c be the grams of carrots. p + c = 250 c = p - 50 => p - c = 50
The total grams of vegetables is 250, and carrots are 50 grams less than potatoes. We rearrange the second equation for elimination.
2
(p + c) + (p - c) = 250 + 50 2p = 300 p = 150
Adding the equations eliminates c because the coefficients of c are opposites (+1 and -1).
3
150 + c = 250 c = 250 - 150 c = 100
Using the first equation, we substitute p=150 and solve for c.
4
The chef should use 150 grams of potatoes and 100 grams of carrots.
We have found both p and c.

Answer: 150 grams of potatoes and 100 grams of carrots (Option A)

Question 6Grade 8 • EE
A train travels at a constant speed, and a car travels at a constant speed. The train's speed is 20 mph faster than the car's speed. If the sum of their speeds is 180 mph, what are their individual speeds?
A. Train: 80 mph, Car: 100 mph
B. Train: 110 mph, Car: 90 mph
C. Train: 100 mph, Car: 80 mph
D. Train: 90 mph, Car: 90 mph
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let t be the train's speed and c be the car's speed. t = c + 20 => t - c = 20 t + c = 180
The train's speed is 20 mph faster than the car's speed, and the sum of their speeds is 180 mph. We rearrange the first equation for elimination.
2
(t - c) + (t + c) = 20 + 180 2t = 200 t = 100
Adding the equations eliminates c because the coefficients of c are opposites (-1 and +1).
3
100 + c = 180 c = 180 - 100 c = 80
Using the second equation, we substitute t=100 and solve for c.
4
Train: 100 mph, Car: 80 mph.
We have found both t and c.

Answer: Train: 100 mph, Car: 80 mph (Option C)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A student collected data on two different types of plants. Plant A grew 3 cm more than Plant B in a week. The total growth for both plants was 25 cm. How much did each plant grow?
A. Plant A: 13 cm, Plant B: 10 cm
B. Plant A: 12.5 cm, Plant B: 12.5 cm
C. Plant A: 11 cm, Plant B: 14 cm
D. Plant A: 14 cm, Plant B: 11 cm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let A be the growth of Plant A and B be the growth of Plant B. A = B + 3 => A - B = 3 A + B = 25
Plant A grew 3 cm more than Plant B, and the total growth was 25 cm. We rearrange the first equation for elimination.
2
(A - B) + (A + B) = 3 + 25 2A = 28 A = 14
Adding the equations eliminates B because the coefficients of B are opposites (-1 and +1).
3
14 + B = 25 B = 25 - 14 B = 11
Using the second equation, we substitute A=14 and solve for B.
4
Plant A: 14 cm, Plant B: 11 cm.
We have found both A and B.

Answer: Plant A: 14 cm, Plant B: 11 cm (Option D)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A laboratory has two solutions of acid. Solution A is 20% acid and Solution B is 50% acid. The chemist needs to mix the two solutions to get 100 ml of a 30% acid solution. How many milliliters of Solution A and Solution B are needed?
A. 75 ml of Solution A and 25 ml of Solution B
B. 50 ml of Solution A and 50 ml of Solution B
C. 20 ml of Solution A and 80 ml of Solution B
D. 66.67 ml of Solution A and 33.33 ml of Solution B
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let A be the volume of Solution A and B be the volume of Solution B. A + B = 100 (Equation 1: total volume) 0.20A + 0.50B = 0.30(100) => 0.20A + 0.50B = 30 (Equation 2: total acid)
The total volume of the mixture is 100 ml, and the total amount of acid in the mixture is 30% of 100 ml.
2
-0.20(A + B) = -0.20(100) -0.20A - 0.20B = -20
Multiplying by -0.20 will make the coefficient of A in the modified Equation 1 the opposite of the coefficient of A in Equation 2.
3
(-0.20A - 0.20B) + (0.20A + 0.50B) = -20 + 30 0.30B = 10 B = 10 / 0.30 B = 33.33 (rounded to two decimal places)
Adding the equations eliminates A, allowing us to solve for B. Division is performed to find B.
4
A + 33.33 = 100 A = 100 - 33.33 A = 66.67
Using Equation 1, we substitute B=33.33 and solve for A.
5
66.67 ml of Solution A and 33.33 ml of Solution B are needed.
We have found both A and B.

Answer: 66.67 ml of Solution A and 33.33 ml of Solution B (Option D)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
Two construction crews are working on a road project. Crew X lays 1.5 times more feet of road per day than Crew Y. Together, they lay 750 feet of road per day. How many feet of road does each crew lay per day?
A. Crew X: 300 feet, Crew Y: 450 feet
B. Crew X: 450 feet, Crew Y: 300 feet
C. Crew X: 500 feet, Crew Y: 250 feet
D. Crew X: 375 feet, Crew Y: 375 feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let X be the feet of road Crew X lays and Y be the feet of road Crew Y lays. X = 1.5Y => X - 1.5Y = 0 X + Y = 750
Crew X lays 1.5 times more than Crew Y, and their combined output is 750 feet. We rearrange the first equation for elimination.
2
1.5(X + Y) = 1.5(750) 1.5X + 1.5Y = 1125
Multiplying the second equation by 1.5 will make the coefficient of Y the opposite of the coefficient of Y in the rearranged first equation.
3
(X - 1.5Y) + (1.5X + 1.5Y) = 0 + 1125 2.5X = 1125 X = 1125 / 2.5 X = 450
Adding the equations eliminates Y, allowing us to solve for X. Division is performed to find X.
4
450 + Y = 750 Y = 750 - 450 Y = 300
Using the second equation, we substitute X=450 and solve for Y.
5
Crew X: 450 feet, Crew Y: 300 feet.
We have found both X and Y.

Answer: Crew X: 450 feet, Crew Y: 300 feet (Option B)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
An online tech store sells two models of smartwatches: Model S and Model L. Model S costs $150 and Model L costs $200. On a particular day, the store sold 30 smartwatches for a total revenue of $5250. How many of each model were sold?
A. 10 Model S smartwatches and 20 Model L smartwatches
B. 20 Model S smartwatches and 10 Model L smartwatches
C. 18 Model S smartwatches and 12 Model L smartwatches
D. 15 Model S smartwatches and 15 Model L smartwatches
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let S be the number of Model S smartwatches and L be the number of Model L smartwatches. S + L = 30 (Equation 1: total smartwatches) 150S + 200L = 5250 (Equation 2: total revenue)
The total number of smartwatches sold is 30, and the total revenue is $5250 based on the price of each model.
2
-150(S + L) = -150(30) -150S - 150L = -4500
Multiplying by -150 will make the coefficient of S in the modified Equation 1 the opposite of the coefficient of S in Equation 2.
3
(-150S - 150L) + (150S + 200L) = -4500 + 5250 50L = 750 L = 750 / 50 L = 15
Adding the equations eliminates S, allowing us to solve for L. Division is performed to find L.
4
S + 15 = 30 S = 30 - 15 S = 15
Using Equation 1, we substitute L=15 and solve for S.
5
15 Model S smartwatches and 15 Model L smartwatches were sold.
We have found both S and L.

Answer: 15 Model S smartwatches and 15 Model L smartwatches (Option D)
