Question 1Grade 6 • NS
Alex spent $7 on snacks each day for 4 days. If we represent spending as a negative integer, what is the total change in his money over these 4 days?
A. 28
B. -28
C. -24
D. -3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Spending $7 is represented as -7. The number of days is 4.
We translate the real-world scenario into integers. Spending money is a decrease, hence negative.
2
To find the total change, multiply the daily spending by the number of days: 4 * (-7).
Since Alex spent the same amount each day for multiple days, multiplication is the correct operation.
3
When multiplying a positive integer by a negative integer, the product is negative. 4 7 = 28, so 4 (-7) = -28.
The product of a positive and a negative integer is always negative. The total change in Alex's money is a decrease of $28.

Answer: -28 (Option B)

Question 9Grade 6 • NS
An art gallery uses a scoring system where each critical negative review lowers a piece's public appeal by 3 points (represented as -3). An influential art blogger published 5 such negative reviews. However, the gallery's website had a bug that reversed the effect of each negative review on the score. What is the total change to the public appeal score due to the blogger's reviews and the website bug?
A. 15
B. 7
C. -7
D. -15
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Each negative review lowers appeal by -3 points. The blogger published 5 reviews. The website bug reversed the effect, which is equivalent to multiplying by -1.
A decrease is negative. 'Reversing the effect' of a negative change is another negative, so we have three factors: -1 (reversal), -3 (per review), and 5 (number of reviews).
2
To find the total change, multiply the 'reversal' factor, the score change per review, and the number of reviews: (-1) (-3) 5.
We combine all factors through multiplication to find the final score change.
3
First, multiply the two negative integers: (-1) * (-3) = 3. (The product of two negatives is positive). Next, multiply the result by the remaining positive integer: 3 * 5 = 15.
Multiplying two negative numbers yields a positive result. Then, multiplying that positive result by another positive number yields a positive result. The total change is an increase of 15 points.

Answer: 15 (Option A)
