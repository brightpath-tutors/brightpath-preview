Question 1Grade 7 • EE
Sarah bought a toy for $12.50. After buying the toy, she had $7.25 left in her wallet. How much money did Sarah have before buying the toy?
A. $5.25
B. $19.00
C. $19.75
D. $1.975
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Money left = $7.25, Money spent = $12.50
Identify the known values: the amount of money Sarah had left and the amount she spent.
2
Original money = Money left + Money spent
To find the original amount of money, we need to reverse the operation of spending. This means adding the money she had left to the money she spent.
3
Original money = $7.25 + $12.50 = $19.75
Perform the addition to find the total amount Sarah had initially.

Answer: $19.75 (Option C)

Question 2Grade 7 • EE
In a basketball game, the home team scored 28 points in the first half. They ended the game with a total of 55 points. How many points did they score in the second half?
A. 83 points
B. 27 points
C. 37 points
D. 33 points
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total points = 55, First half points = 28
Identify the known values: the total points scored and the points scored in the first half.
2
Second half points = Total points - First half points
To find the points scored in the second half, subtract the first half's score from the total score.
3
Second half points = 55 - 28 = 27
Perform the subtraction to find the points scored in the second half.

Answer: 27 points (Option B)

Question 3Grade 7 • EE
A chef used 1.25 cups of sugar from a bag. After baking, there were 2.5 cups of sugar left in the bag. How much sugar was in the bag initially?
A. 3.75 cups
B. 1.25 cups
C. 15.0 cups
D. 3.25 cups
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Sugar used = 1.25 cups, Sugar left = 2.5 cups
Identify the known values: the amount of sugar used and the amount remaining.
2
Initial sugar = Sugar used + Sugar left
To find the initial amount of sugar, add the amount used to the amount left. This is working backward from the depletion of sugar.
3
Initial sugar = 1.25 + 2.5 = 3.75 cups
Perform the addition to find the total sugar initially in the bag.

Answer: 3.75 cups (Option A)

Question 4Grade 7 • EE
A car arrived at its destination at 5:00 PM. The journey took 4 hours and 30 minutes. What time did the car start its journey?
A. 1:30 PM
B. 9:30 PM
C. 12:00 PM
D. 12:30 PM
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Arrival time = 5:00 PM, Journey duration = 4 hours 30 minutes
Identify the known values: the arrival time and the total duration of the journey.
2
Start time = Arrival time - Journey duration
To find the start time, subtract the journey duration from the arrival time. This is a working backward approach in time.
3
5:00 PM - 4 hours = 1:00 PM
First, subtract the full hours from the arrival time.
4
1:00 PM - 30 minutes = 12:30 PM
Then, subtract the remaining minutes. Remember to regroup hours to minutes if necessary (1 hour = 60 minutes).

Answer: 12:30 PM (Option D)

Question 5Grade 7 • EE
Emily completed 3/4 of her homework assignments. If she still has 5 assignments left to complete, how many total assignments were there originally?
A. 20 assignments
B. 3.75 assignments
C. 2 assignments
D. 5.75 assignments
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Fraction completed = 3/4, Assignments left = 5
Identify the given information: the fraction of assignments completed and the number of assignments remaining.
2
Fraction remaining = 1 - 3/4 = 1/4
If 3/4 of the assignments are complete, then the remaining portion is 1 minus 3/4.
3
1/4 of total assignments = 5 assignments
The fraction representing the remaining assignments corresponds to the number of assignments Emily has left.
4
Total assignments = 5 * 4 = 20
To find the total number of assignments, multiply the number of remaining assignments by the reciprocal of the fraction they represent (i.e., multiply by 4 because 5 is one-fourth of the total).

Answer: 20 assignments (Option A)

Question 6Grade 7 • EE
A scientist started with some water in a beaker. She added 250 milliliters of water. Then, she added a chemical that increased the total volume by 120 milliliters. If the final volume of the solution was 400 milliliters, how much water was in the beaker initially (before any additions)?
A. 770 milliliters
B. 30 milliliters
C. 150 milliliters
D. 530 milliliters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Final volume = 400 mL, Chemical added = 120 mL, Water added = 250 mL
Identify the known values: final volume, volume added by chemical, and volume of water added.
2
Volume before chemical = Final volume - Chemical added
Work backward from the final volume by subtracting the volume added by the chemical.
3
Volume before chemical = 400 mL - 120 mL = 280 mL
Perform the first subtraction.
4
Initial water = Volume before chemical - Water added
Now, subtract the amount of water she added to find the volume of water that was initially in the beaker.
5
Initial water = 280 mL - 250 mL = 30 mL
Perform the second subtraction to find the initial amount of water.

Answer: 30 milliliters (Option B)

Question 7Grade 7 • EE
A construction worker needs to cut a wooden plank into three pieces. The first piece is 1.75 meters long. The second piece is twice as long as the first. If the worker had 0.25 meters of plank left after cutting, what was the original length of the plank?
A. 5.25 meters
B. 4.0 meters
C. 3.75 meters
D. 5.5 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
First piece = 1.75 meters, Leftover = 0.25 meters
Identify the known lengths: the first piece and the amount left over.
2
Second piece = 1.75 meters * 2 = 3.5 meters
Calculate the length of the second piece, which is twice the length of the first piece.
3
Total cut = First piece + Second piece
To find the total length of the plank that was cut, add the lengths of the first and second pieces.
4
Total cut = 1.75 meters + 3.5 meters = 5.25 meters
Perform the addition to find the total length cut.
5
Original length = Total cut + Leftover
To find the original length of the plank, add the total length cut to the length that was left over.
6
Original length = 5.25 meters + 0.25 meters = 5.5 meters
Perform the final addition to get the original length of the plank.

Answer: 5.5 meters (Option D)

Question 8Grade 7 • EE
A train arrived at the station at 10:15 AM. It had departed from its previous stop 3 hours and 40 minutes earlier. Before that, it had made a 25-minute stop. What time did the train leave its first point of departure?
A. 6:10 AM
B. 2:45 PM
C. 7:25 AM
D. 6:35 AM
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Arrival time = 10:15 AM, Travel time = 3 hours 40 minutes, Stop duration = 25 minutes
Identify the known values: arrival time, travel duration, and stop duration.
2
Time departed previous stop = Arrival time - Travel time
To find the departure time from the previous stop, subtract the travel time from the arrival time.
3
Time departed previous stop = 10:15 AM - 3 hours 40 minutes = 6:35 AM
Perform the subtraction: 10 hours - 3 hours = 7 hours. 15 minutes - 40 minutes requires borrowing 1 hour (60 minutes) from 7 hours. So, 6 hours and (60 + 15 - 40) minutes = 6 hours and 35 minutes.
4
Time left first departure = Time departed previous stop - Stop duration
To find the time the train left its first point of departure, subtract the stop duration from the time it departed its previous stop.
5
Time left first departure = 6:35 AM - 25 minutes = 6:10 AM
Perform the final subtraction: 35 minutes - 25 minutes = 10 minutes. The hour remains 6.

Answer: 6:10 AM (Option A)

Question 9Grade 7 • EE
The population of a small town decreased by 150 people last year. This year, it increased by 20% to reach a total of 7200 people. What was the population of the town two years ago?
A. 5910 people
B. 5880 people
C. 8790 people
D. 6150 people
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Current population = 7200, This year's increase = 20%, Last year's decrease = 150
Identify the known values: current population, percentage increase this year, and population decrease last year.
2
Population last year (before increase) = Current population / (1 + percentage increase)
To find the population last year before the 20% increase, divide the current population by (1 + 0.20).
3
Population last year = 7200 / 1.20 = 6000
Perform the calculation for the population last year.
4
Population two years ago = Population last year + Last year's decrease
To find the population two years ago, add the 150 people who decreased from the population last year. This reverses the decrease.
5
Population two years ago = 6000 + 150 = 6150
Perform the final addition to find the population two years ago.

Answer: 6150 people (Option D)

Question 10Grade 7 • EE
A file was downloaded onto a computer. Its size doubled, then 15 MB of data was deleted, and finally, the file size was quartered to result in a final size of 20 MB. What was the original size of the file?
A. 36.25 MB
B. 47.5 MB
C. 35 MB
D. 80 MB
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Final size = 20 MB, Deleted data = 15 MB
Identify the known values: the final file size and the amount of data deleted.
2
Reverse quartering: File size before quartered = Final size * 4
Work backward from the last operation. If the file was quartered, multiply the final size by 4 to get its size before this step.
3
File size before quartered = 20 MB * 4 = 80 MB
Perform the multiplication.
4
Reverse deletion: File size before deleted = File size before quartered + Deleted data
Next, reverse the deletion. Add the deleted data to the size calculated in the previous step.
5
File size before deleted = 80 MB + 15 MB = 95 MB
Perform the addition.
6
Reverse doubling: Original size = File size before deleted / 2
Finally, reverse the doubling. Divide the current size by 2 to get the original file size.
7
Original size = 95 MB / 2 = 47.5 MB
Perform the division to find the original size of the file.

Answer: 47.5 MB (Option B)
