Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A grocery store sells 3 pounds of organic apples for $7.47. How much would 8 pounds of the same organic apples cost?
A. $2.49
B. $2.80
C. $19.92
D. $29.88
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
\(\frac{3 \text{ pounds}}{\\)7.47} = \frac{8 \text{ pounds}}{x}$
We set up the ratio of pounds to cost for the known quantity and equate it to the ratio for the unknown cost, where x represents the cost of 8 pounds.
2
$3 \times x = 8 \times 7.47$
Multiply the numerator of the first ratio by the denominator of the second, and the denominator of the first ratio by the numerator of the second.
3
$3x = 59.76$
Perform the multiplication $8 \times 7.47$ to simplify the equation.
4
\(x = \frac{59.76}{3}\)
To isolate x, divide the total cost by the number of pounds.
5
\(x = 19.92\)
The result of the division gives the cost for 8 pounds of apples.
6
8 pounds of apples would cost $19.92.
The calculated value represents the cost in dollars.

Answer: $19.92 (Option C)

\(x = \frac{540}{20}\)

The result of the division gives the expected number of made free throws.

She would expect to make 27 free throws.
The calculated value represents the number of free throws.

Question 10Grade 7 • RP
A 3D printer can print a miniature figure that is 12 cm tall in 1 hour and 45 minutes. How long (in hours and minutes) would it take to print a similar figure that is 28 cm tall, assuming the printing time is directly proportional to height?
A. 2 hours and 33 minutes
B. 4 hours and 5 minutes
C. 3 hours and 30 minutes
D. 2 hours and 45 minutes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$1 \text{ hour and } 45 \text{ minutes} = (1 \times 60) + 45 = 60 + 45 = 105 \text{ minutes}$
Since the time needs to be directly proportional to height, it's easier to work with a single unit of time (minutes) for calculations.
2
\(\frac{12 \text{ cm}}{105 \text{ minutes}} = \frac{28 \text{ cm}}{x \text{ minutes}}\)
We set up the ratio of height to time for the known figure and equate it to the ratio for the new figure, where x represents the unknown printing time in minutes.
3
$12 \times x = 28 \times 105$
Multiply the numerator of the first ratio by the denominator of the second, and the denominator of the first ratio by the numerator of the second.
4
$12x = 2940$
Perform the multiplication $28 \times 105$ to simplify the equation.
5
\(x = \frac{2940}{12}\)
To isolate x, divide the total product by 12.
6
\(x = 245 \text{ minutes}\)
The result of the division gives the total time in minutes.
7
$245 \div 60 = 4 \text{ with a remainder of } 5$. So, 4 hours and 5 minutes.
Divide the total minutes by 60 to find the number of full hours, and the remainder will be the remaining minutes.
8
It would take 4 hours and 5 minutes.
The calculated value represents the time in hours and minutes.

Answer: 4 hours and 5 minutes (Option B)
