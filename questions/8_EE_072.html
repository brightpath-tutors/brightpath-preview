Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Sarah bought a new book for $15.75. After buying the book, she had $24.25 left in her wallet. How much money did Sarah have before buying the book?
A. $400.00
B. $40.00
C. $39.00
D. $8.50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the amount of money Sarah had before buying the book. x - 15.75 = 24.25
The initial amount 'x' minus the cost of the book is equal to the money left.
2
x = 24.25 + 15.75
To find the original amount, add the money spent to the money remaining.
3
x = 40.00
Sarah had $40.00 before buying the book.

Answer: $40.00 (Option B)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A marathon runner completed a race in 3 hours and 28 minutes. If their training goal was to finish 35 minutes faster, what was their target completion time? (Express your answer in hours and minutes.)
A. 3 hours 7 minutes
B. 2 hours 3 minutes
C. 3 hours 63 minutes
D. 2 hours 53 minutes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
3 hours * 60 minutes/hour + 28 minutes = 180 + 28 = 208 minutes.
Convert hours to minutes to work with a single unit of time.
2
Let 'x' be the target time in minutes. x + 35 = 208
The target time plus the 35 minutes faster goal equals the actual completion time.
3
x = 208 - 35
Subtract the 35 minutes faster goal from the actual time to find the target time.
4
x = 173 minutes.
The target time was 173 minutes.
5
173 / 60 = 2 with a remainder of 53. So, 2 hours and 53 minutes.
Divide by 60 to find hours, and the remainder is the minutes.

Answer: 2 hours 53 minutes (Option D)
