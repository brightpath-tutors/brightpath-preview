Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A grocery store sells 6 pounds of potatoes for $4.32. What is the unit price of the potatoes in dollars per pound?
A. $0.072 per pound
B. $0.72 per pound
C. $25.92 per pound
D. $1.39 per pound
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total cost = $4.32, Total pounds = 6 pounds.
The problem asks for the unit price, which is the cost per single unit of weight.
2
Unit price = Total cost / Total pounds = $4.32 / 6 pounds.
To find the cost per pound, divide the total cost by the number of pounds.
3
$4.32 ÷ 6 = $0.72.
Dividing 4.32 by 6 yields 0.72.
4
$0.72 per pound.
The unit price of the potatoes is $0.72 per pound.

Answer: $0.72 per pound (Option B)

0.11 miles per minute.
The runner's average speed is 0.11 miles per minute.

Question 4Grade 7 • RP

A. 0.033 miles per gallon
B. 300 miles per gallon
C. 2764.8 miles per gallon
D. 30 miles per gallon
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Distance = 288 miles, Fuel used = 9.6 gallons.
Fuel efficiency is measured in miles per gallon, which is a unit rate.
2
Fuel efficiency = Distance / Fuel used = 288 miles / 9.6 gallons.
To find miles per gallon, divide the total miles by the total gallons used.
3
Multiply both the dividend and divisor by 10: 288 × 10 = 2880, 9.6 × 10 = 96. So, 2880 ÷ 96.
This makes the division easier to perform without changing the value of the quotient.
4
2880 ÷ 96 = 30.
Dividing 2880 by 96 results in 30.
5
30 miles per gallon.
The car's fuel efficiency is 30 miles per gallon.

Answer: 30 miles per gallon (Option D)

Question 8Grade 7 • RP
A specialized clock gains 18 seconds every 3 hours. How many minutes does the clock gain per day?
A. 432 minutes per day
B. 14.4 minutes per day
C. 1.2 minutes per day
D. 2.4 minutes per day
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Rate = 18 seconds / 3 hours = 6 seconds per hour.
First, find how many seconds the clock gains in a single hour.
2
There are 24 hours in a day. So, 6 seconds/hour × 24 hours/day = 144 seconds per day.
Multiply the hourly gain by 24 to get the total gain per day in seconds.
3
There are 60 seconds in a minute. So, 144 seconds/day / 60 seconds/minute = 2.4 minutes per day.
Divide the total seconds gained per day by 60 to convert it to minutes.
4
2.4 minutes per day.
The clock gains 2.4 minutes per day.

Answer: 2.4 minutes per day (Option D)

Question 10Grade 7 • RP
A 3.6 GB video file takes 2.5 minutes to download. What is the download speed in megabytes per second (MB/s)? (Note: 1 GB = 1024 MB)

B. 1.44 MB/s
C. 0.024576 MB/s
D. 24.576 MB/s
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
3.6 GB × 1024 MB/GB = 3686.4 MB.
Since the desired unit rate is MB/s, first convert GB to MB.
2
2.5 minutes × 60 seconds/minute = 150 seconds.
Since the desired unit rate is MB/s, convert minutes to seconds.
3
Download speed = Total MB / Total seconds = 3686.4 MB / 150 seconds.
Divide the total megabytes by the total seconds to find the speed.
4
3686.4 ÷ 150 = 24.576.
Dividing 3686.4 by 150 results in 24.576.
5
24.576 MB/s.
The download speed is 24.576 megabytes per second.

Answer: 24.576 MB/s (Option D)
