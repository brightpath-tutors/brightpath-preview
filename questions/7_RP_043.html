Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A small toy car is a miniature replica of a real car. The toy car is 10 cm long and 4 cm wide. If the real car is 400 cm long, what is the width of the real car, assuming the toy and real car are similar figures?
A. 160 cm
B. 1600 cm
C. 40 cm
D. 100 cm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let L_toy = 10 cm, W_toy = 4 cm, L_real = 400 cm. Let W_real be the unknown width of the real car. Since the figures are similar, the ratio of corresponding sides is equal: L_toy / L_real = W_toy / W_real.
We set up a proportion comparing the ratio of length to width for both the toy car and the real car. It's crucial to match corresponding sides (length with length, width with width).
2
10 / 400 = 4 / W_real
We plug in the known dimensions into our established proportion.
3
10 W_real = 400 4 10 * W_real = 1600 W_real = 1600 / 10 W_real = 160
We use cross-multiplication to solve for the unknown width. Multiply the numerator of one fraction by the denominator of the other, then divide to isolate W_real.
4
The width of the real car is 160 cm.
Always include appropriate units in the final answer.

Answer: 160 cm (Option A)
