Question 1Grade 7 • NS
Sarah bought a shirt for $15.75 and a pair of socks for $4.25. She paid with a $25.00 bill. Write an expression to represent the change Sarah received.
A. 25.00 + 15.75 + 4.25
B. 25.00 - (15.75 + 4.25)
C. 25.00 - 15.75
D. (15.75 + 4.25) - 25.00
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Shirt cost = $15.75, Socks cost = $4.25, Amount paid = $25.00
Identify the given values from the problem statement: the cost of each item and the total amount paid.
2
Total expenses = $15.75 + $4.25
To calculate the change, first sum up the costs of all the items Sarah bought to find her total expenses.
3
Change = $25.00 - (Total expenses)
Subtract the total expenses from the amount Sarah paid to determine the change she received.

Answer: 25.00 - (15.75 + 4.25) (Option B)

Total cut = 2.25 + 3.75

Question 9Grade 7 • NS
A town's population was 12,500. It decreased by 1/10 in the first year and then increased by 0.05 of the new population in the second year. Write an expression to represent the population after two years.
A. (12500 (1 - 1/10)) (1 + 0.05)
B. 12500 * (1 - 1/10 + 0.05)
C. 12500 1/10 0.05
D. 12500 - (12500 1/10) + (12500 0.05)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial population = 12,500, Decrease factor = 1/10, Increase factor = 0.05
Identify the initial population of the town and the fractional/decimal factors for population change in each year.
2
Population after decrease = Initial population * (1 - Decrease factor)
To calculate a population decrease by a fraction, multiply the original population by (1 minus the fractional decrease).
3
Population after increase = Population after decrease * (1 + Increase factor)
To calculate a population increase by a decimal, multiply the new population (after the first year's change) by (1 plus the decimal increase).
4
Combined expression = (Initial population (1 - Decrease factor)) (1 + Increase factor)
Combine the expressions from steps 2 and 3 into a single, comprehensive expression to represent the population after two years, ensuring sequential application of changes.
5
Final expression: (12500 (1 - 1/10)) (1 + 0.05)
Substitute the specific numerical values into the combined expression.

Answer: (12500 (1 - 1/10)) (1 + 0.05) (Option A)

4
Total data processed = Rate * Total run time

5
Expression: (1.5 / (2/5)) * 3.25
