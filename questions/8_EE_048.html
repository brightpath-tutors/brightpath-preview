Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A new online store is running a promotion where the discount triples every day. If the discount starts at $2 on day 1, what will be the discount on day 4? (Express your answer using exponents, then calculate the value.)
A. $54
B. $24
C. $6
D. $128
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The discount triples each day, so the base is 3. For day 4, starting from day 1, the discount factor 3 is applied 3 times (Day 4 - Day 1 = 3 increments). The initial discount is $2.
The problem describes a multiplicative growth, where the discount is multiplied by 3 for each passing day after the first. From day 1 to day 4, there are 3 such multiplications.
2
Discount = Initial discount (growth factor)^(number of increments) = 2 3^3.
The initial discount is multiplied by the growth factor (3) raised to the power of the number of times it has tripled. For day 4, starting from day 1, it has tripled 3 times.
3
3^3 = 3 3 3 = 27.
To evaluate 3^3, we multiply the base (3) by itself three times.
4
2 * 27 = 54.
Multiply the initial discount by the calculated exponential value to get the final discount amount.

Answer: $54 (Option A)

C. 24 players
D. 6 players
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total players = 2^5 = 2 2 2 2 2 = 32.
To evaluate 2^5, we multiply the base (2) by itself five times.
2
Eliminated players = 2^3 = 2 2 2 = 8.
To evaluate 2^3, we multiply the base (2) by itself three times.
3
Remaining players = 32 - 8 = 24.
The number of remaining players is found by subtracting the number of eliminated players from the initial total number of players.

Answer: 24 players (Option C)

C. 5^6 miles
D. 25^6 miles
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Distance = Speed * Time.
To find the total distance covered, we multiply the speed by the time traveled.
2
Distance = 5^4 miles/hour 5^2 hours = 5^4 5^2.
We are given the speed as 5^4 and the time as 5^2.
3

4
4 + 2 = 6. Final answer: 5^6 miles.
The sum of the exponents gives the new exponent for the base 5.

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A rare plant's growth is modeled by the expression (2^3 * 2^4) / (2^2)^2 over a period of weeks. Simplify this expression to a single exponential term.
A. 2^3
B. 2^8
C. 2^11
D. 2^1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Numerator: 2^3 * 2^4 = 2^(3+4) = 2^7.
When multiplying terms with the same base, add their exponents.
2
Denominator: (2^2)^2 = 2^(2*2) = 2^4.
When raising a power to another power, multiply the exponents.
3
The expression becomes 2^7 / 2^4.
This simplifies the expression before applying the final rule.
4
When dividing terms with the same base, subtract the exponents: a^m / a^n = a^(m-n). So, 2^7 / 2^4 = 2^(7-4).
The quotient rule allows us to combine the terms into a single exponential term.
5
7 - 4 = 3. Final simplified expression: 2^3.
The subtraction of the exponents gives the final exponent.

Answer: 2^3 (Option A)

Step-by-step solution
1
Combined storage capacity = (x^5 y^-2) (x^-2 * y^3).
The problem states that the combined capacity is the product of the two given expressions.
2
(x^5 x^-2) (y^-2 * y^3).
Rearrange the terms to easily apply exponent rules to each base separately.
3
For base x: x^5 * x^-2 = x^(5 + (-2)) = x^(5-2) = x^3.
When multiplying terms with the same base, add their exponents.
4
For base y: y^-2 * y^3 = y^(-2 + 3) = y^1.
When multiplying terms with the same base, add their exponents.
5

Put the simplified x and y terms together. All exponents (3 and 1) are positive, so no further action is needed.
