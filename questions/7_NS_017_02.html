A. $6.10
B. $4.70
C. $5.25
D. $3.90
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost of notebooks = 3 * $1.75 = $5.25
First, calculate the total cost of the notebooks by multiplying the number of notebooks by the price per notebook.
2
Total cost of items = $5.25 + $0.85 = $6.10
Next, add the cost of the notebooks and the pen to find the total amount Sarah spent.
3
Change received = $10.00 - $6.10 = $3.90

D. 191.75 miles
E. None of the above

2
Speed in second part = 60.5 - 10 = 50.5 miles per hour
Next, calculate the new speed for the second part of the journey by subtracting the reduction from the initial speed.

Distance in second part = 50.5 * 1.5 = 75.75 miles

Remaining solution = 13/4 - 13/12 = 39/12 - 13/12 = 26/12 = 13/6 liters

Question 7Grade 7 • NS
A construction worker needs to cut a metal rod that is 15 1/2 feet long into pieces that are 2 1/4 feet each. How many full pieces can be cut, and what length of rod will be left over? (Express the leftover length as a fraction in simplest form.)
A. 6 full pieces with 2 feet left over
B. 7 full pieces with 1/2 foot left over
C. 6 full pieces with 1/2 foot left over
D. 10 full pieces with 0 feet left over
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions: 15 1/2 = 31/2, 2 1/4 = 9/4
Convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions to facilitate division.
2
Divide total length by piece length: 31/2 ÷ 9/4 = 31/2 * 4/9 = 124/18 = 62/9
Divide the total length of the rod by the length of each piece to find out how many pieces can be cut. Simplify the resulting fraction.
3
Number of full pieces = 6 (from 62/9 = 6 and 8/9)
The whole number part of the mixed number (6) represents the number of full pieces that can be cut.
4
Length of 6 pieces = 6 2 1/4 = 6 9/4 = 54/4 = 27/2 = 13 1/2 feet
Calculate the total length used for the 6 full pieces.
5
Length left over = 15 1/2 - 13 1/2 = 2 feet
Subtract the total length of the cut pieces from the original rod length to find the leftover length.

Answer: 6 full pieces with 2 feet left over (Option A)

Question 8Grade 7 • NS

E. None of the above

Convert all fractional and mixed number durations to decimals for consistent addition.
2
Total duration in hours = 3.75 + 0.5 + 2.3 = 6.55 hours
Add all the decimal durations (travel time + stop time + second travel time) to get the total time in hours.
3
Convert 0.55 hours to minutes = 0.55 * 60 = 33 minutes
Convert the decimal part of the total hours into minutes by multiplying by 60.
4
Total duration = 6 hours and 33 minutes
Combine the whole hours and the calculated minutes for the total duration.
5
Add 6 hours to departure time: 8:15 AM + 6 hours = 2:15 PM
Add the whole hours to the departure time, adjusting for AM/PM transition.
6
Add 33 minutes: 2:15 PM + 33 minutes = 2:48 PM
Finally, add the remaining minutes to the adjusted time to find the arrival time.

Question 10Grade 7 • NS
A data server has a total storage capacity of 5 1/4 terabytes. It is currently 2/3 full. If a user uploads a file that is 0.75 terabytes and then deletes another file that is 1/8 terabytes, how much storage space is left on the server? (Express your answer as a mixed number in simplest form.)
A. 1 1/8 terabytes
B. 3 1/2 terabytes
C. 1 3/8 terabytes
D. 2 1/4 terabytes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Convert all values to improper fractions: 5 1/4 = 21/4, 0.75 = 3/4
Convert all mixed numbers and decimals to improper fractions for consistent calculations.
2
Current used space = 21/4 * 2/3 = 42/12 = 7/2 terabytes
Calculate the amount of space currently used by multiplying the total capacity by the fraction it is full.
3
Space after upload = 7/2 + 3/4 = 14/4 + 3/4 = 17/4 terabytes
Add the uploaded file's size to the currently used space, finding a common denominator.
4
Space after deletion = 17/4 - 1/8 = 34/8 - 1/8 = 33/8 terabytes
Subtract the deleted file's size from the updated used space, finding a common denominator.
5
Total capacity in improper fraction = 21/4 = 42/8 terabytes
Express the total server capacity with a common denominator for the final subtraction.
6
Remaining free space = 42/8 - 33/8 = 9/8 terabytes
Subtract the final used space from the total capacity to find the remaining free space.
7
Convert to mixed number = 9/8 = 1 1/8 terabytes
Convert the improper fraction to a mixed number for the final answer.

Answer: 1 1/8 terabytes (Option A)
