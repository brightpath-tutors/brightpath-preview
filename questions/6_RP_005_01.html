Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A grocery store sells 6 oranges for $2.52. What is the unit rate for the price per orange, in simplest form?
A. 2.38 oranges per dollar
B. $4.20 per orange
C. $2.52
D. $0.42 per orange
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total cost = $2.52, Number of oranges = 6
Identify the total cost and the number of items.
2
Price per orange = Total cost / Number of oranges
Set up the ratio as a fraction to find the price per orange.
3
$2.52 / 6 = 0.42
Perform the division to calculate the unit rate.
4
Unit rate = $0.42 per orange
State the final unit rate with appropriate units.

Answer: $0.42 per orange (Option D)

C. 1 liter per day
D. 300 liters per day
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Decrease = 2,400 liters, Time = 8 months
Identify the total decrease in capacity and the total time duration.
2
8 months * 30 days/month = 240 days
Convert the total time from months to days, as the unit rate is requested per day (using the given assumption of 30 days per month).
3
Liters per day = Total decrease / Total time in days
Set up the ratio as a fraction to find liters per day.
4
2,400 / 240 = 10
Perform the division to calculate the unit rate.
5
Unit rate = 10 liters per day
State the final unit rate with appropriate units.

Answer: 10 liters per day (Option A)
