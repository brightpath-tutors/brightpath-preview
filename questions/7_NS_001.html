Question 1Grade 7 • NS
Sarah has $12. She wants to buy small gifts that each cost \(\frac{1}{3}\) of a dollar. How many gifts can she buy?
A. 4 gifts
B. 6 gifts
C. 12 gifts
D. 36 gifts
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The problem asks how many \(\frac{1}{3}\) dollar gifts fit into $12. This is a division problem: $12 \div \frac{1}{3}$.
To find out how many times a smaller amount (the cost of one gift) fits into a larger amount (total money), we use division.
2
$12 = \frac{12}{1}$
To divide a whole number by a fraction, it's helpful to express the whole number as a fraction with a denominator of 1.
3
The divisor is \(\frac{1}{3}\). The reciprocal of \(\frac{1}{3}\) is \(\frac{3}{1}\).
To divide by a fraction, we multiply by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of a fraction is found by flipping the numerator and the denominator.
4
\(\frac{12}{1} \times \frac{3}{1}\)
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal.
5
\(\frac{12 \times 3}{1 \times 1} = \frac{36}{1} = 36\)
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together. Simplify the resulting fraction.
6
