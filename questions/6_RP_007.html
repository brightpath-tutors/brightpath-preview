Rates and Unit Rates

Skill ID: 6.RP.007

Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A baker uses 18 cups of sugar for 3 cakes. How many cups of sugar does she use per cake?
A. 54 cups/cake
B. 6 cups/cake
C. 9 cups/cake
D. 3 cups/cake
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Quantity 1: 18 cups of sugar Quantity 2: 3 cakes Units: cups and cakes
We need to find the amount of sugar per cake, which is a unit rate.
2
\frac{18 \text{ cups}}{3 \text{ cakes}
To find the unit rate (cups per cake), we divide the total cups by the total number of cakes.
3
18 \div 3 = 6
Performing the division gives us the number of cups for one cake.
4
6 \text{ cups/cake
The unit rate is 6 cups of sugar per cake.

Answer: 6 cups/cake (Option B)
