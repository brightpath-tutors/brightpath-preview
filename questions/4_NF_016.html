Question 1Grade 4 • NF

A. \(\frac{60}{100}\)
B. \(\frac{16}{100}\)
C. \(\frac{10}{100}\)
D. \(\frac{6}{100}\)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Fraction given: \(\frac{6}{10}\)
Identify the initial fraction representing the amount spent.
2
$10 \times 10 = 100$
Determine the factor needed to change the denominator from 10 to 100.
3
Numerator: $6 \times 10 = 60$. Denominator: $10 \times 10 = 100$. Equivalent fraction: \(\frac{60}{100}\)
Multiply both the numerator and the denominator by the identified factor (10) to find the equivalent fraction.

Answer: \(\frac{60}{100}\) (Option A)

C. \(\frac{17}{100}\)

2
