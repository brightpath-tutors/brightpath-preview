Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A store offers a discount of $1.50 off each item when you buy 3 items. If each item originally costs $x, write an expression for the total cost of the 3 items.
A. 3x - 1.50
B. 3x + 4.50
C. 3x - 4.50
D. x - 4.50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost per item with discount = x - 1.50
First, determine the cost of a single item after the discount is applied. The discount of $1.50 is taken off the original price 'x'.
2
Total cost = 3 * (x - 1.50)
Since 3 items are purchased, multiply the discounted cost per item by the number of items.
3
3 x - 3 1.50 = 3x - 4.50
Apply the distributive property by multiplying 3 by each term inside the parentheses.

Answer: 3x - 4.50 (Option C)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE

B. 3s - 10
C. 3s - 5
D. 2.5s + 0.5(10 - s)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Distance for first part = 2.5s
Calculate the distance covered in the first part of the trip by multiplying speed 's' by time 2.5 hours.
2
Speed for additional time = s - 10
Determine the car's speed during the additional 0.5 hours, which is 10 mph less than 's'.
3
Distance for additional time = 0.5 * (s - 10)
Calculate the distance covered in the additional 0.5 hours by multiplying the new speed by 0.5.
4
Total distance = 2.5s + 0.5(s - 10)
Add the distances from both parts of the trip to find the total distance.
5
Total distance = 2.5s + 0.5s - 0.5 * 10
Apply the distributive property to the second term: 0.5 s - 0.5 10.
6
Total distance = 2.5s + 0.5s - 5
Perform the multiplication: 0.5 * 10 = 5.
7
Total distance = 3s - 5

A. 2.25d + 0.5
B. 2.25d - 0.5
C. d(2.25) + 2(0.25)
D. d(2.25) + 2.5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Regular daily training hours = 1.5 + 0.75 = 2.25 hours
Calculate the total regular training hours for a day (morning + evening).
2
Number of regular training days = d - 2
Determine the number of days the swimmer trained for the regular amount by subtracting the 2 modified days from 'd'.
3
Morning training on modified days = 1.5 - 0.25 = 1.25 hours
Calculate the morning training hours for each of the 2 modified days.
4
Evening training on modified days = 0.75 + 0.5 = 1.25 hours
Calculate the evening training hours for each of the 2 modified days.
5
Total training on one modified day = 1.25 + 1.25 = 2.5 hours
Sum the modified morning and evening training hours for a single modified day.
6
Total training hours = (d - 2) 2.25 + 2 2.5

Step-by-step solution
1
Annual increase from growth = 1/10 P
Identify the annual population increase due to a factor of 1/10 of the initial population 'P'.
2
Annual increase from new residents = 200
Identify the number of additional people moving in each year.
3
Total annual increase = (1/10 P + 200)
Sum the two sources of annual increase for the population.
4
Total increase over 5 years = 5 * (1/10 P + 200)
Multiply the total annual increase by 5 for the 5-year period.
5
Total increase over 5 years = 5/10 P + 5 * 200 = 1/2 P + 1000 = 0.5P + 1000
Apply the distributive property and simplify the fraction. Calculate 5 * 200 = 1000.
6
Population leaving = 1/20 P = 0.05P
Identify the portion of the initial population 'P' that leaves due to migration.
7
Final population = P + (0.5P + 1000) - 0.05P
Start with the initial population 'P', add the total increase over 5 years, and subtract the population that left.
8
Final population = 1P + 0.5P - 0.05P + 1000 = 1.45P + 1000

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A software company sells 'n' licenses for its program. Each license costs $49.99, but a special promotion offers a $5.50 discount per license if you buy 10 or more. Additionally, for every 5 licenses sold, the company spends $12.50 on server maintenance. Write an expression for the total revenue minus maintenance costs if 'n' is greater than or equal to 10.
A. n(49.99 - 5.50) - n * 12.50
B. 44.49n - 2.5n
C. 49.99n - 5.50n - 12.50n
D. 44.49n - (n/5) + 12.50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Discounted price per license = 49.99 - 5.50
Calculate the price of each license after applying the discount since 'n' is 10 or more.
2
Discounted price per license = 44.49
Perform the subtraction to find the exact discounted price.
3
Total revenue = n * 44.49
Multiply the discounted price per license by the number of licenses 'n' to find the total revenue.
4
Number of maintenance units = n / 5
Determine how many times the company incurs maintenance costs, based on selling 'n' licenses in groups of 5.
5
Total maintenance cost = (n / 5) * 12.50
Multiply the number of maintenance units by the cost per unit ($12.50).
6
Total maintenance cost = 2.5n
Simplify the maintenance cost expression: 12.50 / 5 = 2.5.
7
Total revenue minus maintenance costs = 44.49n - 2.5n
Subtract the total maintenance cost from the total revenue to find the final expression.
