Question 1Grade 6 • NS
Sarah started her day with $10 in her wallet. She then spent $3 on a snack. What is the total amount of money Sarah has now?
A. $-13
B. $-7
C. $13
D. $7
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Sarah started with $10, which can be represented as +10.
Positive integers represent money gained or available.
2
She spent $3, which means her money decreased by $3. This can be represented as -3.
Negative integers represent money spent or lost.
3
To find the total amount, add the initial amount and the change: 10 + (-3).
Adding a negative number is equivalent to subtracting its absolute value.
4
When adding integers with different signs, subtract their absolute values: |10| - |-3| = 10 - 3 = 7. Keep the sign of the number with the larger absolute value. The larger absolute value is 10 (positive), so the result is positive.
The absolute value of 10 is greater than the absolute value of -3, so the sum retains the positive sign.
5
Sarah has $7 now.
The total amount of money Sarah has is $7.

Answer: $7 (Option D)
