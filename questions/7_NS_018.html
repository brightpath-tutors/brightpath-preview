Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A baker used 10.5 kilograms of flour to make a large batch of cookies. If each cookie requires 0.75 kilograms of flour, how many cookies can he make?
A. 0.07 cookies
B. 7.875 cookies
C. 14 cookies
D. 1.4 cookies
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total flour = 10.5 kg Flour per cookie = 0.75 kg Number of cookies = Total flour / Flour per cookie Number of cookies = 10.5 / 0.75
To find out how many cookies can be made, divide the total amount of flour by the amount of flour needed per cookie.
2
10.5 / 0.75 Multiply both by 100 to remove decimals: (10.5 100) / (0.75 100) = 1050 / 75
To make the division easier and ensure accuracy with decimals, shift the decimal point in both numbers until the divisor (0.75) is a whole number. Since 0.75 has two decimal places, multiply by 100.
3
1050 / 75 = 14
Now divide 1050 by 75. You can think of it as 105 divided by 75 is 1 with a remainder, then bring down the 0 to get 300, and 300 divided by 75 is 4. So, 14.

Answer: 14 cookies (Option C)

1
Total distance = 27 miles Course length = 4.5 miles Number of times = Total distance / Course length Number of times = 27 / 4.5
To find out how many times the runner completed the course, divide the total distance covered by the length of one course.

27 / 4.5 Multiply both by 10 to remove decimals: (27 10) / (4.5 10) = 270 / 45

Question 3Grade 7 • NS
A train traveled a distance of 150 kilometers at a constant speed for 3.75 hours. What was the average speed of the train in kilometers per hour?
A. 40 km/h
B. 562.5 km/h
C. 4 km/h
D. 0.025 km/h
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total distance = 150 km Total time = 3.75 hours Average speed = Total distance / Total time Average speed = 150 / 3.75
To find the average speed, divide the total distance traveled by the total time taken.
2
150 / 3.75 Multiply both by 100 to remove decimals: (150 100) / (3.75 100) = 15000 / 375
Shift the decimal point in both numbers until the divisor (3.75) is a whole number. Since 3.75 has two decimal places, multiply by 100.
3
15000 / 375 = 40
Divide 15000 by 375. You can simplify by dividing both by 25 first (15000/25 = 600, 375/25 = 15), then 600/15 = 40. Or perform long division directly.

Answer: 40 km/h (Option A)

Question 5Grade 7 • NS
A scientist has 6 3/8 liters of a chemical solution. If she pours it into test tubes that each hold 1/2 liter, how many test tubes can she fill completely?
A. 12 3/4 test tubes
B. 3 3/16 test tubes
C. 51/16 test tubes
D. 6 3/8 test tubes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total solution = 6 3/8 liters Solution per test tube = 1/2 liter Number of test tubes = Total solution / Solution per test tube Number of test tubes = 6 3/8 / 1/2
To find out how many test tubes can be filled, divide the total volume of the solution by the volume each test tube can hold.
2
6 3/8 = (6 * 8 + 3) / 8 = (48 + 3) / 8 = 51/8
Before dividing fractions, convert any mixed numbers into improper fractions. Multiply the whole number by the denominator and add the numerator, then place this sum over the original denominator.
3
51/8 / 1/2
Replace the mixed number with its improper fraction equivalent.
4
51/8 * 2/1
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal (flip the numerator and denominator of the divisor).
5
(51 2) / (8 1) = 102 / 8 Simplify by dividing both numerator and denominator by 2: 102 / 2 = 51 8 / 2 = 4 Result = 51/4 Convert to mixed number: 51 / 4 = 12 with a remainder of 3, so 12 3/4
Multiply the numerators and the denominators. Then, simplify the resulting fraction and convert it back to a mixed number if necessary.

Answer: 12 3/4 test tubes (Option A)

D. 1/22 pieces
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students

1

2
11/12 * 24/1
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal (flip the numerator and denominator of the divisor).
