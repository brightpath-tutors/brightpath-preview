Question 1Grade 7 • EE
Sarah bought a jacket on sale for $17 less than its original price. If the sale price was $45, what was the original price of the jacket?
A. $62
B. $28
C. $2.65
D. $765
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the original price of the jacket. The sale price is $17 less than the original price, so we can write the equation: x - 17 = 45.
We are looking for the original price, which is decreased by $17 to get the sale price.
2
To find 'x', we need to undo the subtraction. The inverse operation of subtracting 17 is adding 17. So, we add 17 to both sides of the equation.
Applying the inverse operation maintains the equality of the equation.
3
x - 17 + 17 = 45 + 17 x = 62
Adding 17 to both sides gives the original price of $62.

Answer: $62 (Option A)

Question 2Grade 7 • EE
A basketball team scored 78 points in the first three quarters. If their total score for the game was 91 points, how many points did they score in the last quarter?
A. 13 points
B. 169 points
C. 1.16 points
D. 3 points
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'p' be the points scored in the last quarter. The points from the first three quarters plus the points from the last quarter equal the total score: 78 + p = 91.
We are adding the unknown points to a known amount to reach a total.
2
To find 'p', we need to undo the addition. The inverse operation of adding 78 is subtracting 78. So, we subtract 78 from both sides of the equation.
Applying the inverse operation maintains the equality of the equation.
3
78 + p - 78 = 91 - 78 p = 13
Subtracting 78 from both sides gives 13 points for the last quarter.

Answer: 13 points (Option A)

Question 3Grade 7 • EE
A recipe calls for 3 cups of sugar. If you want to make 4 times the recipe, how many cups of sugar will you need?
A. 1 cup
B. 4 cups
C. 7 cups
D. 12 cups
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 's' be the number of cups of sugar needed. We need 4 times the original amount, so we can write the equation: s = 3 * 4. Alternatively, using an unknown for the original amount and a multiplier: s / 4 = 3.
The problem asks for a quantity that is a multiple of another quantity.
2
For s = 3 * 4, no isolation is needed. For s / 4 = 3, to find 's', we need to undo the division. The inverse operation of dividing by 4 is multiplying by 4. So, we multiply both sides of the equation by 4.
Applying the inverse operation maintains the equality of the equation.
3
s = 3 * 4 s = 12
Multiplying 3 by 4 gives 12 cups of sugar.

Answer: 12 cups (Option D)

Question 4Grade 7 • EE
A cyclist completed a 54-mile race. If he maintained an average speed of 18 miles per hour, how many hours did it take him to finish the race?
A. 0.33 hours (in simplest form)
B. 3 hours
C. 972 hours
D. 72 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'h' be the number of hours. We know that Distance = Speed × Time. So, 54 = 18 × h.
The problem relates distance, speed, and time with a multiplication equation.
2
To find 'h', we need to undo the multiplication. The inverse operation of multiplying by 18 is dividing by 18. So, we divide both sides of the equation by 18.
Applying the inverse operation maintains the equality of the equation.
3
54 / 18 = (18 × h) / 18 3 = h
Dividing 54 by 18 gives 3 hours.

Answer: 3 hours (Option B)

Question 5Grade 7 • EE
There were 87 students in the school library. After some students left to go to class, 63 students remained. How many students left the library?
A. 150 students
B. 24 students
C. 5541 students
D. 1.38 students
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 's' be the number of students who left. The initial number of students minus the students who left equals the remaining students: 87 - s = 63.
This is a 'take away' scenario, representing subtraction.
2
To find 's', we can subtract 87 from both sides, which gives -s = 63 - 87, so -s = -24, which means s = 24. Alternatively, think of it as 87 - 63 = s.
To isolate 's', we perform inverse operations. If we move 's' to one side, we subtract 63 from 87.
3
87 - 63 = s s = 24
Subtracting 63 from 87 gives 24 students.

Answer: 24 students (Option B)

B. 20.7 degrees Celsius
C. 324.3 degrees Celsius
D. 16.7 degrees Celsius
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 't' be the initial temperature. The initial temperature plus the increase equals the final temperature: t + 11.5 = 28.2.
This is an 'increase' scenario, representing addition.
2
To find 't', we need to undo the addition. The inverse operation of adding 11.5 is subtracting 11.5. So, we subtract 11.5 from both sides of the equation.
Applying the inverse operation maintains the equality of the equation.
3
t + 11.5 - 11.5 = 28.2 - 11.5 t = 16.7
Subtracting 11.5 from 28.2 gives 16.7 degrees Celsius.

Question 7Grade 7 • EE
A construction worker needs to cut several pieces of wood, each 2.5 meters long. If the total length of wood needed is 30 meters, how many pieces of wood can be cut?
A. 75 pieces
B. 27.5 pieces
C. 32.5 pieces
D. 12 pieces
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'p' be the number of pieces of wood. The length of one piece multiplied by the number of pieces equals the total length: 2.5 × p = 30.
This is a multiplication scenario where we know the product and one factor, and need to find the other factor.
2
To find 'p', we need to undo the multiplication. The inverse operation of multiplying by 2.5 is dividing by 2.5. So, we divide both sides of the equation by 2.5.
Applying the inverse operation maintains the equality of the equation.
3
(2.5 × p) / 2.5 = 30 / 2.5 p = 12
Dividing 30 by 2.5 gives 12 pieces of wood.

Answer: 12 pieces (Option D)

Question 8Grade 7 • EE
A digital clock showed 10:48. After a certain amount of time passed, the clock showed 11:32. How many minutes passed?
A. 84 minutes
B. 16 minutes
C. 44 minutes
D. 1 hour and 16 minutes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
First, convert both times to minutes past the hour (or a common reference point like 10:00). 10:48 is 48 minutes past 10:00. 11:32 is 60 minutes (for the hour) + 32 minutes = 92 minutes past 10:00. Let 'x' be the number of minutes passed. So, 48 + x = 92.
Convert times to a consistent unit (minutes) to form a standard addition equation.
2
To find 'x', we need to undo the addition. The inverse operation of adding 48 is subtracting 48. So, we subtract 48 from both sides of the equation.
Applying the inverse operation maintains the equality of the equation.
3
48 + x - 48 = 92 - 48 x = 44
Subtracting 48 from 92 gives 44 minutes.

Answer: 44 minutes (Option C)

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'm' be the number of computer mice. The cost per mouse multiplied by the number of mice equals the total cost: 15.90 × m = 238.50.
This is a multiplication scenario where we know the product and one factor, and need to find the other factor.
2
To find 'm', we need to undo the multiplication. The inverse operation of multiplying by 15.90 is dividing by 15.90. So, we divide both sides of the equation by 15.90.
Applying the inverse operation maintains the equality of the equation.
3
(15.90 × m) / 15.90 = 238.50 / 15.90 m = 15
Dividing 238.50 by 15.90 gives 15 mice.
