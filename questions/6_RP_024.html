Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A store sells 3 pencils for $0.75. How much would 7 pencils cost?
A. $5.25
B. $0.25
C. $1.75
D. $2.25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
pencils / cost = pencils / cost 3 / 0.75 = 7 / x
We set up a proportion comparing the number of pencils to their cost. Let 'x' be the unknown cost for 7 pencils.
2
3 x = 0.75 7 3x = 5.25
Multiply the numerator of the first ratio by the denominator of the second, and vice-versa.
3
x = 5.25 / 3 x = 1.75
Divide both sides of the equation by 3 to find the value of x.

Answer: $1.75 (Option C)

Question 6Grade 6 • RP
A certain type of bacteria doubles its population every 20 minutes. If you start with 150 bacteria, how many will there be after 1 hour?
A. 900 bacteria
B. 450 bacteria
C. 1200 bacteria
D. 600 bacteria
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
1 hour = 60 minutes
To compare the time intervals, ensure both are in the same unit.
2
Number of intervals = 60 minutes / 20 minutes/interval = 3 intervals
Divide the total time by the doubling time to find how many times the population will double.
3
Initial: 150 bacteria After 1st interval (20 min): 150 * 2 = 300 bacteria After 2nd interval (40 min): 300 * 2 = 600 bacteria After 3rd interval (60 min): 600 * 2 = 1200 bacteria
Multiply the current population by 2 for each doubling interval.

Answer: 1200 bacteria (Option C)

Question 7Grade 6 • RP
A crew of 5 workers can build a small fence in 6 hours. If 3 more workers join the crew (making a total of 8 workers), how long will it take them to build the same fence, assuming they work at the same rate? (Express your answer as a decimal if necessary).
A. 9.6 hours
B. 4 hours
C. 3.75 hours
D. 9 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
More workers means less time, so this is an inverse proportion. The product of workers and time remains constant.
In an inverse proportion, as one quantity increases, the other decreases proportionally. We use the formula workers * time = constant.
2
Work units = 5 workers * 6 hours = 30 work units
Multiply the initial number of workers by the time it takes them to find the total 'work units' required for the job.
3
New workers = 5 + 3 = 8 workers
Add the new workers to the original crew to find the total number of workers.
4
8 workers * x hours = 30 work units x = 30 / 8 x = 3.75 hours
Divide the total work units by the new number of workers to find the time it will take them.

Answer: 3.75 hours (Option C)

Question 8Grade 6 • RP
A clock loses 3 minutes every 4 hours. If the clock is set correctly at 8:00 AM on Monday, what time will it show at 8:00 AM on Wednesday, assuming it continues to lose time at the same rate?
A. 7:42 AM
B. 7:24 AM
C. 6:56 AM
D. 7:27 AM
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Monday 8:00 AM to Tuesday 8:00 AM = 24 hours Tuesday 8:00 AM to Wednesday 8:00 AM = 24 hours Total hours = 24 + 24 = 48 hours
Determine the total duration for which the clock is losing time.
2
minutes lost / hours passed = minutes lost / total hours 3 / 4 = x / 48
Let 'x' be the total minutes lost over 48 hours.
3
4 x = 3 48 4x = 144 x = 144 / 4 x = 36 minutes
Solve the proportion to find the total number of minutes the clock will lose.
4
Actual time: 8:00 AM Wednesday Time shown = 8:00 AM - 36 minutes 8:00 AM is equivalent to 7 hours and 60 minutes. 7 hours 60 minutes - 36 minutes = 7 hours 24 minutes. So, the clock will show 7:24 AM.
Subtract the calculated lost minutes from the actual time to determine what the clock will display.

Answer: 7:24 AM (Option B)

Question 9Grade 6 • RP
A town's population increases by 15% every 5 years. If the current population is 12,000, what will the population be in 10 years?
A. 13,800 people
B. 18,000 people
C. 15,600 people
D. 15,870 people
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Number of intervals = 10 years / 5 years/interval = 2 intervals
Divide the total time by the interval duration to find how many times the population will increase.
2
Population after 5 years = Current population * (1 + 15%) = 12,000 * (1 + 0.15) = 12,000 * 1.15 = 13,800 people
To find the new population, multiply the current population by (1 + the decimal form of the percentage increase).
3
Population after 10 years = Population after 5 years * (1 + 15%) = 13,800 * 1.15 = 15,870 people
Apply the same percentage increase to the new population from the previous interval.

Answer: 15,870 people (Option D)
