Question 5Grade 9 • A-REI

To combine the rates (1/(t/3) and 1/((t-1)/4)) in an equation, what is a common denominator for the simplified rational expressions?
A. t^2 - t
B. t + (t-1)
C. 3(t-1) + 4t
D. t-1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Given expressions are 1/(t/3) and 1/((t-1)/4).
Identify the complex rational expressions.
2
Simplify the expressions: 1/(t/3) = 3/t and 1/((t-1)/4) = 4/(t-1).
Simplify each complex fraction by multiplying by the reciprocal of the denominator.
3
The denominators are t and (t-1).
Identify the denominators of the simplified rational expressions.
4
The least common denominator (LCD) is the product of the distinct factors, which are t and (t-1).
Since t and (t-1) are distinct polynomial factors, their product forms the LCD.
5
Common denominator is t(t-1).
State the common denominator for the variables. (Note: The question asks for 'a common denominator' which can be more general than LCD, but since the numerical part is 1, the variable part is the main focus.)

Answer: 12 (Option A)

Question 6Grade 9 • A-REI
An electronics company is analyzing the impedance in two circuits. The impedances are given by 1/(x+2) ohms and 1/(x-3) ohms. To find the total impedance, what is the least common denominator for these expressions?
A. x^2 - x - 6
B. 2x - 1
C. x+2
D. x-3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Rational expressions are 1/(x+2) and 1/(x-3).
Identify the rational expressions.
2
Denominators are (x+2) and (x-3).
Extract the denominators.
3
Factors are (x+2) and (x-3).
Recognize that the denominators are already in factored form and are distinct.
4
LCM((x+2), (x-3)) = (x+2)(x-3).
The LCM of distinct polynomial factors is their product.
5
The least common denominator is (x+2)(x-3).
State the least common denominator.

Answer: (x+2)(x-3) (Option A)

Question 7Grade 9 • A-REI
A city planner is comparing population growth models. Model A describes growth as 1/(p+1) and Model B as 1/(p^2 - 1). To effectively compare these models in an equation, what is the least common denominator for these rational expressions?
A. p+1
B. p^2 + 2p + 1
C. p^2 - 1
D. p-1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Rational expressions are 1/(p+1) and 1/(p^2 - 1).
Identify the rational expressions from the problem.
2
Factor the denominators: (p+1) and (p-1)(p+1).
Factor each denominator completely to find all prime factors.
3
Distinct factors are (p+1) and (p-1).
Identify all unique factors from the factored denominators.
4
LCM((p+1), (p-1)(p+1)) = (p+1)(p-1) = p^2 - 1.
The LCM is formed by taking the highest power of each distinct factor.
5
The least common denominator is p^2 - 1.
State the least common denominator in its simplified form.

Answer: p^2 - 1 (Option C)

Question 8Grade 9 • A-REI
In a money management simulation, two investments yield returns represented by the rational expressions (x-1)/(x^2 - 4x + 4) and (x+1)/(x^2 - 4). To find a common basis for comparing these returns, what is the least common denominator?
A. (x-2)(x+2)
B. (x-2)^2(x+2)
C. x^2 - 4x + 4
D. x^2 - 4
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Rational expressions are (x-1)/(x^2 - 4x + 4) and (x+1)/(x^2 - 4).
Identify the rational expressions.
2
Factor denominators: x^2 - 4x + 4 = (x-2)^2 and x^2 - 4 = (x-2)(x+2).
Factor each denominator completely. Recognize a perfect square trinomial and a difference of squares.
3
Distinct factors are (x-2), (x+2).
Identify all unique factors from the factored denominators.
4
Highest powers of factors: (x-2)^2 and (x+2)^1.
For each distinct factor, determine the highest power that appears in any of the factored denominators.
5
LCM = (x-2)^2(x+2).
The LCM is the product of these highest powers.
6
The least common denominator is (x-2)^2(x+2).
State the least common denominator.

Answer: (x-2)^2(x+2) (Option B)
