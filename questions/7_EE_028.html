Question 1Grade 7 • EE
A stationery shop sells pencils for $1 each. The cost (y) in dollars for x pencils can be represented by the equation y = x. Plot the line representing this relationship on a coordinate plane. Which of the following ordered pairs (x, y) correctly represents a point on this line?
A. (0, 1)
B. (1, 5)
C. (5, 1)
D. (5, 5)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The equation y = x means that the cost (y) is equal to the number of pencils (x).
To find points on the line, the x-value (number of pencils) must equal the y-value (cost in dollars).
2
If x = 1, y = 1. So, (1, 1). If x = 5, y = 5. So, (5, 5).
By substituting various x-values into the equation, we can find corresponding y-values to create ordered pairs.
3
Option C: (5, 5) satisfies y = x.
We check each option to see if the ordered pair makes the equation true. For (5, 5), 5 = 5, which is true.

Answer: (5, 5) (Option D)

Question 2Grade 7 • EE
A basketball player scores 2 points for every basket they make. The total points scored (y) for x baskets can be modeled by the equation y = 2x. If the player makes 4 baskets, what ordered pair (x, y) represents this scenario on the graph?
A. (8, 4)
B. (4, 8)
C. (2, 4)
D. (4, 2)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The equation y = 2x means that the total points (y) are twice the number of baskets (x).
The value of y is determined by multiplying the x-value by 2.
2
Given x = 4 (number of baskets). Substitute into y = 2x.
We replace x with 4 to find the corresponding y-value.
3
y = 2 * 4 = 8. The ordered pair is (4, 8).
After calculating y, we combine it with the given x to form the ordered pair (x, y).

Answer: (4, 8) (Option B)

Question 4Grade 7 • EE

A. x | y ---|--- 1 | 1 2 | 2 3 | 3
B. x | y ---|--- 1 | 60 2 | 62 3 | 63
C. x | y ---|--- 1 | 6 2 | 12 3 | 18
D. x | y ---|--- 1 | 60 2 | 120 3 | 180
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The equation y = 60x means distance (y) is 60 times the number of hours (x).
To verify the table, we must multiply each x-value by 60 to get the corresponding y-value.
2

The table with (1, 60), (2, 120), (3, 180) matches our calculations.
Only one table will have all correctly calculated points based on the equation.

Question 5Grade 7 • EE

A. 80
B. 75
C. 0
D. 5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The y-intercept is the point where the line crosses the y-axis. This occurs when x = 0.
The y-intercept represents the initial value or the value of y when the independent variable x is zero.
2
y = 5(0) + 75
By setting x to 0, we are finding the point on the y-axis.
3
y = 0 + 75 = 75. The y-intercept is 75 (or the point (0, 75)).
The result of the calculation is the y-coordinate of the y-intercept.

Question 6Grade 7 • EE

A. A graph showing a line passing through (0, 10) and (1, 12).
B. A graph showing a line passing through (0, 10) and (1, 10).
C. A graph showing a line passing through (2, 0) and (12, 1).
D. A graph showing a line passing through (0, 2) and (1, 12).
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
When x = 0, y = 2(0) + 10 = 10. So, the line passes through (0, 10).
The y-intercept is a key point as it's where the line begins on the y-axis.
2
When x = 1, y = 2(1) + 10 = 12. So, (1, 12). When x = 2, y = 2(2) + 10 = 14. So, (2, 14).
Calculating at least one more point helps confirm the slope and direction of the line.
3
Look for the graph that passes through (0, 10) and (1, 12) (or (2, 14)).
Compare the calculated points with the lines shown in the options to find the correct representation.

A. $70
B. $2050
C. $250
D. $100
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The equation y = 20x + 50 means the total cost (y) is $20 times the length (x) plus a $50 fixed fee.
We need to substitute the given length into the equation to calculate the total cost.
2
Given x = 10 (linear feet). Substitute into y = 20x + 50.

Question 8Grade 7 • EE
A phone battery starts with a 100% charge. It drains at a constant rate of 5% per hour. The remaining battery life (y) as a percentage after x hours can be described by the equation y = 100 - 5x. Which graph correctly represents this relationship?
A. A graph showing a horizontal line at y = 100.
B. A graph showing a line starting at (0, 100) and increasing with a slope of 5.
C. A graph showing a line starting at (0, 100) and decreasing with a slope of -5.
D. A graph showing a line starting at (0, 5) and decreasing with a slope of -5.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
When x = 0 (0 hours passed), y = 100 - 5(0) = 100. The y-intercept is (0, 100).
This is the starting battery percentage when no time has passed.
2
The slope is -5, meaning for every 1 unit increase in x (hours), y (battery life) decreases by 5 units. The line should go downwards from left to right.
A negative slope indicates a decreasing relationship.
3
Set y = 0: 0 = 100 - 5x => 5x = 100 => x = 20. The x-intercept is (20, 0).
The x-intercept indicates when the battery life will be 0%.
4
Look for a graph that starts at (0, 100) and decreases, passing through (20, 0).
The correct graph will match both the starting point and the rate of change (slope).

Answer: A graph showing a line starting at (0, 100) and decreasing with a slope of -5 (e.g., passing through (10, 50) and (20, 0)). (Option C)

Question 9Grade 7 • EE
The population of a small town (y) in hundreds can be estimated by the equation y = 3x + 15, where x represents the number of years since 2000. What would be the estimated population in the year 2005?
A. 2005
B. 1500
C. 3000
D. 30
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
x = Year - 2000. For the year 2005, x = 2005 - 2000 = 5.
The variable x represents the number of years passed since the base year 2000.
2
y = 3(5) + 15 = 15 + 15 = 30.
Perform the multiplication and then the addition to find the value of y.
3
Since y is in hundreds, an estimated y = 30 means 30 * 100 = 3000.
The problem states that 'y' represents the population in hundreds, so the calculated 'y' value must be multiplied by 100 to get the actual population count.

Answer: 3000 (Option C)

Question 10Grade 7 • EE
A data plan starts with 800 MB. You use 40 MB of data per day. The remaining data (y) in MB after x days can be modeled by the equation y = 800 - 40x. What are the x- and y-intercepts of the graph of this equation?
A. x-intercept: (20, 0), y-intercept: (0, 40)
B. x-intercept: (20, 0), y-intercept: (0, 800)
C. x-intercept: (40, 0), y-intercept: (0, 800)
D. x-intercept: (0, 20), y-intercept: (800, 0)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Set x = 0: y = 800 - 40(0) = 800. The y-intercept is (0, 800).
The y-intercept is the value of y when x is zero, representing the initial amount of data.
2
Set y = 0: 0 = 800 - 40x. Add 40x to both sides: 40x = 800. Divide by 40: x = 800 / 40 = 20. The x-intercept is (20, 0).
The x-intercept is the value of x when y is zero, representing the number of days until the data runs out.
3
x-intercept: (20, 0), y-intercept: (0, 800).
The final answer requires both intercepts in ordered pair form.

Answer: x-intercept: (20, 0), y-intercept: (0, 800) (Option B)
