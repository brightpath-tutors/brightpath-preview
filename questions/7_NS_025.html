Question 1Grade 7 • NS
Maria buys a shirt for $25.75 and a pair of socks for $8.20. Which expression demonstrates the Commutative Property of Addition for the total cost?
A. $25.75 - 8.20$
B. ($25.75 + 8.20$) + 0
C. $8.20 + 25.75$
D. $25.75 \times 8.20$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The problem asks to represent the total cost using the Commutative Property of Addition.
The Commutative Property of Addition states that the order in which numbers are added does not change the sum (a + b = b + a).
2
The cost of the shirt is $25.75 and the socks are $8.20. The total cost is $25.75 + 8.20$.
To find the total cost, we add the individual prices.
3
$25.75 + 8.20$ is equivalent to $8.20 + 25.75$.
By changing the order of the addends, we demonstrate the Commutative Property of Addition.

Answer: $8.20 + 25.75$ (Option C)

C. 12 + (8 + 5)
D. 12 × 8 + 5
E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1
The problem asks to represent the total points using the Associative Property of Addition.
The Associative Property of Addition states that the way numbers are grouped in an addition problem does not change the sum ((a + b) + c = a + (b + c)).
2
Alex scored 12, 8, and 5 points. An initial expression for the total could be (12 + 8) + 5.
To find the total points, we add all the scores.

Question 8Grade 7 • NS
A freelancer works 7 hours a day for 5 days. Then, due to an urgent project, she works an additional 2 hours each of those 5 days. Which expression correctly calculates the total hours worked using the Distributive Property?
A. (5 + 7) × (5 + 2)
B. 5 × 7 + 2
C. 5 + 7 + 2
D. 5 × (7 + 2)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The problem asks to calculate total hours worked using the Distributive Property.
The Distributive Property states that a(b + c) = ab + ac.
2
She normally works 7 hours a day and an additional 2 hours a day for 5 days. So, her total hours per day are 7 + 2. This is done for 5 days.
First, figure out the total hours worked per day, then multiply by the number of days.
3
The total hours can be represented as $5 \times (7 + 2)$. This shows that the 5 days apply to both the normal 7 hours and the additional 2 hours.
The Distributive Property allows us to multiply the number of days by the sum of hours worked per day.

Answer: 5 × (7 + 2) (Option D)

Question 9Grade 7 • NS
A small town's population was 4,500. Over one year, the population increased by 150 people. In the next year, it decreased by 150 people. Which expression shows the net change in population over the two years, illustrating the Inverse Property of Addition?
A. 150 + (-150)
B. 4500 + 150 - 150
C. 150 × (-150)
D. 150 - 150
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The problem asks to represent the net change in population using the Inverse Property of Addition.
The Inverse Property of Addition states that for any real number 'a', there exists an additive inverse '-a' such that a + (-a) = 0.
2
The population increased by 150 (positive 150) and then decreased by 150 (negative 150).
An increase is a positive change, and a decrease is a negative change.
3
The net change is $150 + (-150)$.
Adding a number and its additive inverse results in zero, demonstrating the Inverse Property of Addition.

Answer: 150 + (-150) (Option A)

Question 10Grade 7 • NS
A digital image has its file size reduced by a factor of 1/4 when compressed. To restore it to its original size, it needs to be scaled up by a factor that demonstrates the Inverse Property of Multiplication. What factor is needed?
A. -1/4
B. 1/4
C. 0
D. 4
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The problem asks for a factor that, when multiplied by 1/4, restores the original size (a factor of 1), demonstrating the Inverse Property of Multiplication.
The Inverse Property of Multiplication states that for any non-zero real number 'a', there exists a multiplicative inverse '1/a' such that a × (1/a) = 1.
2
The image file size is reduced by a factor of 1/4.
This is the 'a' in our property, \(a = 1/4\).
3
The multiplicative inverse (reciprocal) of 1/4 is 4.
To find the reciprocal of a fraction, you flip the numerator and the denominator.
4
\((1/4) \times 4 = 1\).
Multiplying the original factor by its inverse results in 1, indicating the original size is restored.

Answer: 4 (Option D)
