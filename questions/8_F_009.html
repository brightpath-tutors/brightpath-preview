B. From 0 hours to 1 hour
C. From 1 hour to 2 hours
D. From 2 hours to 3 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
A constant speed is represented by a horizontal line segment on a speed-time graph, as the speed value on the y-axis does not change over the given time interval on the x-axis.
This step defines the visual characteristic of constant speed on this type of graph.
2
From 0 hours to 1 hour: The line goes from (0,0) to (1,60), indicating increasing speed. From 1 hour to 2 hours: The line stays at y=60, connecting (1,60) to (2,60). This is a horizontal segment. Systematically analyze each segment of the graph to identify its behavior.
3
The interval where the line is horizontal is from 1 hour to 2 hours.
Based on the analysis in Step 2, this is the only interval where the speed is constant.
4
The train was traveling at a constant speed from 1 hour to 2 hours.
The identified interval corresponds to a constant speed.

Answer: From 1 hour to 2 hours (Option C)

B. 5 cm
C. 16 cm
D. 13 cm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1

2

3
Growth = Height on Day 5 - Height on Day 2 = 16 cm - 8 cm = 8 cm.

The plant grew 8 cm between Day 2 and Day 5.

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students

This defines the visual characteristic of an increasing interval on a graph.

The water depth was strictly increasing from 4 hours to 8 hours and from 12 hours to 16 hours.

Question 9Grade 8 • F
The graph below models the population of a rare species of butterfly in a nature reserve over a 10-year period. During which 2-year interval did the butterfly population experience the most rapid decline?
A. From Year 2 to Year 4
B. From Year 8 to Year 10
C. From Year 4 to Year 6
D. From Year 6 to Year 8
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Most rapid decline refers to the steepest downward slope of the graph. This means calculating the rate of change (slope) for each declining interval. The given points are: (0, 1000), (2, 1200), (4, 1500), (6, 1100), (8, 500), (10, 600).
This defines the mathematical interpretation of 'most rapid decline' in the context of the graph.
2
Rate of change = (Change in Population) / (Change in Years) Interval 0-2 years: (1200 - 1000) / (2 - 0) = 200 / 2 = 100 butterflies/year (Increase) Interval 2-4 years: (1500 - 1200) / (4 - 2) = 300 / 2 = 150 butterflies/year (Increase) Interval 4-6 years: (1100 - 1500) / (6 - 4) = -400 / 2 = -200 butterflies/year (Decline) Interval 6-8 years: (500 - 1100) / (8 - 6) = -600 / 2 = -300 butterflies/year (Decline) Interval 8-10 years: (600 - 500) / (10 - 8) = 100 / 2 = 50 butterflies/year (Increase)
For each segment, calculate the slope by dividing the change in y-values (population) by the change in x-values (years).
3
The rates of decline are -200 butterflies/year (4-6 years) and -300 butterflies/year (6-8 years). The steepest decline is -300 butterflies/year.
Compare the absolute values of the negative slopes to find the largest decline.
4
The most rapid decline occurred from Year 6 to Year 8.
The interval corresponding to the greatest negative rate of change is the answer.

Answer: From Year 6 to Year 8 (Option D)
