Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Sarah wants to buy a new video game that costs $45. She has already saved $20. Write an inequality to represent how much more money (m) Sarah needs to save.
A. m > 25
B. m ≤ 25
C. m ≥ 25
D. m < 25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total cost = $45, Saved amount = $20
The video game costs $45, and Sarah has $20.
2
Amount needed = Total cost - Saved amount = $45 - $20 = $25
To find how much more money Sarah needs, subtract the saved amount from the total cost.
3
m ≥ 25
Sarah needs to save at least $25, so 'm' must be greater than or equal to 25.

Answer: m ≥ 25 (Option C)

Question 3Grade 8 • EE

A. s < 2
B. s > 2
C. s ≥ 2
D. s ≤ 2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Maximum sugar = 3 cups, Added sugar = 1 cup
The recipe limits sugar to 3 cups, and 1 cup has been added.
2
Remaining sugar = Maximum sugar - Added sugar = 3 - 1 = 2 cups
Subtract the amount already added from the maximum allowed amount.
3
s ≤ 2
You can add 2 cups of sugar or less, so 's' must be less than or equal to 2.
