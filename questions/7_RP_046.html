Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A grocery store sells 3 bags of oranges for $5. How much would 9 bags of oranges cost?
A. $15
B. $12
C. $27
D. $18
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let x be the cost of 9 bags of oranges. We can set up the proportion as: bags/cost = bags/cost 3/5 = 9/x
We establish a relationship between the number of bags and their cost, ensuring consistency on both sides of the proportion.
2
3 x = 5 9 3x = 45
To solve a proportion, multiply the numerator of one ratio by the denominator of the other, and vice versa. These products are equal.
3
x = 45 / 3 x = 15
Divide both sides of the equation by 3 to isolate x and find the cost.

Answer: $15 (Option A)
