Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A customer buys c kilograms of coffee beans. If each kilogram costs $12.50, write an expression for the total cost of the coffee beans.
A. 12.50 + c
B. c - 12.50
C. 12.50c
D. 12.50 / c
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost per kilogram = $12.50. Number of kilograms = c.
We are given the unit cost and the quantity in terms of a variable.
2
To find the total cost when given a cost per unit and the number of units, multiplication is used.
The total cost is the unit cost multiplied by the number of units.
3
$12.50 * c
Multiply the cost per kilogram by the number of kilograms.
4
12.50c
The multiplication can be written compactly as 12.50c.

Answer: 12.50c (Option C)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE

A. x + 15
B. 3x + 15
C. 3 + x + 15
D. 3x + 1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Number of three-pointers = x. Points per three-pointer = 3. Points from three-pointers = 3 * x = 3x.
Multiply the number of three-pointers by their point value.
2
Number of free throws = 15. Points per free throw = 1. Points from free throws = 1 * 15 = 15.
Multiply the number of free throws by their point value.
3
Total points = Points from three-pointers + Points from free throws = 3x + 15.
Add the points from each type of shot to get the total.

Answer: 3x + 15 (Option B)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A car travels at an average speed of 65 kilometers per hour for t hours. It then stops for 0.75 hours before continuing for another 45 kilometers. Write an expression for the total distance traveled by the car.
A. 65t + 0.75 * 45
B. 65t + 0.75 + 45
C. (65 + 45)t
D. 65t + 45
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Speed = 65 km/h, Time = t hours. Distance = Speed × Time = 65t km.
The distance traveled is the product of speed and time.
2
The car stops for 0.75 hours. During this time, the distance traveled is 0 km.
A stop implies no movement, so this duration does not add to the total distance.
3
The car continues for another 45 km.
This is a fixed distance added to the journey.
4
Total distance = Distance (part 1) + Distance (part 2) = 65t + 45.
Sum the distances from the moving segments of the journey.

Answer: 65t + 45 (Option D)

D. m - (1/3)m + 18
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial members = m.
The starting point for the club's population.
2
1/3 of the original members left. Amount left = (1/3)m.
Multiply the fraction by the original number of members.
3
Members remaining = m - (1/3)m.
Subtract the members who left from the initial members.
4
New members = 18. Total current members = m - (1/3)m + 18.

E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students

h hours * 60 minutes/hour = 60h minutes.

Travel time = 60h minutes + 25 minutes = 60h + 25 minutes.
Add the converted hours and the given minutes of travel.

60h + 35 minutes.

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
A city has an initial population of P people. Each year, the population decreases by 2% and then increases by 500 people due to migration. Write an expression for the population after one year.
A. 0.02P + 500
B. P - 0.02P + 500
C. P + 0.02P + 500
D. P - 2P + 500
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial population = P.
This is the starting value for the population.
2
Decrease = 2% of P = 0.02P.
Convert the percentage to a decimal and multiply by the initial population.
3
Population after decrease = P - 0.02P.
Subtract the decrease amount from the initial population.
4
Increase = 500. Population after one year = P - 0.02P + 500.
Add the fixed number of migrants to the population after the percentage decrease.
5
P - 0.02P + 500 = (1 - 0.02)P + 500 = 0.98P + 500.
Combine the like terms involving P.

Answer: P - 0.02P + 500 (Option B)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A data plan costs $35 per month, which includes 10 GB of data. For every additional gigabyte (or fraction thereof) used beyond 10 GB, there is an extra charge of $3.50. Write an expression for the total monthly cost if g represents the total gigabytes used, assuming g > 10.
A. 3.50(g + 10) + 35
B. 35g + 3.50(10)
C. 35 + 3.50(g - 10)
D. 35 + 3.50g
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Base cost = $35. Included data = 10 GB.
This is the fixed part of the monthly charge.
2
Total data used = g GB. Additional data = g - 10 GB (since g > 10).
Subtract the included data from the total data to find the excess.
3
Cost per additional GB = $3.50. Cost for additional data = 3.50 * (g - 10).
Multiply the additional data by its per-unit cost.
4
Total cost = Base cost + Cost for additional data = 35 + 3.50(g - 10).
Add the fixed base cost to the variable cost for additional data.

Answer: 35 + 3.50(g - 10) (Option C)
