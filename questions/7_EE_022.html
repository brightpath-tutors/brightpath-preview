Question 1Grade 7 • EE
A square garden has an area of 50 square meters. What is the length of one side of the garden, in simplest radical form?
A. 5√2 meters
B. √50 meters
C. 10√5 meters
D. 2√5 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
If the area of a square is 'A', then the side length 's' is given by s = √A.
The side length of a square is the square root of its area.
2
s = √50
Replace A with 50.
3
50 = 25 × 2
25 is the largest perfect square that divides 50.
4
√50 = √(25 × 2) = √25 × √2 = 5√2
Use the property √(ab) = √a × √b to separate the perfect square and simplify it.

Answer: 5√2 meters (Option A)

Question 2Grade 7 • EE
During a science experiment, the oscillation period of a pendulum was found to be √72 seconds. Simplify this expression to its simplest radical form.
A. 3√8 seconds
B. 6√2 seconds
C. √72 seconds
D. 2√18 seconds
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The radicand is 72.
The number under the square root symbol is the radicand.
2
72 = 36 × 2
36 is the largest perfect square that divides 72 (since 6*6=36).
3
√72 = √(36 × 2)
Substitute the factored form back into the radical.
4
√(36 × 2) = √36 × √2
The square root of a product is the product of the square roots.
5
√36 × √2 = 6 × √2 = 6√2
Calculate the square root of the perfect square factor.

Answer: 6√2 seconds (Option B)
