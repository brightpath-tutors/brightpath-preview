Question 10Grade 5 • OA
A baker made 36 cupcakes. He sold 2/3 of them in the morning and gave 5 to his neighbors. Write an expression that shows the number of cupcakes he has left.
A. 36 + (2/3 x 36) + 5
B. (2/3 x 36) - 5 - 36
C. 36 - 2/3 + 5
D. 36 - (2/3 x 36) - 5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total cupcakes = 36, Fraction sold = 2/3, Given to neighbors = 5
Identify the initial total, the fraction sold, and the number given away.
2
Cupcakes sold = (2/3) x 36
First, calculate the number of cupcakes sold by multiplying the total by the fraction sold.
3
Cupcakes remaining after selling = Total cupcakes - Cupcakes sold
Subtract the sold cupcakes from the total.
4
Final cupcakes left = Cupcakes remaining after selling - Cupcakes given to neighbors
Then, subtract the cupcakes given to neighbors from the remaining amount.
5
Expression: 36 - (2/3 x 36) - 5
Combine all operations into a single expression, using parentheses to ensure the multiplication for the fraction is done first.

Answer: 36 - (2/3 x 36) - 5 (Option D)
