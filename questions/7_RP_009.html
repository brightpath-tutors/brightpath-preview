Constant of Proportionality

Skill ID: 7.RP.009

Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A baker uses 12 cups of sugar to make 3 cakes. What is the constant of proportionality, in cups of sugar per cake?
A. 0.25 cups of sugar per cake
B. 3 cups of sugar per cake
C. 4 cups of sugar per cake
D. 12 cups of sugar per cake
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cups of sugar, cakes
We are comparing cups of sugar to cakes.
2
Constant of proportionality = total cups of sugar / number of cakes
The constant of proportionality in this context is the amount of sugar needed for one cake.
3
12 cups / 3 cakes = 4 cups/cake
Divide the total cups of sugar by the number of cakes to find the constant of proportionality.

Answer: 4 cups of sugar per cake (Option C)

B. 0.33 milliliters of blue paint per milliliter of yellow paint
C. 3 milliliters of blue paint per milliliter of yellow paint
D. 1.8 milliliters of blue paint per milliliter of yellow paint
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Milliliters of blue paint, milliliters of yellow paint
We are comparing blue paint to yellow paint.
2
Constant of proportionality = milliliters of blue paint / milliliters of yellow paint
The constant of proportionality in this context is the ratio of blue paint to yellow paint.
3
5.4 ml blue paint / 1.8 ml yellow paint = 3 ml blue paint/ml yellow paint
Divide the amount of blue paint by the amount of yellow paint to find the constant of proportionality.

Answer: 3 milliliters of blue paint per milliliter of yellow paint (Option C)
