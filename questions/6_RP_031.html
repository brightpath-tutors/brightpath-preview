Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A pack of 8 identical pencils costs $3.60. What is the unit price of one pencil?
A. $0.45 per pencil
B. $0.80 per pencil
C. $2.22 per pencil
D. $0.36 per pencil
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total Cost = $3.60 Number of pencils = 8
To find the unit price, we need to determine the cost for a single item.
2
Unit Price = $3.60 / 8
The 'unit price' means the cost per one unit, so we divide the total cost by the quantity.
3
$3.60 ÷ 8 = $0.45
The calculation shows that each pencil costs $0.45.

Answer: $0.45 per pencil (Option A)

Question 9Grade 6 • RP
A small town has a population density of 120 people per square mile. If the town's area is 1500 acres, what is the total population? (1 square mile = 640 acres). Round to the nearest whole number.
A. 281 people
B. 187.5 people
C. 28125 people
D. 180000 people
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Area in square miles = 1500 acres / 640 acres/square mile = 2.34375 square miles
The population density is given in people per square mile, so the area must also be in square miles for consistent units.
2
Population = Population Density × Area Population = 120 people/square mile × 2.34375 square miles
Multiply the density by the area to find the total number of people.
3
Population = 281.25 Rounded Population = 281
The calculated population is 281.25, which rounds down to 281 people.

Answer: 281 people (Option A)

Question 10Grade 6 • RP
A new 3D printer can print 0.8 meters of filament every 15 minutes. How many meters of filament can it print in 3 hours?
A. 2.4 meters
B. 0.16 meters
C. 14.4 meters
D. 9.6 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
3 hours × 60 minutes/hour = 180 minutes
Since the printing rate is given in meters per 15 minutes, we need the total time in minutes for consistent units.
2
Number of intervals = 180 minutes / 15 minutes = 12 intervals
This tells us how many times the printer will operate for its given rate.
3
Total Filament = 0.8 meters/interval × 12 intervals = 9.6 meters
Multiplying the amount printed in one interval by the total number of intervals gives the total filament printed.

Answer: 9.6 meters (Option D)
