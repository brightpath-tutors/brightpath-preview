Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A diver starts at a depth of -5 meters. He then ascends 3 meters. What is his new depth?
A. 8 meters
B. -8 meters
C. -2 meters
D. 2 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial depth = -5 meters (below sea level) Ascends = +3 meters
Starting at -5 meters indicates a negative integer. Ascending means moving upwards, so we add a positive integer.
2
-5 + 3 = ?
We are combining the initial depth with the change in depth.
3
To add -5 and 3, find the difference between their absolute values: | -5 | - | 3 | = 5 - 3 = 2. Since -5 has a larger absolute value, the result is negative.
When adding integers with different signs, subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger absolute value and use the sign of the number with the larger absolute value.
4
-5 + 3 = -2
The diver's new depth is -2 meters.

Answer: -2 meters (Option C)
