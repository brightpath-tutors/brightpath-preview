Divide Mixed Numbers

Skill ID: 6.NS.052

Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A cashier needs to divide $5 \frac{1}{4}$ pounds of candy equally among 3 customers. How many pounds of candy does each customer receive? Express your answer in simplest form.
A. $1 \frac{2}{3}$ pounds
B. $15 \frac{3}{4}$ pounds
C. $1 \frac{1}{4}$ pounds
D. $1 \frac{3}{4}$ pounds
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$5 \frac{1}{4} = \frac{(5 \times 4) + 1}{4} = \frac{20 + 1}{4} = \frac{21}{4}$
To divide mixed numbers, first convert them to improper fractions. Multiply the whole number by the denominator and add the numerator. Keep the same denominator.
2
$3 = \frac{3}{1}$
Any whole number can be written as a fraction by placing it over 1.
3
\(\frac{21}{4} \div \frac{3}{1} = \frac{21}{4} \times \frac{1}{3}\)
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of \(\frac{3}{1}\) is \(\frac{1}{3}\).
4
\(\frac{21}{4} \times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{21 \times 1}{4 \times 3} = \frac{21}{12}\)
Multiply the top numbers (numerators) together and the bottom numbers (denominators) together.
5
\(\frac{21}{12} = \frac{7}{4} = 1 \frac{3}{4}\)
Divide the numerator by the denominator. The quotient is the whole number part, and the remainder is the new numerator over the original denominator. Simplify the fraction by dividing both numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor (3 in this case for 21/12, then 7/4).

Answer: $1 \frac{3}{4}$ pounds (Option D)

Question 2Grade 6 • NS
A track team ran a total distance of $12 \frac{1}{2}$ miles. If each runner ran \(\frac{5}{2}\) miles, how many runners were on the team? Express your answer in simplest form.
A. $12 \frac{1}{2}$ runners
B. $6 \frac{1}{4}$ runners
C. $25$ runners
D. 5 runners
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$12 \frac{1}{2} = \frac{(12 \times 2) + 1}{2} = \frac{24 + 1}{2} = \frac{25}{2}$
To divide mixed numbers, first convert them to improper fractions. Multiply the whole number by the denominator and add the numerator. Keep the same denominator.
2
\(\frac{25}{2} \div \frac{5}{2}\)
The problem asks to find how many times \(\frac{5}{2}\) fits into \(\frac{25}{2}\).
3
\(\frac{25}{2} \times \frac{2}{5}\)
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of \(\frac{5}{2}\) is \(\frac{2}{5}\).
4
\(\frac{25}{2} \times \frac{2}{5} = \frac{25 \times 2}{2 \times 5} = \frac{50}{10} = 5\)
Multiply the top numbers together and the bottom numbers together. Then, simplify the resulting fraction by dividing the numerator by the denominator.

Answer: 5 runners (Option D)

Question 3Grade 6 • NS

C. $3$ portions
D. $4$ portions
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students

1

First, convert the mixed number to an improper fraction. Multiply the whole number by the denominator and add the numerator. Keep the same denominator.
2
\(\frac{15}{4} \div \frac{3}{4}\)
The problem is to divide the total amount of broth by the size of each portion.
3
\(\frac{15}{4} \times \frac{4}{3}\)

4
\(\frac{15}{4} \times \frac{4}{3} = \frac{15 \times 4}{4 \times 3} = \frac{60}{12} = 5\)
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together. Then, divide the resulting numerator by the denominator to simplify.

Answer: 5 portions (Option B)

Question 4Grade 6 • NS
A car traveled a distance of $110 \frac{1}{4}$ miles at a constant speed for $2 \frac{1}{4}$ hours. What was the car's speed in miles per hour? Express your answer in simplest form.
A. $441$ mph
B. $49$ mph
C. $112 \frac{1}{2}$ mph
D. $247 \frac{3}{4}$ mph
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$110 \frac{1}{4} = \frac{(110 \times 4) + 1}{4} = \frac{440 + 1}{4} = \frac{441}{4}$ $2 \frac{1}{4} = \frac{(2 \times 4) + 1}{4} = \frac{8 + 1}{4} = \frac{9}{4}$
To find the speed, divide the total distance by the time. First, convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions.
2
\(\frac{441}{4} \div \frac{9}{4}\)
The problem is to divide the total distance by the time taken.
3
\(\frac{441}{4} \times \frac{4}{9}\)
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of \(\frac{9}{4}\) is \(\frac{4}{9}\).
4
\(\frac{441}{4} \times \frac{4}{9} = \frac{441 \times 4}{4 \times 9} = \frac{1764}{36} = 49\)
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together. Then, simplify the resulting fraction. Notice that the 4s cancel out, simplifying the multiplication: \(\frac{441}{9} = 49\).

Answer: $49$ mph (Option B)

Question 5Grade 6 • NS
A teacher has $14 \frac{2}{3}$ feet of ribbon to decorate bulletin boards. If each bulletin board requires $2 \frac{1}{6}$ feet of ribbon, how many bulletin boards can the teacher decorate completely? Express your answer in simplest form.
A. $12 \frac{1}{3}$ bulletin boards
B. 6 bulletin boards
C. $6 \frac{10}{13}$ bulletin boards
D. \(\frac{88}{39}\) bulletin boards
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$14 \frac{2}{3} = \frac{(14 \times 3) + 2}{3} = \frac{42 + 2}{3} = \frac{44}{3}$ $2 \frac{1}{6} = \frac{(2 \times 6) + 1}{6} = \frac{12 + 1}{6} = \frac{13}{6}$
To find out how many bulletin boards can be decorated, divide the total ribbon length by the ribbon needed per board. First, convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions.
2
\(\frac{44}{3} \div \frac{13}{6}\)
The problem is to divide the total length of ribbon by the length required for one board.
3
\(\frac{44}{3} \times \frac{6}{13}\)
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of \(\frac{13}{6}\) is \(\frac{6}{13}\).
4
\(\frac{44}{3} \times \frac{6}{13} = \frac{44 \times 6}{3 \times 13} = \frac{264}{39}\) Simplify by dividing numerator and denominator by 3: \(\frac{264 \div 3}{39 \div 3} = \frac{88}{13}\) Convert to mixed number: \(\frac{88}{13} = 6 \frac{10}{13}\) Since the teacher can only decorate 'completely', the answer is 6 bulletin boards.
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together. Then, simplify the resulting fraction. The whole number part of the mixed number represents the number of complete bulletin boards.

Answer: 6 bulletin boards (Option B)

To determine how many beakers can be filled, divide the total volume of the solution by the volume each beaker can hold. First, convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions.
2
\(\frac{36}{5} \div \frac{9}{5}\)
The problem is to divide the total solution volume by the volume per beaker.
3
\(\frac{36}{5} \times \frac{5}{9}\)

4

Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together. Then, simplify the resulting fraction. The 5s cancel out, simplifying the multiplication: \(\frac{36}{9} = 4\).

Question 9Grade 6 • NS
A city has a population that consumes $30 \frac{1}{3}$ million gallons of water over $1 \frac{2}{3}$ weeks. What is the average water consumption rate in millions of gallons per week? Express your answer in simplest form.
A. $15 \frac{1}{3}$ million gallons per week
B. $18 \frac{1}{5}$ million gallons per week
C. $6$ million gallons per week
D. $50 \frac{5}{9}$ million gallons per week
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$30 \frac{1}{3} = \frac{(30 \times 3) + 1}{3} = \frac{90 + 1}{3} = \frac{91}{3}$ $1 \frac{2}{3} = \frac{(1 \times 3) + 2}{3} = \frac{3 + 2}{3} = \frac{5}{3}$
To find the average consumption rate, divide the total water consumed by the total time. First, convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions.
2
\(\frac{91}{3} \div \frac{5}{3}\)
The problem is to divide the total water by the total weeks.
3
\(\frac{91}{3} \times \frac{3}{5}\)
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of \(\frac{5}{3}\) is \(\frac{3}{5}\).
4
\(\frac{91}{3} \times \frac{3}{5} = \frac{91 \times 3}{3 \times 5} = \frac{273}{15}\) Simplify by dividing numerator and denominator by 3: \(\frac{273 \div 3}{15 \div 3} = \frac{91}{5}\) Convert to mixed number: \(\frac{91}{5} = 18 \frac{1}{5}\)
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together. Then, simplify the resulting fraction and convert it to a mixed number.

Answer: $18 \frac{1}{5}$ million gallons per week (Option B)

Question 10Grade 6 • NS
A new 3D printer uses $16 \frac{4}{5}$ feet of filament to print $2 \frac{1}{10}$ identical models. How many feet of filament are used for each model? Express your answer in simplest form.
A. $1 \frac{7}{10}$ feet
B. 8 feet
C. $35 \frac{2}{3}$ feet
D. $16 \frac{4}{5}$ feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$16 \frac{4}{5} = \frac{(16 \times 5) + 4}{5} = \frac{80 + 4}{5} = \frac{84}{5}$ $2 \frac{1}{10} = \frac{(2 \times 10) + 1}{10} = \frac{20 + 1}{10} = \frac{21}{10}$
To find the filament used per model, divide the total filament by the number of models. First, convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions.
2
\(\frac{84}{5} \div \frac{21}{10}\)
The problem is to divide the total filament by the number of models.
3
\(\frac{84}{5} \times \frac{10}{21}\)
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of \(\frac{21}{10}\) is \(\frac{10}{21}\).
4
\(\frac{84}{5} \times \frac{10}{21} = \frac{84 \times 10}{5 \times 21} = \frac{840}{105}\) Simplify by dividing both numerator and denominator by common factors. (e.g., by 5: \(\frac{168}{21}\) Then by 21: \(\frac{168 \div 21}{21 \div 21} = 8\))
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together. Then, simplify the resulting fraction. Cross-simplification can also be used: 5 into 10 (gives 2), 21 into 84 (gives 4). Then $4 \times 2 = 8$.

Answer: 8 feet (Option B)
