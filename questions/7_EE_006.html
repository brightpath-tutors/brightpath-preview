Question 1Grade 7 • EE
A baker uses 12x + 18 ounces of sugar for a batch of cookies. If the baker wants to divide the sugar into equal portions for smaller batches, what is the greatest common factor of the expression 12x + 18?
A. 3(4x + 6)
B. 2(6x + 9)
C. 6(2x + 3)
D. 6(2x + 18)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Expression: 12x + 18 Terms: 12x and 18
The expression consists of two terms: a variable term 12x and a constant term 18.
2
Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12 Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18 GCF(12, 18) = 6
The greatest common factor of 12 and 18 is 6.
3
12x + 18 = 6(12x/6 + 18/6) = 6(2x + 3)
Divide each term in the expression by the GCF, 6, and write the GCF outside the parentheses.

Answer: 6(2x + 3) (Option C)

Question 9Grade 7 • EE
A healthcare provider manages patient records represented by the expression 6h^2 + 24h. To categorize the records, factor the expression 6h^2 + 24h. What is the factored form of the expression?
A. 12h(0.5h + 2)
B. 6h(h + 24)
C. 6(h^2 + 4h)
D. 6h(h + 4)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Expression: 6h^2 + 24h Terms: 6h^2 and 24h
The expression consists of two terms: a variable term 6h^2 and another variable term 24h.
2
Factors of 6: 1, 2, 3, 6 Factors of 24: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24 GCF(6, 24) = 6
The greatest common factor of 6 and 24 is 6.
3
Variable parts: h^2 and h GCF(h^2, h) = h
The lowest power of h in both terms is h^1, so the GCF of the variable parts is h.
4
Overall GCF = 6 * h = 6h
Multiply the GCF of the numerical coefficients by the GCF of the variable parts.
5
6h^2 + 24h = 6h(6h^2 / 6h + 24h / 6h) = 6h(h + 4)
Divide each term in the expression by the overall GCF, 6h, and write the GCF outside the parentheses.

Answer: 6h(h + 4) (Option D)
