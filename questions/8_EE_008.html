Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Sarah had $25.50. After buying a new book, she had $13.25 left. How much did the book cost?
A. $38.75
B. $12.25
C. $12.75
D. $11.25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the cost of the book. The initial amount minus the cost of the book equals the remaining amount: $25.50 - x = $13.25.
We start with the total money Sarah had, subtract the unknown cost of the book, and set it equal to the money she had left.
2
$25.50 - x = $13.25 Subtract $13.25 from both sides: $25.50 - $13.25 = x
To find 'x', we rearrange the equation so that 'x' is by itself on one side. We can do this by adding 'x' to both sides, and then subtracting $13.25 from both sides, or by simply realizing that the cost of the book is the difference between the starting amount and the remaining amount.
3
$25.50 - $13.25 = $12.25
Perform the subtraction to find the cost of the book.

Answer: $12.25 (Option B)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
A basketball team scored 87 points in a game. If they scored 45 points in the first half, how many points did they score in the second half?
A. 32
B. 132
C. 45
D. 42
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the points scored in the second half. Points from first half + points from second half = total points: 45 + x = 87.
We know the points from the first half and the total points. The unknown is the points in the second half.
2
45 + x = 87 Subtract 45 from both sides: x = 87 - 45
To solve for 'x', we perform the inverse operation of addition, which is subtraction, on both sides of the equation.
3
87 - 45 = 42
Perform the subtraction to find the points scored in the second half.

Answer: 42 points (Option D)

Question 3Grade 8 • EE
A recipe calls for 3 cups of sugar. If this amount is one-fourth of the total sugar needed for a large batch, how much total sugar (in cups) is needed for the large batch?
A. 1
B. 0.75
C. 7
D. 12
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the total sugar needed. One-fourth of the total sugar is 3 cups: (1/4) * x = 3.
We are given a fraction of the total amount and its value, so we set up a multiplication equation.
2
(1/4) x = 3 Multiply both sides by 4: x = 3 4
To solve for 'x', we perform the inverse operation of multiplying by a fraction, which is multiplying by its reciprocal (or just multiplying by the denominator).
3
3 * 4 = 12
Perform the multiplication to find the total sugar needed.

Answer: 12 cups (Option D)

2
s × 3.5 = 180 Divide both sides by 3.5: s = 180 / 3.5
To solve for 's', we perform the inverse operation of multiplication, which is division, on both sides of the equation.
3
180 / 3.5 ≈ 51.42857... Rounded to the nearest hundredth, s = 51.43.

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students

1

2

Question 6Grade 8 • EE
The temperature in a freezer was -15°C. After a power outage, the temperature rose by 8°C. What is the new temperature in the freezer?
A. 23°C
B. 7°C
C. -7°C
D. -23°C
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the new temperature. Initial temperature + temperature rise = new temperature: -15 + 8 = x.
When the temperature rises, it means we add the change to the initial temperature. We are working with integers.
2
-15 + 8 = -7
When adding integers with different signs, subtract their absolute values and keep the sign of the integer with the larger absolute value. | -15 | = 15, | 8 | = 8. 15 - 8 = 7. Since -15 has a larger absolute value, the result is negative.

Answer: -7°C (Option C)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A carpenter needs to cut a wooden plank into 6 equal pieces. If the original plank is 14.4 feet long, what is the length of each piece in feet?
A. 2.4 feet
B. 86.4 feet
C. 2.2 feet
D. 6 feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the length of each piece. 6 pieces of length 'x' make up the total length: 6x = 14.4.
The total length of the plank is divided equally among 6 pieces.
2
6x = 14.4 Divide both sides by 6: x = 14.4 / 6
To solve for 'x', we perform the inverse operation of multiplication, which is division, on both sides of the equation.
3
14.4 / 6 = 2.4
Perform the division to find the length of each piece.

Answer: 2.4 feet (Option A)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A flight from New York to London takes 7 hours and 30 minutes. If the flight arrived at 5:15 PM, what time did it depart from New York?
A. 9:45 PM
B. 10:45 AM
C. 9:45 AM
D. 12:45 PM
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
5:15 PM = 17:15
Converting to 24-hour format simplifies time calculations, especially when crossing midnight or dealing with AM/PM.
2
Departure Time + Flight Duration = Arrival Time Departure Time + 7 hours 30 minutes = 17 hours 15 minutes Departure Time = 17 hours 15 minutes - 7 hours 30 minutes
To find the departure time, we subtract the flight duration from the arrival time.
3
Since 15 minutes is less than 30 minutes, borrow 1 hour (60 minutes) from 17 hours: 17 hours 15 minutes becomes 16 hours (15 + 60) minutes = 16 hours 75 minutes. Now, subtract: 16 hours 75 minutes - 7 hours 30 minutes = 9 hours 45 minutes.
We need to borrow from the hours column because we cannot subtract 30 minutes from 15 minutes directly.
4
9 hours 45 minutes is 9:45 AM.
Since the result is less than 12:00, it is AM.

Answer: 9:45 AM (Option C)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
The population of a small town increased by 15% last year. If the current population is 18,400, what was the population before the increase? (Round to the nearest whole number)
A. 21,160 people
B. 15,640 people
C. 16,000 people
D. 18,415 people
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'P' be the population before the increase. 15% as a decimal is 0.15. An increase of 15% means the new population is 100% + 15% = 115% of the original population, or 1.15P.
To account for the increase, we multiply the original population by (1 + the decimal form of the percentage increase).
2
P × 1.15 = 18,400
The original population multiplied by 1.15 equals the current population.
3
P = 18,400 / 1.15
To solve for 'P', we divide the current population by the growth factor (1.15).
4
P = 18,400 / 1.15 = 16,000
Perform the division. The result is a whole number, so no rounding is needed.

Answer: 16,000 people (Option C)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A new smartphone was on sale for $720, which was 80% of its original price. What was the original price of the smartphone?
A. $800
B. $900
C. $1000
D. $576
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'P' be the original price of the smartphone. 80% as a decimal is 0.80.
To work with percentages in an equation, we convert them to their decimal form.
2
Original Price × Percentage = Sale Price P × 0.80 = 720
The sale price is a fraction (80%) of the original price.
3
P = 720 / 0.80
To solve for 'P', we divide the sale price by the decimal equivalent of the percentage.
4
P = 900
Perform the division to find the original price.

Answer: $900 (Option B)
