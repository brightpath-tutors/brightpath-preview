Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A graph shows the total cost (y-axis) in dollars for buying different numbers of t-shirts (x-axis). Which of the following graphs represents a proportional relationship between the number of t-shirts and the total cost?
A. A straight line that passes through (0, 5).
B. A straight line that passes through the origin (0,0).
C. A straight line that passes through (5,0).
D. A curved line that starts at (0,0).
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
A proportional relationship is characterized by two key features: it must be represented by a straight line, and this line must pass through the origin (0,0).
To identify a proportional relationship from a graph, look for a straight line that starts at the origin (0,0).
2
A: A straight line that passes through (0, 5). (Not proportional as it doesn't pass through (0,0)) B: A curved line that starts at (0,0). (Not proportional as it's not a straight line) C: A straight line that passes through the origin (0,0). (This fits the criteria) D: A straight line that passes through (5,0). (Not proportional as it doesn't pass through (0,0))
Evaluate each option against the definition of a proportional graph.
3
Only option C describes a graph that is a straight line and passes through the origin (0,0).
Based on the analysis, option C is the only one that correctly describes a proportional relationship.

Answer: C: A straight line that passes through the origin (0,0). (Option B)

Question 2Grade 7 • RP
The graph shows the distance a runner covers over time. The x-axis represents time in minutes, and the y-axis represents distance in meters. The graph is a straight line passing through the origin and the point (2, 800). What is the constant of proportionality (unit rate) of the runner's speed in meters per minute?
A. 2 meters per minute
B. 800 meters per minute
C. 1600 meters per minute
D. 400 meters per minute
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The graph passes through the origin (0,0) and the point (2, 800). Here, x = 2 minutes and y = 800 meters.
Extract the x and y values from the given point on the graph.
2
For a proportional relationship, the constant of proportionality (k) is found by dividing the y-coordinate by the x-coordinate (k = y/x).
The constant of proportionality is the unit rate, calculated as the ratio of the dependent variable (y) to the independent variable (x).
3
k = 800 meters / 2 minutes = 400 meters/minute.
Divide the distance by the time to find the speed per minute.
4
The constant of proportionality is 400 meters per minute.
The unit rate, or constant of proportionality, is 400 meters per minute.

Answer: C: 400 meters per minute (Option D)

Question 4Grade 7 • RP
A train travels at a constant speed. A graph shows the distance traveled (y-axis) in kilometers versus time (x-axis) in hours. The graph passes through the origin and points (1, 65) and (3, 195). What is the constant of proportionality (speed) of the train in kilometers per hour?
A. 65 kilometers per hour
B. 130 kilometers per hour
C. 3 kilometers per hour
D. 195 kilometers per hour
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The problem states the train travels at a constant speed, and the graph passes through the origin, indicating a proportional relationship. The constant of proportionality (k) represents the speed in km/hr.
A constant speed implies a proportional relationship when distance is plotted against time starting from zero. The constant of proportionality is the unit rate (speed).
2
Choose any point (x, y) on the line (other than the origin) and use the formula k = y/x.
For any point (x,y) on a proportional graph, the ratio y/x gives the constant of proportionality.
3
k = 65 km / 1 hour = 65 km/hr.
Using the point (1, 65), divide the distance (65) by the time (1) to find the speed.
4
k = 195 km / 3 hours = 65 km/hr. Both points yield the same constant.
Using a second point helps confirm the constant of proportionality and ensures accuracy.
5
The constant of proportionality is 65 kilometers per hour.
The train's constant speed, or constant of proportionality, is 65 km/hr.

Question 5Grade 7 • RP
A graph represents the number of pages a student reads (y-axis) over time (x-axis) in minutes. The graph is a straight line passing through the origin and the point (10, 25). Write an equation that represents this proportional relationship, where 'p' is the number of pages and 'm' is the time in minutes.
A. p = 0.4m
B. p = 2.5m
C. m = 2.5p
D. p = 10m + 25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The graph passes through the origin and (10, 25), indicating a proportional relationship. x = minutes (m) and y = pages (p).
A straight line through the origin signifies a proportional relationship, and the variables are assigned to the axes.
2
k = y/x = p/m.
The constant of proportionality is the ratio of the dependent variable (pages) to the independent variable (minutes).
3
k = 25 pages / 10 minutes = 2.5 pages/minute.
Divide the number of pages by the time to find the reading rate per minute.
4
For a proportional relationship, the equation is in the form y = kx. Substituting p for y, m for x, and 2.5 for k, the equation is p = 2.5m.
The general form of a proportional equation is y = kx. Substitute the identified variables and the calculated constant of proportionality.

Answer: C: p = 2.5m (Option B)

Question 6Grade 7 • RP
A scientist is observing the growth of a plant. A proportional graph shows the plant's height (y-axis) in centimeters over time (x-axis) in weeks. The graph includes the point (4, 12). Based on this relationship, what will be the plant's height after 7 weeks?
A. 21 centimeters
B. 19 centimeters
C. 15 centimeters
D. 28 centimeters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The graph is proportional and includes the point (4, 12). This means after 4 weeks, the plant is 12 cm tall.
A proportional graph passing through the origin and a given point indicates a constant rate of growth.
2
k = y/x = 12 cm / 4 weeks = 3 cm/week.
The constant of proportionality represents the growth rate of the plant per week.
3
The equation for this relationship is y = 3x (where y is height and x is weeks).
Use the general form y = kx and substitute the calculated k value and the variables.
4
Substitute x = 7 into the equation: y = 3 * 7 = 21 cm.
To find the height after 7 weeks, substitute 7 for x (weeks) into the equation.
5
The plant's height after 7 weeks will be 21 centimeters.
The calculated height of the plant after 7 weeks is 21 cm.

Answer: C: 21 centimeters (Option A)

The graph is proportional, and the machine produces 750 springs in 15 minutes.

The constant of proportionality is the unit rate of spring production per minute.
3
The equation for the number of springs (s) produced in 'm' minutes is s = 50m.
Formulate an equation using the general proportional form y = kx, substituting the variables and the calculated constant.

In 20 minutes, 1000 springs are produced.

B. 0.000003 square millimeters
C. 5,000,000 square millimeters
D. 333,333.33 square millimeters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The graph is proportional. The point (5,000,000, 15) means 5,000,000 bacteria cover an area of 15 square millimeters. The x-axis is number of bacteria, y-axis is area.
Identify the independent and dependent variables and interpret the given data point in the context of the problem.
2
We need the area covered per one bacterium. This is the constant of proportionality, k = y/x.
The 'average area covered by one bacterium' is the unit rate, which is equivalent to the constant of proportionality (y/x).
3
k = Area / Number of bacteria = 15 sq mm / 5,000,000 bacteria.
Set up the division to find the constant of proportionality.
4
k = 15 / 5,000,000. Divide both numerator and denominator by 5: 3 / 1,000,000. Convert to decimal: 0.000003.
Perform the division and express the result as a decimal to find the area per bacterium.
5
The average area per bacterium is 0.000003 square millimeters.
The unit area covered by one bacterium is 0.000003 square millimeters.

Question 10Grade 7 • RP
A company manufactures microchips. Two different machines, Machine P and Machine Q, produce chips at constant rates. Proportional graphs show the number of chips produced (y-axis) versus time (x-axis) in minutes. Machine P's line passes through (5, 1200) and Machine Q's line passes through (8, 1600). How many more chips will the faster machine produce than the slower machine in 10 minutes?
A. 20 chips
B. 40 chips
C. 400 chips
D. 200 chips
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
k_P = 1200 chips / 5 minutes = 240 chips/minute.
Determine the production rate for Machine P by dividing the number of chips by the time taken.
2
k_Q = 1600 chips / 8 minutes = 200 chips/minute.
Determine the production rate for Machine Q by dividing the number of chips by the time taken.
3
Machine P (240 chips/min) is faster than Machine Q (200 chips/min).
Compare the two rates to identify which machine is faster.
4
Chips_P = 240 chips/min * 10 minutes = 2400 chips.
Multiply Machine P's rate by 10 minutes to find its total production in that time.
5
Chips_Q = 200 chips/min * 10 minutes = 2000 chips.
Multiply Machine Q's rate by 10 minutes to find its total production in that time.
6
Difference = Chips_P - Chips_Q = 2400 - 2000 = 400 chips.
Subtract the slower machine's total production from the faster machine's total production.
7
The faster machine will produce 400 more chips than the slower machine in 10 minutes.
The final difference in chips produced after 10 minutes is 400.

Answer: C: 400 chips (Option C)
