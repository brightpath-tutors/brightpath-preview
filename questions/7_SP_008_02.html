Question 4Grade 7 • SP
A company is developing a new technology. They have tested 45 prototypes, and 27 of them failed to meet specifications. What is the probability that a randomly selected prototype from this batch passed the test, expressed as a decimal?
A. 0.6
B. 0.4
C. 0.27
D. 0.73
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total prototypes = 45
Identify the total number of prototypes tested.
2
Prototypes that failed = 27
Identify the number of prototypes that did not meet specifications.
3
Prototypes that passed = Total prototypes - Prototypes that failed = 45 - 27 = 18
Calculate the number of prototypes that passed the test.
4
Probability = Prototypes that passed / Total prototypes = 18 / 45
Set up the fraction for the probability of a prototype passing.
5
Simplify fraction: 18 ÷ 9 = 2, 45 ÷ 9 = 5. So, 2/5
Simplify the fraction by dividing both numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor (9).
6
Convert to decimal: 2 / 5 = 0.4
Convert the simplified fraction to a decimal by dividing the numerator by the denominator.

Answer: 0.4 (Option B)

Probability of bacterium growing = 85% = 0.85
Convert the given percentage probability of growth into a decimal.

Total probability of all outcomes = 1

2
Residents who own at least one pet = 12,500

B. 0.97
C. 0.96
D. 1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students

Total number of students = 30
Identify the total number of students in the class, representing the total possible outcomes.

Number of winning tickets = 1
Identify the number of available movie tickets.
3
Probability of Sarah winning = 1 / 30
Calculate the probability that Sarah (a specific student) will win the ticket.
4
Probability of Sarah NOT winning = 1 - (1 / 30)

5
Convert fraction to decimal: 1 / 30 = 0.0333...

6
Subtract from 1: 1 - 0.0333... = 0.9666...

Round to two decimal places: 0.97
Round the result to the nearest hundredth as requested.

Question 10Grade 7 • SP
A bus route has 45 scheduled stops. On a particular trip, 3 of these stops are known to be skipped due to road construction. If a passenger randomly selects one of the 45 stops to check if the bus will stop there, what is the probability that the bus will make the stop? Express your answer as a decimal in simplest form.
A. 0.07
B. 0.42
C. 0.93
D. 0.14
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total scheduled stops = 45
Identify the total number of stops on the bus route.
2
Number of skipped stops = 3
Identify the number of stops that will not be made.
3
Number of stops the bus WILL make = Total scheduled stops - Number of skipped stops = 45 - 3 = 42
Calculate the number of stops where the bus will actually stop.
4
Probability = Number of stops the bus WILL make / Total scheduled stops = 42 / 45
Set up the fraction representing the probability of the bus making a stop.
5
Simplify fraction: 42 ÷ 3 = 14, 45 ÷ 3 = 15. So, 14/15
Simplify the fraction by dividing both numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor (3).
6
Convert to decimal: 14 / 15 = 0.9333...
Convert the simplified fraction to a decimal.
7
Round to two decimal places: 0.93
Round the decimal to two decimal places as requested.

Answer: 0.93 (Option C)
