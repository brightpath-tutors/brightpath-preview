Question 10Grade 5 • MD
A line plot shows the download speeds, in Mbps, measured at different times of day on a specific internet connection. The speeds are: 5 1/2, 5 3/4, 6, 5 1/4, 5 1/2, 5 3/4, 6, 5 1/2. Which question can be answered by analyzing this line plot?
A. What is the fastest internet speed available in the market?
B. Which internet provider offers the most reliable connection?
C. How many devices were connected to the internet at each time of day?
D. What is the average download speed, in Mbps, across all recorded times?
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Data: 5 1/2, 5 3/4, 6, 5 1/4, 5 1/2, 5 3/4, 6, 5 1/2
List all the download speeds from the line plot.
2
Count the number of data points: There are 8 data points.
Determine the total count of measurements, which is needed for calculating the average.
3
Calculate the sum of all speeds: 5 1/2 + 5 3/4 + 6 + 5 1/4 + 5 1/2 + 5 3/4 + 6 + 5 1/2.
Set up the addition to find the total of all recorded speeds.
4
Convert to common denominator (4): 5 2/4 + 5 3/4 + 6 + 5 1/4 + 5 2/4 + 5 3/4 + 6 + 5 2/4. Sum of whole numbers: 5+5+6+5+5+5+6+5 = 42. Sum of fractions: 2/4 + 3/4 + 1/4 + 2/4 + 3/4 + 2/4 = 13/4 = 3 1/4. Total sum: 42 + 3 1/4 = 45 1/4 Mbps.
Add the mixed numbers by summing whole parts and fractional parts separately, then combine and simplify.
5
Calculate the average: 45 1/4 ÷ 8. Convert to improper fraction: 45 1/4 = 181/4. So, 181/4 ÷ 8 = 181/4 × 1/8 = 181/32 Mbps. This question can be answered.
Divide the total sum by the number of data points to find the average, confirming the question is answerable.

Answer: What is the average download speed, in Mbps, across all recorded times? (Option D)
