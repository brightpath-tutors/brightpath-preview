Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A student drew a map of their school's main hallway. The actual hallway is 25 meters long, and on the map, it is represented as 10 cm long. What is the scale factor of the map to the actual hallway, in simplest form?
A. 1:25
B. 1:2500
C. 250:1
D. 1:250
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Map length = 10 cm Actual length = 25 meters
We need to compare the map's length to the actual length to find the scale factor.
2
1 meter = 100 cm Actual length = 25 meters * 100 cm/meter = 2500 cm
To form a ratio, both quantities must be in the same unit. Converting meters to centimeters is a common first step.
3
Ratio = Map length : Actual length Ratio = 10 cm : 2500 cm
The scale factor is typically expressed as (model/map dimension) : (actual dimension).
4
Divide both sides of the ratio by the greatest common divisor, which is 10. 10 ÷ 10 : 2500 ÷ 10 1 : 250
Simplifying the ratio provides the most concise representation of the scale factor.

Answer: 1:250 (Option D)

B. 60 meters
C. 6 meters
D. 600 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Scale factor = 1:75 (Blueprint : Actual) Blueprint wall length = 8 cm
The scale factor tells us the relationship between the blueprint and the actual dimensions.
2
1 (blueprint) / 75 (actual) = 8 cm (blueprint) / x cm (actual)
We can use a proportion to find the unknown actual length. The units on the numerator and denominator must match.
3
1 x = 75 8 x = 600 cm
Cross-multiplication is used to solve the proportion for the unknown actual length in centimeters.
4
1 meter = 100 cm 600 cm / 100 cm/meter = 6 meters
The question asks for the answer in meters, so a final unit conversion is required.

Answer: 6 meters (Option C)
