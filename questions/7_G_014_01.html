Question 1Grade 7 • G
A store manager sets up a display stand. Two support beams intersect, forming four angles. If one of the angles measures 75 degrees, what is the measure of the angle vertically opposite to it?
A. 75 degrees
B. 105 degrees
C. 15 degrees
D. 285 degrees
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Angle relationship: Vertical angles are equal.
Identify that the problem describes vertical angles, which have equal measures.
2
Given angle = 75 degrees
Note the measure of the known angle.
3
Unknown angle = 75 degrees
Since vertical angles are equal, the angle vertically opposite to 75 degrees is also 75 degrees.

Answer: 75 degrees (Option A)

Question 2Grade 7 • G
During a soccer practice, a coach places two cones on a straight line from the goalpost, creating an angle of 110 degrees with the sideline. What is the measure of the adjacent angle formed between the cones and the sideline, assuming it forms a straight line with the first angle?
A. 250 degrees
B. 70 degrees
C. 20 degrees
D. 110 degrees
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Angle relationship: Angles on a straight line are supplementary, summing to 180 degrees.
Recognize that the two angles form a linear pair, which means their sum is 180 degrees.
2
Given angle = 110 degrees
Identify the measure of the known angle.
3
Equation: 110 + x = 180
Set up an equation where 'x' is the unknown angle, and the sum equals 180 degrees.
4
Solve for x: x = 180 - 110 = 70 degrees
Subtract the known angle from 180 to find the unknown angle.

Answer: 70 degrees (Option B)

Question 4Grade 7 • G
A drone's flight path creates two adjacent angles on a straight aerial line. One angle is represented by (2x + 15) degrees, and the other is (3x + 25) degrees. What is the value of x?
A. 35
B. 28
C. 40
D. 140
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Angle relationship: Angles on a straight line are supplementary, summing to 180 degrees.
Understand that the two adjacent angles on a straight line form a linear pair, so their sum is 180 degrees.
2
Given angles: (2x + 15) and (3x + 25)
Identify the algebraic expressions for the two angles.
3
Equation: (2x + 15) + (3x + 25) = 180
Set up an equation by adding the two angle expressions and equating them to 180.
4
Combine like terms: 5x + 40 = 180
Simplify the equation by combining the 'x' terms and the constant terms.
5
Subtract 40 from both sides: 5x = 180 - 40 = 140
Isolate the term with 'x' by subtracting 40 from both sides of the equation.
6
Divide by 5: x = 140 / 5 = 28
Solve for 'x' by dividing both sides by 5.

Answer: 28 (Option B)

Question 5Grade 7 • G
In a school classroom, two aisles intersect, creating vertical angles. One angle measures (4x - 10) degrees, and the other measures (2x + 30) degrees. Find the value of x.
A. 40
B. 10
C. 20
D. 5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Angle relationship: Vertical angles are equal.
Recognize that vertical angles formed by intersecting lines have the same measure.
2
Given angles: (4x - 10) and (2x + 30)
Identify the algebraic expressions for the two vertical angles.
3
Equation: 4x - 10 = 2x + 30
Set the two angle expressions equal to each other, as vertical angles are equal.
4
Subtract 2x from both sides: 2x - 10 = 30
Move all 'x' terms to one side of the equation by subtracting 2x from both sides.
5
Add 10 to both sides: 2x = 30 + 10 = 40
Move all constant terms to the other side of the equation by adding 10 to both sides.
6
Divide by 2: x = 40 / 2 = 20
Solve for 'x' by dividing both sides by 2.

Answer: 20 (Option C)

Question 6Grade 7 • G
In a science experiment, three laser beams intersect at a single point, forming angles of 100 degrees, (x + 30) degrees, and (2x + 50) degrees. What is the measure of the largest angle?
A. 60 degrees
B. 90 degrees
C. 100 degrees
D. 170 degrees
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Angle relationship: Angles around a point sum to 360 degrees.
Understand that all angles that meet at a central point add up to 360 degrees.
2
Given angles: 100, (x + 30), and (2x + 50)
Identify the measures of all three angles, including the algebraic expressions.
3
Equation: 100 + (x + 30) + (2x + 50) = 360
Set up an equation by adding all angle expressions and equating them to 360.
4
Combine like terms: 3x + 180 = 360
Simplify the equation by combining the 'x' terms and the constant terms.
5
Subtract 180 from both sides: 3x = 360 - 180 = 180
Isolate the term with 'x' by subtracting 180 from both sides.
6
Divide by 3: x = 180 / 3 = 60
Solve for 'x' by dividing both sides by 3.
7
Calculate each angle: Angle 1 = 100 degrees Angle 2 = x + 30 = 60 + 30 = 90 degrees Angle 3 = 2x + 50 = 2(60) + 50 = 120 + 50 = 170 degrees
Substitute the value of 'x' back into the expressions to find the measure of each angle.
8
Largest angle = 170 degrees
Identify the largest angle from the calculated measures.

Answer: 170 degrees (Option D)

A. 8.75 degrees
B. 48.75 degrees

D. 70 degrees
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Angle relationship: Complementary angles sum to 90 degrees.
Understand that the two angles are complementary, meaning their sum is 90 degrees.
2
Given angles: (5x + 5) and (3x + 15)
Identify the algebraic expressions for the two angles.
3
Equation: (5x + 5) + (3x + 15) = 90
Set up an equation by adding the two angle expressions and equating them to 90.
4
Combine like terms: 8x + 20 = 90
Simplify the equation by combining the 'x' terms and the constant terms.
5
Subtract 20 from both sides: 8x = 90 - 20 = 70
Isolate the term with 'x' by subtracting 20 from both sides of the equation.
6
Divide by 8: x = 70 / 8 = 8.75

7
Calculate each angle: Angle 1 = 5x + 5 = 5(8.75) + 5 = 43.75 + 5 = 48.75 degrees Angle 2 = 3x + 15 = 3(8.75) + 15 = 26.25 + 15 = 41.25 degrees
Substitute the value of 'x' back into the expressions to find the measure of each angle.
8
Smaller angle = 41.25 degrees
Identify the smaller angle from the calculated measures.

Question 8Grade 7 • G
At 3:00, the minute hand and hour hand of a clock form a right angle. Imagine a decorative line extending directly opposite the hour hand. What is the angle formed between this decorative line and the minute hand? (Assume the hands are fixed at 3:00 for the purpose of the problem).
A. 180 degrees
B. 270 degrees
C. 90 degrees
D. 45 degrees
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial angle: At 3:00, the hour hand is on 3 and the minute hand is on 12. These form a 90-degree angle.
Determine the initial angle between the hour and minute hands at 3:00.
2
Decorative line: The decorative line is directly opposite the hour hand. This means it extends from the 3, through the center, towards the 9.

3
Angle relationship: The minute hand is at 12, and the decorative line is pointing at 9. The angle between 9 and 12 on a clock is 90 degrees.
Visualize the positions on the clock face to find the angle between the decorative line and the minute hand.

Question 9Grade 7 • G
A city planner is analyzing population density on a map. Two streets intersect, forming a straight line. Angle A is (4y + 20) degrees, and Angle B is (2y + 10) degrees. Angle C is complementary to Angle B. What is the measure of Angle C?
A. 25 degrees
B. 30 degrees
C. 60 degrees
D. 120 degrees
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Angle relationship 1: Angles A and B form a straight line, so they are supplementary (sum to 180 degrees).
Identify that angles A and B form a linear pair.
2
Equation for A and B: (4y + 20) + (2y + 10) = 180
Set up an equation for angles A and B, summing to 180.
3
Combine like terms: 6y + 30 = 180
Simplify the equation by combining 'y' terms and constant terms.
4
Subtract 30 from both sides: 6y = 180 - 30 = 150
Isolate the 'y' term by subtracting 30 from both sides.
5
Divide by 6: y = 150 / 6 = 25
Solve for 'y' by dividing both sides by 6.
6
Calculate Angle B: Angle B = 2y + 10 = 2(25) + 10 = 50 + 10 = 60 degrees
Substitute the value of 'y' back into the expression for Angle B.
7
Angle relationship 2: Angle C is complementary to Angle B, so Angle C + Angle B = 90 degrees.
Understand that complementary angles sum to 90 degrees.
8
Calculate Angle C: Angle C = 90 - Angle B = 90 - 60 = 30 degrees
Subtract Angle B from 90 degrees to find Angle C.

Answer: 30 degrees (Option B)

Question 10Grade 7 • G
In a technological device, two parallel circuit traces are intersected by a third trace. The angle formed at the top-left intersection is (5z + 10) degrees, and the angle formed at the bottom-left intersection is (3z + 50) degrees. These are consecutive interior angles. What is the measure of the top-left angle?
A. 15 degrees
B. 95 degrees
C. 85 degrees
D. 120 degrees
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Angle relationship: Consecutive interior angles (between parallel lines and a transversal) are supplementary, summing to 180 degrees.
Understand that when a transversal intersects parallel lines, consecutive interior angles add up to 180 degrees.
2
Given angles: (5z + 10) and (3z + 50)
Identify the algebraic expressions for the two consecutive interior angles.
3
Equation: (5z + 10) + (3z + 50) = 180
Set up an equation by adding the two angle expressions and equating them to 180.
4
Combine like terms: 8z + 60 = 180
Simplify the equation by combining the 'z' terms and the constant terms.
5
Subtract 60 from both sides: 8z = 180 - 60 = 120
Isolate the term with 'z' by subtracting 60 from both sides of the equation.
6
Divide by 8: z = 120 / 8 = 15
Solve for 'z' by dividing both sides by 8.
7
Calculate the top-left angle: 5z + 10 = 5(15) + 10 = 75 + 10 = 85 degrees
Substitute the value of 'z' back into the expression for the top-left angle.

Answer: 85 degrees (Option C)
