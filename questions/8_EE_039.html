Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A store is having a sale where all items are 25% off. If a customer buys 3 identical shirts, each costing 's' dollars, which expression is equivalent to the total cost after the discount?
A. 2.25s
B. 3.25s
C. 2.75s
D. 0.75s
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total cost = 3s - 0.25(3s)
The initial cost of 3 shirts is 3s. The discount is 25% of 3s, which is 0.25(3s). To find the final cost, subtract the discount from the initial cost.
2
0.25 * 3s = 0.75s
Multiply the discount percentage (0.25) by the total cost of the shirts (3s) to find the total discount amount.
3
3s - 0.75s = 2.25s
Combine the like terms 3s and -0.75s by subtracting their coefficients (3 - 0.75).

Answer: 2.25s (Option A)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
In a basketball game, a player scores 'x' points from free throws (1 point each), 'y' points from field goals (2 points each), and 'z' points from three-pointers (3 points each). Which expression is equivalent to the player's total score?
A. 6(x + y + z)
B. x + 2y + 3z
C. 6xyz
D. x + y + z
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
1 * x = x
Each free throw is 1 point, so 'x' free throws give x points.
2
2 * y = 2y
Each field goal is 2 points, so 'y' field goals give 2y points.
3
3 * z = 3z
Each three-pointer is 3 points, so 'z' three-pointers give 3z points.
4
x + 2y + 3z
Add the points from each type of shot to get the total score. These are unlike terms, so they cannot be combined further.

Answer: x + 2y + 3z (Option B)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A cyclist travels at a constant speed of 18 miles per hour for 'h' hours. Then, due to traffic, they reduce their speed by 3 miles per hour for an additional 2 hours. Which expression is equivalent to the total distance the cyclist traveled?
A. 18h + 36
B. 18h + 15
C. 3h + 30
D. 18h + 30
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total distance = 18h + (18 - 3)(2)
Distance is speed multiplied by time. The first part is 18h. The second part is (original speed - speed reduction) * additional time.
2
18 - 3 = 15
Calculate the new speed after the reduction.
3
15 * 2 = 30
Calculate the distance traveled in the second part of the journey.
4
18h + 30
Add the distance from the first part and the second part. These are unlike terms, so they cannot be combined further.

Answer: 18h + 30 (Option D)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE
A teacher buys 's' boxes of pens for her students, and each box contains 15 pens. She also buys 8 individual pens. After giving out 5 pens per student to 'x' students, how many pens are left? Which expression is equivalent to the number of pens remaining?
A. 10s + 8x
B. 15s + 8 - 5x
C. 15s + 3x
D. 20sx + 8
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
15s + 8
's' boxes with 15 pens each gives 15s pens. Adding 8 individual pens gives 15s + 8.
2
5x
Each of 'x' students receives 5 pens, so 5x pens are given out.
3
15s + 8 - 5x
Subtract the pens given out from the total pens bought.
4
15s, 8, and -5x are all unlike terms.
Since there are no like terms, the expression is already in its simplest form.

Answer: 15s + 8 - 5x (Option B)

Question 6Grade 8 • EE
A chemist prepares a solution by mixing 'c' milliliters of chemical A, '2c' milliliters of chemical B, and 12 milliliters of chemical C. In another experiment, she uses half of this solution. Which expression is equivalent to the amount of solution used in the second experiment?
A. 3c + 6
B. 1.5c + 6
C. 2.5c + 6
D. 1.5c + 12
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total solution = c + 2c + 12
Sum the amounts of each chemical to find the total volume of the solution.
2
c + 2c = 3c
Combine the terms with 'c'.
3
0.5(3c + 12)
Substitute the combined like terms back into the expression for half the solution.
4
0.5 3c + 0.5 12
Multiply 0.5 by each term inside the parentheses.
5
1.5c + 6
Calculate 0.5 3c and 0.5 12.

Answer: 1.5c + 6 (Option B)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A carpenter has a wooden plank that is 20 feet long. He cuts off two pieces. One piece is 'x' feet long, and the other is 3 feet longer than 'x'. Which expression is equivalent to the length of the plank remaining after these two cuts?
A. 17 - x
B. 17 - 2x
C. 23 - 2x
D. 17 + 2x
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Remaining length = 20 - (x + (x + 3))
Start with the total length and subtract the sum of the lengths of the two cut pieces. The first piece is 'x', the second is x + 3.
2
x + x = 2x
Combine the variable terms for the total length of the cut pieces.
3
20 - (2x + 3)
Substitute 2x back into the expression for the total length of the cut pieces.
4
20 - 2x - 3
When a minus sign precedes parentheses, change the sign of each term inside the parentheses.
5
20 - 3 = 17
Combine the numerical values.
6
17 - 2x
The expression now consists of unlike terms and cannot be simplified further.

Answer: 17 - 2x (Option B)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
An employee's weekly pay is calculated based on a regular rate of 'r' dollars per hour for the first 40 hours, and an overtime rate of 1.5 times the regular rate for any hours worked beyond 40. If the employee works 'h' hours in a week (where h > 40), which expression is equivalent to their total weekly pay?
A. 1.5rh - 40r
B. 40r + 1.5h - 40
C. 1.5rh - 20r
D. 1.5rh + 20r
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total Pay = 40r + (h - 40)(1.5r)
The pay for regular hours is 40r. The overtime hours are h - 40, and the overtime rate is 1.5r.
2
(h - 40)(1.5r) = h 1.5r - 40 1.5r
Multiply 1.5r by each term inside the parentheses.
3
1.5rh - 60r
Calculate 40 * 1.5r.
4
40r + 1.5rh - 60r
Replace the distributed overtime term back into the total pay expression.
5
40r - 60r = -20r
Combine the terms that have 'r' as the variable part.
6
1.5rh - 20r
Rearrange the terms for clarity, often with the rh term first.

Answer: 1.5rh - 20r (Option C)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
The population of Town A increases by 500 people each year from an initial population of 'P'. The population of Town B decreases by 200 people each year from an initial population that is twice Town A's initial population. Which expression is equivalent to the difference in population (Town A - Town B) after 'y' years?
A. 3P + 700y
B. -P + 300y
C. -P + 700y
D. 3P + 300y
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
P_A = P + 500y
Initial population 'P' plus 500 people multiplied by 'y' years.
2
P_B = 2P - 200y
Initial population 2P minus 200 people multiplied by 'y' years.
3
Difference = (P + 500y) - (2P - 200y)
Subtract the expression for Town B's population from Town A's population.
4
P + 500y - 2P + 200y
Change the sign of each term inside the parentheses that are being subtracted.
5
(P - 2P) + (500y + 200y)
Group terms with 'P' and terms with 'y'.
6
-P + 700y
P - 2P = -P and 500y + 200y = 700y.

Answer: -P + 700y (Option C)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A company offers two data plans. Plan A charges a flat fee of $35 plus $0.10 per gigabyte (g) used. Plan B charges a flat fee of $15 plus $0.25 per gigabyte (g) used. Which expression is equivalent to the difference in cost (Plan A - Plan B)?
A. 20 + 0.35g
B. 50 - 0.15g
C. 20 - 0.15g
D. 50 + 0.35g
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost_A = 35 + 0.10g
Flat fee ($35) plus cost per gigabyte ($0.10g).
2
Cost_B = 15 + 0.25g
Flat fee ($15) plus cost per gigabyte ($0.25g).
3
Difference = (35 + 0.10g) - (15 + 0.25g)
Subtract Plan B's cost expression from Plan A's cost expression.
4
35 + 0.10g - 15 - 0.25g
Change the sign of each term inside the parentheses that are being subtracted.
5
(35 - 15) + (0.10g - 0.25g)
Group constant terms and terms with 'g'.
6
20 - 0.15g
35 - 15 = 20 and 0.10g - 0.25g = -0.15g.

Answer: 20 - 0.15g (Option C)
