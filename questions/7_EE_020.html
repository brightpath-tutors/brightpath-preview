Question 1Grade 7 • EE
A square-shaped coin has an area of 49 square millimeters. What is the length of one side of the coin?
A. 49 mm
B. 98 mm
C. 7 mm
D. 24.5 mm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Area of a square = side × side = side²
To find the side length when the area is known, we need to find the square root of the area.
2
side² = 49 side = √49
The side length is the square root of the area.
3
side = 7
Since 7 × 7 = 49, the square root of 49 is 7. The side length is 7 millimeters.

Answer: 7 mm (Option C)

Question 4Grade 7 • EE
The principal wants to put a square-shaped poster on the wall. The poster covers an area of 225 square centimeters. What is the perimeter of the poster?
A. 112.5 cm
B. 450 cm
C. 60 cm
D. 15 cm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Area = side² 225 = side² side = √225 side = 15 cm
The side length is the square root of the area. Since 15 × 15 = 225, the side length is 15 cm.
2
Perimeter = 4 × side Perimeter = 4 × 15 Perimeter = 60 cm
