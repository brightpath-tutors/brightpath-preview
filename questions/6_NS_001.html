Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A bakery has 3/4 of a block of cheese. Each cake recipe requires 1/8 of a block of cheese. How many cake recipes can be made with the available cheese, in simplest form?
A. 3/32 recipes
B. 1/6 recipes
C. 6 recipes
D. 10 2/3 recipes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
To find out how many recipes can be made, divide the total amount of cheese by the amount needed per recipe: (3/4) ÷ (1/8).
Division is used to determine how many times one quantity fits into another.
2
Keep the first fraction (3/4), change the division sign to multiplication, and flip the second fraction (1/8 becomes 8/1). (3/4) × (8/1)
Dividing by a fraction is equivalent to multiplying by its reciprocal. This is the 'keep, change, flip' method.
3
(3 × 8) / (4 × 1) = 24/4
To multiply fractions, multiply the numerators together and multiply the denominators together.
4
24 ÷ 4 = 6
Divide the numerator by the denominator to get the whole number result. Ensure the answer is in simplest form.

Answer: 6 recipes (Option C)

Question 4Grade 6 • NS
A car used 7/10 of its fuel tank to travel a certain distance. If each 1/5 of the fuel tank can take the car 30 miles, how many "1/5-tank" segments of fuel were used, in simplest form?
A. 7/50 segments
B. 9/10 segments
C. 2/7 segments
D. 3 1/2 segments
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Divide the total fuel used by the size of each segment: (7/10) ÷ (1/5).
The information about '30 miles' is extra and not needed to solve for the number of fuel segments. We need to find how many 1/5 portions are in 7/10.
2
Keep (7/10), change ÷ to ×, flip (1/5) to (5/1). (7/10) × (5/1)
This is the 'keep, change, flip' rule for dividing fractions.
3
(7 × 5) / (10 × 1) = 35/10
Multiply the numerators and the denominators.
4
35/10 = 7/2 = 3 1/2
Simplify the fraction by dividing both numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor (5), then convert the improper fraction to a mixed number.

Answer: 3 1/2 segments (Option D)

Question 8Grade 6 • NS
A clock's minute hand moves 3/5 of a full circle in 1 hour. How many hours would it take for the minute hand to move 4 1/2 full circles, in simplest form?
A. 45/6 hours
B. 2 7/10 hours
C. 7 1/2 hours
D. 10/27 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Divide the total number of full circles by the number of circles per hour: 4 1/2 ÷ 3/5.
This is a division problem to find how many times the hourly movement fits into the total movement.
2
4 1/2 = (4 × 2 + 1) / 2 = 9/2. The problem becomes (9/2) ÷ (3/5).
Mixed numbers must be converted to improper fractions before dividing or multiplying.
3
Keep (9/2), change ÷ to ×, flip (3/5) to (5/3). (9/2) × (5/3)
Apply the 'keep, change, flip' rule.
4
(9 × 5) / (2 × 3) = 45/6
Multiply the numerators and the denominators.
5
45/6 = (45 ÷ 3) / (6 ÷ 3) = 15/2 = 7 1/2
Simplify the improper fraction by dividing the numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor (3), then convert to a mixed number.

Answer: 7 1/2 hours (Option C)

Question 10Grade 6 • NS
A software update downloads at a speed of 2 1/3 gigabytes per minute. If the total update size is 10 1/2 gigabytes, how many minutes will the update take to download, in simplest form?
A. 12 5/6 minutes
B. 4 1/2 minutes
C. 2/9 minutes
D. 24 1/2 minutes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Divide the total update size by the download speed: 10 1/2 ÷ 2 1/3.
This is a division problem because you are finding how many 'portions' of download speed fit into the total update size.
2
10 1/2 = (10 × 2 + 1) / 2 = 21/2. 2 1/3 = (2 × 3 + 1) / 3 = 7/3. The problem becomes (21/2) ÷ (7/3).
Mixed numbers must always be converted to improper fractions before performing multiplication or division.
3
Keep (21/2), change ÷ to ×, flip (7/3) to (3/7). (21/2) × (3/7)
Applying the 'keep, change, flip' rule to divide fractions.
4
(21 × 3) / (2 × 7) = 63/14
Multiply the numerators and the denominators.
5
63/14 = (63 ÷ 7) / (14 ÷ 7) = 9/2 = 4 1/2
Simplify the improper fraction by dividing both the numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor (7), then convert to a mixed number.

Answer: 4 1/2 minutes (Option B)
