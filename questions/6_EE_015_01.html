Question 1Grade 6 • EE
Liam had $25 in his wallet. After he earned some money from walking dogs, he now has $43. How much money did Liam earn?
A. $18
B. $78
C. $68
D. $22
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'e' be the amount of money Liam earned. Initial money + earned money = total money $25 + e = $43
Identify the known values and set up the addition equation to represent the situation.
2
To find 'e', subtract the initial money from the total money: e = $43 - $25
Use the inverse operation of addition, which is subtraction, to isolate the variable 'e'.
3
e = $18
Perform the subtraction to find the amount Liam earned.

Answer: $18 (Option A)

Question 9Grade 6 • EE
A company manufactured some smartphones in January. In February, they manufactured 23,750 more smartphones than in January, bringing the total number of smartphones manufactured in both months to 118,200. How many smartphones did they manufacture in January?
A. 47,225 smartphones
B. 118,200 smartphones
C. 141,950 smartphones
D. 70,725 smartphones
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'j' be the number of smartphones manufactured in January. Then, smartphones in February = j + 23,750. Total smartphones = smartphones in January + smartphones in February 118,200 = j + (j + 23,750)
Define variables for January and February production, then set up the total production equation.
2
118,200 = 2j + 23,750
Combine like terms in the equation.
3
Subtract 23,750 from both sides: 118,200 - 23,750 = 2j 94,450 = 2j
Isolate the term with 'j' by using the inverse operation of addition (subtraction).
4
Divide both sides by 2: j = 94,450 / 2
Use the inverse operation of multiplication (division) to solve for 'j'.
5
j = 47,225 smartphones
Perform the division to find the number of smartphones manufactured in January.

Answer: 94,450 smartphones (Option A)

Question 10Grade 6 • EE
A digital clock displayed 03:45 AM. Some time passed, and it now displays 07:10 AM. How much time has passed in minutes?
A. 205 minutes
B. 325 minutes
C. 4 hours 35 minutes
D. 3 hours 25 minutes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Convert both times to minutes from midnight: 03:45 AM = (3 * 60) + 45 = 180 + 45 = 225 minutes 07:10 AM = (7 * 60) + 10 = 420 + 10 = 430 minutes
Convert both start and end times into a single unit (minutes) to simplify calculations.
2
Let 't' be the time passed in minutes. Start time in minutes + time passed = end time in minutes 225 + t = 430
Set up an addition equation to represent the relationship between the start time, time passed, and end time.
3
To find 't', subtract the start time in minutes from the end time in minutes: t = 430 - 225
Use subtraction, the inverse of addition, to isolate the variable 't'.
4
t = 205 minutes
Perform the subtraction to calculate the total time passed in minutes.

Answer: 205 minutes (Option A)
