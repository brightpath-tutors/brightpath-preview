Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A student is saving money for a new video game. They have already saved $25 and need to save at least $70 but no more than $100. Write a compound inequality to represent the amount of money, \(m\), the student still needs to save.
A. $25 \le m \le 100$
B. $70 \le m \le 100$
C. $45 < m < 75$
D. $45 \le m \le 75$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The student has $25. They need at least $70 and no more than $100. Let \(m\) be the additional money needed.
Identify the given information and what needs to be represented by the variable.
2
Minimum needed: $25 + m \ge 70$ Maximum allowed: $25 + m \le 100$
Translate 'at least' to \(\ge\) and 'no more than' to \(\le\).
3
$25 + m \ge 70 \implies m \ge 70 - 25 \implies m \ge 45$ $25 + m \le 100 \implies m \le 100 - 25 \implies m \le 75$
Subtract 25 from both sides of each inequality to isolate \(m\).
4
$45 \le m \le 75$
Since \(m\) must be greater than or equal to 45 AND less than or equal to 75, combine them into a single compound inequality.

Answer: $45 \le m \le 75$ (Option D)
