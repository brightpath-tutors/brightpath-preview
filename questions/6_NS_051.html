Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A baker uses 3/4 of a cup of sugar for a batch of cookies. If he has a total of 6 cups of sugar, how many batches of cookies can he make?
A. 4.5 batches
B. 8 batches
C. 1/8 batch
D. 2 batches
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The problem asks how many 3/4-cup portions are in 6 cups, which means we need to divide: 6 ÷ 3/4.
To find out how many times one quantity fits into another, division is the appropriate operation.
2
6 can be written as 6/1.
Any whole number can be expressed as a fraction by placing it over 1.
3
Keep the first fraction (6/1), change the division sign to multiplication, and flip (find the reciprocal of) the second fraction (3/4 becomes 4/3). The expression becomes: 6/1 × 4/3.
Dividing by a fraction is equivalent to multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of a fraction is found by swapping its numerator and denominator.
4
(6 × 4) / (1 × 3) = 24/3.
To multiply fractions, multiply the numerators together and multiply the denominators together.
5
24/3 = 8.
Divide the numerator by the denominator to simplify the improper fraction to a whole number.
6
The baker can make 8 batches of cookies.
The problem asked for the number of batches, so we include the unit in the final answer.

Answer: 8 batches (Option B)

Question 7Grade 6 • NS

2

3

Multiply the numerators and multiply the denominators.
5
70/4 = 35/2 = 17 1/2.
Simplify the improper fraction by dividing the numerator by the denominator. The quotient is the whole number part, and the remainder becomes the numerator of the fractional part.
6
The question asks for 'how many full pieces'. From 17 1/2, we take the whole number part.

7
He can cut 17 full pieces from the beam.
