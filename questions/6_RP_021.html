Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A grocery store sells 12 oranges for $4. What is the ratio of oranges to dollars in simplest form, if the price is scaled down to $1?
A. 3 oranges : $1
B. 4 oranges : $1
C. 6 oranges : $2
D. 12 oranges : $1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The initial ratio is 12 oranges : $4.
This is the given information in the problem.
2
We want to scale the price down from $4 to $1. To do this, we divide $4 by 4. So, the scaling factor is 4.
To find the equivalent ratio with $1, we need to divide the dollar amount by itself, which is 4.
3
Divide both the number of oranges and the dollar amount by 4: 12 oranges ÷ 4 = 3 oranges $4 ÷ 4 = $1 The new ratio is 3 oranges : $1.
To maintain proportionality, whatever operation is applied to one part of the ratio must be applied to the other part.
4
The ratio is 3 oranges : $1.
The ratio is already in simplest form as $1 is the unit amount.

Answer: 3 oranges : $1 (Option A)

Question 6Grade 6 • RP
A chemist used 48 milliliters of solution to perform 6 experiments. If they want to perform 2 experiments, what is the scaled down ratio of solution to experiments in simplest form?
A. 8 ml : 2 experiments
B. 48 ml : 2 experiments
C. 16 ml : 2 experiments
D. 24 ml : 2 experiments
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The initial ratio is 48 ml : 6 experiments.
This is the given information in the problem.
2
Divide both parts of the ratio by 6: 48 ml ÷ 6 = 8 ml 6 experiments ÷ 6 = 1 experiment Unit rate: 8 ml : 1 experiment.
Finding the unit rate (amount per 1 experiment) helps in scaling to any number of experiments.
3
We want to find the ratio for 2 experiments. Multiply the unit rate by 2: 8 ml × 2 = 16 ml 1 experiment × 2 = 2 experiments The new ratio is 16 ml : 2 experiments.
Multiply both parts of the unit rate by the target number of experiments (2) to find the scaled ratio.
4
The ratio is 16 ml : 2 experiments.
The ratio 16:2 can be simplified to 8:1, but the question asks for the ratio for 2 experiments, so 16 ml : 2 experiments is the requested form.

Answer: 16 ml : 2 experiments (Option C)
