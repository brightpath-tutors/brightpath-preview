Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A chef has 3/2 cups of sugar. If each recipe serving requires 1/4 cup of sugar, how many servings can the chef make?
A. 2/3 servings
B. 1/6 servings
C. 3/8 servings
D. 6 servings
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
To find out how many 1/4-cup servings are in 3/2 cups, we need to divide the total amount of sugar by the amount per serving.
Division is used to determine how many times one quantity is contained within another.
2
3/2 ÷ 1/4 = 3/2 × 4/1
Dividing by a fraction is equivalent to multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of 1/4 is 4/1.
3
3/2 × 4/1 = (3 × 4) / (2 × 1) = 12/2
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.
4
12/2 = 6
Divide the numerator by the denominator to get the final whole number.

Answer: 6 servings (Option D)

Question 4Grade 6 • NS
A cyclist completed a 15/4 mile race, with each lap being 3/8 miles long. How many laps did the cyclist complete, in simplest form?
A. 32/45 laps
B. 45/32 laps
C. 10 laps
D. 1/10 laps
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
To find the number of laps, divide the total race distance by the length of one lap.
Division is used to find how many times one quantity is contained in another.
2
15/4 ÷ 3/8 = 15/4 × 8/3
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of 3/8 is 8/3.
3
(15 × 8) / (4 × 3) = 120/12 = 10
Multiply the numerators and the denominators. Then simplify the resulting fraction. Alternatively, cross-simplify before multiplying: (15/3) × (8/4) = 5 × 2 = 10.

Answer: 10 laps (Option C)

Question 5Grade 6 • NS
A car traveled 25/3 kilometers in 5/6 of an hour. What was its average speed in kilometers per hour, in simplest form?
A. 10 km/h
B. 1/10 km/h
C. 125/18 km/h
D. 18/125 km/h
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Speed is calculated by dividing distance by time. So, we need to divide 25/3 km by 5/6 hours.
The formula for average speed is Distance ÷ Time.
2
25/3 ÷ 5/6 = 25/3 × 6/5
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of 5/6 is 6/5.
3
(25 × 6) / (3 × 5) = 150/15 = 10
Multiply the numerators and the denominators. Simplify the resulting fraction. Cross-simplifying (25/5) × (6/3) = 5 × 2 = 10 is an efficient way.

Answer: 10 km/h (Option A)

Question 7Grade 6 • NS

D. 1 12/13 beakers
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students

1
To find out how many beakers can be filled, divide the total volume of solution by the volume per beaker.
Division is used to determine how many equal parts are in a whole.

Question 8Grade 6 • NS
A worker spent 4 1/2 hours completing a task. If each identical sub-task took 3/4 of an hour, how many sub-tasks were completed?
A. 6 sub-tasks
B. 1/6 sub-tasks
C. 8/27 sub-tasks
D. 27/8 sub-tasks
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
4 1/2 = (4 × 2 + 1) / 2 = 9/2
To convert a mixed number to an improper fraction, multiply the whole number by the denominator, add the numerator, and place the result over the original denominator.
2
To find the number of sub-tasks, divide the total time spent by the time per sub-task.
This is a division problem: Total Time ÷ Time per Sub-task.
3
9/2 ÷ 3/4 = 9/2 × 4/3
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of 3/4 is 4/3.
4
(9 × 4) / (2 × 3) = 36/6 = 6
Multiply the numerators and the denominators. Simplify the resulting fraction. Cross-simplifying (9/3) × (4/2) = 3 × 2 = 6 is efficient.

Answer: 6 sub-tasks (Option A)

Question 9Grade 6 • NS
A hospital clinic has 6 3/5 bottles of disinfectant. Each patient room requires 2/10 of a bottle for daily cleaning. How many patient rooms can be disinfected, in simplest form?
A. 33 patient rooms
B. 1/33 patient rooms
C. 1 8/25 patient rooms
D. 25/33 patient rooms
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
6 3/5 = (6 × 5 + 3) / 5 = 33/5
Multiply the whole number by the denominator, add the numerator, and keep the same denominator.
2
To find the number of rooms, divide the total disinfectant by the amount needed per room.
This is a division problem: Total Disinfectant ÷ Disinfectant per Room.
3
33/5 ÷ 2/10 = 33/5 × 10/2
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of 2/10 is 10/2.
4
(33 × 10) / (5 × 2) = 330/10 = 33
Multiply the numerators and the denominators. Simplify the resulting fraction. Cross-simplifying (33/1) × (10/10) = 33 × 1 = 33, or (33/5) × (5/1) = 33 × 1 = 33 is efficient.

Answer: 33 patient rooms (Option A)
