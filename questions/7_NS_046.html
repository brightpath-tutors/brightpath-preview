Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A shopper notices a discount that is represented by the repeating decimal 0.222... of the original price. What is this discount as a fraction in simplest form?
A. 2/10
B. 2/9
C. 1/5
D. 1/9
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The repeating decimal is 0.2̅.
The line over the '2' indicates that only the digit '2' repeats infinitely.
2
Let x = 0.2̅
Assign a variable to the repeating decimal to set up algebraic manipulation.
3
Multiply x by 10 because one digit repeats: 10x = 2.2̅
Multiplying by 10 shifts the decimal point one place to the right, aligning the repeating part after the decimal.
4
10x = 2.2̅ - x = 0.2̅ ---------------- 9x = 2
Subtracting the original equation (x) from the multiplied equation (10x) cancels out the repeating decimal part, leaving an integer on the right side.
5
x = 2/9
Divide both sides by 9 to find the value of x as a fraction.
6
The fraction 2/9 is already in simplest form.
Check if the numerator and denominator share any common factors other than 1. In this case, they do not.

Answer: 2/9 (Option B)

Question 4Grade 7 • NS
A car's fuel gauge shows that it has consumed 0.1666... of its tank capacity. What fraction of the tank has been consumed, in simplest form?
A. 1/6
B. 15/100
C. 1/9
D. 16/99
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The repeating decimal is 0.16̅. The digit '1' is non-repeating, and the digit '6' is repeating.
The line over the '6' indicates that only the digit '6' repeats infinitely.
2
Let x = 0.16̅
Assign a variable to the repeating decimal.
3
Multiply x by 10: 10x = 1.6̅
This step ensures that only the repeating part is after the decimal point in the new equation.
4
Multiply 10x by 10 (or x by 100): 100x = 16.6̅
Multiplying again by 10 (or by 100 from the start) shifts the repeating block one full cycle past the decimal.
5
100x = 16.6̅ - 10x = 1.6̅ ---------------- 90x = 15
Subtract the equation where the repeating part starts (10x) from the equation where the repeating part aligns after shifting (100x). This eliminates the repeating decimal portion.
6
x = 15/90
Divide both sides by 90 to express x as a fraction.
7
Divide both numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor, 15: 15 ÷ 15 = 1 90 ÷ 15 = 6 So, x = 1/6.
Simplify the fraction by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common factor to get the simplest form.

Answer: 1/6 (Option A)

Question 8Grade 7 • NS
An athlete completes a lap in 1.41666... minutes. What is this time as a mixed number in simplest form?
A. 1 and 5/12
B. 1 and 416/999
C. 1 and 375/900
D. 1 and 416/990
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Whole number part = 1 Repeating decimal part = 0.416̅
Handle the integer part and the fractional part separately.
2
Let y = 0.416̅ Multiply by 10 to move the non-repeating part past the decimal: 10y = 4.16̅ Multiply by 1000 to move the entire repeating block past the decimal: 1000y = 416.6̅ Subtract the equations where the repeating part aligns: 1000y = 416.6̅ - 10y = 4.16̅ ---------------- 990y = 412.5 (Wait, check subtraction: 416 - 4 = 412, so 990y = 412.5 is not right. It should be 416.6̅ - 4.16̅ = (416-4)/990 = 412/990. No, it's (416-41)/900 = 375/900. Let's redo this step carefully.) Correct calculation for 0.416̅: Let y = 0.416̅ 10y = 4.16̅ (non-repeating '4' is before decimal) 100y = 41.6̅ (repeating '6' is before decimal, but the part after decimal is not aligned with original y) 100y = 41.6̅ (This is 10 times 10y, but 10y = 4.1666..., so 100y = 41.6666...) Let's use the standard method: Let x = 0.416̅ 100x = 41.6̅ (moves non-repeating part '41' before decimal) 1000x = 416.6̅ (moves repeating part '6' to just after decimal) 1000x - 100x = 416.6̅ - 41.6̅ 900x = 375 x = 375/900
Set up equations to isolate the repeating part. Multiply the decimal by a power of 10 to move the non-repeating digits before the decimal. Multiply again to move one full repeating block before the decimal. Subtract these two equations to cancel out the repeating decimal portion.
3
The fraction is 375/900. Both are divisible by 25: 375 ÷ 25 = 15, 900 ÷ 25 = 36. So, 15/36. Both are divisible by 3: 15 ÷ 3 = 5, 36 ÷ 3 = 12. So, 5/12.
Simplify the fraction by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common factors until it is in simplest form.
4
The mixed number is 1 and 5/12.
Add the simplified fraction back to the whole number part to get the final mixed number.

Answer: 1 and 5/12 (Option A)
