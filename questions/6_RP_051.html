Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A bakery uses a special blend of sugar and flour for its cookies. The ratio of sugar to flour is 5:8. If a large batch of dough requires 72 ounces of flour, how many ounces of sugar are needed to maintain the same ratio?
A. 115 ounces
B. 40 ounces
C. 45 ounces
D. 80 ounces
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Sugar : Flour = 5 : 8
The problem states the ratio of sugar to flour is 5:8.
2
5/8 = x/72
We need to find an amount of sugar (x) that is proportional to 72 ounces of flour, maintaining the 5:8 ratio.
3
72 ÷ 8 = 9
To find how many times the flour amount (8) was multiplied to get the new flour amount (72), divide 72 by 8. This 'scaling factor' will be applied to the sugar amount as well.
4
5 × 9 = 45
Multiply the sugar part of the ratio (5) by the scaling factor (9) to find the required ounces of sugar.
5
45 ounces of sugar
To maintain the 5:8 ratio, 45 ounces of sugar are needed for 72 ounces of flour.

Answer: 45 ounces (Option C)

Question 2Grade 6 • RP
A basketball team's season record shows a ratio of wins to losses of 13:5. If the team has played 54 games in total, how many games did they win?
A. 41 games
B. 39 games
C. 15 games
D. 27 games
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
13 (wins) + 5 (losses) = 18 parts
Add the number of parts representing wins and losses to find the total number of parts in the ratio, which corresponds to the total number of games played.
2
54 games ÷ 18 parts = 3 games per part
Divide the total number of games played (54) by the total number of ratio parts (18) to find how many games each 'part' of the ratio represents.
3
13 (wins parts) × 3 (games per part) = 39 wins
Multiply the number of parts representing wins (13) by the value of one part (3 games) to find the total number of games won.
4
The team won 39 games.
Following the ratio and total games, the team won 39 games.

Answer: 39 games (Option B)

D. 540 ml olive oil, 270 ml soy sauce

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Olive oil : Balsamic vinegar : Soy sauce = 4 : 3 : 2
The problem provides the ratio of the three ingredients.
2
360 ml (balsamic vinegar) ÷ 3 (balsamic vinegar ratio part) = 120 ml per part
Since we know the actual amount of balsamic vinegar (360 ml) and its corresponding ratio part (3), we can find out how many milliliters one 'part' of the ratio represents.
3
4 (olive oil ratio part) × 120 ml (per part) = 480 ml
Multiply the olive oil's ratio part (4) by the value of one part (120 ml) to find the required amount of olive oil.
4
2 (soy sauce ratio part) × 120 ml (per part) = 240 ml
Multiply the soy sauce's ratio part (2) by the value of one part (120 ml) to find the required amount of soy sauce.
5
480 ml olive oil, 240 ml soy sauce
To maintain the given ratio with 360 ml of balsamic vinegar, 480 ml of olive oil and 240 ml of soy sauce are needed.

Question 5Grade 6 • RP

A. 13 additional teachers
B. 10 additional teachers
C. 1 additional teacher
D. 2 additional teachers
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
24 students/teacher × 11 teachers = 264 students
Multiply the current student-to-teacher ratio (24:1) by the number of current teachers (11) to find the total number of students in the school.
2
264 students ÷ 22 students/teacher = 12 teachers
To achieve the desired ratio of 22 students per teacher, divide the total number of students (which remains constant) by the new student ratio part (22).
3
12 teachers (new needed) - 11 teachers (current) = 1 additional teacher
Subtract the current number of teachers from the number of teachers needed for the new ratio to find how many additional teachers must be hired.
4
1 additional teacher needs to be hired.
To change the ratio from 24:1 to 22:1 with the same number of students, one more teacher is needed.

Answer: 1 additional teacher (Option C)

2

The estimated population of the new district would be 4320 residents.

Question 10Grade 6 • RP
A data transfer service can upload 750 megabytes of data in 5 minutes. At this rate, how many gigabytes of data can be uploaded in 1 hour? (Note: 1 gigabyte = 1000 megabytes)
A. 90 gigabytes
B. 7.5 gigabytes
C. 9 gigabytes
D. 12 gigabytes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
1 hour = 60 minutes
Since the data transfer rate is given in minutes, convert the total time (1 hour) into minutes to match the units.
2
750 MB ÷ 5 minutes = 150 MB per minute
Divide the total megabytes uploaded by the time taken to find the rate of data transfer per minute.
3
150 MB/minute × 60 minutes = 9000 MB
Multiply the data transfer rate (megabytes per minute) by the total minutes in 1 hour to find the total data transferred in megabytes.
4
9000 MB ÷ 1000 MB/GB = 9 GB
Since 1 gigabyte equals 1000 megabytes, divide the total megabytes (9000) by 1000 to convert the data amount into gigabytes.
5
9 gigabytes of data can be uploaded in 1 hour.
Following all conversions and calculations, 9 gigabytes can be uploaded in one hour.

Answer: 9 gigabytes (Option C)
