Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A baker has 7/8 of a pound of chocolate chips. If each cookie recipe requires 1/4 of a pound of chocolate chips, how many cookie recipes can the baker make in simplest form?
A. 32/7
B. 7/2
C. 3 1/2
D. 7/32
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
To find how many recipes can be made, divide the total amount of chocolate chips by the amount needed per recipe: (7/8) ÷ (1/4).
We need to divide the total quantity by the quantity per unit.
2
(7/8) × (4/1)
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.
3
(7 × 4) / (8 × 1) = 28/8
Multiply straight across: numerator by numerator, denominator by denominator.
4
28/8 = (28 ÷ 4) / (8 ÷ 4) = 7/2
Find the greatest common divisor (GCD) of 28 and 8, which is 4, and divide both numerator and denominator by it.
5
7 ÷ 2 = 3 with a remainder of 1. So, 7/2 = 3 1/2.
Divide the numerator by the denominator. The quotient is the whole number, and the remainder over the original denominator is the fraction.

Answer: 3 1/2 (Option C)

Question 9Grade 7 • NS
A region has a total area of 24 1/2 square miles. 7/10 of this area is urbanized. The remaining rural area is to be developed into plots for small communities. If each community plot requires 3/20 of a square mile, how many community plots can be created in the rural area in simplest form?
A. 14.7
B. 147/60
C. 490/3
D. 49
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
1 - 7/10 = 3/10.
If 7/10 is urbanized, the remaining (1 - 7/10) is rural.
2
24 1/2 = (24 × 2 + 1) / 2 = 49/2.
Convert the mixed number to an improper fraction for calculation.
3
(49/2) × (3/10) = 147/20 square miles.
Multiply the total area by the rural fraction.
4
(147/20) ÷ (3/20).

5
(147/20) × (20/3).

6

Question 10Grade 7 • NS
A computer has 15 1/4 gigabytes of free storage space. If a new operating system requires 2 3/8 gigabytes, and each system update takes up 1/16 of the original free space, how many system updates can be installed after the operating system, in simplest form?
A. 13 31/61
B. 12 115/512
C. 61/64
D. 103/8
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
15 1/4 = (15 × 4 + 1) / 4 = 61/4. 2 3/8 = (2 × 8 + 3) / 8 = 19/8.
Convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions for easier calculation.
2
(61/4) - (19/8). To subtract, find a common denominator (8): (61/4) = (61 × 2) / (4 × 2) = 122/8. (122/8) - (19/8) = (122 - 19) / 8 = 103/8 gigabytes.
Subtract the OS space from the total free space.
3
(1/16) × (61/4) = 61/64 gigabytes.
Multiply the fraction for one update by the original total free space.
4
To find how many updates can be installed, divide the remaining space by the space required per update: (103/8) ÷ (61/64).
We need to divide the remaining quantity by the quantity per unit.
5
(103/8) × (64/61).
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.
6
(103 × 64) / (8 × 61) = (103 × 8) / 61 = 824/61.
Simplify by dividing 64 and 8 by their greatest common divisor (8), then multiply the remaining numbers.
7
824 ÷ 61 = 13 with a remainder of 31. So, 13 31/61.
Divide the numerator by the denominator. The quotient is the whole number, and the remainder over the original denominator is the fraction.

Answer: 13 31/61 (Option A)
