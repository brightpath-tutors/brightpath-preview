Question 1Grade 9 • N-RN
A shop offers a discount where an item costs 1/2 of its original price. If the original price was $4/5 of a dollar, what is the discounted price as a single fraction in simplest form?
A. 4/5
B. 5/7
C. 2/5
D. 8/5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Fraction of original price = 1/2
Identify the first rational number representing the discount factor.
2
Original price = 4/5
Identify the second rational number representing the original price.
3
Discounted price = (1/2) * (4/5)
Multiply the two rational numbers to find the discounted price.
4
Discounted price = (1 4) / (2 5) = 4/10
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.
5
Simplify 4/10: Divide numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 2. (4 ÷ 2) / (10 ÷ 2) = 2/5
Simplify the resulting fraction to its simplest form.

Answer: 2/5 (Option C)
