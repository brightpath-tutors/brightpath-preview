Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A plumber charges a flat fee of $50 for a service call plus $30 per hour for labor. If the total cost of a service call was $140, write an equation in point-slope form that represents the cost of the service, given a point (x1, y1) as (3, 140).
A. y - 140 = 30(x - 3)
B. y - 140 = 50(x - 3)
C. y + 140 = 30(x + 3)
D. y - 3 = 30(x - 140)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The rate per hour is the slope, so m = 30. The given point is (3, 140).
The problem states the plumber charges $30 per hour, which is a rate of change, representing the slope. A point (3, 140) means 3 hours of labor costs $140.
2
Point-slope form: y - y1 = m(x - x1) y - 140 = 30(x - 3)
Substitute m = 30, x1 = 3, and y1 = 140 into the point-slope form equation.

Answer: y - 140 = 30(x - 3) (Option A)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
