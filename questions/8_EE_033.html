Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A grocery store sells a bag of oranges for $2.50. Write an expression to represent the total cost of buying o bags of oranges.
A. 2.50 + o
B. o - 2.50
C. 2.50o
D. o / 2.50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost per bag = $2.50 Number of bags = o
We need to find an expression for the total cost based on these values.
2
Total cost = Cost per bag × Number of bags
This is a fundamental relationship for calculating total cost based on unit price and quantity.
3
Total cost = 2.50 × o = 2.50o
The expression 2.50o accurately represents the total cost of buying 'o' bags of oranges.

Answer: 2.50o (Option C)

E. None of the above

Initial stickers = s

Stickers after giving away = s - 3

Expression = s - 3 + 10

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A construction worker lays b bricks per hour. If they work for 8 hours but take a 0.5-hour break, write an expression for the total number of bricks laid.
A. b * (8 + 0.5)
B. b * (8 - 0.5)
C. 8b - 0.5
D. 8b
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Rate = b bricks/hour Scheduled hours = 8 hours Break time = 0.5 hours
We need an expression for the total bricks laid, considering the break.
2
Actual working hours = 8 hours - 0.5 hours = (8 - 0.5) hours
Bricks are only laid during actual working hours, not during breaks.
3
Total bricks = Rate × Actual working hours
This is a standard rate-time-quantity relationship.
4
Expression = b × (8 - 0.5)
The parentheses are crucial here to ensure that the actual working hours are calculated before multiplying by the brick-laying rate.

Answer: b * (8 - 0.5) (Option B)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A website charges a monthly fee of $15 plus $0.05 for every gigabyte of data used. If a user uses g gigabytes of data and also pays a one-time setup fee of $10, write an expression for their total cost in the first month. Simplify your answer.
A. 15g + 0.05 + 10
B. 15 + 0.05 + 10g
C. 15 + 0.05g - 10
D. 0.05g + 25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Monthly fee = $15 Setup fee = $10
These are the initial and recurring base charges.
2
Cost per gigabyte = $0.05 Gigabytes used = g Variable cost = $0.05 × g = 0.05g
This part of the cost depends directly on the quantity of data consumed.
3
Total cost = Monthly fee + Variable cost + Setup fee Total cost = 15 + 0.05g + 10
The total cost is the sum of all individual charges.
4
Total cost = (15 + 10) + 0.05g Total cost = 25 + 0.05g
Combining like terms (constants in this case) is an important step in simplifying algebraic expressions.

Answer: 0.05g + 25 (Option D)
