Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A store sells 3 pencils for $0.75. Complete the ratio table to find the cost of 6 pencils.
A. $1.00
B. $2.25
C. $1.50
D. $1.25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
To go from 3 pencils to 6 pencils, we multiply by 2 (6 / 3 = 2).
We need to find a scaling factor that relates the given number of pencils to the desired number of pencils.
2
Multiply the cost of 3 pencils by 2: $0.75 × 2 = $1.50.
In a ratio table, both quantities must be scaled by the same factor to maintain proportionality.

Answer: $1.50 (Option C)

Step-by-step solution
