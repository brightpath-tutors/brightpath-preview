Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A scientist is observing the acceleration of a new particle in a lab. The acceleration is measured as √48 units/s². Simplify this radical expression to find the most precise value.
A. 4√3 units/s²
B. 2√12 units/s²
C. 16√3 units/s²
D. 4√12 units/s²
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The radicand is 48. Perfect square factors of 48 are 4 and 16. The largest is 16.
To simplify a radical, we look for the largest perfect square that divides the radicand evenly.
2
√48 = √(16 × 3)
We express 48 as the product of its largest perfect square factor (16) and the remaining factor (3).
3
√(16 × 3) = √16 × √3
The square root of a product is equal to the product of the square roots.
4
√16 × √3 = 4 × √3
Calculate the square root of 16, which is 4.
5
4 × √3 = 4√3
The simplified form is 4√3.

Answer: 4√3 units/s² (Option A)
