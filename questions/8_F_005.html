B. 60 points
C. 50 points
D. 110 points
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Points per question (rate) = 4 Bonus points (initial value) = 10
The problem provides the rate at which points are earned per correct answer and a fixed starting amount of bonus points.
2
Let S be the total score and q be the number of correct answers. S = 4q + 10
The total score is found by multiplying the points per question by the number of correct answers and then adding the bonus points.

S = 4 × 15 + 10 S = 60 + 10 S = 70 points
First, multiply the points per question by the number of correct answers, then add the bonus points according to the order of operations.

Question 5Grade 8 • F
A liquid's temperature starts at 20°C and decreases by 2°C every minute. What will the temperature be after 8 minutes?
A. 18°C
B. 36°C
C. 4°C
D. 12°C
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial temperature = 20°C Decrease rate = 2°C per minute
The problem provides the starting temperature (initial value) and the constant rate at which the temperature changes (rate of decrease).
2
Let T be the temperature and m be the number of minutes. T = 20 - 2m
The temperature starts at 20°C and decreases, so we subtract 2°C for each minute that passes.
3
T = 20 - 2 × 8 T = 20 - 16 T = 4°C
First, multiply the rate of decrease by the number of minutes, then subtract that product from the initial temperature.

Answer: 4°C (Option C)

Question 8Grade 8 • F
A small town has a population of 1,200 people. Its population increases by 50 people each year. In how many years will the population reach 1,550 people?
A. 8 years
B. 7 years
C. 31 years
D. 6 years
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial population = 1200 Rate of increase = 50 people/year
The problem provides the starting population (y-intercept) and the constant rate of population growth (slope).
2
Let P be the population and y be the number of years. P = 50y + 1200
The population is the initial population plus the increase over time (rate × years).
3
1550 = 50y + 1200 1550 - 1200 = 50y 350 = 50y y = 350 / 50 y = 7 years
First, subtract the initial population from both sides to isolate the term with 'y', then divide by the rate of increase to find the number of years.

Answer: 7 years (Option B)

A. 20 hours
B. 22 hours
C. 47 hours
D. 18 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Monthly fee = $5 Hourly rate = $0.20
The problem provides a fixed monthly cost (y-intercept) and a variable cost per hour of streaming (slope).
2
Let C be the total cost and h be the number of streaming hours. C = 0.20h + 5
The total cost is the sum of the hourly charge (rate × hours) and the fixed monthly fee.
3
9.40 = 0.20h + 5 9.40 - 5 = 0.20h 4.40 = 0.20h h = 4.40 / 0.20 h = 22 hours
First, subtract the monthly fee from both sides, then divide by the hourly rate to find the number of streaming hours.

Answer: 22 hours (Option B)

Question 10Grade 8 • F
A fitness tracker estimates that a person burns 300 calories by walking for 60 minutes. If the person burns calories at a constant rate, and they have already burned 50 calories this morning, how many additional minutes do they need to walk to burn a total of 250 calories?
A. 60 minutes
B. 50 minutes
C. 40 minutes
D. 30 minutes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Rate = 300 calories / 60 minutes = 5 calories/minute
Divide the total calories burned by the total minutes to find the rate of calorie burning per minute.
2
Initial calories burned = 50 calories
This is the amount of calories already burned, serving as the initial value for our linear function.
3
Let C be the total calories burned and m be the additional minutes walked. C = 5m + 50
The total calories burned is the sum of calories from additional walking (rate × minutes) and the calories already burned.
4
250 = 5m + 50 250 - 50 = 5m 200 = 5m m = 200 / 5 m = 40 minutes
First, subtract the initial calories from both sides to find the calories still needed, then divide by the rate to find the additional minutes required.

Answer: 40 minutes (Option C)
