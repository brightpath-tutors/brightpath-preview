Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A grocery store sells 12 oranges for $3. What is the ratio of oranges to dollars, in simplest form?
A. 4:1
B. 3:12
C. 1:4
D. 12:3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Oranges: 12 Dollars: 3
Identify the quantities for the ratio. The problem asks for the ratio of oranges to dollars.
2
Ratio: 12 oranges : 3 dollars
Form the ratio using the identified quantities in the correct order.
3
GCF of 12 and 3 is 3. 12 ÷ 3 = 4 3 ÷ 3 = 1
To simplify the ratio 12:3, find the greatest common factor (GCF) of 12 and 3, which is 3. Divide both parts of the ratio by the GCF.
4
Simplified Ratio: 4:1
The ratio of oranges to dollars in simplest form is 4:1.

Answer: 4:1 (Option A)

Question 9Grade 7 • RP
In a town with 18,000 residents, 4,500 people own pets. What is the ratio of pet owners to non-pet owners, in simplest form?
A. 4:1
B. 1:3
C. 1:4
D. 45:135
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total residents: 18,000 Pet owners: 4,500
Identify the total number of residents and the number of pet owners.
2
Non-pet owners = 18,000 - 4,500 = 13,500
Calculate the number of non-pet owners by subtracting the number of pet owners from the total residents.
3
Ratio: 4,500 pet owners : 13,500 non-pet owners
Form the ratio of pet owners to non-pet owners.
4
GCF of 4,500 and 13,500 is 4,500. 4,500 ÷ 4,500 = 1 13,500 ÷ 4,500 = 3
To simplify the ratio 4,500:13,500, find the greatest common factor (GCF). Since 13,500 is 3 times 4,500, the GCF is 4,500. Divide both parts of the ratio by the GCF.
5
Simplified Ratio: 1:3
The ratio of pet owners to non-pet owners in simplest form is 1:3.

Answer: 1:3 (Option B)
