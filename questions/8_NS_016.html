Question 1Grade 8 • NS
Sarah bought a toy for $5.75. She paid with a $10 bill. How much change should she receive?
A. $4.25
B. $4.75
C. $15.75
D. $5.25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total paid = $10.00, Cost of toy = $5.75
To find the change, we subtract the cost of the toy from the amount paid.
2
$10.00 - $5.75
Align the decimal points vertically to ensure correct place value subtraction.
3
$10.00 - $ 5.75 --------- $ 4.25
Subtract from right to left, borrowing from the tens and ones places as needed.

Answer: $4.25 (Option A)

Question 2Grade 8 • NS
A runner completed 7/8 of a mile of a track. If the full track is 9/8 of a mile long, how much more of the track does the runner need to complete in simplest form?
A. 2/0 mile
B. 16/8 mile
C. 1/4 mile
D. 2/16 mile
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total length = 9/8 mile, Distance completed = 7/8 mile
To find the remaining distance, subtract the completed distance from the total length.
2
9/8 - 7/8
Since the denominators are already the same, we can proceed with subtracting the numerators.
3
(9 - 7) / 8 = 2/8
Subtract the top numbers (numerators) and keep the bottom number (denominator) the same.
4
2/8 = 1/4
Divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common factor, which is 2.

Answer: 1/4 mile (Option C)

Question 3Grade 8 • NS
A recipe calls for 3 1/4 cups of water. If you have already added 1 3/4 cups, how much more water do you need in simplest form?
A. 1 1/4 cups
B. 2 1/2 cups
C. 2 2/4 cups
D. 1 1/2 cups
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
3 1/4 - 1 3/4
To find out how much more water is needed, subtract the amount already added from the total amount required.
2
Since 1/4 is less than 3/4, borrow 1 (which is 4/4) from the whole number 3. This makes 3 1/4 become 2 (4/4 + 1/4) = 2 5/4.
You cannot subtract 3/4 from 1/4 directly, so you need to 'borrow' from the whole number part of the first mixed number.
3
2 5/4 - 1 3/4 = (2-1) + (5/4 - 3/4) = 1 + 2/4
Subtract the whole numbers and the fractional parts separately.
4
1 2/4 = 1 1/2
The fraction 2/4 can be simplified by dividing both the numerator and denominator by 2.

Answer: 1 1/2 cups (Option D)

Question 4Grade 8 • NS
A submarine started at an elevation of -12.5 meters relative to sea level. It then descended an additional 8.75 meters. What is its new elevation in meters?
A. 3.75 meters
B. -3.75 meters
C. -21.25 meters
D. 21.25 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Starting elevation = -12.5 meters, Descended = 8.75 meters
Descending means moving further down, which corresponds to subtracting a positive value or adding a negative value.
2
-12.5 - 8.75
This can be thought of as combining two negative movements.
3
-12.5 + (-8.75)
Subtracting a positive number is the same as adding its negative counterpart.
4
12.5 + 8.75 = 21.25. Since both numbers are negative, the result is negative. So, -21.25.
When adding two negative numbers, add their absolute values and keep the negative sign.

Answer: -21.25 meters (Option C)

Question 5Grade 8 • NS
A student's math grade changed from 7/10 to 4/5 over the semester. What is the change in grade in simplest form? (Final grade - Initial grade)
A. 1/10
B. 1/5
C. 3/5
D. 11/10
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Final grade = 4/5, Initial grade = 7/10
To find the change, subtract the initial grade from the final grade.
2
4/5 - 7/10
We need to find a common denominator before subtracting these fractions.
3
The least common multiple of 5 and 10 is 10.
10 is the smallest number that both 5 and 10 divide into evenly.
4
4/5 = (4 2) / (5 2) = 8/10
Multiply the numerator and denominator of 4/5 by 2 to get an equivalent fraction with a denominator of 10. The fraction 7/10 already has the common denominator.
5
8/10 - 7/10 = (8 - 7) / 10 = 1/10
Now that the denominators are the same, subtract the numerators and keep the common denominator.

Answer: 1/10 (Option A)

D. 7 1/4 °C
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
15 1/2 - 7 3/4
To find the new temperature, subtract the amount it dropped from the starting temperature.
2
The least common multiple of 2 and 4 is 4.
Convert 1/2 to an equivalent fraction with a denominator of 4: 1/2 = 2/4. So the problem becomes 15 2/4 - 7 3/4.
3
Since 2/4 is less than 3/4, borrow 1 (which is 4/4) from the whole number 15. This makes 15 2/4 become 14 (4/4 + 2/4) = 14 6/4.
You cannot subtract 3/4 from 2/4 directly, so you need to 'borrow' from the whole number part of the first mixed number.
4
14 6/4 - 7 3/4 = (14 - 7) + (6/4 - 3/4) = 7 + 3/4
Subtract the whole numbers and the fractional parts separately.
5
7 3/4
The fraction 3/4 is already in simplest form.

Answer: 7 3/4 °C (Option C)

Question 8Grade 8 • NS

A. 1 3/4 days
B. 1 1/4 days
C. 2 1/4 days
D. 2 3/4 days
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Actual duration = 6 1/4 days, Scheduled duration = 4 1/2 days
To find how much longer the project took, subtract the scheduled duration from the actual duration.
2
6 1/4 - 4 1/2
We need to find a common denominator for the fractions before proceeding.
3
The least common multiple of 4 and 2 is 4. Convert 1/2 to 2/4. So the problem becomes 6 1/4 - 4 2/4.
This allows for proper comparison and subtraction of the fractional parts.
4
Since 1/4 is less than 2/4, borrow 1 (which is 4/4) from the whole number 6. This makes 6 1/4 become 5 (4/4 + 1/4) = 5 5/4.
Regrouping is necessary when the fraction being subtracted is larger than the initial fraction.
5
5 5/4 - 4 2/4 = (5 - 4) + (5/4 - 2/4) = 1 + 3/4
Subtract the whole numbers and the fractional parts separately.
6
1 3/4
The fraction 3/4 is already in simplest form.

Answer: 1 3/4 days (Option A)

4 3/8
