Step-by-step solution
1
The balances are 15, -20, and 0.
Extract the numerical values from the problem statement.
2
Negative numbers are less than zero, and zero is less than positive numbers. On a number line, numbers increase as you move from left to right.
Recall the basic principles of ordering integers, especially the positions of negative, zero, and positive numbers relative to each other.
3
- -20 is the only negative number, so it's the smallest. - 0 is neither positive nor negative, so it comes after -20 but before 15. - 15 is the only positive number, so it's the largest.

The temperatures are -18, -5, and -25.

A. 150 BC, 50 BC, 100 AD, 200 AD
B. 200 AD, 100 AD, 50 BC, 150 BC
C. 100 AD, 200 AD, 50 BC, 150 BC
D. 50 BC, 150 BC, 100 AD, 200 AD
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
- 150 BC = -150 - 200 AD = 200 - 50 BC = -50 - 100 AD = 100
Convert BC dates to negative integers and AD dates to positive integers, with earlier dates having smaller numerical values.
2
The integers to order are -150, 200, -50, 100.
List all the integer representations.
3
- Negative (BC): -150, -50 - Positive (AD): 200, 100
Group the BC dates (negative) and AD dates (positive) as a first step in ordering.
4
-150 < -50.
For BC dates, a larger number (e.g., 150 BC) represents an earlier time, which corresponds to a more negative (smaller) integer value.
5
100 < 200.
For AD dates, a smaller number represents an earlier time.
6
Negative (BC) numbers come before positive (AD) numbers. So, the combined integer order is -150, -50, 100, 200.
All BC dates are chronologically before all AD dates. Combine the ordered groups.
7
150 BC, 50 BC, 100 AD, 200 AD.
