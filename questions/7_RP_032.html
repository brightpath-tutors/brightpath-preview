B. $1.40
C. $0.60
D. $0.50
E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1
4 pencils for $2.80
We are given the total cost for a certain number of pencils.
2
Cost / Number of pencils = $2.80 / 4 pencils
To find the cost of one pencil, we divide the total cost by the number of pencils.
3
$2.80 / 4 = $0.70

Question 3Grade 7 • RP
A recipe calls for 3 cups of water for every 2 cups of rice. If you use 6 cups of rice, how many cups of water do you need?
A. 8 cups
B. 4 cups
C. 12 cups
D. 9 cups
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
3 cups water : 2 cups rice
This is the established ratio of water to rice.
2
3/2 = x/6
We set up a proportion where 'x' represents the unknown amount of water for 6 cups of rice.
3
6 cups rice / 2 cups rice = 3
To go from 2 cups of rice to 6 cups of rice, we multiply by 3.
4
3 cups water * 3 = 9 cups water
Since the amount of rice is tripled, the amount of water must also be tripled to maintain the proportion.

Answer: 9 cups (Option D)

E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
240 miles / 8 gallons = 30 miles/gallon
We find how many miles the car can travel on one gallon of gasoline.
2
Total miles = Unit rate × Gallons
This formula relates total miles, unit rate, and gallons needed.
3
390 miles = 30 miles/gallon × x gallons
We substitute the known values into the equation to solve for the unknown number of gallons (x).
4
x = 390 / 30 = 13 gallons
Dividing the total desired miles by the unit rate gives the number of gallons required.

Answer: 13 gallons (Option B)

Question 5Grade 7 • RP
In a classroom, there are 3 students for every 2 computers. If there are 18 students in the class, how many computers are there?
A. 27 computers
B. 10 computers
C. 9 computers
D. 12 computers
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
3 students : 2 computers
This is the fixed ratio between students and computers.
2
3/2 = 18/x
We set up a proportion where 'x' represents the unknown number of computers for 18 students.
3
3 × x = 2 × 18
To solve a proportion, we cross-multiply the numerator of one ratio by the denominator of the other.
4
3x = 36
Perform the multiplication on the right side of the equation.
5
x = 36 / 3 = 12 computers
Divide both sides by 3 to isolate 'x' and find the number of computers.

Answer: 12 computers (Option D)

Question 8Grade 7 • RP
A clock gains 15 seconds every 2 hours. If the clock is set accurately at 12:00 PM on Monday, what time will it show at 12:00 PM on the following Wednesday, assuming it continues to gain time at the same proportional rate?
A. 12:08 PM
B. 12:15 PM
C. 12:06 PM
D. 12:03 PM
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
2 days × 24 hours/day = 48 hours
There are 2 full days between Monday 12:00 PM and Wednesday 12:00 PM.
2
15 seconds / 2 hours = 7.5 seconds/hour
Determine the rate at which the clock gains time.
3
7.5 seconds/hour × 48 hours = 360 seconds
Multiply the hourly gain rate by the total elapsed hours.
4
360 seconds / 60 seconds/minute = 6 minutes
Since there are 60 seconds in a minute, divide the total seconds gained by 60.
5
12:00 PM + 6 minutes = 12:06 PM
The clock will show the actual time plus the time it has gained.

Answer: 12:06 PM (Option C)

Question 9Grade 7 • RP
A small town's population grows at a constant rate. In 2010, the population was 5,000. In 2015, it was 5,750. What is the projected population in 2025, assuming the growth rate remains proportional?
A. 7,000 people
B. 7,500 people
C. 6,500 people
D. 7,250 people
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
5,750 - 5,000 = 750 people
Find the total increase in population during the first period.
2
2015 - 2010 = 5 years
Calculate the number of years over which the initial growth occurred.
3
750 people / 5 years = 150 people/year
Divide the total growth by the number of years to find the growth per year.
4
2025 - 2015 = 10 years
Calculate the number of years for the projection period.
5
150 people/year × 10 years = 1,500 people
Multiply the annual growth rate by the projection period to find the total projected increase.
6
5,750 + 1,500 = 7,250 people
Add the projected increase to the most recent known population (2015) to get the final projected population.

Answer: 7,250 people (Option D)

Question 10Grade 7 • RP
A new 3D printer can print 5 small objects in 15 minutes. How many hours would it take to print 240 similar objects, assuming the printing rate is constant? Express your answer as a decimal.
A. 8 hours
B. 15 hours
C. 12 hours
D. 10 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
15 minutes / 5 objects = 3 minutes/object
First, find out how long it takes to print one single object.
2
3 minutes/object × 240 objects = 720 minutes
Multiply the time per object by the total number of objects to find the total printing time in minutes.
3
720 minutes / 60 minutes/hour = 12 hours
Since there are 60 minutes in an hour, divide the total minutes by 60 to convert to hours.

Answer: 12 hours (Option C)
