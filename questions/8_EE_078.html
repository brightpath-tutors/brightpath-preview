Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A shopper has a budget of $50 for groceries. They have already spent $15. Write and graph an inequality that represents the remaining amount, x, they can spend.
A. A number line with a closed circle at 35 and an arrow pointing to the left.
B. A number line with an open circle at 35 and an arrow pointing to the left.
C. A number line with a closed circle at 65 and an arrow pointing to the left.
D. A number line with a closed circle at 35 and an arrow pointing to the right.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
15 + x <= 50
The total amount spent (already spent $15 plus the remaining amount x) must be less than or equal to the budget of $50.
2
x <= 50 - 15 x <= 35
Subtract 15 from both sides of the inequality to isolate x.
3
Closed circle at 35, arrow pointing left.
Since x is less than or equal to 35, the graph includes 35 (closed circle) and all values smaller than 35 (arrow pointing left).

Answer: A number line with a closed circle at 35 and an arrow pointing to the left. (Option A)

B. A number line with a closed circle at 65 and an arrow pointing to the left.

s >= 40
The speed (s) must be greater than or equal to 40.
2
s <= 65
The speed (s) must be less than or equal to 65.
3
40 <= s <= 65

Closed circles at 40 and 65, line segment connecting them.

Question 5Grade 8 • EE
To pass a course, a student needs an average score of at least 70% on 3 tests. Their scores on the first two tests are 65% and 72%. Write and graph an inequality that represents the score, t, they need on the third test to pass the course.
A. A number line with a closed circle at 73 and an arrow pointing to the left.
B. A number line with an open circle at 73 and an arrow pointing to the right.
C. A number line with a closed circle at 73 and an arrow pointing to the right.
D. A number line with a closed circle at 210 and an arrow pointing to the right.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
(65 + 72 + t) / 3 >= 70
The sum of the three test scores divided by 3 must be greater than or equal to 70.
2
(137 + t) / 3 >= 70 137 + t >= 210 (Multiply both sides by 3) t >= 210 - 137 t >= 73 (Subtract 137 from both sides)
First, sum the known scores. Then, multiply both sides by 3 to clear the denominator. Finally, subtract 137 to isolate t.
3
Closed circle at 73, arrow pointing right.
Since t is greater than or equal to 73, the graph includes 73 (closed circle) and all values larger than 73 (arrow pointing right).

Answer: A number line with a closed circle at 73 and an arrow pointing to the right. (Option C)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A project has a deadline in 3 weeks. A team has already worked 25 hours. If the project requires at least 100 hours of work in total, and they plan to work for the remaining 2 weeks, write and graph an inequality for the minimum average hours per week, h, they need to work for the remaining time.
A. A number line with a closed circle at 37.5 and an arrow pointing to the right.
B. A number line with a closed circle at 37.5 and an arrow pointing to the left.
C. A number line with a closed circle at 100 and an arrow pointing to the right.
D. A number line with an open circle at 37.5 and an arrow pointing to the right.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
3 weeks total - 1 week already passed = 2 weeks remaining.
Since work has already been done, we calculate the remaining time for future work.
2
25 + 2h >= 100
The hours already worked (25) plus the hours worked in the remaining 2 weeks (2h) must be at least 100.
3
2h >= 100 - 25 2h >= 75 h >= 75 / 2 h >= 37.5
First, subtract 25 from both sides. Then, divide by 2 to isolate h.
4
Closed circle at 37.5, arrow pointing right.
Since h is greater than or equal to 37.5, the graph includes 37.5 (closed circle) and all values larger than 37.5 (arrow pointing right).

Answer: A number line with a closed circle at 37.5 and an arrow pointing to the right. (Option A)

Step-by-step solution
1
12000 + 200y < 15000

2
200y < 15000 - 12000 200y < 3000 y < 3000 / 200 y < 15
First, subtract 12000 from both sides. Then, divide by 200 to isolate y.

Open circle at 15, arrow pointing left.
Since y is strictly less than 15, the graph includes an open circle at 15 and an arrow pointing to the left.

Answer: A number line with an open circle at 15 and an arrow pointing to the left. (Option B)

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
100 - 5h > 20
The initial charge (100%) minus the drained charge (5% per hour for 'h' hours) must be greater than 20%.
2
-5h > 20 - 100 -5h > -80 h < -80 / -5 (Divide by -5 and reverse inequality sign) h < 16
First, subtract 100 from both sides. Then, divide by -5, remembering to reverse the inequality sign because you are dividing by a negative number.
3
Open circle at 16, arrow pointing left.
Since h is strictly less than 16, the graph includes an open circle at 16 and an arrow pointing to the left.

Answer: A number line with an open circle at 16 and an arrow pointing to the left. (Option C)
