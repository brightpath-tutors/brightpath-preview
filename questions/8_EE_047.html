Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A clothing store is having a sale where items priced at more than $15 but less than or equal to $40 are eligible for an additional discount. Which compound inequality represents the price range (p) in dollars for items that qualify for the discount?
A. 15 ≤ p < 40
B. 15 > p ≥ 40
C. p < 15 or p ≥ 40
D. 15 < p ≤ 40
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The phrase 'more than $15' means the price (p) must be greater than 15. This can be written as p > 15.
Translating the verbal description into a mathematical inequality.
2
The phrase 'less than or equal to $40' means the price (p) must be less than or equal to 40. This can be written as p ≤ 40.
Translating the verbal description into a mathematical inequality.
3
Since an item must satisfy both conditions to be eligible for the discount, we use the logical connector 'AND'. The compound inequality is p > 15 AND p ≤ 40. This can be written in a compact form as 15 < p ≤ 40.
Combining two inequalities with 'AND' means finding the intersection of their solution sets. The compact form clearly shows the variable 'p' is bounded by two values.

Answer: 15 < p ≤ 40 (Option D)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A train's operating speed (s) in km/h must be at least 150 km/h, but not more than 200 km/h. Additionally, for safety reasons, its speed must be less than 185 km/h. Which compound inequality represents the valid operating speed range?
A. 150 ≤ s < 200 or s < 185
B. 150 ≤ s ≤ 185
C. 150 < s < 185
D. 150 ≤ s < 185
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
'At least 150 km/h' means s ≥ 150. 'Not more than 200 km/h' means s ≤ 200. Combining these with 'AND' gives 150 ≤ s ≤ 200.
Breaking down the initial compound statement into two individual inequalities and combining them with the appropriate logical operator ('AND').
2
'Less than 185 km/h' means s < 185.
Translating the safety constraint into a mathematical inequality.
3
We need to satisfy both the initial range and the safety constraint. So, we combine (150 ≤ s ≤ 200) AND (s < 185). We look for the intersection of these two conditions. The lower bound is still s ≥ 150. For the upper bound, s ≤ 200 and s < 185. The stricter condition is s < 185. Therefore, the combined inequality is 150 ≤ s < 185.
To find the overall valid range, we find the intersection of all given conditions. When multiple upper bounds or lower bounds are present, the most restrictive one applies.

Answer: 150 ≤ s < 185 (Option D)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A construction worker needs to cut a metal rod (l) whose length must be within 3 mm of 250 mm. Which compound inequality represents the acceptable range of lengths for the rod?
A. 247 < l < 253
B. l < 247 or l > 253
C. 247 ≤ l ≤ 253
D. l ≤ 247 or l ≥ 253
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The phrase 'within 3 mm of 250 mm' means the absolute difference between the actual length (l) and the ideal length (250) must be less than or equal to 3. This is written as |l - 250| ≤ 3.
Understanding 'within a certain range of a value' as an absolute value inequality is a key first step.
2
For an absolute value inequality of the form |x| ≤ a, it can be rewritten as -a ≤ x ≤ a. Applying this rule, we get -3 ≤ l - 250 ≤ 3.
This is a standard rule for converting 'less than or equal to' absolute value inequalities into compound inequalities.
3
To isolate l, add 250 to all parts of the inequality: -3 + 250 ≤ l - 250 + 250 ≤ 3 + 250 247 ≤ l ≤ 253.
Performing the same operation (addition) on all three parts of the compound inequality maintains its balance and isolates the variable.

Answer: 247 ≤ l ≤ 253 (Option C)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A stage production's rehearsal period (d) in days is set to be either at most 25 days, or longer than 40 days. However, the total rehearsal period cannot exceed 50 days due to theater availability. Which compound inequality represents the possible rehearsal durations?
A. d ≤ 25 or 40 < d ≤ 50
B. d ≤ 50
C. d ≤ 25 or d > 40
D. 25 < d ≤ 40
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
'At most 25 days' means d ≤ 25. 'Longer than 40 days' means d > 40. These are connected by 'OR': (d ≤ 25 OR d > 40).
Breaking down the initial flexible conditions into a disjunction (OR statement).
2
'Cannot exceed 50 days' means d ≤ 50. This constraint must apply to the entire primary condition.
Identifying a universal constraint that must be true in conjunction with the primary conditions.
3
We need to satisfy (d ≤ 25 OR d > 40) AND (d ≤ 50).
The overall solution must satisfy both the primary OR condition and the universal AND condition.
4
(d ≤ 25 AND d ≤ 50) OR (d > 40 AND d ≤ 50).
Distributing the AND condition across each part of the OR statement simplifies the expression for easier evaluation.
5
For (d ≤ 25 AND d ≤ 50), the stricter condition is d ≤ 25. For (d > 40 AND d ≤ 50), this simplifies to 40 < d ≤ 50.
For an AND condition, the intersection of the intervals is taken. The most restrictive upper and lower bounds determine the new interval.
6
The simplified parts are connected by 'OR': d ≤ 25 OR 40 < d ≤ 50.
Recombining the simplified intervals with the OR operator yields the final compound inequality.

Answer: d ≤ 25 or 40 < d ≤ 50 (Option A)

B. P < 1050 or P > 1350
C. 1050 ≤ P ≤ 1350
D. 1050 < P < 1350
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
This means the difference between P and 1200 must be less than or equal to 150. So, |P - 1200| ≤ 150.
The phrase 'within X of Y' translates to an absolute value inequality |variable - Y| ≤ X.
2
For |x| ≤ a, we have -a ≤ x ≤ a. So, -150 ≤ P - 1200 ≤ 150.
This is a standard rule for converting absolute value inequalities into compound inequalities.
3
Add 1200 to all parts: 1200 - 150 ≤ P - 1200 + 1200 ≤ 1200 + 150, which simplifies to 1050 ≤ P ≤ 1350.
Adding the same value to all parts of a compound inequality maintains its validity and helps isolate the variable.
4
This means P ≠ 1200.
This is an exclusion condition that must be applied to the previously found range.
5
We have 1050 ≤ P ≤ 1350 AND P ≠ 1200. To incorporate P ≠ 1200, we must exclude 1200 from the interval. This splits the interval into two parts: 1050 ≤ P < 1200 OR 1200 < P ≤ 1350.
Excluding a single point from a continuous interval requires splitting the interval into two, using strict inequalities at the excluded point and connecting the two new intervals with 'OR'.

Answer: 1050 ≤ P < 1200 or 1200 < P ≤ 1350 (Option A)
