Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A special artisan bread costs exactly √50 dollars per loaf. Which of the following is the best rational approximation for the cost of one loaf of bread to the nearest dollar?
A. 7
B. 6
C. 7.5
D. 8
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
We know that 7^2 = 49 and 8^2 = 64.
To approximate the square root of a non-perfect square, identify the nearest perfect squares.
2
Since 49 < 50 < 64, it follows that √49 < √50 < √64. Therefore, 7 < √50 < 8.
The square root of 50 must lie between the square roots of 49 and 64.
3
The difference between 50 and 49 is 50 - 49 = 1. The difference between 64 and 50 is 64 - 50 = 14. Since 50 is much closer to 49 than to 64, √50 is closer to 7 than to 8.
Compare the distance of 50 from each perfect square to find the closest integer approximation.
4
The best rational approximation to the nearest dollar is 7. (Using a calculator, √50 ≈ 7.071).
Since 7.071 is less than 7.5, it rounds down to 7.

Answer: 7 (Option A)

A. 3 and 4
B. 22 and 24
C. 4 and 5
D. 5 and 6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
We know that 4^2 = 16. We know that 5^2 = 25.
Find the perfect squares that 'sandwich' the number inside the square root.
2
Since 16 < 23 < 25, it means √16 < √23 < √25.
If a number is between two perfect squares, its square root will be between the square roots of those perfect squares.
3
Therefore, the distance √23 feet lies between 4 and 5.
Substitute the integer values for the perfect square roots.

Locate the nearest perfect squares to the number inside the square root.

Question 5Grade 6 • NS
In a geometry class, students are calculating the length of the diagonal of a square with side length 5 units. The length of the diagonal is exactly √50 units. Which value below is a rational number that is a good approximation of the diagonal's length, rounded to the nearest hundredth?
A. 7.0
B. 7.07
C. 7.1
D. 7.50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
For a square with side length s, the diagonal d is found by s^2 + s^2 = d^2. Given s=5, 5^2 + 5^2 = d^2 \implies 25 + 25 = d^2 \implies d^2 = 50. So, d = √50.
The diagonal of a square forms a right triangle with two sides of the square. Apply the Pythagorean theorem.
2
We know 7^2 = 49 and 8^2 = 64. So √50 is between 7 and 8. Using a calculator, √50 ≈ 7.07106....
Use known perfect squares or a calculator to find the decimal approximation of √50.
3
The digit in the thousandths place is 1, which is less than 5. Therefore, we round down, keeping the hundredths digit as it is. The approximation rounded to the nearest hundredth is 7.07.
Look at the digit in the thousandths place. If it's less than 5, keep the hundredths digit as is. If it's 5 or greater, round up the hundredths digit.

Answer: 7.07 (Option B)

Question 6Grade 6 • NS
A scientist is observing a microscopic organism moving across a petri dish. The total path it takes measures exactly π millimeters in length. If π ≈ 3.14159265..., what is the length of the path rounded to the nearest thousandth of a millimeter?
A. 3.141
B. 3.1416
C. 3.142
D. 3.14
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The given value is π ≈ 3.14159265....
Start with the provided decimal representation of pi.
2
The thousandths place is the third digit after the decimal point, which is 1. The digit immediately to its right (in the ten-thousandths place) is 5.
To round to the thousandths, look at the digit in the ten-thousandths place.
3
Since the digit in the ten-thousandths place (5) is 5 or greater, round up the digit in the thousandths place. So, 3.141 becomes 3.142.
If the 'next' digit is 5 or more, add 1 to the digit in the rounding place. Otherwise, keep it the same.
4
The length of the path rounded to the nearest thousandth is 3.142 millimeters.
The final answer is the rounded decimal.

Answer: 3.142 (Option C)

2

Multiply the time for one swing by the number of swings.

Question 9Grade 6 • NS
A small, isolated population of rare insects grows such that its density (in insects per square meter) is determined by the formula D = √11 / 3. Which of the following numbers accurately describes the nature of this population density?
A. Integer
B. Whole number
C. Rational
D. Irrational
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
11 is not a perfect square (3^2 = 9, 4^2 = 16). Therefore, √11 is an irrational number (its decimal representation is non-terminating and non-repeating).
A square root of a non-perfect square is an irrational number.
2
3 is an integer, and can be expressed as a fraction 3/1. Therefore, 3 is a rational number.
Any integer can be written as a fraction, making it a rational number.
3
The quotient of an irrational number and a non-zero rational number is always an irrational number.
Understand the closure properties of number systems under division.
4
Thus, D = √11 / 3 is an irrational number.
Since the numerator is irrational and the denominator is rational, the result is irrational.

Answer: Irrational (Option D)
