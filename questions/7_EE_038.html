Question 1Grade 7 • EE
A baker is organizing 15 chocolate chip cookies and 20 oatmeal cookies onto trays. What is the greatest number of identical trays the baker can make if each tray must have the same number of each type of cookie?
A. 3 trays
B. 5 trays
C. 4 trays
D. 10 trays
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The quantities are 15 (chocolate chip cookies) and 20 (oatmeal cookies).
We need to find a common factor for both quantities to determine the number of identical trays.
2
Prime factors of 15: 3 * 5 Prime factors of 20: 2 2 5
Breaking numbers down into their prime factors helps identify common factors easily.
3
The common prime factor is 5. GCF(15, 20) = 5
The greatest common factor (GCF) represents the greatest number of identical trays that can be made.
4
The baker can make 5 identical trays.
Each tray will have 15/5 = 3 chocolate chip cookies and 20/5 = 4 oatmeal cookies.

Answer: 5 trays (Option B)
