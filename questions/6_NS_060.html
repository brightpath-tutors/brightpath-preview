Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A shopper's bank account had a balance of $ -25. They then made a withdrawal of $10. What is the new balance?
A. -35
B. 15
C. -15
D. 35
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial balance = -25. Withdrawal = 10. Withdrawal means taking money out, which is a subtraction.
Start by identifying the initial state and the action taking place. A withdrawal reduces the balance, so it's a subtraction.
2
-25 - 10
The initial balance is -25, and we subtract the withdrawal amount of 10.
3
-25 + (-10)
Subtracting a positive number is the same as adding a negative number. Keep the first number, change the subtraction to addition, and change the sign of the second number.
4
|-25| = 25, |-10| = 10 25 + 10 = 35 Since both numbers are negative, the sum is negative.
When adding two negative integers, add their absolute values and keep the negative sign.
5
-35
The new balance in the shopper's account is -35 dollars.

Answer: -35 (Option A)
