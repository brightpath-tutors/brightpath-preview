Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Maya bought a new book for $15. She now has $23 left. How much money did Maya have before buying the book?
A. $53
B. $8
C. $345
D. $38
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' represent the amount of money Maya had before buying the book.
We need to find the initial amount of money.
2
x - 15 = 23
The initial amount ('x') minus the cost of the book ($15) equals the remaining amount ($23).
3
x - 15 + 15 = 23 + 15
To undo the subtraction of 15, add 15 to both sides of the equation.
4
x = 38
Adding 23 and 15 gives us 38. So, Maya had $38 initially.

Answer: $38 (Option D)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
A football team scored 42 points in their last game. If this brought their total season score to 185 points, how many points had they scored before this game?
A. 143
B. 4
C. 133
D. 227
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' represent the points scored before the last game.
We need to find the total score before the most recent game.
2
x + 42 = 185
The points scored before ('x') plus the points from the last game (42) equals the total season score (185).
3
x + 42 - 42 = 185 - 42
To undo the addition of 42, subtract 42 from both sides of the equation.
4
x = 143
Subtracting 42 from 185 gives us 143. So, they had scored 143 points before the last game.

Answer: 143 points (Option A)

Question 3Grade 8 • EE
A recipe calls for 3/4 cup of sugar. If Liam has already added 1/4 cup, how much more sugar does he need to add, in simplest form?
A. 2/4
B. 1/2
C. 1
D. 1/4
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' represent the amount of sugar Liam still needs to add.
We need to find the remaining quantity of sugar.
2
x + 1/4 = 3/4
The amount already added (1/4) plus the amount still needed ('x') equals the total amount required (3/4).
3
x + 1/4 - 1/4 = 3/4 - 1/4
To undo the addition of 1/4, subtract 1/4 from both sides of the equation.
4
x = 2/4
Subtracting 1/4 from 3/4 gives us 2/4.
5
x = 1/2
The fraction 2/4 can be simplified by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, 2.

Answer: 1/2 cup (Option B)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A car traveled 240 miles at a constant speed. If the journey took 4 hours, what was the car's speed in miles per hour?
A. 960
B. 244
C. 236
D. 60
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 's' represent the car's speed in miles per hour.
We need to find the rate at which the car traveled.
2
240 = s × 4 or 4s = 240
The total distance (240 miles) is equal to the speed ('s') multiplied by the time taken (4 hours).
3
4s / 4 = 240 / 4
To undo the multiplication by 4, divide both sides of the equation by 4.
4
s = 60
Dividing 240 by 4 gives us 60. So, the car's speed was 60 mph.

Answer: 60 mph (Option D)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE
In a class, 28 students passed the math test, which was 7/8 of the total number of students. How many students are in the class?
A. 4
B. 24.5
C. 35
D. 32
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 's' represent the total number of students in the class.
We need to find the total count of students.
2
(7/8)s = 28
Seven-eighths of the total number of students ('s') equals 28 students.
3
(8/7) × (7/8)s = 28 × (8/7)
To undo the multiplication by 7/8, multiply both sides of the equation by its reciprocal, 8/7.
4
s = (28/1) × (8/7) = (4 × 7 × 8) / 7 = 4 × 8 = 32
Multiply 28 by 8/7. We can simplify by dividing 28 by 7 first, which is 4, then multiply 4 by 8 to get 32.

Answer: 32 students (Option D)

E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students

1
Let 'x' represent the amount of substance used in the experiment.
We need to find the quantity of substance that was consumed.
2
17.5 - x = 12.3
The initial mass (17.5 grams) minus the amount used ('x') equals the remaining mass (12.3 grams).
3
17.5 - 12.3 = x
To solve for 'x', subtract 12.3 from both sides of the equation, or rearrange it directly to find the difference.
4
x = 5.2

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A construction worker needs to cut a metal rod into 1.5-meter pieces. If he has a total of 18 meters of rod, how many pieces can he cut?
A. 12
B. 27
C. 15
D. 10.5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'p' represent the number of pieces the worker can cut.
We need to find how many equal segments can be made from the total length.
2
1.5p = 18
The length of each piece (1.5 meters) multiplied by the number of pieces ('p') equals the total length of the rod (18 meters).
3
1.5p / 1.5 = 18 / 1.5
To undo the multiplication by 1.5, divide both sides of the equation by 1.5.
4
p = 12
Dividing 18 by 1.5 gives us 12. So, the worker can cut 12 pieces.

Answer: 12 pieces (Option A)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A movie started at 7:10 PM and ended after 'm' minutes. If the movie ended at 9:05 PM, how long was the movie in minutes?
A. 115
B. 195
C. 105
D. 155
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'm' represent the duration of the movie in minutes.
We need to find the total time in minutes from the start to the end of the movie.
2
7:10 PM = 19 hours and 10 minutes = (19 * 60) + 10 = 1140 + 10 = 1150 minutes. 9:05 PM = 21 hours and 5 minutes = (21 * 60) + 5 = 1260 + 5 = 1265 minutes.
Converting times to a single unit (minutes) makes calculation easier. Using 24-hour format simplifies PM conversions.
3
1150 + m = 1265
The start time in minutes plus the movie duration ('m') equals the end time in minutes.
4
m = 1265 - 1150
To find 'm', subtract the start time from the end time.
5
m = 115
Subtracting 1150 from 1265 gives us 115. So, the movie was 115 minutes long.

Answer: 115 minutes (Option A)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A software update downloads at a constant rate. If 2.5 gigabytes have been downloaded and this represents 0.4 of the total update, what is the total size of the update in gigabytes?
A. 6.25
B. 2.9
C. 1
D. 10
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' represent the total size of the update in gigabytes.
We need to find the full size of the download.
2
0.4x = 2.5
0.4 (or 40%) of the total update size ('x') is equal to 2.5 gigabytes.
3
0.4x / 0.4 = 2.5 / 0.4
To undo the multiplication by 0.4, divide both sides of the equation by 0.4.
4
x = 6.25
Dividing 2.5 by 0.4 gives us 6.25. So, the total size of the update is 6.25 gigabytes.

Answer: 6.25 gigabytes (Option A)
