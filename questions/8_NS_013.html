Question 2Grade 8 • NS

A. 6/8 kilometers
B. 1 1/2 kilometers
C. 1 1/4 kilometers
D. 2 3/4 kilometers
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total distance = Length of one lap × Number of laps
The phrase 'completes 2 laps' implies repeating the length of one lap, which is a multiplication scenario.
2
(3/4) × 2 = (3/4) × (2/1)
Any whole number 'n' can be written as 'n/1' to facilitate fraction multiplication.
3
(3 × 2) / (4 × 1) = 6/4
When multiplying fractions, multiply straight across: numerator by numerator, denominator by denominator.
4
6/4 = (6 ÷ 2) / (4 ÷ 2) = 3/2
Simplifying fractions means expressing them in their most reduced form.
5
3/2 = 1 1/2
Divide the numerator (3) by the denominator (2). The quotient (1) is the whole number part, and the remainder (1) becomes the new numerator over the original denominator (2).
6
The final answer is expressed as a mixed number in simplest form.

Answer: 1 1/2 kilometers (Option B)

Question 5Grade 8 • NS

A. 8/11
B. 1/2
C. 25/18
D. 15/30
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Fraction preferring both = (Fraction preferring math) × (Fraction of math students who prefer science)
The phrase '3/5 also enjoy science' refers to 3/5 OF the students who prefer math, indicating multiplication.
2
(5/6) × (3/5)
This directly translates the problem into a fraction multiplication.
3
Using cross-cancellation: (5/6) × (3/5) = (5/5) × (3/6) = 1 × (1/2) = 1/2 Without cross-cancellation: (5 × 3) / (6 × 5) = 15/30
Cross-cancellation simplifies the numbers before multiplication, making the final simplification easier. If not used, multiply numerators and denominators.
4
15/30 = (15 ÷ 15) / (30 ÷ 15) = 1/2
Simplifying ensures the fraction is in its lowest terms.
5
The result is the fraction of the whole class that meets both criteria.

Question 7Grade 8 • NS

D. 4 pieces
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Number of pieces = Total plank length / Length of each piece
This is a division problem that asks 'how many groups of 1/4 are in 7/8'.
2
(7/8) / (1/4)
This directly translates the problem into a fraction division.
3
(7/8) × (4/1)
The rule for dividing fractions is to 'keep, change, flip' (keep the first fraction, change division to multiplication, flip the second fraction).
4
Using cross-cancellation: (7/8) × (4/1) = (7/(8÷4)) × ((4÷4)/1) = (7/2) × (1/1) = 7/2 Without cross-cancellation: (7 × 4) / (8 × 1) = 28/8
Cross-cancellation simplifies the fractions before multiplication, reducing the numbers involved and often making the final simplification step easier.
5
28/8 = (28 ÷ 4) / (8 ÷ 4) = 7/2 7/2 = 3 1/2
An improper fraction should be converted to a mixed number for better understanding of the quantity. Simplifying ensures the fraction part is in its lowest terms.
6
3 1/2 pieces means 3 full pieces.
The question specifically asks for 'full pieces', so any fractional part is discarded.

Answer: 3 pieces (Option B)

C. 3 5/12 hours
D. 3 1/2 hours

Step-by-step solution
1
Actual time = Estimated time × Multiplier
Understanding 'times longer' implies multiplication to find the new duration.
2
2 1/4 = (2 × 4 + 1) / 4 = 9/4 1 2/3 = (1 × 3 + 2) / 3 = 5/3
To convert a mixed number to an improper fraction, multiply the whole number by the denominator, add the numerator, and place the result over the original denominator.
3
(9/4) × (5/3)
The problem is now ready for fraction multiplication.
4
Using cross-cancellation: (9/4) × (5/3) = ((9÷3)/4) × (5/(3÷3)) = (3/4) × (5/1) = 15/4 Without cross-cancellation: (9 × 5) / (4 × 3) = 45/12
Cross-cancellation makes the numbers smaller and easier to work with. Otherwise, multiply straight across.
5
45/12 = (45 ÷ 3) / (12 ÷ 3) = 15/4 15/4 = 3 with a remainder of 3, so 3 3/4.
Divide the numerator by the denominator. The quotient is the whole number, the remainder is the new numerator, and the denominator stays the same. Ensure the fraction part is in simplest form.
6
The final answer is presented as a mixed number in simplest form.

6
The final answer is the calculated actual download time in minutes.
