Question 1Grade 9 • N-RN
A bank calculates compound interest on an investment. If an initial investment grows by a factor of $7^3$ in the first year and then by another factor of $7^4$ in the second year, what is the total growth factor over two years, expressed as a single power of 7?
A. $7^{12}$
B. $49^7$
C. $7^7$
D. $7^1$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial growth factor = $7^3$ Second year growth factor = $7^4$
Identify the given growth factors for each year.
2
Total growth factor = $7^3 \times 7^4$
To find the total growth factor over two years, multiply the growth factors from each year.
3
$7^3 \times 7^4 = 7^{3+4}$
Apply the product of powers property: \(a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}\).
4
$7^{3+4} = 7^7$
Add the exponents to find the final power.

Answer: $7^7$ (Option C)

Question 2Grade 9 • N-RN
During a cycling race, an athlete's power output starts at \(P^{10}\) watts. As the race progresses, their power output decreases by a factor of \(P^6\) per hour. What is the athlete's power output after one hour, expressed as a single power of P?
A. \(P^{16}\)
B. \(P^4\)
C. \(P^6\)
D. $1^4$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial power output = \(P^{10}\) Decrease factor = \(P^6\)
Identify the initial power output and the factor by which it decreases.
2
Power output after one hour = \(P^{10} \div P^6\)
To find the power output after one hour, divide the initial power output by the decrease factor.
3
\(P^{10} \div P^6 = P^{10-6}\)
Apply the quotient of powers property: \(a^m \div a^n = a^{m-n}\).
4
\(P^{10-6} = P^4\)
Subtract the exponents to find the final power.

Answer: \(P^4\) (Option B)

Question 8Grade 9 • N-RN
In a laboratory, the concentration of a chemical solution is given by \((2x^4)^2\). This solution then undergoes a process where its concentration is multiplied by \(x^7\) and finally divided by $4x^6$. What is the final simplified concentration, expressed as a single algebraic term in simplest form?
A. \(x^{21}\)
B. $4x^9$
C. \(x^9\)
D. \(x^{13}\)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Expression: \((2x^4)^2 \cdot x^7 \div (4x^6)\)
Identify the given mathematical expression for the concentration.
2
Simplify \((2x^4)^2\): $2^2 \cdot (x^4)^2 = 4x^{4 \times 2} = 4x^8$
Apply the power of a product and power of a power properties to simplify the first term: \((ab)^m = a^m b^m\) and \((a^m)^n = a^{m \times n}\).
3
Expression becomes: $4x^8 \cdot x^7 \div (4x^6)$
Substitute the simplified term back into the expression.
4
Simplify multiplication: $4x^8 \cdot x^7 = 4x^{8+7} = 4x^{15}$
Apply the product of powers property: \(a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}\).
5
Expression becomes: $4x^{15} \div (4x^6)$
Substitute the simplified product back into the expression.
6
Simplify division: \((4/4) \cdot (x^{15}/x^6)\)
Group coefficients and variable terms for division.
7
\((4/4) = 1\) \((x^{15}/x^6) = x^{15-6} = x^9\)
Divide the coefficients and apply the quotient of powers property: \(a^m \div a^n = a^{m-n}\).
8
Final simplified concentration = $1 \cdot x^9 = x^9$
Combine the simplified terms to get the final answer.

Answer: \(x^9\) (Option C)

2

Question 10Grade 9 • N-RN
A drone delivery system calculates its effective speed. Its base speed is \(v^9\). The navigation system adds a factor of \((v^4)^2\). However, strong wind resistance reduces its speed by a factor of \(v^{14}\). What is the drone's final effective speed, expressed as a single power of v?
A. \(v^{31}\)
B. \(v^3\)
C. \(v^2\)
D. \(v^{28}\)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Base speed = \(v^9\) Navigation factor = \((v^4)^2\) Wind resistance factor = \(v^{14}\)
Identify all given factors for the drone's speed.
2
Simplify navigation factor: \((v^4)^2 = v^{4 \times 2} = v^8\)
Apply the power of a power property: \((a^m)^n = a^{m \times n}\) to the navigation factor.
3
Effective speed = Base speed \(\times\) Navigation factor \(\div\) Wind resistance factor
Set up the expression for the final effective speed.
4
Expression: \(v^9 \cdot v^8 \div v^{14}\)
Substitute the simplified factors into the expression.
5
Simplify multiplication: \(v^9 \cdot v^8 = v^{9+8} = v^{17}\)
Apply the product of powers property to the multiplication part: \(a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}\).
6
Expression becomes: \(v^{17} \div v^{14}\)
Substitute the simplified product back into the expression.
7
Simplify division: \(v^{17-14}\)
Apply the quotient of powers property: \(a^m \div a^n = a^{m-n}\).
8
Final effective speed = \(v^3\)
Subtract the exponents to find the final power.

Answer: \(v^3\) (Option B)
