Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A grocery store sells 3 oranges for $2. If you were to plot this relationship on a graph where the x-axis represents the number of oranges and the y-axis represents the cost in dollars, which ordered pair would represent this specific ratio?
A. (2, 3)
B. (3, 2)
C. (1, 2)
D. (6, 4)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Number of oranges = 3, Cost = $2
The problem states '3 oranges for $2', clearly defining the two quantities and their amounts.
2
x-axis = number of oranges, y-axis = cost in dollars
The problem specifies 'x-axis represents the number of oranges and the y-axis represents the cost in dollars'.
3
(number of oranges, cost) = (3, 2)
An ordered pair is always written as (x-coordinate, y-coordinate). Therefore, (3 oranges, $2) becomes (3, 2).

Answer: (3, 2) (Option B)

C. (20, 15)
D. (20, 30)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Ratio: 10 minutes : 15 pages. x-axis: minutes, y-axis: pages read.
The problem provides the initial ratio and the axis assignments.
2
Desired time = 20 minutes.
The question asks for pages read in 20 minutes.
3
20 minutes / 10 minutes = 2. The time increased by a factor of 2.
Divide the new time by the original time to find the scaling factor.

5
(20, 30)
