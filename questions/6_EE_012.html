Question 1Grade 6 • EE
Sarah wants to buy a new book that costs $15. She currently has $8. How much more money (m) does she need to save to buy the book? Write and solve an inequality.
A. m > 7
B. m ≥ 7
C. m = 7
D. m ≤ 7
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Sarah needs $15. She has $8. She needs to save 'm' more dollars. The total money she has must be at least $15. So, 8 + m ≥ 15.
The phrase 'at least' indicates that the total amount of money must be greater than or equal to $15. We add her current money to the money she needs to save.
2
8 + m ≥ 15 8 + m - 8 ≥ 15 - 8
To solve for 'm', we need to undo the addition of 8. We do this by subtracting 8 from both sides of the inequality to keep it balanced.
3
m ≥ 7
After subtracting, we find that Sarah needs to save at least $7.

Answer: m ≥ 7 (Option B)

p ≥ 15

Question 3Grade 6 • EE
A chef uses 3 scoops of sugar for each batch of cookies. He wants to use no more than 15 scoops of sugar today. How many batches (b) of cookies can he make? Write and solve an inequality.
A. b ≥ 5
B. b ≤ 5
C. b = 5
D. b > 5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Scoops per batch × number of batches ≤ total scoops 3b ≤ 15
The phrase 'no more than' means the total number of scoops must be less than or equal to 15. We multiply the scoops per batch by the number of batches.
2
3b ≤ 15 3b / 3 ≤ 15 / 3
To solve for 'b', we need to undo the multiplication by 3. We divide both sides of the inequality by 3 to keep it balanced.
3
b ≤ 5
The chef can make at most 5 batches of cookies.

Answer: b ≤ 5 (Option B)

D. s > 60

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Speed × Time > Distance s × 3 > 180 (or 3s > 180)
The phrase 'more than' indicates that the product of speed and time must be strictly greater than 180 miles.
2
3s > 180 3s / 3 > 180 / 3
To solve for 's', we need to undo the multiplication by 3. We divide both sides of the inequality by 3 to keep it balanced.
3
s > 60
Mark must maintain an average speed greater than 60 miles per hour.

Answer: s > 60 (Option D)

Question 7Grade 6 • EE
A construction worker needs to cut pieces of wood that are 6 inches long. He has a total of 42 inches of wood. What is the maximum number of pieces (p) he can cut? Write and solve an inequality.
A. p > 7
B. p = 7
C. p ≥ 7
D. p ≤ 7
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Length per piece × number of pieces ≤ total length 6p ≤ 42
The phrase 'maximum number' means the total length used must be less than or equal to 42 inches. We multiply the length of each piece by the number of pieces.
2
6p ≤ 42 6p / 6 ≤ 42 / 6
To solve for 'p', divide both sides of the inequality by 6 to undo the multiplication.
3
p ≤ 7
The worker can cut at most 7 pieces of wood.

Answer: p ≤ 7 (Option D)

Question 8Grade 6 • EE
A movie theater wants to show a film that is 145 minutes long. They have a time slot of at most 160 minutes, including a 10-minute intermission. How much extra time (t) can they add to the intermission without exceeding the slot? Write and solve an inequality.
A. t ≤ 5
B. t = 5
C. t < 5
D. t ≥ 5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Film length + initial intermission + extra time ≤ total slot time 145 + 10 + t ≤ 160 155 + t ≤ 160
The phrase 'at most' means the total time must be less than or equal to 160 minutes. We sum the film length, the existing intermission, and the unknown extra time.
2
155 + t ≤ 160 155 + t - 155 ≤ 160 - 155
To solve for 't', subtract 155 from both sides of the inequality to keep it balanced.
3
t ≤ 5
They can add at most 5 minutes of extra time to the intermission.

Answer: t ≤ 5 (Option A)

Question 9Grade 6 • EE
A city council wants to ensure that each of its 8 parks has at least 500 visitors per month. What is the minimum total number of visitors (v) they should aim for across all parks in a month? Write and solve an inequality.
A. v ≤ 4000
B. v ≥ 4000
C. v = 4000
D. v > 4000
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total visitors / number of parks ≥ visitors per park v / 8 ≥ 500
The phrase 'at least' means the average visitors per park must be greater than or equal to 500. We divide the total visitors by the number of parks.
2
v / 8 ≥ 500 (v / 8) × 8 ≥ 500 × 8
To solve for 'v', multiply both sides of the inequality by 8 to undo the division and keep it balanced.
3
v ≥ 4000
The city council should aim for at least 4000 total visitors across all parks.

Answer: v ≥ 4000 (Option B)

Question 10Grade 6 • EE
A cell phone plan costs $0.15 per text message. If you want to keep your text message bill under $7.50 for the month, what is the maximum number of text messages (t) you can send? Write and solve an inequality.
A. t = 50
B. t ≤ 50
C. t < 50
D. t > 50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost per text × number of texts < total bill 0.15t < 7.50
The phrase 'under $7.50' means the total cost must be strictly less than $7.50. We multiply the cost per text by the number of texts.
2
0.15t < 7.50 0.15t / 0.15 < 7.50 / 0.15
To solve for 't', divide both sides of the inequality by 0.15 to undo the multiplication. Since we are dividing by a positive number, the inequality sign does not flip.
3
t < 50
You can send a maximum of 49 text messages to keep the bill under $7.50 (since text messages must be whole numbers, and t must be less than 50).

Answer: t < 50 (Option C)
