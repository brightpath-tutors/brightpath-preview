Question 1Grade 7 • SP
A coin jar contains 5 quarters and 3 dimes. If you randomly pick one coin, what is the probability that it is a quarter?
A. 3/8
B. 8/5
C. 5/8
D. 5/3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total coins = 5 + 3 = 8
First, find the total number of possible outcomes by adding the number of quarters and dimes in the jar.
2
Favorable outcomes (quarters) = 5
Next, identify the number of favorable outcomes, which is the number of quarters.
3
Probability = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes = 5 / 8
Finally, calculate the probability by dividing the number of favorable outcomes by the total number of outcomes.

Answer: 5/8 (Option C)

Question 4Grade 7 • SP
A bus arrives at a stop every 15 minutes. If you arrive at the bus stop randomly, what is the probability that you will wait 5 minutes or less for the next bus, in simplest form?
A. 1/15
B. 2/3
C. 5/15
D. 1/3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total possible waiting time = 15 minutes
The bus arrives every 15 minutes, so the total range of possible waiting times is 15 minutes.
2
Favorable waiting time = 5 minutes
You want to wait 5 minutes or less, so the favorable range is 5 minutes.
3
Initial probability fraction = 5 / 15
Calculate the probability as the ratio of favorable waiting time to total possible waiting time.
4
Simplified fraction = 5 ÷ 5 / 15 ÷ 5 = 1 / 3
Simplify the fraction by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 5.

Answer: 1/3 (Option D)

Question 5Grade 7 • SP
In a class of 24 students, 8 students prefer reading, 10 prefer sports, and the rest prefer art. If you randomly select a student, what is the probability that they prefer art, in simplest form?
A. 1/3
B. 4
C. 1/4
D. 6/24
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total students = 24
Identify the total number of students in the class.
2
Students who prefer reading or sports = 8 + 10 = 18
Add the number of students who prefer reading and sports to find the total for these two categories.
3
Students who prefer art = 24 - 18 = 6
Subtract the sum from Step 2 from the total number of students to find the number of students who prefer art.
4
Initial probability fraction = 6 / 24
Form the probability fraction by dividing the number of students who prefer art by the total number of students.
5
Simplified fraction = 6 ÷ 6 / 24 ÷ 6 = 1 / 4
Simplify the fraction by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 6.

Answer: 1/4 (Option C)

Question 9Grade 7 • SP

A. 2/5
B. 5/3
C. 3/5
D. 300/500
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total residents = 500
Identify the total number of residents in the town.
2
Residents owning cats or dogs = 120 + 80 = 200
Calculate the total number of residents who own either a cat or a dog.
3
Residents owning neither = 500 - 200 = 300
Subtract the number of residents owning pets from the total residents to find those who own neither.
4
Initial probability fraction = 300 / 500
Form the probability fraction by dividing the number of residents owning neither by the total number of residents.
5
Simplified fraction = 300 ÷ 100 / 500 ÷ 100 = 3 / 5
Simplify the fraction by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 100.

Answer: 3/5 (Option C)

Question 10Grade 7 • SP
A tech company manufactures 200 smartphones. During quality control, it's found that 10 phones have screen defects and 5 phones have battery defects. No phone has both defects. What is the probability that a randomly chosen phone has no defects, in simplest form?
A. 3/40
B. 37/40
C. 200/185
D. 185/200
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total smartphones = 200
Identify the total number of smartphones manufactured.
2
Total phones with defects = 10 (screen) + 5 (battery) = 15
Add the number of phones with screen defects and battery defects to find the total number of defective phones. (Since no phone has both defects, we simply add them.)
3
Phones with no defects = 200 - 15 = 185
Subtract the total number of defective phones from the total smartphones to find the number of phones with no defects (favorable outcomes).
4
Initial probability fraction = 185 / 200
Form the probability fraction by dividing the number of non-defective phones by the total number of smartphones.
5
Simplified fraction = 185 ÷ 5 / 200 ÷ 5 = 37 / 40
Simplify the fraction by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 5.

Answer: 37/40 (Option B)
