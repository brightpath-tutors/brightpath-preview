Question 1Grade 8 • NS
A cashier scans several items. Which of the following prices represents a rational number?
A. $ \pi $ (approximately $3.14159...)
B. $ \frac{5}{2} $ ($2.50)
C. $ \sqrt{3} $ (approximately $1.73)
D. $ 0.121121112... $
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
A rational number is any number that can be expressed as a fraction \( \frac{p}{q} \), where \( p \) and \( q \) are integers and \( q eq 0 \). This includes terminating and repeating decimals.
Understanding the definition of a rational number is crucial for classification.
2
Option A: \( \sqrt{3} \) is approximately 1.73205... It is a non-terminating, non-repeating decimal. Therefore, \( \sqrt{3} \) is an irrational number.
Square roots of non-perfect squares are irrational.
3
Option B: \( \pi \) is approximately 3.14159265... It is a non-terminating, non-repeating decimal. Therefore, \( \pi \) is an irrational number.
Pi is a well-known irrational constant.
4
Option C: \( \frac{5}{2} \) is a fraction of two integers (5 and 2). It can be written as the terminating decimal 2.5. Therefore, \( \frac{5}{2} \) is a rational number.
Fractions of integers (with a non-zero denominator) are rational numbers.
5
Option D: \( 0.121121112... \) has a decimal representation that is non-terminating and non-repeating (the pattern of '1's increasing after '2' means it doesn't repeat). Therefore, it is an irrational number.
Non-repeating, non-terminating decimals are irrational.
6
Based on the evaluations, only \( \frac{5}{2} \) represents a rational number.
Comparing all options to the definition.

Answer: C (Option B)

A. The $ \sqrt{30} $ foot piece is longer by approximately 0.02 feet.
B. The $ \frac{11}{2} $ foot piece is longer by approximately 0.02 feet.
C. The $ \sqrt{30} $ foot piece is longer by approximately 0.03 feet.
D. The $ \frac{11}{2} $ foot piece is longer by approximately 0.03 feet.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The goal is to compare the lengths of the two pieces of wood and find their approximate difference.
Understand the two main parts of the question: comparison and calculating the difference.
2
Convert both lengths to decimal form for easy comparison: - Length of the first piece: \( \frac{11}{2} = 11 \div 2 = 5.5 \) feet. - Length of the second piece: \( \sqrt{30} \). We know that \( \sqrt{25}=5 \) and \( \sqrt{36}=6 \), so \( \sqrt{30} \) is between 5 and 6. Calculating to a few decimal places: \( \sqrt{30} \approx 5.4772 \) feet. For calculation of difference, we'll use 5.477.
Convert all numbers to decimals for direct comparison. Approximate irrational numbers to a suitable precision.
3
Compare the lengths: - First piece: \( 5.5 \) feet - Second piece: \( \approx 5.477 \) feet Since \( 5.5 > 5.477 \), the \( \frac{11}{2} \) foot piece is longer.
Compare the decimal values to determine which length is greater.
4
Calculate the difference in lengths: - Difference = Longer length - Shorter length = \( 5.5 - 5.477 = 0.023 \) feet.
Subtract the smaller length from the larger length.
5
Round the difference to the nearest hundredth: - \( 0.023 \) rounded to the nearest hundredth is \( 0.02 \) feet (since the digit in the thousandths place is 3, which is less than 5, we round down).
Apply rounding rules to the calculated difference as specified in the question.
6
Conclusion: The \( \frac{11}{2} \) foot piece is longer by approximately 0.02 feet.
State the final answer, including the longer piece and the approximate difference.

C. 3
D. $ 2\sqrt{3} $
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The plant population multiplies by a growth factor of \( \sqrt{3} \) each year.
Understand the given annual growth factor.
2
To find the factor by which the population has multiplied after 2 years, we need to apply the annual growth factor twice. This means we multiply the annual growth factor by itself: \( (\sqrt{3}) \times (\sqrt{3}) \).
For growth over multiple years, the factors multiply. For 2 years, it's the square of the annual factor.
3
Simplify the expression: \( (\sqrt{3})^2 = \sqrt{3 \times 3} = \sqrt{9} = 3 \).

The exact numerical factor by which the population has multiplied after 2 years is 3.
State the final calculated factor. The initial population (150 plants) is extra information not needed to find the 'factor'.
