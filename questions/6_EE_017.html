Question 1Grade 6 • EE
In a video game, a player scores 3^2 points in the first round and 3^4 points in the second round. What is the total score in exponential form?
A. 3^24
B. 3^8
C. 9^6
D. 3^6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Points from Round 1: 3^2 Points from Round 2: 3^4 Total Score = 3^2 * 3^4
When finding a total from two separate quantities that are expressed exponentially with the same base, the operation is multiplication of the exponential terms.
2
3^2 * 3^4 = 3^(2+4) 3^(2+4) = 3^6
The Product Rule states that for any non-zero base 'a' and integers 'm' and 'n', a^m * a^n = a^(m+n). Here, the base is 3, and the exponents are 2 and 4.

Answer: 3^6 (Option D)

Question 2Grade 6 • EE
A chef uses 5^6 milliliters of oil for a large batch of soup. To make a smaller batch, he needs to divide the amount of oil by 5^2. How much oil will he use for the smaller batch, in exponential form?
A. 5^3
B. 5^8
C. 1^4
D. 5^4
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Original oil amount: 5^6 Division factor: 5^2 Smaller batch oil amount = 5^6 / 5^2
The problem explicitly states 'divide the amount of oil by 5^2', indicating a division operation.
2
5^6 / 5^2 = 5^(6-2) 5^(6-2) = 5^4
The Quotient Rule states that for any non-zero base 'a' and integers 'm' and 'n', a^m / a^n = a^(m-n). Here, the base is 5, and the exponents are 6 and 2.

Answer: 5^4 (Option D)

Question 3Grade 6 • EE
A new server has a storage capacity of (7^3)^2 gigabytes. What is the storage capacity in exponential form?
A. 49^6
B. 7^9
C. 7^5
D. 7^6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Storage capacity: (7^3)^2
The parentheses and outer exponent indicate that the entire term 7^3 is being raised to the power of 2.
2
(7^3)^2 = 7^(3*2) 7^(3*2) = 7^6
The Power of a Power Rule states that for any non-zero base 'a' and integers 'm' and 'n', (a^m)^n = a^(m*n). Here, the base is 7, and the exponents are 3 and 2.

Answer: 7^6 (Option D)

Question 4Grade 6 • EE
A store sells a pack of trading cards for 3 dollars. Each pack contains 2^3 * 2^5 cards. If a customer buys 3 packs, what is the total number of cards purchased, in exponential form?
A. 3 * 2^15
B. 6^8
C. 3 * 2^8
D. 2^8
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cards per pack = 2^3 * 2^5 = 2^(3+5) = 2^8
When multiplying exponential terms with the same base, add their exponents.
2
Total cards = 3 * 2^8
Since the customer buys 3 packs, multiply the number of cards in one pack by 3. The coefficient 3 cannot be expressed as a power of 2, so it remains a separate factor.

Answer: 3 * 2^8 (Option C)

Question 5Grade 6 • EE
In a chemical experiment, the amount of substance A is 2^7 units and substance B is 2^5 units. If these are combined, and then 2^3 units react away, what is the final amount of the combined substance in exponential form?
A. 2^4
B. 2^11
C. 2^9
D. 2^15
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Combined amount = 2^7 * 2^5 = 2^(7+5) = 2^12
When quantities with the same exponential base are combined, their exponential forms are multiplied, and their exponents are added.
2
Final amount = 2^12 / 2^3 = 2^(12-3) = 2^9
When a quantity is reduced by an exponential factor, their exponential forms are divided, and their exponents are subtracted.

Answer: 2^9 (Option C)

Question 6Grade 6 • EE
A square garden plot has a side length of (3 * 4)^2 meters. What is the area of the garden in exponential form, expressed with prime number bases?
A. 3 * 2^4
B. 3^2 * 2^4
C. 12^2
D. 3^2 * 2^2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Side length = (3 4)^2 = (3 2^2)^2
To express the final answer with prime number bases, it's helpful to break down any composite bases into their prime factors first. 4 is 2 * 2, or 2^2.
2
Area = (3 2^2)^2 = 3^2 (2^2)^2
The Power of a Product Rule states that for any non-zero bases 'a' and 'b' and integer 'n', (ab)^n = a^n b^n. Here, 'a' is 3 and 'b' is 2^2.
3
3^2 (2^2)^2 = 3^2 2^(22) = 3^2 2^4
The Power of a Power Rule states that for any non-zero base 'a' and integers 'm' and 'n', (a^m)^n = a^(m*n). Here, for 2^2 raised to the power of 2, the exponents 2 and 2 are multiplied.

Answer: 3^2 * 2^4 (Option B)

Question 7Grade 6 • EE
A rocket's launch sequence involves several steps. The last step takes (5^0 + 2^1) seconds. How many seconds does the last step take?
A. 7
B. 5 + 2 = 7
C. 3
D. 0 + 2 = 2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
5^0 = 1
The Zero Exponent Rule states that any non-zero base raised to the power of 0 is equal to 1. So, 5^0 equals 1.
2
2^1 = 2
Any base raised to the power of 1 is equal to the base itself.
3
1 + 2 = 3
Perform the addition to find the total number of seconds.

Answer: 3 (Option C)

Question 8Grade 6 • EE
A wildlife park has an initial population of (6^5 / 6^3)^2 of a certain species. If a natural event further reduces this population by a factor of 6^2, what is the remaining population in exponential form?
A. 6^0
B. 6^2
C. 6^8
D. 6^6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
6^5 / 6^3 = 6^(5-3) = 6^2 So, the initial population is (6^2)^2
For division of powers with the same base, subtract the exponents.
2
(6^2)^2 = 6^(2*2) = 6^4
For a power raised to another power, multiply the exponents.
3
6^4 / 6^2 = 6^(4-2) = 6^2
Reducing by a factor means dividing. So, subtract the exponents for this division.

Answer: 6^2 (Option B)

Question 9Grade 6 • EE
An artist is designing a complex pattern. The first part covers an area of 2^3 * 3^2. The second part is 2^4 / 3^1. If these two parts are combined (multiplied) to form a larger pattern, what is the total area in simplest exponential form with prime bases?
A. 2^12 * 3^2
B. 2^7 * 3^3
C. 2^1 * 3^1
D. 2^7 * 3^1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
First part: 2^3 * 3^2 Second part: 2^4 / 3^1 Total Area = (2^3 3^2) (2^4 / 3^1)
The problem states the two parts are 'combined (multiplied)', so we multiply their expressions.
2
Total Area = (2^3 2^4) (3^2 / 3^1)
Rearrange the terms to easily apply exponent rules for each base.
3
For base 2: 2^3 * 2^4 = 2^(3+4) = 2^7 For base 3: 3^2 / 3^1 = 3^(2-1) = 3^1 Total Area = 2^7 * 3^1
Add exponents for multiplication and subtract exponents for division, keeping the respective bases.

Answer: 2^7 * 3^1 (Option D)

Question 10Grade 6 • EE
A new computer processor operates at a base speed of 2^4 units. Its turbo boost function increases the speed by a factor of (2^2 * 3^1)^2. What is the maximum operating speed of the processor in simplest exponential form with prime bases?
A. 2^8 * 3^1
B. 2^8 * 3^2
C. 2^16 * 3^2
D. 2^6 * 3^2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Base speed: 2^4 Turbo boost factor: (2^2 * 3^1)^2 Maximum speed = 2^4 (2^2 3^1)^2
The turbo boost 'increases the speed by a factor of' the given expression, which implies multiplication.
2
(2^2 3^1)^2 = (2^2)^2 (3^1)^2
Each factor inside the parentheses must be raised to the outer power (2).
3
(2^2)^2 = 2^(2*2) = 2^4 (3^1)^2 = 3^(1*2) = 3^2 So, the turbo boost factor is 2^4 * 3^2
For a power raised to another power, multiply the exponents.
4
Maximum speed = 2^4 (2^4 3^2) Maximum speed = (2^4 2^4) 3^2 Maximum speed = 2^(4+4) * 3^2 Maximum speed = 2^8 * 3^2
Group terms with the same base and add their exponents. The base 3 term remains separate.

Answer: 2^8 * 3^2 (Option B)
