Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A grocery store sells 3 pounds of apples for $7. Graph the relationship between the weight of apples (in pounds) and the total cost (in dollars). Which ordered pair on the graph represents the cost of 9 pounds of apples?
A. (21, 9)
B. (9, 21)
C. (9, 18)
D. (9, 7)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The ratio is 3 pounds of apples : $7. Let the x-axis represent the weight of apples in pounds and the y-axis represent the total cost in dollars. The ordered pairs will be (pounds, cost).
We set up the ratio and define the variables for the x and y axes according to the problem statement.
2
To find the cost for 9 pounds of apples, we can create a ratio table: | Pounds (x) | Cost (y) | | :--------- | :------- | | 3 | 7 | | 6 | 14 | | 9 | 21 | To get from 3 pounds to 9 pounds, we multiply by 3. So, we multiply the cost ($7) by 3 as well: $7 \times 3 = $21.
We extend the ratio by finding a common multiplier (3) that transforms 3 pounds to 9 pounds, and apply the same multiplier to the cost.
3
Plot the points (3, 7), (6, 14), and (9, 21) on a coordinate plane. Draw a straight line through these points and the origin (0,0) as this is a proportional relationship. The ordered pair representing the cost of 9 pounds of apples is (9, 21).
Plotting the calculated points and drawing a line confirms the proportional relationship. The specific point for 9 pounds of apples is (9, 21).

Answer: (9, 21) (Option B)
