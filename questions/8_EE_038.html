Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A store manager wants to rearrange a display. They have 15 promotional items (p) and 20 regular items (r). They want to group them into sets, with each set having the same number of promotional items and the same number of regular items, factoring out the greatest common factor of the number of items. Which expression represents the factored form of the total number of items, 15p + 20r?
A. 5(15p + 20r)
B. 5(3p + 4r)
C. 3(5p + 7r)
D. 5(3p + 20r)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms in the expression are 15p and 20r.
Break down the given expression into its individual parts.
2
The coefficients are 15 and 20. Factors of 15: 1, 3, 5, 15 Factors of 20: 1, 2, 4, 5, 10, 20 The GCF of 15 and 20 is 5.
List all factors for each coefficient and identify the largest factor common to both.
3
The terms are 15p and 20r. There are no common variables between 'p' and 'r'.
Look for variables that appear in all terms. If they do, take the one with the lowest exponent.
4
Divide each term by the GCF (5): 15p ÷ 5 = 3p 20r ÷ 5 = 4r
Divide each original term by the GCF found. These results will go inside the parentheses.
5
Place the GCF outside the parentheses and the results of the division inside: 5(3p + 4r)
The factored form is the GCF multiplied by the sum/difference of the divided terms in parentheses.

Answer: 5(3p + 4r) (Option B)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A project manager is tracking tasks. Task A takes 12xy hours, Task B takes 18x hours, and Task C takes 30x^2 hours. The expression for the total time is 12xy + 18x + 30x^2. Factor out the greatest common factor from this expression.
A. 6x(2y + 3 + 5x)
B. 6x(12xy + 18x + 30x^2)
C. 6(2xy + 3x + 5x^2)
D. x(12y + 18 + 30x)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms in the expression are 12xy, 18x, and 30x^2.
Break down the given expression into its individual parts.
2
The coefficients are 12, 18, and 30. Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12 Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18 Factors of 30: 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 10, 15, 30 The GCF of 12, 18, and 30 is 6.
List all factors for each coefficient and identify the largest factor common to all three.
3
Common variables across all terms: 'x'. The lowest power of 'x' is x^1 (from 18x). The variable 'y' is not common to all terms. So, the variable GCF is x.
For each common variable, select the one with the smallest exponent. Combine these to get the variable part of the GCF.
4
The overall GCF of the expression is the product of the numerical GCF and the variable GCF: 6 * x = 6x.
Multiply the numerical GCF by the variable GCF to get the complete GCF of the expression.
5
Divide each term by the GCF (6x): 12xy ÷ 6x = 2y 18x ÷ 6x = 3 30x^2 ÷ 6x = 5x
Divide each original term by the combined GCF. These results will go inside the parentheses.
6
Place the GCF outside the parentheses and the results of the division inside: 6x(2y + 3 + 5x)
The factored form is the GCF multiplied by the sum/difference of the divided terms in parentheses.

Answer: 6x(2y + 3 + 5x) (Option A)
