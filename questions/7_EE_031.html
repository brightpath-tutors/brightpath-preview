Question 1Grade 7 • EE
A store sells two types of candy. One type costs $2 per bag (x), and the other costs $3 per bag (y). If a customer buys a total of 5 bags and spends $13, how many bags of the $2 candy (x) did they buy?
A. 1 bag
B. 2 bags
C. -2 bags
D. 3 bags
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Equation 1 (total bags): x + y = 5 Equation 2 (total cost): 2x + 3y = 13
Let 'x' be the number of bags of $2 candy and 'y' be the number of bags of $3 candy. The total number of bags is 5, and the total cost is $13.
2
x + y = 5 y = 5 - x
Isolate 'y' in the first equation, as it's the easiest variable to solve for.
3
2x + 3(5 - x) = 13
Replace 'y' in the second equation with the expression '5 - x' from Step 2.
4
2x + 15 - 3x = 13 -x + 15 = 13 -x = 13 - 15 -x = -2 x = 2
Apply the distributive property, combine like terms, and then isolate 'x' by subtracting 15 from both sides and dividing by -1.
5
x = 2
The problem asks for the number of bags of $2 candy (x), which we found to be 2.

Answer: 2 bags (Option B)

Question 2Grade 7 • EE
A basketball team scored a total of 34 points in a game. They made some 2-point shots (x) and some 3-point shots (y). If they made 2 more 2-point shots than 3-point shots, how many 3-point shots (y) did they make?
A. 30 shots
B. 6 shots
C. 7 shots
D. 8 shots
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Equation 1 (total points): 2x + 3y = 34 Equation 2 (relationship between shots): x = y + 2
Let 'x' be the number of 2-point shots and 'y' be the number of 3-point shots. The total points are 34, and they made 2 more 2-point shots than 3-point shots.
2
2(y + 2) + 3y = 34
Since 'x' is already isolated in the second equation, substitute 'y + 2' for 'x' in the first equation.
3
2y + 4 + 3y = 34 5y + 4 = 34 5y = 34 - 4 5y = 30 y = 30 / 5 y = 6
Apply the distributive property, combine like terms, and then isolate 'y' by subtracting 4 from both sides and dividing by 5.
4
y = 6
The problem asks for the number of 3-point shots (y), which we found to be 6.

Answer: 6 shots (Option B)

A chef is mixing two types of spices. The chef bought a total of 10 ounces of spices. If the amount of Spice B (y) was 2 ounces less than the amount of Spice A (x), how many ounces of Spice A (x) did the chef buy?

B. 8 ounces
C. 4 ounces
D. 6 ounces
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Equation 1 (total ounces): x + y = 10 Equation 2 (relationship between spices): y = x - 2
Let 'x' be the ounces of Spice A and 'y' be the ounces of Spice B. The total ounces are 10, and Spice B is 2 ounces less than Spice A.
2
x + (x - 2) = 10
Since 'y' is already isolated in the second equation, substitute 'x - 2' for 'y' in the first equation.
3
2x - 2 = 10 2x = 10 + 2 2x = 12 x = 12 / 2 x = 6
Combine the 'x' terms, then isolate 'x' by adding 2 to both sides and dividing by 2.
4
x = 6
The problem asks for the ounces of Spice A (x), which we found to be 6.

B. 4 hours
C. 3 hours

Step-by-step solution
1
Equation 1 (total time): x + y = 5 Equation 2 (total distance): 60x + 40y = 260
Let 'x' be the hours at 60 mph and 'y' be the hours at 40 mph. The total time is 5 hours, and total distance is 260 miles (distance = speed × time).
2
x + y = 5 y = 5 - x
Isolate 'y' in the first equation.
3
60x + 40(5 - x) = 260
Replace 'y' in the second equation with '5 - x'.
4
60x + 200 - 40x = 260 20x + 200 = 260 20x = 260 - 200 20x = 60 x = 60 / 20 x = 3
Apply the distributive property, combine like terms, and then isolate 'x' by subtracting 200 from both sides and dividing by 20.
5
x = 3

Question 5Grade 7 • EE
In a classroom, there are a total of 27 students. The number of students who prefer math (m) is twice the number of students who prefer science (s), plus 3. How many students prefer math (m)?
A. 19 students
B. 21 students
C. 8 students
D. 23 students
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Equation 1 (total students): m + s = 27 Equation 2 (relationship of preferences): m = 2s + 3
Let 'm' be the number of students who prefer math and 's' be the number of students who prefer science. The total number of students is 27, and the number preferring math is 3 more than twice the number preferring science.
2
(2s + 3) + s = 27
Since 'm' is already isolated in the second equation, substitute '2s + 3' for 'm' in the first equation.
3
3s + 3 = 27 3s = 27 - 3 3s = 24 s = 24 / 3 s = 8
Combine the 's' terms, then isolate 's' by subtracting 3 from both sides and dividing by 3.
4
m = 2(8) + 3 m = 16 + 3 m = 19
Use the value of 's' to find 'm' using the simpler second equation.
5
m = 19
The problem asks for the number of students who prefer math (m), which we found to be 19.

Answer: 19 students (Option A)

Question 6Grade 7 • EE
A chemist is mixing two solutions. Solution A contains 10% acid, and Solution B contains 20% acid. The chemist wants to create 100 ml of a mixture that is 16% acid. How many ml of Solution A (x) should be used?
A. 4 ml
B. 40 ml
C. 60 ml
D. 20 ml
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Equation 1 (total volume): x + y = 100 Equation 2 (total amount of acid): 0.10x + 0.20y = 0.16(100) => 0.10x + 0.20y = 16
Let 'x' be the volume of Solution A and 'y' be the volume of Solution B. The total volume is 100 ml, and the total amount of acid is 16% of 100 ml, which is 16 ml.
2
x + y = 100 y = 100 - x
Isolate 'y' in the first equation.
3
0.10x + 0.20(100 - x) = 16
Replace 'y' in the second equation with '100 - x'.
4
0.10x + 20 - 0.20x = 16 -0.10x + 20 = 16 -0.10x = 16 - 20 -0.10x = -4 x = -4 / -0.10 x = 40
Apply the distributive property, combine like terms, and then isolate 'x' by subtracting 20 from both sides and dividing by -0.10.
5
x = 40
The problem asks for the volume of Solution A (x), which we found to be 40 ml.

Answer: 40 ml (Option B)

Question 8Grade 7 • EE
Two clocks are set to different times. Clock 1 gains 2 minutes every hour. Clock 2 gains 3 minutes every hour. At noon, both clocks showed the correct time. After 'x' hours, Clock 1 shows a total gain of 'y' minutes. After 'x' hours, Clock 2 shows a total gain of 'y + 10' minutes. How many hours ('x') have passed?
A. 20 hours
B. -10 hours
C. 5 hours
D. 10 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Equation 1 (Clock 1 gain): y = 2x Equation 2 (Clock 2 gain): y + 10 = 3x
Let 'x' be the number of hours passed and 'y' be the minutes gained by Clock 1. Clock 2 gains 'y + 10' minutes, which is also 3 minutes per hour 'x'.
2
2x + 10 = 3x
Since 'y' is isolated in the first equation, substitute '2x' for 'y' in the second equation.
3
10 = 3x - 2x 10 = x
Subtract '2x' from both sides to isolate 'x'.
4
x = 10
The problem asks for the number of hours passed (x), which we found to be 10.

Answer: 10 hours (Option D)

Question 9Grade 7 • EE
Two towns, Town A and Town B, are tracking their population changes. Town A's population (p_A) can be modeled by starting at 5000 and decreasing by 100 people per year (t). Town B's population (p_B) can be modeled by starting at 3000 and increasing by 150 people per year (t). In how many years (t) will their populations be the same?
A. 10 years
B. 40 years
C. 20 years
D. 8 years
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Town A: p_A = 5000 - 100t Town B: p_B = 3000 + 150t
Let 't' be the number of years. Town A starts at 5000 and decreases by 100 per year. Town B starts at 3000 and increases by 150 per year.
2
5000 - 100t = 3000 + 150t
We want to find the time 't' when p_A = p_B.
3
5000 - 3000 = 150t + 100t 2000 = 250t t = 2000 / 250 t = 8
Rearrange the equation by moving constant terms to one side and 't' terms to the other, then combine like terms and divide to isolate 't'.
4
t = 8
The problem asks for the number of years (t), which we found to be 8.

Answer: 8 years (Option D)

B. 100 licenses

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Equation 1 (relationship of licenses): b = p + 30 Equation 2 (total revenue): 50b + 120p = 10000
Let 'b' be the number of basic licenses and 'p' be the number of premium licenses. They sold 30 more basic licenses than premium, and the total revenue was $10,000.
2
50(p + 30) + 120p = 10000
Since 'b' is already isolated in the first equation, substitute 'p + 30' for 'b' in the second equation.
3
50p + 1500 + 120p = 10000 170p + 1500 = 10000 170p = 10000 - 1500 170p = 8500 p = 8500 / 170 p = 50
Apply the distributive property, combine like terms, and then isolate 'p' by subtracting 1500 from both sides and dividing by 170.
4
p = 50
The problem asks for the number of premium licenses (p), which we found to be 50.
