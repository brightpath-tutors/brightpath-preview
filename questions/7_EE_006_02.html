Question 1Grade 7 • EE
Sarah went shopping and bought a shirt for $15.75, a pair of socks for $4.25, and a book for $12.50. She started with $50.00. She then found a $5.00 bill in her pocket. How much money does Sarah have left?
A. $32.50
B. $12.50
C. $22.50
D. $42.50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total cost of items = $15.75 + $4.25 + $12.50
First, sum the cost of all items Sarah bought to find her total spending.
2
Total cost of items = $20.00 + $12.50 = $32.50
Perform the addition. $15.75 + $4.25 equals $20.00, and adding $12.50 to that gives $32.50.
3
Money remaining after purchases = $50.00 - $32.50
Next, subtract the total spending from the initial amount of money Sarah had.
4
Money remaining after purchases = $17.50
Subtracting $32.50 from $50.00 leaves $17.50.
5
Final amount of money = $17.50 + $5.00
Finally, add the $5.00 bill Sarah found in her pocket to the remaining money.
6
Final amount of money = $22.50
Adding $5.00 to $17.50 gives a final amount of $22.50.

Answer: $22.50 (Option C)

Question 2Grade 7 • EE
A golfer played 3 holes. On the first hole, she scored 2 strokes below par (-2). On the second hole, she scored 1 stroke above par (+1). On the third hole, she scored 3 strokes below par (-3). If par for 3 holes is 12, what is her total score for the 3 holes?
A. 18
B. 16
C. 8
D. 10
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total strokes relative to par = (-2) + (+1) + (-3)
First, sum the scores relative to par for each hole to find the overall score relative to par.
2
Total strokes relative to par = -1 + (-3) = -4
Adding -2 and +1 gives -1. Adding -3 to -1 results in -4.
3
Total score for 3 holes = Par score + Total strokes relative to par
Next, add this total relative score to the given par score for the 3 holes.
4
Total score for 3 holes = 12 + (-4)
Substitute the calculated relative score and the given par score into the equation.
5
Total score for 3 holes = 8
Adding 12 and -4 is equivalent to 12 - 4, which equals 8.

Answer: 8 (Option C)

B. 3/4 cups
C. 5/4 cups
D. 1 cup
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution

Sugar needed per batch = 1/2 cup
Identify the amount of sugar needed for one batch from the recipe.
2
Total sugar needed for 3 batches = 3 * (1/2) cups
Multiply the sugar needed per batch by the number of batches to find the total sugar required.

Total sugar needed for 3 batches = 3/2 cups

More sugar needed = 3/2 cups - 1/4 cup

Question 5Grade 7 • EE
In a math class, homework counts for 1/5 of the final grade, quizzes count for 1/4, and tests count for the remaining portion. If a student has an average of 85.5 for homework, 78.0 for quizzes, and 92.2 for tests, what is their final grade?
A. 85.2
B. 87.31
C. 86.85
D. 89.1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Test weight = 1 - 1/5 - 1/4
First, calculate the weight of the tests by subtracting the weights of homework and quizzes from the total grade (1, or 100%).
2
Test weight = 20/20 - 4/20 - 5/20 = 11/20
Find a common denominator (20) for the fractions and perform the subtraction.
3
Homework weight (decimal) = 1/5 = 0.2 Quiz weight (decimal) = 1/4 = 0.25 Test weight (decimal) = 11/20 = 0.55
Convert all fractional weights to decimals for easier multiplication with the average scores.
4
Weighted Homework = 85.5 * 0.2 = 17.1 Weighted Quizzes = 78.0 * 0.25 = 19.5 Weighted Tests = 92.2 * 0.55 = 50.71
Multiply each average score by its corresponding decimal weight.
5
Final Grade = 17.1 + 19.5 + 50.71
Add the weighted scores for homework, quizzes, and tests to find the final grade.
6
Final Grade = 87.31
Perform the addition to get the final grade.

Question 6Grade 7 • EE
At the start of an experiment, the temperature of a chemical solution was -4.8 °C. Over the next 2 hours, the temperature decreased by 1.2 °C each hour. Then, for the next 3 hours, it increased by 0.7 °C each hour. What was the final temperature of the solution?
A. -4.1 °C
B. -5.1 °C
C. -6.2 °C
D. -3.8 °C
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total temperature change during decrease period = 2 hours * (-1.2 °C/hour)
Calculate the total temperature change during the first 2 hours by multiplying the number of hours by the rate of decrease. Note that decrease is represented by a negative value.
2
Total temperature change during decrease period = -2.4 °C
Perform the multiplication.
3
Total temperature change during increase period = 3 hours * 0.7 °C/hour
Calculate the total temperature change during the next 3 hours by multiplying the number of hours by the rate of increase.
4
Total temperature change during increase period = 2.1 °C
Perform the multiplication.
5
Final temperature = Initial temperature + Total decrease + Total increase
Add the initial temperature and all the temperature changes to find the final temperature.
6
Final temperature = -4.8 °C + (-2.4 °C) + 2.1 °C
Substitute the values into the equation.
7
Final temperature = -7.2 °C + 2.1 °C = -5.1 °C
First, add -4.8 and -2.4 to get -7.2. Then, add 2.1 to -7.2 to get -5.1 °C.

Answer: -5.1 °C (Option B)

Question 7Grade 7 • EE
A rectangular garden has a length of 12 1/4 feet and a width of 8 1/2 feet. A fence is to be built around its perimeter. If the fence posts are to be placed every 1 1/3 feet, how many fence posts are needed? (Assume a post is needed at the start and end of each segment, so round up if there's a fractional part of a segment.)
A. 31 posts
B. 32 posts
C. 28 posts
D. 40 posts
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Length = 12 1/4 feet = 49/4 feet Width = 8 1/2 feet = 17/2 feet Post spacing = 1 1/3 feet = 4/3 feet
Convert all mixed numbers to improper fractions for easier calculation.
2
Perimeter = 2 * (Length + Width)
Use the formula for the perimeter of a rectangle.
3
Perimeter = 2 (49/4 + 17/2) = 2 (49/4 + 34/4)
Substitute the improper fractions and find a common denominator for the sum inside the parentheses.
4
Perimeter = 2 * (83/4) = 83/2 feet
Perform the addition and then the multiplication to find the total perimeter.
5
Number of segments = Perimeter / Post spacing
Divide the total perimeter by the spacing between posts to find the number of segments.
6
Number of segments = (83/2) / (4/3) = (83/2) * (3/4)
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.
7
Number of segments = 249/8 = 31.125 segments
Perform the multiplication. The result is 31.125 segments.
8
Number of posts = ceil(31.125) = 32 posts
Since a post is needed at the start and end of each segment (and around a perimeter), round up to the nearest whole number to ensure full coverage. 31.125 segments implies 32 posts.

Answer: 32 posts (Option B)

Question 8Grade 7 • EE
A flight from London (GMT+0) departs at 10:30 AM and lands in New York (GMT-5) 7.5 hours later. Due to unexpected turbulence, the flight was delayed by 1/4 of its original flight duration. What is the arrival time in New York local time?
A. 7:52:30 PM
B. 2:52:30 PM
C. 1:07:30 PM
D. 8:07:30 PM
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Original flight duration = 7.5 hours
Identify the original flight duration.
2
Delay duration = 1/4 7.5 hours = 0.25 7.5 hours
Calculate the duration of the delay, which is 1/4 of the original flight duration. Convert 1/4 to its decimal equivalent.
3
Delay duration = 1.875 hours
Perform the multiplication to find the delay duration.
4
New total flight duration = 7.5 hours + 1.875 hours = 9.375 hours
Add the delay duration to the original flight duration to get the new total flight time.
5
Departure time in London (decimal hours) = 10:30 AM = 10.5 hours past midnight
Convert the departure time to a decimal hour format for easier addition.
6
Arrival time in London local time (decimal hours) = 10.5 hours + 9.375 hours = 19.875 hours past midnight
Add the new total flight duration to the London departure time to find the arrival time in London's local time.
7
Arrival time in New York local time (decimal hours) = 19.875 hours - 5 hours
Adjust for the time zone difference. New York (GMT-5) is 5 hours behind London (GMT+0), so subtract 5 hours from the London arrival time.
8
Arrival time in New York local time = 14.875 hours = 2:52:30 PM
Convert 14.875 hours back to HH:MM:SS format (14 hours = 2 PM; 0.875 60 minutes = 52.5 minutes; 0.5 60 seconds = 30 seconds). So, 2:52:30 PM.

Answer: 2:52:30 PM (Option B)

Question 10Grade 7 • EE
A laptop has a 500 GB hard drive. 1/8 of the drive is used by the operating system, and 0.15 of the remaining space is used for applications. If 1 GB = 1024 MB, and a user downloads a movie that is 8,192 MB, how much free space (in GB) is left on the hard drive?
A. 368.125 GB
B. 363.875 GB
C. 371.375 GB
D. 375.0 GB
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Space used by OS = 500 GB * (1/8)
Calculate the amount of space used by the operating system by multiplying the total hard drive size by the fraction used.
2
Space used by OS = 62.5 GB
Perform the multiplication.
3
Remaining space after OS = 500 GB - 62.5 GB
Subtract the OS space from the total hard drive size to find the remaining space.
4
Remaining space after OS = 437.5 GB
Perform the subtraction.
5
Space used by applications = 437.5 GB * 0.15
Calculate the space used by applications by multiplying the remaining space by 0.15.
6
Space used by applications = 65.625 GB
Perform the multiplication.
7
Movie size in GB = 8192 MB / 1024 MB/GB = 8 GB
Convert the movie size from MB to GB using the given conversion factor.
8
Total used space = 62.5 GB + 65.625 GB + 8 GB = 136.125 GB Free space = 500 GB - 136.125 GB = 363.875 GB
Sum all the used spaces (OS, applications, and movie) and then subtract this total from the initial hard drive size to find the free space.

Answer: 363.875 GB (Option B)
