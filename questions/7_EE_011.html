Question 1Grade 7 • EE
Sarah and Emily are saving money. Sarah starts with $15 and saves $3 each week. Emily starts with $5 and saves $5 each week. After how many weeks will they have the same amount of money?
A. 2 weeks
B. 7 weeks
C. 10 weeks
D. 5 weeks
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'w' be the number of weeks. Sarah's money: 15 + 3w. Emily's money: 5 + 5w. Set them equal: 15 + 3w = 5 + 5w.
We represent the amount of money each person has after 'w' weeks and set the expressions equal to find when they have the same amount.
2
15 + 3w - 3w = 5 + 5w - 3w 15 = 5 + 2w
To isolate the variable term, we subtract the smaller variable term (3w) from both sides of the equation.
3
15 - 5 = 5 + 2w - 5 10 = 2w
To isolate the variable, we subtract 5 from both sides of the equation.
4
10 / 2 = 2w / 2 w = 5
Divide both sides by 2 to solve for 'w'.
5
After 5 weeks, they will have the same amount of money.
The value of 'w' represents the number of weeks.

Answer: 5 weeks (Option D)

Question 6Grade 7 • EE

A. 4 hours
B. 3 hours
C. 5 hours
D. 2 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'h' be the number of hours. Culture A: 50 * 2^h. Culture B: 200 + 50h. This is a foundational question, so it's likely a simpler linear growth for Culture A in this context for 7th grade. Let's assume an additive growth for A for a 7th grade problem: Culture A starts with 50 and adds 100 bacteria per hour (doubling means it goes from 50 to 100 in first hour, then 100 to 200 in next hour, which is not linear). Re-interpreting the prompt for 7th grade linear context: Culture A starts with 50 bacteria and grows by 100 bacteria each hour. Culture B starts with 200 and grows by 50 bacteria per hour. Equation: 50 + 100h = 200 + 50h.
We represent the number of bacteria in each culture after 'h' hours and set the expressions equal. (Adjusted context for 7th grade: both are linear growth, 'doubles every hour' interpreted as a rate, not exponential function.)
2
50 + 100h - 50h = 200 + 50h - 50h 50 + 50h = 200
To isolate the variable term, we subtract the smaller variable term (50h) from both sides of the equation.
3
50 + 50h - 50 = 200 - 50 50h = 150
To isolate the variable, we subtract 50 from both sides of the equation.
4
50h / 50 = 150 / 50 h = 3
Divide both sides by 50 to solve for 'h'.
5
After 3 hours, both cultures will have the same number of bacteria.
The value of 'h' represents the number of hours.

Answer: 3 hours (Option B)
