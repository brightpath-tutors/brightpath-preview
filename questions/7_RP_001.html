Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A shop sells 8 blue shirts and 12 red shirts. What is the ratio of blue shirts to red shirts, in simplest form?
A. 2:3
B. 4:6
C. 12:8
D. 8:12
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Blue shirts = 8, Red shirts = 12
We need to identify the number of each item to form the ratio.
2
Ratio of blue shirts to red shirts = 8:12
The order matters in a ratio. 'Blue shirts to red shirts' means blue shirts come first.
3
The GCF of 8 and 12 is 4. Divide both numbers by 4: 8 ÷ 4 = 2, 12 ÷ 4 = 3.
To simplify a ratio, divide both parts by their greatest common factor until they can no longer be divided evenly by any common number other than 1.
4
The simplified ratio is 2:3.
The ratio 2:3 represents the relationship between blue shirts and red shirts in its most simplified form.

Answer: 2:3 (Option A)
