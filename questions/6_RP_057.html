Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A grocery store sells organic berries where 3 pounds cost $12.75. What is the constant of proportionality representing the cost per pound?
A. 4.25 per pound
B. 9.75 per pound
C. 0.235 per pound
D. 38.25 per pound
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost is proportional to the number of pounds. The constant of proportionality (k) is found by dividing the cost by the number of pounds (k = cost/pounds).
The problem asks for the cost 'per pound', which indicates a unit rate. This is the definition of the constant of proportionality in this context.
2
Cost = $12.75 Pounds = 3 k = 12.75 / 3
Substitute the given values into the formula k = cost/pounds.
3
12.75 ÷ 3 = 4.25
Perform the division to find the value of k.
4
The constant of proportionality is $4.25 per pound.
Include the appropriate units to describe the constant of proportionality.

Answer: $4.25 per pound (Option A)
