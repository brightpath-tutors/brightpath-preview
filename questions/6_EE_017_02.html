Question 1Grade 6 • EE
Sarah bought 5 identical notebooks for a total of $25. If 'n' represents the cost of one notebook, which equation can be used to find the cost of one notebook, and what is the cost?
A. $20
B. $30
C. $5
D. $0.20
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Identify the given information: Total cost = $25, Number of notebooks = 5.
The problem states the total cost and the number of identical items bought.
2
Formulate the equation: 5n = 25
Since 'n' is the cost of one notebook, 5 notebooks would cost 5 times 'n'. The total cost is $25, so 5n must equal 25.
3
Apply the division property of equality: Divide both sides by 5. (5n) / 5 = 25 / 5
To isolate 'n', perform the inverse operation of multiplication, which is division. Divide both sides of the equation by the coefficient of 'n', which is 5.
4
Calculate the value of n: n = 5
25 divided by 5 equals 5. So, each notebook costs $5.

Answer: $5 (Option C)
