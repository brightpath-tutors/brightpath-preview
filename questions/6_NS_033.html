Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A scientist recorded the daily low temperatures in degrees Celsius for four cities. City A: -5°C, City B: 2°C, City C: -1°C, City D: 0°C. Order these temperatures from coldest to warmest.
A. -1°C, -5°C, 0°C, 2°C
B. 2°C, 0°C, -1°C, -5°C
C. -5°C, -1°C, 0°C, 2°C
D. -5°C, 0°C, -1°C, 2°C
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Coldest to warmest means ordering from least to greatest.
To order temperatures from coldest to warmest, we need to arrange them from the lowest numerical value to the highest numerical value.
2
Negative temperatures are -5°C and -1°C. On a number line, -5 is to the left of -1, so -5 < -1. Thus, -5°C is colder than -1°C.
Numbers further to the left on the number line are smaller. For negative numbers, a larger absolute value means a smaller (colder) temperature.
3
Zero is greater than any negative number, and positive numbers are greater than zero. So, -1 < 0 and 0 < 2.
Zero serves as the reference point. All negative numbers are less than zero, and all positive numbers are greater than zero.
4
Combining the comparisons: -5°C < -1°C < 0°C < 2°C.
The final ordered list from coldest to warmest (least to greatest) is -5°C, -1°C, 0°C, 2°C.

Answer: -5°C, -1°C, 0°C, 2°C (Option C)

Question 2Grade 6 • NS
Sarah tracked her bank account balance changes over four months. The changes were: +$15 (gain), -$20 (loss), +$5 (gain), -$10 (loss). Order these changes from the smallest change to the largest change.
A. -$20, +$5, -$10, +$15
B. -$20, -$10, +$5, +$15
C. +$15, +$5, -$10, -$20
D. -$10, -$20, +$5, +$15
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Smallest change to largest change means ordering from least to greatest. The values are 15, -20, 5, -10.
Positive values represent gains, and negative values represent losses. 'Smallest to largest change' implies arranging the numbers on a number line from left to right.
2
Negative values are -20 and -10. -20 is less than -10 because it is further to the left on the number line (a greater loss).
For negative numbers, the number with the larger absolute value is actually smaller. A loss of $20 is a smaller change than a loss of $10.
3
Positive values are 15 and 5. 5 is less than 15.
For positive numbers, the larger number represents a greater gain, and thus a larger change.
4
Combining the comparisons: -20 < -10 < 5 < 15.
The final ordered list from smallest change to largest change is -$20, -$10, +$5, +$15.

Answer: -$20, -$10, +$5, +$15 (Option B)

Question 3Grade 6 • NS
In a mini-golf tournament, scores are recorded relative to par. Player A scored +3, Player B scored -1, Player C scored 0, and Player D scored +5. Order these scores from best (lowest) to worst (highest).
A. -1, +3, 0, +5
B. +5, +3, 0, -1
C. 0, -1, +3, +5
D. -1, 0, +3, +5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Best (lowest) to worst (highest) means ordering from least to greatest. The scores are 3, -1, 0, 5.
In golf, a lower score is better. Scores below par are negative, scores at par are zero, and scores above par are positive. We need to arrange them in increasing order.
2
The only negative score is -1. This is the lowest score, as all other scores are 0 or positive.
Negative numbers are always less than zero or any positive number, so -1 is the best score.
3
The remaining scores are 0, 3, and 5. In ascending order, these are 0 < 3 < 5.
After the negative score, zero is the next lowest, followed by the positive scores in their natural order.
4
Combining the comparisons: -1 < 0 < 3 < 5.
The final ordered list from best to worst is -1, 0, +3, +5.

Answer: -1, 0, +3, +5 (Option D)

B. +35 meters, +20 meters, 0 meters, -10 meters, -15 meters

Step-by-step solution
1
Highest to lowest elevation means ordering from greatest to least. The elevations are: 0, 20, -10, 35, -15.
Positive values represent elevations above ground, zero is ground level, and negative values are below ground. We need to arrange them in decreasing order.
2
Positive elevations: 20, 35. In descending order: 35 > 20.
Start with the highest positive number, which is the highest point.
3

The ground floor (0) comes after all floors above ground.
4

For negative numbers, the number closer to zero is greater. So, Basement 1 (-10m) is higher than Basement 2 (-15m).
5
Combining: 35 > 20 > 0 > -10 > -15.

Question 5Grade 6 • NS
A nutritionist recorded a client's weekly weight changes in pounds: Week 1: -3 lbs, Week 2: +2 lbs, Week 3: 0 lbs, Week 4: -5 lbs, Week 5: +1 lb. Order these weight changes from greatest weight loss to greatest weight gain.
A. -3 lbs, -5 lbs, 0 lbs, +1 lb, +2 lbs
B. -5 lbs, -3 lbs, 0 lbs, +1 lb, +2 lbs
C. -5 lbs, 0 lbs, -3 lbs, +1 lb, +2 lbs
D. +2 lbs, +1 lb, 0 lbs, -3 lbs, -5 lbs
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Greatest weight loss to greatest weight gain means ordering from least to greatest. The changes are: -3, 2, 0, -5, 1.
Weight loss is represented by negative numbers, no change by zero, and weight gain by positive numbers. 'Greatest loss' implies the most negative number, and 'greatest gain' implies the most positive number. This is equivalent to ordering from least to greatest.
2
Negative changes: -3, -5. On a number line, -5 is to the left of -3, so -5 < -3. This means a 5 lb loss is greater than a 3 lb loss.
The larger the absolute value of a negative number, the smaller the number, and in this context, the 'greater' the loss.
3
Zero (no change) comes after all losses but before all gains. So, -3 < 0.
Zero is the neutral point between loss and gain.
4
Positive changes: 1, 2. In ascending order: 1 < 2.
The smaller positive number represents a smaller gain.
5
Combining: -5 < -3 < 0 < 1 < 2.
The final ordered list from greatest weight loss to greatest weight gain is -5 lbs, -3 lbs, 0 lbs, +1 lb, +2 lbs.

Answer: -5 lbs, -3 lbs, 0 lbs, +1 lb, +2 lbs (Option B)

Question 6Grade 6 • NS
A teacher recorded students' deviations from the average test score: -7, +4, -2, +9, 0, -10. Order these deviations from the highest deviation above average to the lowest deviation below average.
A. +9, +4, -10, -7, -2, 0
B. +9, +4, 0, -2, -7, -10
C. -10, -7, -2, 0, +4, +9
D. 0, -2, -7, -10, +4, +9
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Highest deviation above average to lowest deviation below average means ordering from greatest to least. The deviations are: -7, 4, -2, 9, 0, -10.
Positive deviations are above average, negative deviations are below average, and zero is exactly average. We need to arrange them in decreasing order.
2
Positive deviations: 4, 9. In descending order: 9 > 4.
Start with the largest positive number, representing the highest score above average.
3
Zero (average) comes after all positive deviations but before all negative deviations. So, 4 > 0.
A score exactly at average is better than any score below average.
4
Negative deviations: -2, -7, -10. On a number line, -2 is closest to zero, then -7, then -10. So, -2 > -7 > -10.
The negative number closest to zero is the largest, representing the smallest deviation below average.
5
Combining: 9 > 4 > 0 > -2 > -7 > -10.
The final ordered list is +9, +4, 0, -2, -7, -10.

Answer: +9, +4, 0, -2, -7, -10 (Option B)

A. +25,000, +10,000, +5,000, -32,000, -18,000, -7,000
B. -32,000, -18,000, -7,000, +5,000, +10,000, +25,000

1
Largest increase to largest decrease means ordering from greatest to least. The changes are: 25000, -18000, 5000, -32000, 10000, -7000.
Positive values indicate population increases, and negative values indicate decreases. We need to arrange them in descending order.

The deviations were: Device A: -12GB, Device B: +8GB, Device C: -25GB, Device D: +3GB, Device E: -5GB, Device F: 0GB. Order these storage deviations from the least available space to the most available space.

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Least available space to most available space means ordering from least to greatest. The deviations are: -12, 8, -25, 3, -5, 0.
Negative values indicate less space than the baseline, positive values more space, and zero is exactly the baseline. We need to arrange them in increasing order.
2
Negative deviations: -12, -25, -5. On a number line, -25 is furthest left, then -12, then -5. So, -25 < -12 < -5.
