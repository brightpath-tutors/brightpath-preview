Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A store offers a discount where an item costs $2 \div 3$ of its original price. Express this fraction as a repeating decimal.
A. 0.233...
B. 0.333...
C. 0.\u00af6
D. 0.66
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
2 \div 3
Understanding that a fraction bar represents division is the first step.
2
`` 0.666... 3 | 2.000 1 8 --- 20 18 --- 20 18 --- 2 ``
We divide 2 by 3. Since 3 doesn't go into 2, we add a decimal point and a zero, making it 20. 3 goes into 20 six times (18), leaving a remainder of 2. This process repeats indefinitely.
3
The digit '6' keeps repeating.
Observing the remainders in the long division, we see that the remainder '2' continuously appears, causing the quotient digit '6' to repeat.
4
0.\u00af6
A bar is placed over the repeating digit(s) to indicate that they continue infinitely.

Answer: 0.\u00af6 (Option C)
