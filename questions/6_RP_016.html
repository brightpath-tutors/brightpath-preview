2

5
The annual interest rate was 3%.

Question 9Grade 6 • RP
A city government invested a total of $7000 in two different community projects. Project A earns 6% simple interest annually, and Project B earns 4% simple interest annually. After 2 years, the total interest earned from both projects was $760. How much money was invested in Project A?
A. $5000
B. $3500
C. $840
D. $4000
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let P_A be the principal invested in Project A. Let P_B be the principal invested in Project B. Total Principal: P_A + P_B = 7000 => P_B = 7000 - P_A Interest from Project A: I_A = P_A 0.06 2 = 0.12 * P_A Interest from Project B: I_B = P_B 0.04 2 = 0.08 * P_B Total Interest: I_A + I_B = 760
Set up a system of equations based on the total principal invested and the total interest earned. Express P_B in terms of P_A to simplify later substitution.
2
0.12 P_A + 0.08 (7000 - P_A) = 760
Replace P_B with its expression in terms of P_A in the total interest equation.
3
0.12 P_A + (0.08 7000) - (0.08 * P_A) = 760 0.12 P_A + 560 - 0.08 P_A = 760 (0.12 - 0.08) * P_A = 760 - 560 0.04 * P_A = 200 P_A = 200 / 0.04 P_A = $5000
Distribute, combine like terms, and isolate P_A to find the principal invested in Project A.
4
The amount invested in Project A was $5000.
The calculated value is the principal for Project A.

Answer: $5000 (Option A)
