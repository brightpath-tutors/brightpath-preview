Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A small business sells custom-printed t-shirts. They charge a one-time design fee of $20 and then $8 per t-shirt. Let y represent the total cost and x represent the number of t-shirts ordered. Which equation correctly represents the total cost, and how would you graph it?
A. y = 8x - 20. The graph has a y-intercept at (0, -20) and a slope of 8.
B. y = 20 - 8x. The graph has a y-intercept at (0, 20) and a slope of -8.
C. y = 20x + 8. The graph has a y-intercept at (0, 8) and a slope of 20.
D. y = 8x + 20. The graph has a y-intercept at (0, 20) and a slope of 8.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The one-time design fee is $20. This is the starting value, or the y-intercept (b).
The y-intercept is the value of y when x is 0, representing the initial cost before any t-shirts are made.
2
The cost per t-shirt is $8. This is the rate at which the total cost increases for each additional t-shirt, which is the slope (m).
The slope represents how much the dependent variable (total cost) changes for every unit increase in the independent variable (number of t-shirts).
3
Substitute m = 8 and b = 20 into the equation: y = 8x + 20.
The slope-intercept form is a standard way to represent linear equations, where m is the slope and b is the y-intercept.
4
Plot the y-intercept at (0, 20). From this point, use the slope (rise over run) to find another point. Since the slope is 8 (or 8/1), move up 8 units and right 1 unit to plot a second point. Then, draw a straight line through these two points.
To graph a linear equation, start by plotting the y-intercept. Then, use the slope to find additional points, which helps in drawing the line accurately.

Answer: y = 8x + 20. The graph has a y-intercept at (0, 20) and a slope of 8. (Option D)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
A car starts a road trip with 15 gallons of fuel in its tank. It consumes fuel at a rate of 0.5 gallons per 10 miles. Let y be the amount of fuel remaining (in gallons) and x be the distance traveled (in miles). Which equation represents this situation, and what would the graph look like?
A. y = 15x - 0.05. The graph has a y-intercept at (0, -0.05) and a slope of 15.
B. y = -0.5x + 15. The graph has a y-intercept at (0, 15) and a slope of -0.5.
C. y = 0.05x + 15. The graph has a y-intercept at (0, 15) and a slope of 0.05.
D. y = -0.05x + 15. The graph has a y-intercept at (0, 15) and a slope of -0.05.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The car starts with 15 gallons of fuel. This is the starting value, or the y-intercept (b).
The y-intercept represents the amount of fuel in the tank when the distance traveled (x) is 0.
2
The car consumes 0.5 gallons per 10 miles. To find the rate per mile, divide 0.5 by 10: 0.5 / 10 = 0.05 gallons per mile. Since fuel is consumed, the rate is negative. So, the slope (m) is -0.05.
The slope is the rate of change. Since the fuel is decreasing, the slope must be negative. It's important to convert the rate to units per single mile for the equation.
3
Substitute m = -0.05 and b = 15 into the equation: y = -0.05x + 15.
Combining the calculated slope and identified y-intercept gives the complete linear equation.
4
Plot the y-intercept at (0, 15). From this point, use the slope to find another point. Since the slope is -0.05 (or -5/100), move down 5 units and right 100 units to plot a second point. Then, draw a straight line through these two points.
The graph starts at the initial fuel level and decreases at a constant rate, representing the car consuming fuel over distance.

Answer: y = -0.05x + 15. The graph has a y-intercept at (0, 15) and a slope of -0.05. (Option D)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A construction worker is laying bricks. After 2 hours, they have laid 150 bricks. After 5 hours, they have laid 375 bricks. Assume a constant rate of bricklaying. Let y be the total number of bricks laid and x be the time in hours. Determine the linear equation that models this situation and interpret its graph.
A. y = 112.5x. The graph passes through the origin and has a slope of 112.5.
B. y = 75x. The graph passes through the origin (0,0) and has a slope of 75, indicating 75 bricks laid per hour.
C. y = 225x. The graph passes through the origin and has a slope of 225.
D. y = 75x + 150. The graph has a y-intercept at (0, 150) and a slope of 75.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Point 1: (2 hours, 150 bricks) -> (2, 150) Point 2: (5 hours, 375 bricks) -> (5, 375)
Each pair of (time, bricks laid) represents a point on the linear graph.
2
m = (y2 - y1) / (x2 - x1) m = (375 - 150) / (5 - 2) m = 225 / 3 m = 75
The slope represents the rate of bricklaying in bricks per hour.
3
Using y = mx + b and point (2, 150): 150 = 75(2) + b 150 = 150 + b b = 0
The y-intercept represents the number of bricks laid at time x=0 (the start of the work).
4
Substitute m = 75 and b = 0 into y = mx + b: y = 75x + 0, which simplifies to y = 75x.
The equation shows the total bricks laid is directly proportional to the hours worked, with a proportionality constant of 75.
5
The graph passes through the origin (0,0) because the y-intercept is 0. From the origin, move up 75 units and right 1 unit (or up 225 units and right 3 units) to find another point, then draw a straight line through these points. The slope of 75 means 75 bricks are laid every hour.
A graph passing through the origin indicates no initial quantity, and the slope visually represents the rate of change.

Answer: y = 75x. The graph passes through the origin (0,0) and has a slope of 75, indicating 75 bricks laid per hour. (Option B)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE
A small town's population was 1,200 in the year 2000 and has been increasing at a constant rate. In 2010, the population reached 1,450. Let P represent the population and t represent the number of years since 2000. Write the linear equation representing this growth and describe how to graph it.
A. P = 25t + 1200. The graph has a P-intercept at (0, 1200) and a slope of 25, showing the initial population and its annual growth.
B. P = -25t + 1200. The graph has a P-intercept at (0, 1200) and a slope of -25.
C. P = 250t + 1200. The graph has a P-intercept at (0, 1200) and a slope of 250.
D. P = 1200t + 25. The graph has a P-intercept at (0, 25) and a slope of 1200.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Year 2000 is t=0, so Point 1: (0, 1200). Year 2010 is t=10 (2010 - 2000), so Point 2: (10, 1450).
Representing the years as time since a reference year (2000) simplifies calculations and directly gives the y-intercept.
2
m = (P2 - P1) / (t2 - t1) m = (1450 - 1200) / (10 - 0) m = 250 / 10 m = 25
The slope represents the annual increase in population.
3
Since t=0 corresponds to the year 2000, the population in 2000 (1200) is the y-intercept (b).
The y-intercept is directly given by the population at t=0.
4
Substitute m = 25 and b = 1200 into P = mt + b: P = 25t + 1200.
This equation models the population growth over time.
5
Plot the P-intercept at (0, 1200). From this point, use the slope (rise over run) to find another point. Since the slope is 25 (or 25/1), move up 25 units and right 1 unit to plot a second point. Then, draw a straight line through these two points. The graph visually shows the starting population and its constant annual increase.
Graphing provides a visual representation of the population trend, starting from the baseline year.

Answer: P = 25t + 1200. The graph has a P-intercept at (0, 1200) and a slope of 25, showing the initial population and its annual growth. (Option A)

A. D = 6000d + 3500. The graph has a D-intercept at (0, 3500) and a slope of 6000.
B. D = 14000d - 8000. The graph has a D-intercept at (0, -8000) and a slope of 14000.
C. D = 1500d + 3500. The graph has a D-intercept at (0, 3500) and a slope of 1500, representing initial downloads and daily growth.
D. D = 1500d + 8000. The graph has a D-intercept at (0, 8000) and a slope of 1500.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Point 1: (Day 3, 8000 downloads) -> (3, 8000) Point 2: (Day 7, 14000 downloads) -> (7, 14000)
Each data point provides an (x, y) coordinate pair for the linear function.
2
m = (D2 - D1) / (d2 - d1) m = (14000 - 8000) / (7 - 3) m = 6000 / 4 m = 1500
The slope represents the daily increase in app downloads.
3
Using D = md + b and point (3, 8000): 8000 = 1500(3) + b 8000 = 4500 + b b = 8000 - 4500 b = 3500
The y-intercept represents the estimated number of downloads on day 0.
4
Substitute m = 1500 and b = 3500 into D = md + b: D = 1500d + 3500.
This equation models the linear growth of app downloads over days.
5
The graph has a D-intercept at (0, 3500). From this point, use the slope (rise over run) to find another point. Since the slope is 1500 (or 1500/1), move up 1500 units and right 1 unit to plot a second point. Then, draw a straight line through these two points. The graph shows the initial downloads and the consistent daily increase.
The visual representation helps in understanding the starting point and the rate of increase of app downloads.

Answer: D = 1500d + 3500. The graph has a D-intercept at (0, 3500) and a slope of 1500, representing initial downloads and daily growth. (Option C)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE

Assuming a consistent linear change in weight, let W be the weight in pounds and w be the week number. Determine the linear equation that represents this data and describe its graph.
A. W = -2w + 188. The graph has a W-intercept at (0, 188) and a slope of -2.
B. W = -12w + 188. The graph has a W-intercept at (0, 188) and a slope of -12.
C. W = -2w + 196. The graph has a W-intercept at (0, 196) and a slope of -2, showing the patient's initial weight and weekly weight loss.
D. W = 2w + 180. The graph has a W-intercept at (0, 180) and a slope of 2.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Point 1: (Week 4, 188 pounds) -> (4, 188) Point 2: (Week 10, 176 pounds) -> (10, 176)
Each (week, weight) pair forms a coordinate for the linear model.
2
m = (W2 - W1) / (w2 - w1) m = (176 - 188) / (10 - 4) m = -12 / 6 m = -2
The negative slope indicates a decrease in weight over time.
3
Using W = mw + b and point (4, 188): 188 = -2(4) + b 188 = -8 + b b = 188 + 8 b = 196
The y-intercept represents the patient's estimated weight at week 0 (the start of monitoring).
4
Substitute m = -2 and b = 196 into W = mw + b: W = -2w + 196.
This equation models the patient's weight change over the weeks.
5
The graph has a W-intercept at (0, 196). From this point, use the slope (rise over run) to find another point. Since the slope is -2 (or -2/1), move down 2 units and right 1 unit to plot a second point. Then, draw a straight line through these two points. The graph shows the initial weight and the consistent weekly decrease.
The descending line of the graph illustrates the patient's weight loss trend.

Answer: W = -2w + 196. The graph has a W-intercept at (0, 196) and a slope of -2, showing the patient's initial weight and weekly weight loss. (Option C)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A marathon runner's distance from the finish line decreases linearly over time. At 30 minutes, they are 11.5 kilometers from the finish. At 90 minutes, they are 2.5 kilometers from the finish. Let D be the distance from the finish line (in km) and t be the time elapsed (in minutes). Write the linear equation representing their progress and describe the graph. What is the interpretation of the y-intercept in this context?
A. D = -0.15t + 11.5. The y-intercept of 11.5 km represents the distance from the finish line at 30 minutes.
B. D = -0.15t + 16. The graph has a D-intercept at (0, 16) and a slope of -0.15. The y-intercept of 16 km represents the runner's starting distance from the finish line at time t=0.
C. D = 0.15t + 7. The y-intercept of 7 km represents the runner's initial distance from the finish line.
D. D = -9t + 16. The y-intercept of 16 km represents the runner's initial distance from the finish line.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Point 1: (30 minutes, 11.5 km) -> (30, 11.5) Point 2: (90 minutes, 2.5 km) -> (90, 2.5)
These coordinates represent (time, distance from finish) and are used to determine the linear relationship.
2
m = (D2 - D1) / (t2 - t1) m = (2.5 - 11.5) / (90 - 30) m = -9 / 60 m = -0.15
The slope is negative because the distance from the finish line is decreasing. It represents the runner's speed towards the finish line (km per minute).
3
Using D = mt + b and point (30, 11.5): 11.5 = -0.15(30) + b 11.5 = -4.5 + b b = 11.5 + 4.5 b = 16
The y-intercept represents the distance from the finish line at the start of the race (t=0).
4
Substitute m = -0.15 and b = 16 into D = mt + b: D = -0.15t + 16.
This equation accurately models the runner's distance from the finish line over time.
5
The graph has a D-intercept at (0, 16). From this point, use the slope (-0.15) to find another point. For example, move down 15 units and right 100 units. Then, draw a straight line through these points. The y-intercept of 16 km signifies the runner's starting distance from the finish line at the beginning of the race (t=0 minutes).
The graph visually represents the runner's constant pace, starting from their initial position relative to the finish line.

Answer: D = -0.15t + 16. The graph has a D-intercept at (0, 16) and a slope of -0.15. The y-intercept of 16 km represents the runner's starting distance from the finish line at time t=0. (Option B)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
A digital clock battery's charge level (in percent) decreases over time. At 4 hours, it's at 85% charge. At 12 hours, it's at 45% charge. If the battery discharges linearly, let C be the charge level (in percent) and h be the hours elapsed. Find the linear equation for the battery charge. When would the battery completely die (0% charge)?
A. C = -5h + 85. The battery would completely die at 17 hours.
B. C = -40h + 105. The battery would completely die at 2.625 hours.
C. C = 5h + 65. The battery would completely die at -13 hours (incorrect).
D. C = -5h + 105. The battery would completely die at 21 hours.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Point 1: (4 hours, 85% charge) -> (4, 85) Point 2: (12 hours, 45% charge) -> (12, 45)
These coordinates represent (time, charge level) and are essential for defining the linear discharge.
2
m = (C2 - C1) / (h2 - h1) m = (45 - 85) / (12 - 4) m = -40 / 8 m = -5
The negative slope indicates that the charge level is decreasing. The slope represents the percentage of charge lost per hour.
3
Using C = mh + b and point (4, 85): 85 = -5(4) + b 85 = -20 + b b = 85 + 20 b = 105
The y-intercept represents the estimated initial charge level (at h=0 hours).
4
Substitute m = -5 and b = 105 into C = mh + b: C = -5h + 105.
This equation models the battery's charge level over time.
5
Set C = 0 in the equation: 0 = -5h + 105 5h = 105 h = 105 / 5 h = 21
Setting the charge to zero and solving for 'h' finds the x-intercept, which is the time when the battery is fully discharged.

Answer: C = -5h + 105. The battery would completely die at 21 hours. (Option D)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A student is tracking their cumulative grade in a class. After 5 assignments, their average grade is 78%. After 12 assignments, their average grade is 86%. Assuming the grade improves linearly, let G be the average grade (in percent) and n be the number of assignments. What is the linear equation that models their grade, and what would their average grade be after 20 assignments?
A. G = 8n + 38. After 20 assignments, the average grade would be 198%.
B. G = (8/7)n + (506/7). After 20 assignments, the average grade would be approximately 95.14%.
C. G = (8/7)n + 78. After 20 assignments, the average grade would be approximately 100.86%.
D. G = (1/7)n + 77.29. After 20 assignments, the average grade would be approximately 80.15%.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Point 1: (5 assignments, 78% grade) -> (5, 78) Point 2: (12 assignments, 86% grade) -> (12, 86)
Each data point (number of assignments, average grade) helps to define the linear relationship.
2
m = (G2 - G1) / (n2 - n1) m = (86 - 78) / (12 - 5) m = 8 / 7
The slope represents the increase in average grade per assignment.
3
Using G = mn + b and point (5, 78): 78 = (8/7)(5) + b 78 = 40/7 + b b = 78 - 40/7 b = (546/7) - (40/7) b = 506/7
The y-intercept represents the estimated average grade at 0 assignments, or the theoretical starting point for the linear progression.
4
Substitute m = 8/7 and b = 506/7 into G = mn + b: G = (8/7)n + (506/7).
This equation models the student's average grade as a function of the number of assignments.
5
Substitute n = 20 into the equation: G = (8/7)(20) + 506/7 G = 160/7 + 506/7 G = 666/7 G ≈ 95.14%
Using the derived equation, we can predict the grade for any given number of assignments.

Answer: G = (8/7)n + (506/7). After 20 assignments, the average grade would be approximately 95.14%. (Option B)
