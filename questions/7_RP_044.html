Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A grocery store sells apples and oranges in a ratio of 3:5. If they want to scale up their inventory to have 21 apples, how many oranges should they have?
A. 35
B. 42
C. 21
D. 15
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Ratio of apples to oranges = 3:5. New number of apples = 21.
Understanding the initial ratio and the target quantity is the first step.
2
Scaling factor = New number of apples / Original number of apples in ratio = 21 / 3 = 7.
The scaling factor tells us how many times the original quantities in the ratio have been multiplied to reach the new quantities.
3
Number of oranges = Original number of oranges in ratio Scaling factor = 5 7 = 35.
To maintain the proportional relationship, the number of oranges must also be multiplied by the same scaling factor.

Answer: 35 (Option A)

Question 10Grade 7 • RP
A 3D printer takes 45 minutes to print 3 small prototypes. If you need to print 7 small prototypes, how many minutes will it take, assuming the same print speed?
A. 60
B. 135
C. 90
D. 105
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Ratio of minutes to prototypes = 45:3. New number of prototypes = 7.
Understanding the printer's speed ratio and the target number of prototypes is the initial step.
2
Unit rate = Total minutes / Number of prototypes = 45 / 3 = 15 minutes/prototype.
Finding the time required for one prototype simplifies scaling to any number of prototypes.
3
Total time = Unit rate New number of prototypes = 15 7 = 105 minutes.
Multiply the time per prototype by the desired number of prototypes to find the total time.

Answer: 105 (Option D)
