Question 1Grade 8 • NS
Sarah has $12. She wants to buy small gifts that each cost $3/4. How many gifts can Sarah buy?
A. 12 gifts
B. 9 gifts
C. 4 gifts
D. 16 gifts
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total money = $12
This is the total quantity to be divided.
2
Cost per gift = $3/4
This is the size of each part we are dividing by.
3
$12 \div \frac{3}{4
To find out how many gifts can be bought, divide the total money by the cost of each gift.
4
$12 \times \frac{4}{3
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of 3/4 is 4/3.
5
\frac{12}{1} \times \frac{4}{3} = \frac{48}{3
Multiply the numerators (12 × 4) and the denominators (1 × 3).
6
\frac{48}{3} = 16
Divide 48 by 3 to get the final number of gifts.

Answer: 16 gifts (Option D)

Question 8Grade 8 • NS
A nurse has 7 and 1/2 liters of a cough syrup solution. Each patient receives a dose of 3/8 of a liter. How many patients can the nurse provide with a full dose of cough syrup?
A. 15 patients
B. 22 patients
C. 18 patients
D. 20 patients
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total solution = 7 and 1/2 liters
This is the total quantity to be divided.
2
$7 \frac{1}{2} = \frac{(7 \times 2) + 1}{2} = \frac{15}{2}$ liters
To perform division with mixed numbers, convert them to improper fractions first.
3
Dose per patient = 3/8 liter
This is the size of each part we are dividing by.
4
$\frac{15}{2} \div \frac{3}{8
To find out how many patients can receive a full dose, divide the total solution by the dose per patient.
5
$\frac{15}{2} \times \frac{8}{3
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of 3/8 is 8/3.
6
$\frac{15 \times 8}{2 \times 3} = \frac{120}{6
Multiply the numerators (15 × 8) and the denominators (2 × 3).
7
\(\frac{120}{6} = 20\)
Divide 120 by 6 to get the final number of patients. Since the question asks for 'full dose', ignore any remainder.

Answer: 20 patients (Option D)

Question 9Grade 8 • NS
An artist has a large canvas with an area of 22 and 3/4 square feet. She wants to divide it into smaller rectangular sections, each with an area of 1 and 1/8 square feet, for a mosaic project. How many sections can she create? Express your answer in simplest form.
A. 20 and 1/2 sections
B. 23 sections
C. 25 and 1/2 sections
D. 20 and 2/9 sections
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total area = 22 and 3/4 square feet
This is the total quantity to be divided.
2
$22 \frac{3}{4} = \frac{(22 \times 4) + 3}{4} = \frac{88 + 3}{4} = \frac{91}{4}$ square feet
Convert the mixed number to an improper fraction for easier calculation.
3
Area per section = 1 and 1/8 square feet
This is the size of each part we are dividing by.
4
$1 \frac{1}{8} = \frac{(1 \times 8) + 1}{8} = \frac{8 + 1}{8} = \frac{9}{8}$ square feet
Convert the mixed number to an improper fraction.
5
$\frac{91}{4} \div \frac{9}{8
To find out how many sections can be created, divide the total area by the area of each section.
6
$\frac{91}{4} \times \frac{8}{9
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of 9/8 is 8/9.
7
$\frac{91 \times 8}{4 \times 9} = \frac{728}{36
Multiply the numerators and the denominators.
8
Divide both numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 4: \(\frac{728 \div 4}{36 \div 4} = \frac{182}{9}\)
Simplify the fraction before converting to a mixed number.
9
$\frac{182}{9} = 20 \frac{2}{9
Divide 182 by 9. The quotient is 20 with a remainder of 2, so the mixed number is 20 and 2/9.

Answer: 20 and 2/9 sections (Option D)

Step-by-step solution

Total area = 25 and 1/4 acres
This is the total quantity to be divided.
2
$25 \frac{1}{4} = \frac{(25 \times 4) + 1}{4} = \frac{100 + 1}{4} = \frac{101}{4}$ acres
To perform division with mixed numbers, convert them to improper fractions first.
3
Area per plot = 7/16 acre
This is the size of each part we are dividing by.
4
$\frac{101}{4} \div \frac{7}{16
To find out how many garden plots can be created, divide the total area by the area of each plot.
5
$\frac{101}{4} \times \frac{16}{7
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of 7/16 is 16/7.

$\frac{101 \times 16}{4 \times 7} = \frac{1616}{28

7

8
\(\frac{404}{7} = 57 \frac{5}{7}\). Since the question asks for 'full garden plots', we take the whole number part, which is 57.
Divide 404 by 7 to get the number of full plots and any remaining fraction.
