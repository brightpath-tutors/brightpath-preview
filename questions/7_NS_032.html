B. Emily
C. Cannot be determined
D. Sarah
E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1
The question asks to identify who has 'more money.' In the context of bank balances, a higher numerical value (even if negative) means more money.
We need to determine which negative balance represents a greater amount of money.
2
Sarah's balance: -$15 Emily's balance: -$8
These are the two integer values we need to compare.
3
We need to compare -15 and -8.
To compare integers, we can think about their positions on a number line.
4
On a number line, numbers increase as you move to the right. -8 is to the right of -15.
Since -8 is to the right of -15, -8 is greater than -15.
5
Therefore, -8 > -15. Emily has more money in her account.
A balance of -$8 means a smaller debt compared to -$15, thus representing more money.
