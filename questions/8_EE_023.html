Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Sarah bought a new book for $12. If she has $3 left after the purchase, how much money did she start with?
A. $12
B. $15
C. $9
D. $18
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the amount of money Sarah started with. The cost of the book is $12, and she has $3 left. So, the equation is x - 12 = 3.
We represent the unknown starting amount with 'x'. When she spends money, it's subtracted. The remaining amount is on the other side of the equals sign.
2
To find 'x', we need to undo the subtraction of 12. We do this by adding 12 to both sides of the equation: x - 12 + 12 = 3 + 12.
To maintain equality, any operation performed on one side of the equation must also be performed on the other side. Adding 12 to both sides cancels out the -12 on the left side, isolating 'x'.
3
x = 15.
Adding 3 and 12 gives 15. Therefore, Sarah started with $15.

Answer: $15 (Option B)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
A basketball team scored 80 points in total during a game. If they scored 45 points in the first half, how many points did they score in the second half?
A. 35 points
B. 45 points
C. 80 points
D. 125 points
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the points scored in the second half. The total points are 80, and 45 points were scored in the first half. So, the equation is 45 + x = 80.
We know the points from the first half and the total points. We add the unknown second half points ('x') to the first half points to get the total.
2
To find 'x', we need to undo the addition of 45. We do this by subtracting 45 from both sides of the equation: 45 + x - 45 = 80 - 45.
Subtracting 45 from both sides cancels out the +45 on the left, leaving 'x' isolated. This maintains the equality of the equation.
3
x = 35.
Subtracting 45 from 80 gives 35. Therefore, the team scored 35 points in the second half.

Answer: 35 points (Option A)

Question 3Grade 8 • EE
A recipe calls for 1.5 cups of sugar. If you have already added 0.75 cups, how much more sugar do you need?

E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the amount of sugar still needed. The total required is 1.5 cups, and 0.75 cups have been added. So, the equation is 0.75 + x = 1.5.

2
To find 'x', subtract 0.75 from both sides of the equation: 0.75 + x - 0.75 = 1.5 - 0.75.
Subtracting 0.75 from both sides of the equation cancels it out on the left side, isolating 'x' and maintaining the balance of the equation.
3
x = 0.75.
Subtracting 0.75 from 1.5 gives 0.75. Therefore, you need 0.75 more cups of sugar.

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A cyclist rode at an average speed of 15 mph for a certain time. After a 2-hour break, they rode for another 1 hour at the same speed. If the total distance covered was 75 miles, how long did they ride before the break?
A. 3 hours
B. 6 hours
C. 4 hours
D. 5 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the time (in hours) the cyclist rode before the break. The distance covered in this period is 15x miles (Distance = Speed × Time).
We use 'x' for the unknown time. The fundamental formula for distance, speed, and time is applied to the first part of the ride.
2
The cyclist rode for another 1 hour at 15 mph, covering 15 × 1 = 15 miles.
This calculates the distance covered in the second segment of the ride using the given speed and time.
3
The total distance covered was 75 miles. So, 15x + 15 = 75.
The sum of the distances from both riding periods must equal the total distance given in the problem.
4
Subtract 15 from both sides of the equation: 15x + 15 - 15 = 75 - 15, which simplifies to 15x = 60.
To begin isolating 'x', we first move the constant term to the right side by performing the inverse operation (subtraction) on both sides.
5
Divide both sides by 15: 15x / 15 = 60 / 15, which gives x = 4.
Finally, to solve for 'x', we divide both sides by its coefficient (15). This tells us the number of hours ridden before the break.

Answer: 4 hours (Option C)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE
In a 7th-grade class, there are 28 students in total. If there are 4 more girls than boys, how many boys are in the class?
A. 14 boys
B. 16 boys
C. 12 boys
D. 10 boys
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'b' represent the number of boys.
We assign a variable to the unknown quantity we need to find, which is the number of boys.
2
Since there are 4 more girls than boys, the number of girls is b + 4.
We use the given relationship to write the number of girls in terms of 'b'.
3
The total number of students is 28. So, the number of boys plus the number of girls equals 28: b + (b + 4) = 28.
The sum of the number of boys and girls must equal the total number of students in the class.
4
Simplify the equation: b + b + 4 = 28 -> 2b + 4 = 28.
We combine the 'b' terms on the left side of the equation to simplify it.
5
Subtract 4 from both sides of the equation: 2b + 4 - 4 = 28 - 4, which simplifies to 2b = 24.
To start isolating 'b', we perform the inverse operation of adding 4, which is subtracting 4, from both sides.
6
Divide both sides by 2: 2b / 2 = 24 / 2, which gives b = 12.
Finally, we divide both sides by the coefficient of 'b' (which is 2) to find the value of 'b', representing the number of boys.

Answer: 12 boys (Option C)

Question 6Grade 8 • EE
The temperature in Celsius (°C) is 20 degrees less than half the temperature in Fahrenheit (°F). If the Celsius temperature is 10°C, what is the Fahrenheit temperature?
A. 60°F
B. 40°F
C. -20°F
D. 30°F
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'F' be the Fahrenheit temperature. The Celsius temperature 'C' is 10°C. The relationship given is C = (F/2) - 20. Substituting C, we get 10 = (F/2) - 20.
We translate the word problem into a mathematical equation. 'Half the temperature in Fahrenheit' is F/2, and '20 degrees less than' means subtracting 20 from that value.
2
To undo the subtraction of 20, add 20 to both sides of the equation: 10 + 20 = (F/2) - 20 + 20, which simplifies to 30 = F/2.
We use the inverse operation to move the constant term to the other side of the equation, starting to isolate the term containing 'F'.
3
To undo the division by 2, multiply both sides by 2: 30 × 2 = (F/2) × 2, which gives 60 = F.
Multiplying both sides by 2 cancels out the division by 2, revealing the value of 'F'.

Answer: 60°F (Option A)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A builder needs to cut a 12-foot metal beam into two pieces. One piece must be twice as long as the other. What is the length of the shorter piece?
A. 8 feet
B. 6 feet
C. 2 feet
D. 4 feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the length of the shorter piece.
We assign a variable to the length of the shorter, unknown piece.
2
The longer piece is twice as long as the shorter piece, so its length is 2x.
Using the given relationship, we express the length of the longer piece in terms of 'x'.
3
The total length of the beam is 12 feet. So, the sum of the two pieces equals 12: x + 2x = 12.
The total length of the beam is the sum of the lengths of the two pieces.
4
Simplify the equation: x + 2x = 12 -> 3x = 12.
We combine the variable terms on the left side of the equation to simplify it.
5
Divide both sides by 3: 3x / 3 = 12 / 3, which gives x = 4.
To find 'x', we divide both sides by its coefficient (3).

Answer: 4 feet (Option D)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
John started working on a complex project at 9:00 AM and finished at 5:00 PM. He spent twice as much time researching as he did writing, and 1.5 hours less on editing than on researching. How much time did he spend writing? Round to the nearest tenth if necessary.
A. 2.1 hours
B. 3.8 hours
C. 1.9 hours
D. 1.5 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
From 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM is 8 hours (17:00 - 9:00 = 8).
First, determine the total duration John worked on the project.
2
Let 'w' be the time John spent writing (in hours).
We assign a variable to the time spent writing, as this is what the question asks for.
3
Research time = 2w (twice as much as writing). Editing time = 2w - 1.5 (1.5 hours less than researching).
Translate the given relationships between different project phases into algebraic expressions using 'w'.
4
The sum of all times must equal the total work time: w + 2w + (2w - 1.5) = 8.
The total time spent on all parts of the project must add up to the overall work time.
5
Combine the 'w' terms: (w + 2w + 2w) - 1.5 = 8, which simplifies to 5w - 1.5 = 8.
Simplify the equation by combining all the terms that contain 'w'.
6
Add 1.5 to both sides of the equation: 5w - 1.5 + 1.5 = 8 + 1.5, which simplifies to 5w = 9.5.
To isolate the term with 'w', we perform the inverse operation of subtracting 1.5, which is adding 1.5, to both sides of the equation.
7
Divide both sides by 5: 5w / 5 = 9.5 / 5, which gives w = 1.9.
Divide both sides by the coefficient of 'w' to find its value. The result is 1.9 hours.

Answer: 1.9 hours (Option C)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
The population of a small town increased by a net of 150 residents over 5 years. In the first 3 years, the population increased by an average of 60 residents per year. In the last 2 years, the population decreased by an average of 'x' residents per year. What is the value of 'x'?
A. 75 residents per year
B. 10 residents per year
C. 15 residents per year
D. 30 residents per year
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
In the first 3 years, the population increased by 3 years × 60 residents/year = 180 residents.
Determine the total increase in population during the initial period by multiplying the average annual increase by the number of years.
2
In the last 2 years, the population decreased by 2 years × x residents/year = 2x residents.
Represent the total decrease in population during the latter period as an expression involving the unknown variable 'x'.
3
The net increase over 5 years is the increase from the first period minus the decrease from the second period: 180 - 2x = 150.
Formulate an equation where the net change (total increase) is the difference between the initial increase and the subsequent decrease.
4
Subtract 180 from both sides of the equation: 180 - 2x - 180 = 150 - 180, which simplifies to -2x = -30.
To isolate the term containing 'x', move the constant term (180) to the right side by subtracting it from both sides.
5
Divide both sides by -2: -2x / -2 = -30 / -2, which gives x = 15.
Divide both sides by the coefficient of 'x' (-2) to solve for 'x'. A negative divided by a negative results in a positive. Thus, the average decrease was 15 residents per year.

Answer: 15 residents per year (Option C)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A mobile phone plan charges a flat fee of $30 per month plus $0.50 per GB of data used from the standard allowance. This month, a user paid $52.50 in total, which included the flat fee, the data usage, and an additional data bundle of 5 GB for a fixed price of $10. How many GB of data did they use from the standard allowance?
A. 25 GB
B. 15 GB
C. 20 GB
D. 35 GB
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'g' be the number of GB used from the standard allowance.
We assign a variable to the unknown quantity, which is the amount of data used from the standard allowance.
2
Flat fee: $30. Cost for standard data usage: 0.50g. Fixed price for data bundle: $10. Total bill: $52.50.
Identify and list all components contributing to the total phone bill.
3
The sum of all costs equals the total bill: 30 + 0.50g + 10 = 52.50.
Construct an equation by adding all the cost components and setting them equal to the total amount paid.
4
Combine the numerical constants on the left side: (30 + 10) + 0.50g = 52.50, which simplifies to 40 + 0.50g = 52.50.
Simplify the equation by adding together the known dollar amounts (flat fee and bundle cost).
5
Subtract 40 from both sides of the equation: 40 + 0.50g - 40 = 52.50 - 40, which simplifies to 0.50g = 12.50.
Perform the inverse operation to move the combined constant (40) to the right side, isolating the term that includes 'g'.
6
Divide both sides by 0.50: 0.50g / 0.50 = 12.50 / 0.50, which gives g = 25.
Divide both sides by the rate (0.50) to find the number of gigabytes used from the standard allowance. Dividing by 0.50 is equivalent to multiplying by 2.

Answer: 25 GB (Option A)
