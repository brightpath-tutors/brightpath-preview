Question 1Grade 4 • OA
Maria buys 3 notebooks for $2 each and a pencil case for $5. She pays with a $20 bill. How much change does she receive?
A. $9
B. $13
C. $11
D. $15
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost of notebooks = 3 notebooks * $2/notebook = $6
First, calculate the total cost of the notebooks by multiplying the number of notebooks by the cost per notebook.
2
Total cost = $6 (notebooks) + $5 (pencil case) = $11
Next, add the cost of the notebooks and the pencil case to find the total amount Maria spent.
3
Change received = $20 (paid) - $11 (total cost) = $9
Finally, subtract the total cost from the amount Maria paid to find the change she received.

Answer: $9 (Option A)

A. 5 points
B. 11 points
C. 10 points
D. 1 point
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students

Question 5Grade 4 • OA
For a school field trip, 75 students are going. Each minivan can hold 15 students. The school pays $50 for each minivan. Additionally, there is a $8 entrance fee per student. What is the total cost for the field trip?
A. $650
B. $800
C. $850
D. $325
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Number of minivans needed = 75 students / 15 students/minivan = 5 minivans
Divide the total number of students by the capacity of each minivan to find out how many minivans are needed.
2
Cost of minivans = 5 minivans * $50/minivan = $250
Multiply the number of minivans by the cost per minivan to find the total cost for transportation.
3
Cost of entrance fees = 75 students * $8/student = $600
Multiply the total number of students by the entrance fee per student to find the total cost for entrance fees.
4
Total cost for the trip = $250 (minivans) + $600 (entrance fees) = $850
Add the total cost of minivans and the total cost of entrance fees to get the grand total for the field trip.

Answer: $850 (Option C)

Question 6Grade 4 • OA
A scientist has 3 beakers, each containing 150 milliliters of solution A. She pours all of solution A into a large container. She then adds 250 milliliters of solution B to the container. If the container can hold a maximum of 1000 milliliters, how much more solution can she add?
A. 600 milliliters
B. 400 milliliters
C. 300 milliliters
D. 700 milliliters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total solution A = 3 beakers * 150 milliliters/beaker = 450 milliliters
Calculate the total volume of solution A by multiplying the number of beakers by the volume in each beaker.
2
Total solution in container = 450 milliliters (solution A) + 250 milliliters (solution B) = 700 milliliters
Add the total volume of solution A and solution B to find the current total volume in the large container.
3
Remaining capacity = 1000 milliliters (max capacity) - 700 milliliters (current total) = 300 milliliters
Subtract the current total volume from the maximum capacity of the container to find how much more solution can be added.

Answer: 300 milliliters (Option C)

Question 8Grade 4 • OA
A concert's performance time is 2 hours and 45 minutes. There is also a 30-minute intermission. If the concert starts at 7:00 PM, and people arrive 15 minutes before the concert begins and stay for 10 minutes after the entire event (performance + intermission) ends, what is the total duration, in minutes, from when people arrive to when they leave?
A. 220 minutes
B. 205 minutes
C. 190 minutes
D. 180 minutes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Performance time in minutes = (2 hours * 60 minutes/hour) + 45 minutes = 120 + 45 = 165 minutes
Convert the performance time from hours and minutes into total minutes.
2
Total event duration = 165 minutes (performance) + 30 minutes (intermission) = 195 minutes
Add the performance time to the intermission time to find the total duration of the concert event.
3
Total time people are present = 15 minutes (before arrival) + 195 minutes (event) + 10 minutes (after departure) = 220 minutes
Add the time people arrive before the event, the total event duration, and the time they stay after the event to find the total duration they are present.

Answer: 220 minutes (Option A)

Question 9Grade 4 • OA
A wildlife sanctuary had 500 animals. In January, 1/5 of the animals were moved to another sanctuary. In February, 80 new animals were born. In March, 1/4 of the total animals at that time were adopted. How many animals were left in the sanctuary at the end of March?
A. 360 animals
B. 300 animals
C. 460 animals
D. 375 animals
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Animals moved in January = 500 animals / 5 = 100 animals
Calculate the number of animals moved in January by dividing the initial population by 5.
2
Animals remaining after January = 500 - 100 = 400 animals
Subtract the moved animals from the initial population to find the number of animals remaining.
3
Animals after February births = 400 (remaining) + 80 (newborn) = 480 animals
Add the new animals born in February to the remaining population.
4
Animals adopted in March = 480 animals / 4 = 120 animals
Calculate the number of animals adopted in March by dividing the current total population by 4.
5
Animals left at the end of March = 480 (before adoption) - 120 (adopted) = 360 animals
Subtract the adopted animals from the population before adoption to find the final number of animals.

Answer: 360 animals (Option A)

Question 10Grade 4 • OA
A company's server has a total storage capacity of 1000 gigabytes (GB). Currently, 3 files, each 75 GB in size, are stored on it. There are also 2 backup folders, each containing 150 GB of data. How much more storage space, in GB, is available on the server?
A. 525 GB
B. 475 GB
C. 500 GB
D. 700 GB
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Storage used by 3 files = 3 files * 75 GB/file = 225 GB
Calculate the total storage used by the files by multiplying the number of files by the size of each file.
2
Storage used by 2 backup folders = 2 folders * 150 GB/folder = 300 GB
Calculate the total storage used by the backup folders by multiplying the number of folders by the size of each folder.
3
Total storage used = 225 GB (files) + 300 GB (backups) = 525 GB
Add the storage used by files and backup folders to find the total storage currently in use.
4
Available storage = 1000 GB (total capacity) - 525 GB (total used) = 475 GB
Subtract the total used storage from the server's total capacity to find the remaining available space.

Answer: 475 GB (Option B)
