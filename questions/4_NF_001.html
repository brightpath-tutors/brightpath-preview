Question 1Grade 4 • NF
A store is having a sale where a toy costs 1/2 of its original price. To make it easier for customers to see the discount, the store decides to show the price in terms of fourths. Using a visual fraction model, explain why 1/2 is equivalent to 2/4.
A. 2/4
B. 3/4
C. 1/4
D. 1/3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Original fraction: 1/2. Target denominator: 4.
Identify the initial fraction and the target denominator for the equivalent fraction.
2
Draw a rectangle divided into 2 equal parts. Shade 1 part to represent 1/2.
Create a visual model for the initial fraction 1/2.
3
To change the denominator from 2 to 4, multiply the denominator by 2 (4 ÷ 2 = 2). This means n = 2.
Determine the multiplier (n) needed to get the new denominator.
4
Divide each of the 2 original parts in the model into 2 smaller equal parts.
Apply the multiplier visually by subdividing each existing part.
5
Total parts: 2 2 = 4. Shaded parts: 1 2 = 2.
Count the total number of new parts and the total number of shaded parts in the new model.
6
Equivalent fraction: 2/4. This shows that 1/2 = (1 × 2) / (2 × 2) = 2/4.
Form the equivalent fraction and show the mathematical principle.

Answer: 2/4 (Option A)

Question 8Grade 4 • NF
A student spends 5/8 of their study time on math. If they want to track this time in segments that are 3 times smaller than their current segments, effectively dividing their study time into 3 times as many total units, what equivalent fraction represents the time spent on math? Explain using a visual fraction model, showing how (n × a) / (n × b) applies.
A. 5/24
B. 10/24
C. 15/24
D. 8/24
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Original fraction: 5/8. The phrase 'segments that are 3 times smaller' means the total units are multiplied by 3, so n = 3.
Identify the initial fraction and interpret the problem to find the multiplier (n).
2
Draw a strip divided into 8 equal parts. Shade 5 parts to represent 5/8.
Create a visual model for the fraction 5/8.
3
To find the equivalent fraction, multiply both the numerator (a) and the denominator (b) by n = 3.
State the principle of finding equivalent fractions.
4
New numerator: 5 × 3 = 15. New denominator: 8 × 3 = 24.
Perform the multiplication for both the numerator and the denominator.
5
Equivalent fraction: 15/24. This shows that 5/8 = (5 × 3) / (8 × 3) = 15/24.
Form the equivalent fraction and clearly state the mathematical relationship.
6
Visually, divide each of the original 8 parts in the model into 3 smaller equal parts. Observe that there are 24 total parts and 15 shaded parts.
Describe how the visual model confirms the equivalent fraction after subdivision.

Answer: 15/24 (Option C)
