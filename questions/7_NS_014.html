Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A store is having a sale, and the price of an item dropped by $1.75. Which point on the number line represents the change in price, where 0 is the original price?
A. Point at -1.075
B. Point at 1.75
C. Point at -1.75
D. Point at -2.75
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The price 'dropped' by $1.75, which indicates a decrease. Therefore, the number representing this change will be negative. We need to locate -1.75 on the number line.
A drop in price means the value is less than the original, so it's represented by a negative number.
2
The number is -1.75. First, find -1 on the number line.
The number is between -1 and -2.
3
After -1, move towards -2. The decimal 0.75 is three-quarters of the way between -1 and -2. This means it's halfway between -1.5 and -2.
0.75 is equivalent to 3/4. The point should be exactly 3/4 of the distance from -1 to -2.
4
Place a point at -1.75 on the number line.
This point accurately reflects a $1.75 decrease from 0.

Answer: Point at -1.75 (Option C)

E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution

Understanding the scale is crucial for accurate placement. Each tick represents 1/4.
2

Converting to a common denominator (or equivalent representation) makes comparison and placement easier.
3
Since 1/2 is equivalent to 2/4, locate the second tick mark from 0.
Counting two 1/4 segments from 0 brings us to the point representing 2/4, or 1/2.
