Question 1Grade 7 • EE
A small movie theater sells tickets for $8 for adults and $5 for children. On a particular evening, 300 tickets were sold, and the total revenue was $2100. How many adult tickets were sold?
A. 150 adult tickets
B. 250 adult tickets
C. 200 adult tickets
D. 100 adult tickets
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'a' be the number of adult tickets sold. Let 'c' be the number of children's tickets sold.
Assign variables to the unknown quantities to set up the system of equations.
2
Equation 1 (total tickets): a + c = 300 Equation 2 (total revenue): 8a + 5c = 2100
Formulate two equations based on the given information: one for the total number of tickets and one for the total revenue.
3
Multiply Equation 1 by -5 to eliminate 'c': -5(a + c) = -5(300) -5a - 5c = -1500
To eliminate 'c', we need the coefficients of 'c' in both equations to be additive inverses. Multiplying the first equation by -5 achieves this.
4
(-5a - 5c) + (8a + 5c) = -1500 + 2100 3a = 600
Add the two equations together. The 'c' terms cancel out, leaving an equation with only 'a'.
5
3a = 600 a = 600 / 3 a = 200
Divide both sides by 3 to find the number of adult tickets sold.
6
200 adult tickets were sold.
The question asks for the number of adult tickets, which is 'a'.

Answer: 200 adult tickets (Option C)
