Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A store is having a clearance sale, offering an additional \(\frac{1}{4}\) off the sale price. If an item is already marked down to \(\frac{2}{3}\) of its original price, what fraction of the original price is the final sale price? (in simplest form)
A. \(\frac{8}{9}\)
B. \(\frac{1}{2}\)
C. \(\frac{1}{3}\)
D. \(\frac{5}{7}\)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
An additional \(\frac{1}{4}\) off means the customer pays $1 - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{4}$ of the sale price.
If an item is \(\frac{1}{4}\) off, you pay $1 - \frac{1}{4}$ of the price.
2
Final sale price = (Initial sale price fraction) \(\times\) (Fraction remaining after additional discount) Final sale price = \(\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{3}{4}\)
To find the final fraction of the original price, multiply the two fractions representing the successive price reductions.
3
$2 \times 3 = 6$
Multiply the top numbers of the fractions together.
4
$3 \times 4 = 12$
Multiply the bottom numbers of the fractions together.
5
Resulting fraction = \(\frac{6}{12}\)
The product is the new numerator over the new denominator.
6
To simplify \(\frac{6}{12}\), divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 6. \(\frac{6 \div 6}{12 \div 6} = \frac{1}{2}\)
Divide both the numerator and denominator by the largest number that divides into both evenly.

Answer: \(\frac{1}{2}\) (Option B)

Question 2Grade 7 • NS
A basketball player makes $0.6$ of his free throws. If he attempts $15$ free throws in a game, how many free throws is he expected to make?
A. $15.6$
B. $90.0$
C. $0.9$
D. $9.0$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The player makes $0.6$ of his free throws, and he attempts $15$ free throws. So we need to calculate $0.6 \times 15$.
To find the number of free throws he's expected to make, multiply the fraction (as a decimal) of throws made by the total number of attempts.
2
$6 \times 15 = 90$
Perform standard multiplication as if the numbers were whole numbers.
3
The number $0.6$ has one decimal place. The number $15$ has zero decimal places. Therefore, the product must have $1 + 0 = 1$ decimal place. Moving the decimal point one place from the right in $90$ gives $9.0$.
The total number of decimal places in the product is the sum of the decimal places in the numbers being multiplied.

Answer: $9.0$ (Option D)

Question 3Grade 7 • NS
A recipe calls for \(\frac{2}{3}\) of a cup of milk. If you want to make $2$ full batches of the recipe, how many cups of milk do you need? (in simplest form)
A. \(\frac{4}{3}\)
B. \(\frac{2}{5}\)
C. \(\frac{2}{6}\)
D. \(\frac{4}{6}\)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
You need \(\frac{2}{3}\) of a cup of milk per batch and you want to make $2$ batches. So, you need to calculate $2 \times \frac{2}{3}$.
To find the total milk needed, multiply the amount per batch by the number of batches.
2
$2 = \frac{2}{1}$
Any whole number can be written as a fraction by placing it over 1.
3
\(\frac{2}{1} \times \frac{2}{3} = \frac{2 \times 2}{1 \times 3} = \frac{4}{3}\)
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.
4
The fraction \(\frac{4}{3}\) cannot be simplified further as the greatest common divisor of 4 and 3 is 1.
A fraction is in simplest form when its numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1. (It can also be written as a mixed number $1 \frac{1}{3}$, but the prompt asks for simplest form, which is an improper fraction in this case).

Answer: \(\frac{4}{3}\) (Option A)

Question 4Grade 7 • NS
A submarine descends at an average rate of $-1.2$ meters per second. If it descends for $25.5$ seconds, what is its total change in depth?
A. $-3.06$
B. $24.3$
C. $-30.6$
D. $30.6$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The rate of descent is $-1.2$ meters per second, and the time is $25.5$ seconds. To find the total change in depth, we multiply the rate by the time: $-1.2 \times 25.5$.
To find the total change, multiply the rate of change by the duration.
2
Multiply $1.2 \times 25.5$: First, multiply as whole numbers: $12 \times 255 = 3060$.
Ignore the decimal points and the sign for now, and multiply the whole numbers.
3
The number $1.2$ has one decimal place. The number $25.5$ has one decimal place. So, the product must have $1 + 1 = 2$ decimal places. Counting two places from the right in $3060$ gives $30.60$.
The total number of decimal places in the product is the sum of the decimal places in the factors.
4
We are multiplying a negative number ($-1.2$) by a positive number ($25.5$). The product of a negative and a positive number is always negative.
Remember the rule for multiplying integers: negative times positive equals negative.
5
The product is $-30.60$, which simplifies to $-30.6$.
The final answer is the absolute value with the determined sign.

Answer: $-30.6$ meters (Option C)

Question 5Grade 7 • NS
A student spends $1 \frac{1}{2}$ hours on homework each day. If they complete their homework for $4 \frac{1}{3}$ days this week, how many total hours did they spend on homework? (in simplest form)
A. $5 \frac{1}{6}$
B. $12 \frac{1}{2}$
C. $6 \frac{1}{2}$
D. $4 \frac{1}{6}$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$1 \frac{1}{2} = \frac{(1 \times 2) + 1}{2} = \frac{3}{2}$ $4 \frac{1}{3} = \frac{(4 \times 3) + 1}{3} = \frac{13}{3}$
To multiply mixed numbers, first convert them into improper fractions. Multiply the whole number by the denominator and add the numerator, keeping the same denominator.
2
\(\frac{3}{2} \times \frac{13}{3} = \frac{3 \times 13}{2 \times 3} = \frac{39}{6}\)
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.
3
The fraction \(\frac{39}{6}\) can be simplified by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 3. \(\frac{39 \div 3}{6 \div 3} = \frac{13}{2}\)
Divide both the numerator and denominator by the largest number that divides into both evenly.
4
To convert \(\frac{13}{2}\) to a mixed number, divide 13 by 2. $13 \div 2 = 6$ with a remainder of $1$. So, \(\frac{13}{2} = 6 \frac{1}{2}\).
The quotient is the whole number part, and the remainder becomes the numerator over the original denominator.

Answer: $6 \frac{1}{2}$ hours (Option C)

Question 6Grade 7 • NS
A chemical solution has a concentration of $0.45$. If a scientist needs to use \(\frac{2}{5}\) of this solution for an experiment, what is the resulting concentration of the portion used? (Express as a decimal)
A. $0.18$
B. $0.018$
C. $0.85$
D. $1.125$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The fraction \(\frac{2}{5}\) can be converted to a decimal by dividing the numerator by the denominator: $2 \div 5 = 0.4$.
To multiply a decimal by a fraction, it's often easiest to convert the fraction to a decimal first.
2
Now multiply $0.45 \times 0.4$. First, multiply the numbers as if they were whole numbers: $45 \times 4 = 180$.
Perform standard multiplication, ignoring decimal points for now.
3
The number $0.45$ has two decimal places. The number $0.4$ has one decimal place. The sum of the decimal places is $2 + 1 = 3$. Therefore, the product must have three decimal places. Counting three places from the right in $180$ gives $0.180$, which is $0.18$.
The total number of decimal places in the product is the sum of the decimal places in the numbers being multiplied.

Answer: $0.18$ (Option A)

1
The required depth is $-\frac{7}{8}$ meters, and the crew digs to \(\frac{3}{4}\) of that depth. So we multiply $-\frac{7}{8} \times \frac{3}{4}$.
To find the actual depth, multiply the required depth by the fraction of the depth dug.
2
Multiply the absolute values of the numerators: $7 \times 3 = 21$.
Multiply the top numbers of the fractions.
3
Multiply the denominators: $8 \times 4 = 32$.
Multiply the bottom numbers of the fractions.
4
The product of the absolute values is \(\frac{21}{32}\).
The product is the new numerator over the new denominator.
5
We are multiplying a negative number ($-\frac{7}{8}$) by a positive number (\(\frac{3}{4}\)). The product of a negative and a positive number is always negative.
Remember the rule for multiplying integers: negative times positive equals negative.
6
The fraction $-\frac{21}{32}$ cannot be simplified further as the greatest common divisor of 21 and 32 is 1.
A fraction is in simplest form when its numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1.

Question 8Grade 7 • NS
A project manager estimates that a specific task will take $3 \frac{1}{4}$ days to complete. However, due to unforeseen delays, the task now requires $1 \frac{3}{5}$ times the original estimated time. How many days will the task actually take? (in simplest form)
A. $5 \frac{1}{5}$
B. $3 \frac{3}{20}$
C. $4 \frac{4}{9}$
D. $2 \frac{1}{10}$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$3 \frac{1}{4} = \frac{(3 \times 4) + 1}{4} = \frac{13}{4}$ $1 \frac{3}{5} = \frac{(1 \times 5) + 3}{5} = \frac{8}{5}$
To multiply mixed numbers, first convert them into improper fractions. Multiply the whole number by the denominator and add the numerator, keeping the same denominator.
2
Multiply \(\frac{13}{4} \times \frac{8}{5}\). We can cross-simplify before multiplying: Divide 4 (denominator of first fraction) and 8 (numerator of second fraction) by their greatest common divisor, which is 4. \(\frac{13}{\cancel{4}_1} \times \frac{\cancel{8}^2}{5} = \frac{13 \times 2}{1 \times 5} = \frac{26}{5}\)
Multiply the numerators and the denominators. Cross-simplifying beforehand can make the numbers smaller and easier to work with.
3
To convert \(\frac{26}{5}\) to a mixed number, divide 26 by 5. $26 \div 5 = 5$ with a remainder of $1$. So, \(\frac{26}{5} = 5 \frac{1}{5}\).
The quotient is the whole number part, and the remainder becomes the numerator over the original denominator.

Answer: $5 \frac{1}{5}$ days (Option A)

Question 10Grade 7 • NS
An artist is mixing a custom glaze for pottery. She starts with a base liquid that weighs $4 \frac{1}{2}$ pounds. She adds a special additive, which has a weight of $-0.15$ times the base liquid's weight (a negative factor means it causes a reduction in the overall mass, perhaps due to evaporation or chemical reaction). Then, she decides to use only \(\frac{2}{3}\) of this mixture for a specific batch. What is the effective weight (in pounds) of the additive in this specific batch? (Express as a decimal, rounded to two decimal places).
A. $0.45$
B. $-0.90$
C. $-0.45$
D. $-0.68$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$4 \frac{1}{2} = 4.5$ pounds
Convert the mixed number to a decimal for easier multiplication with other decimals.
2
Weight of additive = (Base liquid weight) \(\times\) (Additive factor) Weight of additive = $4.5 \times (-0.15)$ First, multiply $45 \times 15 = 675$. $4.5$ has one decimal place, $0.15$ has two decimal places. Total $1+2=3$ decimal places. So, $0.675$. Since we are multiplying a positive number by a negative number, the result is negative: $-0.675$ pounds.
Multiply the base weight by the additive's weight factor. Remember to handle the decimal placement and the sign correctly.
3
The artist uses only \(\frac{2}{3}\) of this mixture, so we need to multiply the additive's weight by \(\frac{2}{3}\). Effective weight = $-0.675 \times \frac{2}{3}$. Convert $-0.675$ to a fraction: $-0.675 = -\frac{675}{1000}$. Simplify the fraction: Divide numerator and denominator by 25: $- \frac{675 \div 25}{1000 \div 25} = -\frac{27}{40}$. Now multiply: $-\frac{27}{40} \times \frac{2}{3}$. Cross-simplify: Divide 27 by 3 (gives 9) and 3 by 3 (gives 1). Divide 2 by 2 (gives 1) and 40 by 2 (gives 20). So, $-\frac{9}{20} \times \frac{1}{1} = -\frac{9}{20}$.
To find the portion of the additive's weight used, multiply its calculated weight by the fraction of the mixture used. Converting the decimal to a fraction can simplify the multiplication and ensure an exact answer before converting back to a decimal.
4
To express $-\frac{9}{20}$ as a decimal, divide 9 by 20: $9 \div 20 = 0.45$. So, the effective weight is $-0.45$ pounds.
Divide the numerator by the denominator to convert the fraction to a decimal.

Answer: $-0.45$ pounds (Option C)
