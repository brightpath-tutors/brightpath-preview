Rational Numbers on Number Line

Skill ID: 7.NS.039

Question 1Grade 7 • NS
Marcus checked his checking account balance and noticed a transaction for -$23.75. Which point on the number line accurately represents this change in his balance?
A. A point located at -23.00.
B. A point located between -20 and -25, closer to -20.
C. A point located between -20 and -25, closer to -25.
D. A point located between 20 and 25, closer to 25.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
A change of -$23.75 indicates a decrease in the balance.
Negative numbers represent a decrease or movement to the left on a number line.
2
The absolute value of 23.75 is between 20 and 25. Since it's negative, it will be between -20 and -25.
On a number line, negative numbers decrease as you move further left from zero.
3
0.75 is closer to 1 than to 0. So, -23.75 is closer to -25 than to -20.
The decimal part helps to pinpoint the number's exact location between two integers.

Answer: A point located between -20 and -25, closer to -25. (Option C)

Question 4Grade 7 • NS
A hiker starts at a base camp (0). They hike 7/8 miles forward, then turn back 1/4 miles. What is their final position relative to the base camp, represented on a number line?
A. A point at 5/8.
B. A point at 6/8.
C. A point at 3/8.
D. A point at 9/8.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Hiker moves 7/8 miles forward, so starting position is 0, then +7/8.
Forward movement is represented by a positive value.
2
Hiker turns back 1/4 miles, which means a negative movement of -1/4.
Turning back is represented by a negative value.
3
Final position = 7/8 - 1/4. To subtract, find a common denominator: 1/4 = 2/8. So, 7/8 - 2/8 = 5/8.
Subtracting rational numbers requires a common denominator. The result is a positive fraction, located to the right of zero.

Answer: A point at 5/8. (Option A)

C. -1/5
D. -0.3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Convert -1/5 to a decimal: -1 ÷ 5 = -0.2.
Comparing decimals is generally easier than comparing a mix of decimals and fractions.
2
We need to compare -0.3 and -0.2. On a number line, numbers further to the left are smaller.
For negative numbers, the number with the larger absolute value is smaller (further left).
3
-0.3 is smaller than -0.2, so -0.3 is further to the left on the number line.
Imagine moving left from zero: -0.2 is reached first, then -0.3.

Question 6Grade 7 • NS
A science team recorded an elevation change of -3/4 meters for a submersible. Then, it recorded another change of -1/2 meters. What is the total elevation change, relative to the starting point (0), in simplest form, represented on a number line?
A. A point at -1/4.
B. A point at -2/4.
C. A point at -4/6.
D. A point at -5/4.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial change: -3/4 meters. Subsequent change: -1/2 meters.
Both changes are negative, meaning downward movement from the starting point.
2
Total change = -3/4 + (-1/2). Find a common denominator: -1/2 = -2/4. So, -3/4 + (-2/4) = -5/4.
Adding two negative numbers results in a larger negative number. Keep the sign and add their absolute values.
3
-5/4 is an improper fraction but cannot be simplified further (no common factors between 5 and 4).
Always check for simplification after performing operations on fractions.

Answer: -5/4 (Option D)

A. Both are at the same distance from zero.
B. -3/32 inch
C. 1/16 inch
D. It depends on the zero point.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
1/16 inch too long means +1/16. -3/32 inch too short means -3/32.
Positive values represent 'too long' (right of zero), and negative values represent 'too short' (left of zero).
2
We need to compare +1/16 and -3/32. Any negative number is always to the left of any positive number on a number line.
This is a fundamental property of number lines: negatives are left, positives are right.
3
Since -3/32 is a negative number and +1/16 is a positive number, -3/32 is further to the left.
No complex calculations are needed, just understanding the signs.

Answer: -3/32 inch (Option B)

Question 9Grade 7 • NS
The population of a rare species of fish changed by +1/8 in one year (relative to its initial population). The next year, it changed by -0.2 of its initial population. What is the net change in the fish population over these two years, relative to the initial population (0), in simplest form?
A. 3/40
B. -0.075
C. -1/40
D. -3/40
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Year 1: +1/8. Year 2: -0.2 (relative to initial).
Positive for increase, negative for decrease. Both changes are relative to the 'initial' population, so they are directly additive.
2
0.2 = 2/10 = 1/5.
Converting decimals to fractions or vice versa allows for consistent calculation.
3
Net change = 1/8 + (-1/5) = 1/8 - 1/5. Find a common denominator, which is 40. 1/8 = 5/40. 1/5 = 8/40. So, 5/40 - 8/40 = -3/40.
Subtracting a larger number from a smaller number results in a negative value. Keep the common denominator and subtract the numerators.
4
-3/40 is in simplest form as 3 and 40 share no common factors other than 1.
Final fractions should always be simplified.

Answer: -3/40 (Option D)

Question 10Grade 7 • NS
A drone's battery level was at 1/3 of its full charge. After a short flight, it used up 0.4 of its full charge. What fraction of the full charge is remaining, in simplest form, represented on a number line?
A. -1/15
B. 1/15
C. 11/15
D. -2/15
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial level: +1/3. Charge used: -0.4 (since it was used up).
Initial charge is positive; charge used is negative as it's a reduction.
2
0.4 = 4/10 = 2/5.
Using fractions makes it easier to work with 1/3.
3
Remaining charge = 1/3 - 2/5. Find a common denominator, which is 15. 1/3 = 5/15. 2/5 = 6/15. So, 5/15 - 6/15 = -1/15.
Subtracting 6/15 from 5/15 results in a negative value, indicating the battery is 'below zero' or indicates a deficit.
4
-1/15 is in simplest form.
Check for common factors in the numerator and denominator.

Answer: -2/15 (Option A)
