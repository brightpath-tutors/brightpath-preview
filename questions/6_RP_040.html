Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A small business owner takes out a loan of $800 to buy new inventory. The loan has a simple interest rate of 4% per year. If the loan is for 3 years, how much simple interest will the owner pay?
A. $896.00
B. $96.00
C. $9,600.00
D. $32.00
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
P = $800 r = 4% = 0.04 t = 3 years
The principal is the initial amount of the loan. The interest rate must be converted from a percentage to a decimal by dividing by 100. The time is given in years.
2
I = 800 × 0.04 × 3
Substitute the identified values into the formula.
3
I = 32 × 3 I = $96
Multiply the principal by the decimal rate and then by the time in years to find the total simple interest.

Answer: $96.00 (Option B)

Question 8Grade 6 • RP

A. $1,200.00
B. $12.00
C. $18.75
D. $6,000.00
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
I = $150 r = 2.5% = 0.025 t = 5 years
The interest is the amount charged. The interest rate must be converted from a percentage to a decimal. The time is given in years.
2
P = 150 ÷ (0.025 × 5)
To find the principal, divide the total interest by the product of the rate and time.
3
P = 150 ÷ 0.125 P = $1,200
First, multiply the rate and time in the denominator. Then, divide the interest by this product.

Answer: $1,200.00 (Option A)

A. 0.03 years
B. 10,800 years
C. 60 years
D. 3 years
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
I = 180 (population growth) P = 3,000 (initial population) r = 2% = 0.02
The 'interest' here represents the population growth. The 'principal' is the initial population. The rate must be converted from a percentage to a decimal.
2
t = 180 ÷ (3,000 × 0.02)
To find the time, divide the total growth by the product of the initial population and the growth rate.
3
t = 180 ÷ 60 t = 3 years
First, multiply the principal and rate in the denominator. Then, divide the growth by this product.

Answer: 3 years (Option D)
