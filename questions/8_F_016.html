Question 1Grade 8 • F
A small toy rocket's height (h) in meters above the ground after t seconds is modeled by the function h(t) = -4.9t^2 + 30t + 10. Which of the following best describes this type of function?
A. Quadratic function.
B. Exponential function.
C. Linear function.
D. Absolute value function.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
In h(t) = -4.9t^2 + 30t + 10, the variable is 't'. The powers of 't' are 2 (in -4.9t^2) and 1 (in 30t). The constant term 10 has t to the power of 0.
The exponents on the variable 't' are important for classifying the function type.
2
The highest power of 't' is 2. Functions with a highest power of 2 for the independent variable are quadratic functions.
A quadratic function is defined by its highest degree term being a squared variable (e.g., x^2).

Answer: Quadratic function. (Option A)

Question 2Grade 8 • F
The path of a baseball hit by a player is described by the equation y = -0.01x^2 + x + 2, where y is the height in meters and x is the horizontal distance in meters. In which direction does the parabola representing the baseball's path open?
A. Upwards
B. Downwards
C. Left
D. Right
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
In the equation y = -0.01x^2 + x + 2, the coefficient of the x^2 term is -0.01.
The sign of the coefficient of the squared term (a) determines the direction a parabola opens.
2
Since the coefficient of the x^2 term (-0.01) is negative, the parabola opens downwards.
If 'a' > 0, the parabola opens upwards. If 'a' < 0, the parabola opens downwards.

Answer: Downwards (Option B)

D. (1, 4)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The lowest point of an upward-opening parabola is its vertex.

2

A. -10 degrees Celsius
B. 200 degrees Celsius
C. 0 degrees Celsius
D. 2 degrees Celsius
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The 'initial' temperature refers to the temperature when the time (m) is 0 minutes.
The initial value of a function is its y-intercept, which occurs when the independent variable (in this case, m) is zero.
2
T(0) = 2(0)^2 - 10(0) + 200 T(0) = 0 - 0 + 200 T(0) = 200
When m=0, the terms with 'm' become zero, leaving only the constant term.

Question 6Grade 8 • F
A modern art sculpture has a parabolic shape described by the equation y = 2x^2 - 16x + 35. What is the equation of the axis of symmetry for this parabolic sculpture?
A. x = -4
B. y = 4
C. x = 4
D. x = 35
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The equation is in the form y = ax^2 + bx + c. Here, a = 2, b = -16, and c = 35.
The coefficients 'a', 'b', and 'c' are used in the formula for the axis of symmetry.
2
The formula for the axis of symmetry is x = -b / (2a). x = -(-16) / (2 * 2) x = 16 / 4 x = 4
Substitute the identified 'a' and 'b' values into the formula and simplify to find the equation of the axis of symmetry.

Answer: x = 4 (Option C)

Question 7Grade 8 • F
The distance (d) in feet a car travels after the brakes are applied is given by the function d(s) = 0.05s^2 + s, where s is the speed in miles per hour. If a car is traveling at 20 mph, how much further does it travel compared to a car traveling at 10 mph?
A. 5 feet
B. 25 feet
C. 10 feet
D. 30 feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
d(10) = 0.05(10)^2 + 10 d(10) = 0.05(100) + 10 d(10) = 5 + 10 d(10) = 15 feet
Substitute s=10 into the function to find the braking distance at 10 mph.
2
d(20) = 0.05(20)^2 + 20 d(20) = 0.05(400) + 20 d(20) = 20 + 20 d(20) = 40 feet
Substitute s=20 into the function to find the braking distance at 20 mph.
3
Difference = d(20) - d(10) Difference = 40 - 15 Difference = 25 feet
Subtract the smaller distance from the larger distance to find how much further the car travels.

Answer: 25 feet (Option B)

Question 8Grade 8 • F
The population of a certain species of fish in a lake is modeled by P(t) = -50t^2 + 1000t + 5000, where P is the population and t is the number of years since 2000. What is the maximum population of fish according to this model?
A. 10,000 fish
B. 9000 fish
C. 5000 fish
D. 50,000 fish
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
For P(t) = at^2 + bt + c, the axis of symmetry is t = -b / (2a). Here, a = -50 and b = 1000. t = -1000 / (2 * -50) t = -1000 / -100 t = 10 years
The maximum or minimum value of a quadratic function occurs at its vertex, which lies on the axis of symmetry.
2
P(10) = -50(10)^2 + 1000(10) + 5000 P(10) = -50(100) + 10000 + 5000 P(10) = -5000 + 10000 + 5000 P(10) = 10000
This value represents the maximum population because the parabola opens downwards (since a = -50 is negative).

Answer: 10,000 fish (Option A)

Question 9Grade 8 • F
A small business's daily profit P (in hundreds of dollars) can be modeled by the function P(u) = -0.5(u - 4)(u - 10), where u is the number of units sold (in tens). At what number of units sold does the business break even (i.e., profit is zero)?
A. 4 units and 10 units
B. 7 units
C. 70 units
D. 40 units and 100 units
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
P(u) = -0.5(u - 4)(u - 10) = 0
Breaking even means that the profit is zero. So, we set the function equal to zero and solve for 'u'.
2
For the product to be zero, one or more of the factors must be zero. (u - 4) = 0 => u = 4 (u - 10) = 0 => u = 10
The Zero Product Property states that if a product of factors is zero, then at least one of the factors must be zero. The -0.5 factor does not affect the roots.
3
The problem states that 'u' is the number of units sold (in tens). So, u=4 means 4 10 = 40 units, and u=10 means 10 10 = 100 units.
Always refer back to the problem description to correctly interpret the meaning of the variables and their units.

Answer: 40 units and 100 units. (Option D)

Question 10Grade 8 • F
The concentration (C) of a medication in a patient's bloodstream (in mg/L) after t hours is modeled by C(t) = -0.2t^2 + 2t. Does a concentration of 3.2 mg/L occur after 2 hours?
A. No, the concentration is 2.4 mg/L after 2 hours.
B. No, the concentration is 2.8 mg/L after 2 hours.
C. Yes, it also occurs at 8 hours.
D. Yes, the concentration is 3.2 mg/L after 2 hours.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
C(2) = -0.2(2)^2 + 2(2) C(2) = -0.2(4) + 4 C(2) = -0.8 + 4 C(2) = 3.2
To check if a specific concentration occurs at a given time, substitute the time value into the function and evaluate.
2
The calculated concentration after 2 hours is 3.2 mg/L, which matches the target concentration of 3.2 mg/L.
Since the calculated value matches the question's specified concentration, the statement is true.
3
Set C(t) = 3.2: -0.2t^2 + 2t = 3.2 -0.2t^2 + 2t - 3.2 = 0 Multiply by -10: 2t^2 - 20t + 32 = 0 Divide by 2: t^2 - 10t + 16 = 0 Factor: (t - 2)(t - 8) = 0 So, t=2 or t=8.
A quadratic function can have two x-values (times) for a single y-value (concentration), unless it's the vertex.

Answer: Yes, the concentration is 3.2 mg/L after 2 hours. (Option D)
