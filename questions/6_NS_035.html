Question 3Grade 6 • NS
The temperature in a freezer was 5 degrees Celsius. It then dropped by 8 degrees Celsius. What is the new temperature inside the freezer?
A. -13 degrees Celsius
B. -3 degrees Celsius
C. 13 degrees Celsius
D. 3 degrees Celsius
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial temperature = 5°C
The freezer started at 5 degrees Celsius.
2
Change in temperature = -8°C
A drop of 8 degrees is represented by subtracting 8.
3
New temperature = 5 - 8
To find the new temperature, subtract the drop from the initial temperature.
4
5 - 8 = 5 + (-8) = -3
When subtracting a larger number from a smaller number, the result is negative.

Question 4Grade 6 • NS
A submarine was at a depth of -150 feet (150 feet below sea level). Its initial depth was -75 feet. What is the difference between its current depth and its initial depth?
A. 225 feet
B. -225 feet
C. 75 feet
D. -75 feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Current depth = -150 feet
The submarine is currently 150 feet below sea level.
2
Initial depth = -75 feet
The initial position was 75 feet below sea level.
3
Difference = -150 - (-75)
To find the difference, subtract the initial depth from the current depth.
4
-150 - (-75) = -150 + 75
Subtracting a negative number is the same as adding its positive counterpart.
5
-150 + 75 = -75
When adding numbers with different signs, subtract their absolute values and keep the sign of the number with the larger absolute value. (150 - 75 = 75, and since 150 > 75, the result is negative).

Answer: -75 feet (Option D)
