Question 1Grade 5 • OA
Sarah went shopping and bought 3 notebooks for $2 each and 5 pens for $1 each. Her mom gave her $20. How much money does Sarah have left after her purchases?
A. $9
B. $15
C. $14
D. $13
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost of notebooks = 3 * 2 = $6
Calculate the total cost of the notebooks.
2
Cost of pens = 5 * 1 = $5
Calculate the total cost of the pens.
3
Total cost = 6 + 5 = $11
Add the cost of notebooks and pens to find the total spending.
4
Money left = 20 - 11 = $9
Subtract the total cost from the initial amount of money to find the remaining balance.

Answer: $9 (Option A)

Question 7Grade 5 • OA

E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total bags of cement = 10 * 50 = 500
Calculate the total weight of cement ordered.
2
Bags used for foundation = 3 * 50 = 150
Calculate the weight of cement used for the foundation.
3
Remaining bags after foundation = 10 - 3 = 7
Calculate the number of bags remaining after the foundation work.
4
Bags used for walls = 7 / 2 = 3.5 bags (or 3 full bags and half of another)

6
Total cement used = 150 + 175 = 325

Cement left = 500 - 325 = 175

Question 10Grade 5 • OA
A company produces 2 types of microchips. Type A costs $15 to make and Type B costs $20 to make. In a month, they produced 100 Type A microchips and 75 Type B microchips. If they sold all the microchips for $30 each, what was their total profit?
A. $2500
B. $2000
C. $1750
D. $2250
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost of Type A microchips = 100 * 15 = 1500
Calculate the total manufacturing cost for Type A microchips.
2
Cost of Type B microchips = 75 * 20 = 1500
Calculate the total manufacturing cost for Type B microchips.
3
Total manufacturing cost = 1500 + 1500 = 3000
Add the costs of both types of microchips to find the total manufacturing cost.
4
Total microchips produced = 100 + 75 = 175
Calculate the total number of microchips produced.
5
Total revenue = 175 * 30 = 5250
Multiply the total microchips by the selling price per microchip to find the total revenue.
6
Total profit = 5250 - 3000 = 2250
Subtract the total manufacturing cost from the total revenue to find the total profit.

Answer: $2000 (Option D)
