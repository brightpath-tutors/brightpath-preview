Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A shopper has a store credit balance of -$5 (meaning they owe the store $5). If this debt triples, what is the new amount of money owed?
A. -$8
B. -$2
C. $15
D. -$15
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial debt = -$5, Multiplier = 3
The problem states an initial debt of $5, which is represented as -$5. The debt 'triples', meaning it is multiplied by 3.
2
(-5) * 3
To find the new amount of money owed, we multiply the initial debt by the factor by which it triples.
3
|-5| |3| = 5 3 = 15
First, multiply the numerical parts of the integers without considering their signs.
4
Negative Positive = Negative, so -5 3 = -15
When multiplying a negative integer by a positive integer, the result is always a negative integer. Therefore, the new amount of money owed is -$15.

Answer: -$15 (Option D)
