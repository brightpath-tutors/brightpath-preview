Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A baker uses 3 cups of sugar for every 2 cakes she bakes. If she bakes 8 cakes, how many cups of sugar will she use?
A. 16 cups
B. 10 cups
C. 6 cups
D. 12 cups
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Sugar : Cakes = 3 : 2
The problem states that 3 cups of sugar are used for every 2 cakes.
2
3/2 = x/8
Let 'x' be the unknown amount of sugar. We set up a proportion comparing the ratio of sugar to cakes in both scenarios.
3
2 x = 3 8 2x = 24 x = 24 / 2 x = 12
Cross-multiply to solve for x. (3 8 = 24) and (2 x = 2x). Then divide by 2 to find x.
4
The baker will use 12 cups of sugar.
The amount of sugar needed for 8 cakes is 12 cups.

Answer: 12 cups (Option D)

Question 10Grade 7 • RP
A certain type of bacteria doubles its population every 25 minutes. If there are initially 300 bacteria, how many will there be after 125 minutes?
A. 15000 bacteria
B. 9600 bacteria
C. 3750 bacteria
D. 1500 bacteria
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total time / Doubling time = 125 minutes / 25 minutes = 5 periods
Since the population doubles every 25 minutes, we divide the total time by the doubling time to find how many times the population will double.
2
Initial population = 300 After 1st period: 300 * 2 = 600 After 2nd period: 600 * 2 = 1200 After 3rd period: 1200 * 2 = 2400 After 4th period: 2400 * 2 = 4800 After 5th period: 4800 * 2 = 9600
Multiply the population by 2 for each doubling period.
3
There will be 9600 bacteria.
After 125 minutes, the population will be 9600 bacteria.

Answer: 9600 bacteria (Option B)
