Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A map uses a scale of 1 inch = 10 miles. If two cities are 3 inches apart on the map, what is the actual distance between them?
A. 3 miles
B. 30 miles
C. 0.3 miles
D. 13 miles
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Scale: 1 inch (map) = 10 miles (actual)
This step defines the relationship between the map distance and the actual distance.
2
Map distance = 3 inches
This is the measurement provided in the problem.
3
Actual distance = Map distance × (Actual miles / Map inch) Actual distance = 3 inches × (10 miles / 1 inch)
We multiply the map distance by the number of actual miles represented by each map inch.
4
Actual distance = 3 × 10 = 30 miles
The actual distance between the cities is 30 miles.

Answer: 30 miles (Option B)

A medical illustration shows a virus with a diameter of 3.5 cm. The actual diameter of the virus is 0.000007 mm. What is the scale of the illustration in the form 1 cm : x mm?
A. 1 cm : 0.000002 mm
B. 1 cm : 0.000007 mm
C. 1 cm : 0.0002 mm
D. 1 cm : 0.00002 mm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Illustration diameter = 3.5 cm Actual diameter = 0.000007 mm
These are the given values for the illustration and the real virus.
2
Desired format: 1 cm (illustration) : x mm (actual)
We need to set up a ratio that relates 1 cm on the illustration to its actual size in mm.
3
Actual diameter / Illustration diameter = x mm / 1 cm 0.000007 mm / 3.5 cm = x mm / 1 cm
We can directly find 'x' by dividing the actual measurement by the illustration measurement, assuming the units are handled correctly.
4
x = 0.000007 / 3.5
Perform the division to find the scale factor in mm per cm.
5
x = 0.000002
The scale factor is 0.000002. So, 1 cm on the illustration represents 0.000002 mm in reality.
6
Scale: 1 cm : 0.000002 mm
This is the final scale in the specified format.

Answer: 1 cm : 0.000002 mm (Option A)
