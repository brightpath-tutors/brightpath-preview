Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Sarah bought a book for $15 and 3 pens. She spent a total of $24. How much did each pen cost?
A. $3
B. $9
C. $6
D. $13
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'p' be the cost of each pen. Cost of book + Cost of pens = Total spent 15 + 3p = 24
We define 'p' as the unknown cost of each pen. The total cost is the sum of the book's cost and the cost of 3 pens.
2
15 + 3p = 24 3p = 24 - 15 3p = 9
To get the term '3p' by itself, we subtract 15 from both sides of the equation.
3
3p = 9 p = 9 / 3 p = 3
To find the value of 'p', we divide both sides of the equation by 3.

Answer: $3 (Option A)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
A basketball team scored 63 points in the first three quarters. In the fourth quarter, they scored some additional points, bringing their total score to 75 points. If they scored 3 points per successful shot in the fourth quarter, how many shots did they make?
A. 4 shots
B. 12 shots
C. 9 shots
D. 2 shots
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 's' be the number of successful shots in the fourth quarter. Points from first three quarters + Points from fourth quarter = Total score 63 + 3s = 75
We define 's' as the unknown number of shots. The total score is the sum of points from the first three quarters and points scored in the fourth quarter (3 points per shot).
2
63 + 3s = 75 3s = 75 - 63 3s = 12
To get the term '3s' by itself, we subtract 63 from both sides of the equation.
3
3s = 12 s = 12 / 3 s = 4
To find the value of 's', we divide both sides of the equation by 3.

Answer: 4 shots (Option A)

Question 3Grade 8 • EE
A chef is making a large batch of soup. He adds 20 milliliters of broth, then divides the mixture equally into 5 bowls. If each bowl contains 35 milliliters of soup, how much water did he add initially?
A. 155 milliliters
B. 15 milliliters
C. 195 milliliters
D. 7 milliliters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the initial amount of water in milliliters. (Initial water + Broth) / Number of bowls = Milliliters per bowl (x + 20) / 5 = 35
We define 'x' as the unknown initial amount of water. The total volume (water + broth) divided by the number of bowls gives the volume in each bowl.
2
(x + 20) / 5 = 35 x + 20 = 35 * 5 x + 20 = 175
To undo the division by 5, we multiply both sides of the equation by 5.
3
x + 20 = 175 x = 175 - 20 x = 155
To find the value of 'x', we subtract 20 from both sides of the equation.

Answer: 155 milliliters (Option A)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A train travels for 'h' hours at a speed of 60 mph. It then slows down to 40 mph for 0.5 hours. If the total distance covered was 380 miles, how many hours did it travel at 60 mph?
A. 5.67 hours
B. 6 hours
C. 360 hours
D. 6.67 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'h' be the number of hours traveled at 60 mph. Distance at 60 mph + Distance at 40 mph = Total distance 60h + 40(0.5) = 380 60h + 20 = 380
We define 'h' as the unknown hours at 60 mph. The total distance is the sum of the distance covered at 60 mph and the distance covered at 40 mph for 0.5 hours.
2
60h + 20 = 380 60h = 380 - 20 60h = 360
To get the term '60h' by itself, we subtract 20 from both sides of the equation.
3
60h = 360 h = 360 / 60 h = 6
To find the value of 'h', we divide both sides of the equation by 60.

Answer: 6 hours (Option B)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE
A student has completed 12 homework assignments so far this semester. This represents 3/4 of the total assignments for the semester. How many assignments are there in total for the semester?
A. 9 assignments
B. 16 assignments
C. 4 assignments
D. 48 assignments
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the total number of assignments. (3/4) of total assignments = Completed assignments (3/4)x = 12
We define 'x' as the unknown total number of assignments. 3/4 of 'x' is equal to the 12 assignments completed.
2
(3/4)x = 12 x = 12 * (4/3) x = 48 / 3 x = 16
To solve for 'x', we multiply both sides of the equation by the reciprocal of 3/4, which is 4/3.

Answer: 16 assignments (Option B)

Question 6Grade 8 • EE
A scientist is observing a bacterial culture. The current population of bacteria is 250. This is 50 less than twice the population observed last week. What was the population last week?
A. 100
B. 200
C. 600
D. 150
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the population last week. Twice the population last week minus 50 = Current population 2x - 50 = 250
We define 'x' as the unknown population last week. The current population (250) is described as '50 less than twice' last week's population (2x - 50).
2
2x - 50 = 250 2x = 250 + 50 2x = 300
To get the term '2x' by itself, we add 50 to both sides of the equation.
3
2x = 300 x = 300 / 2 x = 150
To find the value of 'x', we divide both sides of the equation by 2.

Answer: 150 (Option D)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A construction worker is laying bricks. He has laid 80 bricks, which is 10 more than half the number of bricks he needs to lay for the entire wall. How many bricks does he need for the entire wall?
A. 140 bricks
B. 70 bricks
C. 160 bricks
D. 35 bricks
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the total number of bricks needed for the wall. Half the total bricks plus 10 = Bricks laid (1/2)x + 10 = 80
We define 'x' as the unknown total number of bricks. The 80 bricks laid are described as '10 more than half' (1/2)x + 10.
2
(1/2)x + 10 = 80 (1/2)x = 80 - 10 (1/2)x = 70
To get the term '(1/2)x' by itself, we subtract 10 from both sides of the equation.
3
(1/2)x = 70 x = 70 * 2 x = 140
To find the value of 'x', we multiply both sides of the equation by 2 (the reciprocal of 1/2).

Answer: 140 bricks (Option A)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A digital timer started counting up from 0 at 10:00 AM. It has a slight error and adds 0.2 minutes for every actual hour that passes. If the timer currently shows 4 hours and 15 minutes, what is the actual time that has passed since 10:00 AM in hours? (Round to two decimal places)
A. 4.23 hours
B. 4.25 hours
C. 3.54 hours
D. 4.24 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Timer reading: 4 hours 15 minutes = 4 + 15/60 hours = 4 + 0.25 hours = 4.25 hours. Let 'x' be the actual hours passed. Error in minutes = 0.2x. Error in hours = 0.2x / 60. Actual hours + Error hours = Timer reading x + (0.2x)/60 = 4.25
We convert the timer's reading to a decimal value in hours. We define 'x' as the actual time in hours. The timer reading is the sum of the actual time and the accumulated error (0.2 minutes per actual hour, converted to hours).
2
x + (0.2x)/60 = 4.25 x + (1/300)x = 4.25 (300/300)x + (1/300)x = 4.25 (301/300)x = 4.25
We simplify the fraction 0.2/60 to 1/300. Then, we find a common denominator to combine the 'x' terms.
3
(301/300)x = 4.25 x = 4.25 * (300/301) x = 1275 / 301 x ≈ 4.23588... x ≈ 4.24
To find 'x', we multiply both sides by the reciprocal of (301/300). We then perform the division and round the result to two decimal places.

Answer: 4.24 hours (Option D)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
The population of a small town increased by 15% this year. If the current population is 1840, what was the population last year? (Round to the nearest whole number)
A. 1600
B. 1564
C. 1825
D. 2116
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the population last year. Increase = 15% of x = 0.15x Population last year + Increase = Current population x + 0.15x = 1840
We define 'x' as the unknown population last year. The current population is the sum of last year's population and the 15% increase.
2
x + 0.15x = 1840 1x + 0.15x = 1840 1.15x = 1840
We combine the 'x' terms (1x and 0.15x) to simplify the equation.
3
1.15x = 1840 x = 1840 / 1.15 x = 1600
To find 'x', we divide both sides of the equation by 1.15.

Answer: 1600 (Option A)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A healthcare clinic charges a $25 registration fee and $15 for each consultation. If a patient paid a total of $100, how many consultations did they have?
A. 6.67 consultations
B. 75 consultations
C. 8.33 consultations
D. 5 consultations
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'c' be the number of consultations. Registration fee + Cost of consultations = Total paid 25 + 15c = 100
We define 'c' as the unknown number of consultations. The total amount paid is the sum of the registration fee and the cost of 'c' consultations.
2
25 + 15c = 100 15c = 100 - 25 15c = 75
To get the term '15c' by itself, we subtract 25 from both sides of the equation.
3
15c = 75 c = 75 / 15 c = 5
To find the value of 'c', we divide both sides of the equation by 15.

Answer: 5 consultations (Option D)
