Question 1Grade 7 • G
A carpenter is building a fence and needs to cut two pieces of wood to fit perfectly into a corner. If one piece forms an angle of 35 degrees with the fence post, and the corner is a perfect right angle, what is the measure of the complementary angle formed by the second piece of wood?
A. 145°
B. 55°
C. 125°
D. 65°
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total angle for a right corner = 90 degrees
Identify that a right angle measures 90 degrees.
2
Complementary angle = 90 degrees - given angle
Recognize that complementary angles sum to 90 degrees.
3
Unknown angle = 90 - 35 = 55 degrees
Subtract the given angle from 90 to find the unknown angle.

Answer: 55 degrees (Option B)

Question 5Grade 7 • G
A display stand for jewelry is being designed. Two sections, when placed together, form a straight line, making them supplementary angles. The angle of the first section is represented by (3x + 10) degrees, and the second section by (2x + 10) degrees. What is the value of x?
A. 34
B. 32
C. 14
D. 30
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Supplementary angles sum to 180 degrees.
Recall the definition of supplementary angles.
2
(3x + 10) + (2x + 10) = 180
Set up the equation by summing the two angle expressions and equating to 180.
3
5x + 20 = 180
Combine like terms on the left side of the equation.
4
5x = 180 - 20
Subtract 20 from both sides of the equation.
5
5x = 160
Perform the subtraction.
6
x = 160 / 5
Divide both sides by 5 to solve for x.
7
x = 32
Calculate the value of x.

Answer: 32 (Option B)

Question 6Grade 7 • G
A drone's flight path is plotted on a grid, forming two intersecting lines. The angle of ascent is given as (4y - 15) degrees, and its vertical angle (angle of descent on the opposite side) is (2y + 25) degrees. What is the measure of the angle of ascent?
A. 65°
B. 20°
C. 40°
D. 95°
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Vertical angles are equal in measure.
Recall the property of vertical angles.
2
4y - 15 = 2y + 25
Set up the equation by equating the expressions for the two vertical angles.
3
4y - 2y = 25 + 15
Rearrange the equation to gather y terms on one side and constants on the other.
4
2y = 40
Combine like terms.
5
y = 40 / 2
Divide both sides by 2 to solve for y.
6
y = 20
Calculate the value of y.
7
Angle of ascent = 4y - 15 = 4(20) - 15
Substitute the value of y back into the expression for the angle of ascent.
8
Angle of ascent = 80 - 15 = 65 degrees
Calculate the measure of the angle of ascent.

Answer: 65 degrees (Option A)

Adjacent angles combine to form a larger angle.

2

∠ABC = 45 degrees

Question 10Grade 7 • G
A demographer is analyzing population growth data represented by intersecting lines on a graph. Two angles, (5x + 10) degrees and (2x + 70) degrees, are vertical angles. A third angle, (y + 15) degrees, is supplementary to (5x + 10) degrees. What is the value of y?
A. 55
B. 20
C. 110
D. 100
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Vertical angles are equal in measure.
Recall the property of vertical angles to set up the first equation.
2
5x + 10 = 2x + 70
Equate the expressions for the vertical angles.
3
5x - 2x = 70 - 10
Rearrange the equation to isolate x terms on one side and constants on the other.
4
3x = 60
Combine like terms.
5
x = 60 / 3
Divide both sides by 3 to solve for x.
6
x = 20
Calculate the value of x.
7
First angle = 5x + 10 = 5(20) + 10 = 100 + 10 = 110 degrees
Substitute x back into the expression for the first angle (5x + 10).
8
Supplementary angles sum to 180 degrees, so (y + 15) + 110 = 180
Set up the equation for the supplementary angles.
9
y + 15 = 180 - 110
Subtract 110 from both sides.
10
y + 15 = 70
Perform the subtraction.
11
y = 70 - 15
Subtract 15 from both sides.
12
y = 55
Calculate the value of y.

Answer: 55 (Option A)
