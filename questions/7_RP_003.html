Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A grocery store sells a small bottle of juice for $2 and a large bottle for $6. What is the ratio of the cost of the small bottle to the large bottle, in simplest form?
A. 3:1
B. 2:6
C. 1:2
D. 1:3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The ratio of the cost of the small bottle to the large bottle is $2:$6.
Identify the two quantities being compared and write them as a ratio in the given order.
2
To simplify the ratio $2:6$, find the greatest common divisor (GCD) of 2 and 6. The GCD is 2. Divide both parts of the ratio by the GCD: $2 \div 2 : 6 \div 2 = 1:3$.
Divide both parts of the ratio by their GCD to express it in simplest form.

Answer: 1:3 (Option D)
