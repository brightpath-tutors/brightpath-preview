Question 1Grade 8 • NS
A shop owner has 7 1/2 pounds of candy. If she wants to put 1 1/4 pounds of candy into each bag, how many bags can she fill completely?
A. 5 3/5 bags
B. 6 bags
C. 1/6 bag
D. 9 3/8 bags
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
7 1/2 = (7 × 2 + 1)/2 = 15/2 1 1/4 = (1 × 4 + 1)/4 = 5/4
To convert a mixed number to an improper fraction, multiply the whole number by the denominator, add the numerator, and place the result over the original denominator.
2
15/2 ÷ 5/4
Now the division problem is expressed with improper fractions, making it easier to solve.
3
15/2 × 4/5
Dividing by a fraction is equivalent to multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of 5/4 is 4/5.
4
(15 × 4) / (2 × 5) = 60 / 10
Multiply the top numbers together and the bottom numbers together.
5
60 / 10 = 6
Divide the numerator by the denominator to get the final whole number.

Answer: 6 bags (Option B)

Question 2Grade 8 • NS
A track event requires athletes to run a total distance of 10 2/3 miles. If each lap around the track is 1 1/3 miles long, how many laps do the athletes need to complete?
A. 8 laps
B. 7 1/2 laps
C. 1/8 lap
D. 14 2/9 laps
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
10 2/3 = (10 × 3 + 2)/3 = 32/3 1 1/3 = (1 × 3 + 1)/3 = 4/3
Multiply the whole number by the denominator and add the numerator to get the new numerator, keeping the same denominator.
2
32/3 ÷ 4/3
The problem is now ready for fraction division rules.
3
32/3 × 3/4
To divide fractions, invert the divisor (the second fraction) and multiply.
4
(32 × 3) / (3 × 4) = 96 / 12
Perform the multiplication across the numerators and denominators.
5
96 / 12 = 8
Divide the numerator by the denominator to find the total number of laps.

Answer: 8 laps (Option A)

Question 3Grade 8 • NS
A recipe calls for 5 1/4 cups of broth. If you only have a measuring cup that holds 3/4 of a cup, how many times will you need to fill the measuring cup to get the required amount of broth?
A. 3 15/16 times
B. 7 times
C. 16/63 times
D. 1 2/3 times
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
5 1/4 = (5 × 4 + 1)/4 = 21/4
Convert 5 1/4 to an improper fraction by multiplying the whole number (5) by the denominator (4) and adding the numerator (1). Keep the same denominator.
2
21/4 ÷ 3/4
The problem is now in a form suitable for fraction division.
3
21/4 × 4/3
To divide by 3/4, multiply by its reciprocal, which is 4/3.
4
(21 × 4) / (4 × 3) = 84 / 12
Multiply straight across: numerator by numerator and denominator by denominator.
5
84 / 12 = 7
Divide 84 by 12 to find the whole number result.

Answer: 7 times (Option B)

Question 4Grade 8 • NS
A car traveled 180 3/4 miles in 3 1/2 hours. What was its average speed in miles per hour, in simplest form?
A. 51 9/14 mph
B. 632 5/8 mph
C. 1446/28 mph
D. 120 1/2 mph
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
180 3/4 = (180 × 4 + 3)/4 = 723/4 3 1/2 = (3 × 2 + 1)/2 = 7/2
To find the average speed, we need to divide the total distance by the total time. First, convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions.
2
723/4 ÷ 7/2
The division is now set up with improper fractions.
3
723/4 × 2/7
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of 7/2 is 2/7.
4
(723 × 2) / (4 × 7) = 1446 / 28
Perform the multiplication of the fractions.
5
1446 / 28 = 723 / 14 (divide numerator and denominator by 2) 723 ÷ 14 = 51 with a remainder of 9 So, 51 9/14
Simplify the improper fraction by dividing the numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor (GCD). Then, convert the improper fraction to a mixed number for the final answer in simplest form.

Answer: 51 9/14 mph (Option A)
