Question 1Grade 5 • NF
A baker has 5 pounds of sugar. If each cake recipe requires 1/4 pound of sugar, how many cakes can the baker make?
A. 1.25 cakes
B. 5 cakes
C. 0.2 cakes
D. 20 cakes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total sugar = 5 pounds
Identify the total amount of sugar the baker has.
2
Sugar per cake = 1/4 pound
Identify the amount of sugar required for each cake recipe.
3
Number of cakes = 5 ÷ (1/4)
To find the number of cakes, divide the total sugar by the sugar per cake.
4
Number of cakes = 5 × 4 = 20
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal. The reciprocal of 1/4 is 4.

Answer: 20 cakes (Option D)
