Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A shopper tracks their bank account balance over a week. The daily balances are: -$16, $35, -$4. Order these balances from least to greatest.
A. -$4, -$16, $35
B. $35, -$16, -$4
C. $35, -$4, -$16
D. -$16, -$4, $35
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Balances: -16, 35, -4
The given daily balances are -16 dollars, 35 dollars, and -4 dollars. We need to order these numerical values.
2
Negative numbers are less than positive numbers. For two negative numbers, the one with the larger absolute value is smaller.
To order numbers from least to greatest, we first consider the negative numbers, then zero (if present), and finally positive numbers. Among negative numbers, the number further from zero (larger absolute value) is actually smaller in value.
3
Negative numbers: -16, -4 Absolute values: |-16| = 16, |-4| = 4 Since 16 > 4, then -16 < -4.
Comparing -16 and -4, since -16 is further from zero on the number line than -4, -16 is the smaller value. So, -16 comes before -4.
4
Positive number: 35 -16 < -4 < 35
The positive number 35 is greater than both negative numbers. Therefore, it will be the largest value in the ordered list.
5
Ordered list: -16, -4, 35
The final order from least to greatest is -16, -4, 35.

Answer: -$16, -$4, $35 (Option D)
