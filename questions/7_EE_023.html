D. $18.50
E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1
Cost of shirt = $18.50 Total bill = $24.75 Cost of socks = s (unknown)
We are given the cost of the shirt and the total bill, and we need to find the cost of the socks.
2
18.50 + s = 24.75
The cost of the shirt plus the cost of the socks equals the total bill.
3
18.50 + s - 18.50 = 24.75 - 18.50 s = 6.25

4
The cost of the socks was $6.25.

Question 4Grade 7 • EE
A car travels at a constant speed. After driving for 4 hours, it has covered 280 miles. If it had already traveled 40 miles before this 4-hour period, what was its speed during these 4 hours?
A. 60 mph
B. 50 mph
C. 120 mph
D. 80 mph
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Driving time = 4 hours Total distance covered = 280 miles Initial distance = 40 miles Speed during 4 hours = s (unknown)
We need to find the speed for the 4-hour period, knowing the total distance and a starting distance.
2
4s + 40 = 280
The distance covered during the 4 hours (4s) plus the initial distance (40) equals the total distance (280).
3
4s + 40 - 40 = 280 - 40 4s = 240
Subtract 40 from both sides to isolate the '4s' term.
4
4s / 4 = 240 / 4 s = 60
Divide both sides by 4 to solve for 's'.
5
The car's speed during these 4 hours was 60 mph.
The calculated value for 's' represents the speed.

Answer: 60 mph (Option A)

Cost of supplies = $75 Selling price per t-shirt = $12 Total profit = $201 Number of t-shirts sold = t (unknown)

2
12t - 75 = 201

3
12t - 75 + 75 = 201 + 75 12t = 276

4
12t / 12 = 276 / 12 t = 23

5
They sold 23 t-shirts.
The calculated value for 't' represents the number of t-shirts sold.

Question 8Grade 7 • EE
Mark and Julia are reading the same book. Mark has already read 50 pages and reads 15 pages per day. Julia has read 20 pages and reads 20 pages per day. After how many days will they have read the same number of pages?
A. 3.5 days
B. 6 days
C. 14 days
D. 1.5 days
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Mark's initial pages = 50 Mark's reading rate = 15 pages/day Julia's initial pages = 20 Julia's reading rate = 20 pages/day Number of days = d (unknown)
We need to find the number of days until their total pages read are equal.
2
Mark's total pages: 50 + 15d Julia's total pages: 20 + 20d Equation: 50 + 15d = 20 + 20d
Set the expressions for Mark's and Julia's total pages equal to each other.
3
50 - 20 = 20d - 15d 30 = 5d
Subtract 15d from both sides and subtract 20 from both sides to gather like terms.
4
30 / 5 = 5d / 5 d = 6
Divide both sides by 5 to solve for 'd'.
5
They will have read the same number of pages after 6 days.
The calculated value for 'd' represents the number of days.

Answer: 6 days (Option B)

D. 100 years
E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1

We need to find when the populations of the two cities will be equal.
2
City A population: 12500 + 300y City B population: 10000 + 400y Equation: 12500 + 300y = 10000 + 400y
Set the expressions for City A's and City B's populations equal to each other.
3
12500 - 10000 = 400y - 300y 2500 = 100y
Subtract 300y from both sides and subtract 10000 from both sides to gather like terms.
4
2500 / 100 = 100y / 100 y = 25
Divide both sides by 100 to solve for 'y'.
5
Their populations will be equal in 25 years.
The calculated value for 'y' represents the number of years.

Answer: 25 years (Option B)

B. 60 GB
C. 0.25 GB
D. 25 GB
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Plan A fixed fee = $35 Plan A rate = $0.50/GB Plan B fixed fee = $20 Plan B rate = $0.75/GB Data usage = g (unknown)
We need to find the data usage at which the total cost for both plans is equal.
2
Cost for Plan A: 35 + 0.50g Cost for Plan B: 20 + 0.75g Equation: 35 + 0.50g = 20 + 0.75g
Set the expressions for the costs of Plan A and Plan B equal to each other.
3
35 - 20 = 0.75g - 0.50g 15 = 0.25g
Subtract 0.50g from both sides and subtract 20 from both sides to gather like terms.
4
15 / 0.25 = 0.25g / 0.25 g = 60
Divide both sides by 0.25 to solve for 'g'.
5
The two plans will cost the same for 60 GB of data usage.
The calculated value for 'g' represents the data usage.

Answer: 60 GB (Option B)
