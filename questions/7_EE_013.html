Question 1Grade 7 • EE
A baker wants to make at least 35 cupcakes. She has already made 11 cupcakes. If she can bake 6 cupcakes per batch, what is the minimum number of batches, b, she needs to bake?
A. b ≥ 7
B. b ≥ 4
C. b ≥ 3
D. b ≤ 4
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total cupcakes needed: at least 35 Cupcakes already made: 11 Cupcakes per batch: 6
The baker wants to make 'at least' 35 cupcakes, which means the total should be greater than or equal to 35. She has 11 already and adds batches of 6.
2
11 + 6b ≥ 35
The number of cupcakes already made (11) plus the number of cupcakes from 'b' batches (6b) must be greater than or equal to 35.
3
6b ≥ 35 - 11 6b ≥ 24
To isolate the term with 'b', subtract 11 from both sides of the inequality.
4
b ≥ 24 / 6 b ≥ 4
Divide both sides by 6 to solve for 'b'. Since we are dividing by a positive number, the inequality sign remains the same.
5
b ≥ 4
The minimum number of batches she needs to bake is 4.

Answer: b ≥ 4 (Option B)

Question 10Grade 7 • EE
A scientist is growing bacteria in a petri dish. She starts with 100 bacteria and observes that the population doubles every hour. If she wants the population to be at most 1000 bacteria, what is the maximum number of doublings, h (hours), that can occur?
A. h ≥ 10
B. h ≤ 3.32
C. h ≤ 10
D. h ≥ 3.32
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial population: 100 Target population: at most 1000 Growth factor: doubles (multiplies by 2)
The scientist wants the population to be 'at most' 1000, so the total population must be less than or equal to 1000. The population starts at 100 and doubles (multiplies by 2) 'h' times.
2
100 * 2^h ≤ 1000
The initial population (100) multiplied by 2 raised to the power of 'h' (number of doublings) must be less than or equal to 1000.
3
2^h ≤ 1000 / 100 2^h ≤ 10
To isolate the exponential term, divide both sides by 100.
4
log(2^h) ≤ log(10) h * log(2) ≤ log(10) h ≤ log(10) / log(2) h ≤ 1 / 0.301 (approximately) h ≤ 3.32 (approximately)
Take the logarithm of both sides to bring down the exponent. Then divide by log(2) to solve for 'h'. Since log(2) is positive, the inequality sign remains the same.
5
h ≤ 3.32
The maximum number of doublings that can occur is approximately 3.32 hours.

Answer: h ≤ 3.32 (Option B)
