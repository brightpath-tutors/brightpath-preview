Question 1Grade 7 • NS
Sarah had $50 in her bank account. She withdrew $25. What is her new account balance?
A. $75
B. -$25
C. $25
D. $0
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial balance = $50
Sarah started with $50 in her account, which is a positive value.
2
Withdrawal = $25
Withdrawing money means taking it out, so this is a negative change to the balance.
3
$50 - $25 = $25
Subtract the withdrawn amount from the initial balance to find the new balance.

Answer: $25 (Option C)

Question 2Grade 7 • NS
A football team gained 12 yards on one play and then lost 5 yards on the next play. What was the total change in yardage?
A. 17 yards
B. -7 yards
C. 7 yards
D. 5 yards
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Gained 12 yards = +12
A gain is represented by a positive number.
2
Lost 5 yards = -5
A loss is represented by a negative number.
3
+12 + (-5) = 12 - 5 = 7
Add the positive and negative changes to find the net change. When adding a negative number, it is equivalent to subtraction.

Question 9Grade 7 • NS
A town's population decreased by 250 people in January, then increased by 180 people in February. In March, it decreased by another 300 people. What is the net change in population over these three months?
A. 370 people
B. 730 people
C. -370 people
D. -230 people
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
January: -250 February: +180 March: -300
Decreases are negative, and increases are positive.
2
-250 + 180 - 300
Add the changes sequentially to find the total net change.
3
-250 + 180 = -70
Combine the first two numbers. Since the signs are different, subtract their absolute values (250 - 180 = 70) and use the sign of the number with the larger absolute value (-250).
4
-70 - 300 = -370
Combine the result with the last change. Since the signs are the same (both negative), add their absolute values (70 + 300 = 370) and keep the negative sign.

Answer: -370 people (Option C)

Question 10Grade 7 • NS
A patient's blood pressure changed by -8 mmHg, then by +12 mmHg, and finally by -5 mmHg over three consecutive readings. What is the overall change in blood pressure?
A. 1 mmHg
B. -25 mmHg
C. -1 mmHg
D. 25 mmHg
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Change 1: -8 mmHg Change 2: +12 mmHg Change 3: -5 mmHg
These are the given changes in order.
2
-8 + 12 - 5
Add the positive and negative changes to find the net effect.
3
-8 + 12 = 4
Combine -8 and 12. Since the signs are different, subtract their absolute values (12 - 8 = 4) and use the sign of the number with the larger absolute value (+12).
4
4 - 5 = -1
Combine 4 and -5. Since the signs are different, subtract their absolute values (5 - 4 = 1) and use the sign of the number with the larger absolute value (-5).

Answer: -1 mmHg (Option C)
