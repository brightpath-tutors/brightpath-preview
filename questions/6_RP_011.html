Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A pair of jeans costs $40. There is a 25% discount. What is the discount amount in dollars?
A. $10
B. $160
C. $25
D. $1000
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
We need to find 25% of $40.
The problem asks for the discount amount, which is a percentage of the original price.
2
25% = 25/100 = 0.25
To calculate a percentage of a number, it's often easiest to convert the percentage to its decimal form by dividing by 100.
3
0.25 * $40 = $10
Multiply the decimal equivalent of the percentage by the original price to find the discount amount.

Answer: $10 (Option A)

Question 2Grade 6 • RP
In a basketball game, Elena attempted 30 shots and made 21 of them. What percentage of her shots did Elena make?
A. 7%
B. 14.29%
C. 70%
D. 21%
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Part (made shots) = 21, Whole (total shots) = 30
To find the percentage, we need to compare the number of made shots (part) to the total number of shots (whole).
2
21/30 = 7/10 = 0.7
The fraction representing made shots is part/whole. Convert this fraction to a decimal.
3
0.7 * 100% = 70%
To convert a decimal to a percentage, multiply by 100 and add the percent symbol.

Answer: 70% (Option C)
