Question 1Grade 7 • NS
During a particularly tight month, a student tracked their daily bank account balance. The balances recorded were: -$15, $30, -$5, $0, -$20. Order these balances from least to greatest.
A. -$5, -$15, -$20, $0, $30
B. $30, $0, -$5, -$15, -$20
C. -$20, -$15, -$5, $0, $30
D. $0, -$5, -$15, -$20, $30
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
When ordering integers, especially negative numbers, think of their position on a number line. Numbers further to the left are smaller (lesser value), and numbers further to the right are larger (greater value).
Negative numbers are less than zero and positive numbers. A larger absolute value for a negative number means it is a smaller actual value.
2
Numbers: -15 (negative), 30 (positive), -5 (negative), 0 (zero), -20 (negative).
Separating numbers by their sign helps in preliminary ordering. All negative numbers are less than 0, and 0 is less than all positive numbers.
3
Comparing -15, -5, -20: -20 is furthest to the left on the number line. -15 is to the right of -20. -5 is to the right of -15. So, the order of negative numbers from least to greatest is: -20, -15, -5.
For negative numbers, the one with the larger absolute value is actually smaller. |-20| = 20, |-15| = 15, |-5| = 5. Since 20 > 15 > 5, then -20 < -15 < -5.
4
The ordered negative numbers are: -20, -15, -5. Zero comes next: 0. The positive number is: 30. Combining them: -20, -15, -5, 0, 30.
The final order from least to greatest is all negative numbers, then zero, then all positive numbers.

Answer: -$20, -$15, -$5, $0, $30 (Option C)

Question 3Grade 7 • NS

A. -2°C, -7°C, -10°C, -15°C
B. -15°C, -10°C, -7°C, -2°C
C. -10°C, -15°C, -2°C, -7°C
D. -7°C, -2°C, -10°C, -15°C
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Coldest means the least value, warmest means the greatest value. For negative numbers, the number with the larger absolute value represents a colder temperature (lesser value).
Temperatures work like a number line: lower numbers are colder, higher numbers are warmer. All given temperatures are negative, so we are ordering negative integers.
2
Numbers: -10, -2, -15, -7.
All numbers are negative, which simplifies the comparison as they are all on the left side of zero on the number line.
3
Comparing -10, -2, -15, -7: -15 is the smallest (coldest). -10 is next smallest. -7 is next. -2 is the largest (warmest). So, the order from least to greatest is: -15, -10, -7, -2.

B. +200, +120, -10, -50, -150
