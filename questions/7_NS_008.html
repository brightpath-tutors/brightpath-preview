Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A financial analyst is reviewing bank account balances for three clients: Client A has -$15, Client B has $0, and Client C has $10. Order these balances from least to greatest.
A. $0, $10, -$15
B. $0, -$15, $10
C. -$15, $0, $10
D. $10, $0, -$15
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The balances are -$15 (negative integer), $0 (zero), and $10 (positive integer).
Understanding the nature of each number is the first step in ordering.
2
Negative numbers are always less than zero and positive numbers. Zero is less than positive numbers. The further a negative number is from zero, the smaller its value.
These rules are fundamental for comparing integers.
3
-$15 is the smallest (least) value because it is a negative number. $0 is greater than -$15 but less than $10. $10 is the greatest value because it is a positive number.
Apply the ordering rules systematically to compare all values.
4
Least to greatest: -$15, $0, $10.
Combine the comparisons into the final ordered list.

Answer: -$15, $0, $10 (Option C)

Question 3Grade 7 • NS
During an experiment, a scientist recorded the following temperatures in different solutions: -8°C, -5°C, 2°C, and 4°C. Order these temperatures from coldest to warmest.
A. -5°C, -8°C, 2°C, 4°C
B. 4°C, 2°C, -5°C, -8°C
C. 2°C, 4°C, -5°C, -8°C
D. -8°C, -5°C, 2°C, 4°C
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The temperatures are -8°C (negative), -5°C (negative), 2°C (positive), and 4°C (positive).
Coldest refers to the least value, warmest to the greatest value.
2
For negative numbers, the one with the largest absolute value is the smallest. So, -8°C is colder than -5°C. Ordered: -8°C, -5°C.
The further a negative number is from zero, the colder it is.
3
For positive numbers, the one with the smaller digit is the smaller value. So, 2°C is colder than 4°C. Ordered: 2°C, 4°C.
Standard comparison for positive numbers.
4
Combining the negative and positive orders, from coldest to warmest: -8°C, -5°C, 2°C, 4°C.
All negative numbers are colder than all positive numbers.

Answer: -8°C, -5°C, 2°C, 4°C (Option D)
