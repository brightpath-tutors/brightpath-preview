Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A store sells a large bag of cat food for $28.50, containing 15 pounds. Another store sells a smaller bag for $15.75, containing 8 pounds. Which store offers a better unit price per pound, and by how much, to the nearest cent?
A. Store 2 by $0.07
B. Store 1 by $0.06
C. Store 1 by $0.07
D. Store 2 by $0.06
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Price / Quantity = $28.50 / 15 pounds = $1.90 per pound
To find the unit price, divide the total cost by the total quantity for the large bag.
2
Price / Quantity = $15.75 / 8 pounds = $1.96875 per pound
Similarly, divide the total cost by the total quantity for the smaller bag.
3
$1.96875 rounds to $1.97 per pound.
The problem asks for the difference to the nearest cent, so round the unit price of the smaller bag to two decimal places.
4
$1.90 (Store 1) < $1.97 (Store 2). Store 1 has a lower price per pound, making it the better deal.
A lower unit price indicates a better deal.
5
$1.97 - $1.90 = $0.07
Subtract the lower unit price from the higher unit price to find the savings.

Answer: Store 1 by $0.07 (Option C)

Question 2Grade 6 • RP
A competitive swimmer completes 12 laps of a 50-meter pool in 7 minutes and 30 seconds. What is her average speed in meters per second, rounded to two decimal places?
A. 80 m/s
B. 8 m/s
C. 1.30 m/s
D. 1.33 m/s
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total distance = 12 laps * 50 meters/lap = 600 meters
Multiply the number of laps by the length of each lap to get the total distance.
2
7 minutes * 60 seconds/minute = 420 seconds 420 seconds + 30 seconds = 450 seconds
To find speed in meters per second, the time must be in seconds. Convert minutes to seconds and add the remaining seconds.
3
Average speed = Total distance / Total time = 600 meters / 450 seconds = 1.3333... meters/second
Divide the total distance by the total time in seconds to find the average speed.
4
1.3333... rounds to 1.33 m/s
The problem specifies rounding to two decimal places.

Answer: 1.33 m/s (Option D)

Question 3Grade 6 • RP
A cookie recipe calls for 3/4 cup of sugar for every 24 cookies. If you want to make 60 cookies, how many cups of sugar will you need, in simplest form?
A. 45/4 cups
B. 15/8 cups
C. 18 cups
D. 3/2 cups
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Unit rate = (3/4 cups) / 24 cookies = 3 / (4 * 24) = 3 / 96 = 1/32 cups per cookie
Divide the amount of sugar by the number of cookies to find how much sugar is needed for one cookie.
2
Total sugar = (1/32 cups/cookie) * 60 cookies = 60/32 cups
Multiply the unit rate by the desired number of cookies.
3
60/32. Both are divisible by 4: 60 / 4 = 15, 32 / 4 = 8. So, 15/8 cups.
Divide the numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor (4) to simplify the fraction.

Answer: 15/8 cups (Option B)

Question 6Grade 6 • RP
A bean sprout grew from 2.5 cm to 14.5 cm in 15 days. What was its average growth rate in millimeters per day, rounded to one decimal place?
A. 1.2 mm/day
B. 0.8 cm/day
C. 0.8 mm/day
D. 8.0 mm/day
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total growth = 14.5 cm - 2.5 cm = 12 cm
Subtract the initial height from the final height to find the total growth.
2
12 cm * 10 mm/cm = 120 mm
Since 1 cm = 10 mm, multiply the growth in centimeters by 10.
3
Average growth rate = 120 mm / 15 days = 8 mm/day
Divide the total growth in millimeters by the number of days.
4
8 mm/day can be written as 8.0 mm/day.
The problem specifies rounding to one decimal place. Since 8 is a whole number, express it as 8.0.

Answer: 8.0 mm/day (Option D)

Question 7Grade 6 • RP
A painter can cover 120 square feet of wall in 1 hour and 15 minutes. How many square feet can the painter cover in 4 hours and 30 minutes, assuming the same rate?
A. 360 sq ft
B. 540 sq ft
C. 432 sq ft
D. 384 sq ft
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
1 hour * 60 minutes/hour + 15 minutes = 75 minutes
To calculate a consistent rate, convert the entire time period into a single unit (minutes).
2
Rate = 120 sq ft / 75 minutes = 1.6 sq ft/minute
Divide the total area covered by the time taken to find the rate per minute.
3
4 hours * 60 minutes/hour + 30 minutes = 240 minutes + 30 minutes = 270 minutes
Convert the target duration into minutes for consistency with the rate.
4
Total area = 1.6 sq ft/minute * 270 minutes = 432 sq ft
Multiply the rate per minute by the total target time in minutes.

Answer: 432 sq ft (Option C)

Question 8Grade 6 • RP

A. 432 forms
B. 540 forms
C. 480 forms
D. 450 forms
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
2 hours * 60 minutes/hour + 15 minutes = 120 + 15 = 135 minutes
Convert the given time for processing forms into minutes to calculate the rate per minute.
2
Rate = 135 forms / 135 minutes = 1 form/minute
Divide the number of forms by the time taken in minutes.
3
8 hours * 60 minutes/hour = 480 minutes
Convert the total workday duration into minutes to match the rate's unit of time.
4
Total forms = 1 form/minute * 480 minutes = 480 forms
Multiply the rate per minute by the total minutes in the workday.

Answer: 480 forms (Option C)

Question 10Grade 6 • RP
A computer downloads a 3.6 gigabyte file in 18 minutes. How many megabytes per second is the download speed? (1 gigabyte = 1024 megabytes, 1 minute = 60 seconds).
A. 3.41 MB/second
B. 204.8 MB/second
C. 61.44 MB/second
D. 3.33 MB/second
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
File size = 3.6 GB * 1024 MB/GB = 3686.4 MB
Multiply the gigabytes by the conversion factor (1024) to get megabytes.
2
Time = 18 minutes * 60 seconds/minute = 1080 seconds
Multiply the minutes by the conversion factor (60) to get seconds.
3
Download speed = Total megabytes / Total seconds = 3686.4 MB / 1080 seconds = 3.4133... MB/second
Divide the total file size in megabytes by the total time in seconds.
4
3.4133... rounds to 3.41 MB/second
Round the calculated speed to two decimal places as appropriate for a technology context.

Answer: 3.41 MB/second (Option A)
