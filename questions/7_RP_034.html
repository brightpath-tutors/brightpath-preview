Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A graph shows the cost of different quantities of berries. The x-axis represents the quantity of berries in pounds, and the y-axis represents the total cost in dollars. The graph is a straight line passing through the origin (0,0) and the point (2, $5.50). Is the relationship between the quantity of berries and the total cost proportional? If so, what is the constant of proportionality (unit price)?
A. No, because it does not pass through the origin.
B. Yes, $2.75 per pound.
C. No, because it is a straight line.
D. Yes, but the constant of proportionality is $5.50 per pound.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
A relationship is proportional if its graph is a straight line that passes through the origin (0,0). The problem states the graph is a straight line passing through the origin. Therefore, the relationship is proportional.
A key characteristic of a proportional relationship on a graph is that it must be a straight line and pass through the origin (0,0).
2
The constant of proportionality is the ratio of y to x (y/x) for any point (x,y) on the line, which represents the unit rate. Using the point (2 pounds, $5.50): Unit price = Cost / Quantity = $5.50 / 2 pounds = $2.75 per pound.
The constant of proportionality, also known as the unit rate, is found by dividing the y-value (cost) by the x-value (quantity) for any point on the line other than the origin.

Answer: Yes, $2.75 per pound. (Option B)

A. 36 liters.
B. 12 liters.
C. 60 liters.
D. 7.5 liters.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Since it's a proportional relationship, find k = y/x. k = 12 liters / 5 grams = 2.4 liters/gram.
The constant of proportionality (k) represents the amount of gas produced per gram of reactant. It's found by dividing the y-value (volume) by the x-value (reactant amount).
2
Volume = k * Amount of reactant Volume = 2.4 liters/gram * 15 grams = 36 liters.
Multiply the constant of proportionality by the new amount of reactant to find the total volume of gas produced.

Answer: 36 liters. (Option A)

Question 8Grade 7 • RP
A graph displays the amount of data transferred (y-axis, in gigabytes) over time (x-axis, in minutes) for two different internet providers, A and B. Both graphs are straight lines. Provider A's line passes through (0,0) and (4, 12). Provider B's line passes through (1, 4) and (3, 8). Which provider offers a proportional data transfer rate, and what is its constant of proportionality?
A. Provider B, 2 gigabytes per minute.
B. Neither provider, because data transfer is not proportional.
C. Provider A, 3 gigabytes per minute.
D. Both providers, 3 gigabytes per minute.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The graph for Provider A is a straight line passing through the origin (0,0) and the point (4, 12). * Passes through origin: Yes. * Straight line: Yes. Conclusion: Provider A represents a proportional relationship. Constant of proportionality (rate) = y/x = 12 GB / 4 minutes = 3 GB/minute.
For a relationship to be proportional, its graph must be a straight line and pass through the origin. Provider A meets both criteria. The constant of proportionality is the unit rate (y/x).
2
The graph for Provider B is a straight line passing through (1, 4) and (3, 8). * Check if it passes through the origin. We can find the equation of the line. * Slope = (8-4)/(3-1) = 4/2 = 2. * Using point-slope form: y - y1 = m(x - x1) => y - 4 = 2(x - 1) => y - 4 = 2x - 2 => y = 2x + 2. * Since the y-intercept is 2 (not 0), the line does not pass through the origin. Conclusion: Provider B represents a linear relationship, but it is not proportional.
Although Provider B's graph is a straight line, it does not pass through the origin (as its y-intercept is 2, not 0). Therefore, it is a linear relationship but not a proportional one.
3
Only Provider A is proportional, with a rate of 3 gigabytes per minute.
Based on the analysis, only Provider A's data transfer rate is proportional.

Answer: Provider A, 3 gigabytes per minute. (Option C)

Use the original dosage rate to determine the dosage required for a patient weighing 60 kg.
