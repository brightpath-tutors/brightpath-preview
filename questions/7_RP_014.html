Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A shirt originally cost $20. During a sale, its price increased to $25. What was the percent increase in the price of the shirt?
A. 20%
B. 125%
C. 25%
D. 5%
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$25 - $20 = $5
Subtract the original price from the new price to find the absolute increase.
2
$5 / $20 = 0.25
Divide the amount of increase by the original price. The original price is the base for comparison.
3
0.25 × 100 = 25%
Multiply the decimal by 100 to express it as a percentage.

Answer: 25% (Option C)
