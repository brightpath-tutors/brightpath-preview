Question 1Grade 6 • RP
In a basketball game, Team A scored 12 points and Team B scored 18 points. What is the ratio of Team A's score to Team B's score in simplest form?
A. 3:2
B. 1:2
C. 2:3
D. 12:18
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Team A's score: 12 points Team B's score: 18 points Ratio of Team A's score to Team B's score: 12:18
The problem asks for the ratio of Team A's score to Team B's score, so the order is important. Team A's score comes first, followed by Team B's score.
2
Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12 Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18 GCF(12, 18) = 6
To simplify a ratio, we divide both terms by their greatest common factor. This ensures the ratio is in its simplest form.
3
12 ÷ 6 = 2 18 ÷ 6 = 3 Simplified ratio: 2:3
Dividing both numbers by their GCF gives us the ratio in its simplest form, where the terms no longer share any common factors other than 1.

Answer: 2:3 (Option C)

B. 3:5
C. 1:5

Step-by-step solution

Total paint: 70 liters Blue paint: 21 liters Yellow paint: 35 liters Paint used (Blue + Yellow) = 21 + 35 = 56 liters Green paint = Total paint - (Blue + Yellow paint) = 70 - 56 = 14 liters
First, find the combined amount of blue and yellow paint. Then, subtract this sum from the total paint used to find the amount of green paint.
2
Green paint: 14 liters Yellow paint: 35 liters Ratio of green paint to yellow paint: 14:35
Ensure the ratio is formed in the correct order as specified: green paint first, then yellow paint.
3
Factors of 14: 1, 2, 7, 14 Factors of 35: 1, 5, 7, 35 GCF(14, 35) = 7
To simplify the ratio, find the largest number that divides evenly into both terms.
4
14 ÷ 7 = 2 35 ÷ 7 = 5 Simplified ratio: 2:5
Dividing both numbers by their GCF results in the ratio expressed in its simplest form.

Answer: 2:5 (Option D)
