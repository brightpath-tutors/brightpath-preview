Question 1Grade 6 • EE
Sarah buys 2 bags of candy and 3 boxes of crackers. Each bag of candy costs 'c' dollars and each box of crackers costs 'r' dollars. Later, she buys 1 more bag of candy. Which expression represents the total cost of her purchases?
A. 2c + 4r
B. 6cr
C. 3c + 6r
D. 3c + 3r
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial expression: 2c + 3r + c
Sarah buys 2 bags of candy (2c), 3 boxes of crackers (3r), and then 1 more bag of candy (c).
2
(2c + c) + 3r
Group the like terms, which are the terms involving 'c'.
3
3c + 3r
Combine the coefficients of the 'c' terms (2 + 1 = 3). The '3r' term remains as there are no other 'r' terms to combine with.

Answer: 3c + 3r (Option D)

Question 2Grade 6 • EE
A coach tracks the total points scored by two players, Alex and Ben, in a game. Alex scores 'p' points and Ben scores 'q' points. In the next game, Alex scores 'p' points again, and Ben scores 'q' points, plus 5 more points from free throws. Which expression represents the total points scored by both players over both games?
A. p + q + 5
B. 2p + 2q + 5
C. 4pq + 5
D. 2p + 7q
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial expression: (p + q) + (p + q + 5)
The points from the first game are (p + q). The points from the second game are (p + q + 5).
2
p + q + p + q + 5
Remove the parentheses as addition is associative.
3
(p + p) + (q + q) + 5
Group the like terms together: 'p' terms, 'q' terms, and the constant.
4
2p + 2q + 5
Combine the like terms (p + p = 2p and q + q = 2q). The constant 5 remains.

Answer: 2p + 2q + 5 (Option B)

3 e + 3 4

3
3e + 12

Initial expression: 4w + 3(1) + 2w
He has 4 textbooks (4w), 3 notebooks (3*1), and then 2 more textbooks (2w).

4w + 3 + 2w

3
(4w + 2w) + 3

4
6w + 3

Question 5Grade 6 • EE
A chemist is mixing two compounds. He has 'x' grams of Compound A and 4 grams of Compound B. He prepares 5 identical mixtures. Then he adds an additional 'x' grams of Compound A to one of the mixtures. Which expression represents the total mass of chemicals in all mixtures?
A. 6x + 4
B. 5x + 20
C. 6x + 20
D. 5x + 24
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial expression: 5(x + 4) + x
Each mixture has (x + 4) grams. He prepares 5 such mixtures, so 5(x + 4). He then adds an additional 'x' grams.
2
5x + 5 * 4 + x
Apply the distributive property to 5(x + 4), multiplying 5 by 'x' and by '4'.
3
5x + 20 + x
Perform the multiplication: 5 * 4 = 20.
4
(5x + x) + 20
Group the like terms (terms with 'x') together.
5
6x + 20
Combine the coefficients of the 'x' terms (5 + 1 = 6). The constant 20 remains.

Answer: 6x + 20 (Option C)

One project requires (b + 7) and the other identical project requires (b + 3).
2
Simplify one set: b + 7 + b + 3 = 2b + 10
Combine like terms (b + b = 2b) and constants (7 + 3 = 10).
3
Total expression: 2 * (2b + 10)
Since he is working on 2 such sets simultaneously, multiply the simplified expression for one set by 2.
4
2 2b + 2 10
Apply the distributive property, multiplying 2 by each term inside the parentheses.
5
4b + 20
Perform the multiplications.

Answer: 4b + 20 (Option B)

Question 7Grade 6 • EE

B. 10h
C. 5h + 5
D. 3h + 2h + 5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial expression: 3h + 2(h + 5)
For the first 3 days, they work 3h. For the next 2 days, they work 2 times (h + 5) hours.
2
3h + 2 h + 2 5
Apply the distributive property to 2(h + 5), multiplying 2 by 'h' and by '5'.
3
3h + 2h + 10
Perform the multiplications.
4
(3h + 2h) + 10
Group the like terms (terms with 'h') together.
5
5h + 10

Question 8Grade 6 • EE
A car rental company charges 'd' dollars per day plus a flat fee of 15 dollars. A customer rents a car for 3 days. Another customer rents a car for 5 days, but gets a 2-dollar discount per day. Which expression represents the total cost for both customers?
A. 8d + 13
B. 8d + 55
C. 8d + 47
D. 8d + 35
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost for first customer: 3(d + 15)
First customer rents for 3 days. Each day costs 'd' dollars plus a $15 flat fee, so (d + 15).
2
Cost for second customer: 5(d - 2)
Second customer rents for 5 days. Each day costs 'd' dollars minus a $2 discount, so (d - 2).
3
Total expression: 3(d + 15) + 5(d - 2)
Add the costs for both customers.
4
(3 d + 3 15) + (5 d - 5 2)
Apply the distributive property to both terms.
5
3d + 45 + 5d - 10
Perform the multiplications.
6
(3d + 5d) + (45 - 10)
Group the like terms (terms with 'd') and constants together.
7
8d + 35
Combine the coefficients of the 'd' terms (3 + 5 = 8) and combine the constants (45 - 10 = 35).

Answer: 8d + 35 (Option D)

At the same time, another nearby town, which also started with 'p' people, experiences a decrease of 20 people, and this decrease is tripled due to migration. Which expression represents the total combined population of both towns after these changes?

B. 5p + 260
C. 5p + 180
D. 5p + 140
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Population of first town: 2(p + 100)
Initial population 'p' increases by 100, then this new total is doubled.
2
Population of second town: 3(p - 20)
Initial population 'p' decreases by 20, then this new total is tripled.
3
Total combined population: 2(p + 100) + 3(p - 20)
Add the expressions for both towns.
4
(2 p + 2 100) + (3 p - 3 20)
Apply the distributive property to both terms.
5
2p + 200 + 3p - 60
Perform the multiplications.
6
(2p + 3p) + (200 - 60)
Group the like terms (terms with 'p') and constants together.
7
5p + 140

Question 10Grade 6 • EE
A tech company sells two types of apps. Let 'p' be the price of a premium app and 's' be the price of a standard app. For Bundle A, they sell 3 premium apps and 2 standard apps, with a $2 discount on each premium app. For Bundle B, they sell 5 premium apps and 4 standard apps, with a $1 discount on each standard app. Which expression represents the total revenue from both bundles?
A. 8p + 6s + 10
B. 8p + 6s - 3
C. 8p + 6s - 10
D. 8p + 6s - 2
E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1
Revenue from Bundle A: 3(p - 2) + 2s
3 premium apps at (p - 2) each, plus 2 standard apps at 's' each.
2
Revenue from Bundle B: 5p + 4(s - 1)
5 premium apps at 'p' each, plus 4 standard apps at (s - 1) each.
3
Total revenue: (3(p - 2) + 2s) + (5p + 4(s - 1))
Add the revenue expressions for both bundles.
4
3p - 3 2 + 2s + 5p + 4 s - 4 * 1
Apply the distributive property to 3(p - 2) and 4(s - 1).
5
3p - 6 + 2s + 5p + 4s - 4
Perform the multiplications.
6
(3p + 5p) + (2s + 4s) + (-6 - 4)
Group the like terms (terms with 'p', terms with 's', and constants) together.
7
8p + 6s - 10
Combine the coefficients of the 'p' terms (3 + 5 = 8), 's' terms (2 + 4 = 6), and constants (-6 - 4 = -10).

Answer: 8p + 6s - 10 (Option C)
