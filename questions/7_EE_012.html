Question 1Grade 7 • EE
Sarah has $25 to spend at the grocery store. She wants to buy a box of cereal for $3.75. How much more money, m, can she spend?
A. m <= 21.25
B. m <= 28.75
C. m >= 21.25
D. m < 21.25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
m + 3.75 <= 25
The total money spent (the money Sarah can still spend, m, plus the $3.75 for cereal) must be less than or equal to the total money Sarah has ($25).
2
m + 3.75 - 3.75 <= 25 - 3.75 m <= 21.25
To find out how much more money Sarah can spend, subtract the cost of the cereal from her total money.

Answer: m <= 21.25 (Option A)

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1

Question 6Grade 7 • EE
The temperature inside a refrigerator must be kept below 40 degrees Fahrenheit. If the current temperature is 52 degrees Fahrenheit, how many degrees, d, must the temperature decrease?
A. d < -12
B. d > 12
C. d > -12
D. d < 12
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
52 - d < 40
The current temperature (52 degrees) minus the decrease (d) must be less than 40 degrees.
2
52 - d - 52 < 40 - 52 -d < -12
Subtract 52 from both sides to begin isolating d.
3
(-d) (-1) > (-12) (-1) d > 12
When multiplying or dividing both sides of an inequality by a negative number, the direction of the inequality sign must be reversed.

Answer: d > 12 (Option B)

Question 7Grade 7 • EE
A construction worker needs to cut a metal rod into pieces that are each at least 0.75 meters long. If the rod is 6 meters long, what is the maximum number of pieces, p, he can cut?
A. p <= 4.5
B. p <= 0.125
C. p <= 8
D. p >= 8
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
0.75 * p <= 6
The total length of the pieces (0.75 meters per piece multiplied by p pieces) must be less than or equal to the total length of the rod (6 meters).
2
0.75 * p / 0.75 <= 6 / 0.75 p <= 8
To find the maximum number of pieces, divide the total length of the rod by the length of each piece.

Answer: p <= 8 (Option C)

D. t <= 3.75

Step-by-step solution
1
1 hour and 15 minutes = 1 + 15/60 hours = 1 + 0.25 hours = 1.25 hours.

2
1.25 + t <= 2.5

Question 10Grade 7 • EE
A doctor advises a patient to drink at least 80 ounces of water per day. If the patient drinks water in 10-ounce glasses, what is the minimum number of glasses, g, the patient should drink?
A. g >= 8
B. g <= 8
C. g >= 70
D. g > 8
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
10 * g >= 80
The total ounces of water (g glasses multiplied by 10 ounces per glass) must be greater than or equal to the minimum recommended (80 ounces).
2
10 * g / 10 >= 80 / 10 g >= 8
To find the minimum number of glasses, divide the total recommended ounces by the ounces per glass.

Answer: g >= 8 (Option A)
