A. $37
B. $70
C. $35
D. $50
E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1
The common multiplier is 2 (since Sarah buys 2 of each item). The costs are $15 for shirts and $20 for pants.
This sets up the expression for the distributive property.
2
2(15 + 20)
This expression represents the total cost: 2 times the sum of the cost of one shirt and one pair of pants.
3
2 15 + 2 20
Multiply the outside number (2) by each term inside the parentheses.

5
70
Add the results to find the total cost.

Answer: $70 (Option B)
