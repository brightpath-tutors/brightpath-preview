Question 1Grade 4 • NF
Maya had 5 7/8 meters of ribbon. She used 2 3/8 meters for a craft project. How much ribbon does she have left, in simplest form?
A. 3 1/2 meters
B. 3 4/0 meters
C. 3 4/8 meters
D. 8 2/8 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
5 \frac{7}{8} - 2 \frac{3}{8
Set up the subtraction problem with the given mixed numbers.
2
\frac{7}{8} - \frac{3}{8} = \frac{4}{8
Subtract the fractional parts of the mixed numbers. Since the denominators are the same, subtract the numerators.
3
5 - 2 = 3
Subtract the whole number parts of the mixed numbers.
4
3 \frac{4}{8
Combine the subtracted whole number and fractional parts.
5
\frac{4}{8} = \frac{1}{2
Simplify the fractional part. Divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 4.
6
3 \frac{1}{2} \text{ meters
State the final answer in simplest form with the correct units.

Answer: 3 1/2 meters (Option A)

Step-by-step solution
1
8 \frac{1}{3} - 2 \frac{2}{3
Set up the subtraction problem with the given mixed numbers.
2
\frac{1}{3} < \frac{2}{3
Compare the fractional parts. Since 1/3 is less than 2/3, regrouping is needed for the first mixed number.
3
8 \frac{1}{3} = 7 + \frac{3}{3} + \frac{1}{3} = 7 \frac{4}{3
Regroup 8 1/3 by taking 1 from the whole number (8 becomes 7) and adding it as 3/3 to the fraction (1/3 becomes 4/3).
4
\frac{4}{3} - \frac{2}{3} = \frac{2}{3
Subtract the fractional parts after regrouping.
5
7 - 2 = 5
Subtract the whole number parts.
6
5 \frac{2}{3

\frac{2}{3} \text{ is in simplest form
