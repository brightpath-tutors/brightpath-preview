Question 1Grade 8 • NS
A shopper has a gift card with a balance of $20.50. They purchase an item that costs $15.75. On a number line where 0 represents no balance, which point would represent their new balance?
A. 36.25
B. 5.25
C. -4.75
D. 4.75
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial balance = $20.50 Cost of item = $15.75
The problem states the starting gift card balance and the amount spent.
2
$20.50 - $15.75 = $4.75
Spending money reduces the balance, so subtraction is the correct operation. Align the decimal points and subtract: $20.50 - $15.75 = $4.75.
3
The new balance is $4.75.
On a number line where 0 is the starting point, a positive balance of $4.75 would be located at the point 4.75 to the right of 0.

Answer: 4.75 (Option D)

Question 2Grade 8 • NS
A diver starts at sea level (0 meters). They dive to a depth of -4.8 meters. Then they ascend 2.3 meters. On a vertical number line where positive values are above sea level and negative values are below, which point represents the diver's final position?
A. -2.5
B. 7.1
C. 2.5
D. -7.1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial dive depth = -4.8 meters Ascent = +2.3 meters
Diving below sea level is represented by a negative number, and ascending is represented by a positive number.
2
-4.8 + 2.3 = -2.5 meters
To add numbers with different signs, subtract their absolute values (4.8 - 2.3 = 2.5) and keep the sign of the number with the larger absolute value (4.8 is negative, so the result is negative). Thus, -4.8 + 2.3 = -2.5.
3
The final position is -2.5 meters.
On a vertical number line, -2.5 would be located 2.5 units below 0.

Answer: -2.5 (Option A)

Question 3Grade 8 • NS

A. 4/3
B. 1/2
C. 6/9
D. 4/6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Required amount = 5/6 cup Available amount = 1/3 cup
The problem gives the two quantities that need to be compared.
2
Difference = 5/6 - 1/3
To find how much more is needed, subtract the available amount from the required amount.
3
The least common multiple of 6 and 3 is 6. Convert 1/3 to an equivalent fraction with a denominator of 6: 1/3 = (1 2) / (3 2) = 2/6.
Fractions can only be added or subtracted if they have the same denominator. The smallest common denominator for 6 and 3 is 6.
4
5/6 - 2/6 = (5 - 2) / 6 = 3/6 Simplify: 3/6 = 1/2
Subtract the numerators while keeping the common denominator. Then, simplify the resulting fraction by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 3.
5
The additional amount needed is 1/2 cup.
On a number line from 0 to 1, 1/2 is exactly halfway between 0 and 1.

Answer: 1/2 (Option B)

Question 7Grade 8 • NS
A construction crew has a metal beam that is 10 3/4 feet long. They need to cut off a section that is 4 1/2 feet long. How much of the beam is left? On a number line, which mixed number, in simplest form, represents the length of the remaining beam?
A. 6 5/4
B. 6 1/4
C. 15 1/4
D. 6 1/2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial length = 10 3/4 feet Length to cut off = 4 1/2 feet
The problem provides the starting length and the amount that needs to be removed.
2
10 3/4 - 4 1/2
To find the remaining length, subtract the portion removed from the original length.
3
The least common multiple of 4 and 2 is 4. Convert 1/2 to an equivalent fraction with a denominator of 4: 1/2 = 2/4. So, the problem becomes: 10 3/4 - 4 2/4.
Before subtracting mixed numbers, ensure their fractional parts have a common denominator.
4
Whole numbers: 10 - 4 = 6 Fractions: 3/4 - 2/4 = 1/4 Combine: 6 1/4 feet
Subtract the whole numbers and the numerators of the fractions separately, keeping the common denominator. The fraction 1/4 is already in simplest form.
5
The remaining length is 6 1/4 feet.
On a number line, 6 1/4 would be located between 6 and 7, specifically one-quarter of the way from 6 to 7.

Answer: 6 1/4 (Option B)

Question 8Grade 8 • NS
A digital timer reads 3.75 minutes. If it then counts down for 5.5 minutes, what will the timer display? (Represent time on a number line where positive values are minutes elapsed and negative values are minutes before a reference point). Which point represents the final reading?
A. -1.75
B. 1.75
C. -2.25
D. 9.25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial reading = 3.75 minutes Countdown duration = 5.5 minutes (represented as -5.5 for elapsed time from the reading)
Counting down means time is being subtracted from the current reading. If the countdown goes past zero, the result will be a negative value, representing time before the initial reference.
2
Final reading = 3.75 - 5.5
This is equivalent to adding a positive and a negative number. Since 5.5 has a larger absolute value than 3.75, the result will be negative. Subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger: $5.5 - 3.75 = 1.75$. Keep the sign of the larger absolute value (5.5 is effectively negative), so the result is -1.75.
3
The final reading is -1.75 minutes.
On a number line, -1.75 is located 1.75 units to the left of 0, indicating time before the zero mark.

Answer: -1.75 (Option A)

Question 10Grade 8 • NS
A software update is downloading. 2/5 of the update has been downloaded, and then another 1/3 of the update downloads. What fraction of the software update still needs to be downloaded? On a number line from 0 to 1, what fraction, in simplest form, represents the remaining portion?
A. 4/15
B. 11/15
C. 3/8
D. 1/2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial download = 2/5 Additional download = 1/3
These are the two parts of the update that have been downloaded.
2
Total downloaded = 2/5 + 1/3
To add fractions with different denominators, find a common denominator. The least common multiple of 5 and 3 is 15.
3
2/5 = (2 3) / (5 3) = 6/15 1/3 = (1 5) / (3 5) = 5/15 Total downloaded = 6/15 + 5/15 = 11/15
Multiply the numerator and denominator by the factor that makes the denominator 15. Then add the numerators.
4
Remaining = 1 - 11/15 1 = 15/15 Remaining = 15/15 - 11/15 = 4/15
The entire update is represented by 1. Subtract the downloaded portion from 1. Ensure 1 is written as a fraction with the same denominator as the total downloaded portion.
5
The remaining portion is 4/15.
On a number line from 0 to 1, 4/15 is located between 0 and 1, specifically 4 units past 0 if the line is divided into 15 equal parts.

Answer: 4/15 (Option A)
