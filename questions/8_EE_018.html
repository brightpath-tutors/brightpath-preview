Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A large company had annual sales of $23,500,000. Write this amount in scientific notation.
A. 2.35 x 10^-7
B. 235 x 10^5
C. 2.35 x 10^7
D. 23.5 x 10^6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The significant digits are 2, 3, and 5.
Scientific notation uses only the non-zero digits and zeros between them.
2
Move the decimal from its implied position at the end of $23,500,000 to after the first non-zero digit (2), making it 2.35.
The coefficient in scientific notation must be a number between 1 and 10 (inclusive of 1, exclusive of 10).
3
The decimal point moved 7 places to the left (from after the last zero to after the 2).
This count determines the magnitude of the exponent.
4
Since the original number ($23,500,000) is greater than 1, the exponent will be positive.
Large numbers have positive exponents, and small numbers (less than 1) have negative exponents.
5
2.35 x 10^7
The final scientific notation combines the coefficient and the power of 10.

Answer: 2.35 x 10^7 (Option C)

20 = 2 x 10^1
It's easier to perform multiplication when both numbers are in scientific notation.
2
3.2 x 2 = 6.4
Multiply the numerical parts of the scientific notation numbers.
3

6.4 x 10^9

The coefficient must always be a number from 1 up to (but not including) 10.

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A company's server farm stores 7.5 x 10^9 bytes of data. They add a new server with 2.3 x 10^8 bytes of storage. What is the total storage capacity in scientific notation?
A. 7.523 x 10^9
B. 7.73 x 10^9
C. 9.8 x 10^9
D. 7.73 x 10^8
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The numbers are 7.5 x 10^9 and 2.3 x 10^8. To add them, the exponents must be the same. It's usually easier to adjust the number with the smaller exponent.
Numbers in scientific notation can only be added or subtracted if they have the same power of 10.
2
Convert 2.3 x 10^8 to have an exponent of 9. To change 10^8 to 10^9 (an increase of 1 in the exponent), we must move the decimal point in the coefficient 2.3 one place to the left. So, 2.3 x 10^8 = 0.23 x 10^9.
If the exponent increases, the coefficient's decimal moves left. If the exponent decreases, the coefficient's decimal moves right.
3
Now add the coefficients: 7.5 x 10^9 + 0.23 x 10^9 = (7.5 + 0.23) x 10^9 = 7.73 x 10^9.
Once exponents are the same, simply add or subtract the coefficients and keep the common power of 10.
4
7.73 is between 1 and 10, so no further adjustment is needed.
The coefficient must always be a number from 1 up to (but not including) 10.

Answer: 7.73 x 10^9 (Option B)
