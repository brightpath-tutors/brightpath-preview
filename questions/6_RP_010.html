Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A store sells pens. The table shows the cost for different numbers of pens. | Number of Pens (x) | Cost in Dollars (y) | |---|---| | 2 | 3.00 | | 4 | 6.00 | | 6 | 9.00 | What is the constant of proportionality for this relationship?
A. 3.00
B. 1.50
C. 5.00
D. 0.67
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The constant of proportionality (k) in a proportional relationship is found by dividing the y-value by the x-value (k = y/x).
A proportional relationship means that for any pair (x, y), the ratio y/x is always the same constant value, k.
2
For (2, 3.00): k = 3.00 / 2 = 1.50 For (4, 6.00): k = 6.00 / 4 = 1.50 For (6, 9.00): k = 9.00 / 6 = 1.50
We calculate the ratio y/x for each given pair of values in the table.
3
Since the constant of proportionality is the same (1.50) for all pairs, the relationship is proportional. The constant of proportionality is 1.50.
Because the ratio y/x is consistent across all data points, we confirm the relationship is proportional and identify 1.50 as the constant.

Answer: 1.50 (Option B)

1

2
The points (0,0), (1,2), (2,4), (3,6) all lie on a straight line. The line clearly starts at the origin (0,0).
We visually inspect the graph to see if it meets both conditions.
3
Since both conditions are met (it's a straight line and passes through the origin), the graph represents a proportional relationship.
Confirming both characteristics leads to the conclusion that the relationship is proportional.

Question 4Grade 6 • RP
A train travels at a constant speed. It covers 180 miles in 3 hours. Which equation represents the relationship between the distance traveled (d) in miles and the time (t) in hours?
A. d = 180t
B. d = t / 60
C. d = 60t
D. d = t + 180
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
d = distance, t = time. Since the speed is constant, the relationship between distance and time is proportional. This means d = kt, where k is the constant speed (miles per hour).
A constant speed implies a proportional relationship, meaning distance is directly proportional to time, with speed as the constant of proportionality.
2
k = distance / time = 180 miles / 3 hours = 60 miles per hour.
The constant of proportionality (k) is the unit rate, which in this context is the speed of the train.
3
Substitute k into the proportional equation: d = 60t.
Replacing k with its calculated value gives the equation that models the relationship between distance and time.

Answer: d = 60t (Option C)

A straight line passes through (0,0), (3, 45), (6, 90), (9, 135).] Is this a proportional relationship? If so, what is the constant of proportionality, and how much would the person earn in 11 hours?

Step-by-step solution
1
The graph is a straight line and the line passes through the origin (0,0). Therefore, it is a proportional relationship.
A graph represents a proportional relationship if and only if it is a straight line that passes through the origin.
2
Pick a point from the graph, e.g., (3, 45). k = y/x = 45 / 3 = 15. Verify with another point: (6, 90) -> 90 / 6 = 15. The constant of proportionality is 15.

Question 9Grade 6 • RP
The cost (C) of downloading data (D) in gigabytes from a cloud service is given by the equation C = 0.75D. Which of the following points (D, C) would lie on the graph of this proportional relationship? I. (5, 3.75) II. (10, 7.00) III. (12, 9.00)
A. I and II only
B. II and III only
C. I only
D. I and III only
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The equation C = 0.75D represents a proportional relationship where the constant of proportionality k = 0.75. For any point (D, C) on the graph, the ratio C/D must equal 0.75.
In a proportional relationship C = kD, the constant k is the unit cost, meaning C/D should always equal k.
2
C/D = 3.75 / 5 = 0.75. This matches the constant of proportionality. So, point I lies on the graph.
We divide the 'cost' (C) by the 'data' (D) for the first point to see if it equals the established constant k.
3
C/D = 7.00 / 10 = 0.70. Since 0.70 ≠ 0.75, this point does not lie on the graph.
We repeat the division for the second point. If the ratio doesn't match k, the point is not part of this proportional relationship.
4
C/D = 9.00 / 12 = 0.75. This matches the constant of proportionality. So, point III lies on the graph.
We repeat the division for the third point. If the ratio matches k, the point is part of this proportional relationship.
5
Points I and III only lie on the graph of the proportional relationship C = 0.75D.
Based on our checks, we identify all points that satisfy the proportional relationship.

Answer: I and III only (Option D)

2

We use the constant of proportionality to generate additional pairs that maintain the same proportional relationship.
3

The general form of a proportional equation is y = kx. Substituting the calculated k gives the specific equation for this relationship.
