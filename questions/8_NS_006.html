Question 1Grade 8 • NS
Sarah's bank account balance is -$25. She wants to know how much money she owes the bank. What is the absolute value of her balance?
A. $-25
B. $0
C. $25
D. $50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The absolute value of a number is its distance from zero on a number line, regardless of direction. It is always non-negative.
The absolute value represents the magnitude of a quantity, such as debt, without considering its direction (positive or negative).
2
The balance is -$25.
The negative sign indicates debt.
3
|-25| = 25
The distance from -25 to 0 on a number line is 25 units. Therefore, Sarah owes $25.

Answer: $25 (Option C)

Question 8Grade 8 • NS
An architect is designing a building. One floor is 12 meters below ground level, represented as -12 meters. Another floor is 28 meters above ground level, represented as +28 meters. What is the absolute difference in height between these two floors?
A. 40 meters
B. 28 meters
C. 16 meters
D. -40 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Floor 1: -12 meters (below ground) Floor 2: +28 meters (above ground)
Ground level is considered 0. Below is negative, above is positive.
2
Difference = 28 - (-12) = 28 + 12 = 40 meters
Subtract the lower height from the higher height. Subtracting a negative is equivalent to adding a positive.
3
|40| = 40
The absolute difference is always positive, representing the total vertical distance between the floors.

Answer: 40 meters (Option A)
