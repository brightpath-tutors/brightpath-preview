Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A store offers a discount of 3/4 of the original price. Can this fraction be represented as a terminating decimal? If so, what is it?
A. Yes, 1.333...
B. Yes, 0.34
C. Yes, 0.75
D. No, it's a repeating decimal
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Fraction: 3/4
Identify the given fraction.
2
Simplest form: 3/4
Confirm the fraction is simplified by checking for common factors between the numerator and denominator. 3 and 4 have no common factors other than 1.
3
Denominator: 4 Prime factors of 4: 2, 2
Find the prime factors of the denominator.
4
Prime factors are only 2s. Therefore, terminating decimal.
A fraction can be expressed as a terminating decimal if and only if the prime factors of its simplified denominator are only 2s and/or 5s.
5
3 ÷ 4 = 0.75
Perform the division to find the decimal value.

Answer: Yes, 0.75 (Option C)

Question 4Grade 7 • NS
A car traveled 120 miles in 2 and 1/2 hours. If the speed is expressed as a decimal, will it be a terminating decimal?
A. Yes, because the speed is a whole number
B. No, because 1/2 is involved
C. Yes, but only if rounded
D. No, it will be a repeating decimal
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Distance = 120 miles Time = 2 1/2 hours
Identify the given values for distance and time.
2
2 1/2 = 5/2
Convert the mixed number to an improper fraction for easier calculation.
3
Speed = 120 / (5/2) = 120 * (2/5)
Set up the division for speed and convert it to multiplication by the reciprocal.
4
Speed = 240/5
Perform the multiplication in the numerator.
5
240/5 = 48/1 = 48
Simplify the fraction to its lowest terms.
6
48 is a whole number (48.0). This is a terminating decimal.
Any whole number can be expressed as a terminating decimal (e.g., 48 = 48.0).

Answer: Yes (Option A)
