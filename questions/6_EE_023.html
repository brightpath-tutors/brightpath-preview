Question 1Grade 6 • EE
A pair of shoes costs $45. Sarah saved $28, and her aunt gave her the rest of the money she needed. How much money did Sarah's aunt give her?
A. $28
B. $15
C. $73
D. $17
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost of shoes = $45; Money saved = $28; Money from aunt = x
We know the total cost and how much Sarah saved. We need to find the amount her aunt gave her.
2
28 + x = 45
The money Sarah saved plus the money her aunt gave her equals the total cost of the shoes.
3
28 + x - 28 = 45 - 28 x = 17
To isolate 'x', subtract 28 from both sides of the equation.
4
Sarah's aunt gave her $17.
The value of x represents the money Sarah's aunt gave her.

Answer: $17 (Option D)

E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution

Total class time = 50 minutes; Warm-up time = 5 minutes; Duration per activity = 15 minutes; Number of activities = x

2
Lesson time = 50 - 5 = 45 minutes

15x = 45

5
There are 3 activities.
The value of x represents the number of activities.

Question 10Grade 6 • EE
A company charges a fixed monthly fee of $25 for internet service, plus $3 for every gigabyte of data used. If a customer's bill for one month was $49, how many gigabytes of data did they use?
A. 8 gigabytes
B. 24 gigabytes
C. 7 gigabytes
D. 16 gigabytes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Fixed fee = $25; Cost per gigabyte = $3; Total bill = $49; Gigabytes used = x
We know the fixed cost, the cost per gigabyte, and the total bill. We need to find the number of gigabytes used.
2
25 + 3x = 49
The fixed fee plus the cost of data (cost per gigabyte multiplied by gigabytes used) equals the total bill.
3
25 + 3x - 25 = 49 - 25 3x = 24
First, subtract 25 from both sides of the equation to isolate the term with 'x'.
4
3x / 3 = 24 / 3 x = 8
Next, divide both sides by 3 to find the value of 'x'.
5
The customer used 8 gigabytes of data.
The value of x represents the number of gigabytes used.

Answer: 8 gigabytes (Option A)
