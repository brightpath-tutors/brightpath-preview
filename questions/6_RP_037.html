Question 10Grade 6 • RP
A laptop battery starts at 100% charge. After 2 hours of use, it drops to 75% of its initial charge. If it then drops an additional 20% of its current charge in the next hour, what percentage of the initial charge remains?
A. 55%
B. 75%
C. 80%
D. 60%
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The battery drops to 75% of its initial charge. Then, it drops an additional 20% of that new value. We need to find the final percentage relative to the initial 100%.
2
100% \times 0.75 = 75% of initial charge
After the first 2 hours, 75% of the initial 100% charge remains.
3
1. Convert second drop percentage to decimal: 20% = 0.20 2. Calculate the amount of the second drop: 75% \times 0.20 = 15% 3. Subtract the second drop from the current charge: 75% - 15% = 60% of initial charge
An additional 20% drop from the current 75% means a drop of 15%. Subtract this from 75% to find the final remaining charge.

Answer: 60% (Option D)
