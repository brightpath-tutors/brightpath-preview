Question 1Grade 6 • EE
Sarah bought 5 packets of stickers. Each packet contains 3 sheets of stickers. Which property of operations allows us to say that the total number of stickers is the same as if she bought 3 packets with 5 sheets each?
A. Distributive Property
B. Associative Property of Multiplication
C. Commutative Property of Multiplication
D. Identity Property of Multiplication
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Sarah buys 5 packets, each with 3 sheets. This can be represented as 5 3. The question asks which property shows this is the same as 3 packets with 5 sheets each, which is 3 5.
The problem is comparing two multiplication expressions (5 3 and 3 5) and asking which property states they are equal.
2
The operation involved is multiplication.
The number of packets multiplied by the number of sheets per packet gives the total number of sheets, which is a multiplication operation.
3
The order of the numbers being multiplied is changed (5 3 becomes 3 5).
The factors are rearranged, but the product remains the same.
4
The property that states changing the order of factors does not change the product is the Commutative Property of Multiplication.
This property applies to both addition and multiplication, allowing numbers to be swapped without affecting the result.

Answer: Commutative Property of Multiplication (Option C)

Question 2Grade 6 • EE
A chef is preparing a dish that requires 8 ounces of chicken and 2 ounces of vegetables for each serving. If he plans to make 4 servings, which expression represents the total amount of ingredients needed, using the Distributive Property?
A. 8 + 2 + 4
B. 4 + 8 + 2
C. 4 × 8 + 4 × 2
D. 4 × (8 + 2)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Chicken per serving = 8 ounces. Vegetables per serving = 2 ounces. Number of servings = 4.
These are the given values in the problem.
2
Total ingredients per serving = 8 + 2 ounces. Total ingredients for 4 servings = 4 × (8 + 2) ounces.
First, sum the ingredients for one serving, then multiply by the number of servings.
3
The Distributive Property states a × (b + c) = a × b + a × c. So, 4 × (8 + 2) = 4 × 8 + 4 × 2.
The factor outside the parentheses (4) is distributed to each term inside the parentheses (8 and 2).

Answer: 4 × 8 + 4 × 2 (Option C)

The most fitting property for reorganizing sums (like adding all instances of '15' then all instances of '7' across sections) is the Associative Property of Addition. While the Distributive Property could be used to write 2 15 + 2 7, the phrasing 'grouping... or vice versa' suggests a more fundamental grouping rearrangement.
