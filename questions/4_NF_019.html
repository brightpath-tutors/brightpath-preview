D. $0.75
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Fraction = 75/100
Identify the given fraction representing the cost.
2
Denominator is 100, which means there are two decimal places.
Understand that a denominator of 100 corresponds to hundredths, requiring two digits after the decimal point.
3
75 ÷ 100 = 0.75
Divide the numerator by the denominator to convert the fraction to a decimal.
4
Decimal notation: $0.75
Write the decimal value with the appropriate currency symbol.

Answer: $0.75 (Option D)

2
Recognize that 125/100 can be thought of as 100/100 + 25/100, which is 1 whole and 25 hundredths.

Decimal notation: 1.25 km

Separate the whole number and fractional parts: 250/100 = 200/100 + 50/100.

3
Convert the whole number part: 200/100 = 2.
Convert the whole number portion of the fraction to an integer.
4
Convert the fractional part to a decimal: 50/100 = 0.50.
Convert the remaining proper fraction to a decimal. The denominator 100 means two decimal places.
5
Combine the parts: 2 + 0.50 = 2.50.
Add the whole number and the decimal part to get the final decimal notation.
6
Decimal notation: 2.50
State the final decimal representation.
