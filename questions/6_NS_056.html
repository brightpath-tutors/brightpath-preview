Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A company's stock value fluctuated throughout the day. It experienced a maximum drop of $11.50 from its opening price and a maximum gain of $9.25 from its opening price. What is the greatest absolute change in the stock's value during the day?
A. 20.75
B. 9.25
C. 11.5
D. 2.25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Maximum drop = -$11.50 Maximum gain = +$9.25
A drop is represented by a negative value, and a gain by a positive value.
2
Absolute value of drop = |-11.50| = 11.50 Absolute value of gain = |9.25| = 9.25
The absolute value of a number is its distance from zero, always a non-negative value. It indicates the magnitude of the change regardless of direction.
3
Comparing 11.50 and 9.25, the greatest absolute change is 11.50.
The greatest absolute change is simply the larger of the two positive absolute values.

Answer: 11.5 (Option C)

Question 3Grade 6 • NS
A deep freezer's temperature changed from -18°F to -3°F during a power outage. After power was restored, it changed from -3°F to -25°F. What was the greatest absolute temperature change (in °F) during these two periods?
A. 18
B. 22
C. 25
D. 15
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Change 1 = Final Temperature - Initial Temperature = -3°F - (-18°F) = -3°F + 18°F = 15°F.
Subtract the initial temperature from the final temperature. Subtracting a negative is equivalent to adding a positive.
2
Change 2 = Final Temperature - Initial Temperature = -25°F - (-3°F) = -25°F + 3°F = -22°F.
Again, subtract the initial temperature from the final temperature. The result is negative because the temperature decreased.
3
Absolute change 1 = |15| = 15°F. Absolute change 2 = |-22| = 22°F.
The absolute value of the change indicates the magnitude of the temperature difference, regardless of whether it increased or decreased.
4
Comparing 15 and 22, the greatest absolute temperature change is 22°F.
Select the larger of the two positive absolute values.

Answer: 22 (Option B)

Question 4Grade 6 • NS

A. 110
B. 305
C. 185
D. -185
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Descends 150 meters: -150 Rises 60 meters: +60 Descends 95 meters: -95
Descending means moving in the negative direction, and rising means moving in the positive direction.
2
Final position = -150 + 60 - 95 Final position = -90 - 95 Final position = -185 meters
Combine the signed numbers to find the net change in depth from the surface.
3
Absolute value of final depth = |-185| = 185 meters.
The absolute value represents the distance from the surface, which is always a positive quantity.

Answer: 185 (Option C)

Question 5Grade 6 • NS
On a history quiz, Maya scored 17 points below the class average, and Ben scored 24 points above the class average. What is the absolute value of the difference between Maya's score and Ben's score, relative to the class average?
A. 41
B. 48
C. -41
D. 7
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Maya's score deviation = -17 points.
A score below the average is represented by a negative value.
2
Ben's score deviation = +24 points.
A score above the average is represented by a positive value.
3
Difference = Ben's score - Maya's score = 24 - (-17) = 24 + 17 = 41. (Alternatively, Maya's score - Ben's score = -17 - 24 = -41).
Subtract one score from the other to find the numerical difference. The order of subtraction determines the sign, but the absolute value will be the same.
4
Absolute difference = |41| = 41 (or |-41| = 41).
The absolute value represents the magnitude of the difference, always a positive value.

Answer: 41 (Option A)

Question 6Grade 6 • NS
During a chemical reaction, the temperature of a solution started at 15°C. It then decreased by 25°C, increased by 8°C, and finally decreased by 13°C. What is the absolute value of the final temperature of the solution?
A. 15
B. 5
C. -15
D. 61
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial temperature: 15°C After decreasing by 25°C: 15 - 25 = -10°C After increasing by 8°C: -10 + 8 = -2°C After decreasing by 13°C: -2 - 13 = -15°C
Perform the addition and subtraction of integers in the order they occur to find the final temperature. A decrease is subtraction, an increase is addition.
2
The final temperature is -15°C. Absolute final temperature = |-15| = 15°C.
The absolute value represents the magnitude of the temperature, which is always a positive value.

Answer: 15 (Option A)

1

2

3

The absolute value indicates the distance from ground level, regardless of whether it's above or below.

C. 3
D. 18
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
City X offset = -6 hours (behind UTC) City Y offset = +3 hours (ahead of UTC)
Time behind UTC is negative, and time ahead of UTC is positive.
2
Difference = (+3) - (-6) = 3 + 6 = 9 hours. (Alternatively, Difference = (-6) - (+3) = -9 hours).
To find the difference between two quantities, subtract one from the other. Subtracting a negative number is the same as adding its positive counterpart.
3
Absolute difference = |9| = 9 (or |-9| = 9).
The absolute difference represents the magnitude of the time zone spread, which is always a positive value.
