Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A grocery store sells 6 oranges for $3.00. What is the unit price of one orange?
A. $2.00 per orange
B. $18.00 per orange
C. $0.60 per orange
D. $0.50 per orange
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total cost = $3.00, Number of oranges = 6
To find the unit price, we need to determine the cost per single item.
2
$3.00 ÷ 6 = $0.50
The unit price is the cost for one unit, so we divide the total cost by the quantity.
3
The unit price is $0.50 per orange.
The unit rate is expressed as 'cost per item'.

Answer: $0.50 per orange (Option D)

Question 2Grade 7 • RP
A swimmer completed 10 laps in 5 minutes. What was the swimmer's average speed in laps per minute?
A. 10 laps per minute
B. 0.5 laps per minute
C. 50 laps per minute
D. 2 laps per minute
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total laps = 10, Total time = 5 minutes
To find the average speed, we need to determine the number of laps completed per minute.
2
10 laps ÷ 5 minutes = 2 laps/minute
The average speed is the rate of laps per unit of time, so we divide the laps by the minutes.
3
The average speed is 2 laps per minute.
The unit rate is expressed as 'quantity per unit of time'.

Question 3Grade 7 • RP

C. 2 cups of water per cup of rice
D. 0.5 cups of water per cup of rice
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Water = 4 cups, Rice = 2 cups
To find the unit rate of water per cup of rice, we need to determine how much water is used for one cup of rice.
2
4 cups water ÷ 2 cups rice = 2 cups water/cup of rice
The unit rate is the ratio of water to rice, expressed as 'water per unit of rice'.
3
The unit rate is 2 cups of water per cup of rice.
The unit rate specifies the quantity of water for a single unit of rice.

Question 8Grade 7 • RP
A clock gained 18 seconds over 2 hours. What is the clock's rate of gain in seconds per minute, in simplest form?
A. 0.3 seconds per minute
B. 0.6 seconds per minute
C. 9 seconds per minute
D. 0.15 seconds per minute
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total gain = 18 seconds, Total time = 2 hours
We need to find the rate in seconds per minute, so we must convert the time unit from hours to minutes.
2
2 hours × 60 minutes/hour = 120 minutes
There are 60 minutes in 1 hour, so multiply the number of hours by 60.
3
18 seconds ÷ 120 minutes = 0.15 seconds/minute
Now that both units are compatible, divide the total seconds by the total minutes to get the unit rate.
4
The rate of gain is 0.15 seconds per minute.
The unit rate is expressed as 'quantity per unit of time'.

Answer: 0.15 seconds per minute (Option D)
