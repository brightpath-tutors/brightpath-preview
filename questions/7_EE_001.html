Question 1Grade 7 • EE
Write an algebraic expression for the phrase: "5 less than twice a number x.
A. 2x - 5
B. 2x + 5
C. 5 - 2x
D. 2(x - 5)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Identify the unknown quantity and represent it with a variable.
The problem states 'a number x', so we use 'x' to represent this unknown number.
2
Translate the part 'twice a number x' into an algebraic term.
'Twice' means to multiply by 2. So, 'twice a number x' is 2 * x, or simply 2x.
3
Translate the phrase '5 less than' into an operation and apply it to the previous term.
'5 less than' means to subtract 5 from the quantity that follows it. In this case, it's 5 less than '2x'. Therefore, we subtract 5 from 2x.
4
Combine the terms to form the complete algebraic expression.
Putting it all together, '5 less than twice a number x' is written as 2x - 5.

Answer: 2x - 5 (Option A)

Question 2Grade 7 • EE
Liam has y comic books. He buys 7 more comic books. Then, he sells one-third of the new total number of comic books. Write an expression that represents the number of comic books Liam has left.
A. (y + 7) / 3
B. (2/3)(y + 7)
C. y + 7 - 1/3
D. y / 3 + 7
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial comic books: y
Start by identifying the initial number of comic books Liam has, which is represented by the variable y.
2
Total after buying more: y + 7
Liam buys 7 more comic books, so we add 7 to his initial amount to find the new total number of comic books.
3
Fraction of comic books left: 1 - 1/3 = 2/3
He sells one-third of his total comic books. If he sells 1/3, then the fraction of comic books he has left is 1 (the whole) minus 1/3, which equals 2/3.
4
Expression for comic books left: (2/3)(y + 7)
To find the number of comic books Liam has left, multiply the total number of comic books (y + 7) by the fraction he has left (2/3).

Answer: (2/3)(y + 7) (Option B)

Question 3Grade 7 • EE
Write an algebraic expression for '5 less than twice a number x'.
A. 2(x - 5)
B. 5 - 2x
C. 2x + 5
D. 2x - 5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Identify the variable and the first operation.
The phrase 'a number x' refers to the variable x. 'Twice a number x' means to multiply x by 2, which can be written as 2x.
2
Identify the second operation and construct the expression.
The phrase '5 less than' means to subtract 5 from the result of the previous operation (2x). Therefore, the expression is 2x - 5.

Answer: 2x - 5 (Option D)
