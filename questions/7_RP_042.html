Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A toy car costs $15. Its real-life counterpart costs $30,000. What is the scale factor of the toy car's price to the real car's price, in simplest form?
A. 1/15
B. 2000
C. 1/200
D. 1/2000
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Toy car price = $15 Real car price = $30,000
The problem provides the price of the toy car and the real car.
2
Scale factor = (Toy car price) / (Real car price) Scale factor = 15 / 30000
The scale factor is the ratio of the model's dimension (toy car's price) to the actual object's corresponding dimension (real car's price).
3
Divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 15. 15 ÷ 15 = 1 30000 ÷ 15 = 2000 Scale factor = 1/2000
To express the scale factor in simplest form, divide both parts of the ratio by the largest number that divides into both evenly.

Answer: 1/2000 (Option D)

Question 4Grade 7 • RP
On a road map, the distance between two cities is 8 centimeters. The actual distance between these cities is 160 kilometers. What is the scale factor of the map to the actual distance, in simplest form?
A. 1/200,000
B. 1/2,000,000
C. 2,000,000
D. 1/20
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Map distance = 8 cm Actual distance = 160 km
The problem provides both dimensions, but in different units.
2
There are 1000 meters in 1 kilometer, and 100 centimeters in 1 meter. So, 1 km = 1000 100 cm = 100,000 cm. Actual distance = 160 km 100,000 cm/km = 16,000,000 cm.
For a scale factor to be dimensionless, both quantities must be in the same units. Converting kilometers to centimeters is necessary.
3
Scale factor = (Map distance) / (Actual distance) Scale factor = 8 cm / 16,000,000 cm = 8 / 16,000,000
Set up the ratio of the map dimension to the corresponding actual dimension.
4
Divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 8. 8 ÷ 8 = 1 16,000,000 ÷ 8 = 2,000,000 Scale factor = 1/2,000,000
Simplify the fraction to its simplest form.

Answer: 1/2,000,000 (Option B)

Question 10Grade 7 • RP
A microchip engineer is designing a new chip. A component on the design diagram is 15 cm long. The actual component on the chip will be 3 micrometers long. What is the scale factor of the design diagram to the actual component, in simplest form?
A. 1/5
B. 5,000
C. 1/50,000
D. 50,000
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Design diagram length = 15 cm Actual component length = 3 micrometers
The problem provides both dimensions, but in different units.
2
There are 10 millimeters in 1 centimeter, and 1000 micrometers in 1 millimeter. So, 1 cm = 10 mm 1000 µm/mm = 10,000 µm. Design diagram length = 15 cm 10,000 µm/cm = 150,000 µm.
For a scale factor to be dimensionless, both quantities must be in the same units. Converting centimeters to micrometers is necessary due to the large difference in scale.
3
Scale factor = (Design diagram length) / (Actual component length) Scale factor = 150,000 µm / 3 µm = 150,000 / 3
Set up the ratio of the design dimension to the corresponding actual dimension.
4
150,000 ÷ 3 = 50,000 Scale factor = 50,000
Divide the numerator by the denominator. Since the result is a whole number, it is already in simplest form.

Answer: 50,000 (Option D)
