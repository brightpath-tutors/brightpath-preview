Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A shirt costs $15.75. Which of the following represents the price of the shirt as a rational number?
A. 15 3/4
B. 1575/1000
C. 15.075
D. 15 75/10
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The price is $15.75. The whole number part is 15. The decimal part is 0.75.
First, we separate the whole number from the fractional part of the decimal.
2
0.75 can be written as 75/100.
Since 0.75 represents 75 hundredths, we write it as a fraction with a denominator of 100.
3
Divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 25. 75 ÷ 25 = 3, and 100 ÷ 25 = 4. So, 75/100 simplifies to 3/4.
To get the fraction in simplest form, we divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common factor.
4
The rational number is 15 3/4.
Finally, we combine the whole number part with the simplified fractional part to form the mixed number.

Answer: C. 15 3/4 (Option A)

Question 9Grade 6 • NS
A project manager estimated that a task would take 1 1/2 hours. Due to an unexpected issue, the task took an additional 0.75 hours. What was the total time spent on the task, expressed as a fraction in simplest form?
A. 2 1/4 hours
B. 2 1/2 hours
C. 2 hours
D. 1 4/6 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial time: 1 1/2 hours = (1 × 2 + 1)/2 = 3/2 hours. Additional time: 0.75 hours = 75/100 = 3/4 hours.
To add these values, it's best to convert them both to fractions, preferably improper fractions for easier arithmetic.
2
We need a common denominator for 3/2 and 3/4. The least common denominator is 4. Convert 3/2 to an equivalent fraction with a denominator of 4: (3 × 2)/(2 × 2) = 6/4. Now, add: 6/4 + 3/4 = 9/4 hours.
Before adding fractions, ensure they have a common denominator. Then, add the numerators and keep the common denominator.
3
Divide the numerator (9) by the denominator (4): 9 ÷ 4 = 2 with a remainder of 1. So, 9/4 hours = 2 1/4 hours.
An improper fraction can be expressed as a mixed number by dividing the numerator by the denominator. The quotient is the whole number, and the remainder over the original denominator is the fraction.

Answer: C. 2 1/4 hours (Option A)

Question 10Grade 6 • NS
An artist is creating a canvas with a width between 1.25 meters and 1 3/8 meters. Which of the following could be the exact width of the canvas?
A. 1.4 meters
B. 1.2 meters
C. 1 1/2 meters
D. 1 5/16 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Lower bound: 1.25 meters. Upper bound: 1 3/8 meters. To convert 3/8 to a decimal, divide 3 by 8: 3 ÷ 8 = 0.375. So, 1 3/8 meters = 1.375 meters. The range for the canvas width is between 1.25 meters and 1.375 meters.
To identify numbers within a range that includes both decimals and fractions, it is helpful to convert all values to a single format, such as decimals.
2
A. 1 5/16 meters: Convert 5/16 to a decimal: 5 ÷ 16 = 0.3125. So, 1 5/16 meters = 1.3125 meters. Is 1.25 < 1.3125 < 1.375? Yes. B. 1.2 meters: Is 1.25 < 1.2 < 1.375? No, 1.2 is less than 1.25. C. 1.4 meters: Is 1.25 < 1.4 < 1.375? No, 1.4 is greater than 1.375. D. 1 1/2 meters: Convert 1/2 to a decimal: 1 ÷ 2 = 0.5. So, 1 1/2 meters = 1.5 meters. Is 1.25 < 1.5 < 1.375? No, 1.5 is greater than 1.375.
We systematically convert each option to a decimal and compare it with the established decimal range to find the correct value.
3
Only 1 5/16 meters (1.3125 meters) falls between 1.25 meters and 1.375 meters.
The correct option is the one whose decimal equivalent is numerically greater than the lower bound and less than the upper bound of the given range.

Answer: A. 1 5/16 meters (Option D)
