E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1

Identify what the coordinates represent in the context of the problem.

Question 9Grade 6 • RP
A data transfer graph shows the amount of data downloaded (in megabytes) over time (in seconds). The graph includes points (2, 50) and (5, 125). Which of the following statements is true about the relationship, and what is the unit rate of download?
A. The relationship is proportional, and the unit rate is 50 MB/second
B. The relationship is proportional, and the unit rate is 25 MB/second
C. The relationship is not proportional because the points don't explicitly show (0,0)
D. The relationship is not proportional because the x-values are different
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
For point (2, 50): Unit rate = 50 MB / 2 seconds = 25 MB/second. For point (5, 125): Unit rate = 125 MB / 5 seconds = 25 MB/second.
Calculate the unit rate for each given point. If the unit rates are consistent, the relationship is proportional.
2
Since the unit rate is the same for both points (25 MB/second) and data transfer starts from zero (0 MB in 0 seconds), the relationship is proportional, and the unit rate is 25 MB/second.
A consistent unit rate confirms proportionality for a relationship that passes through the origin.

Answer: The relationship is proportional, and the unit rate is 25 MB/second. (Option B)

Question 10Grade 6 • RP
An artist mixes red and yellow paint to create orange. A graph shows the relationship between red paint (x-axis, in milliliters) and yellow paint (y-axis, in milliliters). The graph is a straight line that passes through the point (0, 0) and (150, 225). If the artist wants to make a batch of orange paint using 400 milliliters of red paint, how much yellow paint will they need, and what is the ratio of red to yellow paint in simplest form?
A. 266.67 ml yellow paint; ratio is 3:2
B. 600 ml yellow paint; ratio is 3:2
C. 600 ml yellow paint; ratio is 2:3
D. 450 ml yellow paint; ratio is 2:3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
From (150, 225), the artist uses 225 ml of yellow paint for 150 ml of red paint. Unit rate = 225 ml yellow / 150 ml red = 1.5 ml yellow per ml red.
Calculate the amount of yellow paint needed for every milliliter of red paint.
2
Yellow paint needed = Unit rate × Red paint used = 1.5 × 400 ml = 600 ml yellow paint.
Multiply the unit rate by the desired amount of red paint to find the corresponding yellow paint.
3
The ratio is Red : Yellow = 150 : 225. Divide both by their greatest common factor (75): 150 ÷ 75 : 225 ÷ 75 = 2 : 3.
Form the ratio using the given quantities and simplify it by dividing both parts by their greatest common factor.

Answer: 600 ml yellow paint; ratio is 2:3 (Option C)
