Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A baker has a large batch of cookie dough weighing 8 and 3/4 pounds. If each cookie requires 1/8 pound of dough, how many cookies can the baker make?
A. 70 cookies
B. 35 cookies
C. 17.5 cookies
D. 7 cookies
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total dough = 8 3/4 pounds = 35/4 pounds Dough per cookie = 1/8 pound
Convert the mixed number to an improper fraction and identify the given quantities.
2
Number of cookies = (35/4) ÷ (1/8)
To find the number of cookies, divide the total dough by the dough required per cookie.
3
(35/4) × (8/1)
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.
4
(35 × 8) / (4 × 1) = 280 / 4
Multiply the numerators and the denominators.
5
280 / 4 = 70
Simplify the fraction to get the final number of cookies.

Answer: 70 cookies (Option A)

Question 5Grade 7 • NS
In a competitive gaming league, a player's score changed by -15 and 3/5 points over 4 equal rounds. What was the average score change per round? Express your answer as a mixed number in simplest form.
A. -3 9/10 points per round
B. 3 9/10 points per round
C. -62 2/5 points per round
D. -15 3/20 points per round
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total score change = -15 3/5 points = -78/5 points Number of rounds = 4
Convert the mixed number total score change to an improper fraction and identify the number of rounds.
2
Average score change = (-78/5) ÷ 4
To find the average score change, divide the total score change by the number of rounds.
3
(-78/5) ÷ (4/1)
Represent the whole number 4 as a fraction 4/1.
4
(-78/5) × (1/4)
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.
5
(-78 × 1) / (5 × 4) = -78 / 20
Multiply the numerators and the denominators. The product of a negative and a positive number is negative.
6
78 ÷ 2 = 39 20 ÷ 2 = 10 Result = -39/10
Simplify the fraction by dividing the numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 2.
7
39 ÷ 10 = 3 with a remainder of 9 So, -39/10 = -3 9/10
Convert the improper fraction to a mixed number, keeping the negative sign.

Answer: -3 9/10 points per round (Option A)

Question 6Grade 7 • NS
A science experiment tracks the change in temperature of a substance. Over 3 and 1/3 hours, the temperature decreased by a total of 8 and 1/2 degrees Celsius. What was the average hourly temperature change? Express your answer as a mixed number in simplest form.
A. 2 11/20 degrees Celsius per hour
B. -2 11/20 degrees Celsius per hour
C. -28 1/3 degrees Celsius per hour
D. -4 1/4 degrees Celsius per hour
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total temperature change = -8 1/2 degrees Celsius = -17/2 degrees Celsius Total time = 3 1/3 hours = 10/3 hours
Convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions. A decrease in temperature is represented by a negative value.
2
Average hourly change = (-17/2) ÷ (10/3)
To find the average hourly change, divide the total temperature change by the total time.
3
(-17/2) × (3/10)
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.
4
(-17 × 3) / (2 × 10) = -51 / 20
Multiply the numerators and the denominators. The product of a negative and a positive number is negative.
5
51 ÷ 20 = 2 with a remainder of 11 So, -51/20 = -2 11/20
Convert the improper fraction to a mixed number, retaining the negative sign.

Answer: -2 11/20 degrees Celsius per hour (Option B)
