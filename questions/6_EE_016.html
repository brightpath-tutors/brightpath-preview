Question 1Grade 6 • EE
A shop owner wants to display a special item on a square pedestal. The side length of the pedestal is 3 decimeters. What is the area of the top surface of the pedestal in square decimeters? Express your answer using exponents.
A. 9 square decimeters
B. 12 square decimeters
C. 6 square decimeters
D. 3 square decimeters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Area = side × side = side^2
The area of a square is found by multiplying its side length by itself.
2
Area = 3 dm × 3 dm = 3^2 dm^2
The side length is 3 decimeters, so we raise 3 to the power of 2.
3
3^2 = 3 × 3 = 9
3 squared means 3 multiplied by itself once.
4
Area = 9 square decimeters
The area of the pedestal's top surface is 9 square decimeters.

Answer: 9 square decimeters (Option A)

3
2^5 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2

4

5
After 5 hours, there would be 32 units of bacteria.
