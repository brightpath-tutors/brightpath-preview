Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Sarah wants to buy a new video game that costs $55. She has $10 saved and earns $5 per hour babysitting. How many hours, h, must Sarah babysit to have at least $55?
A. h ≥ 9 hours
B. h ≥ 13 hours
C. h ≤ 13 hours
D. h ≤ 9 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
10 + 5h ≥ 55
The current savings ($10) plus the money earned from babysitting (5h) must be greater than or equal to the cost of the game ($55).
2
5h ≥ 55 - 10 5h ≥ 45
This step determines how much more money Sarah needs to earn from babysitting.
3
h ≥ 45 / 5 h ≥ 9
Dividing by 5 gives the number of hours required. Since 5 is a positive number, the inequality sign does not flip.

Answer: h ≥ 9 hours (Option A)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
A student needs an average of at least 80% on 3 tests to pass a class. If their scores on the first two tests were 75% and 82%, what score, s, must they get on the third test to pass?
A. s ≥ 78%
B. s ≤ 83%
C. s ≥ 80%
D. s ≥ 83%
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
(75 + 82 + s) / 3 ≥ 80
The sum of the known scores (75, 82) and the unknown score (s) divided by the number of tests (3) must be at least 80.
2
75 + 82 + s ≥ 80 * 3 75 + 82 + s ≥ 240
Multiplying by 3 isolates the sum of the scores. Since 3 is a positive number, the inequality sign does not flip.
3
157 + s ≥ 240
Combine the constant terms on the left side of the inequality.
4
s ≥ 240 - 157 s ≥ 83
Subtracting 157 from both sides isolates 's', giving the minimum score required. Since we are subtracting, the inequality sign does not flip.

Answer: s ≥ 83% (Option D)

Question 3Grade 8 • EE
A basketball team must score more than 120 points in a game to break a school record. So far, they have scored 75 points in the first three quarters. If each basket is worth 2 points, how many baskets, b, must they make in the last quarter to break the record?
A. b ≤ 22 baskets
B. b ≥ 21 baskets
C. b ≥ 23 baskets
D. b ≥ 22 baskets
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
75 + 2b > 120
The current score (75) plus the points from additional baskets (2b) must be greater than 120.
2
2b > 120 - 75 2b > 45
This step determines the minimum number of additional points the team needs.
3
b > 45 / 2 b > 22.5
Dividing by 2 gives the number of baskets required. Since 2 is a positive number, the inequality sign does not flip.
4
b ≥ 23
As baskets are whole units, 22.5 baskets is not possible. To score more than 45 points, they need to make at least 23 baskets (23 * 2 = 46 points).

Answer: b ≥ 23 baskets (Option C)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A construction crew needs to pour at least 25 cubic meters of concrete for a foundation. They have already poured 8 cubic meters. If their concrete mixer can mix 3.5 cubic meters per batch, how many batches, b, must they mix to meet the requirement?
A. b ≥ 5 batches
B. b ≤ 4 batches
C. b ≥ 6 batches
D. b ≥ 4 batches
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
8 + 3.5b ≥ 25
The concrete already poured (8 m³) plus the concrete from additional batches (3.5 m³ per batch 'b') must be greater than or equal to the required amount (25 m³).
2
3.5b ≥ 25 - 8 3.5b ≥ 17
This step determines how much more concrete the crew needs to mix.
3
b ≥ 17 / 3.5 b ≥ 4.857...
Dividing by 3.5 gives the minimum number of batches. Since 3.5 is a positive number, the inequality sign does not flip.
4
b ≥ 5
As batches are whole units, 4.857 batches is not possible. To meet or exceed the requirement, they need to mix at least 5 batches.

Answer: b ≥ 5 batches (Option A)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A chemist is mixing a solution. The concentration of a certain chemical needs to be less than 6% to avoid a dangerous reaction. The current solution has a 2% concentration, and adding each milliliter, m, of a stronger chemical increases the overall concentration by 0.25%. What is the maximum number of milliliters of the stronger chemical that can be added?
A. m < 16 ml
B. m < 14 ml
C. m > 16 ml
D. m < 8 ml
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
2 + 0.25m < 6
The current concentration (2%) plus the increase in concentration from adding 'm' milliliters of the stronger chemical (0.25m) must be strictly less than 6%.
2
0.25m < 6 - 2 0.25m < 4
This step determines the maximum allowable increase in concentration.
3
m < 4 / 0.25 m < 16
Dividing by 0.25 (or multiplying by 4) gives the maximum milliliters. Since 0.25 is a positive number, the inequality sign does not flip.

Answer: m < 16 ml (Option A)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
A project manager needs to finish a task in no more than 40 hours. She has already spent 15 hours on the task. If she can delegate the remaining work to two team members who each work at a rate of 1.25 hours per section, how many sections, s, can each team member work on to complete the task within the time limit?
A. s ≤ 8 sections
B. s ≤ 12 sections
C. s ≤ 10 sections
D. s ≥ 10 sections
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
15 + 2 * (1.25s) ≤ 40
The hours already spent (15) plus the total hours worked by the two team members (2 * 1.25s, since there are two members and each works 's' sections at 1.25 hours/section) must be less than or equal to the total time limit (40 hours).
2
15 + 2.5s ≤ 40
Multiply 2 by 1.25 to get the combined hourly rate for one section for both team members.
3
2.5s ≤ 40 - 15 2.5s ≤ 25
This step determines the maximum number of hours remaining for the team members to work.
4
s ≤ 25 / 2.5 s ≤ 10
Dividing by 2.5 gives the maximum number of sections. Since 2.5 is a positive number, the inequality sign does not flip.

Answer: s ≤ 10 sections (Option C)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A technician needs to install updates on several computers. Each update takes 7 minutes, plus an initial setup time of 15 minutes for the first computer. He wants to finish the installations in under 90 minutes. How many computers, c, can he update?
A. c ≥ 11 computers
B. c ≤ 9 computers
C. c ≤ 11 computers
D. c ≤ 10 computers
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
15 + 7c < 90
The initial setup time (15 minutes) plus the time for updating 'c' computers (7 minutes per computer) must be strictly less than 90 minutes.
2
7c < 90 - 15 7c < 75
This step determines the maximum time available for the actual updates on the computers.
3
c < 75 / 7 c < 10.71...
Dividing by 7 gives the maximum number of computers. Since 7 is a positive number, the inequality sign does not flip.
4
c ≤ 10
As computers are whole units, 10.71 computers is not possible. To stay under the time limit, he can update a maximum of 10 computers.

Answer: c ≤ 10 computers (Option D)
