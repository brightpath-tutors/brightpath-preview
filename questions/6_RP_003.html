Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A grocery store sells 2 pounds of apples for $3. If you buy 6 pounds of apples, how much will it cost?
A. $6
B. $3
C. $9
D. $12
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
2 pounds : $3
The problem states that 2 pounds of apples cost $3.
2
Desired pounds / Given pounds = 6 pounds / 2 pounds = 3
To find how many times the quantity of apples is increasing, divide the desired amount (6 pounds) by the given amount (2 pounds). The scaling factor is 3.
3
$3 × 3 = $9
Since the quantity of apples is multiplied by 3, the cost must also be multiplied by 3 to maintain an equivalent ratio.

Answer: $9 (Option C)

18 bricks : 3 bags cement

2
Desired cement / Given cement = 1 bag / 3 bags = 1/3

Question 9Grade 6 • RP

A. 300 people
B. 1800 people
C. 360 people
D. 150 people
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
150 people / 5 square miles = 30 people per square mile
Divide the total population by the total area to find the population density for one square mile.
2
30 people/square mile × 12 square miles = 360 people
Multiply the unit rate by the new area (12 square miles) to find the total population for that area. The answer is already in simplest form.

Answer: 360 people (Option C)
