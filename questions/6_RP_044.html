Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A store sells juice boxes in a ratio of 2 boxes for $3. If you want to buy 8 boxes, how much will it cost?
A. $12
B. $6
C. $9
D. $11
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Given ratio: 2 boxes : $3. Target quantity: 8 boxes.
We need to find the equivalent cost for 8 juice boxes based on the given ratio.
2
To go from 2 boxes to 8 boxes, we divide 8 by 2. Scaling factor = 8 / 2 = 4.
The scaling factor tells us how many times we need to multiply the original ratio to get the new ratio.
3
Multiply the original cost by the scaling factor: $3 * 4 = $12.
To maintain an equivalent ratio, both parts of the ratio must be multiplied by the same scaling factor.
4
It will cost $12 to buy 8 juice boxes.
The cost for 8 juice boxes is $12.

Answer: $12 (Option A)
