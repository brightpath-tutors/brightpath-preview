Question 1Grade 6 • EE
A movie ticket costs $12. If Ben buys 't' tickets, which expression represents the total cost?
A. 12t
B. 12 + t
C. t / 12
D. t - 12
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost per ticket = $12, Number of tickets = t
We are given the price of one ticket and a variable for the number of tickets.
2
To find the total cost, multiply the cost per ticket by the number of tickets.
When you have a cost per item and a quantity, multiplication is used to find the total.
3
12 * t = 12t
The expression 12t represents the total cost in dollars.

Answer: 12t (Option A)

Question 5Grade 6 • EE

B. 6c + 24
C. 24c - 6
D. 24 + c + 6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Number of boxes = c, Crayons per box = 24. Total from boxes = 24 * c = 24c
Multiply the number of boxes by the crayons in each box to get the total from boxes.
2
Loose crayons = 6
This is a fixed number given in the problem.
3
Total crayons = (crayons from boxes) + (loose crayons) = 24c + 6
Add the two quantities to find the overall total.

Question 6Grade 6 • EE

B. 50 - 2m
C. 2 / (50 - m)
D. (50 - m) / 2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial amount = 50 ml, Amount used = m ml. Remaining amount = 50 - m
Subtract the amount used from the initial amount to find what's left.
2
Remaining amount = (50 - m), Number of beakers = 2. Amount per beaker = (50 - m) / 2
Divide the remaining solution by the number of beakers to find the amount in each.
3
(50 - m) / 2

Question 10Grade 6 • EE
A phone plan includes 10 gigabytes of data. If a user has used 'g' gigabytes and each additional gigabyte costs $8, write an expression for the cost of additional data if 'g' is greater than 10.
A. 8 * (g - 10)
B. 8g - 10
C. 10 - 8g
D. 8g + 10
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total data used = g GB, Included data = 10 GB. Additional data = g - 10
Since 'g' is greater than 10, subtract the included amount to find the extra data used.
2
Additional data = (g - 10), Cost per additional GB = $8. Total cost = 8 * (g - 10)
Multiply the amount of additional data by the cost per additional gigabyte.
3
8 * (g - 10)
Parentheses ensure the subtraction is performed before multiplication, correctly calculating the cost of only the additional data.

Answer: 8 * (g - 10) (Option A)
