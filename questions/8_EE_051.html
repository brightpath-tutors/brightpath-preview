2

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
An artist is painting a mural that requires 2.5 × 10¹² paint molecules per square centimeter. If the mural has an area of 1.2 × 10⁵ square centimeters, and each tube of paint contains 3.0 × 10¹⁸ paint molecules, how many tubes of paint are needed for the mural?
A. 1.0 × 10⁻³⁰ tubes
B. 1.0 × 10⁻¹ tubes
C. 1.0 × 10¹ tubes
D. 1.0 × 10³⁰ tubes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
First, calculate the total number of paint molecules required for the mural (molecules per cm² × total cm²). Then, divide this total by the number of molecules per tube to find the number of tubes. Operations: Multiplication, then Division.
This is a multi-step problem. We need to find the total quantity first, then divide by the quantity per unit to find the number of units.
2
Total molecules = (2.5 × 10¹²) × (1.2 × 10⁵) Multiply coefficients: 2.5 × 1.2 = 3.0 Add exponents: 12 + 5 = 17 Result: 3.0 × 10¹⁷ molecules
Multiply the coefficients and add the exponents for the first part of the calculation.
3
Number of tubes = (3.0 × 10¹⁷) ÷ (3.0 × 10¹⁸) Divide coefficients: 3.0 ÷ 3.0 = 1.0 Subtract exponents: 17 - 18 = -1 Result: 1.0 × 10⁻¹ tubes
Now, divide the total molecules by the molecules per tube. Divide the coefficients and subtract the exponents.
4
The final result is 1.0 × 10⁻¹ tubes. The coefficient 1.0 is between 1 and 10, so it is in correct scientific notation.
Ensure the final answer is presented in correct scientific notation.

Answer: 1.0 × 10⁻¹ tubes (Option B)
