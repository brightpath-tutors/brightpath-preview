Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A baker is making a cake and needs to measure out \(\sqrt{40}\) grams of sugar. Approximately how many grams of sugar does the baker need to measure? Round your answer to the nearest tenth.
A. 6.2 grams
B. 6.0 grams
C. 6.3 grams
D. 6.4 grams
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$6^2 = 36$ $7^2 = 49$ So, \(\sqrt{36} < \sqrt{40} < \sqrt{49}\), which means $6 < \sqrt{40} < 7$.
To approximate an irrational number, find the nearest perfect squares above and below it.
2
Since 40 is closer to 36 than 49, \(\sqrt{40}\) will be closer to 6. Let's try 6.3: $6.3 \times 6.3 = 39.69$ Let's try 6.4: $6.4 \times 6.4 = 40.96$ So, \(\sqrt{40}\) is between 6.3 and 6.4.
Refine the estimate by testing decimal values. Since 39.69 is closer to 40 than 40.96 (40 - 39.69 = 0.31, 40.96 - 40 = 0.96), 6.3 is the better approximation.
3
Comparing $39.69$ (for 6.3) and $40.96$ (for 6.4) to 40, $39.69$ is closer (difference of 0.31) than $40.96$ (difference of 0.96). Therefore, \(\sqrt{40} \approx 6.3\).
Choose the decimal that results in a square closest to the original number.

Answer: 6.3 grams (Option C)
