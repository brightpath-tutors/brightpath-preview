Question 1Grade 7 • NS
Sarah had $5/2 dollars in her wallet. She spent $3/4 dollars on candy. What is her current balance, in simplest form?
A. $4/3
B. $-7/4
C. $13/4
D. $7/4
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial amount = $5/2, Amount spent = $3/4
Identify the initial amount of money and the amount spent.
2
$5/2 - 3/4
Set up the subtraction to find the remaining balance.
3
Least Common Denominator (LCD) of 2 and 4 is 4. Convert $5/2 to an equivalent fraction with denominator 4: $5/2 = (5 \times 2)/(2 \times 2) = 10/4$.
Find a common denominator for the fractions and convert them.
4
10/4 - 3/4 = (10 - 3)/4 = 7/4
Subtract the numerators while keeping the common denominator.
5
7/4
The fraction 7/4 is already in simplest form, as 7 and 4 have no common factors other than 1.

Answer: $7/4 (Option D)

Question 8Grade 7 • NS
A clock is losing time. On Monday, it lost 1/5 of an hour. On Tuesday, it lost another 1/10 of an hour. On Wednesday, it gained 1/2 an hour due to a repair. What is the total net change in time for the clock over these three days? Express your answer as a fraction in simplest form.
A. 1/5 hour
B. 4/5 hour
C. -1/5 hour
D. 2/10 hour
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Monday: -1/5 hour, Tuesday: -1/10 hour, Wednesday: +1/2 hour
Represent time lost as negative fractions and time gained as a positive fraction.
2
-1/5 + (-1/10) + 1/2
Set up the addition to find the total net change in time.
3
LCD of 5, 10, and 2 is 10.
Find the least common denominator for all three fractions.
4
Convert fractions: -1/5 = -2/10, -1/10 remains -1/10, 1/2 = 5/10.
Convert each fraction to an equivalent fraction with the common denominator.
5
-2/10 + (-1/10) + 5/10 = (-2 - 1 + 5)/10 = (-3 + 5)/10 = 2/10
Add the numerators, following rules for adding positive and negative numbers, while keeping the common denominator.
6
2/10 = 1/5
Simplify the fraction by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common factor, 2.
