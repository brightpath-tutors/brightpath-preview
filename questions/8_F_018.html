Question 1Grade 8 • F
A football is kicked, and its height in meters above the ground is modeled by the function h(t) = -5(t - 2)^2 + 25, where t is the time in seconds after it is kicked. What is the maximum height the football reaches?
A. 2 meters
B. 25 meters
C. -25 meters
D. -2 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The general vertex form is y = a(x - h)^2 + k.
Understanding the vertex form is crucial for identifying the vertex.
2
Given: h(t) = -5(t - 2)^2 + 25. Comparing with y = a(x - h)^2 + k, we have a = -5, h = 2, k = 25.
The 'h' value is subtracted from 'x' (or 't' in this case), so the sign is flipped. The 'k' value is added at the end, retaining its sign.
3
The vertex is (h, k) = (2, 25).
The vertex represents the turning point of the parabola.
4
Since 'a' = -5 (which is negative), the parabola opens downwards, meaning the vertex (2, 25) represents the maximum point. The maximum height is the 'k' value.
For a downward-opening parabola, the y-coordinate of the vertex (k) is the maximum value of the function.
5
The maximum height the football reaches is 25 meters.
The 'k' value, 25, directly corresponds to the maximum height in the function.

Answer: 25 meters (Option B)
