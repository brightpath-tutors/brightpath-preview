Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A baker uses 1/2 pound of sugar for every batch of cookies. If she has 3 pounds of sugar, how many batches of cookies can she make?
A. 3.5 batches
B. 6 batches
C. 1.5 batches
D. 0.166... batches
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total sugar = 3 pounds Sugar per batch = 1/2 pound
Identify the total amount of sugar and the amount needed per batch.
2
Number of batches = 3 ÷ 1/2
To find the number of batches, divide the total amount of sugar by the amount needed for each batch.
3
Number of batches = 3 × 2/1 = 3 × 2 = 6
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal (flip the second fraction and change the operation to multiplication).

Answer: 6 batches (Option B)

Question 4Grade 6 • NS
A car travels 7/8 mile in 1/4 hour. What is the car's speed in miles per hour, in simplest form?
A. 3 1/2 miles per hour
B. 1/2 miles per hour
C. 1 1/8 miles per hour
D. 7/32 miles per hour
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Distance = 7/8 mile Time = 1/4 hour
Identify the distance traveled and the time taken.
2
Speed = Distance ÷ Time Speed = 7/8 ÷ 1/4
To find speed, divide the distance by the time.
3
Speed = 7/8 × 4/1
To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.
4
Speed = (7 × 4) / (8 × 1) = 28/8
Multiply the numerators and the denominators.
5
28/8 = 7/2 = 3 1/2
Simplify the fraction by dividing both numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor (4), then convert the improper fraction to a mixed number.

Answer: 3 1/2 miles per hour (Option A)
