Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A candy bar costs 1/3 of a dollar. Express this amount as a repeating decimal.
A. $0.13$
B. $0.3$
C. $0.33$
D. $0.\overline{3}$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
\(\text{1 \div 3}\)
Set up the long division problem.
2
`` 0.333... 3 | 1.000 - 9 --- 10 - 9 ---- 10 - 9 ----- 1 ``
When you divide 1 by 3, you get 0 with a remainder of 1. Bringing down a zero makes it 10, which divides by 3 three times with a remainder of 1. This pattern continues indefinitely.
3
The digit '3' repeats. So, $0.333... = 0.\overline{3}$
The bar indicates that the digit or sequence of digits underneath it repeats infinitely.

Answer: $0.\overline{3}$ (Option D)
