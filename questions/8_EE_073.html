Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A store sells a particular brand of sneakers for $45. Each pair of socks costs $3. If Sarah buys one pair of sneakers and several pairs of socks, and her total bill is $60, how many pairs of socks did Sarah buy?
A. 6 pairs of socks
B. 4 pairs of socks
C. 5 pairs of socks
D. 3 pairs of socks
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let s be the number of pairs of socks. Cost of sneakers + Cost of socks = Total bill 45 + 3s = 60
Define the variable for the number of socks and set up the equation based on the given information.
2
45 + 3s = 60 3s = 60 - 45 3s = 15
Subtract the cost of the sneakers from the total bill to find the total cost of the socks.
3
3s = 15 s = 15 / 3 s = 5
Divide the total cost of the socks by the cost per pair to find the number of pairs of socks.

Answer: 5 pairs of socks (Option C)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
A basketball team scored 82 points in a game. This total included 12 free throws, each worth 1 point. The remaining points were from 2-point shots. How many 2-point shots did the team make?
A. 47 shots
B. 41 shots
C. 38 shots
D. 35 shots
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let x be the number of 2-point shots. Total points = points from free throws + points from 2-point shots 82 = 12(1) + 2x 82 = 12 + 2x
Define the variable for 2-point shots and set up the equation based on the total points and points from different types of shots.
2
82 = 12 + 2x 82 - 12 = 2x 70 = 2x
Subtract the points from free throws from the total points to find the points from 2-point shots.
3
70 = 2x x = 70 / 2 x = 35
Divide the total points from 2-point shots by 2 to find the number of 2-point shots.

Answer: 35 shots (Option D)

Question 3Grade 8 • EE
A recipe calls for 2.5 cups of flour for the main dough, plus an additional 0.5 cups for every loaf of bread made. If a baker used a total of 5 cups of flour, how many loaves of bread did they make?
A. 5 loaves
B. 3 loaves
C. 6 loaves
D. 2.5 loaves
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let L be the number of loaves of bread. Total flour = initial flour + flour per loaf * number of loaves 5 = 2.5 + 0.5L
Define the variable for the number of loaves and set up the equation based on the total flour used.
2
5 = 2.5 + 0.5L 5 - 2.5 = 0.5L 2.5 = 0.5L
Subtract the initial amount of flour from the total flour to find the flour used for the loaves.
3
2.5 = 0.5L L = 2.5 / 0.5 L = 5
Divide the flour used for loaves by the flour per loaf to find the number of loaves.

Answer: 5 loaves (Option A)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE
A student has a goal to read a total of 150 pages this week. They read 42 pages on Monday. If they plan to read an equal number of pages each of the remaining 7 days, how many pages must they read per day?
A. 16 pages
B. 14 pages
C. 15.43 pages (approximately)
D. 13 pages
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let p be the number of pages read per day for the remaining 7 days. Total pages = pages on Monday + pages remaining days 150 = 42 + 7p
Define the variable for pages per day and set up the equation based on the total pages and pages read so far.
2
150 = 42 + 7p 150 - 42 = 7p 108 = 7p
Subtract the pages read on Monday from the total pages to find the pages remaining to be read.
3
108 = 7p p = 108 / 7 p ≈ 15.43
Divide the remaining pages by the number of remaining days to find the pages per day.

Answer: 15.43 pages (approximately) (Option C)

Question 6Grade 8 • EE
A scientist is conducting an experiment where the temperature starts at -15 degrees Celsius and increases by 3 degrees Celsius every hour. If the final temperature recorded was 3 degrees Celsius, how many hours did the experiment run?
A. 4 hours
B. 6 hours
C. 7 hours
D. 5 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let h be the number of hours the experiment ran. Final temperature = initial temperature + rate of change * hours 3 = -15 + 3h
Define the variable for hours and set up the equation based on the temperature changes.
2
3 = -15 + 3h 3 + 15 = 3h 18 = 3h
Add 15 to both sides to isolate the term with the variable.
3
18 = 3h h = 18 / 3 h = 6
Divide by 3 to find the number of hours.

Answer: 6 hours (Option B)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A carpenter charges a flat fee of $55 for a service call, plus $25 per hour for labor. If a customer's total bill was $130, how many hours did the carpenter work?
A. 2 hours
B. 3 hours
C. 3.5 hours
D. 2.5 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let h be the number of hours the carpenter worked. Total bill = flat fee + hourly rate * hours 130 = 55 + 25h
Define the variable for hours and set up the equation based on the total bill.
2
130 = 55 + 25h 130 - 55 = 25h 75 = 25h
Subtract the flat fee from the total bill to find the labor cost.
3
75 = 25h h = 75 / 25 h = 3
Divide the labor cost by the hourly rate to find the number of hours worked.

Answer: 3 hours (Option B)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A certain country's population was 32 million in 2010. It has been growing at an average rate of 0.8 million people per year. In what year will the population reach 40 million?
A. 2019
B. 2020
C. 2021
D. 2018
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let y be the number of years after 2010. Final population = initial population + growth rate * years 40 = 32 + 0.8y
Define the variable for years and set up the equation based on population growth.
2
40 = 32 + 0.8y 40 - 32 = 0.8y 8 = 0.8y
Subtract the initial population from the final population to find the total population increase.
3
8 = 0.8y y = 8 / 0.8 y = 10
Divide the total population increase by the annual growth rate to find the number of years.
4
Since y = 10 years after 2010, the year will be 2010 + 10 = 2020.
Add the number of years to the starting year to find the target year.

Answer: 2020 (Option B)
