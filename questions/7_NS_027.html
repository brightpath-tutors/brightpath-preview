Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A cashier has 3 1/2 rolls of quarters. If each customer gets 1/4 of a roll, how many customers can the cashier serve?
A. 7/8 customers
B. 14 customers
C. 3 3/4 customers
D. 7 customers
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
3 1/2 = (3 × 2 + 1) / 2 = 7/2
To divide mixed numbers, first convert them into improper fractions. For 3 1/2, multiply the whole number (3) by the denominator (2) and add the numerator (1), keeping the same denominator.
2
7/2 ÷ 1/4
Now the problem is 7/2 divided by 1/4.
3
7/2 × 4/1 = (7 × 4) / (2 × 1) = 28/2
To divide fractions, you multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal (inverted form) of the second fraction. The reciprocal of 1/4 is 4/1.
4
28/2 = 14
Divide 28 by 2 to get the final answer.

Answer: 14 customers (Option B)

Question 4Grade 7 • NS
A car travels 75 3/5 miles in 2 1/2 hours. What is the car's average speed in miles per hour, in simplest form?
A. 37.5 miles per hour
B. 189 miles per hour
C. 30 6/25 miles per hour
D. 10 2/5 miles per hour
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
75 3/5 = (75 × 5 + 3) / 5 = 378/5 2 1/2 = (2 × 2 + 1) / 2 = 5/2
Convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions. For 75 3/5, multiply 75 by 5 and add 3. For 2 1/2, multiply 2 by 2 and add 1.
2
378/5 ÷ 5/2
The problem is now 378/5 divided by 5/2.
3
378/5 × 2/5 = (378 × 2) / (5 × 5) = 756/25
Multiply the first fraction (378/5) by the reciprocal of the second fraction (5/2), which is 2/5.
4
756 ÷ 25 = 30 with a remainder of 6. So, 30 6/25.
Divide the numerator by the denominator. The quotient is the whole number, and the remainder over the original denominator forms the fraction part. The fraction 6/25 is already in simplest form.

Answer: 30 6/25 miles per hour (Option C)

Question 5Grade 7 • NS
A teacher has 10 1/8 boxes of pencils. If she distributes 1 1/2 boxes to each classroom, how many classrooms receive pencils, in simplest form?
A. 10 classrooms
B. 6 3/4 classrooms
C. 1 1/2 classrooms
D. 15 3/16 classrooms
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
10 1/8 = (10 × 8 + 1) / 8 = 81/8 1 1/2 = (1 × 2 + 1) / 2 = 3/2
Convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions. For 10 1/8, multiply 10 by 8 and add 1. For 1 1/2, multiply 1 by 2 and add 1.
2
81/8 ÷ 3/2
The problem is now 81/8 divided by 3/2.
3
81/8 × 2/3 = (81 × 2) / (8 × 3) = 162/24
Multiply the first fraction (81/8) by the reciprocal of the second fraction (3/2), which is 2/3.
4
162/24. Divide both numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor, 6: 162 ÷ 6 = 27, 24 ÷ 6 = 4. So, 27/4. Convert 27/4 to a mixed number: 27 ÷ 4 = 6 with a remainder of 3. So, 6 3/4.
Simplify the improper fraction 162/24 by dividing the numerator and denominator by 6. Then, convert the simplified improper fraction 27/4 to a mixed number.

Answer: 6 3/4 classrooms (Option B)
