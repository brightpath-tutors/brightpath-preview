Question 1Grade 8 • EE
The cost of a small coffee is $2, and the cost of a large coffee is $4. On Monday, a coffee shop sold a total of 10 coffees for a total of $28. How many small coffees and large coffees were sold?
A. (4 small coffees, 6 large coffees)
B. (6 small coffees, 4 large coffees)
C. (7 small coffees, 3 large coffees)
D. (5 small coffees, 5 large coffees)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let x be the number of small coffees and y be the number of large coffees. Equation 1 (total number of coffees): x + y = 10 Equation 2 (total revenue): 2x + 4y = 28
Assign variables to the unknown quantities and translate the word problem into two linear equations based on the given information.
2
From x + y = 10, subtract x from both sides: y = -x + 10 From 2x + 4y = 28, subtract 2x from both sides: 4y = -2x + 28. Then divide by 4: y = (-2/4)x + (28/4) = -0.5x + 7
Rearrange both equations to isolate y, making them easier to graph. This involves basic algebraic manipulation.
3
Graph y = -x + 10 (y-intercept at (0,10), slope -1) Graph y = -0.5x + 7 (y-intercept at (0,7), slope -0.5)
Plot the y-intercepts and use the slopes to find additional points for each line. Draw the lines representing each equation on the same coordinate plane.
4
The two lines intersect at the point (6, 4).
Visually identify the coordinates of the point where the two graphed lines cross. This point represents the solution to the system.
5
x = 6, y = 4. This means 6 small coffees and 4 large coffees were sold.
Relate the x and y values of the intersection point back to the variables defined in Step 1 to answer the original question.

Answer: (6 small coffees, 4 large coffees) (Option B)

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution

Let x be the time (in seconds) Athlete 1 ran and y be the time (in seconds) Athlete 2 ran. Equation 1 (total distance): 7x + 3y = 100 Equation 2 (total time): x + y = 20
Assign variables to the unknown times for each athlete. Use the formula distance = speed × time to set up the total distance equation and simply sum the times for the total time equation.
2
From 7x + 3y = 100, subtract 7x: 3y = -7x + 100. Divide by 3: y = (-7/3)x + (100/3). From x + y = 20, subtract x: y = -x + 20.
Isolate y in both equations to get them into a graphable format.
3
Graph y = (-7/3)x + 100/3 (y-intercept approx (0, 33.3), slope -7/3) Graph y = -x + 20 (y-intercept at (0,20), slope -1)
Plot the y-intercepts and use the slopes to draw the lines for each equation on the same coordinate plane.
4
The two lines intersect at the point (10, 10).
Locate the point where the two lines cross on the graph.
5
x = 10, y = 10. This means Athlete 1 ran for 10 seconds and Athlete 2 ran for 10 seconds.
Relate the x and y values back to the original context to answer the question.

From x + y = 10, subtract x: y = -x + 10. From 0.2x + 0.5y = 3.2, subtract 0.2x: 0.5y = -0.2x + 3.2. Divide by 0.5: y = (-0.2/0.5)x + (3.2/0.5) = -0.4x + 6.4.

Graph y = -x + 10 (y-intercept at (0,10), slope -1) Graph y = -0.4x + 6.4 (y-intercept at (0,6.4), slope -0.4)
