Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A grocery store sells 24 oranges for $6.00. What is the ratio of oranges to cost, in simplest form, if the price is scaled down to represent the cost per orange?
A. 1 orange per $4.00
B. 4 oranges per $1.00
C. 12 oranges per $3.00
D. 6 oranges per $1.00
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial ratio: 24 oranges : $6.00
The problem provides the quantity of oranges and the total cost, forming the initial ratio.
2
To find the ratio in simplest form (or per $1.00), we divide both parts of the ratio by the dollar amount, which is 6.
To scale down the ratio to a unit cost of $1.00, we need to divide both parts of the ratio by the current dollar amount.
3
Oranges: 24 ÷ 6 = 4 Cost: $6.00 ÷ 6 = $1.00
Perform the division on both quantities to find the equivalent ratio.
4
Simplified ratio: 4 oranges : $1.00
The resulting numbers form the ratio in its simplest form, representing 4 oranges per $1.00.

Answer: 4 oranges per $1.00 (Option B)
