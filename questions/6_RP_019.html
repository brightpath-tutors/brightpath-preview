Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A small toy car costs $12 and is a similar figure to a larger toy car. The small car has a length of 5 cm, and the large car has a length of 15 cm. If the width of the small car is 3 cm, what is the width of the large car?
A. 13 cm
B. 1 cm
C. 6 cm
D. 9 cm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Scale factor = Large Length / Small Length = 15 cm / 5 cm = 3
To find the scale factor, divide the corresponding side length of the larger figure by the corresponding side length of the smaller figure.
2
Large Width = Small Width × Scale Factor = 3 cm × 3 = 9 cm
Multiply the width of the small car by the scale factor to find the width of the large car.

Answer: 9 cm (Option D)

Question 2Grade 6 • RP
A basketball court has a length of 28 meters and a width of 15 meters. A smaller replica court for a video game display is built to be similar. If the replica's length is 7 meters, what is its width?
A. 13.07 meters
B. 3.75 meters
C. 3 meters
D. 4 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'w' be the width of the replica court. The ratio of corresponding sides must be equal: Large Length / Small Length = Large Width / Small Width 28 / 7 = 15 / w
Form a proportion by equating the ratio of the lengths to the ratio of the widths, ensuring corresponding sides are in the same order.
2
28w = 7 × 15 28w = 105 w = 105 / 28 w = 3.75 meters
Cross-multiply and divide to find the unknown width 'w'.

Answer: 3.75 meters (Option B)

B. 0.5 cups
C. 2 cups
D. 2.5 cups
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Scale factor = New Sugar / Original Sugar = 1 cup / 2 cups = 0.5
Divide the new amount of sugar by the original amount of sugar to find the scale factor for the recipe.
2
New Flour = Original Flour × Scale Factor = 3 cups × 0.5 = 1.5 cups
Multiply the original amount of flour by the scale factor to find the new amount of flour needed for the smaller batch.

Question 6Grade 6 • RP
A scientist creates a scale model of a virus for a presentation. The actual virus has a length of 200 nanometers and a width of 120 nanometers. If the model is 10 cm long and is similar to the actual virus, what is the width of the model in centimeters?
A. 20 cm
B. 10 cm
C. 4 cm
D. 6 cm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Scale factor (model to actual) = Model Length / Actual Length = 10 cm / 200 nm = 0.05 cm/nm
Divide the length of the model by the actual length of the virus to find the scale factor, ensuring consistent units (or noting the unit conversion implicitly).
2
Model Width = Actual Width × Scale Factor = 120 nm × (10 cm / 200 nm) = 120 / 20 cm = 6 cm
Multiply the actual width of the virus by the scale factor to find the width of the model. The nanometer units cancel out, leaving centimeters.

Answer: 6 cm (Option D)

Question 7Grade 6 • RP

A. 8 feet
B. 16 feet
C. 12.5 feet
D. 10 feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'w' be the actual width of the room. Blueprint Length / Blueprint Width = Actual Length / Actual Width 8 inches / 5 inches = 20 feet / w feet
The ratio of length to width must be the same for both the blueprint and the actual room. Set up the proportion.
2
8w = 5 × 20 8w = 100 w = 100 / 8 w = 12.5 feet

Question 8Grade 6 • RP
A digital image has dimensions of 1920 pixels by 1080 pixels. If you want to resize it to a similar image with a height of 720 pixels, what will be the new width in simplest form as a ratio to the original width?
A. 1/2
B. 2/3
C. 3/2
D. 16/9
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Scale factor = New Height / Original Height = 720 pixels / 1080 pixels = 2/3
Divide the new height by the original height to find the scale factor for the resizing. Simplify the fraction.
2
New Width = Original Width × Scale Factor = 1920 pixels × (2/3) = 1280 pixels
Multiply the original width by the scale factor to find the new width.
3
Ratio = New Width / Original Width = 1280 pixels / 1920 pixels = 128/192 = 2/3
Form a ratio of the new width to the original width and simplify it by dividing both numbers by their greatest common divisor (which is 64).

Answer: 2/3 (Option B)

Question 9Grade 6 • RP
An artist created a rectangular painting with dimensions 60 cm by 90 cm. For a gallery exhibition, they need to create a smaller, similar version of the painting. If the smaller painting has an area of 600 cm², what are the dimensions of the smaller painting?
A. 10 cm by 15 cm
B. 20 cm by 45 cm
C. 60 cm by 90 cm
D. 20 cm by 30 cm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Original Area = Length × Width = 90 cm × 60 cm = 5400 cm²
Multiply the length and width of the original painting to find its area.
2
Ratio of Areas = Smaller Area / Original Area = 600 cm² / 5400 cm² = 1/9
Divide the area of the smaller painting by the area of the original painting. The ratio of the areas of similar figures is equal to the square of the scale factor.
3
Scale Factor = √(Ratio of Areas) = √(1/9) = 1/3
Take the square root of the ratio of the areas to find the linear scale factor between the side lengths.
4
Smaller Width = Original Width × Scale Factor = 60 cm × (1/3) = 20 cm Smaller Length = Original Length × Scale Factor = 90 cm × (1/3) = 30 cm
Multiply each dimension of the original painting by the scale factor to find the corresponding dimensions of the smaller painting.

Answer: 20 cm by 30 cm (Option D)

Question 10Grade 6 • RP
A doctor uses X-rays to compare the bone structure of a patient's hand to a standard model. The standard model shows a metacarpal bone with a length of 8 cm and a phalange bone with a length of 4 cm. In the patient's X-ray, the same metacarpal bone is 10 cm long. Assuming the patient's hand structure is similar to the standard model, what is the expected length of the patient's corresponding phalange bone?
A. 2 cm
B. 6 cm
C. 4 cm
D. 5 cm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Scale factor = Patient's Metacarpal Length / Standard Metacarpal Length = 10 cm / 8 cm = 1.25
Divide the length of the patient's metacarpal bone by the corresponding length in the standard model to find the scale factor.
2
Patient's Phalange Length = Standard Phalange Length × Scale Factor = 4 cm × 1.25 = 5 cm
Multiply the length of the standard phalange bone by the scale factor to find the expected length of the patient's corresponding phalange bone.

Answer: 5 cm (Option D)
