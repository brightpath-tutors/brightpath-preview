Question 1Grade 7 • NS
The temperature in Anchorage, Alaska was 5 degrees Celsius in the morning. By evening, the temperature had dropped to -3 degrees Celsius. What is the difference in temperature?
A. 8 degrees Celsius
B. -2 degrees Celsius
C. 2 degrees Celsius
D. -8 degrees Celsius
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial temperature = 5°C Final temperature = -3°C
We need to find the difference between these two values.
2
Difference = Initial temperature - Final temperature Difference = 5 - (-3)
Subtract the final temperature from the initial temperature to find the change.
3
Subtracting a negative number is the same as adding its positive counterpart. 5 - (-3) = 5 + 3
The rule 'minus a minus becomes a plus' is applied here.
4
5 + 3 = 8
Adding the numbers gives the total temperature difference.
5
The difference in temperature is 8 degrees Celsius.
The difference is positive because the temperature dropped from a higher value to a lower value, and we are looking for the magnitude of the change.

Answer: 8 degrees Celsius (Option A)

Question 4Grade 7 • NS
A submarine was at a depth of -50 feet. It then rose 30 feet. What is the submarine's new depth?
A. 80 feet
B. 20 feet
C. -80 feet
D. -20 feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial depth = -50 feet Rise = 30 feet (represented as +30)
Depth below sea level is negative, and rising is positive.
2
New depth = Initial depth + Rise New depth = -50 + 30
We are combining the initial depth with the movement.
3
-50 + 30 = -20
Subtract the absolute values and keep the sign of the number with the larger absolute value: |50| - |30| = 20. Since 50 is larger and negative, the result is negative.
4
The submarine's new depth is -20 feet.
The submarine is 20 feet below sea level.

Answer: -20 feet (Option D)
