Question 1Grade 8 • EE
At a concession stand, 2 bags of popcorn and 3 sodas cost $13. Another customer bought 2 bags of popcorn and 5 sodas for $19. How much does one soda cost?
A. $4
B. $3
C. $2
D. $1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'p' be the cost of one bag of popcorn and 's' be the cost of one soda. Equation 1: 2p + 3s = 13 Equation 2: 2p + 5s = 19
We translate the given information into two linear equations. The number of items multiplied by their cost equals the total cost for each transaction.
2
(2p + 5s) - (2p + 3s) = 19 - 13 2s = 6
Since the coefficient for 'p' (2) is the same in both equations, we can subtract the first equation from the second to eliminate 'p' and solve for 's'.
3
2s = 6 s = 6 / 2 s = 3
Divide both sides by 2 to find the cost of one soda.

Answer: $3 (Option B)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
A sports store sells soccer balls and basketballs. This week, 4 soccer balls and 2 basketballs were sold for a total of $140. Last week, 4 soccer balls and 5 basketballs were sold for $230. How much does one basketball cost?
A. $50
B. $20
C. $10
D. $30
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 's' be the cost of one soccer ball and 'b' be the cost of one basketball. Equation 1: 4s + 2b = 140 Equation 2: 4s + 5b = 230
We translate the given information into two linear equations representing the total sales for each week.
2
(4s + 5b) - (4s + 2b) = 230 - 140 3b = 90
The coefficient for 's' (4) is the same in both equations. Subtract the first equation from the second to eliminate 's' and solve for 'b'.
3
3b = 90 b = 90 / 3 b = 30
Divide both sides by 3 to find the cost of one basketball.

Answer: $30 (Option D)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A chef is preparing two types of pastries. One recipe for croissants uses 2 eggs (E) and 3 cups of sugar (S), yielding 19 pastries. A recipe for muffins uses 1 egg and 4 cups of sugar, yielding 22 pastries. If an egg and a cup of sugar contribute a consistent amount to the number of pastries, how many pastries does one egg (E) contribute?
A. 4 pastries
B. 5 pastries
C. 3 pastries
D. 2 pastries
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'E' be the contribution of one egg and 'S' be the contribution of one cup of sugar. Equation 1: 2E + 3S = 19 Equation 2: E + 4S = 22
We create two equations based on the pastry recipes, where E and S are variables representing their contributions.
2
Multiply Equation 2 by 2: 2 (E + 4S) = 2 22 2E + 8S = 44 (New Equation 2)
To eliminate 'E', we need its coefficients to be the same. Multiplying the second equation by 2 makes the 'E' coefficient 2, matching the first equation.
3
(2E + 8S) - (2E + 3S) = 44 - 19 5S = 25 S = 25 / 5 S = 5
Subtract the first equation from the new second equation to eliminate 'E'. Then, solve for 'S'.
4
Substitute S = 5 into Equation 1: 2E + 3(5) = 19 2E + 15 = 19 2E = 19 - 15 2E = 4 E = 4 / 2 E = 2
Substitute the value of S back into one of the original equations to find E. We chose Equation 1. Then, isolate E.

Answer: 2 pastries (Option D)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE
A delivery service charges a base fee (B) and a per-mile rate (M). Delivering a package 3 miles away costs $18, with a base fee that is twice the standard. Delivering a package 4 miles away costs $19, with the standard base fee. What is the per-mile rate (M)?
A. $4
B. $6
C. $3
D. $5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'B' be the standard base fee and 'M' be the per-mile rate. Equation 1: 2B + 3M = 18 Equation 2: B + 4M = 19
We formulate two equations based on the delivery costs. The first delivery has a base fee of 2B, while the second has B.
2
Multiply Equation 2 by 2: 2 (B + 4M) = 2 19 2B + 8M = 38 (New Equation 2)
To eliminate 'B', we multiply the second equation by 2 so its coefficient matches that of 'B' in the first equation.
3
(2B + 8M) - (2B + 3M) = 38 - 18 5M = 20 M = 20 / 5 M = 4
Subtract the first equation from the new second equation to eliminate 'B'. Then, solve for 'M'.
4
Substitute M = 4 into Equation 2: B + 4(4) = 19 B + 16 = 19 B = 19 - 16 B = 3
Substitute the value of M back into one of the original equations to find B. We chose Equation 2.

Answer: $4 (Option A)

Question 6Grade 8 • EE

B. $7
C. $6
D. $8
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'X' be the cost per liter of solution X and 'Y' be the cost per liter of solution Y. Equation 1: 2X + 3Y = 34 Equation 2: X + 5Y = 45
We translate the two mixture compositions and their total costs into linear equations.
2
Multiply Equation 2 by 2: 2 (X + 5Y) = 2 45 2X + 10Y = 90 (New Equation 2)
To eliminate 'X', we multiply the second equation by 2 to make its coefficient match that of 'X' in the first equation.
3
(2X + 10Y) - (2X + 3Y) = 90 - 34 7Y = 56 Y = 56 / 7 Y = 8
Subtract the first equation from the new second equation to eliminate 'X'. Then, solve for 'Y'.
4
Substitute Y = 8 into Equation 2: X + 5(8) = 45 X + 40 = 45 X = 45 - 40 X = 5
Substitute the value of Y back into one of the original equations (Equation 2 is simpler) to find X. Then, isolate X.

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A construction worker bought bags of cement (C) and bags of sand (S). The first order was 3 bags of cement and 2 bags of sand, costing $56. The second order was 6 bags of cement and 5 bags of sand, costing $122. What is the cost of one bag of cement (C)?
A. $10
B. $11
C. $12
D. $13
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'C' be the cost of one bag of cement and 'S' be the cost of one bag of sand. Equation 1: 3C + 2S = 56 Equation 2: 6C + 5S = 122
We write two equations to represent the two orders, where C and S are the costs of cement and sand bags, respectively.
2
Multiply Equation 1 by 2: 2 (3C + 2S) = 2 56 6C + 4S = 112 (New Equation 1)
To eliminate 'C', we multiply the first equation by 2 so its coefficient matches that of 'C' in the second equation.
3
(6C + 5S) - (6C + 4S) = 122 - 112 S = 10
Subtract the new first equation from the second original equation to eliminate 'C'. Then, solve for 'S'.
4
Substitute S = 10 into Equation 1: 3C + 2(10) = 56 3C + 20 = 56 3C = 56 - 20 3C = 36 C = 36 / 3 C = 12
Substitute the value of S back into one of the original equations (Equation 1 is simpler) to find C. Then, isolate C.

Answer: $12 (Option C)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
An employee works two types of shifts: morning (M) and evening (E). Last week, they worked 3 morning shifts and 4 evening shifts for a total of 31 hours. This week, they worked 2 morning shifts and 3 evening shifts for a total of 23 hours. How long is one evening shift (E)?
A. 8 hours
B. 7 hours
C. 5 hours
D. 6 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'M' be the length of a morning shift and 'E' be the length of an evening shift. Equation 1: 3M + 4E = 31 Equation 2: 2M + 3E = 23
We create two equations representing the total hours worked in each of the two weeks.
2
Multiply Equation 1 by 2: 2 (3M + 4E) = 2 31 => 6M + 8E = 62 (New Equation 1) Multiply Equation 2 by 3: 3 (2M + 3E) = 3 23 => 6M + 9E = 69 (New Equation 2)
To eliminate 'M', we find the least common multiple of its coefficients (3 and 2), which is 6. We multiply each equation by the factor that makes the 'M' coefficient 6.
3
(6M + 9E) - (6M + 8E) = 69 - 62 E = 7
Subtract New Equation 1 from New Equation 2 to eliminate 'M'. This directly gives the value of 'E'.
4
Substitute E = 7 into Equation 1: 3M + 4(7) = 31 3M + 28 = 31 3M = 31 - 28 3M = 3 M = 1
Substitute the found value of E back into one of the original equations (Equation 1 is chosen here) to find M. This step helps verify the solution.

Answer: 7 hours (Option B)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
A wildlife reserve tracks two species: deer (D) and rabbits (R). Last year, there were 5 groups of deer and 6 groups of rabbits, totaling 118 animals. This year, there are 2 groups of deer and 3 groups of rabbits, totaling 52 animals. Assuming each "group" represents a consistent number of animals, how many animals are in one group of deer (D)?
A. 14 animals
B. 12 animals
C. 8 animals
D. 16 animals
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'D' be the number of animals in a group of deer and 'R' be the number of animals in a group of rabbits. Equation 1: 5D + 6R = 118 Equation 2: 2D + 3R = 52
We translate the total animal counts from the two different years into linear equations.
2
Multiply Equation 2 by 2: 2 (2D + 3R) = 2 52 4D + 6R = 104 (New Equation 2)
To eliminate 'R', we multiply the second equation by 2 so its coefficient matches that of 'R' in the first equation.
3
(5D + 6R) - (4D + 6R) = 118 - 104 D = 14
Subtract New Equation 2 from Equation 1 to eliminate 'R'. This directly gives the value of 'D'.
4
Substitute D = 14 into Equation 2: 2(14) + 3R = 52 28 + 3R = 52 3R = 52 - 28 3R = 24 R = 8
Substitute the found value of D back into one of the original equations (Equation 2 is simpler) to find R. This step helps verify the solution.

Answer: 14 animals (Option A)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A tech company stores data on two types of servers, standard (S) and high-capacity (H). Server farm A has 4 standard servers and 5 high-capacity servers, storing a total of 130 terabytes (TB). Server farm B has 3 standard servers and 2 high-capacity servers, storing a total of 66 TB. What is the storage capacity of one standard server (S) in terabytes?
A. 10 TB
B. 18 TB
C. 8 TB
D. 12 TB
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'S' be the capacity of a standard server and 'H' be the capacity of a high-capacity server. Equation 1: 4S + 5H = 130 Equation 2: 3S + 2H = 66
We translate the server configurations and their total storage capacities into two linear equations.
2
To eliminate 'H', we find the least common multiple of its coefficients (5 and 2), which is 10. Multiply Equation 1 by 2: 2 (4S + 5H) = 2 130 => 8S + 10H = 260 (New Equation 1) Multiply Equation 2 by 5: 5 (3S + 2H) = 5 66 => 15S + 10H = 330 (New Equation 2)
We multiply the first equation by 2 and the second equation by 5 to make the 'H' coefficients 10 in both equations, allowing for elimination.
3
(15S + 10H) - (8S + 10H) = 330 - 260 7S = 70 S = 70 / 7 S = 10
Subtract New Equation 1 from New Equation 2 to eliminate 'H'. Then, solve for 'S'.
4
Substitute S = 10 into Equation 2: 3(10) + 2H = 66 30 + 2H = 66 2H = 66 - 30 2H = 36 H = 18
Substitute the found value of S back into one of the original equations (Equation 2 is simpler) to find H. This step verifies the solution.

Answer: 10 TB (Option A)
