Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Sarah bought a shirt for $15 and a book for $8. Which expression uses the Commutative Property of Addition to show the total cost in a different way?
A. (15 + 8) + 0
B. 15 * 8
C. 8 + 15
D. 15 + (8 + 0)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial expression: 15 + 8
The cost of the shirt and the book are added together to find the total.
2
Commutative Property of Addition: a + b = b + a
This property states that the order in which numbers are added does not change their sum.
3
15 + 8 = 8 + 15
By changing the order of the addends (15 and 8), we create an equivalent expression using the Commutative Property.

Answer: 8 + 15 (Option C)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE

A. 18 + 12 + 10
B. 12 + (18 + 10)
C. 12 + 18 + 10 + 0
D. 12 18 10
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial expression: (12 + 18) + 10
The points from each quarter are added together. The parentheses show one possible grouping.
2
Associative Property of Addition: (a + b) + c = a + (b + c)
This property states that the way numbers are grouped in an addition problem does not change their sum.
3
(12 + 18) + 10 = 12 + (18 + 10)
By changing the grouping of the addends (12, 18, and 10), we create an equivalent expression using the Associative Property.

Answer: 12 + (18 + 10) (Option B)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A car travels at a speed of 60 miles per hour for 2 hours, then at 50 miles per hour for another 2 hours. Which expression uses the Distributive Property to find the total distance traveled?
A. (60 + 2) * (50 + 2)
B. 2 * (60 + 50)
C. 120 + 100
D. 60 + 50 + 2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Distance for first part: 60 2 Distance for second part: 50 2 Total distance: (60 2) + (50 2)
Distance is calculated as speed multiplied by time. The total distance is the sum of distances from both parts of the trip.
2
Distributive Property: a(b + c) = ab + ac or ab + ac = a(b + c)
This property allows us to factor out a common multiplier from a sum of products.
3
(60 2) + (50 2) = 2 * (60 + 50)
The common multiplier is 2 (the time in hours). We factor it out from both terms.

Answer: 2 * (60 + 50) (Option B)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE
A student has 25 pencils in their pencil case. They don't add or remove any pencils. Which property describes the number of pencils remaining?
A. 25 * 1 = 25
B. 25 + 0 = 25
C. 25 - 0 = 25
D. 0 + 25 = 25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial pencils: 25 Change: 0 pencils added or removed
The number of pencils remains unchanged.
2
Identity Property of Addition: a + 0 = a
This property states that adding zero to any number results in that same number.
3
25 + 0 = 25
The number of pencils (25) remains the same when 0 are added, which is a direct application of the Identity Property of Addition.

Answer: 25 + 0 = 25 (Option B)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A software update takes 2 minutes per computer. In Department A, 15 computers receive the update. In Department B, 20 computers receive the update. In Department C, 10 computers receive the update. Which expression uses the Distributive Property to find the total time spent updating?
A. (2 + 15) (2 + 20) (2 + 10)
B. 30 + 40 + 20
C. 2 * (15 + 20 + 10)
D. 2 + 15 + 20 + 10
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Time for Dept A: 2 15 Time for Dept B: 2 20 Time for Dept C: 2 10 Total time: (2 15) + (2 20) + (2 10)
The time per update is multiplied by the number of computers in each department, then all department times are added.
2
Distributive Property: a(b + c + d) = ab + ac + ad or ab + ac + ad = a(b + c + d)
This property allows us to factor out a common multiplier from a sum of products, even with multiple terms.
3
(2 15) + (2 20) + (2 10) = 2 (15 + 20 + 10)
The common multiplier is 2 (minutes per computer). We factor it out from all three terms.

Answer: 2 * (15 + 20 + 10) (Option C)
