Question 7Grade 6 • EE
A scientist is observing bacterial growth. She starts with a colony that doubles every 20 minutes. If the current colony size is 3,200 bacteria and she started with an unknown number of bacteria and observed for 1 hour, what was the initial number of bacteria?
A. 2800 bacteria
B. 9600 bacteria
C. 400 bacteria
D. 1600 bacteria
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Observation time = 1 hour = 60 minutes
Convert the observation time from hours to minutes.
2
Doubling periods = Total time / Doubling time = 60 minutes / 20 minutes = 3 doubling periods
Calculate how many times the colony doubled during the observation period.
3
Final colony size = 3200 bacteria
Identify the final size of the bacterial colony.
4
Initial size = Final size / (2 2 2) = 3200 / 2^3
Since the colony doubled 3 times, divide the final size by 2 for each doubling period (2^3).
5
Initial size = 3200 / 8 = 400
Perform the division to find the initial number of bacteria.

Answer: 400 bacteria (Option C)

Question 8Grade 6 • EE
A city's population increases by a factor of 1.02 each year. If the current population is 153,000 and it was 150,000 some years ago, how many years did it take to reach the current population, assuming the growth was uniform over those years?
A. 3 years
B. 1.02 years
C. 1 year
D. 2 years
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Current population = 153000, Initial population = 150000
Identify the current and initial population values.
2
Population increase factor = Current population / Initial population = 153000 / 150000
Calculate the total factor by which the population increased.
3
Population increase factor = 1.02
Perform the division.
4
Annual growth factor = 1.02
Identify the annual growth factor given in the problem.
5
Since Total Factor = (Annual Growth Factor)^Number of Years, and 1.02 = (1.02)^Number of Years
Set up an equation where the total growth factor is the annual growth factor raised to the power of the number of years. In this case, the powers match, so the exponent is 1.
6
Number of Years = 1
Solve for the number of years. Since 1.02 equals 1.02 raised to the power of 1, the number of years is 1.

Answer: 1 year (Option C)
