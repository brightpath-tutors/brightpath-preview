Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A square rug has an area of 144 square feet. What is the length of one side of the rug?
A. 72 feet
B. 14.4 feet
C. 12 feet
D. 144 feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Area (A) = side² (s²)
The area of a square is calculated by squaring the length of one of its sides.
2
s² = 144
Substitute the given area, 144 square feet, into the area formula.
3
s = √144
To find 's', perform the inverse operation of squaring, which is taking the square root.
4
√144 = 12
The square root of 144 is 12 because 12 * 12 = 144. Since length must be positive, we take the positive root.
5
s = 12 feet
The length of one side of the rug is 12 feet.

Answer: 12 feet (Option C)

D. 10√20 miles
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Area (A) = side² (s²)
The track is square, so its area is the square of its side length.
2
s² = 200

3
s = √200
Take the square root of 200. Since length must be positive, we take the positive root.
4
200 = 100 * 2 s = √(100 * 2)
The largest perfect square factor of 200 is 100.
5
s = √100 * √2

s = 10√2 miles
The length of one side of the track is 10√2 miles.
