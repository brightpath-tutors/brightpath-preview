Question 1Grade 8 • NS
A customer's bank account has a balance of -$25. Another customer's account has a balance of -$18. Which balance is greater?
A. $18
B. $0
C. -$18
D. -$25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
A balance of -$25 means the customer owes $25. A balance of -$18 means the customer owes $18.
In debt, a smaller absolute value indicates a lesser debt, which is a greater balance.
2
-$18 is to the right of -$25 on a number line.
On a number line, numbers increase as you move to the right. Therefore, -18 is greater than -25.

Answer: -$18 (Option C)

Question 4Grade 8 • NS
A submarine is at an elevation of -200 feet relative to sea level. A diver is at an elevation of -75 feet. Which object is closer to the surface (sea level)?
A. Sea level (0 feet)
B. The diver (-75 feet)
C. The submarine (-200 feet)
D. They are both equally close.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Sea level is represented by 0 feet. Negative elevations mean below sea level.
Closer to the surface means having an elevation closer to 0.
2
The absolute value represents the distance from zero. | -200 | = 200. | -75 | = 75.
A smaller absolute value means the object is closer to the surface.
3
75 < 200.
Since 75 is less than 200, the diver at -75 feet is closer to the surface than the submarine at -200 feet.

Answer: The diver (-75 feet) (Option B)

E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students

2

Question 6Grade 8 • NS
The temperature on Monday was -8°C. On Tuesday, it was -2°C. On Wednesday, it was -12°C. Order the temperatures from warmest to coldest.
A. -2°C, -12°C, -8°C
B. -8°C, -2°C, -12°C
C. -12°C, -8°C, -2°C
D. -2°C, -8°C, -12°C
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Warmer temperatures are represented by larger numbers. Colder temperatures are represented by smaller numbers (more negative).
To order from warmest to coldest, we need to arrange the numbers from largest to smallest.
2
On a number line: -12 is furthest left, then -8, then -2 is furthest right.
Therefore, -2 is the warmest, followed by -8, and -12 is the coldest.
3
Warmest to coldest: -2°C, -8°C, -12°C
This sequence goes from the largest (warmest) number to the smallest (coldest) number.

Answer: -2°C, -8°C, -12°C (Option D)

Question 8Grade 8 • NS
The local time in City A is 2 hours behind GMT (Greenwich Mean Time), represented as -2. The local time in City B is 5 hours behind GMT, represented as -5. City C is 1 hour ahead of GMT, represented as +1. Order the cities from earliest time to latest time.
A. City B (-5), City A (-2), City C (+1)
B. City B (-5), City C (+1), City A (-2)
C. City A (-2), City B (-5), City C (+1)
D. City C (+1), City A (-2), City B (-5)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
City A: -2; City B: -5; City C: +1
These integers represent the offset from a common reference point (GMT).
2
On a time scale or number line, earlier times are represented by smaller numbers (further to the left), and later times by larger numbers (further to the right).
We need to order the integers from smallest to largest.
3
Comparing -2, -5, and +1: -5 is the smallest, followed by -2, and +1 is the largest.
Therefore, City B has the earliest time, then City A, and City C has the latest time.
4
City B (-5), City A (-2), City C (+1)
This is the final order from earliest to latest.

Answer: City B (-5), City A (-2), City C (+1) (Option A)

Question 9Grade 8 • NS
Over the last decade, the population of Town X changed by -1500 people. Town Y changed by -900 people. Town Z changed by +500 people. Which town experienced the smallest population change (closest to zero)?
A. Town Z (+500)
B. Town Y (-900)
C. Town X (-1500)
D. All changes are equally small.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
This refers to the magnitude of the change, meaning the absolute value of the number. We are looking for the absolute value closest to zero.
A change of +500 or -500 both represent a magnitude of 500. We want the smallest magnitude.
2
Town X: | -1500 | = 1500 Town Y: | -900 | = 900 Town Z: | +500 | = 500
These absolute values represent how far each change is from zero.
3
Comparing 1500, 900, and 500: 500 is the smallest value.
The smallest absolute value corresponds to the smallest population change (closest to zero).
4
The smallest absolute value (500) belongs to Town Z.
Town Z experienced the smallest population change.

Answer: Town Z (+500) (Option A)

This means we need to find the temperature change that has the largest distance from zero, regardless of whether it's an increase or decrease.
