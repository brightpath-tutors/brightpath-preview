Question 1Grade 6 • NS
Sarah's bank account has a balance of -$25. John's bank account has a balance of -$15. Whose account has a greater balance?
A. Sarah's account
B. Both accounts have an equal balance
C. Cannot be determined
D. John's account
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
A negative balance indicates debt. A smaller negative number means less debt and therefore a greater balance.
In financial contexts, negative numbers represent money owed. The closer a negative number is to zero, the less money is owed, and thus the higher the balance.
2
Sarah's balance: -$25 John's balance: -$15 On a number line, -15 is to the right of -25. Therefore, -15 > -25.
Numbers on a number line increase from left to right. Since -15 is closer to zero (and to the right of -25), it is the greater value.
3
Since -$15 is greater than -$25, John's account has a greater balance.
John has less debt than Sarah, so his account balance is higher.

Answer: John's account (Option D)

E. None of the above

2
Initial depth: -200 meters Later depth: -350 meters
The given values directly represent the depths as integers.
3
Distance for -200 meters: |-200| = 200 meters Distance for -350 meters: |-350| = 350 meters

4
200 meters vs 350 meters 350 > 200
Comparing the positive distances shows which one is greater.
5
Since 350 meters is greater than 200 meters, -350 meters represents a greater distance from the surface.
The depth of -350 meters means the submarine is 350 meters away from the surface, which is a greater distance than 200 meters.

2
A higher score indicates better performance. Therefore, we are looking for the greater integer.

3

4
Since -10 is greater than -15, Student A performed better.
Student A lost fewer points, resulting in a higher (less negative) score.
