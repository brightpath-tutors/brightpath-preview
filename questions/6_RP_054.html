Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A grocery store sells a pack of 12 soda cans for $4.80. What is the unit price per can?
A. $4.80 per can
B. $2.50 per can
C. $0.04 per can
D. $0.40 per can
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total cost = $4.80, Number of cans = 12
We need to find the price for one can, so we'll divide the total cost by the number of cans.
2
$4.80 \div 12 = 0.40$
Performing the division gives the cost per can.
3
$0.40 per can
The unit price is $0.40 for each can of soda.

Answer: $0.40 per can (Option D)

Question 3Grade 6 • RP
A recipe calls for 6 ounces of chocolate chips for 18 cookies. How many ounces of chocolate chips are needed per cookie? Express your answer as a simplified fraction.
A. 3 ounces per cookie
B. 1/6 ounce per cookie
C. 1/3 ounce per cookie
D. 6 ounces per cookie
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total chocolate chips = 6 ounces, Total cookies = 18
To find the amount per cookie, we divide the total chocolate chips by the number of cookies.
2
6 ounces \div 18 cookies = 6/18 ounces/cookie = 1/3 ounce/cookie
Divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 6. (6 \div 6) / (18 \div 6) = 1/3.
3
1/3 ounce per cookie
The unit rate is 1/3 ounce of chocolate chips for each cookie.

Answer: 1/3 ounce per cookie (Option C)
