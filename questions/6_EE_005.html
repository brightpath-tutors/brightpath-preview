Question 1Grade 6 • EE
Emily earned some money by selling handmade cards and bookmarks. She sold 4 cards for 'c' dollars each and 5 bookmarks for 'b' dollars each. The next day, she sold 2 more cards and 3 more bookmarks. Write an expression in simplest form to represent the total amount of money Emily earned.
A. 24cb
B. 6c + 3b + 5b
C. 6c + 8b
D. 4c + 5b + 2c + 3b
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial cards: 4c Initial bookmarks: 5b
The initial sales were 4 cards at 'c' dollars each and 5 bookmarks at 'b' dollars each.
2
Additional cards: 2c Additional bookmarks: 3b
The next day, Emily sold 2 more cards and 3 more bookmarks.
3
(4c + 2c) + (5b + 3b)
Group the terms with the same variable ('c' for cards and 'b' for bookmarks) together.
4
(4 + 2)c + (5 + 3)b = 6c + 8b
Add the coefficients for the 'c' terms and the 'b' terms separately to find the total for each.

Answer: 6c + 8b (Option C)

Question 2Grade 6 • EE
During a basketball practice, a player made 'f' free throws and 't' two-point shots. In the next practice session, the player made 5 more free throws and 2 more two-point shots. Write an expression in simplest form representing the total number of free throws and two-point shots made over both sessions.
A. 6f + 3t
B. f + t + 5f + 2t
C. 11ft
D. 6f + 2t
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Free throws: f (which is 1f) Two-point shots: t (which is 1t)
The variables 'f' and 't' implicitly have a coefficient of 1 when written alone.
2
Additional free throws: 5f Additional two-point shots: 2t
The player made 5 more free throws and 2 more two-point shots.
3
(1f + 5f) + (1t + 2t)
Group the terms representing free throws ('f') and two-point shots ('t').
4
(1 + 5)f + (1 + 2)t = 6f + 3t
Add the coefficients for the 'f' terms and the 't' terms separately.

Answer: 6f + 3t (Option A)

Question 3Grade 6 • EE
A chef is preparing a recipe that calls for 'x' grams of salt and 'y' grams of pepper. For a larger batch, she needs to double the recipe, so she adds another 'x' grams of salt and 'y' grams of pepper. Additionally, she adds 3 extra grams of salt and 2 extra grams of pepper for seasoning. Write an expression in simplest form for the total amount of salt and pepper needed.
A. 12xy
B. 5x + 3y
C. 6xy
D. 5x + 4y
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Salt: x (which is 1x) Pepper: y (which is 1y)
The initial recipe calls for 'x' grams of salt and 'y' grams of pepper.
2
Additional salt: x (which is 1x) Additional pepper: y (which is 1y)
Doubling the recipe means adding another 'x' grams of salt and 'y' grams of pepper.
3
Extra salt: 3x Extra pepper: 2y
She adds 3 extra grams of salt and 2 extra grams of pepper.
4
(x + x + 3x) + (y + y + 2y)
Group all terms with 'x' (salt) and all terms with 'y' (pepper).
5
(1 + 1 + 3)x + (1 + 1 + 2)y = 5x + 4y
Add the coefficients for the 'x' terms and the 'y' terms separately.

Answer: 5x + 4y (Option D)

Question 4Grade 6 • EE
A car travels for 'h' hours at a speed of 60 miles per hour, then for another 'h' hours at a speed of 70 miles per hour. Afterwards, it stops for 2 hours. Write an expression in simplest form representing the total time the car was traveling or stopped.
A. 2h + 2
B. 60h + 70h + 2
C. 2h
D. 130h + 2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
First leg travel time: h hours (which is 1h)
The car travels for 'h' hours during the first part of the journey.
2
Second leg travel time: h hours (which is 1h)
The car travels for another 'h' hours during the second part.
3
Stop time: 2 hours
The car stops for 2 hours.
4
(h + h) + 2
Group the terms representing time ('h') together, and keep the constant separate.
5
(1 + 1)h + 2 = 2h + 2
Add the coefficients for the 'h' terms. The constant term remains as is.

Answer: 2h + 2 (Option A)

Question 5Grade 6 • EE
In a classroom, there are 's' students and 'd' desks. The teacher brings in 3 more students and removes 2 desks. Later, 4 students leave, and 5 more desks are brought in. Write an expression in simplest form representing the total number of students and desks in the classroom.
A. s + d + 3s - 2d - 4s + 5d
B. 4d
C. 8sd
D. s + 4d
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Students: s (which is 1s) Desks: d (which is 1d)
The initial state of the classroom is 's' students and 'd' desks.
2
Added students: +3s Removed desks: -2d
The teacher brings in 3 students and removes 2 desks.
3
Students leaving: -4s Added desks: +5d
Later, 4 students leave and 5 more desks are brought in.
4
(s + 3s - 4s) + (d - 2d + 5d)
Group all terms with 's' (students) and all terms with 'd' (desks).
5
(1 + 3 - 4)s + (1 - 2 + 5)d = 0s + 4d = 4d
Add and subtract the coefficients for the 's' terms and the 'd' terms separately. Since 0s is 0, the term disappears.

Answer: 4d (Option B)

Question 6Grade 6 • EE
In a chemistry experiment, a student mixes 'a' milliliters of acid, 'b' milliliters of base, and 10 milliliters of water. They then add 2 more milliliters of acid, remove 3 milliliters of base, and add 5 milliliters of water. Write an expression in simplest form for the total volume of the mixture.
A. 3a - 2b + 15
B. a + b + 10 + 2a - 3b + 5
C. 16ab
D. 3a + 2b + 15
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Acid: a (1a) Base: b (1b) Water: 10
The experiment starts with 'a' ml of acid, 'b' ml of base, and 10 ml of water.
2
Added acid: +2a Removed base: -3b Added water: +5
The student adds 2 ml of acid, removes 3 ml of base, and adds 5 ml of water.
3
(a + 2a) + (b - 3b) + (10 + 5)
Group terms with 'a', terms with 'b', and constant terms (water volume).
4
(1 + 2)a + (1 - 3)b + (10 + 5) = 3a - 2b + 15
Add the coefficients for 'a' terms, subtract for 'b' terms, and add for constant terms.

Answer: 3a - 2b + 15 (Option A)

Question 7Grade 6 • EE
A builder orders 'p' planks of wood, 'n' pounds of nails, and 15 roof tiles. For the next phase of the project, they need 5 fewer planks, 2 more pounds of nails, and 8 fewer roof tiles. Write an expression in simplest form to represent the total quantity of these materials.
A. 3pn + 7
B. -4p + 3n - 7
C. -4p + 3n + 7
D. 4p + 3n + 7
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Planks: p (1p) Nails: n (1n) Tiles: 15
The builder starts with 'p' planks, 'n' pounds of nails, and 15 roof tiles.
2
Fewer planks: -5p More nails: +2n Fewer tiles: -8
For the next phase, they need 5 fewer planks, 2 more pounds of nails, and 8 fewer roof tiles.
3
(p - 5p) + (n + 2n) + (15 - 8)
Group terms with 'p', terms with 'n', and constant terms.
4
(1 - 5)p + (1 + 2)n + (15 - 8) = -4p + 3n + 7
Perform the subtraction for 'p' terms, addition for 'n' terms, and subtraction for constant terms.

Answer: -4p + 3n + 7 (Option C)

Question 8Grade 6 • EE
A student dedicates 'x' hours to homework, 'y' hours to extracurricular activities, and 30 minutes to chores each day. Over a week (7 days), they spend twice this amount on homework, 3 fewer hours on extracurriculars, and the same 30 minutes on chores daily. Write an expression in simplest form for the total time spent on these activities over the week, where time is measured in hours. (Hint: 30 minutes = 0.5 hours)
A. 2x + y - 3 + 0.5
B. 14x + 7y - 17.5
C. 14x + 7y + 24.5
D. 14x + 7y - 21.5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Homework: 2x hours Extracurriculars: (y - 3) hours Chores: 0.5 hours
30 minutes is 0.5 hours. Homework is doubled (2x). Extracurriculars are 3 fewer hours (y-3). Chores remain 0.5 hours daily.
2
Total weekly time = 7 (2x) + 7 (y - 3) + 7 * (0.5)
Since these are daily amounts, multiply each by 7 to get the total for the week.
3
14x + 7y - 21 + 3.5
Distribute the 7 to each term within the parentheses and multiply by the chore time.
4
14x + 7y + (-21 + 3.5) = 14x + 7y - 17.5
Combine the numerical constants -21 and 3.5.

Answer: 14x + 7y - 17.5 (Option B)

Question 9Grade 6 • EE
A small town's population is being tracked. Initially, there are 'm' men, 'w' women, and 'c' children. Over a decade, the number of men triples, the number of women decreases by 200, and the number of children increases by 150. Write an expression in simplest form to represent the total population after the decade.
A. 3m + w - 50
B. 3m + w + c + 50
C. 3mwc - 50
D. 3m + w + c - 50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Men: m (1m) Women: w (1w) Children: c (1c)
The initial population consists of 'm' men, 'w' women, and 'c' children.
2
Men: becomes 3m Women: becomes w - 200 Children: becomes c + 150
The number of men triples (3m), women decrease by 200, and children increase by 150.
3
Total population = 3m + (w - 200) + (c + 150)
Sum the new quantities for men, women, and children.
4
3m + w + c + (-200 + 150) = 3m + w + c - 50
Combine the numerical constants -200 and +150.

Answer: 3m + w + c - 50 (Option D)

Question 10Grade 6 • EE
A technician is tracking the performance of two computers. Computer A has 'r' gigabytes of RAM, 's' gigabytes of storage, and 5 processing cores. Computer B has 1.5 times the RAM of Computer A, 0.5 times the storage of Computer A, and 2 fewer processing cores than Computer A. Write an expression in simplest form that represents the total combined RAM, storage, and processing cores of both computers.
A. 1.5r + 0.5s + 3
B. 4.0rs + 8
C. 2.5r + 1.5s + 8
D. 2.5r + 1.5s + 3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
RAM: r (1r) Storage: s (1s) Cores: 5
Computer A has 'r' GB RAM, 's' GB storage, and 5 processing cores.
2
RAM: 1.5r Storage: 0.5s Cores: 5 - 2 = 3
Computer B has 1.5 times Computer A's RAM, 0.5 times Computer A's storage, and 2 fewer cores than Computer A (5-2=3).
3
(r + 1.5r) + (s + 0.5s) + (5 + 3)
Group RAM terms, storage terms, and core terms together.
4
(1 + 1.5)r + (1 + 0.5)s + (5 + 3) = 2.5r + 1.5s + 8
Add the coefficients for 'r' terms, 's' terms, and add the constant terms.

Answer: 2.5r + 1.5s + 8 (Option C)
