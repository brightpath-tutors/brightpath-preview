Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A unique handcrafted item at a market is priced at $17.\overline{33}$. Classify this number.
A. Whole number
B. Rational
C. Integer
D. Irrational
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The number is $17.\overline{33}$.
We are given a number with a repeating decimal part.
2
The bar over '33' indicates that the digits '33' repeat infinitely ($17.3333...$).
Understanding the notation for repeating decimals is crucial for classification.
3
A rational number is any number that can be expressed as a fraction \(\frac{p}{q}\) where \(p\) and \(q\) are integers and $q eq 0$. Terminating and repeating decimals are rational numbers.
Recalling the definition of rational numbers helps in classifying the given number.
4
Since $17.\overline{33}$ is a repeating decimal, it can be written as a fraction (e.g., $17 \frac{33}{99} = 17 \frac{1}{3} = \frac{52}{3}$). Therefore, it is a rational number.
Because it is a repeating decimal, it fits the definition of a rational number.

Answer: Rational (Option B)

Question 2Grade 7 • NS
In a relay race, Athlete A ran their leg in $10 \frac{1}{3}$ seconds, and Athlete B ran their leg in $10.3$ seconds. Which athlete was faster?
A. They ran at the same speed.
B. Athlete A
C. Not enough information to determine.
D. Athlete B
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$10 \frac{1}{3} = 10 + \frac{1}{3} = 10 + 0.333... = 10.\overline{3}$ seconds.
To compare times, it's easiest to have them in the same format, usually decimals.
2
Athlete B's time is $10.3$ seconds.
This time is already in decimal format.
3
To be faster, the time must be less. Comparing $10.\overline{3}$ and $10.3$: - $10.333...$ - $10.300...$ Since $10.3 < 10.\overline{3}$, Athlete B was faster.
Compare the decimal digits from left to right. The '3' in the hundredths place for Athlete A's time makes it larger than Athlete B's time, which has a '0' in the hundredths place.
4
Athlete B was faster.
The athlete with the lower time is the faster one.

Answer: Athlete B (Option D)

Question 6Grade 7 • NS
A scientist measures the length of a microscopic organism to be \(\sqrt{29}\) micrometers. Approximate this length to the nearest tenth of a micrometer.
A. 5.4
B. 5.0
C. 5.5
D. 5.3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
We know that $5^2 = 25$ and $6^2 = 36$. Therefore, \(\sqrt{29}\) is between 5 and 6.
Finding the nearest perfect squares helps to narrow down the range for the approximation.
2
- $5.1^2 = 26.01$ - $5.2^2 = 27.04$ - $5.3^2 = 28.09$ - $5.4^2 = 29.16$
Systematically test decimal values in the range by squaring them to see which is closest to 29.
3
- Distance from $5.3^2$: $|29 - 28.09| = 0.91$ - Distance from $5.4^2$: $|29 - 29.16| = 0.16$
Calculate the absolute difference between 29 and the squares of the tested decimal values.
4
Since 0.16 is smaller than 0.91, $5.4^2$ is closer to 29 than $5.3^2$. Thus, \(\sqrt{29}\) approximated to the nearest tenth is 5.4.
The decimal whose square is closest to 29 is the best approximation to the nearest tenth.

Answer: 5.4 (Option A)

Question 7Grade 7 • NS
A square tile has a side length of \(\sqrt{7}\) inches. What is the area of the tile in simplest form?
A. \(\sqrt{49}\) square inches
B. \(\sqrt{14}\) square inches
C. 7 square inches
D. $7\sqrt{7}$ square inches
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Area = side \(\times\) side.
The area of a square is found by multiplying its side length by itself.
2
Area = \(\sqrt{7} \times \sqrt{7}\).
Replace 'side' in the formula with the given value \(\sqrt{7}\).
3
\(\sqrt{7} \times \sqrt{7} = \sqrt{7 \times 7} = \sqrt{49}\).
When multiplying two identical square roots, the result is the number inside the square root.
4
\(\sqrt{49} = 7\).
Evaluate the perfect square root to get an integer.
5
The area is 7 square inches.
Always include appropriate units for the final answer.

Answer: 7 square inches (Option C)

Question 9Grade 7 • NS

A. They performed equally well, with no difference.
B. Last year's event, by approximately 0.2 percentage points.
C. This year's event, by approximately 0.1 percentage points.
D. This year's event, by approximately 0.2 percentage points.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
\(\frac{15}{22} \approx 0.681818...\) As a percentage, this is approximately $68.18\%$.
Convert the fraction to a decimal by division, then express it as a percentage for comparison.
2
Last year's event achievement was $68\%$.
This value is already in percentage format.
3
Comparing $68.18\%$ (this year) with $68\%$ (last year), we see that $68.18\% > 68\%$. So, this year's event performed better.
Directly compare the percentage values to determine which is greater.
4

Subtract the smaller percentage from the larger one to find the difference.
5
$0.18\%$ rounds to $0.2\%$.
Apply rounding rules to express the difference to the specified precision.

A. Not enough information to determine.
B. They download at the same rate.
C. The second file downloads faster.
D. The first file downloads faster.
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Rate 1 = $2 \times 10^3$ bytes/second = 2000 bytes/second.
Convert the scientific notation to a standard numerical value for easier comparison.
2
Rate 2 = \(\frac{1}{500}\) megabytes/second.
Note the given rate for the second file.
3
Given 1 megabyte = $10^6$ bytes. Rate 2 = \(\frac{1}{500} \times 10^6\) bytes/second Rate 2 = \(\frac{1000000}{500}\) bytes/second Rate 2 = $2000$ bytes/second.
Use the provided conversion factor to express the second file's download rate in bytes per second, matching the unit of the first file.
4
- Rate 1 = 2000 bytes/second - Rate 2 = 2000 bytes/second
Now that both rates are in the same units, they can be directly compared.
5
Both files download at the same rate.
Since the numerical values are identical, the rates are the same.
