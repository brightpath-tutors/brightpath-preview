Question 1Grade 6 • NS
Sarah bought a scarf for 1/4 of the original price. Does the decimal representation of the discount terminate?
A. Yes, 0.25
B. No, it is a repeating decimal.
C. No, because 1 cannot be divided by 4 evenly.
D. Yes, 0.14
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
To determine if the decimal representation of 1/4 terminates, we need to convert the fraction to a decimal.
A terminating decimal is a decimal that has a finite number of digits after the decimal point. We convert a fraction to a decimal by dividing the numerator by the denominator.
2
1 ÷ 4 = 0.25
Divide 1 by 4. Since 1 is smaller than 4, we add a decimal point and a zero to 1, making it 1.0. 10 divided by 4 is 2 with a remainder of 2. Add another zero to make it 20. 20 divided by 4 is 5 with a remainder of 0. The division ends, resulting in 0.25.
3
The decimal is 0.25, which has a finite number of digits.
Since the division of 1 by 4 resulted in 0.25, and there are no more digits after the 5, the decimal terminates.

Answer: Yes, 0.25 (Option A)

Question 9Grade 6 • NS
In a small town, 15 out of 160 residents own a bicycle. Does the decimal representation of the fraction of residents who own a bicycle terminate?
A. No, it is a repeating decimal.
B. No, because 160 is a large number so it must repeat.
C. Yes, 0.15160
D. Yes, 0.09375
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The fraction is 15/160.
The number of residents who own a bicycle is the numerator, and the total number of residents is the denominator.
2
15/160 = (15 ÷ 5) / (160 ÷ 5) = 3/32
To determine termination, it's often helpful to simplify the fraction first. The greatest common factor of 15 and 160 is 5. Divide both by 5.
3
3 ÷ 32 = 0.09375
Perform long division of 3 by 32. Add a decimal point and zeros to 3 (3.00000). 30 ÷ 32 = 0 remainder 30. 300 ÷ 32 = 9 remainder 12. 120 ÷ 32 = 3 remainder 24. 240 ÷ 32 = 7 remainder 16. 160 ÷ 32 = 5 remainder 0. The division ends.
4
The decimal 0.09375 has a finite number of digits.
Since the division resulted in a remainder of zero, 0.09375 is a terminating decimal. Alternatively, the prime factors of the simplified denominator 32 are only 2s (32 = 2^5), which means the decimal must terminate.

Answer: Yes, 0.09375 (Option D)
