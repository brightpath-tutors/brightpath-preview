Skill ID: 7.NS.002

Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A shop owner has 15 3/4 pounds of candy. She wants to divide it into bags, each containing 1 1/2 pounds of candy. How many bags of candy can she make, in simplest form?
A. 2/21 bags
B. 23 5/8 bags
C. 10 1/2 bags
D. 16 1/2 bags
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
15 3/4 = (15 4 + 3) / 4 = 63/4 1 1/2 = (1 2 + 1) / 2 = 3/2
To divide mixed numbers, first convert them into improper fractions. Multiply the whole number by the denominator and add the numerator. Keep the same denominator.
2
63/4 ÷ 3/2 = 63/4 * 2/3
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal (flip the second fraction).
3
(63 2) / (4 3) = 126/12
Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.
4
126/12 = 21/2 = 10 1/2
Divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor (GCD). The GCD of 126 and 12 is 6. So, 126 ÷ 6 = 21 and 12 ÷ 6 = 2. Finally, convert the improper fraction 21/2 back to a mixed number by dividing 21 by 2, which gives 10 with a remainder of 1. So, 10 1/2.

Answer: 10 1/2 bags (Option C)

Question 2Grade 7 • NS
A running track is 2 1/4 miles long. If a runner completes 5 5/8 miles, how many laps did they run, in simplest form?
A. 12 21/32 laps
B. 11 1/4 laps
C. 2 1/2 laps
D. 4 1/2 laps
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
5 5/8 = (5 8 + 5) / 8 = 45/8 2 1/4 = (2 4 + 1) / 4 = 9/4
Convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions to facilitate division. Multiply the whole number by the denominator and add the numerator, keeping the same denominator.
2
45/8 ÷ 9/4 = 45/8 * 4/9
Change the division operation to multiplication, and flip the second fraction (the divisor) to its reciprocal.
3
(45 4) / (8 9) = 180/72 To simplify, divide 45 by 9 (which is 5) and 4 by 8 (which is 1/2). So, 5 * 1/2 = 5/2
Multiply the numerators and denominators. It's often easier to simplify before multiplying. Divide 45 by 9 to get 5. Divide 4 by 4 to get 1 and 8 by 4 to get 2. The new fractions are 5/8 and 4/9. We can cross-cancel further: 45 and 9 share a common factor of 9 (45/9=5, 9/9=1). 4 and 8 share a common factor of 4 (4/4=1, 8/4=2). So, (5/2) * (1/1) = 5/2.
4
5/2 = 2 1/2
Divide the numerator (5) by the denominator (2). The quotient (2) is the whole number part, and the remainder (1) becomes the new numerator over the original denominator (2).

Answer: 2 1/2 laps (Option C)

Question 3Grade 7 • NS
A recipe calls for 3 1/3 cups of water. If you only have a measuring cup that holds 2/3 of a cup, how many times will you need to fill the measuring cup?
A. 5 times
B. 4 1/2 times
C. 2 2/9 times
D. 9/20 times
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
3 1/3 = (3 * 3 + 1) / 3 = 10/3
Convert the total amount of water (3 1/3 cups) into an improper fraction.
2
10/3 ÷ 2/3 = 10/3 * 3/2
To divide 10/3 cups by 2/3 cups per fill, multiply 10/3 by the reciprocal of 2/3.
3
(10 3) / (3 2) = 30/6 30/6 = 5
Multiply the numerators and denominators. Then, simplify the resulting fraction. 30 divided by 6 equals 5.

Answer: 5 times (Option A)

Question 4Grade 7 • NS
A car travels 120 1/2 miles in 2 3/4 hours. What is its average speed in miles per hour, in simplest form?
A. 60 2/3 mph
B. 22 2/11 mph
C. 331 3/8 mph
D. 43 9/11 mph
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
120 1/2 = (120 2 + 1) / 2 = 241/2 2 3/4 = (2 4 + 3) / 4 = 11/4
To find the average speed, divide the total distance by the total time. First, convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions.
2
241/2 ÷ 11/4 = 241/2 * 4/11
Dividing by a fraction is equivalent to multiplying by its reciprocal.
3
(241 4) / (2 11) = 964/22 Simplify by dividing both numerator and denominator by 2: 964 ÷ 2 = 482 22 ÷ 2 = 11 So, 482/11
Multiply the numerators and the denominators. Then, simplify the resulting fraction by dividing both parts by their greatest common divisor, which is 2. (Alternatively, you could have cross-simplified earlier: 4/2 = 2/1, so 241 * 2 / 11 = 482/11).
4
482/11 = 43 with remainder 9 = 43 9/11
Divide 482 by 11. The quotient is 43 and the remainder is 9. So the mixed number is 43 9/11.

Answer: 43 9/11 mph (Option D)

Question 6Grade 7 • NS
A scientist has 8 3/4 liters of a chemical solution. She wants to pour it into test tubes, each holding 7/8 of a liter. How many test tubes can she fill completely?
A. 7 21/32 test tubes
B. 10 test tubes
C. 3 10/11 test tubes
D. 6 6/7 test tubes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
8 3/4 = (8 * 4 + 3) / 4 = 35/4
To find out how many test tubes can be filled, divide the total solution by the capacity of one test tube. First, convert the mixed number to an improper fraction.
2
35/4 ÷ 7/8 = 35/4 * 8/7
Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal (flip the second fraction).
3
(35 8) / (4 7) Simplify by cross-cancelling: 35 ÷ 7 = 5, and 8 ÷ 4 = 2. So, 5 * 2 = 10
Multiply the numerators and denominators. It's more efficient to simplify by cross-cancelling common factors first. 35 and 7 share a common factor of 7 (35/7=5, 7/7=1). 8 and 4 share a common factor of 4 (8/4=2, 4/4=1). This leaves (5/1) * (2/1) = 10.

Answer: 10 test tubes (Option B)

Question 7Grade 7 • NS
A construction worker has a 12 1/3 foot long metal beam. He needs to cut pieces that are 1 1/6 feet long. How many full pieces can he cut from the beam?
A. 14 7/18 pieces
B. 10 full pieces
C. 37 pieces
D. 10 4/7 pieces
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
12 1/3 = (12 3 + 1) / 3 = 37/3 1 1/6 = (1 6 + 1) / 6 = 7/6
To find out how many pieces can be cut, divide the total length of the beam by the length of each piece. Convert both mixed numbers into improper fractions.
2
37/3 ÷ 7/6 = 37/3 * 6/7
Change the division operation to multiplication and flip the second fraction (the divisor) to its reciprocal.
3
(37 6) / (3 7) = 222/21 Simplify by cross-cancelling: 6 ÷ 3 = 2. So, (37 * 2) / 7 = 74/7
Multiply the numerators and denominators. It's efficient to simplify by cross-cancelling common factors first. 6 and 3 share a common factor of 3 (6/3=2, 3/3=1). This leaves (37/1) * (2/7) = 74/7.
4
74/7 = 10 with remainder 4 = 10 4/7 Therefore, 10 full pieces can be cut.
Divide 74 by 7. The quotient is 10 and the remainder is 4. This means 10 full pieces can be cut, with 4/7 of a piece remaining. The question asks for 'full pieces', so the answer is the whole number part of the mixed number.

Answer: 10 full pieces (Option B)

To find the number of items produced, divide the total time by the time it takes to produce one item. Convert both mixed numbers into improper fractions.

Question 10Grade 7 • NS
A 3D printer needs 3 1/8 hours to complete a complex model. If it prints continuously for 18 3/4 hours, how many complex models can it complete?
A. 6 models
B. 58 19/32 models
C. 12 models
D. 10 6/11 models
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
18 3/4 = (18 4 + 3) / 4 = 75/4 3 1/8 = (3 8 + 1) / 8 = 25/8
To find how many models can be completed, divide the total printing time by the time required for one model. Convert both mixed numbers into improper fractions.
2
75/4 ÷ 25/8 = 75/4 * 8/25
Change the division operation to multiplication and flip the second fraction (the divisor) to its reciprocal.
3
(75 8) / (4 25) Simplify by cross-cancelling: 75 ÷ 25 = 3, and 8 ÷ 4 = 2. So, 3 * 2 = 6
Multiply the numerators and denominators. It's efficient to simplify by cross-cancelling common factors first. 75 and 25 share a common factor of 25 (75/25=3, 25/25=1). 8 and 4 share a common factor of 4 (8/4=2, 4/4=1). This leaves (3/1) * (2/1) = 6.

Answer: 6 models (Option A)
