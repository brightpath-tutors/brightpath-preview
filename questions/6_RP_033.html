Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A store sells 7 notebooks for $14.00. What is the constant of proportionality that relates the total cost to the number of notebooks?
A. $0.50 per notebook
B. $98.00 per notebook
C. $2.00 per notebook
D. $21.00 per notebook
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total Cost (C) = $14.00 Number of Notebooks (N) = 7
In a proportional relationship, we look for a constant ratio between two quantities. Here, the cost depends on the number of notebooks.
2
k = C / N k = $14.00 / 7 notebooks
The constant of proportionality is found by dividing the dependent variable (total cost) by the independent variable (number of notebooks).
3
k = 14 / 7 = 2
Performing the division gives the constant of proportionality.
4
k = $2.00 per notebook
The constant means that for every notebook, the cost is $2.00.

Answer: $2.00 per notebook (Option C)

Question 8Grade 6 • RP
A specific type of antique clock loses 15 seconds every 3 hours. What is the constant of proportionality relating the total seconds lost to the number of minutes that have passed, in simplest form?
A. 1/5 seconds per minute
B. 5 seconds per minute
C. 1/12 seconds per minute
D. 12 seconds per minute
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Seconds Lost (S) = 15 seconds Time (T) = 3 hours Convert hours to minutes: 3 hours * 60 minutes/hour = 180 minutes
The problem asks for seconds lost per minute, so we must convert the given hours into minutes before finding the ratio.
2
k = S / T k = 15 seconds / 180 minutes
The constant of proportionality is found by dividing the total seconds lost by the total minutes passed.
3
k = 15 / 180 k = 15 ÷ 15 / 180 ÷ 15 = 1 / 12
Perform the division and simplify the resulting fraction to its simplest form.
4
k = 1/12 seconds per minute
This means the clock loses 1/12 of a second every minute.

Answer: 1/12 seconds per minute (Option C)
