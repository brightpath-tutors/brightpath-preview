Question 1Grade 6 • NS
Sarah had $1/2$ of a dollar. She spent $1/4$ of a dollar on a piece of candy. How much of a dollar does Sarah have left?
A. $3/4$
B. $1/2$
C. $1/4$
D. $0$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Sarah starts with $1/2$ dollar and spends $1/4$ dollar. The operation is subtraction: $1/2 - 1/4$.
We need to find the difference between the initial amount and the amount spent.
2
The denominators are 2 and 4. The least common multiple (LCM) of 2 and 4 is 4.
To subtract fractions, they must have the same denominator.
3
$1/2 = (1 \times 2) / (2 \times 2) = 2/4$. The other fraction, $1/4$, already has the common denominator.
Multiply the numerator and denominator by the same number to get an equivalent fraction.
4
$2/4 - 1/4 = (2 - 1) / 4 = 1/4$.
Subtract the numerators and keep the common denominator.
5
$1/4$ is already in simplest form.
Check if the fraction can be reduced further.

Answer: $1/4$ of a dollar (Option C)

Question 4Grade 6 • NS

B. $2 \frac{4}{10}$ miles
C. $1 \frac{2}{10}$ miles
D. $2 \frac{2}{10}$ miles
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
We need to find the difference between $5 \frac{3}{10}$ and $3 \frac{1}{10}$. The operation is subtraction: $5 \frac{3}{10} - 3 \frac{1}{10}$.
To find 'how much farther,' subtract the shorter distance from the longer distance.
2
$5 - 3 = 2$.
Subtract the whole number parts of the mixed numbers.
3
$3/10 - 1/10 = (3 - 1) / 10 = 2/10$.
Subtract the fractional parts. Since they have the same denominator, subtract the numerators and keep the denominator.
4
The result is $2 \frac{2}{10}$.
Put the whole number and fractional difference together.
5
$2/10$ can be simplified by dividing both numerator and denominator by 2: $2 \div 2 / 10 \div 2 = 1/5$. So the final answer is $2 \frac{1}{5}$.
Always simplify fractions to their simplest form.
