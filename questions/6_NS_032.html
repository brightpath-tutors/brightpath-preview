B. Mark's account

D. It cannot be determined
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Sarah's balance = -$25 Mark's balance = -$18
We need to compare the two negative integer balances.
2
On a number line, numbers to the right are greater. For negative numbers, the number closer to zero (the one with the smaller absolute value) is greater.
This rule helps us determine which negative number has a higher value.
3
-18 > -25
Since -18 is closer to 0 than -25 on the number line, -18 is the greater value.
4
Mark's account has -$18, which is greater than Sarah's -$25.
Mark's account has a greater balance.
