Question 6Grade 6 • NS
A diver starts at a depth of -4 meters relative to the surface. He then rises 2 meters. What is his new position?
A. 6 meters
B. -6 meters
C. 2 meters
D. -2 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The diver starts at a depth of -4 meters.
Initial depth is given as a negative integer, indicating below the surface.
2
He 'rises 2 meters'. Rising implies a positive change.
Movement upwards is represented by a positive integer.
3
-4 + 2 = -2
When adding integers with different signs, subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger absolute value and keep the sign of the number with the larger absolute value. | -4 | = 4, | 2 | = 2. 4 - 2 = 2. Since 4 is larger and negative, the result is -2.

Answer: -2 meters (Option D)

Question 10Grade 6 • NS
A painter tracks the height of their brush relative to the center of a large canvas. The brush starts at 0 cm. It moves down 8 cm, then up 5 cm, and finally down another 2 cm. What is the final vertical position of the brush?
A. 5 cm
B. 15 cm
C. -15 cm
D. -5 cm
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Starts at 0. Moves down 8 cm: -8. Moves up 5 cm: +5. Moves down another 2 cm: -2.
Down movements are negative, up movements are positive relative to the starting point.
2
0 + (-8) + 5 + (-2) = -8 + 5 - 2 = -3 - 2 = -5
Combine the changes. Start with -8, add 5 to get -3, then subtract 2 to get -5.

Answer: -5 cm (Option D)
