Question 8Grade 6 • NS
A construction crew is tiling a floor. They have 3 sections, each 5 feet wide. The lengths of these sections are 9 feet, 11 feet, and 13 feet. To calculate the total area, they could use the expression 5 × (9 + 11 + 13). If they first choose to add (9 + 11) then add 13, and then later apply the multiplication, which two properties of real numbers are primarily being used to justify this sequence of calculations?
A. Commutative Property of Addition and Identity Property of Multiplication
B. Associative Property of Addition and Distributive Property
C. Distributive Property and Zero Property of Multiplication
D. Inverse Property of Addition and Associative Property of Multiplication
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial: 5 × (9 + 11 + 13). First step: 'first choose to add (9 + 11) then add 13'. This relates to grouping within the sum.
Break down the problem into the given expression and the first suggested operation.
2
The ability to choose to add (9 + 11) first and then 13, without changing the sum, is an application of the Associative Property of Addition (a + (b + c) = (a + b) + c).
The change in grouping for addition directly points to the Associative Property of Addition.
3
The overall structure of multiplying a number by a sum (5 × (9 + 11 + 13)) can also be calculated by distributing the multiplication to each term in the sum (5 × 9) + (5 × 11) + (5 × 13). This is the Distributive Property.
The problem implies flexibility in how the multiplication is applied to the sum, which is the core of the Distributive Property. Although the prompt asks about a sequence where multiplication is applied later, the choice of 'which two properties...justify this' implies the underlying principles that allow such flexibility.
4
The Associative Property of Addition allows for flexible grouping of the lengths, and the Distributive Property allows for the multiplication factor to be applied to each length independently or to the total sum.
The combination of these two properties provides the mathematical justification for the described calculation flexibility.

Answer: Associative Property of Addition and Distributive Property (Option B)
