Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A shirt costs $35.00. If there is a 20% discount, how much is the discount amount?
A. $7.00
B. $34.80
C. $700.00
D. $1.75
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
20% = 20/100 = 0.20
Convert the percentage to a decimal by dividing by 100.
2
Discount amount = $35.00 × 0.20
Multiply the original price by the decimal form of the discount percentage to find the discount amount.
3
Discount amount = $7.00
Perform the multiplication to get the final discount amount.

Answer: $7.00 (Option A)

Question 10Grade 6 • RP
A new smartphone costs $950.00. A store offers a 15% discount, but you also have to pay a 5% sales tax on the discounted price. What is the final price you pay for the smartphone?
A. $855.00
B. $807.50
C. $142.50
D. $847.88
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Discount rate = 15% = 0.15
Convert the discount percentage to a decimal.
2
Discount amount = $950.00 × 0.15 = $142.50
Calculate the amount of the discount.
3
Discounted price = $950.00 - $142.50 = $807.50
Subtract the discount amount from the original price to find the price after discount.
4
Sales tax rate = 5% = 0.05
Convert the sales tax percentage to a decimal.
5
Sales tax amount = $807.50 × 0.05 = $40.375
Calculate the sales tax on the discounted price.
6
Final price = $807.50 + $40.375 = $847.875
Add the sales tax amount to the discounted price to get the final price.
7
Final price = $847.88
Round the final price to two decimal places (nearest cent).

Answer: $847.88 (Option D)
