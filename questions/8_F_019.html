Question 1Grade 8 • F
A company's daily profit P can be modeled by the function P(x) = (x - 7)(x - 18), where x is the number of items sold. What are the two break-even points (number of items sold where profit is zero)?
A. 11
B. -7 and -18
C. 7 and 18
D. 126
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Break-even points occur when the profit P(x) is zero.
To find the break-even points, we need to set the profit function P(x) equal to zero.
2
(x - 7)(x - 18) = 0
This equation represents the condition where the profit is zero.
3
x - 7 = 0 OR x - 18 = 0
The Zero Product Property states that if the product of two or more factors is zero, then at least one of the factors must be zero. So, we set each factor equal to zero.
4
x - 7 = 0 => x = 7 x - 18 = 0 => x = 18
Solving these simple linear equations gives us the values of x that make the profit zero.
5
The two break-even points are 7 and 18 items sold.
These are the number of items the company must sell to neither make a profit nor incur a loss.

Answer: 7 and 18 (Option C)

Question 3Grade 8 • F
The area of a square-shaped tray for baking is represented by the expression x^2 - 100 square inches. What is the factored form of this expression?
A. (x - 10)^2
B. (x - 2)(x + 50)
C. (x - 10)(x + 10)
D. (x + 10)^2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The expression x^2 - 100 is a difference of two squares, since x^2 is (x)^2 and 100 is (10)^2.
Recognizing the pattern A^2 - B^2 is key to factoring this type of quadratic expression.
2
The formula for difference of squares is A^2 - B^2 = (A - B)(A + B).
This formula allows us to directly factor expressions of this form.
3
Here, A = x and B = 10. So, x^2 - 100 = (x - 10)(x + 10).
By substituting x for A and 10 for B, we get the factored form of the expression.

Answer: (x - 10)(x + 10) (Option C)

Question 4Grade 8 • F
The distance a boat travels can be modeled by the function d(t) = t^2 + 13t + 40, where t is the time in hours. Factor the quadratic expression for d(t).
A. (t + 5)(t + 8)
B. (t + 2)(t + 20)
C. (t - 5)(t - 8)
D. (t + 4)(t + 10)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The expression is in the form x^2 + bx + c, where b = 13 and c = 40.
This is a standard trinomial that can be factored into two binomials of the form (x + p)(x + q).
2
We need two numbers that multiply to 40 and add to 13. Factors of 40: (1, 40), (2, 20), (4, 10), (5, 8). Sums of factors: 1+40=41, 2+20=22, 4+10=14, 5+8=13. The numbers are 5 and 8.
This is the key step in factoring trinomials of this form. The product condition relates to the constant term, and the sum condition relates to the coefficient of the middle term.
3
Using the numbers 5 and 8, the factored form is (t + 5)(t + 8).
Once the two correct numbers are found, they are placed into the binomial factors.

Answer: (t + 5)(t + 8) (Option A)

C. (4t + 3)(t + 1)

Step-by-step solution
1
The expression is in the form ax^2 + bx + c, where a = 4, b = 13, and c = 3.
Since the leading coefficient 'a' is not 1, we use methods like grouping or trial and error (un-FOIL).
2
We need factors of a (4) and factors of c (3). Factors of 4: (1, 4), (2, 2). Factors of 3: (1, 3). Test combinations to get the middle term (13t).
This step sets up the structure for trial and error, where we're looking for combinations of factors that satisfy both the product of 'a' and 'c', and the sum of the inner and outer products for 'b'.
3
Consider (4t + ?)(t + ?): If we use 1 and 3 for the constants: (4t + 1)(t + 3) => 4tt + 4t3 + 1t + 13 = 4t^2 + 12t + t + 3 = 4t^2 + 13t + 3 (Correct!) Consider (2t + ?)(2t + ?): (2t + 1)(2t + 3) => 4t^2 + 6t + 2t + 3 = 4t^2 + 8t + 3 (Incorrect middle term)
Through trial and error, we find the combination of factors that, when multiplied using FOIL, results in the original trinomial. The 'outer' and 'inner' products must sum to the middle term 'bx'.
4
The factored form is (4t + 1)(t + 3).
This is the final factored expression.

Answer: (4t + 1)(t + 3) (Option D)

1
The expression is in the form ax^2 + bx + c, where a = 5, b = -17, and c = 6.

2
Factors of a (5): (1, 5). Factors of c (6): (1, 6), (2, 3). Since 'b' is negative and 'c' is positive, both constant terms in the binomials must be negative.
The signs of the constant terms in the binomials are determined by the signs of 'b' and 'c'. If 'c' is positive and 'b' is negative, both constant terms must be negative.

A. (4p - 49)(4p + 1)
B. (4p - 7)(4p + 7)
C. (16p - 7)(p + 7)
D. (16p + 49)(p - 1)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The expression 16p^2 - 49 is a difference of two squares, since 16p^2 is (4p)^2 and 49 is (7)^2.
Recognizing both terms as perfect squares and the subtraction operation indicates that the difference of squares formula should be used.
2
The formula for difference of squares is A^2 - B^2 = (A - B)(A + B).
This is a fundamental algebraic identity for factoring such expressions.
3
Here, A = 4p and B = 7. So, 16p^2 - 49 = (4p - 7)(4p + 7).
By correctly identifying A and B, we can directly substitute them into the formula to get the factored form.

Answer: (4p - 7)(4p + 7) (Option B)
