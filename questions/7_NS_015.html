3

Question 9Grade 7 • NS
A student spends \(\frac{2}{3}\) of an hour on math homework and \(\frac{1}{2}\) of an hour on science homework. What is the total time spent on homework, in simplest form?
A. \(\frac{3}{5}\) hours
B. \(\frac{7}{12}\) hours
C. 1 \(\frac{1}{2}\) hours
D. 1 \(\frac{1}{6}\) hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Math homework: \(\frac{2}{3}\) hours Science homework: \(\frac{1}{2}\) hours
To find the total time, we add the time spent on each subject's homework.
2
The least common multiple of 3 and 2 is 6. Convert \(\frac{2}{3}\) to \(\frac{2 \times 2}{3 \times 2} = \frac{4}{6}\) Convert \(\frac{1}{2}\) to \(\frac{1 \times 3}{2 \times 3} = \frac{3}{6}\)
To add fractions with different denominators, we need to find the least common denominator (LCD), which is 6. We convert both fractions to equivalent fractions with this denominator.
3
\(\frac{4}{6} + \frac{3}{6} = \frac{4+3}{6} = \frac{7}{6}\)
Now that both fractions have the same denominator, we add the numerators and keep the common denominator.
4
\(\frac{7}{6}\) is an improper fraction. To convert it to a mixed number, divide 7 by 6: 7 \(\div\) 6 = 1 with a remainder of 1. So, \(\frac{7}{6} = 1\frac{1}{6}\). The fraction \(\frac{1}{6}\) is in simplest form.
The sum is an improper fraction, so we convert it to a mixed number. The fractional part of the mixed number should also be in simplest form.

Answer: 1 \(\frac{1}{6}\) hours (Option D)
