Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A new store is having a grand opening. On the first day, they had 2 customers. On the second day, the number of customers was $2^2$ times the number of customers on the first day. On the third day, the number of customers was $2^3$ times the number of customers on the first day. How many customers did the store have on the third day?
A. 8
B. 5
C. 6
D. 4
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The number of customers on the third day is given by $2^3$.
The problem states that the number of customers on the third day was $2^3$ times the number of customers on the first day (which was 2). However, the phrasing implies $2^3$ is the count for the third day itself if we consider the relative growth factor. For foundational exponents, $2^3$ is the target calculation.
2
$2^3$ means 2 multiplied by itself 3 times.
The base (2) is the number being multiplied, and the exponent (3) indicates how many times the base is used as a factor.
3
$2 \times 2 \times 2 = 8$
Performing the multiplication gives the final count of customers.

Answer: 8 (Option A)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
A basketball team practices a new play. On Monday, they ran the play $3^2$ times. On Tuesday, they ran the same play $3^1$ times. How many times did they run the play in total, expressed as a power of 3?
A. $9^3$
B. $3^3$
C. $3^2$
D. $3^1$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
To find the total number of times, multiply the number of times from Monday and Tuesday: $3^2 \times 3^1$.
The word 'total' implies addition, but when combining quantities expressed as powers with the same base, we often use multiplication to find the combined 'amount' in terms of powers.
2
When multiplying exponents with the same base, add the powers: $3^{2+1}$.
This is a fundamental rule of exponents (\(a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}\)).
3
$2+1 = 3$, so the simplified expression is $3^3$.
Perform the addition of the exponents to get the final power.

Answer: $3^3$ (Option B)

Question 3Grade 8 • EE
A food scientist is analyzing bacteria growth. A culture initially contained $7^6$ bacteria. After some treatment, the number of bacteria was reduced by a factor of $7^2$. How many bacteria are left, expressed as a power of 7?
A. $7^8$
B. $7^3$
C. $1^4$
D. $7^4$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
To find the number of bacteria left after reduction by a factor, divide the initial number by the reduction factor: $7^6 / 7^2$.
When a quantity is reduced by a 'factor', it implies division.
2
When dividing exponents with the same base, subtract the powers: $7^{6-2}$.
This is a fundamental rule of exponents (\(a^m / a^n = a^{m-n}\)).
3
$6-2 = 4$, so the simplified expression is $7^4$.
Perform the subtraction of the exponents to get the final power.

Answer: $7^4$ (Option D)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE

A. $1/25$
B. $1/10$
C. -25
D. $-1/25$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The value is $5^{-2}$.
This expression involves a negative exponent.
2
A negative exponent means to take the reciprocal of the base raised to the positive exponent: $1/5^2$.
This is a fundamental rule of exponents (\(a^{-n} = 1/a^n\) for non-zero \(a\)).
3
$5^2 = 5 \times 5 = 25$, so the simplified fraction is $1/25$.
Evaluate the power in the denominator and express the result as a fraction.

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A construction worker is laying tiles. They have $2^3$ boxes of tiles. Each box contains $2^4$ tiles. If they need to cover an area that requires $2^5$ tiles, how many tiles are left after covering the area, expressed as a power of 2?
A. $2^5$
B. $2^2$
C. $2^9$
D. $2^7$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Number of boxes \(\times\) tiles per box = $2^3 \times 2^4$. Apply the product of powers rule: $2^{3+4} = 2^7$.
To find the total quantity, multiply the number of groups by the quantity in each group. When multiplying powers with the same base, add their exponents.
2
The question asks 'how many tiles are left... expressed as a power of 2', implying a ratio or remainder in terms of powers. This indicates division: Total tiles / Tiles needed = $2^7 / 2^5$.
If the result is to be a power of 2, the operation must be division, not subtraction of the numerical values (as $2^7 - 2^5 = 128 - 32 = 96$, which is not a power of 2).
3
When dividing powers with the same base, subtract the exponents: $2^{7-5} = 2^2$.
Perform the subtraction of the exponents to get the final power.

Answer: $2^2$ (Option B)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A digital clock displays time in a unique way. The number of hours in a cycle is \(y^5\). The number of minutes in an hour is \(y^3\). The number of seconds in a minute is \(y^2\). How many seconds are in a full cycle, expressed as a power of \(y\)?
A. \(y^{30}\)
B. $3y^{10}$
C. \(y^7\)
D. \(y^{10}\)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
To find the total number of seconds in a cycle, multiply the number of hours by the number of minutes per hour by the number of seconds per minute.
This is a unit conversion problem where each unit is expressed as a power.
2
Total seconds = (hours in cycle) \(\times\) (minutes in hour) \(\times\) (seconds in minute) = \(y^5 \times y^3 \times y^2\).
Substitute the given expressions into the multiplication setup.
3
When multiplying exponents with the same base, add the powers: \(y^{5+3+2}\).
The rule \(a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}\) extends to multiple terms.
4
$5+3+2 = 10$, so the simplified expression is \(y^{10}\).
Perform the addition of the exponents to get the final power.

Answer: \(y^{10}\) (Option D)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
The population density of a certain microorganism in a solution can be described by the expression \((m^2)^{-3}\). What is the simplified form of this expression with a positive exponent?

D. $1/m^6$
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The expression is \((m^2)^{-3}\).
This expression involves a power raised to a negative power.
2
Multiply the exponents: \(m^{2 \times (-3)} = m^{-6}\).
This is a fundamental rule of exponents ($ (a^m)^n = a^{m \times n} $). Remember to apply integer multiplication rules for the exponents.
3
Rewrite the expression with a positive exponent by taking the reciprocal: $1/m^6$.
This is a fundamental rule of exponents (\(a^{-n} = 1/a^n\) for non-zero \(a\)).

Answer: $1/m^6$ (Option D)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A tech company is designing a new data storage system. They determined that the storage capacity of one unit is \((a^4 \cdot a^2) / a^3\) gigabytes. Simplify this expression.
A. \(a^6\)
B. \(a^3\)
C. \(a^9\)
D. \(a^5\)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
\(a^4 \cdot a^2 = a^{4+2} = a^6\).
When multiplying powers with the same base, add their exponents.
2
The expression becomes \(a^6 / a^3\).
Substitute the simplified numerator back into the original expression.
3
When dividing powers with the same base, subtract the exponents: \(a^{6-3} = a^3\).
Perform the subtraction of the exponents to get the final power.

Answer: \(a^3\) (Option B)
