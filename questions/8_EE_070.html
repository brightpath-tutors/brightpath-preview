Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A cashier is tallying the cost of 'x' identical souvenir keychains and a fixed service charge. The total cost is represented by the expression 7x + 21. Factor this expression to show the price per keychain and the quantity related to the service charge.
A. 7(x + 3)
B. 7(x + 7)
C. 7x(1 + 3)
D. x(7 + 21)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The expression is 7x + 21. The terms are 7x and 21.
Break down the expression into its individual terms.
2
The GCF of 7x and 21 is 7.
The GCF is the largest factor that divides all terms in the expression.
3
Divide each term by the GCF: 7x / 7 = x 21 / 7 = 3
Divide each term by the GCF to find the remaining terms inside the parentheses.
4
Place the GCF outside the parentheses and the results of the division inside: 7(x + 3)
The factored form is the product of the GCF and the sum/difference of the divided terms.

Answer: 7(x + 3) (Option A)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A network's data transfer rate, in megabytes per second, is given by the polynomial expression 4t^3 - 16t^2 + 12t, where 't' is time in seconds. Factor this expression completely to analyze its components.
A. 4t(t^2 - 4t + 3)
B. t(4t^2 - 16t + 12)
C. 4t(t + 1)(t + 3)
D. 4t(t - 1)(t - 3)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms are 4t^3, -16t^2, and 12t. The GCF is 4t.
Find the greatest common factor for all terms in the polynomial.
2
4t(4t^3/4t - 16t^2/4t + 12t/4t) 4t(t^2 - 4t + 3)
Divide each term by the GCF and write the remaining expression inside parentheses.
3
For t^2 - 4t + 3, find two numbers that multiply to 3 and add to -4. These numbers are -1 and -3. So, t^2 - 4t + 3 = (t - 1)(t - 3).
Factor the trinomial by finding two binomials whose product is the trinomial.
4
4t(t - 1)(t - 3)
Combine the GCF with the factored trinomial to get the final completely factored expression.

Answer: 4t(t - 1)(t - 3) (Option D)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE

A. 5(2h - 3)(2h + 3)
B. 5(2h - 3)^2
C. (10h - 9)(2h + 5)
D. 20(h^2 - 2.25)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms are 20h^2 and 45. The GCF is 5.
Find the greatest common factor for all terms in the expression.
2
5(20h^2/5 - 45/5) 5(4h^2 - 9)
Divide each term by the GCF and write the remaining expression inside parentheses.
3
The expression 4h^2 - 9 is a difference of squares: (2h)^2 - 3^2. Using the formula a^2 - b^2 = (a - b)(a + b): (2h - 3)(2h + 3)
Recognize that the remaining binomial is in the form of a difference of squares and apply the corresponding factorization formula.
4
5(2h - 3)(2h + 3)
Combine the GCF with the factored difference of squares to get the final completely factored expression.

Answer: 5(2h - 3)(2h + 3) (Option A)
