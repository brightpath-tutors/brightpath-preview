Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A chef is preparing a large meal and needs to combine two batches of sauce. Batch A has 3 containers, each holding 2.5 liters of sauce. Batch B has 2 containers, each holding 2.5 liters of sauce. To find the total amount of sauce, the chef adds (3 × 2.5) + (2 × 2.5). Which property of operations allows the chef to simplify this to (3 + 2) × 2.5?
A. Commutative Property
B. Distributive Property
C. Associative Property
D. Identity Property
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Expression: (3 × 2.5) + (2 × 2.5)
The problem provides two terms being added, where each term is a product of two numbers.
2
Both terms (3 × 2.5) and (2 × 2.5) share a common factor of 2.5.
This common factor is key to applying the distributive property.
3
According to the distributive property, a × b + c × b = (a + c) × b. So, (3 × 2.5) + (2 × 2.5) can be rewritten as (3 + 2) × 2.5.
The distributive property allows us to 'factor out' the common multiplier from the sum of products.
4
The property used is the Distributive Property.
This property simplifies calculations by combining the multipliers before performing the final multiplication.

Answer: Distributive Property (Option B)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A financial analyst is tracking stock performance. A particular stock increased by $1.25, then decreased by $0.75, then increased by $2.50. The total change is calculated as (1.25 - 0.75) + 2.50. Which property of operations could be used to rearrange this calculation to 1.25 + (2.50 - 0.75) to make it easier to group the increases and then subtract the decrease?
A. Commutative Property of Addition
B. Associative Property of Addition
C. Identity Property of Addition
D. Distributive Property
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Original expression: (1.25 - 0.75) + 2.50. This can be viewed as (1.25 + (-0.75)) + 2.50.
Subtraction can be rewritten as addition of a negative number, which is important for applying properties of addition.
2
Target expression: 1.25 + (2.50 - 0.75). This can be viewed as 1.25 + (2.50 + (-0.75)).
Similarly, rewrite subtraction as addition of a negative.
3
The numbers involved are 1.25, -0.75, and 2.50. The order of these numbers is changed, and the grouping of the additions is changed. Specifically, the -0.75 moves from being grouped with 1.25 to being grouped with 2.50. The actual order of the numbers in the sequence (1.25, -0.75, 2.50) is maintained, but the order of operations changes, effectively shifting the -0.75 to be added later.
This rearrangement of grouping while maintaining the sequence of terms points to the Associative Property when viewed as addition of signed numbers. The Commutative Property would change the order of the actual numbers being operated on, e.g. 1.25 + (-0.75) = (-0.75) + 1.25.
4
Since we are essentially re-grouping the terms for addition (even if some terms are negative), the Associative Property of Addition applies. (a + b) + c = a + (b + c). In this case, if a = 1.25, b = -0.75, and c = 2.50, then (1.25 + (-0.75)) + 2.50 becomes 1.25 + (-0.75 + 2.50), which is 1.25 + (2.50 - 0.75).
The Associative Property allows us to change how terms are grouped in a sum without altering the final result. Although it appears the -0.75 'moved', it's actually the grouping of the addition that changed.

Answer: Associative Property of Addition (Option B)
