Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Sarah wants to buy a new video game that costs $45. She has $12 saved and earns $5 per hour babysitting. How many hours, h, must Sarah work to have at least enough money to buy the game?
A. h >= 57
B. h <= 57
C. h >= 6.6
D. h <= 6.6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
5h + 12 >= 45
Sarah's earnings (5h) plus her savings ($12) must be greater than or equal to the cost of the game ($45).
2
5h + 12 - 12 >= 45 - 12 5h >= 33
This isolates the term representing her earnings.
3
5h / 5 >= 33 / 5 h >= 6.6
Dividing by a positive number does not change the direction of the inequality. Sarah must work at least 6.6 hours. In a real-world context where only whole hours can be paid, she would need to work at least 7 hours.

Answer: h >= 6.6 (Option C)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
A basketball team needs to score at least 80 points to win their current game. They currently have 35 points and score 3 points for each successful shot. How many successful shots, s, do they need to make to win?
A. s >= 45
B. s <= 15
C. s >= 115
D. s >= 15
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
3s + 35 >= 80
The points from successful shots (3s) plus their current points (35) must be greater than or equal to 80 points.
2
3s + 35 - 35 >= 80 - 35 3s >= 45
This isolates the term with the variable.
3
3s / 3 >= 45 / 3 s >= 15
Dividing by a positive number does not change the direction of the inequality. The team needs to make at least 15 successful shots.

Answer: s >= 15 (Option D)

Question 3Grade 8 • EE
A recipe requires at most 180 grams of sugar. You have already added 75 grams and will add more in portions of 15 grams. How many portions, p, can you add?
A. p <= 7
B. p <= 17
C. p >= 7
D. p <= 255
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
15p + 75 <= 180
The amount of sugar from additional portions (15p) plus the already added amount (75) must be less than or equal to 180 grams.
2
15p + 75 - 75 <= 180 - 75 15p <= 105
This isolates the term with the variable.
3
15p / 15 <= 105 / 15 p <= 7
Dividing by a positive number does not change the direction of the inequality. You can add at most 7 portions.

Answer: p <= 7 (Option A)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A car rental company charges $30 per day plus $0.25 per mile. If a traveler wants to spend no more than $100 on a one-day rental, how many miles, m, can they drive?
A. m <= 70.25
B. m <= 280
C. m <= 17.5
D. m >= 280
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
0.25m + 30 <= 100
The cost per mile (0.25m) plus the daily charge ($30) must be less than or equal to the maximum budget ($100).
2
0.25m + 30 - 30 <= 100 - 30 0.25m <= 70
This isolates the term with the variable.
3
0.25m / 0.25 <= 70 / 0.25 m <= 280
Dividing by a positive number does not change the direction of the inequality. The traveler can drive at most 280 miles.

Answer: m <= 280 (Option B)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE
To pass a course, a student must have an average score of at least 75 on four tests. The scores on the first three tests are 68, 82, and 70. What is the minimum score, x, the student must get on the fourth test?
A. x <= 80
B. x >= 25
C. x >= 80
D. x >= 375
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
68 + 82 + 70 = 220
This is the current total score before the fourth test.
2
(220 + x) / 4 >= 75
The sum of all four test scores divided by 4 must be greater than or equal to 75.
3
(220 + x) / 4 4 >= 75 4 220 + x >= 300
This removes the division and simplifies the inequality.
4
220 + x - 220 >= 300 - 220 x >= 80
Subtracting a positive number does not change the direction of the inequality. The student needs at least an 80 on the fourth test.

Answer: x >= 80 (Option C)

Question 6Grade 8 • EE
A scientist needs to maintain a chemical solution's temperature below 15°C. The current temperature is 28°C and it decreases by 3°C every hour, h, due to a cooling agent. How many hours must the cooling agent run?
A. h > 4.33
B. h < 4.33
C. h < -4.33
D. h > -4.33
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
28 - 3h < 15
The current temperature (28) minus the temperature decrease (3h) must be less than 15°C.
2
28 - 3h - 28 < 15 - 28 -3h < -13
This isolates the term with the variable.
3
-3h / -3 > -13 / -3 h > 4.333...
When dividing or multiplying an inequality by a negative number, the direction of the inequality sign must be reversed. The cooling agent must run for more than 4.33 hours.

Answer: h > 4.33 (Option A)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A construction worker needs to cut pieces of wood that are 2.5 feet long from a plank that is 20 feet long. He also needs to remove a 1.5-foot damaged section from the plank. How many 2.5-foot pieces, p, can he get from the plank after removing the damaged section?
A. p >= 7.4
B. p <= 7.4
C. p <= 8.6
D. p <= 46.25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
2.5p + 1.5 <= 20
The total length of the pieces (2.5p) plus the damaged section (1.5) must be less than or equal to the total plank length (20).
2
2.5p + 1.5 - 1.5 <= 20 - 1.5 2.5p <= 18.5
This isolates the term with the variable.
3
2.5p / 2.5 <= 18.5 / 2.5 p <= 7.4
Dividing by a positive number does not change the direction of the inequality. The worker can get at most 7.4 pieces. Practically, this means he can get 7 full pieces.

Answer: p <= 7.4 (Option B)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A project manager needs to complete a task in less than 48 hours. She has already spent 10 hours on it and estimates that she can complete the remaining work at a rate of 4.75 hours per day. How many additional days, d, can she take to finish the task?
A. d < 8
B. d < 12.21
C. d > 8
D. d < 180.5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
4.75d + 10 < 48
The time spent on the remaining work (4.75d) plus the hours already spent (10) must be less than 48 hours.
2
4.75d + 10 - 10 < 48 - 10 4.75d < 38
This isolates the term with the variable.
3
4.75d / 4.75 < 38 / 4.75 d < 8
Dividing by a positive number does not change the direction of the inequality. She can take less than 8 additional days.

Answer: d < 8 (Option A)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
The population of a small town is currently 7,500 people. It is decreasing by 60 people per year. A local researcher predicts that the town's population will fall below 6,000 in 'y' years. What is the minimum number of years for this to happen?
A. y < 25
B. y < -25
C. y > -25
D. y > 25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
7500 - 60y < 6000
The current population (7500) minus the decrease over 'y' years (60y) must be less than 6000.
2
7500 - 60y - 7500 < 6000 - 7500 -60y < -1500
This isolates the term with the variable.
3
-60y / -60 > -1500 / -60 y > 25
When dividing or multiplying an inequality by a negative number, the direction of the inequality sign must be reversed. The population will fall below 6000 after more than 25 years (i.e., starting from the 26th year).

Answer: y > 25 (Option D)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A nutritionist recommends consuming at most 2,000 calories per day. If a person has already consumed 750 calories and each serving of their favorite snack contains 125 calories, how many servings, s, of the snack can they eat?
A. s >= 10
B. s <= 2750
C. s <= 15.625
D. s <= 10
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
125s + 750 <= 2000
The calories from the snack (125s) plus the already consumed calories (750) must be less than or equal to 2000.
2
125s + 750 - 750 <= 2000 - 750 125s <= 1250
This isolates the term with the variable.
3
125s / 125 <= 1250 / 125 s <= 10
Dividing by a positive number does not change the direction of the inequality. The person can eat at most 10 servings of the snack.

Answer: s <= 10 (Option D)
