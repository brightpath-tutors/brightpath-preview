A. 5 × (12 - 7)
B. 5 × 12 - 7
C. 5 + 12 - 7
D. 12 ÷ 5 - 7
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
stickers per pack = 12, number of packs = 5
Identify the numbers and the operation for the total stickers bought initially.
2
total stickers bought = 5 × 12
Formulate the multiplication part of the expression to find the total stickers bought.
3
stickers used = 7
Identify the number of stickers Sophia used and the operation to find the remaining stickers.
4

Question 8Grade 5 • OA
A family budgeted $1,200 for their vacation. They spent $350 on flights, and then for 5 days, they spent $90 each day on accommodation. Which expression shows the amount of money they have left from their budget?
A. 1200 - 350 + 5 × 90
B. 1200 - (350 + 5 + 90)
C. (1200 - 350 - 5) × 90
D. 1200 - 350 - 5 × 90
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
total budget = $1,200
Identify the initial budget amount.
2
spent on flights = $350
Identify the amount spent on flights.
3
days for accommodation = 5, cost per day = $90
Identify the number of days and the daily cost for accommodation.
4
total spent on accommodation = 5 × 90
Formulate the multiplication for the total cost of accommodation.
5
money left = 1200 - 350 - (5 × 90)
Subtract the flight cost and then the total accommodation cost from the budget. Parentheses are implicit for order of operations (multiplication before subtraction).
6
final expression = 1200 - 350 - 5 × 90
State the final expression, understanding multiplication takes precedence.

Answer: 1200 - 350 - 5 × 90 (Option D)
