Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Sarah and Emily are saving money. Sarah starts with $20 and saves $5 each week. Emily starts with $10 and saves $7 each week. In how many weeks will they have the same amount of money?
A. 3 weeks
B. 15 weeks
C. 5 weeks
D. 2 weeks
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'w' be the number of weeks. Sarah's money: 20 + 5w Emily's money: 10 + 7w Equation: 20 + 5w = 10 + 7w
We represent the amount of money each person has as an expression and set them equal to find when they have the same amount.
2
20 + 5w - 5w = 10 + 7w - 5w 20 = 10 + 2w
This isolates the variable term on one side of the equation.
3
20 - 10 = 10 + 2w - 10 10 = 2w
This isolates the variable term completely.
4
10 / 2 = 2w / 2 w = 5
This solves for the number of weeks.

Answer: 5 weeks (Option C)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
A gym offers two membership options. Option A costs $30 per month plus an $8 enrollment fee. Option B costs $25 per month plus a $28 enrollment fee. After how many months will the total cost of both options be the same?
A. 2 months
B. 4 months
C. 5 months
D. 6 months
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'm' be the number of months. Cost of Option A: 30m + 8 Cost of Option B: 25m + 28 Equation: 30m + 8 = 25m + 28
We represent the total cost for each option as an expression and set them equal to find when the costs are the same.
2
30m + 8 - 25m = 25m + 28 - 25m 5m + 8 = 28
This isolates the variable term on one side of the equation.
3
5m + 8 - 8 = 28 - 8 5m = 20
This isolates the variable term completely.
4
5m / 5 = 20 / 5 m = 4
This solves for the number of months.

Answer: 4 months (Option B)

Question 3Grade 8 • EE
A chef is mixing two types of spices. Spice A costs $6 per ounce, and Spice B costs $9 per ounce. He wants to create a blend of 12 ounces that costs $8 per ounce. How many ounces of Spice A should he use?
A. 4 ounces
B. 6 ounces
C. 8 ounces
D. 5 ounces
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the amount of Spice A in ounces. Then (12 - x) is the amount of Spice B in ounces. Cost of Spice A: 6x Cost of Spice B: 9(12 - x) Total cost of blend: 12 * 8 = 96 Equation: 6x + 9(12 - x) = 96
We express the total cost of each spice type based on the amount used and sum them to equal the total cost of the blend.
2
6x + 108 - 9x = 96
Apply the distributive property to simplify the equation.
3
-3x + 108 = 96
Combine the 'x' terms.
4
-3x + 108 - 108 = 96 - 108 -3x = -12
Isolate the variable term.
5
-3x / -3 = -12 / -3 x = 4
Solve for 'x', the amount of Spice A.

Answer: 4 ounces (Option A)

C. 10 hours
D. 0.5 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1

We represent the distance traveled by each car as an expression and set them equal to find when Car B catches up to Car A.
2
10 + 50h - 50h = 60h - 50h 10 = 10h

3
10 / 10 = 10h / 10 h = 1
This solves for the number of hours.
