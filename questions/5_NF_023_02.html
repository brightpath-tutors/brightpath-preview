Question 1Grade 5 • NF
Sarah has $1/2 of a dollar. She wants to buy candy that costs $1/10 of a dollar per piece. Which equation represents how many pieces of candy (x) she can buy?
A. (1/10) ÷ (1/2) = x
B. (1/2) * (1/10) = x
C. (1/2) + (1/10) = x
D. (1/2) ÷ (1/10) = x
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total amount Sarah has = $1/2
Identify the total amount of money Sarah has.
2
Cost per piece of candy = $1/10
Identify the cost of one piece of candy.
3
Operation needed: Division
To find out how many pieces of candy can be bought, divide the total amount of money by the cost per piece.
4
(1/2) ÷ (1/10) = x
Set up the equation with the total amount as the dividend and the cost per piece as the divisor, representing the number of pieces as 'x'.

Answer: (1/2) ÷ (1/10) = x (Option D)
