Question 1Grade 7 • EE
A shop sells 5 items at \(x\) dollars each and adds a $2 charge for a bag. The total cost is represented by the expression $5x + 2$. Which of the following is the simplest equivalent expression for the total cost?
A. 7x
B. 5(x+2)
C. 2x + 5
D. 5x + 2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The expression is $5x + 2$. The terms are $5x$ and $2$.
Identify all parts of the expression separated by addition or subtraction.
2
$5x$ is a term with a variable (\(x\)), and $2$ is a constant term.
Like terms have the same variable raised to the same power. A variable term and a constant term are not like terms.
3
Since there are no like terms to combine, the expression $5x + 2$ is already in its simplest equivalent form.
If there are no like terms, the expression cannot be simplified further.

Answer: 5x + 2 (Option D)

Question 2Grade 7 • EE
A soccer team's performance can be described by the expression \(s + 3s\), where \(s\) represents the number of points from goals scored in the first half and $3s$ represents the points from goals scored in the second half. Which of the following is the simplest equivalent expression for the total points scored?
A. s+3
B. 4s
C. 3s^2
D. 3+s
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms in the expression are \(s\) and $3s$.
Both terms contain the variable \(s\) raised to the power of 1, so they are like terms.
2
Treat \(s\) as $1s$. So, $1s + 3s = (1+3)s = 4s$.
To combine like terms, add their coefficients while keeping the variable part the same.
3
The expression $4s$ is in its simplest equivalent form.
There are no other terms to combine.

Answer: 4s (Option B)

Question 3Grade 7 • EE
A chef is preparing a dish and uses an amount of spice represented by \(q\) units. For extra flavor, he plans to add two more portions of \(q\) units each, plus an additional 7 units of a different spice. The total amount of spice is represented by the expression \(q + 2q + 7\). Which of the following is the simplest equivalent expression?
A. 3q + 7
B. 10q
C. 3(q+7)
D. q+9
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms are \(q\), $2q$, and $7$. The like terms are \(q\) and $2q$.
Terms with the same variable (\(q\)) raised to the same power (1) are like terms.
2
Treat \(q\) as $1q$. So, $1q + 2q = (1+2)q = 3q$.
Add the coefficients of the like terms.
3
The expression becomes $3q + 7$.
The constant term $7$ cannot be combined with $3q$ as they are unlike terms.
4
The expression $3q + 7$ cannot be simplified further.
The expression is in its simplest form when all like terms are combined.

Answer: 3q + 7 (Option A)

Question 4Grade 7 • EE
A cyclist records their journey in two parts. The first part is 2 hours at \(v\) mph. The second part is 3 hours at a speed 7 mph faster than the first part. The total distance traveled in miles is represented by the expression $2v + 3(v+7)$. Which of the following is the simplest equivalent expression?
A. 5v + 7
B. 5v + 21
C. 6v + 21
D. 5v + 35
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
For the second part of the journey, $3(v+7) = 3 \times v + 3 \times 7 = 3v + 21$.
Multiply the term outside the parentheses by each term inside the parentheses.
2
The total distance expression becomes $2v + 3v + 21$.
Substitute the distributed term back into the expression.
3
The like terms are $2v$ and $3v$. The constant term is $21$.
Identify terms that have the same variable part.
4
$2v + 3v = (2+3)v = 5v$.
Add the coefficients of the like terms.
5
The expression becomes $5v + 21$. This cannot be simplified further.
The variable term and the constant term are unlike terms and cannot be combined.

Answer: 5v + 21 (Option B)

$4k + 2k = (4+2)k = 6k$.

3
The expression becomes $6k + 8$.

Question 6Grade 7 • EE
In a chemical reaction, the amount of reactant A used is \(x\) grams. Reactant B used is 3 grams more than twice the amount of reactant A. Reactant C used is 5 grams less than reactant B. The total mass of all three reactants can be represented by the expression \(x + (2x+3) + ((2x+3)-5)\). Which of the following is the simplest equivalent expression for the total mass in grams?
A. 3x - 2
B. 5x + 8
C. 5x + 1
D. 5x - 2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
\((2x+3)-5 = 2x + (3-5) = 2x - 2\).
Combine the constant terms within the parentheses first.
2
The total mass expression becomes \(x + (2x+3) + (2x-2)\).
Replace the simplified part back into the main expression.
3
\(x + 2x + 3 + 2x - 2\).
Since there's addition before the parentheses, they can simply be removed.
4
The like terms with variables are \(x\), $2x$, and $2x$. The constant terms are $3$ and $-2$.
Group terms with the same variable and terms that are just numbers.
5
\(x + 2x + 2x = (1+2+2)x = 5x\).
Add the coefficients of the variable terms.
6
$3 - 2 = 1$.
Perform the arithmetic operation on the constant terms.
7
The expression becomes $5x + 1$. This cannot be simplified further.
The variable term and the constant term are unlike terms.

Answer: 5x + 1 (Option C)

Question 7Grade 7 • EE
A landscaper is using two types of decorative rocks. For the first area, he uses \(r\) bags of river rocks. For the second area, he uses 4 fewer bags of river rocks than the first, and also 6 bags of polished pebbles. The total number of bags of rocks and pebbles is represented by the expression \(r + (r-4) + 6\). Which of the following is the simplest equivalent expression for the total number of bags?
A. 2r + 10
B. r + 2
C. 2r - 10
D. 2r + 2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The expression is \(r + r - 4 + 6\).
Since the parentheses are preceded by an addition sign, they can be removed directly.
2
The like terms are \(r\) and \(r\). The constant terms are $-4$ and $6$.
Group terms with the same variable part and terms that are just numbers.
3
\(r + r = 1r + 1r = (1+1)r = 2r\).
Add the coefficients of the like terms.
4
$-4 + 6 = 2$.
Perform the arithmetic operation on the constant terms.
5
The expression becomes $2r + 2$. This cannot be simplified further.
The variable term and the constant term are unlike terms.

Answer: 2r + 2 (Option D)

Question 8Grade 7 • EE
A student tracks their study time. On Monday, they study for \(h\) hours. On Tuesday, they study for half of Monday's time plus an extra 1.5 hours. On Wednesday, they study for twice the amount of time they studied on Tuesday. The total study time for the three days is represented by the expression \(h + (0.5h + 1.5) + 2(0.5h + 1.5)\). Which of the following is the simplest equivalent expression for the total study time in hours?
A. 2.5h + 3
B. 2.5h + 4.5
C. 3h + 4.5
D. h + 1.5 + 2(0.5h + 1.5)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
$2(0.5h + 1.5) = 2 \times 0.5h + 2 \times 1.5 = 1h + 3$.
Multiply the factor outside the parentheses by each term inside the parentheses.
2
The total study time expression becomes \(h + 0.5h + 1.5 + h + 3\).
Substitute the distributed term and remove other parentheses, as they are preceded by addition.
3
The like terms with variables are \(h\), $0.5h$, and \(h\). The constant terms are $1.5$ and $3$.
Group terms that share the same variable part and terms that are constants.
4
\(h + 0.5h + h = 1h + 0.5h + 1h = (1+0.5+1)h = 2.5h\).
Add the coefficients of the variable terms.
5
$1.5 + 3 = 4.5$.
Add the constant terms.
6
The expression becomes $2.5h + 4.5$. This cannot be simplified further.
The variable term and the constant term are unlike terms.

Answer: 2.5h + 4.5 (Option B)

Question 9Grade 7 • EE
The population of a town, in thousands, is initially \(P\). It is projected to increase by 15% next year. In two years, it is expected to decrease by 5% of its original population, and then gain an additional 2 thousand people. The projected population in two years is represented by the expression \(P + 0.15P - 0.05P + 2\). Which of the following is the simplest equivalent expression for this projected population?
A. 1.10P + 2
B. 1.2P + 2
C. P + 0.10P + 2
D. 1.15P + 2
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms with variables are \(P\), $0.15P$, and $-0.05P$. The constant term is $2$.
Group terms that have the same variable part and terms that are just numbers.
2
Treat \(P\) as $1P$. So, $1P + 0.15P - 0.05P = (1 + 0.15 - 0.05)P$.
Factor out the common variable \(P\) and perform arithmetic on the coefficients.
3
$1 + 0.15 = 1.15$. Then $1.15 - 0.05 = 1.10$.
Carefully add and subtract the decimal coefficients.
4
The combined variable term is $1.10P$.
Place the combined coefficient back with the variable.
5
The expression becomes $1.10P + 2$. This cannot be simplified further.
The variable term and the constant term are unlike terms.

Answer: 1.10P + 2 (Option A)

Question 10Grade 7 • EE
A tech company charges \(R\) dollars for a basic software license. They offer a premium package that costs $1.5$ times the basic license fee, plus an additional $25 for support. If a customer buys 2 basic licenses and 1 premium package, the total cost is represented by the expression $2R + (1.5R + 25)$. Which of the following is the simplest equivalent expression for the total cost in dollars?
A. 4.5R + 25
B. 3.5R + 50
C. 3.5R + 25
D. 2R + 1.5R + 25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The expression is $2R + 1.5R + 25$.
Since the parentheses are preceded by an addition sign, they can be removed directly.
2
The like terms are $2R$ and $1.5R$. The constant term is $25$.
Group terms that have the same variable part and terms that are just numbers.
3
$2R + 1.5R = (2+1.5)R = 3.5R$.
Add the coefficients of the variable terms.
4
The expression becomes $3.5R + 25$. This cannot be simplified further.
The variable term and the constant term are unlike terms.

Answer: 3.5R + 25 (Option C)
