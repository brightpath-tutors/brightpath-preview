Question 1Grade 6 • EE
A square-shaped baseball field has an area of 81 square meters. What is the length of one side of the field?
A. 81 meters
B. 162 meters
C. 9 meters
D. 40.5 meters
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Area = side × side (s²). We are given Area = 81 square meters. We need to find 's'.
The area of a square is found by squaring its side length. To find the side length when given the area, we need to calculate the square root of the area.
2
s = √81 = 9
The square root of 81 is 9, because 9 multiplied by 9 equals 81.
3
The length of one side of the field is 9 meters.
Always include the correct units in the final answer.

Answer: 9 meters (Option C)

Question 8Grade 6 • EE
A small town's population density can be modeled as a square area. If the town covers 200 square kilometers, what is the approximate length of one side of the town, rounded to the nearest whole number?
A. 15 kilometers
B. 400 kilometers
C. 100 kilometers
D. 14 kilometers
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Area = 200 sq km. To find the side length 's', we need to calculate √Area and round to the nearest whole number.
To find the side length of a square given its area, you must calculate the square root of the area. Since it's an approximation, we'll need to round.
2
We know 14² = 196 and 15² = 225. So, √200 is between 14 and 15. Since 200 is closer to 196 (difference of 4) than to 225 (difference of 25), √200 is closer to 14.
By comparing the given area to known perfect squares, we can estimate the range of the square root. Then, compare the differences to determine the closest whole number.
3
√200 ≈ 14.14. Rounded to the nearest whole number, this is 14.
Using a calculator, the square root of 200 is approximately 14.14. Rounding to the nearest whole number means looking at the first decimal place. Since 1 is less than 5, we round down to 14.
4
The approximate length of one side of the town is 14 kilometers.
The length is measured in kilometers.

Answer: 14 kilometers (Option D)
