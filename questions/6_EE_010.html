Question 1Grade 6 • EE
Sarah bought 4 identical notebooks and a pencil. The pencil cost $1.50. If her total bill was $21.50, what was the cost of one notebook?
A. $23.00
B. $5.38
C. $5.00
D. $20.00
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'n' be the cost of one notebook. 4n + 1.50 = 21.50
We know the cost of 4 notebooks (4n) plus the cost of the pencil ($1.50) equals the total bill ($21.50).
2
4n + 1.50 - 1.50 = 21.50 - 1.50 4n = 20
To isolate the term with 'n', we perform the inverse operation of adding 1.50, which is subtracting 1.50 from both sides of the equation.
3
4n / 4 = 20 / 4 n = 5
To find the value of 'n', we perform the inverse operation of multiplying by 4, which is dividing by 4 on both sides of the equation.
4
The cost of one notebook is $5.00.
The variable 'n' represents the cost of one notebook.

Answer: $5.00 (Option C)

1

Add the calories from breakfast and lunch to find the total calories consumed so far.
2

Subtract the consumed calories from the daily goal to find how many more calories can be eaten.
3
Let 's' be the calories per snack. 3s = 830
The remaining calories (830) must be divided equally among 3 snacks (3s).
4

Each snack can contain approximately 276.67 calories.

Question 10Grade 6 • EE

In the second week, they sold three times the number of units from the first week, but then had to process a certain number of returns. A. 150 units
B. 250 units
C. 750 units
D. 450 units
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Second week sales (gross) = 300 (first week) * 3 = 900 units
Multiply the first week's sales by 3 to get the gross sales for the second week.
2
Let 'R' be the number of returned units. 300 (first week) + (900 - R) (second week net) = 1050 (total net)
The sum of the first week's sales and the second week's sales (gross minus returns) equals the total net sales over two weeks.
3
1200 - R = 1050
Add the sales from the first week and the gross sales from the second week.
4
1200 - R - 1200 = 1050 - 1200 -R = -150
To isolate '-R', subtract 1200 from both sides of the equation.
5
-R (-1) = -150 (-1) R = 150
Multiply by -1 to change -R to R.
6
150 units were returned in the second week.
The variable 'R' represents the number of units returned.

Answer: 150 units (Option A)
