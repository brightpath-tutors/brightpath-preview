Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A store sells 3 pencils for $2. If you want to buy 12 pencils, how much will it cost?
A. $4
B. $8
C. $18
D. $6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Given ratio: 3 pencils : $2. Target: 12 pencils.
We need to find the cost for a larger quantity of pencils, maintaining the same ratio.
2
Scale factor = (Desired number of pencils) / (Original number of pencils) = 12 / 3 = 4.
The scale factor tells us how many times larger the new quantity is compared to the original.
3
New cost = (Original cost) (Scale factor) = $2 4 = $8.
To maintain the proportionality, we must multiply the original cost by the same scale factor.

Answer: $8 (Option B)

2

Question 10Grade 6 • RP
A drone uploads a video file at a rate of 120 megabytes (MB) in 30 seconds. How many megabytes can it upload in 2.5 minutes?
A. 600 MB
B. 250 MB
C. 300 MB
D. 24 MB
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Given ratio: 120 MB : 30 seconds. Target time: 2.5 minutes. Convert 2.5 minutes to seconds: 2.5 * 60 = 150 seconds.
It's crucial for the units to be consistent when working with ratios. Convert minutes to seconds before calculating the scale factor.
2
Scale factor = (Desired time in seconds) / (Original time in seconds) = 150 / 30 = 5.
The scale factor tells us how many times longer the new upload time is compared to the original.
3
New megabytes = (Original megabytes) (Scale factor) = 120 MB 5 = 600 MB.
To maintain the proportionality, we must multiply the original amount of megabytes by the same scale factor.

Answer: 600 MB (Option A)
