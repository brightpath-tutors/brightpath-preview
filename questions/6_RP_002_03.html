Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A baker uses 2 cups of sugar for every 3 cups of flour in a recipe. What is the ratio of sugar to flour?
A. 3-2
B. 3:2
C. 2:3
D. 2+3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Sugar = 2 cups, Flour = 3 cups
Identify the quantities of sugar and flour given in the problem.
2
Ratio of sugar to flour = 2 : 3
Express the relationship as a ratio, placing the quantity of sugar first as requested by 'sugar to flour'.
3
GCD(2, 3) = 1, so 2:3 is already in simplest form.
Check if the ratio can be simplified. Since 2 and 3 share no common factors other than 1, the ratio is already in simplest form.

Answer: 2:3 (Option C)

Question 4Grade 6 • RP

D. 0.80:1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cost = $2.40, Pounds = 3
Identify the total cost and the quantity in pounds.
2
Ratio of cost to pounds = $2.40 : 3 pounds
Express the relationship as a ratio, with cost first and pounds second.

Simplified ratio = 0.80 : 1
Rewrite the ratio with the unit cost for 1 pound to express it in simplest form.

Question 5Grade 6 • RP
In a basketball game, a player scored 18 points per 15 shots taken. What is the ratio of points to shots, in simplest form?
A. 18/15
B. 15:18
C. 6:5
D. 5:6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Points = 18, Shots = 15
Identify the number of points scored and the number of shots taken.
2
Ratio of points to shots = 18 : 15
Form the initial ratio as points to shots.
3
GCD(18, 15) = 3
Find the greatest common divisor (GCD) of 18 and 15 to simplify the ratio.
4
Simplified ratio = 18/3 : 15/3 = 6 : 5
Divide both parts of the ratio by their GCD to express it in simplest form.

Answer: 6:5 (Option C)
