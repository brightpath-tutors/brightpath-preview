Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A toy car is a miniature replica of a real car. The real car is 180 inches long. The toy car is 15 inches long. What is the scale factor of the toy car to the real car, in simplest form?
A. 12
B. 15/180
C. 1/10
D. 1/12
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Real car length = 180 inches, Toy car length = 15 inches.
To find the scale factor of the toy car to the real car, we need to compare their lengths.
2
Scale Factor = (Toy Car Length) / (Real Car Length) = 15 / 180.
The scale factor is defined as the ratio of the scaled dimension (toy car) to the original dimension (real car).
3
15 ÷ 15 = 1, 180 ÷ 15 = 12. So, 15/180 = 1/12.
Divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor, which is 15, to simplify the fraction to its simplest form.
4
The scale factor is 1/12.
The simplified ratio represents the scale factor.

Answer: 1/12 (Option D)

Question 3Grade 6 • RP

A. 3 feet
B. 20 feet
C. 23 feet
D. 60 feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Scale: 1 inch = 20 feet.
The scale tells us how many real-world units correspond to one unit on the map.
2
Sandbox on map = 3 inches.
This is the dimension given on the map that we need to convert to actual length.
3
Actual Length = Map Length × Scale = 3 inches × 20 feet/inch.

5
The actual sandbox is 60 feet long.

Question 8Grade 6 • RP
An artist is creating a mural based on a sketch. The sketch is 15 inches wide and 20 inches tall. If the mural will be 60 inches wide, what will be its height?
A. 80 inches
B. 45 inches
C. 16 inches
D. 75 inches
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Sketch: 15 inches wide, 20 inches tall. Mural: 60 inches wide.
We are given the original dimensions and one dimension of the scaled version. We need to find the other scaled dimension.
2
Scale Factor = (Mural Width) / (Sketch Width) = 60 inches / 15 inches = 4.
Since the sketch is being enlarged to a mural, we find the scale factor by dividing the mural's width by the sketch's width.
3
Mural Height = (Sketch Height) × Scale Factor = 20 inches × 4.
To maintain proportionality, the same scale factor must be applied to the height of the sketch to find the height of the mural.
4
20 × 4 = 80 inches.
Complete the multiplication to find the mural's height.
5
The mural will be 80 inches tall.
This is the final height of the scaled mural.

Answer: 80 inches (Option A)

Question 9Grade 6 • RP

A. 67500 square pixels
B. 45000 square pixels
C. 30000 square pixels
D. 90000 square pixels
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Original dimensions: 200 pixels by 150 pixels. Scale factor = 1.5.
We need to first find the new dimensions before calculating the area.
2
New Width = Original Width × Scale Factor = 200 pixels × 1.5 = 300 pixels.
Multiply the original width by the scale factor to find the new width.
3
New Height = Original Height × Scale Factor = 150 pixels × 1.5 = 225 pixels.
Multiply the original height by the scale factor to find the new height.
4
New Area = New Width × New Height = 300 pixels × 225 pixels.
The area of a rectangle is found by multiplying its width by its height.
5
300 × 225 = 67500 square pixels.
Complete the multiplication to get the total new area.
6
The new area of the image is 67500 square pixels.
This is the final area of the scaled image.

Answer: 67500 square pixels (Option A)

Question 10Grade 6 • RP
A city's population grew from 80,000 residents to 120,000 residents over a decade. What is the scale factor of the new population to the old population, in simplest form?
A. 40,000
B. 6/4
C. 3/2
D. 2/3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Old population = 80,000 residents, New population = 120,000 residents.
We need to compare these two population figures to find the scale factor.
2
Scale Factor = (New Population) / (Old Population) = 120,000 / 80,000.
The scale factor is the ratio of the new (scaled) population to the old (original) population.
3
120,000 / 80,000 = 12/8. Divide both by 4: 12 ÷ 4 = 3, 8 ÷ 4 = 2. So, 12/8 = 3/2.
First, simplify by dividing both numerator and denominator by common powers of 10. Then, divide by the greatest common divisor of the remaining numbers, which is 4.
4
The scale factor is 3/2.
The simplified fraction represents the scale factor.

Answer: 3/2 (Option C)
