Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Sarah has $18 to spend on a new book. If she wants to have at least $5 left, how much can the book cost, x, in dollars?
A. x >= 13
B. x >= 23
C. x <= 13
D. x <= 23
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
x + 5 <= 18
Let x be the cost of the book. Sarah has $18 total. If she wants to have at least $5 left, the cost of the book plus the $5 she wants to save must be less than or equal to her total money.
2
x + 5 - 5 <= 18 - 5 x <= 13
To find the maximum cost of the book, subtract 5 from both sides of the inequality.
3
x <= 13
The cost of the book can be $13 or less.

Answer: x <= 13 (Option C)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE

B. a < 35
C. a > 8
D. a < 45
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
5a < 40
Let a be the number of aliquots. Each aliquot is 5 ml. The total amount of reagent, which is 5 times the number of aliquots, must be less than 40 ml.
2

3
a < 8
Less than 8 aliquots can be added.

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
The population of a town is decreasing by 50 people each month. If the current population is 12,500 and needs to remain above 12,000, how many months, m, can pass before the population drops too low?
A. m > 10
B. m < -10
C. m < 10
D. m < 250
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
12500 - 50m > 12000
Let m be the number of months. The population decreases by 50 people each month, so after m months, the population will be 12500 - 50m. This population must remain above 12,000.
2
12500 - 50m - 12500 > 12000 - 12500 -50m > -500
First, subtract 12500 from both sides of the inequality.
3
-50m / -50 < -500 / -50 m < 10
Next, divide both sides by -50. Remember to reverse the inequality sign when dividing by a negative number.
4
m < 10
Less than 10 months can pass before the population drops too low.

Answer: m < 10 (Option C)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
An artist is creating a sculpture that requires a minimum of 2.75 pounds of clay. If each block of clay weighs 0.5 pounds, how many whole blocks, b, must the artist use?
A. b >= 6
B. b >= 1.375
C. b >= 5
D. b <= 5.5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
0.5b >= 2.75
Let b be the number of blocks of clay. Each block weighs 0.5 pounds. The sculpture requires a minimum of 2.75 pounds, so the total weight of clay must be greater than or equal to 2.75.
2
0.5b / 0.5 >= 2.75 / 0.5 b >= 5.5
To find b, divide both sides of the inequality by 0.5.
3
Since b must be at least 5.5, and the artist must use whole blocks, the number of blocks must be rounded up to the next whole number. b >= 6
If the artist used 5 blocks, they would only have 2.5 pounds of clay, which is less than the minimum required. Therefore, they must use at least 6 blocks.

Answer: b >= 6 (Option A)
