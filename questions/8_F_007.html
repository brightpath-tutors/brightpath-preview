Question 1Grade 8 • F
A store sells 3 pencils for $0.75 and 7 pencils for $1.75. What is the rate of change of cost per pencil?
A. 4 pencils
B. $0.25 per pencil
C. $1.00
D. $0.50 per pencil
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Point 1: (3 pencils, $0.75) Point 2: (7 pencils, $1.75)
We have two pairs of (number of pencils, cost).
2
Change in cost = $1.75 - $0.75 = $1.00
Subtract the initial cost from the final cost.
3
Change in pencils = 7 - 3 = 4 pencils
Subtract the initial number of pencils from the final number of pencils.
4
Rate of change = $1.00 / 4 pencils = $0.25 per pencil
The rate of change is 'cost per pencil', so cost is in the numerator and pencils in the denominator.

Answer: $0.25 per pencil (Option B)

Question 10Grade 8 • F
A streaming service charges $15 for the first 100 hours of viewing in a month. For every hour exceeding 100, the cost increases by $0.20 per hour. If a user watches 120 hours in a month, what is the overall average rate of change of cost per hour for their total viewing, rounded to four decimal places?
A. $0.15/hour
B. $0.1583/hour
C. $0.20/hour
D. $0.19/hour
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Hours exceeding 100 = 120 hours - 100 hours = 20 hours
This is the period where the higher per-hour rate applies.
2
Cost for additional hours = 20 hours * $0.20/hour = $4.00
Multiply the additional hours by the per-hour rate for those hours.
3
Total cost = $15 (for first 100 hours) + $4.00 (for additional 20 hours) = $19.00
Sum the cost for the initial block and the additional hours.
4
Overall average rate = Total cost / Total hours = $19.00 / 120 hours = $0.158333...
Divide the total cost by the total number of hours watched to find the average rate.
5
$0.1583
The fifth decimal place is 3, so round down.

Answer: $0.1583/hour (Option B)
