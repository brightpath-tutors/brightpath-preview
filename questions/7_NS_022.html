Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A school is designing a new square sandbox. The side length of the sandbox is calculated to be exactly √25 meters. Is the length of one side of this sandbox a rational or irrational number?
A. Rational
B. Irrational
C. Both rational and irrational
D. Neither rational nor irrational
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
√25 = 5
The square root of 25 is 5, because 5 * 5 = 25.
2
5 can be written as the fraction 5/1.
A rational number is any number that can be expressed as a fraction p/q, where p and q are integers and q is not zero. Since 5 can be written as 5/1, it is a rational number.

Answer: Rational (Option A)

B. Irrational
C. Both rational and irrational
D. Rational
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1

Pi (π) is a mathematical constant.
2

Numbers with non-terminating and non-repeating decimal expansions cannot be expressed as a simple fraction of two integers. Therefore, π is an irrational number.

Step-by-step solution

2

3
Since 30 is not a perfect square, √30 is an irrational number.

Question 10Grade 7 • NS

B. Cannot be determined without a calculator
C. Both rational and irrational
D. Irrational
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
√5 √20 = √(5 20) = √100
When multiplying square roots, you can multiply the numbers under the radical sign first.
2
√100 = 10
The square root of 100 is 10, because 10 * 10 = 100.
3
10 can be written as the fraction 10/1.
Since 10 can be expressed as a fraction of two integers, it is a rational number.

Answer: Rational (Option A)
