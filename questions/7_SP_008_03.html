Step-by-step solution
1

Question 4Grade 7 • SP
On a highway, out of 75 vehicles that passed by, 15 were motorcycles, 30 were cars, and the rest were trucks. What is the probability of the next vehicle being a truck, expressed as a fraction in simplest form?
A. 45/75
B. 2/5
C. 1/5
D. 30/75
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total vehicles = 75
Identify the total number of possible outcomes.
2
Motorcycles = 15, Cars = 30
Identify the number of known vehicle types.
3
Trucks = Total vehicles - Motorcycles - Cars = 75 - 15 - 30 = 30 trucks
Calculate the number of favorable outcomes (number of trucks).
4
Probability (truck) = Number of trucks / Total vehicles = 30 / 75
Form the probability as a fraction.
5
Divide numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor (GCD). GCD(30, 75) = 15. 30 ÷ 15 = 2 75 ÷ 15 = 5
Simplify the fraction to its lowest terms by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their GCD.
6
Simplified fraction = 2/5
State the probability in simplest fractional form.

Answer: 2/5 (Option B)

B. 14/24
C. 5/12

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total operating hours in a day = 24 hours
Identify the total number of possible outcomes.
2
Hours producing widgets = 8 hours
Identify the hours for widget production.
3
Hours performing maintenance = 6 hours
Identify the hours for maintenance.
4
Hours producing gadgets = Total hours - Hours producing widgets - Hours performing maintenance = 24 - 8 - 6 = 10 hours

Question 10Grade 7 • SP
A batch of 800 smartphones was tested for manufacturing defects. 32 phones had screen defects, 16 phones had battery defects, and 8 phones had both screen and battery defects. What is the probability that a randomly selected phone has either a screen defect or a battery defect (or both), expressed as a decimal rounded to three decimal places?
A. 0.060
B. 0.040
C. 0.010
D. 0.050
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total smartphones = 800
Identify the total number of possible outcomes.
2
Number of phones with screen defects (S) = 32
Identify the number of phones with screen defects.
3
Number of phones with battery defects (B) = 16
Identify the number of phones with battery defects.
4
Number of phones with both defects (S and B) = 8
Identify the number of phones with both types of defects.
5
Probability (S or B) = P(S) + P(B) - P(S and B)
Use the formula for the probability of the union of two events: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B).
6
Probability (S or B) = (32/800) + (16/800) - (8/800)
Substitute the values into the formula.
7
Probability (S or B) = (32 + 16 - 8) / 800 = 40 / 800
Perform the addition and subtraction in the numerator and keep the common denominator.
8
40 / 800 = 1/20 = 0.05 = 0.050
Convert the fraction to a decimal and round to three decimal places.

Answer: 0.050 (Option D)
