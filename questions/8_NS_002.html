Question 1Grade 8 • NS
A cashier needs to calculate the price of an item that is 3/4 of a dollar. What is this amount as a decimal?
A. 0.34
B. 0.4
C. 0.3
D. 0.75
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Fraction = 3/4
Identify the given fraction representing the price.
2
Division: 3 ÷ 4
To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator.
3
3 ÷ 4 = 0.75
Perform the long division. 3 divided by 4 is 0 with a remainder of 3. Add a decimal point and a zero to get 30. 30 divided by 4 is 7 with a remainder of 2. Add another zero to get 20. 20 divided by 4 is 5 with a remainder of 0. The decimal terminates.
4
Decimal = 0.75
State the final decimal representation.

Answer: 0.75 (Option D)

2

Question 8Grade 8 • NS
A meeting lasted for 2 and 1/6 hours. What is the decimal expansion of the total time, using bar notation for the fractional part?
A. 2.16
B. 2.1
C. 2.16̅
D. 2.6̅
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Mixed number = 2 1/6
Identify the given mixed number representing the total time.
2
Separate whole and fractional parts: 2 + 1/6
The whole number part will remain as is, and the fraction needs to be converted to a decimal.
3
Convert 1/6 to decimal: 1 ÷ 6
Divide the numerator by the denominator for the fractional part.
4
1 ÷ 6 = 0.166...
Perform the long division. 1 divided by 6 is 0 with a remainder of 1. Add a decimal point and a zero to get 10. 10 divided by 6 is 1 with a remainder of 4. Add another zero to get 40. 40 divided by 6 is 6 with a remainder of 4. The remainder 4 is now repeating.
5
Repeating digit = 6, so 1/6 = 0.16̅
Since the remainder 4 is repeating, the digit 6 in the quotient will repeat infinitely. Use bar notation.
6
Combine parts: 2 + 0.16̅ = 2.16̅
Add the whole number part back to the decimal representation of the fraction.

Answer: 2.16̅ (Option C)
