Question 1Grade 8 • EE
A small bakery charges a $7 flat fee for delivery, plus $3 for each cupcake ordered. Which graph correctly represents the total cost (y) for ordering x cupcakes?
A. A line passing through (0, 0) and (1, 10).
B. A line passing through (0, 7) and (1, 10).
C. A line passing through (0, 7) and (1, 4).
D. A line passing through (0, 3) and (1, 10).
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let x be the number of cupcakes and y be the total cost. The flat fee is $7, which is the initial cost (y-intercept). The cost per cupcake is $3, which is the rate of change (slope).
Understanding the components of the problem allows us to set up the linear equation.
2
m = 3 (cost per cupcake) b = 7 (flat delivery fee) Equation: y = 3x + 7
The total cost is the sum of the initial fee and the cost of the cupcakes, where the cost of cupcakes depends on the number ordered.
3
Plot the y-intercept at (0, 7). From (0, 7), move up 3 units and right 1 unit (because slope = 3/1) to find another point (1, 10). Draw a line through these points.
The y-intercept is the point where the line crosses the y-axis (when x=0). The slope tells us how much y changes for every 1 unit change in x.

Answer: A line passing through (0, 7) and (1, 10). (Option B)

Question 2Grade 8 • EE
In a basketball game, a team starts with 22 points and loses 4 points for every technical foul (x). Which graph shows the team's score (y) as technical fouls increase?
A. A line passing through (0, 22) and (1, 18).
B. A line passing through (0, 4) and (1, 22).
C. A line passing through (0, 22) and (1, 26).
D. A line passing through (0, 0) and (1, 18).
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let x be the number of technical fouls and y be the team's score. The initial score is 22 points (y-intercept). The team loses 4 points per foul, so the rate of change is -4 (slope).
Breaking down the problem into initial value and rate of change helps in forming the equation.
2
m = -4 b = 22 Equation: y = -4x + 22
The score decreases with each foul, so the slope is negative.
3
Plot the y-intercept at (0, 22). From (0, 22), move down 4 units and right 1 unit to find another point (1, 18). Draw a line through these points.
A negative slope means the line goes downwards from left to right.

Answer: A line passing through (0, 22) and (1, 18). (Option A)

Question 3Grade 8 • EE
A recipe requires 3 cups of water as a base, plus 0.5 cups for every additional serving (x). Which graph shows the total amount of water (y) needed for x additional servings?
A. A line passing through (0, 0.5) and (1, 3.5).
B. A line passing through (0, 3) and (2, 4).
C. A line passing through (0, 3) and (1, 2.5).
D. A line passing through (0, 3.5) and (1, 4).
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let x be the number of additional servings and y be the total water needed. The base water amount is 3 cups (y-intercept). The additional water per serving is 0.5 cups (slope).
The problem describes an initial amount and a constant rate of increase.
2
m = 0.5 b = 3 Equation: y = 0.5x + 3
The total water is the base amount plus the amount for additional servings.
3
Plot the y-intercept at (0, 3). Since the slope is 0.5 (or 1/2), move up 1 unit and right 2 units from (0, 3) to find another point (2, 4). Draw a line through these points.
A decimal or fractional slope can be used to find points by treating it as rise over run.

Answer: A line passing through (0, 3) and (2, 4). (Option B)

Question 4Grade 8 • EE
A cyclist travels at a constant speed of 75 kilometers per hour. Which graph correctly shows the distance (d) traveled over time (t) in hours?
A. A line passing through (0, 1) and (1, 75).
B. A line passing through (0, 0) and (75, 1).
C. A line passing through (0, 0) and (1, 75).
D. A line passing through (0, 75) and (1, 76).
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Distance (d) = Speed × Time (t). The speed is 75 km/h. There is no initial distance, so the starting point is 0.
This is a direct proportional relationship, often expressed as d = rt where r is speed.
2
Using y for d and x for t, the equation is y = 75x. Here, m = 75 and b = 0.
The slope represents the constant speed, and the y-intercept is 0 because no distance has been covered at time t=0.
3
Plot the y-intercept at (0, 0). From (0, 0), move up 75 units and right 1 unit to find another point (1, 75). Draw a line through these points.
The graph starts at the origin and rises steeply due to the high speed.

Answer: A line passing through (0, 0) and (1, 75). (Option C)

Question 5Grade 8 • EE

B. A line passing through (0, 10) and (10, 0.25).
C. A line passing through (0, 10) and (1, 9.75).
D. A line passing through (0, 0.25) and (1, 10.25).
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial score (y-intercept) = 10 points. Points per correct answer (slope) = 0.25.
The bonus points are the starting value, and points per answer are the rate of change.
2
m = 0.25 (which is 1/4) b = 10 Equation: y = 0.25x + 10
The total score is the sum of bonus points and points from correct answers.
3
Plot the y-intercept at (0, 10). Since the slope is 0.25 (or 1/4), move up 1 unit and right 4 units from (0, 10) to find another point (4, 11). Draw a line through these points.
Using the fractional form of the slope (rise over run) can make plotting easier, especially with decimals.

Answer: A line passing through (0, 10) and (4, 11). (Option A)

Question 6Grade 8 • EE
The temperature (T) in a mountain town starts at 35°C at sea level and decreases by 6°C for every kilometer (h) of altitude. Which graph shows the temperature as altitude increases?
A. A line passing through (0, 35) and (1, 29).
B. A line passing through (0, 0) and (1, 29).
C. A line passing through (0, 6) and (1, 35).
D. A line passing through (0, 35) and (1, 41).
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial temperature (y-intercept) = 35°C. Temperature decrease per kilometer (slope) = -6°C/km.
The starting temperature is the y-intercept, and the decrease per kilometer is the negative slope.
2
m = -6 b = 35 Equation: T = -6h + 35 (or y = -6x + 35)
The temperature decreases, so the slope must be negative.
3
Plot the y-intercept at (0, 35). From (0, 35), move down 6 units and right 1 unit to find another point (1, 29). Draw a line through these points.
A negative slope indicates a downward trend in temperature as altitude increases.

Answer: A line passing through (0, 35) and (1, 29). (Option A)

Question 7Grade 8 • EE
A construction company charges a $120 setup fee for installing a fence, plus $28.50 per meter (L) of fencing. Which graph shows the total cost (C) for a fence of length L?
A. A line passing through (0, 120) and (2, 177).
B. A line passing through (0, 28.50) and (1, 148.50).
C. A line passing through (0, 120) and (1, 91.50).
D. A line passing through (0, 0) and (1, 148.50).
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial cost (y-intercept) = $120. Cost per meter (slope) = $28.50.
The setup fee is a one-time cost, and the per-meter charge is the rate.
2
m = 28.50 b = 120 Equation: C = 28.50L + 120 (or y = 28.50x + 120)
The total cost is the sum of the fixed fee and the length-dependent cost.
3
Plot the y-intercept at (0, 120). To avoid small decimal movements, choose a value for L (e.g., L=2) and calculate C: C = 28.50(2) + 120 = 57 + 120 = 177. Plot the point (2, 177). Draw a line through these points.
With large numbers and decimals, choosing a convenient x-value to calculate a second point can be more practical than using the slope's rise/run directly.

Answer: A line passing through (0, 120) and (2, 177). (Option A)

Question 8Grade 8 • EE
A water tank holds 250 liters of water and is being drained at a constant rate of 20 liters per minute (t). Which graph correctly shows the amount of water (W) remaining in the tank over time?
A. A line passing through (0, 0) and (5, 150).
B. A line passing through (0, 250) and (1, 270).
C. A line passing through (0, 250) and (5, 150).
D. A line passing through (0, 20) and (1, 250).
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial amount of water (y-intercept) = 250 liters. Draining rate (slope) = -20 liters/minute (negative because water is decreasing).
The initial volume is the starting point on the y-axis, and the draining rate is a constant decrease.
2
m = -20 b = 250 Equation: W = -20t + 250 (or y = -20x + 250)
The amount of water remaining is the initial amount minus the amount drained over time.
3
Plot the y-intercept at (0, 250). Choose a convenient time, for example, t = 5 minutes. W = -20(5) + 250 = -100 + 250 = 150. Plot the point (5, 150). Draw a line through these points.
For larger numbers, calculating a point by substituting a reasonable x-value is often simpler than relying on the slope's rise/run for every single unit.

Answer: A line passing through (0, 250) and (5, 150). (Option C)

Question 9Grade 8 • EE
A small town has a current population of 1500 people and is growing at a rate of 75 people per year (t). Which graph represents the town's population (P) over the next several years?
A. A line passing through (0, 1500) and (4, 1800).
B. A line passing through (0, 0) and (4, 1800).
C. A line passing through (0, 75) and (1, 1575).
D. A line passing through (0, 1500) and (1, 1425).
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Initial population (y-intercept) = 1500 people. Growth rate (slope) = 75 people/year.
The current population is the starting point, and the growth rate is the constant increase per year.
2
m = 75 b = 1500 Equation: P = 75t + 1500 (or y = 75x + 1500)
The population increases over time, so the slope is positive.
3
Plot the y-intercept at (0, 1500). Choose a convenient number of years, for example, t = 4 years. P = 75(4) + 1500 = 300 + 1500 = 1800. Plot the point (4, 1800). Draw a line through these points, considering an appropriate scale for the axes.
With larger numbers, it's crucial to select an appropriate scale for the axes to clearly show the relationship. Calculating points for larger intervals of x can be more effective.

Answer: A line passing through (0, 1500) and (4, 1800). (Option A)

Question 10Grade 8 • EE
A mobile data plan costs a flat fee of $12 per month plus $0.05 for each gigabyte (d) of data used. Which graph shows the total monthly cost (C) based on data usage?
A. A line passing through (0, 12) and (1, 11.95).
B. A line passing through (0, 0.05) and (1, 12.05).
C. A line passing through (0, 12) and (100, 17).
D. A line passing through (0, 0) and (100, 17).
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Flat fee (y-intercept) = $12. Cost per gigabyte (slope) = $0.05.
The monthly fee is the base cost, and the cost per gigabyte is the variable charge.
2
m = 0.05 b = 12 Equation: C = 0.05d + 12 (or y = 0.05x + 12)
The total cost is the sum of the flat fee and the data usage cost.
3
Plot the y-intercept at (0, 12). Choose a convenient amount of data, for example, d = 100 GB. C = 0.05(100) + 12 = 5 + 12 = 17. Plot the point (100, 17). Draw a line through these points, noting that data usage (d) cannot be negative.
When dealing with small decimal slopes, choosing a larger x-value for the second point makes plotting more accurate and visually clear. The graph should only exist for non-negative data usage.

Answer: A line passing through (0, 12) and (100, 17). (Option C)
