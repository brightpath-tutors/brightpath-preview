Question 1Grade 5 • NF

A. Factors: \(\frac{3}{4}\) and \(\frac{1}{2}\), Product: \(\frac{5}{4}\)
B. Factors: \(\frac{3}{4}\) and \(\frac{1}{2}\), Product: \(\frac{3}{8}\)
C. Factors: $3$ and $1$, Product: $3$
D. Factors: \(\frac{3}{4}\) and \(\frac{1}{2}\), Product: \(\frac{1}{2}\)
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Factors: \frac{3}{4}, \frac{1}{2
Identify the two quantities being multiplied to find the amount Sarah paid.
2
\frac{3}{4} \times \frac{1}{2
Set up the multiplication expression using the identified factors.
3
\frac{3 \times 1}{4 \times 2} = \frac{3}{8

State the final product in simplest form.

Answer: Factors: \(\frac{3}{4}\) and \(\frac{1}{2}\), Product: \(\frac{3}{8}\) (Option B)
