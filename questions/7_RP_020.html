Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A baker uses 2 cups of sugar for every 3 loaves of bread. If she wants to bake 12 loaves of bread, how many cups of sugar will she need?
A. 9 cups
B. 8 cups
C. 6 cups
D. 10 cups
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Given ratio: 2 cups sugar : 3 loaves bread. Desired loaves: 12.
We need to find out how much sugar is needed for 12 loaves, based on the initial ratio.
2
Scaling factor = Desired loaves / Original loaves = 12 / 3 = 4.
To find out how many times larger the new batch is, divide the new number of loaves by the original number of loaves.
3
Cups of sugar = Original cups sugar Scaling factor = 2 4 = 8.
Multiply the original amount of sugar by the scaling factor to find the new amount of sugar needed.
4
The baker will need 8 cups of sugar.
The final answer is 8 cups of sugar.

Answer: 8 cups (Option B)
