Question 1Grade 6 • RP
In a soccer game, Sarah scored 3 goals and Emily scored 2 goals. What is the ratio of goals scored by Sarah to goals scored by Emily?
A. 3:5
B. 3:2
C. 2:5
D. 2:3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Goals by Sarah = 3
The first quantity in the ratio is the number of goals Sarah scored.
2
Goals by Emily = 2
The second quantity in the ratio is the number of goals Emily scored.
3
Ratio = Sarah's goals : Emily's goals = 3 : 2
Write the numbers in the order specified in the question: Sarah's goals first, then Emily's goals.

Answer: 3:2 (Option B)

Question 8Grade 6 • RP
In a small town, there are 24 adults and 16 children. What is the ratio of adults to children, in simplest form?
A. 3:2
B. 2:3
C. 24:40
D. 24:16
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Adults = 24, Children = 16
First, identify the quantities given in the problem.
2
Ratio = 24 : 16
Form the ratio according to the order specified: adults first, then children.
3
Factors of 24: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24 Factors of 16: 1, 2, 4, 8, 16 GCF = 8
To simplify the ratio, find the largest number that divides evenly into both parts of the ratio.
4
24 ÷ 8 = 3 16 ÷ 8 = 2 Simplified Ratio = 3 : 2
Divide both numbers in the ratio by the GCF to get the ratio in its simplest form.

Answer: 3:2 (Option A)
