Question 1Grade 6 • NS
A baker is making a special cake. The recipe calls for a square pan with a side length of \(\sqrt{17}\) inches. Approximately how long is the side length of the pan, rounded to the nearest tenth of an inch?
A. 4.1 inches
B. 4.2 inches
C. 4.0 inches
D. 4.3 inches
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The side length is \(\sqrt{17}\).
The problem asks for an approximation of \(\sqrt{17}\).
2
$4^2 = 16$, $5^2 = 25$. So, \(\sqrt{16} < \sqrt{17} < \sqrt{25}\).
This shows that \(\sqrt{17}\) is between 4 and 5.
3
Since 17 is closer to 16 than to 25, \(\sqrt{17}\) will be closer to 4. Let's try 4.1. $4.1^2 = 16.81$. Let's try 4.2. $4.2^2 = 17.64$.
We check values in tenths to see which one squares closest to 17.
4
$17 - 16.81 = 0.19$ $17.64 - 17 = 0.64$ Since 16.81 is closer to 17, \(\sqrt{17}\) is approximately 4.1.
Compare the differences to determine the closest approximation.
5
Rounded to the nearest tenth, \(\sqrt{17} \approx 4.1\).
The value 4.1 is the closest tenth.

Answer: 4.1 inches (Option A)
