Question 1Grade 8 • EE
Sarah has $28.50 in her wallet and wants to buy a book. After buying the book, she wants to have at least $10.00 remaining for other expenses. What is the maximum price, p, the book can be?
A. p <= 18.50
B. p >= 18.50
C. p < 18.50
D. p <= 38.50
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
28.50 - p >= 10.00
Set up the inequality: Sarah starts with $28.50. If she buys a book for price p, she will have 28.50 - p left. She wants this amount to be 'at least' $10.00, which means greater than or equal to $10.00.
2
-p >= 10.00 - 28.50 -p >= -18.50
Isolate the variable term by subtracting 28.50 from both sides.
3
(-1)(-p) <= (-1)(-18.50) p <= 18.50
Solve for p by multiplying both sides by -1. Remember to flip the inequality sign when multiplying or dividing by a negative number.

Answer: p <= 18.50 (Option A)

Question 6Grade 8 • EE
A specific biological experiment requires the ambient temperature to be maintained below 95 degrees Celsius. The current temperature in the lab is 108 degrees Celsius, and it is actively being cooled at a rate of 3 degrees Celsius per minute. How many full minutes, m, must pass before the temperature is at or below the required threshold?
A. m <= 4
B. m >= 5
C. m < 5
D. m <= 5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
108 - 3m <= 95
Set up the inequality: The current temperature is 108 degrees Celsius. It drops by 3 degrees per minute, so after m minutes, the temperature will be 108 - 3m. This temperature must be 'at or below' 95 degrees Celsius.
2
-3m <= 95 - 108 -3m <= -13
Isolate the variable term by subtracting 108 from both sides.
3
m >= -13 / -3 m >= 4.333...
Solve for m by dividing both sides by -3. Remember to flip the inequality sign.
4
m >= 5
Since m represents full minutes, and the temperature must be at or below 95, 4 minutes is not enough (temperature would be 96). After 5 minutes, the temperature would be 93, which meets the condition. So, the minimum number of full minutes is 5.

Answer: m >= 5 (Option B)

780 + 60i <= 1500

i <= 720 / 60 i <= 12

B. x >= 61
C. x <= 60
D. x <= 59
E. None of the above

Step-by-step solution
1
180 - x < 120
Set up the inequality: Your current screen time is 180 minutes. You reduce it by x minutes, so the new screen time is 180 - x. This new screen time must be 'below' 120 minutes, meaning strictly less than 120.
2
-x < 120 - 180 -x < -60

3
(-1)(-x) > (-1)(-60) x > 60

4
x >= 61
