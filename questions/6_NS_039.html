Question 1Grade 6 • NS

A. 12.50
B. 1.25
C. 12.05
D. 12.5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The price is $12.50.
The problem states the price directly as a decimal number.
2
12.50 is a positive decimal number. It lies between the integers 12 and 13.
Decimal numbers fit between whole numbers on a number line. 12.50 is exactly halfway between 12 and 13.
3

Question 8Grade 6 • NS
A digital clock shows the time as 7:45 AM. If 7:00 AM is marked as 0 on a number line representing hours past 7 AM, what rational number, in simplest form, represents 7:45 AM?
A. 45/60
B. 3/4
C. 1/4
D. 0.45
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The time is 7:45 AM, and the reference point is 7:00 AM. The elapsed time is 45 minutes.
To represent time on a number line where a specific hour is 0, we need to calculate the time difference in minutes.
2
There are 60 minutes in an hour. So, 45 minutes is 45/60 of an hour.
To represent minutes as a rational number of an hour, divide the number of minutes by 60.
3
Divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor (GCD). The GCD of 45 and 60 is 15. So, 45 ÷ 15 = 3 and 60 ÷ 15 = 4. The simplified fraction is 3/4.
Always simplify fractions as requested in the problem or for clarity.
4
The point representing 3/4 (or 0.75) would be three-quarters of the way from 0 to 1 on the number line representing hours past 7 AM.
The simplified fraction 3/4 directly represents the position on the number line relative to 0 (7:00 AM) and 1 (8:00 AM).

Answer: 3/4 (Option B)
