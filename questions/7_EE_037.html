Combine Like Terms

Skill ID: 7.EE.037

Question 1Grade 7 • EE
Maria is organizing her stationery. She has 8 blue pens, 5 red pens, and 3 notebooks. If p represents a pen and n represents a notebook, which expression correctly shows the total number of pens and notebooks?
A. 16pn
B. 8p + 5p + 3n
C. 13p + 3n
D. 16p + n
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms are 8p (blue pens), 5p (red pens), and 3n (notebooks). Terms with the same variable are like terms. So, 8p and 5p are like terms.
Like terms have the same variables raised to the same power. Both 'blue pens' and 'red pens' are simply 'pens', so they are represented by the same variable p.
2
(8p + 5p) + 3n
It's helpful to group the like terms before combining them to avoid confusion.
3
(8 + 5)p + 3n = 13p + 3n
Add the numerical coefficients of the like terms (p). The term 3n remains separate as it is an unlike term.

Answer: 13p + 3n (Option C)

Question 2Grade 7 • EE
During a soccer practice, Leo ran 4 laps around the field, then did 2 sets of drills, and later ran 6 more laps. Let L represent a lap and D represent a set of drills. Which expression shows the total number of laps and drill sets?
A. 12L + D
B. 4L + 6L + 2D
C. 12LD
D. 10L + 2D
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms are 4L (laps), 2D (drill sets), and 6L (more laps). Terms with the same variable are like terms. So, 4L and 6L are like terms.
Both 'laps' terms are represented by the variable L, making them like terms. 'Drill sets' are represented by D, making it an unlike term.
2
(4L + 6L) + 2D
Group the terms with L together for easier combination.
3
(4 + 6)L + 2D = 10L + 2D
Add the numerical coefficients of the like terms (L). The term 2D remains as it is an unlike term.

Answer: 10L + 2D (Option D)

Question 4Grade 7 • EE
A cyclist travels at a speed of 15 miles per hour for x hours and then 20 miles per hour for 2x hours. She also takes a break for 10 minutes (which can be represented as 1/6 of an hour). Which expression represents the total distance traveled, assuming the 10 minutes break does not contribute to distance?
A. 15x + 20(2x)
B. 75x
C. 55x
D. 55x + 1/6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Distance = speed × time. First segment: 15 * x = 15x miles. Second segment: 20 * 2x = 40x miles. The break time (1/6 hour) does not contribute to the distance traveled.
Apply the distance formula for each part of the journey. The break is irrelevant for total distance traveled.
2
15x + 40x
Add the distances from each segment to find the total distance.
3
15x + 40x = (15 + 40)x = 55x
Both terms have the variable x raised to the first power, so they are like terms. Add their coefficients.

Answer: 55x (Option C)

Question 5Grade 7 • EE
In a school play, there are 9 actors, 4 stage crew members, and 3 more actors joined. Additionally, 2 ushers were hired. Let a represent an actor, c a stage crew member, and u an usher. Write an expression for the total number of people involved.
A. 9a + 4c + 3a + 2u
B. 18acu
C. 16a + 2c + u
D. 12a + 4c + 2u
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms are 9a (actors), 4c (stage crew), 3a (more actors), and 2u (ushers). Terms with the same variable are like terms. So, 9a and 3a are like terms.
Terms with the variable a are like terms. Terms with c and u are unlike terms because they have different variables.
2
(9a + 3a) + 4c + 2u
Group the terms representing actors together.
3
(9 + 3)a + 4c + 2u = 12a + 4c + 2u
Add the numerical coefficients of the like terms (a). The terms 4c and 2u remain separate as they are unlike terms.

Answer: 12a + 4c + 2u (Option D)

Question 6Grade 7 • EE
A botanist is studying plant growth. Plant A grew 2x centimeters, Plant B grew 5 centimeters, and Plant C grew 3x centimeters. Additionally, she observed y new leaves on another plant. Write an expression for the total growth in centimeters and the number of new leaves.
A. 5x + 5 + y
B. 2x + 5 + 3x + y
C. 5x + 6y
D. 10xy
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms are 2x (Plant A growth), 5 (Plant B growth - constant), 3x (Plant C growth), and y (new leaves). Terms with the same variable are like terms. So, 2x and 3x are like terms. 5 is a constant and y is a different variable, making them unlike terms.
Terms with the variable x are like terms. A constant term (5) and a term with a different variable (y) are unlike terms.
2
(2x + 3x) + 5 + y
Group the x terms together.
3
(2 + 3)x + 5 + y = 5x + 5 + y
Add the numerical coefficients of the like terms (x). The constant term 5 and the y term remain separate.

Answer: 5x + 5 + y (Option A)

Question 7Grade 7 • EE
A construction worker is calculating the total length of pipes needed for a project. He needs 3a feet of PVC pipe, 7 feet of copper pipe, 5a feet of another PVC pipe, and 4b feet of steel pipe. Write an expression for the total length of all pipes.
A. 19ab
B. 8a + 4b + 7
C. 8a + 11b
D. 3a + 7 + 5a + 4b
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms are 3a (PVC pipe), 7 (copper pipe - constant), 5a (another PVC pipe), and 4b (steel pipe). Terms with the same variable are like terms. So, 3a and 5a are like terms. 7 is a constant and 4b is a different variable term.
Terms with the variable a are like terms. The constant 7 and the term with variable b (4b) are unlike terms.
2
(3a + 5a) + 4b + 7
Group the a terms together.
3
(3 + 5)a + 4b + 7 = 8a + 4b + 7
Add the numerical coefficients of the like terms (a). The 4b term and the constant 7 remain separate.

Answer: 8a + 4b + 7 (Option B)

Question 8Grade 7 • EE
A project manager is tracking time spent on different tasks. Task 1 took t^2 hours, Task 2 took 14t hours, Task 3 took 6 hours, and Task 4 took 3t^2 hours. Task 5 took 5t hours. Write an expression for the total time spent on all tasks.
A. t^2 + 14t + 6 + 3t^2 + 5t
B. 29t^2 + 6
C. 28t^3 + 6
D. 4t^2 + 19t + 6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms are t^2, 14t, 6, 3t^2, and 5t. Like terms for t^2: t^2 and 3t^2. Like terms for t: 14t and 5t. The constant term is 6.
Like terms must have the same variable raised to the same power. t^2 and t are considered different variable parts.
2
(t^2 + 3t^2) + (14t + 5t) + 6
Arrange the terms by grouping those with t^2, those with t, and the constant term.
3
(1 + 3)t^2 + (14 + 5)t + 6 = 4t^2 + 19t + 6
Add the coefficients for each set of like terms. The constant term remains unchanged.

Answer: 4t^2 + 19t + 6 (Option D)

Question 9Grade 7 • EE
A zoologist is tracking animal populations in a wildlife reserve. She observes 2m^2 deer, 9k wild boars, 5m^2 rabbits, and 12k foxes. She also notes m new bird nests. Write an expression for the total count of animals and nests.
A. 28m^3k
B. 7m^2 + 21k + m^2
C. 7m^2 + 21k + m
D. 29m^2k + m
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms are 2m^2, 9k, 5m^2, 12k, and m. Like terms for m^2: 2m^2 and 5m^2. Like terms for k: 9k and 12k. The term m is an unlike term.
Remember that m and m^2 are not like terms because their exponents are different. Variables must match exactly, including their exponents.
2
(2m^2 + 5m^2) + (9k + 12k) + m
Group the m^2 terms and the k terms. The m term remains separate.
3
(2 + 5)m^2 + (9 + 12)k + m = 7m^2 + 21k + m
Add the coefficients for each set of like terms. The m term remains unchanged.

Answer: 7m^2 + 21k + m (Option C)

Question 10Grade 7 • EE
A software developer is optimizing code. Module A requires 1.5xy processing units, Module B uses 8x^2 memory units, Module C needs 4.5xy processing units, and Module D consumes 10x^2 memory units. Module E needs 2y display units. Write an expression for the total resource consumption.
A. 26x^2y + 2y
B. 6xy + 18x^2 + 2y
C. 6xy^2 + 18x^4 + 2y
D. 26xy + 2y
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The terms are 1.5xy, 8x^2, 4.5xy, 10x^2, and 2y. Like terms for xy: 1.5xy and 4.5xy. Like terms for x^2: 8x^2 and 10x^2. The term 2y is an unlike term.
Terms are like terms if they have the exact same variables raised to the exact same powers. xy, x^2, and y are all distinct variable parts.
2
(1.5xy + 4.5xy) + (8x^2 + 10x^2) + 2y
Group the xy terms, the x^2 terms, and leave the y term separate.
3
(1.5 + 4.5)xy + (8 + 10)x^2 + 2y = 6xy + 18x^2 + 2y
Add the coefficients for each set of like terms. The 2y term remains unchanged.

Answer: 6xy + 18x^2 + 2y (Option B)
