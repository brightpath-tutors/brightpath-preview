Question 1Grade 8 • NS
Due to market fluctuations, a company's stock value changed by a fractional amount that, when expressed as a decimal, is 0.5̅. Convert this repeating decimal to a fraction in simplest form.
A. 1/2
B. 5/11
C. 5/9
D. 1/5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
x = 0.555...
Let 'x' represent the repeating decimal.
2
10x = 5.555...
Multiply both sides of the equation by 10, as there is 1 repeating digit, to shift the repeating part past the decimal point.
3
10x - x = 5.555... - 0.555...
Subtract the original equation (x = 0.555...) from the new equation (10x = 5.555...) to eliminate the repeating decimal part.
4
9x = 5
Perform the subtraction on both sides of the equation.
5
x = 5/9
Divide both sides by 9 to solve for x, expressing the result as a fraction.
6
5/9 (already in simplest form)
Simplify the fraction if necessary. In this case, 5/9 is already in simplest form.

Answer: 5/9 (Option C)
