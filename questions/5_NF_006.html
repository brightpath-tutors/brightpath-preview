Question 1Grade 5 • NF
Sarah had 5 1/2 pounds of sugar for baking. She used 2 1/4 pounds for a cake. How much sugar does she have left?
A. 2 1/4 pounds
B. 3 1/8 pounds
C. 3 pounds
D. 3 1/4 pounds
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
5 1/2 - 2 1/4
Set up the subtraction problem with the given mixed numbers.
2
1/2 = 2/4
Find a common denominator for the fractions. The least common multiple of 2 and 4 is 4, so convert 1/2 to 2/4.
3
5 2/4 - 2 1/4 = (5-2) + (2/4 - 1/4) = 3 + 1/4 = 3 1/4
Subtract the whole numbers and then subtract the fractions. Simplify the result if necessary.

Answer: 3 1/4 pounds (Option D)
