Question 1Grade 7 • EE
A baker uses 3x + 5 grams of sugar for a cake and x + 2 grams of sugar for cookies. Which expression represents the total amount of sugar used?
A. 4x + 5
B. 4x + 7
C. 3x + 10
D. 3x + 7
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Cake sugar: 3x + 5, Cookie sugar: x + 2
The problem provides the amount of sugar for each item as algebraic expressions.
2
(3x + 5) + (x + 2)
To find the total amount, we need to sum the expressions for sugar used in the cake and cookies.
3
(3x + x) + (5 + 2) = 4x + 7
Combine the 'x' terms and the constant terms separately. 3x + x = 4x, and 5 + 2 = 7.

Answer: 4x + 7 (Option B)

Question 2Grade 7 • EE
A store offers a discount on a shirt. The original price is represented by 50 - 2y dollars, and the discount is 10 + y dollars. Which expression represents the final price of the shirt after the discount?
A. 60 - y
B. 40 - y
C. 60 - 3y
D. 40 - 3y
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Original price: 50 - 2y, Discount: 10 + y
The problem provides the original price and the discount as algebraic expressions.
2
(50 - 2y) - (10 + y)
To find the final price after a discount, subtract the discount amount from the original price.
3
50 - 2y - 10 - y
When subtracting an expression, remember to change the sign of each term within the subtracted parentheses.
4
(50 - 10) + (-2y - y) = 40 - 3y
Combine the constant terms and the 'y' terms separately. 50 - 10 = 40, and -2y - y = -3y.

Answer: 40 - 3y (Option D)

E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Chicken needed: 4w - 7
The problem provides the amount of chicken for one recipe as an algebraic expression.
2
2 * (4w - 7)

2 4w - 2 7 = 8w - 14

Question 4Grade 7 • EE
An athlete's training schedule involves running (6t + 8) miles per week. Due to injury, they reduce their training by (2t + 3) miles per week. Which expression represents the new weekly training distance?
A. 4t + 5
B. 8t + 11
C. 4t + 11
D. 8t + 5
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Original distance: 6t + 8, Reduction: 2t + 3
The problem provides the original training distance and the reduction as algebraic expressions.
2
(6t + 8) - (2t + 3)
To find the new distance after a reduction, subtract the reduction amount from the original distance.
3
6t + 8 - 2t - 3
When subtracting an expression, remember to change the sign of each term within the subtracted parentheses.
4
(6t - 2t) + (8 - 3) = 4t + 5
Combine the 't' terms and the constant terms separately. 6t - 2t = 4t, and 8 - 3 = 5.

Answer: 4t + 5 (Option A)

Question 8Grade 7 • EE
A traveler estimates their driving time to be (15m + 25) minutes. If they encounter traffic that adds 1/3 of their estimated time, which expression represents the new total driving time?
A. 20m + 50
B. 20m + 33.33
C. 15m + 33.33
D. 20m + 25
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Estimated time: 15m + 25
The problem provides the estimated driving time as an algebraic expression.
2
Additional time = (1/3) * (15m + 25) = 5m + 25/3
Multiply the estimated time by 1/3 to find the additional time. Distribute 1/3 to both terms.
3
(15m + 25) + (5m + 25/3)
The new total driving time is the sum of the estimated time and the additional traffic time.
4
(15m + 5m) + (25 + 25/3) = 20m + 75/3 + 25/3 = 20m + 100/3
Combine the 'm' terms and the constant terms. Convert 25 to 75/3 to add fractions. 100/3 is approximately 33.33.

Answer: 20m + 33.33 (Option B)

Question 9Grade 7 • EE
A city's population is currently (10000 + 150y) people. Due to urban development, the population is projected to increase by 20% over the next year. Which expression represents the new projected population?
A. 12000 + 180y
B. 12000 + 30y
C. 12000 + 150y
D. 10000 + 180y
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Current population: 10000 + 150y
The problem provides the current population as an algebraic expression.
2
Increase = 0.20 * (10000 + 150y) = 2000 + 30y
Multiply the current population expression by 0.20 (20%) to find the increase. Distribute 0.20 to both terms.
3
(10000 + 150y) + (2000 + 30y)
The new projected population is the sum of the current population and the increase.
4
(10000 + 2000) + (150y + 30y) = 12000 + 180y
Combine the constant terms and the 'y' terms separately. 10000 + 2000 = 12000, and 150y + 30y = 180y.

Answer: 12000 + 180y (Option A)

Question 10Grade 7 • EE
A company produces (250z + 500) computer chips each day. If 1/5 of the chips produced are defective and recycled, which expression represents the number of non-defective chips produced daily?
A. 250z + 400
B. 200z + 400
C. 200z + 600
D. 300z + 600
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Total chips: 250z + 500
The problem provides the total number of chips as an algebraic expression.
2
Defective chips = (1/5) * (250z + 500) = 50z + 100
Multiply the total chips by 1/5 to find the number of defective chips. Distribute 1/5 to both terms.
3
(250z + 500) - (50z + 100)
The number of non-defective chips is the total chips minus the defective chips.
4
250z + 500 - 50z - 100
When subtracting an expression, remember to change the sign of each term within the subtracted parentheses.
5
(250z - 50z) + (500 - 100) = 200z + 400
Combine the 'z' terms and the constant terms separately. 250z - 50z = 200z, and 500 - 100 = 400.

Answer: 200z + 400 (Option B)
