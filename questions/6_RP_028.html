Question 1Grade 6 • RP
A store sells 3 pencils for $1.00. Complete the ratio table to find out how many pencils you can buy for $4.00. | Pencils | Cost ($) | | :------ | :------- | | 3 | 1 | | ? | 4 |
A. 12 pencils
B. 7 pencils
C. 13 pencils
D. 9 pencils
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The given ratio is 3 pencils for $1.00.
Understand the initial relationship between pencils and cost.
2
The cost increased from $1.00 to $4.00. To find the factor, divide the new cost by the old cost: $4.00 ÷ $1.00 = 4.
Find how many times the cost has increased.
3
Multiply the initial number of pencils by the scaling factor: 3 pencils × 4 = 12 pencils.
Since the cost was scaled up by a factor of 4, the number of pencils must also be scaled up by the same factor to maintain the ratio.

Answer: 12 pencils (Option A)

Question 9Grade 6 • RP

A. 8 doctors
B. 7 doctors
C. 10 doctors
D. 6 doctors
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The given ratio is 5 doctors for 15,000 residents.
Understand the initial relationship between doctors and residents.
2
Divide the number of residents by the number of doctors: 15,000 residents ÷ 5 doctors = 3,000 residents per doctor.
Determine how many residents each doctor serves to apply this to the new population.
3
Divide the new number of residents by the unit rate: 21,000 residents ÷ 3,000 residents/doctor = 7 doctors.
Use the unit rate to find the required number of doctors for the increased population.

Answer: 7 doctors (Option B)
