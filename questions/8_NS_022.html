Question 1Grade 8 • NS
A square baseball diamond has sides of 90 feet. A player runs from home plate to second base along the diagonal. What is the exact distance the player runs?
A. √180 feet
B. 127.28 feet
C. 90√2 feet
D. 180 feet
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
A square baseball diamond forms a right-angled triangle with two sides and the diagonal as the hypotenuse. The Pythagorean theorem states a² + b² = c².
The diagonal of a square creates a right triangle with two sides of the square. For a square, both legs (sides) are equal in length.
2
For a square with side length 's' and diagonal 'd', the theorem simplifies to s² + s² = d², or 2s² = d². Taking the square root of both sides, d = s√2.
This is a common shortcut for finding the diagonal of a square, derived directly from the Pythagorean theorem.
3
Given s = 90 feet. So, d = 90√2 feet.
Substitute the known side length into the diagonal formula.
4
Since √2 is an irrational number, the product of a rational number (90) and an irrational number (√2) results in an irrational number. Therefore, 90√2 feet is an exact irrational distance.
An irrational number cannot be expressed as a simple fraction. The square root of a non-perfect square is irrational.

Answer: 90√2 feet (Option C)

E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The given number is 4√6.
Break down the number into its component parts: an integer and a square root.
2
The number consists of a rational part (4) and a radical part (√6). Since 6 is not a perfect square (i.e., there is no integer whose square is 6), √6 is an irrational number.
A square root of a non-perfect square is an irrational number. Rational numbers can be expressed as a fraction of two integers.
3
The product of a non-zero rational number (4) and an irrational number (√6) is always an irrational number.
This is a fundamental property of real numbers. Multiplying a rational number by an irrational number will always result in an irrational number (unless the rational number is zero).
4
Therefore, 4√6 is an irrational number.
Based on the properties, the classification is irrational.

Answer: Irrational (Option A)

The volume V is given as 8π√13.
