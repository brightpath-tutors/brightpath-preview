Question 1Grade 7 • EE
The cost of a taxi ride is $2.50 per mile, plus a fixed service charge. If a 5-mile ride costs $15, write an equation in point-slope form that represents the cost of a taxi ride.
A. y - 5 = 2.50(x - 15)
B. y + 15 = 2.50(x + 5)
C. y - 15 = 2.50(x - 5)
D. y = 2.50x + 15
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The problem states that a 5-mile ride costs $15. This gives us a point (x1, y1) = (5, 15). The cost per mile is $2.50, which is the rate of change, or slope (m = 2.50).
A point is given by (miles, total cost) and the cost per mile is the slope.
2
The point-slope form is y - y1 = m(x - x1). Substitute the values: y - 15 = 2.50(x - 5).
Substitute the identified point (5, 15) and slope (2.50) into the formula.

Answer: y - 15 = 2.50(x - 5) (Option C)

Question 10Grade 7 • EE
A patient's medication dosage needs to be increased by 0.1 mg every 2 hours. If after 6 hours, the dosage is 0.3 mg, write an equation in point-slope form for the dosage over time.
A. y - 0.3 = 0.05(x - 6)
B. y + 0.3 = 0.05(x + 6)
C. y - 6 = 0.05(x - 0.3)
D. y = 0.05x + 0.3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
The problem states that after 6 hours, the dosage is 0.3 mg. This gives us a point (x1, y1) = (6, 0.3).
A point is given by (hours, total dosage).
2
The dosage increases by 0.1 mg every 2 hours. This is a rate of change (slope). m = (change in y) / (change in x) = 0.1 mg / 2 hours = 0.05 mg/hour.
The rate of change is the increase in dosage divided by the time interval.
3
The point-slope form is y - y1 = m(x - x1). Substitute the values: y - 0.3 = 0.05(x - 6).
Substitute the identified point (6, 0.3) and calculated slope (0.05) into the formula.

Answer: y - 0.3 = 0.05(x - 6) (Option A)
