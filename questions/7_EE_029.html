Question 1Grade 7 • EE
Clara bought 2 toy cars and 3 toy planes for a total of $26. Each toy car costs $4. How much does each toy plane cost?
A. $4
B. $5
C. $7
D. $6
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'c' be the cost of one toy car and 'p' be the cost of one toy plane. Equation 1 (total cost): 2c + 3p = 26 Equation 2 (cost of a car): c = 4
We use two variables to represent the unknown costs and form two equations based on the given information.
2
Substitute c = 4 into Equation 1: 2(4) + 3p = 26
Since we know the cost of a toy car, we can replace 'c' with '4' in the total cost equation.
3
8 + 3p = 26 3p = 26 - 8 3p = 18 p = 18 / 3 p = 6
First, multiply 2 by 4. Then, subtract 8 from both sides to isolate the term with 'p'. Finally, divide by 3 to find the value of 'p'.

Answer: $6 (Option D)

Question 2Grade 7 • EE
In a basketball game, Liam scored 21 points. He made a total of 10 baskets, consisting of 2-point shots and 3-point shots. How many 2-point shots did Liam make?
A. 9 shots
B. 10 shots
C. 7 shots
D. 1 shot
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the number of 2-point shots and 'y' be the number of 3-point shots. Equation 1 (total points): 2x + 3y = 21 Equation 2 (total baskets): x + y = 10
We create two equations based on the points scored and the total number of baskets.
2
From Equation 2, isolate 'y': y = 10 - x
This prepares the expression for substitution into the other equation.
3
Substitute y = 10 - x into Equation 1: 2x + 3(10 - x) = 21 2x + 30 - 3x = 21 -x + 30 = 21 -x = 21 - 30 -x = -9 x = 9
Distribute the 3, combine like terms, and then solve the resulting one-variable equation for 'x'.
4
Liam made 9 two-point shots.
The question asked for the number of 2-point shots, which is 'x'.

Answer: 9 shots (Option A)

Question 3Grade 7 • EE
A recipe calls for a total of 12 teaspoons of spices, which is a mix of salt and pepper. The amount of salt needed is twice the amount of pepper. How many teaspoons of salt are needed?
A. 4 teaspoons
B. 6 teaspoons
C. 8 teaspoons
D. 10 teaspoons
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 's' be the amount of salt and 'p' be the amount of pepper (both in teaspoons). Equation 1 (total spices): s + p = 12 Equation 2 (salt relative to pepper): s = 2p
We translate the problem's conditions into two algebraic equations.
2
Substitute s = 2p into Equation 1: 2p + p = 12
Since 's' is already defined in terms of 'p', we can replace 's' in the first equation.
3
3p = 12 p = 12 / 3 p = 4
Combine the 'p' terms, then divide by the coefficient to find the amount of pepper.
4
Substitute p = 4 into Equation 2: s = 2 * 4 s = 8
Now that we know 'p', we can easily find 's' using the second equation.

Answer: 8 teaspoons (Option C)

Question 4Grade 7 • EE
A car and a motorcycle traveled a total distance of 330 miles. The car traveled 50 miles per hour for 'h' hours, and the motorcycle traveled 60 miles per hour for the same 'h' hours. How many hours did they travel?
A. 3.5 hours
B. 2 hours
C. 3 hours
D. 2.5 hours
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'h' be the number of hours they traveled. Let 'd_c' be the distance the car traveled and 'd_m' be the distance the motorcycle traveled. Equation 1 (distance for car): d_c = 50h Equation 2 (distance for motorcycle): d_m = 60h Equation 3 (total distance): d_c + d_m = 330
We use 'h' for time and separate variables for each vehicle's distance, then form equations based on rate x time = distance and total distance.
2
Substitute d_c = 50h and d_m = 60h into Equation 3: 50h + 60h = 330
This step combines the information into a single equation with only one variable, 'h'.
3
110h = 330 h = 330 / 110 h = 3
Combine like terms (the 'h' terms), then divide by the coefficient to find the number of hours.

Answer: 3 hours (Option C)

Question 5Grade 7 • EE

D. 35 adult tickets
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'a' be the number of adult tickets and 's' be the number of student tickets. Equation 1 (total tickets): a + s = 50 Equation 2 (total money collected): 10a + 6s = 420
We use 'a' and 's' for the number of each type of ticket and set up equations based on the total quantity and total value.
2
From Equation 1, isolate 's': s = 50 - a
This makes it easy to substitute into the second equation.
3
Substitute s = 50 - a into Equation 2: 10a + 6(50 - a) = 420 10a + 300 - 6a = 420 4a + 300 = 420 4a = 420 - 300 4a = 120 a = 120 / 4 a = 30
Apply the distributive property, combine like terms, and then solve the resulting one-variable equation for 'a'.
4
30 adult tickets were sold.
The question specifically asked for the number of adult tickets, which is 'a'.

Question 6Grade 7 • EE
A chemist is mixing two solutions. Solution A is 10% acid and Solution B is 30% acid. She wants to create 500 ml of a solution that is 18% acid. How many milliliters of Solution A should she use?
A. 350 ml
B. 300 ml
C. 250 ml
D. 200 ml
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'a' be the volume (in ml) of Solution A and 'b' be the volume (in ml) of Solution B. Equation 1 (total volume): a + b = 500 Equation 2 (total amount of acid): 0.10a + 0.30b = 0.18 * 500 Simplify Equation 2: 0.10a + 0.30b = 90
We set up equations for the total volume of the mixture and the total amount of acid contributed by each solution.
2
From Equation 1, isolate 'b': b = 500 - a
This allows for substitution into the second equation.
3
Substitute b = 500 - a into 0.10a + 0.30b = 90: 0.10a + 0.30(500 - a) = 90 0.10a + 150 - 0.30a = 90 -0.20a + 150 = 90 -0.20a = 90 - 150 -0.20a = -60 a = -60 / -0.20 a = 300
Apply the distributive property, combine like terms, and solve the resulting one-variable equation for 'a'.
4
The chemist should use 300 ml of Solution A.
The question asked for the amount of Solution A, which is 'a'.

Answer: 300 ml (Option B)

Step-by-step solution
1

2

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'x' be the time (in minutes) Machine 1 ran. Let 'y' be the time (in minutes) Machine 2 ran. Equation 1 (total flyers): 100x + 150y = 1600 Equation 2 (time relationship): y = x + 2
We use 'x' and 'y' for the running times of each machine and set up equations based on their printing rates and total output, and the given time difference.
2
Substitute y = x + 2 into Equation 1: 100x + 150(x + 2) = 1600
This reduces the system to a single equation with one variable 'x'.
3
100x + 150x + 300 = 1600 250x + 300 = 1600 250x = 1600 - 300 250x = 1300 x = 1300 / 250 x = 130 / 25 x = 5.2
Apply the distributive property, combine like terms, isolate the 'x' term, and divide to find 'x'. Simplify the fraction 130/25 by dividing both by 5, then convert to decimal.
4
Machine 1 ran for 5.2 minutes.

Question 9Grade 7 • EE
In a wildlife sanctuary, the population of deer is currently 100 and increases by 10 deer per year. The population of wolves is currently 10 and increases by 3 wolves per year. In how many years will the deer population be exactly 4 times the wolf population?
A. 25 years
B. 30 years
C. 20 years
D. 35 years
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 't' be the number of years from now. Let 'd' be the deer population after 't' years. Let 'w' be the wolf population after 't' years. Equation 1 (deer population): d = 100 + 10t Equation 2 (wolf population): w = 10 + 3t Equation 3 (relationship condition): d = 4w
We create expressions for the populations over time and a third equation representing the condition when the deer population is four times the wolf population.
2
Substitute Equation 1 and Equation 2 into Equation 3: 100 + 10t = 4(10 + 3t)
This forms a single equation with one variable, 't'.
3
100 + 10t = 40 + 12t 100 - 40 = 12t - 10t 60 = 2t t = 60 / 2 t = 30
Apply the distributive property, gather 't' terms on one side and constants on the other, then solve for 't'.
4
In 30 years, the deer population will be 4 times the wolf population.
The question asked for the number of years, which is 't'.

Answer: 30 years (Option B)

Question 10Grade 7 • EE
A mobile phone company offers two data plans. Plan A costs $35 per month plus $0.60 per GB of data used. Plan B costs $50 per month plus $0.30 per GB of data used. For how many gigabytes of data would the two plans cost the same?
A. 50 GB
B. 25 GB
C. 60 GB
D. 30 GB
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'c' be the total cost per month. Let 'g' be the number of gigabytes of data used. Equation 1 (Plan A cost): c = 35 + 0.60g Equation 2 (Plan B cost): c = 50 + 0.30g
We set up two equations representing the cost of each data plan based on a fixed monthly fee and a per-gigabyte charge.
2
Since we want to find when the costs are the same, set Equation 1 equal to Equation 2: 35 + 0.60g = 50 + 0.30g
This creates a single equation with one variable 'g', which can be solved.
3
0.60g - 0.30g = 50 - 35 0.30g = 15 g = 15 / 0.30 g = 150 / 3 g = 50
Gather 'g' terms on one side and constants on the other. Perform subtraction, then divide to find the value of 'g'.
4
The two plans would cost the same for 50 gigabytes of data.
The question asked for the number of gigabytes, which is 'g'.

Answer: 50 GB (Option A)
