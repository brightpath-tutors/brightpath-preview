Question 1Grade 8 • NS
The temperature in Anchorage dropped from 5 degrees Celsius to -3 degrees Celsius. How many degrees did the temperature change?
A. 3 degrees Celsius
B. 8 degrees Celsius
C. 5 degrees Celsius
D. 2 degrees Celsius
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Starting temperature = 5°C, Ending temperature = -3°C
The problem states the initial and final temperatures.
2
5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8 OR count from -3 to 0 (3 units) and from 0 to 5 (5 units); 3 + 5 = 8
To find the change, we calculate the absolute difference between the two temperatures. On a number line, we count the number of units from -3 to 5.
3
The temperature changed by 8 degrees Celsius.
The difference calculated represents the total change in temperature.

Answer: 8 degrees Celsius (Option B)

E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution

Digging deeper means moving downwards, represented by negative integers.
2

3
The final depth is -11 feet.
The sum represents the worker's final position relative to ground level.

Question 10Grade 8 • NS
A digital thermometer registered a reading of -2 degrees Celsius. The actual temperature was 6 degrees Celsius. What is the difference between the actual temperature and the thermometer's reading?
A. 4 degrees Celsius
B. -8 degrees Celsius
C. -4 degrees Celsius
D. 8 degrees Celsius
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Actual temperature = 6°C, Thermometer reading = -2°C
The problem provides two distinct temperature values.
2
6 - (-2) = 6 + 2 = 8
To find the difference, subtract the smaller value from the larger value. Subtracting a negative number is equivalent to adding its positive counterpart.
3
The difference is 8 degrees Celsius.
The result represents how far off the thermometer's reading was from the actual temperature.

Answer: 8 degrees Celsius (Option D)
