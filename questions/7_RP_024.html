Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A store sells 3 pencils for $0.75. How much would 7 pencils cost?
A. $1.75
B. $2.25
C. $1.50
D. $0.32
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'c' be the cost of 7 pencils. (3 pencils) / ($0.75) = (7 pencils) / (c)
We set up a proportion comparing the number of pencils to their cost.
2
3 c = 0.75 7
To solve a proportion, we cross-multiply the numerator of one ratio by the denominator of the other.
3
3c = 5.25
Multiply the numbers on the right side of the equation.
4
c = 5.25 / 3 c = 1.75
Divide both sides by 3 to find the value of c.
5
7 pencils would cost $1.75.
The cost for 7 pencils is $1.75.

Answer: $1.75 (Option A)
