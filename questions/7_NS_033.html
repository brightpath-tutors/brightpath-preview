Question 1Grade 7 • NS
A financial analyst is reviewing daily bank account balances for five different clients. The balances are: -$30, $12, -$7, $0, and $18. Arrange these bank account balances in ascending order (from least to greatest).
A. $0, $12, $18, -$7, -$30
B. -$7, -$30, $0, $12, $18
C. $18, $12, $0, -$7, -$30
D. -$30, -$7, $0, $12, $18
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Ascending order means arranging numbers from the smallest (least) to the largest (greatest).
For integers, numbers further to the left on a number line are smaller, and numbers further to the right are larger. Negative numbers are smaller than positive numbers, and zero is between them.
2
Negative integers: -$30, -$7. Comparing these, -$30 is further to the left on the number line than -$7, so -$30 < -$7.
When comparing two negative numbers, the number with the greater absolute value is actually smaller.
3
Positive integers: $12, $18. Comparing these, $12 < $18.
For positive numbers, the number with the smaller value is indeed smaller.
4
Zero is greater than all negative numbers and less than all positive numbers.
On the number line, zero is to the right of negative numbers and to the left of positive numbers.
5
Combining the ordered negative numbers, zero, and ordered positive numbers gives: -$30, -$7, $0, $12, $18.
The final ascending order is formed by placing the smallest negative number first, followed by larger negative numbers, then zero, and finally the positive numbers from smallest to largest.

Answer: -$30, -$7, $0, $12, $18 (Option D)

Question 6Grade 7 • NS

A. 0m, -3m, -6m, -9m, -14m
B. 0m, -14m, -9m, -6m, -3m
C. -14m, -9m, -6m, -3m, 0m
D. 0m, -3m, -6m, -14m, -9m

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Descending order means arranging numbers from greatest to least. In the context of depth, 'closest to ground level' implies a greater (less negative or positive) number, while 'deepest' implies a smaller (more negative) number.
0m is ground level. Negative numbers indicate depth below ground. A smaller negative number is closer to 0 and thus 'closer to ground level' in this context.
2
There are no positive depths, but 0m represents ground level.
0m is the highest point among the given depths.
3
Negative integers: -9m, -3m, -14m, -6m. Ordered from greatest (closest to 0) to least (deepest): -3m > -6m > -9m > -14m.
When ordering negative numbers from greatest to least, the number with the smallest absolute value comes first, as it's closest to zero.
4
Combining 0m with the ordered negative numbers: 0m, -3m, -6m, -9m, -14m.
The final descending order places ground level (0m) first, then the negative depths from shallowest to deepest.

Question 10Grade 7 • NS
An archeologist is studying artifacts from five different historical periods. The years associated with these artifacts are: 150 AD, 75 BC, 250 BC, 25 AD, and the reference year 0. Arrange these years in ascending order (from oldest to most recent). (Hint: Treat BC years as negative numbers.)
A. 0, 25 AD, 75 BC, 150 AD, 250 BC
B. 250 BC, 75 BC, 0, 25 AD, 150 AD
C. 150 AD, 25 AD, 0, 75 BC, 250 BC
D. 75 BC, 250 BC, 0, 25 AD, 150 AD
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
150 AD = +150 75 BC = -75 250 BC = -250 25 AD = +25 Reference Year = 0
BC years are represented as negative integers because they occurred before the reference year 0. AD years are positive integers as they occurred after year 0.
2
Ascending order means arranging numbers from smallest to largest. For historical years, this translates to oldest to most recent.
Numbers further to the left on the number line represent older dates (more in the past), while numbers further to the right represent more recent dates.
3
Negative integers: -75, -250. Ordered from smallest (oldest) to largest (more recent): -250 (250 BC) < -75 (75 BC).
The BC year with the larger number (e.g., 250 BC) corresponds to a smaller integer, meaning it occurred earlier.
4
Positive integers: +150, +25. Ordered from smallest (oldest) to largest (most recent): +25 (25 AD) < +150 (150 AD).
AD years are ordered conventionally from smallest to largest.
5
Zero is the reference year, which is more recent than any BC year and older than any AD year.
Zero falls between BC years and AD years on the historical timeline (number line).
6
Combining the ordered negative years, zero, and ordered positive years: -250, -75, 0, +25, +150. Converting back: 250 BC, 75 BC, 0, 25 AD, 150 AD.
The final ascending order (oldest to most recent) is formed by starting with the earliest BC year, progressing to later BC years, the reference year, and then the AD years in increasing order.

Answer: 250 BC, 75 BC, 0, 25 AD, 150 AD (Option B)
