Question 1Grade 6 • EE
Sarah bought 3 notebooks and a pen. The pen cost $2. She spent a total of $11. How much did each notebook cost?
A. $13
B. $9
C. $1
D. $3
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'n' be the cost of one notebook. The total cost is 3 notebooks plus the pen cost, which equals $11. So, 3n + 2 = 11.
Translate the word problem into a mathematical equation. '3 notebooks' means 3 multiplied by the cost of one notebook (3n). 'A pen cost $2' is an additional cost. 'Total of $11' is the sum.
2
3n + 2 - 2 = 11 - 2 3n = 9
To get the term with 'n' by itself, subtract the constant term (2) from both sides of the equation. This maintains the balance of the equation.
3
3n / 3 = 9 / 3 n = 3
To find the value of 'n', divide both sides of the equation by the coefficient of 'n' (which is 3). This isolates 'n' and gives its value.

Answer: $3 (Option D)

Question 2Grade 6 • EE
A football team scored 4 times as many points as their opponent, plus 5 extra points. If the team scored a total of 29 points, how many points did their opponent score?
A. 6 points
B. 34 points
C. 7 points
D. 24 points
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'p' be the points scored by the opponent. The team scored 4p + 5 points, which equals 29. So, 4p + 5 = 29.
Represent the opponent's score with 'p'. '4 times as many points' means 4p. 'Plus 5 extra points' is an addition. 'Total of 29 points' is the sum.
2
4p + 5 - 5 = 29 - 5 4p = 24
Subtract the constant term (5) from both sides of the equation to isolate the term with 'p'.
3
4p / 4 = 24 / 4 p = 6
Divide both sides of the equation by the coefficient of 'p' (which is 4) to find the value of 'p'.

Answer: 6 points (Option A)

7w + 8 - 8 = 64 - 8 7w = 56

Question 4Grade 6 • EE
A recipe calls for 9 cups of sugar, plus 12 tablespoons for each cake. If a baker uses a total of 57 tablespoons of sugar, how many cakes did they make?
A. 4 cakes
B. 48 cakes
C. 5 cakes
D. 66 cakes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'c' be the number of cakes. The total tablespoons of sugar used are 12 tablespoons per cake plus 9 cups (converted to tablespoons if necessary, but here 9 cups is just a fixed amount of sugar, so 9 is the constant). So, 12c + 9 = 57.
Define 'c' as the number of cakes. '12 tablespoons for each cake' means 12c. '9 cups of sugar' represents a fixed amount (constant 9). 'Total of 57 tablespoons' is the sum.
2
12c + 9 - 9 = 57 - 9 12c = 48
Subtract the constant term (9) from both sides of the equation to isolate the term with 'c'.
3
12c / 12 = 48 / 12 c = 4
Divide both sides of the equation by the coefficient of 'c' (which is 12) to find the value of 'c'.

Answer: 4 cakes (Option A)

Question 5Grade 6 • EE
John spent 75 minutes on homework, which was 10 times the amount of time he spent reading, minus 15 minutes. How many minutes did John spend reading?
A. 7.5 minutes
B. 6 minutes
C. 9 minutes
D. 90 minutes
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'r' be the time John spent reading. The homework time was 10 times the reading time, minus 15 minutes, which equals 75 minutes. So, 10r - 15 = 75.
Represent the reading time with 'r'. '10 times the amount of time' means 10r. 'Minus 15 minutes' is a subtraction. '75 minutes on homework' is the total.
2
10r - 15 + 15 = 75 + 15 10r = 90
To get the term with 'r' by itself, add the constant term (15) to both sides of the equation. This is the inverse operation of subtraction.
3
10r / 10 = 90 / 10 r = 9
Divide both sides of the equation by the coefficient of 'r' (which is 10) to find the value of 'r'.

Answer: 9 minutes (Option C)

🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1

2
14s + 1000 - 1000 = 29000 - 1000 14s = 28000
Subtract the constant term (1000) from both sides of the equation to isolate the term with 's'.
3
14s / 14 = 28000 / 14 s = 2000

Question 8Grade 6 • EE
A train travels at a constant speed for 17 hours. After a stop, it travels at the same speed for another 3 hours, covering an additional 120 miles. If the total distance traveled was 800 miles, what was the train's speed?
A. 680 mph
B. 37.5 mph
C. 46 mph
D. 34 mph
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 's' be the train's speed. The distance for the first part is 17s. The distance for the second part is 3s. The additional 120 miles is a constant. The total distance is 800 miles. So, 17s + 3s + 120 = 800.
Represent the speed with 's'. The first leg is 17s, the second is 3s. They combine. 'Additional 120 miles' is a fixed value. 'Total distance of 800' is the sum.
2
(17s + 3s) + 120 = 800 20s + 120 = 800
Add the terms with 's' together (17s + 3s) to simplify the equation.
3
20s + 120 - 120 = 800 - 120 20s = 680
Subtract the constant term (120) from both sides of the equation to isolate the term with 's'.
4
20s / 20 = 680 / 20 s = 34
Divide both sides of the equation by the coefficient of 's' (which is 20) to find the value of 's'.

Answer: 34 mph (Option D)

Question 9Grade 6 • EE
The current population of a wildlife reserve is 405 animals. This is 18 times the number of animals that were there last year, after 63 new animals were added. How many animals were there last year?
A. 468 animals
B. 19 animals
C. 342 animals
D. 20 animals
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Let 'a' be the number of animals last year. The current population is 18 times 'a' plus 63 new animals, which equals 405. So, 18a + 63 = 405.
Represent the number of animals last year with 'a'. '18 times the number of animals' means 18a. 'After 63 new animals were added' is an addition. 'Current population of 405' is the sum.
2
18a + 63 - 63 = 405 - 63 18a = 342
Subtract the constant term (63) from both sides of the equation to isolate the term with 'a'.
3
18a / 18 = 342 / 18 a = 19
Divide both sides of the equation by the coefficient of 'a' (which is 18) to find the value of 'a'.

Answer: 19 animals (Option B)

3
