Question 1Grade 7 • RP
A map in a history textbook has a scale of 1 cm = 50 km. If two cities are 3.5 cm apart on the map, what is the actual distance between them?
A. 53.5 km
B. 17.5 km
C. 175 km
D. 14.28 km
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Scale: 1 cm (map) = 50 km (actual) Map distance = 3.5 cm
Identify the given scale and the distance on the map.
2
Actual distance = Map distance × Scale factor Actual distance = 3.5 cm × 50 km/cm
To find the actual distance, multiply the map distance by the scale factor.
3
Actual distance = 175 km
Perform the multiplication to get the final actual distance.

Answer: 175 km (Option C)

Question 5Grade 7 • RP
A diagram illustrating a recipe shows a cookie with a diameter of 2 cm. If the actual cookie should have a diameter of 8 cm, what is the scale factor of the diagram to the actual cookie, in simplest form?
A. 4:1
B. 2:8
C. 1:8
D. 1:4
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Diagram diameter = 2 cm Actual diameter = 8 cm
Identify the corresponding lengths for the diagram and the actual cookie.
2
Ratio (Diagram : Actual) = 2 cm : 8 cm
Form a ratio comparing the diagram's measurement to the actual measurement.
3
Simplify the ratio: 2/2 : 8/2 = 1 : 4
Divide both parts of the ratio by their greatest common factor (2) to simplify.
4
Scale factor = 1:4
Express the simplified ratio as the scale factor.

Answer: 1:4 (Option D)

C. 1:1000
D. 100:1
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Drawing length = 5 mm Actual length = 0.005 mm
Identify the given lengths for the drawing and the actual cell.
2
Ratio (Drawing : Actual) = 5 mm : 0.005 mm
Form a ratio comparing the drawing's measurement to the actual measurement.
3
Eliminate decimal: Multiply both sides by 1000 (5 × 1000) : (0.005 × 1000) = 5000 : 5

Divide both parts of the ratio by their greatest common factor (5) to simplify.
5
Scale = 1000:1
Express the simplified ratio as the scale.

Question 9Grade 7 • RP
A scientist draws a diagram of a cell that is 4.8 cm wide. If the actual cell is 60 micrometers wide, what is the scale of the drawing to the actual cell, in simplest form? (Note: 1 cm = 10,000 micrometers)
A. 800:1
B. 1:800
C. 80:1
D. 48000:60
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Drawing width = 4.8 cm Actual width = 60 micrometers Conversion: 1 cm = 10,000 micrometers
Identify the given measurements and the unit conversion factor.
2
Convert drawing width to micrometers: 4.8 cm × 10,000 micrometers/cm = 48,000 micrometers
Convert the drawing's width to the same units as the actual width (micrometers).
3
Form the ratio (Drawing : Actual) = 48,000 micrometers : 60 micrometers
Form a ratio comparing the drawing's measurement to the actual measurement, both in micrometers.
4
Simplify the ratio: 48,000/60 : 60/60 = 800 : 1
Divide both parts of the ratio by their greatest common factor (60) to simplify.
5
Scale = 800:1
Express the simplified ratio as the scale.

Answer: 800:1 (Option A)

Question 10Grade 7 • RP
A city planning map shows a new park design. On the map, the park is a rectangle measuring 15 cm by 20 cm. If the map's scale is 1:500, what is the actual area of the park in square meters?
A. 1500 m²
B. 7500 m²
C. 300 m²
D. 750,000 m²
E. None of the above
🔒 Backend review only — not visible to students
Step-by-step solution
1
Map dimensions: 15 cm by 20 cm Scale: 1:500
Identify the given map dimensions and the scale.
2
Interpret the scale: 1 unit on map = 500 units in reality. So, 1 cm (map) = 500 cm (actual). Convert actual cm to meters: 500 cm = 5 meters (since 100 cm = 1 m).
Convert the ratio scale into a linear scale with more practical units (meters) for actual measurements.
3
Calculate actual dimensions in meters: Actual length = 20 cm (map) × (5 m / 1 cm) = 100 m Actual width = 15 cm (map) × (5 m / 1 cm) = 75 m
Multiply each map dimension by the converted linear scale to get the actual dimensions in meters.
4
Calculate the actual area: Area = Actual length × Actual width Area = 100 m × 75 m = 7500 m²
Multiply the actual length and width to find the actual area of the park.

Answer: 7500 m² (Option B)
